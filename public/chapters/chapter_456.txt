Definition of Creature (2)

The Plateau of Corruption.

A sulfurous wind howled across the barren wasteland. Not a single blade of grass broke the cracked, lifeless earth. Dark clouds stretched endlessly to the horizon, splitting open with jagged lightning. It was a desolate, forsaken place—dry, silent, and utterly devoid of life.

The 3rd Special Task Force crashed onto the plateau in a violent descent, far more brutal than any landing Shirone had experienced during her time with Elijah. She tumbled across the ground, her body battered by the impact of an uncontrollable force.

“Aw, that’s gotta be the head. Senior, are you okay?”

Shirone blinked, her vision swimming as she took in the surroundings.

Eight massive rectangular panels, each four meters high, stood in a ring around them. Beyond them, fields of two-meter-long metallic needles stretched as far as the eye could see, densely packed like a forest of steel. Lightning struck again, and a surge of raw electricity coursed through the needles, flooding into the panels that enclosed the area.

“This…”

Joe’s voice was low, tense. “This isn’t a natural convergence of emotions. It’s an artificial device—engineered to collect and channel light.”

“Built by the Meccans?”

“No one knows. It predates the Subjects’ arrival in Heaven. Only the angels might remember who constructed this cursed place.”

Another bolt of lightning split the sky. Thousands of electric tendrils lashed downward, vanishing into the earth. The ground pulsed with a faint blue glow, like veins of light spreading beneath the surface.

A blinding flash erupted from one of the panels. Shirone flinched, then snapped her head up.

“We need to move. Staying here is too dangerous.”

The team took flight, veering away from the field of needles. Though they could all fly, the air itself felt charged, unstable—unsafe. They landed four kilometers away, in a massive crater carved into the plateau.

At its center, the ground had caved in, as if something had plummeted at terrifying speed. After clearing the debris, they uncovered a large iron plate embedded deep underground. Something lay buried beneath it.

“This is why they call it the Tomb of Angels.”

Joe nodded grimly. “For centuries, cults have tried to excavate these ruins. All failed. At one point, an entire excavation team attempted to remove the entire section. But dig past a certain depth, and the underground tributaries electrocute you. Hundreds died in a single incident.”

Shirone knelt, running her fingers over the iron plate. The engravings weren’t in any human language—this was *henna*, the script of angels.

A Nephilim might be able to open it. Maybe.

She glanced back at Fleur.

Activating the Immortal Function was no issue. But beyond that? Unknown.

“We’ll open it,” Shirone said. “If it’s dangerous, you can retreat immediately.”

Fleur stepped forward. The fact that heretics had risked their lives for centuries to reach this place meant something valuable lay below. And Shirone had her scalpel teleport—she could vanish in an instant if needed.

“Then let’s begin.”

She placed her hand on the steel plate and activated the Immortal Function.

The henna flared to life, glowing with an intense radiance that shot skyward like a beacon. The team staggered back as a deep, resonant echo vibrated through the soles of their feet, rising from far below.

“Damn. Let’s keep our distance.”

Jo Won’s voice was firm. Without hesitation, Shirone retreated from the crater.

*Cuckoo! Cuckoo!*

The iron plate trembled violently. Soil cascaded down as something began to rise from beneath.

“That thing?”

The 3rd Special Task Force stared in silence as a massive structure emerged—a towering, bell-shaped construct of dark metal, its surface etched with henna.

At three meters tall, the base split open in two, revealing an entrance.

After a long moment of stillness, Shirone flew forward, landing cautiously at the threshold.

Above the dark maw of the entrance, letters were carved into an iron plaque. Not henna—this was the language of the Subjects.

Shirone deployed a drone to scan the inscription. The translation came through via radio waves directly into her mind.

**Babel.**

“Bar… Bell?”

The crew murmured in confusion. That single word was in the Subjects’ tongue, yet none of them had ever heard it before.

Fleur turned to the group. “Babel. What does it mean?”

“I don’t know. It’s our language, but it’s not a word we’ve encountered.”

Not helpful.

“Shirone, what now?”

“We go in. Exploration is the mission.”

Without hesitation, Shirone led the way, casting Shining Magic as she stepped through the entrance.

A staircase spiraled downward into darkness. With each step she took, lights embedded in the walls flickered on, illuminating the path ahead.

Shirone paused, staring.

The staircase stretched on endlessly. Judging by scale, the structure above ground was merely the tip of something vast.

Eventually, they reached a circular chamber. Above the doorway, an iron plate bore the words:

**Control Room.**

“In the Subjects’ language,” Shirone noted.

“Hmm. Control room. That’s… understandable.”

Jo Won’s dry remark drew no laughter.

The lights flared to life, revealing a chamber filled with machinery—devices resembling those from the Great World War of Jebul, interconnected by thick bundles of wires.

Beyond the control panels, a massive cone-shaped pillar rose from the floor. Another, identical in shape, descended from the ceiling. Between their pointed tips, a writhing mass of pure electricity pulsed and shifted, trapped like a living thing.

“I—what…?”

The crew stood frozen, stunned into silence.

This was no ordinary energy. It was a *spirit of light*—a phenomenon believed impossible to stabilize in its pure, natural form.

Fleur studied it from a distance, hand on her chin. “Hmm. Is that the power source?”

*‘That’s ridiculous. It’s far more than that.’*

The others chuckled at her naivety.

What they were seeing was the rarest entity in Purgatory—a light spirit forged by the singularity of the Plateau of Corruption, where lightning struck daily. This entire facility was designed to harvest and contain it, on a scale and over a timespan beyond comprehension.

The team exchanged glances.

By unit protocol, when operating abroad, standard rules applied: any special item recovered during a mission was to be evaluated by the commanding officer, with rewards distributed based on contribution.

Such treasures were rarely encountered in the field. But this—this was different.

*‘That’s mine.’*

They all thought it.

The mission hadn’t officially begun, but by regulation, the first to claim a major artifact had to present it to Shirone. Still, the light spirit was an opportunity beyond price—something no rebel army could afford to miss, even at the cost of their lives.

“First, I’ll check it out!”

Jo Won bolted forward, making a flimsy excuse.

“Wait! If you approach carelessly—!”

Fleur’s warning came too late.

Jo Won reached the spirit, hand outstretched, ready to initiate a contract.

*‘Done. With this, I—!’*

His heart pounded. Anticipation surged, so intense it nearly paralyzed him.

“I’ll make the contract!”

At that moment, marbles shot from hidden slots in the walls on both sides.

He flinched, raising his arms—

*Puff! Puff! Puff!*

Explosions ripped through his body.

“Ghk—!”

Shirone spun, eyes wide.

The steel walls were lined with grooves—embossed tracks where the marbles had launched. Yet not a single projectile lay on the ground. They ricocheted off the walls, vanishing into matching grooves on the opposite side.

The precision was terrifying. The force, unstoppable.

*‘I couldn’t even react. They passed through everything.’*

*Thud.*

Jo Won collapsed to his knees. His arms fell limp.

Then his face came into view—riddled with holes, mirroring the pattern of the wall.

The team tensed, weapons ready.

Grieving would come later. If they survived.

“You said this was the cockpit,” Shirone said, voice steady. “Then why the traps?”

“It’s not a trap.”

*Clank. Clank. Clank. Clank.*

As Shirone spoke, the walls of the control room began to shift. Sections peeled apart, revealing hidden compartments.

Inside each, a two-meter-tall steel golem stood encased like a corpse in a coffin.

Then, one by one, red lights ignited in their sunken eyes. They stepped forward, gripping the walls for balance.

**“Those who approach Babel will incur the wrath of God.”**

Two team members raised their hands in unison.

“Prepare collective magic!”

“Destroy with Gardin!”

Gardin was a rare, high-yield spell—only usable during lightning surges, but capable of unparalleled destruction. Even steel golems wouldn’t withstand it in this charged environment.

“Back off! I’ll destroy it!”

But instead of retreating, the others surged forward—pushing past Shirone and Fleur, ignoring the danger.

“Goes! Gardin!”

Shirone froze.

Fleur turned, eyes narrowing at Jo Won’s corpse—then back at the others.

No magic came.

The air didn’t crackle. No energy gathered.

“What—what? Why isn’t it working…?”

Collective magic required synchronized spirit force. But not a spark responded.

Shirone’s voice cut through the silence.

“It’s not an electric spirit.”

**“Punish those who approach Babel.”**

The golems split up, each targeting a different member.

“Fight! Everyone, ready!”

Fleur summoned a phoenix, slamming it into the ground. A blazing fireball erupted, engulfing one golem—but the metal body didn’t even scorch.

The Norse members, capable of wind magic, could do little in the confined space except dodge.

“Shirone! Use the laser!”

Fleur shouted for support.

But Shirone didn’t move.

“Shirone! What are you doing? It’s dangerous!”

Five golems advanced, their footsteps shaking the floor.

A shadow fell over Shirone.

A massive fist swung toward her face—

*Whoosh! Whoosh! Whoosh!*

But it stopped.

Centimeters from her nose, the golem’s fist froze in midair.

Shirone looked up.

The golem’s red eyes flickered rapidly, processing.

**Target Category – Human.**

**Attribute – Nephilim.**

**Code Search – Iris Pattern Identification.**

**Data Retrieval – Phase 1. Complete. Phase 2. Complete.**

**Result… … .**

**Result… … .**

**Result… … .**

Inside the golem, vast data streams converged. A single conclusion emerged.

*Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!*

All forty golems shut down simultaneously, their red eyes extinguishing as they collapsed forward like felled statues.

The impact shook the chamber, the tremor so strong it felt as if the entire structure might collapse.

“What? Why—suddenly…?”

The rest of the team stood paralyzed.

Fleur, still glowing from her phoenix spell, approached Shirone.

“What happened? Why did they shut down?”

“I don’t know.”

“You don’t know? But why didn’t you attack?”

Shirone hesitated.

She had felt no threat. No instinct to fight.

*‘Just like back then…’*

When the Behemoth had merged with her incarnation, the memory had faded—but the *feeling* remained. A sense of unity. Recognition.

Now, it was happening again.

Not through words. Not through logic.

But through something deeper.

“Fleur,” Shirone said slowly, her voice distant.

“I… I think I’ve been here before.”
