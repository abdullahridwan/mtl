Chapter 438  
Final Decision (8)  
~*~

It was a gloomy day. Shirone rolled up his clothes and packed dry food and first-aid kits for emergencies. As he lifted the fully loaded military backpack, he activated his [Kubrick] skill, slotting the bag into its designated space. Holding Armand in one hand, he examined the scabbard before securing it to Kubrick’s second slot. The total loaded weight came to 38.7 kilograms. He had packed it optimally, leaving just enough extra space for flexibility.

“….”

Shirone scanned the room to ensure he hadn’t forgotten anything, then checked the time. After confirming it was near midnight, he pulled Maya’s letter from his desk drawer and read it once more.

It was the day of the Supernatural Psychic Research Society’s presentation—the day Maya had fallen in love with him. It felt like destiny when he had stood before her at the senior year welcome party. He had asked who the girl he wanted to kiss, half-joking and half-serious, but his foolish delusion had convinced him she would choose him.

Her heart was engraved across four pages of letter paper. It was a confession where she had done her utmost to convey her sincerity, rather than embellish or soften the truth.

*If you accept my heart, please come out to the park in two days at midnight.*

*Even if you don’t come, I will never resent or hate you.*

*I want you to show your sincerity this time. I love you.*

Shirone folded the letter along Maya’s original creases and slipped it into an envelope. Then, as if handling something precious, he tucked it into his inner pocket and gently pressed it flat with his palm.

“Maya….”

He left the dorm earlier than the promised time. *Should I make her wait until this day?*

He had arrived at the park ten minutes ago. Beneath the single streetlamp, he waited for Maya to appear.

*Dong. Dong. Dong.*

The midnight bell tolled.

As the clouds thickened, wind and rain swept in. Thunder rumbled across the distant sky, and cold raindrops began to fall. The storm’s percussion drowned out his thoughts. Streams of water trickled down his wet hair, and his clothes grew heavy as they soaked through. Shirone kept his eyes fixed on the path Maya would take.

*Dong.*

The bell announcing one o’clock struck.

Maya still hadn’t come.

He heard the sound of splashing water and footsteps approaching from behind. It was only when the raindrops falling on his head were suddenly blocked that Shirone finally turned around.

Amy stood there, holding an umbrella.

The two simply stared into each other’s eyes. Amy wanted Shirone to speak first, but he had nothing to say.

Amy’s suppressed emotions finally erupted. Resentment, anger, bewilderment, betrayal, and perhaps even desperation welled up inside her. She gritted her teeth and spat out a trembling voice.

“Why did you come out?”

Shirone remained silent.

“Say it. Why did you come here? Really? Maya and….”

“It has nothing to do with you.”

Amy’s heart sank.

She had known Maya would confess. The reason she had agreed to help, despite knowing it, was the confidence she felt after seeing Shirone’s true feelings with her own eyes. She had agreed, and what she regretted most was her own insecurity about Maya’s courage. It had been Amy who paced nervously in the room, couldn’t stand the tension any longer, and ran to the park to find him.

But Shirone had been waiting for Maya. And now, he was looking at her with such cold, distant eyes.

“No. It can’t be.”

Amy’s voice cracked.

“What’s going on? I mean, you’ve been so weird lately. It’s still weird! Say something! Tell me what’s going on! If you don’t tell me, I won’t see you anymore.”

“Yeah. I don’t care.”

Amy couldn’t believe it. *Why do you have to treat me so coldly?* It wasn’t the Shirone she used to know. She was afraid. She felt completely exposed, emotionally naked before him for the first time.

“Why? Why do you do this? Why won’t you say anything? Is it because I’m not like Maya? Foolish… me too, I… you….”

Shirone stepped forward and hugged Amy tightly. It was the only way to hide the pained, distorted expression on his face. Hot tears mixed with the cold rainwater and streamed down his cheeks.

“Amy, thank you so much.”

Overwhelmed and embarrassed, Amy burst into tears. She grabbed Shirone’s collar and tried to push him away, but her strength failed her.

“No. Don’t hug me. I mean, say it! Tell me why! You bastard!”

Her hands lacked any real force. Time seemed to push her backward, a cliff waiting at her feet. For the first time, she wished she could turn back the clock.

Shirone spoke his final words with a sad smile.

“Goodbye, Amy….”

*Thud.*

The umbrella fell from Amy’s hand. The sound of the stinging rain seemed to swell. Even as Shirone walked away, she couldn’t move, her face frozen in a daze. Tears blurred her vision until she couldn’t see anything.

“Ugh… ugh…”

The moment the sobs trapped in her throat finally broke free, the sky seemed to weep alongside her.

* * *

*Thump. Thump. Thump.*

Collie, the head teacher of the senior class, was awakened by frantic knocking on the door of the annex. He hurriedly pulled on his gown, rubbed the sleep from his eyes, and opened the door to find Maya soaked to the bone.

“Maya? What are you doing out at this hour?”

“Teacher….”

Maya couldn’t control her emotions and collapsed into Collie’s arms, weeping uncontrollably.

Collie was taken aback, but he quickly assessed her condition. His first priority was to calm her trembling body. He built a fire in the fireplace, sat Maya down, and handed her a towel. He boiled some cocoa and set it in front of her, but she only stared blankly at the flames, mesmerized.

*It must have been a heartbreak.*

Collie had a strong hunch that the day Maya had spoken of was today. He hadn’t shown it during the senior schedule, but she was a disciple he loved deeply—so much so that he had personally enrolled her in the magic school and taken her under his wing.

*A young child. She can’t even take care of her own feelings because she’s too busy worrying about others.*

Collie first saw Maya at the opening of an outdoor concert by the singer Canary. Commoners were allowed outside the barricades to watch, a setup the nobles seemed to enjoy. The event planner had devised a strategy to highlight the main act by featuring a commoner in the opening performance. That was how fourteen-year-old Maya was recruited.

But the planner’s scheme backfired. Maya’s voice was so beautiful it gave everyone goosebumps. No wonder the nobles were offended. As soon as her song ended, they began to boo and criticize. It was said that some nobles even summoned the tribal chief, offering a large sum of money to have Maya brought to their beds. The chief attacked a nobleman in protest, was beaten to death, and the tribe was expelled. Maya gave up her dream of becoming a singer that very day.

Collie had recommended she become a wizard instead. She had a gentle temperament, but she possessed immense, almost omnipotent talent. Within two years, she had succeeded in entering the Spirit Zone.

*I never wanted her to become a great wizard. Wouldn’t it be enough if she could just become a wizard, support her tribe, and sing the songs she truly wanted to sing?*

“Did Shirone-kun not come out?”

Maya gently lifted the corner of her mouth. It was the only comfort she could find in her pain.

“No. He gave it to me. Like an idiot, I told him not to come out like that. It wouldn’t have mattered if I had just walked away, but I came out and confessed. For me, he waited over an hour.”

Maya covered her face with both hands.

“It hurts my heart. Because that kind of Shirone is so good. Because I want to be his woman.”

“Shouldn’t you have walked away? Could it be that he really liked you?”

Maya shook her head.

“I knew from the beginning that I couldn’t win Shirone’s heart. I confessed without knowing the outcome. I liked him so much that I couldn’t stand keeping it inside. So I went in knowing it was hell.”

Maya looked back at Collie with a terrified expression.

“But what should I do? It hurts so much. I feel sick, and I think I might die. Can I forget Shirone? Can I go back to the way I was before meeting him?”

Collie spoke his mind frankly.

“Maya, you cannot erase past memories. It is something you will have to carry for the rest of your life.”

“I don’t think I can. Being so sick… how….”

Maya finally sobbed.

Collie moved closer, wrapped his arms around her, and patted her back gently.

“There is no need to be afraid. Scars don’t disappear, but as you live, so many new scars form that you’ll eventually forget which ones Shirone left behind. Then you’ll be fine again. Just keep living like that.”

Maya pressed her face against Collie’s chest.

“It isn’t living. With no hope… how can I live?”

“Maya, I don’t live for hope. I create hope in order to live. You may never forget it, but everything will be alright. You can create another hope.”

“I hate that. I want Shirone to come to me. I want him to love me with his own words.”

When a knife cuts you, it doesn’t hurt at first. What really hurts is the moment you realize you’ve been cut. That was why the emotion Maya felt now wasn’t pain—it was fear. Fear of the pain to come.

The more tender the heart, the harder it will break. But Maya, an artist is someone who observes humanity. Even when looking down from a high place, they never truly see people. So remember today’s pain. Sing from the lowest place. Then, one day, everyone will help heal your wounds.

Collie stroked Maya’s back. Her shoulders trembled pitifully, like a fledgling bird whose wings had been snapped by a storm.

* * *

3 a.m.

Since he had no intention of sleeping anyway, Shirone returned to the inn, washed up, and changed into fresh clothes. As he sat at his desk and tried to quiet his thoughts, the lingering echo of Amy’s crying seemed to leak out on its own.

*Has everything been sorted out now?*

He had withdrawn from the Supernatural Psychic Research Society. Nade had been furious, but eventually hugged him and told him to come back no matter what. And today, he had finally said his goodbyes to Maya and Amy.

He wanted to sever all ties before going to heaven. No, he *had* to. If he harbored any regrets here, he would hesitate at the decisive moment. He would falter in a situation where death was inevitable.

In a short span of time, Shirone had untangled every tie that could be untangled.

Yet, the faces of precious people still surfaced in his mind. Lian, Tess, and… his parents, who had always wanted nothing more than their son’s happiness. He had been fortunate enough to say “I love you” before leaving home. It was, truly, the best thing in Shirone’s life.

He opened his desk drawer and took out a clean sheet of stationery. The greetings he could no longer speak aloud had no choice but to be conveyed in writing.

Spinning the nib between his fingers, Shirone finally began to write.

*History.*

*Black. Sobbing.*

Tears flowed from his sorrow. He had wanted to graduate from magic school. He had wanted to experience the world alongside his friends. He had wanted to feel again the thrill he experienced when he held Amy’s hand.

“Mom….”

Shirone’s only parents were Vincent and Olina. That fact would never change. He should have known this before going to heaven. No, he hadn’t met Ikael from the beginning. But he had finally found out.

He hadn’t expressed it to anyone, but in truth, he finally held the answer to the question that had weighed on his heart for his entire life.

*I’m sorry. I’m sorry.*

Shirone wiped his tears with his sleeve and continued writing his final letter. He poured out his gratitude to those who had helped him, recounted how happy he had been because he had received so much love, and expressed his deep respect for his parents.

Everything that had been stored within him for nineteen years was finally being emptied.
