Divine Transcendence (2)

Tess’s classmates looked at her with envy.

“Tess, I’ve thought this before, but you really have a gorgeous body. Do you work out separately?”

“What workout? It’s hard enough just keeping up with training. Besides, it’s not all good. A body like this is a nuisance on days like today when we have to fight at close range.”

One of the girls gave her a sympathetic smile, as if she understood.

“That’s true. All kinds of boys show up in these matches. Some of them try to use it as an excuse to touch you.”

“Of course, if anyone tried that with me, I’d break his back.”

Another classmate joined in.

“It is a disadvantage. We have to compete against muscle monsters who look like they eat meat ten meals a day.”

Tess, who had been wearing a tank top, pulled on her training uniform.

“There are other events where women have the advantage. Anyway, this is the last evaluation, so let’s do our best.”

“Yeah. Once vacation starts, I’m sleeping first. Then I’m losing some muscle. I’m definitely getting a boyfriend this year.”

The girl, warming up lightly after changing, glanced at Tess.

“By the way, what about you? Are things with Lian still the same? That boy is seriously dense.”

Tess bent one knee back, pulling her foot up toward her hip as she laughed.

“Haha! It’s fine. Lian has big dreams. If my boyfriend succeeds, that’s good for me too.”

“Ugh. Don’t be like that. Make him chase you a little. If a pretty girl like you likes him, he should be on his knees thanking you.”

“I’m really fine. I’m not going anywhere, am I?”

She laughed it off in front of her friends, but in truth, she was not fine. Lately, Lian had been talking even less than before. It was obvious something was weighing on him, but he never told her what it was.

‘What is it? Is he hiding it even from me?’

She had decided to endure it only until today. Vacation started tomorrow. Even if his worries were serious, she would be able to ask him about them more comfortably then.

After leaving the locker room, the cadets headed down the hallway toward the training ground. A man broke away from the crowd and approached Tess. The moment she saw him, Tess’s face showed clear displeasure, but he was used to her attitude and spoke to her with a friendly look.

“Hello, Tess. Fancy meeting you here.”

“We’re in the same building. Of course we’d run into each other. The way you say it, people will think we met in hell.”

The man’s name was Rock. He came from a first-rate family, and his father was the current Deputy Minister of Defense of Tormia.

His father and Tess’s father, a commander, had been close comrades for a long time, so Rock had known Tess since childhood.

With his square jaw, blond hair, and deep-set eyes, he was considered attractive by almost anyone. His body was well built, and his swordsmanship was excellent. In social circles, there were already rumors that he had marriage prospects lined up.

However, Rock had chosen Tess as the woman he wanted most. The prestige of the Elzain family was considerable, and Tess herself, with her beauty and figure, lacked nothing as a potential partner.

“Tess, how about dinner tonight? We’ve trained hard for half a year, haven’t we?”

“Sorry, but no. I already have plans with Lian.”

“Don’t lie. Every cadet knows he doesn’t even notice you. What are you doing with him? You can have dinner with an old friend, can’t you?”

“No. I don’t like you, and I don’t want Lian paying attention to you for any reason. So let’s forget our old connection and act like strangers from now on.”

Rock sighed. He could not understand why Tess, who was clever and sensible, had fallen for Lian.

Lian’s skills were poor, and above all, he had no future. How could a swordsman with no knowledge ever become a commander?

“Tess, you need to think carefully. How long are you going to keep acting like a child? You have dreams too. With me, you can make those dreams come true. Lian will never be able to make you happy.”

Tess pressed her lips together, as if he was not even worth answering. Rock felt the iron wall in her gaze. But the more she rejected him, the stronger his desire to possess her became.

“Tess. What are you doing? Let’s go.”

Tess’s cold expression brightened in an instant. Without even saying goodbye to Rock, she ran toward Lian, who was waiting at the end of the hallway.

‘That bastard…!’

Rock glared at Lian, trembling with humiliation. But, as always, Lian merely met his murderous gaze with calm, indifferent eyes, then turned away.

Lian and Tess walked in silence. Lian had been taciturn lately, and Tess was worried he might be bothered by her relationship with Rock.

It was not until they had left the building that Lian finally spoke.

“Is Rock bothering you again?”

Tess hurriedly waved her hands.

“No. How could he bother me? You really don’t need to worry about it. I told him not to talk to me from now on.”

“Don’t worry. I don’t care.”

Tess pursed her lips. If Lian had been bothered by Rock, that would have caused its own problems, but hearing such an indifferent reply made her feel strangely wounded, as if she had been treated lightly.

“Lian. Lately, you’ve been…”

“How strong do you think Shirone has become?”

Tess closed her mouth.

What had she expected? From the beginning, there had been no room for her in Lian’s thoughts.

The reason Lian trained himself to the limit every day was to become a knight worthy of standing beside Shirone, who was growing at an astonishing speed.

If this was a master-servant relationship sworn by a knight’s oath, then that was only natural.

But Shirone was also a precious friend to Tess, and that was why she sometimes forgot the relationship between the two of them.

Tess brushed aside her disappointment and patted Lian on the shoulder.

“He must have gotten really strong, right? Shirone is a genius. But you’ve done your best too. When he sees you now, he’ll probably faint from shock.”

Lian smiled bitterly. Every time he did his best, what he felt most keenly was just how extraordinary Shirone truly was.

But now, Lian had also found the answer to the long agony he had carried. There would be no more hesitation.

“Yeah. I’ll have to surprise him properly.”

When they entered the training ground, many cadets had already gathered. After today, the accomplishments of the kingdom’s aspiring knights would be recorded once again as grades.

Tess had taken the top spot in most of the evaluations and was already among the leading group.

Lian, on the other hand, still had not escaped last place.

“Attention!”

When Quan entered the training ground, all the cadets straightened. The nervous tension they had shown when they first entered the facility was gone; now, they carried themselves with disciplined spirit.

“At ease. Today is the wrestling evaluation. You know what that means?”

“Yes, sir!”

Their unified response rang out.

It was enough to satisfy even the picky Quan, but he seemed to be in his worst mood of the year, and his tone was harsh.

“What are you waiting for? Take off your tops.”

As if it were a race, buttons came undone all at once. The male cadets stripped to the waist, exposing their bare upper bodies, and focused on Quan without flinching despite the cold.

No one looked at the female cadets. For anyone receiving knight training, that would be disrespectful, and even if someone did have such thoughts, the day Quan caught him would be the day he was finished.

However, Quan did not place any particular restrictions on the men looking at one another. Sizing up an opponent’s body was part of male aggression and wild instinct.

As the evaluation period approached, the number of cadets using the public bathhouse dropped sharply. The body was honest; it bore the process of training exactly as it had happened.

Showing one’s body before a competition was tactically unwise. That was why the topless wrestling evaluation was held on the final day.

The cadets’ attention focused on Rock’s body. His large muscles were separated like plates of armor, and the trapezius muscles rising from his neck were intimidating just to look at.

‘That’s an enormous bulk-up. How much did he grow in one month?’

‘He’s a human golem. If he catches me, there won’t even be bones left to collect.’

In terms of swordsmanship, Tess, who handled a sharp blade, ranked first. But in terms of physical ability, Rock, who aimed to become heavy infantry, held the advantage. Wrestling, in particular, was the skill he was most confident in, and he was skilled enough to break a bear’s neck with his bare hands.

“Wow…”

Exclamations rose here and there. Rock turned his head with a triumphant expression.

But the object of their admiration was not Rock.

It was Lian.

Everyone stared at him, unable to look away.

Lian’s body had developed without neglecting even the smallest muscle. It was surprising to see how many different kinds of muscles could exist beneath human skin. Every part of him seemed perfectly integrated. His body looked as solid as if it had been poured into a mold and compressed.

Even the male cadets, who were confident in their own physiques, were mesmerized. It was only natural that Tess had hearts in her eyes.

‘Lian…’

However, her gaze soon turned wistful. She knew better than anyone how much pain Lian had endured to make his body so perfect.

After consuming Ymir’s arm, Lian’s right arm had developed abnormally. Gaining stronger physical power was good, but losing balance was never a good thing.

From then on, Lian had subjected himself to extreme training, swinging a club heavier than his greatsword three thousand times a day.

Biological feedback was truly astonishing. As time passed, his entire body had begun to develop in a way that balanced out his right arm.

The result was Lian’s current body.

Having achieved perfect physical balance, his muscular strength was four times greater than it had been half a year ago.

He might have been the strongest person among those who could not use schema.

‘His body has been forged by the sword.’

While the cadets admired Lian’s appearance, Quan focused on its functional aspect.

It was not a body built for one-dimensional goals such as strength or endurance.

It was a form optimized entirely for the act of wielding a sword.

‘But it’s bizarre. This isn’t the first time I’ve seen the body of someone who has trained, but it feels like I’m seeing one for the first time.’

Quan’s sense of incongruity was accurate.

Through feedback from his abnormal right arm, Lian’s body had surpassed the limits of perfection that an ordinary human could reach.

‘In any case, I’ll know once I watch him. Let’s see what that body can do among schema users.’

Quan set aside his thoughts and raised his head.

“Everyone, attention. I’ll briefly explain today’s evaluation.”

The cadets came to their senses and focused on Quan. Rock, who had been glaring at Lian in irritation, turned his head away.

“Wrestling is a major subject for the heavy infantry position. So I’m not going to demand any special techniques from kids like you. As long as you’re engaged one-on-one at close range, I don’t care if you hit each other. I wouldn’t be able to separate it all anyway. Since your weight classes are different, victory and defeat will not be included in the evaluation criteria. And you may use [folding].”

At Quan’s final words, the cadets’ eyes changed.

Schema was a virtual diagram of the human body that produced its effects by overlapping multiple diagrams according to a specific build.

[Folding] was an advanced schema technique. It folded a specific human-body diagram in half, producing an effect as though two diagrams had been overlapped.

For example, if someone whose technique tree was based on muscular strength performed folding, their strength increased by more than double.

At a higher level, two folds could be applied, which was called a [double].

From there, it developed into [triple], [quadra], and [penta], and the known limit of folding a human could reach was seven times.

Of course, at the cadet level, even a single fold was undoubtedly difficult. However, Kaizen Swordsmanship Academy was a prestigious school where the most talented people in Tormia gathered.

Even if the instructors still treated them as children, more than half of the cadets could use basic folding.

Tess turned to Lian. As expected, his expression had darkened. Lian’s skills were not inferior to those of the other cadets. But as long as schema was included in the evaluation, he was always in last place.

Schema techniques were not limited to folding.

Countless advanced techniques had been developed: [transmission], which expressed the properties of two human-body diagrams at once; [reversal], which flipped the technique build upside down; and [intersection], which folded the human-body diagram diagonally.
