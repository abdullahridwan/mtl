Opportunity for Revenge (8)

“Geumgang Armed Forces.”

Shirone planted Armand upright into the ground and murmured a command. In an instant, the sentient demonic sword dissolved into organic matter, spreading across his body like living armor and reshaping itself into a sleek, form-fitting robe.

A cheer erupted from the Nord crew.

Shirone was currently touring the units under the 2nd Command, integrating rebel factions into the growing force. Today marked the final day of the schedule. When one of the Norse mages voiced skepticism upon seeing Shirone’s sword—after all, blades were the exclusive domain of the Kergoins in Heaven—Shirone had demonstrated its power.

The name “Light of Area 73” carried immense weight. On the battlefield, the reaction was far more enthusiastic than at headquarters.

– Concentration point shape change. Unified signaling system detected.

Shirone narrowed his eyes. These were terms he’d never encountered before.

As he focused, Armand transmitted a new insight directly into his mind.

– User cerebral search. Synchronization. Presenting the possibility of integration into a single mental system.

“Ahh… the Ultima System.”

Whenever a profound shift occurred within Shirone’s consciousness, Armand’s functions evolved in response. It made sense—Armand’s processing power was drawn directly from Shirone’s own brain.

– Name: Ultima System. Upon integration into a single mental system, the bond cannot be reproduced, transferred, or terminated.

– Awaiting user decision.

“Cannot be canceled? Is that really the case?”

It meant transitioning from a contract-based weapon—one that could be discarded at will—to a permanent, inseparable extension of the self. A perfect personalization.

In a way, it was ideal. Armand would become truly his. But it also meant relinquishing control over future ownership.

“Once it’s mine, I can’t even sell it.”

The market value was 4 billion gold. Not that Shirone would ever consider selling Armand, no matter the price. But eliminating the possibility of transfer—even outside of commerce—felt like a loss of freedom.

“What if we integrate?”

– Unpredictable.

Armand had no answer. It couldn’t analyze what Shirone himself didn’t understand.

“Either way, I don’t have a choice.”

Shirone set aside his hesitation. The Ultima System had to be activated.

If they could share thoughts through a single, unified signal, Armand would feel like a part of his own body. The uncertainty of the future paled in comparison to the desperate need for strength in the present.

Since arriving in Heaven, tomorrow was never guaranteed. If there was even a sliver of power to be gained, it was only rational to seize it with both hands.

– Constructing integrated mental system.

“Huh!”

Shirone threw his head back and spread his arms wide. Armand’s organic mass tightened violently around his body, constricting like a second skin. Dozens of tendril-like appendages burst forth, writhing like thorns before coiling into a whirlwind of motion, reshaping and reconfiguring.

“Whoaaaa!”

The rebels stared, wide-eyed.

The ragged, tattered robe had transformed into a clean, streamlined form—elegant, functional, and perfectly tailored to Shirone’s will. The organic edges moved as if alive, responding instantly to his thoughts.

[<Demonic Sword Armand> Ultima Version (Equipment: 0)]

“It’s incredible… it feels just like my own body.”

Until now, information had flowed through the brain. Now, signals passed through the realm of instinct, bypassing reason entirely. This was true integration—Shirone’s complex thoughts distilled into pure, immediate signals, seamlessly transmitted to the weapon.

Perfect compatibility. A complete neural link to the very ends of the ganglion.

‘Two Ultima Systems…’

Now, there existed another object capable of unified signal control—whether living or non-living. Even Shirone couldn’t predict how this would unfold in the future. But one thing was certain.

“Hey, Nephilim! Lead us to victory!”

“Until the light of Area 73 becomes the light of Heaven!”

The moment was perfect for propaganda.

* * *

Jinmai Sikjong Galtomic.

An S-rank creature in Purgatory Hunting. A gelatinous, shape-shifting entity capable of absorbing corrupt laws and transforming into a far more powerful form when attacked.

The Galtomic currently battling Armin’s party had taken the form of a centaur—human torso, equine lower body. With every punch, black lightning erupted, shattering boulders the size of cliffs.

S-rank meant it had no natural predators within a 100-kilometer radius. But its true power lay in its magical absorption—converting absorbed laws into raw strength.

“Crow! Crow!”

With a shriek, Galtomic launched into the air, fists swinging like hurricanes. The ground cratered beneath the impact, as if distance itself had been erased.

Armin’s party scattered, then counterattacked in unison.

A blizzard erupted, temperatures plummeting. Quan and Etella closed in, flanking the beast.

Rhythm layered upon rhythm—sword and fist moving as one. The relentless tempo overwhelmed Galtomic, forcing it to its knees.

As Armin’s slow magic took hold, Shiina unleashed a glacier—sixty tons of ice crashing down onto the immobilized monster.

The earth trembled.

Galtomic, battered and half-frozen, exploded upward in a final burst of energy, hurtling toward Shiina like a living spear.

At that moment, Quan shot forward in an S-shaped blur, appearing before Shiina mid-rotation.

His blade spun at ten revolutions per second, the sonic vibration piercing eardrums. The air itself seemed to split—and Galtomic was cleaved in two.

Whoops.

The rotational force carried through the wind, transferring to Shiina. Combined with her own freezing magic, it sent a sharp chill up her nose.

Shiina had over a dozen ways to counter surprise attacks—honed through experience, study, and training. But this time, she hadn’t needed any of them.

“Thank you,” she said quietly. “I was safe because of you.”

“I was just waiting for the right moment to counter.”

Shiina fell silent, watching Quan walk away.

It was something she’d come to realize since arriving in Heaven—he was cold, yes, but fundamentally different from her own detachment.

‘I’d like to have a real conversation with you… just once.’

She was growing curious about the swordsman named Quan. But for Quan, the encounter only deepened his discomfort.

“Damn it… it’s gone.”

There had been no flaw in the technique. The cut was perfect. The problem was his heart.

At the moment of the strike, all he’d wanted was for Shiina to acknowledge him.

A foreign emotion. He’d never cared what others thought—never wanted to peer into someone’s mind.

But now, he did. He wanted to know how his actions affected her.

‘Calm down. This is a battlefield. This is my grave.’

“Shiina, are you okay?”

Armin approached. Shiina immediately turned away, her expression cold once more.

“I’m not a child. Stop worrying.”

Etella observed the three of them—Armin, Quan, Shiina—and mentally mapped the tangled emotional currents.

Their teamwork wasn’t seamless, but somehow, this friction created a strange synergy.

‘Still… you’re amazing, Mr. Armin.’

Galtomic was a monster that could rival any apex predator on the continent. Etella had never been weak in hunts, but even so, this victory belonged to Armin.

Crowd control created the opening for lethal strikes. And Armin was, without question, the most powerful crowd control mage in the world.

‘If I keep this up, I’ll be able to run my own factory soon.’

As Etella had expected, when they returned to Nightmaeng, the bodyguards whispered among themselves.

At first, they’d been skeptical. But after a single day, they’d bagged an A-rank creature—and the resentment over the beheading of a colleague on the first day had faded into irrelevance.

Frank Wein, inspecting Galtomic’s corpse, smirked.

“Outstanding. I didn’t think you’d find a strategy so quickly.”

He’d never considered such an approach. Armin’s control had been flawless.

“Just out of curiosity—what elixirs came from it?”

Etella answered promptly.

“Twelve White Elixirs, thirty-two Intermediate Green Elixirs, fifteen Advanced Red Elixirs.”

“Outstanding. You’ve earned a break. Take some time—go spend it on the mainland.”

Armin shook his head.

“I’m not here to play. Feeder Kuzen, Photosynthesizing Monster Olkir, Carnivorous Mineral Ringer. Once we’ve killed all three, the contract is fulfilled.”

“Fair enough. After that, the entire factory building is yours. Build whatever you want.”

“Then let’s go.”

As Armin’s party left, Mitgan, standing beside Frank Wein, spoke up.

“Still… isn’t this too fast? Even the best mainland hunters take days to bring down a Galtomic.”

“Perhaps it’s that blind man. The others are capable, but he’s different. In hunting terms, he’s above Triple S.”

Frank Wein’s instincts were sharp—both in business and in the hunt.

“That’s what worries me. He might become a problem for Night Blind in the future.”

Frank Wein waved a hand.

“Relax. Merchants don’t chase people. Send the corpse to Resis. And don’t forget our terms.”

Mitgan opened his mouth as if to say more, then closed it and left.

Alone, Frank Wein’s smile stretched grotesquely.

“Keuk kuk… turns out this is going to be a very big game. Right, Resis?”

* * *

The Fuzix Machine flashed.

The glass tube disassembled, and Shirone emerged—his biological fusion complete.

The Type A clone, lacking the Ultima System, was visibly inferior to the Type B.

Until now, the body had accepted foreign traits without issue. But this time, the integration had triggered an uncontrollable collapse—form and structure destabilized.

Shirone’s body was now covered in crimson scales. Smoke curled from one eye.

It was the day of comprehensive fusion—corpses gathered from Night Blind were being combined. And for the first time, the traits of Galtomic, a Jinmai plant, were being added.

“Yes, Commander! Look at these numbers! Incredible!”

The in-house horsepower meter spiked exponentially.

Galtomic’s magic amplification, fused with Shirone’s incarnation, was generating unprecedented magical output.

Akamai trembled as Shirone’s mana surged.

Even enhanced beyond the prototype, Akamai struggled to contain the Antithesis.

Whoosh!

Photon cannons fired in every direction. Raysis watched, awestruck.

“Ah, Shirone… you’re beautiful.”

It was demonic love—obsessive, possessive, devoid of empathy.

“Commander! We must act! The lab—!”

The researcher’s scream was drowned out by another cannon blast. Raysis didn’t move.

With each fusion, Shirone drew closer to becoming the strongest being imaginable. And every scrap of data would be used to evolve Akamai.

“Whoop whoop. Shall we start with our first kiss?”

Raysis’s body began to transform.

Her skin flushed red. Her spine arched, vertebrae protruding like spikes. Her thighs hardened into rock-like muscle. Her feet stretched, reptilian claws forming.

“Kuaaaaa!”

Fire and lightning erupted from her mouth in a torrent, engulfing Shirone.

“Magnificent!”

Shirone’s body melted under the assault—then regenerated instantly.

The trait of Sensor: immortal cell proliferation.

“Kyakyakyakyakya! That’s amazing, Shirone! Don’t you think so?”

Shirone vanished—using Hororos’s invisibility ability.

Without the Ultima System, his mind was unstable, the traits of countless monsters warring within.

“He’s gone! Find him—now!”

The researcher lunged for the scanner—just as a jet of corrosive slime melted him into nothing.

“Aaaaaaa!”

Acid Poison King Muusa’s ability.

Even elixir-forged supermetal would dissolve under a direct hit.

Toxic fumes rose as the slime spread across the floor.

Researchers clutched their throats, choking. Raysis raised her hands to block the ooze.

In seconds, her flesh melted away, bones crumbling like burnt matchsticks.

“Keugh!”

Raysis had reached her limit.

If this continued, the lab would be destroyed—just as the researcher had warned.

“Release Akamai!”

The near-unconscious researcher slammed the scanner.

Shirone’s coordinates locked in.

Three more Akamai units burst through the side walls, activating the Antithesis.

Veins bulged in their eyes as they strained to contain Shirone.

Even then, Shirone moved—slowly, deliberately.

Raysis, now a grotesque monster, twisted her face in frustration.

‘Only four Akamai can hold him.’

It wasn’t just the new traits.

Through biological feedback, Shirone’s potential was exploding beyond prediction.

* * *

For the first time since taking the Night Blind contract, Armin’s group faced real difficulty.

The obstacle: Ringer, a Double S-rank carnivorous mineral creature.

Two meters tall. Fourteen tons.

It had four legs, but when moving at speed, it curled into a perfect sphere.

Its velocity made it hard to hit. But the real problem was its carapace—grayish-white metal, nearly indestructible.

Kwak Kwah Kwah Kwah!

Wherever Ringer rolled, a path of destruction followed. Trees, rocks—everything exploded.

Armin cast slow magic on both flanks. The time distortion caused Ringer to ricochet at insane speeds.

Bang! Kwak Kwa Kwa Kwam!

The impact should have shattered reinforced steel. But Ringer just bounced, unharmed, like a ball hitting a wall.

“Whoa… this is something else.”

Armin had never seen a creature like this.

Strength came in many forms, but Ringer was unmatched in sheer durability.

When Quan’s sword failed to even scratch the surface, Etella made her decision.

“At this rate, we won’t catch it before nightfall. We need to cooperate.”

Shiina nodded.

“Brother, slow it down. I’ll cast Absolute Zero.”

Etella agreed.

“Then I’ll finish it.”

After a brief huddle, they moved.

As Ringer slowed under Armin’s magic, Shiina unleashed her ultimate freezing spell—Absolute Zero.

“Kiaaaaaa!”

At the same moment, Etella charged.

Thousands Arms Guanyin Lightning Strike.

Her body became a blur, delivering rapid uppercuts that left afterimages.

As the dual fire field—the peak of flame interference—activated, 68,000 interference waves erupted.

Cuckoo coo coo!

Ringer’s body shuddered violently.

Five seconds later, its carapace developed hairline fractures.

Then—bang!

The creature exploded.

“Whoa.”

Etella collapsed, exhausted.

The most shocking part? The Yin-Yang Wave Fist had only caused minor structural failure.

“It’s insane. Definitely Double S level.”

Armin nodded.

“Without the wave series, this would’ve been impossible. But I’m curious… why does Night Blind want these corpses?”

Match. Match. Match.

Suddenly, applause echoed from the forest.

A chill ran through them. Armin’s group turned slowly.

Silence.

Then, from the trees, stepped Cage B Team—whom they’d met on the mainland. At the front, Assistant Team Leader Horkin approached, hands behind his back.

“Meet again in a place like this.”

Etella smiled.

“Isn’t fate something? Must’ve been connected in a past life.”

“Quite the performance. As I’d heard. By the way…” Horkin paused, eyes narrowing. “Where’s Gaold now?”

“Yes?”

Etella feigned ignorance, her expression unchanged.

Horkin smirked.

“I didn’t realize back then. Bishop of Carsis Monastery… Miss Romi Etela.”

As if on cue, energy surged through every member of Cage B.
