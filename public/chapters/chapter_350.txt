Chapter 350  
Second Outing (1)  
~*~

Arriving at the Karmis estate, Shirone checked his clothes once and walked toward the arched front gate.

Beyond the white iron bars stretched the carefree garden unique to the Karmis family. The guards, still as sharp-eyed and vigilant as ever, watched the surroundings without relaxing for even a moment.

As expected, the instant Shirone approached, one guard’s right hand moved to the hilt of the sword at his waist.

“Stop. What business brings you here?”

What had changed from half a year ago was his tone. This time, the guard used polite speech.

The guards at an aristocratic mansion usually changed shifts every four hours, and their assigned posts rotated each month. There was no chance the guards who had been here during Shirone’s last visit were standing at the gate today.

Even so, the guard could not treat Shirone carelessly. There was an unusual presence about him, something unlike an ordinary person.

After a year at the magic academy, Shirone had shed much of the air of the mountain boy he had once been. In particular, the high level of mental strength he had gained through real combat, experience, and training emitted an aura that even guards unfamiliar with magic could sense.

“Amy is my friend. Is she inside?”

The guards tilted their heads, then exchanged glances.

Half a year ago, Shirone had entered Amy’s room. However, he had never returned since then as an officially approved visitor, and the guards knew nothing of what had happened at the time. It was only natural that they could not simply trust his word and let him in.

“There are no guests scheduled for today. If you have business with the young lady, please follow the proper procedure.”

It was the answer Shirone had expected.

He clicked his tongue softly and turned around. He had come hoping that, if he was lucky, he might be allowed in right away. Since that had failed, he would have no choice but to write a letter and send it into the mansion.

“It’s all right. Let him in.”

Just then, a familiar voice came from inside the gate.

When Shirone turned back, Adele, the head butler of the Karmis family, stood there with a smile on his face.

The guards’ backs snapped straight.

To the people who lived and worked in the mansion, Adele must have felt like a messenger from hell.

Adele crossed through the front gate with his cane and stopped in front of Shirone, looking him in the eye.

Remembering what had happened half a year ago, Shirone braced himself in advance.

The pressure Adele had exerted through his gaze had been the first time Shirone had experienced such a technique. And as the head butler of the Karmis estate, Adele surely had not shown his full strength back then. Shirone could not afford to be careless.

But Adele did not use any technique.

Instead, he bowed with perfect courtesy.

“Welcome, Master Shirone.”

Shirone stood blankly for a moment, thrown off by the unexpected greeting, then hurriedly came to his senses and lowered his head.

“Yes. Have you been well?”

“Heh heh. The daily life of an old man never changes much. You have come to see Miss Amy, I presume?”

“Yes. I’m sorry for coming without sending word first. Is she home?”

Adele scratched his eyebrow awkwardly.

“You are aware of the situation, I assume. Perhaps because of that, Miss Amy has been feeling somewhat unwell lately. Miss Seriel came to visit the other day as well, but she was sent back.”

“Ah, I see.”

Seriel was Amy’s best friend. If even she had been turned away, then Amy truly must not want to see anyone.

But Shirone did not give up.

“Please tell her I’m here. If she says she doesn’t want to see me, I’ll leave.”

Adele hesitated for a moment, then nodded.

In truth, ever since Amy failed her graduation exam, he had been troubled by the atmosphere in the mansion. Amy was not the type to vent her irritation outwardly, so no one could soothe her properly. They could only watch from the sidelines.

But perhaps things would be different with Shirone.

In any case, this boy was the only male friend who had ever been allowed into Amy’s room.

“Very well. Please wait here.”

After instructing the guards to treat Shirone with respect, Adele climbed into the carriage waiting in the garden.

‘It has been about half a year. Just what happened to him?’

As the carriage began to move, Adele’s expression finally turned serious.

Pressuring the men who approached Amy had been one of the head butler’s few private amusements.

But this time, he had not even dared to try.

Shirone’s mind had become far sharper than before.

No—sharp was not nearly enough to describe it.

‘It was eerie.’

The moment Adele had made eye contact to begin exerting pressure, the hairs all over his body had stood on end.

Shirone’s overall temperament did not seem to have changed greatly. His intelligent, bright, and benevolent nature was the same as it had been half a year ago.

However, the energy in his eyes had changed subtly.

Something else now lived there.

A terrifying monster.

Adele could sense it because he was the head butler of the Karmis family, one of the greatest noble houses.

He could not guess what it was. But if he harmed Shirone, whatever lurked inside him would surely leap out, chew him up, and swallow him whole.

The moment their eyes met, Adele had understood that such a thing was not merely possible.

It was violent. Extreme. A monster beyond reason.

‘Is it truly all right to let him in? Of course, there is no way he came to visit a friend while unable to control his own mind…’

Adele withheld judgment.

There was no way Shakora, the head of the household, would fail to notice something that he had noticed. And in the end, it was not his place to decide.

Thinking that, he smiled bitterly.

‘What a frightening boy.’

How long had it been since Adele had felt even a brief chill like that?

Shirone had sealed such a monster deep within himself.

‘He has grown stronger in only half a year. What remarkable talent.’

The Karmis family was known as a lineage of geniuses. Among them, Amy’s talent had been unrivaled.

However, the scale of Shirone’s growth defied imagination.

If Amy was the fastest sprinter among humans, Shirone was a giant whose very stride belonged to a different order.

‘What drives him?’

The carriage stopped, and Adele’s thoughts stopped with it.

Holding his unanswered question in his heart, he opened the door to the mansion.

Quite some time had passed since the graduation exams ended, yet the house was still wrapped in a solemn atmosphere.

For Adele, who had spent his life loving the vibrant and colorful mood of the Karmis family, it was a difficult situation to endure.

“My lady.”

Shakora, who had been reading a newspaper, turned her gaze toward him.

“Master Shirone has come to visit Lady Amy. However, the young lady has informed the household that she will not meet anyone…”

“Hmm. Shirone?”

Shakora understood Amy’s feelings.

How could she not? Her fiercely proud daughter had made a mess of her graduation exam.

However, if it was Shirone, there was room to consider the matter.

Even though Seriel had been rejected, the feelings between a man and a woman were entirely different.

Isis, who had been listening from the kitchen, came out and spoke.

“I’ll ask Amy first.”

Isis went upstairs and entered Amy’s room on the second floor.

After a brief exchange inside, she came back out, walked to the railing, and shook her head.

Having received Amy’s refusal, Adele bowed his head without further thought.

“Then I will send Master Shirone back.”

“No. Let him come in.”

Isis looked worried at Shakora’s words.

“But, dear, Amy…”

“It’s fine. When Amy saw Shirone at school, she asked him to come find her. It would be rude to send him away like this. I also want to meet him. Let him in.”

“Very well.”

Adele left the mansion with a much brighter expression, relieved that at least he would not have to disappoint Shirone immediately.

Ten minutes later, Shirone arrived at the mansion.

Shakora sat waiting on the sofa, and just then Isis brought out a tray of tropical fruits.

Shirone walked over to Shakora and bowed his head.

“Hello, Lady Shakora.”

“Yes, welcome. Sit down.”

Shirone sat on the sofa and waited for the conversation to begin.

He had sensed the heavy atmosphere of the house the moment he entered, so he did not bring up Amy first.

Like Adele, Shakora noticed Shirone’s subtly sharpened temperament at a glance.

During the graduation exam, she had been too worried about her daughter to observe him closely. But after Amy failed, Shakora, as a parent, had become interested in Shirone’s performance as well.

‘The kingdom’s best prospect…’

Of course, that evaluation excluded the senior class.

Even so, Shakora did not think Shirone’s standing would fall significantly even if the senior class were included.

She had not said it to Amy, but after Amy entered school, Shakora had become so invested that she even collected articles about the magic society. And the student whose name she had heard until her ears were worn out was Dante.

When Shirone defeated Dante, the top student in the kingdom, even Shakora had been unable to hide her surprise.

But looking at him now, she could tell it had not been a fluke or a matter of luck.

In Shakora’s eyes, a desire to transcend the world burned within Shirone.

Adele might have called it a monster, but Shakora thought differently.

That, too, was Shirone.

‘This is not something a person can obtain just anywhere. Did some latent tendency manifest through acquired experience? But how?’

She had already heard the stories of the harsh events that had taken place in the Kingdom of Kazura. Even so, this presence was too transcendent for an eighteen-year-old boy to carry.

Realizing that she had been silent for too long, Shakora smiled bitterly and returned to the role of a parent.

“In truth, she isn’t physically unwell.”

“Yes, I know.”

Shirone had suspected as much.

But he did not think it was a lie. There were conditions more serious than physical pain.

Shakora scratched her head, troubled.

“To be honest, I don’t know what to do. She doesn’t seem to be suffering in any obvious way, but she seems dispirited. Do you understand what I mean?”

“Of course. I would have been the same.”

If anything, the beginning was easier to bear.

Once something had already happened, one could decide to start over.

However, once the thought settled in that one had to repeat another year of training, the failure of the graduation exam would sink into the bones.

With each passing day, regret would grow heavier, and shame would press endlessly against the heart.

“Amy is strong. She has truly done her best to make it this far since she was twelve. Perhaps that is why the wound is deeper.”

“Yes. For now, it may be better to leave her alone. In any case, that is how Amy is right now.”

Shakora shrugged as if declaring defeat. She meant that even she could not do much about her daughter’s mood.

Shirone turned his gaze to the second floor and confirmed that the door to Amy’s room was tightly shut.

He seemed to hear again the sorrowful crying he had heard outside her dormitory door on the day the graduation exams ended.

“May I go up?”

Isis looked troubled.

“I asked her, and Amy said she was ill and told me to send you back. With her personality, if you approach her blindly…”

“Haha. If she hits me, then I’ll take it.”

Shirone answered lightly. But beneath the humor, he made it clear that he had no intention of giving up.

Shakora sighed and fell into thought.

Among them, only Shirone could understand Amy’s current state of mind completely.

And the fact that Shirone, despite being so considerate, still insisted on seeing her meant that Amy’s condition might be worse than they had expected.

If there was no other breakthrough, a shock might be the better option.

“If you want to go to Amy’s room, I won’t stop you.”

“Thank you.”

Shirone rose from the sofa and climbed the stairs to the second floor.

Watching him go, Isis asked Shakora with an anxious expression.

“Dear, what are you going to do? With Amy’s personality…”

“It’s fine. He probably understands Amy’s current state better than anyone. Besides, he is Shirone. If she truly tells him to leave, he will leave. But if he still wants to meet her to the end, it means he believes she cannot be left like this.”

“And what if things get worse?”

“It will be all right. More than anything, he is a friend Amy trusts.”

Isis glanced at Shakora and pursed her lips.

‘Why are you praising some boy so much? You daughter fool…’
