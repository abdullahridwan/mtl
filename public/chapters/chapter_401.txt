Chapter 401  
Senior Class Information Session (1)  
~*~

A peculiar tradition had begun: the Class Two students were welcoming the new students of Class Three.

The moment Shirone stepped through the steel door, the booing vanished as if it had never existed. The students split to the left and right, opening a path for them.

Shirone walked forward through the applause. It was welcoming applause, but there was no laughter in it.

‘So these are Class Two.’

When the ten students in the graduating class finally graduated, the remaining twenty were ranked according to their graduation exam scores. In other words, the ten students standing here to welcome them were those who had fallen relatively early in last year’s graduation exam.

Amy was among them, clapping as well.

Last year, she had been eliminated in the third round, placing her in Class Two. Her overall rank in the graduating class was eighteenth.

Keeping his eyes fixed straight ahead, Shirone studied their faces through the edge of his vision.

Men and women.

For now, that was all he could sense from them.

Of course, he remembered some of their majors from watching last year’s graduation exam. But now that they were standing in front of him as seniors, they felt like complete strangers.

‘If I can’t break through here, I’ll never make it to the top.’

Class Two was the middle tier of the graduating class. It formed the boundary between the upper and lower ranks.

The average age of this year’s graduating class was 20.6 years old.

Shirone was 1.6 years younger than that average. However, considering that there were older students like Fermi among the seniors, most of them were likely around Shirone’s age, and some were even younger than him.

After passing through the path made by Class Two, the students of Class Three turned around in front of the steel door. They then stood face-to-face with the Class Two students, who had lined up opposite them.

“Welcome to the senior class.”

A man with a confident expression stepped forward.

Shirone recognized him the moment he heard his voice. Before they passed through the steel door, this had been the student who mocked them the loudest.

He was a handsome man with a flashy air about him. His blond hair was slicked straight back, and unlike most wizards, he wore a blue training suit that fit his body perfectly.

He was about 175 centimeters tall. Though slender, his build was so well-balanced that he did not look weak in the slightest.

His eyes were sharp, his nose high and wolf-like, and whenever he smiled, the tongue that flashed between his teeth looked strangely red, as if it had been soaked in blood.

“I’m Screamer, ranked first in Class Two.”

The instant the students of Class Three heard his introduction, the same thought crossed all their minds.

‘So he’s eleventh.’

‘Eleventh overall in the graduating class.’

Although Screamer majored in light, his field was different from Shirone’s photon magic and Dante’s information magic.

Strictly speaking, he was a close-combat type.

Like a Gunner, he was a hybrid. He mainly used various light-type spells to confuse his opponents, then finished them off with critical blows enhanced by schema’s physical abilities.

The only other person in the senior class who could use schema was Amy.

However, unlike Amy, who had only learned it for a time when she was young, Screamer’s schema was at a fairly high level.

In last year’s graduation exam, his ability to slip into an opponent’s blind spot with flashy teleportation movements and end the fight with his fists had drawn everyone’s attention.

It was said that after graduating from magic school, he planned to go to the Colosseum and study martial arts all over again.

After Screamer’s turn, Pony stepped forward.

Her straight blond hair fell neatly along her forehead, curving from side to side like a half-moon. Her characteristic expressionless face made it seem as though she intended to blend her beautiful appearance into the scenery itself.

With her pale face, crescent-shaped eyebrows, high nose, and pointed chin, one could easily mistake her for a doll standing there from a distance.

“I’m Pony. Rank twelve. My major is Hydropower. My series is Torrent.”

If Screamer’s introduction had been long, Pony’s was brief.

Even so, the students of Class Three could not take their eyes off her. She was certainly pretty, but more than that, they all knew the most important thing had been left out of her introduction.

‘Ah, so that’s Pony.’

The only member of the royal family at Alpheas Magic School.

Though she came from the maternal line and was far from the center of political power, she was still royalty. To the aristocrats, she was someone who could not be treated carelessly.

Of course, competition in the senior class was fair. Pony had not mentioned her lineage in her self-introduction because she knew there was no need to invite hostility with unnecessary ostentation.

Pride befitting royalty shone in Pony’s eyes. It reminded Shirone of Jion, the prince of Khazra. Of course, her gaze fell far short of Uorin’s absolute stare.

After Pony, the self-introductions continued.

Shirone listened carefully to each one. Then he felt the gaze of the woman standing opposite him and turned his head.

Everyone else was paying attention to the student currently introducing himself, but she had been looking at Shirone with mischievous eyes from beginning to end.

‘Maya… was it?’

She had brown skin, a plump body, and a round face, but her features did not give her a cute impression.

To Shirone, she was memorable in a particular sense.

She had been the first person eliminated from last year’s graduation exam. In addition, he had heard that she had placed last in the graduation exam the year before that as well.

Her specialty was sound magic. She mainly used sound to apply buffs and debuffs, but at the time, Shirone had not found her particularly impressive.

In a word, she lacked talent.

Her sound magic could not penetrate a wizard’s mind, and she was holding on with the basic magic she had learned in the advanced class.

Her fundamentals were solid, but her position was ambiguous. She did not have enough skill to deal with geniuses. One could call her the boundary between the advanced class and the senior class.

When Shirone looked up, Maya smiled and raised a hand.

“Hi.”

Shirone glanced around. It was not proper to hold a conversation while another student was introducing himself.

Maya, however, did not seem to care. She leaned toward him and spoke in a low voice.

“I know you. Arian Shirone.”

It was an awkward situation. Shirone could not decide whether to answer or ignore her. After struggling for a moment, he eventually whispered back.

“Ah, yes. I see.”

“I saw you at the research presentation last year. The ghost commotion. You really surprised me. How did you even come up with that idea? It was genius. I’ve been a huge fan ever since.”

Maya still remembered that day vividly.

She had been lying in bed, listening to the midnight bell, when a ghost came out of the ceiling. She had been so horrified that she ran out with the other students, and before she knew it, she had found herself in Central Park.

Then the three members of the Supernatural Psychic Research Society appeared on the roof of a building.

The presentation had exposed a loophole in everyone’s minds, and Maya had laughed so hard her stomach hurt.

“I really enjoyed the play too. Did the dress fit you? What did you put in your chest?”

Screamer frowned and turned around.

“Hey, Maya. We’re in the middle of introductions.”

“Oh, sorry. I was just so happy.”

“Tch. You really have no sense of occasion…”

Maya stuck out her tongue at Shirone with an embarrassed expression.

Shirone still could not get a read on her. However, the way the other Class Two students looked at her was not favorable.

Since Maya was always in last place, some of them did not truly acknowledge her as a senior.

The prime example was Screamer.

Having failed to enter Class One by a difference of a single rank, he found it even more irritating to be in the same class as Maya.

After several interruptions, the self-introductions finally ended. Screamer, his mood recovered, spoke with a bright expression.

“All right, now that we’ve gotten familiar with each other’s faces, shall we begin the Steel Gate’s traditional ‘Courage of Truth’?”

Pony’s expression turned sour, and she muttered under her breath.

“What tradition…? How vulgar.”

“Huh? Courage of Truth?”

As Shirone raised his eyebrows and waited for an explanation, Screamer opened his mouth with his characteristically exaggerated gesture.

“The Class Two student facing you will ask a question. For example, ‘Who’s the most handsome?’ Something like that. Then you just have to choose one of us.”

‘So that’s what it is.’

There was no better way to intimidate newcomers.

He had used “who is the most handsome” as an example, but no student here would ask such a pleasant question.

‘That must be why Amy told me to choose her no matter what.’

Screamer looked back at a young-looking boy and said, “Eider, you go first. You’re the youngest.”

“Tch. It’s not like anyone here is that much older.”

Eider was seventeen years old. He was also the reason Amy had not received the title of youngest graduating senior last year.

He had large, cat-like eyes, and dimples appeared on both sides of his face when he smiled, making him look even younger than his age. His short height for a man only strengthened that impression.

‘Why does age matter so much?’

Shirone tilted his head inwardly.

What did it mean to be the youngest in a class where everyone was competing against one another?

Even so, Eider only grumbled for a moment before obediently following Screamer’s instruction.

‘Maybe because the title of youngest is useful.’

If one acknowledged being young, one might be bothered with trivial tasks, but there were definitely advantages that came with it.

‘Still, even I lower my guard when I look at Eider.’

The person Eider chose to question was Arin.

The students of Class Two watched with interest.

It was Arin, of all people. No matter what question he asked her, the result would likely be interesting.

Eider thought for a moment, then smiled mischievously.

“Who is the ugliest person?”

“Huh?”

It was an unexpected question for Arin.

She could not properly recognize the shapes of objects. Moreover, rumors of this had spread throughout the school, so the seniors must already know about it.

The Class Two students naturally grew tense.

That was because, in Arin’s eyes, ugliness could refer not to outward appearance, but to inner distortion.

“The ugliest person?”

“Yes. Someone so ugly you wonder how a human being can look like that. Just choose someone like that.”

“Hmm.”

Arin stared at Eider, who was smiling innocently.

Then she pressed a finger to her lips and looked carefully over everyone in Class Two.

Some students avoided her gaze.

‘Why did he ask a question like that? Is he testing how I see people?’

Arin raised her index finger and pointed to someone on the right.

“Then I’ll choose him.”

He was such a fat student that one felt hot just looking at him.

Even though this was a senior class event, he was holding a bag of potato chips in one hand, as if he could not stop eating.

His eyes were narrow, buried beneath his cheeks, and his hair was a tangled mess that showed no sign of care.

It might have been fine if he had worn loose clothes, but the sight of him in tight garments that exposed every curve of his body was almost embarrassing.

‘That’s Luman.’

Luman, ranked thirteenth in the graduating class.

His specialty was trap magic, and his class was Crowd Control.

Since he belonged to a type that did not stand out in one-on-one fights, he was often at a disadvantage. On top of that, his eccentric personality made things even harder for him, despite his excellent skills.

Screamer clapped as if to punctuate the moment.

“Yeah, as expected, a woman’s eye is sharp. He looks like a monster. Well, who knows? If his outside looks like that, his heart probably looks the same.”

Some of the students burst into laughter.

Among them was Boyle of Class Three. However, there was no sincerity in his smile.

The psychological battle had already begun, and it seemed Class Two had decided from the start to take an aggressive approach.

Despite being mocked, Luman only continued chewing his potato chips. Then he pointed at Boyle and asked, “But why are you laughing? Should you really be laughing?”

Boyle frowned exaggeratedly.

“What? Is there a rule saying I can’t laugh?”

“No, it doesn’t matter. But you’re fat too. If you laugh, isn’t that self-deprecation?”

Boyle stared at Luman in disbelief, then shot back with the kind of line his mother had always told him.

“I’m chubby. You’re fat. They’re completely different. I’m pleasantly plump. You’re just a pig.”

For a moment, Luman stopped chewing the potato chips in his mouth.

Then his jaw began moving again.

That was his only reaction.
