Chapter 413  
The Meaning of Survival (5)  
~*~

Crunch. Crunch.

At the sound of insects being crushed underfoot as someone approached, Shirone slowly lifted his head.

Fermi was walking toward him with both hands in his pockets. The warped smile on his face had already spread until more than half of it looked like a skull.

He deliberately chewed the insects that had crawled into his mouth, then spat out the shells. The students watching through the vision grimaced.

“Ugh!”

Even seeing it through a screen was hard to endure.

“Fermi really isn’t easy to deal with,” Olivia said.

Collie nodded.

“Yes. Last year, he made it all the way to Stage 5 and became the final survivor. But with Shirone here this time, I don’t think we can guarantee the outcome.”

“Hmm…”

Olivia’s gaze turned cold.

She knew Fermi had been involved in collusion unrelated to graduation. There was simply no evidence, so she could not accuse him.

As expected, he’s definitely an outsider.

If recognizing an apple as an apple was omniscience, then accepting an apple as a strawberry was the omniscience of an outsider.

It was cognitive distortion taken to the point of denying reality itself.

If Fermi was not immortal like Shirone, or self-denying like Gaold, then he could only be called a twisted human being from the start.

Pop!

Shirone’s right eye burst as though it had been crushed. Disgusting maggots swarmed out from the ruined socket.

Fermi watched calmly, chewing and spitting out worms.

Even so, his throat kept itching.

This is definitely not pleasant.

To be honest, he had not expected Shirone to last this long.

In Fermi’s graduating year, Shirone was the first person who had followed him all the way to the end of Stage 5.

I thought he was nothing special…

Even during the ghost disturbance, Shirone had only been a kid. Yet in the span of a year, he had grown at an astonishing rate and reached this point.

Should I go a little further?

Fermi himself had never seen anything beyond Stage 5. Since he was not particularly obsessed with winning or scores, stopping here would normally have been fine.

But if the opponent was Shirone, the story changed.

Fermi was the kind of person who believed any element that interfered with business had to be removed by any means necessary.

“This won’t settle anything. Let’s move on. I wonder what’s waiting next.”

After speaking, Fermi spat out another insect.

“…”

Shirone remained silent.

If the state of his natural body wavered even slightly, his mind would be overturned like a sea caught in a storm.

Pop!

Shirone’s remaining eye burst as well.

And with that, Stage 5, the Insect King, ended.

* * *

“Stage 6! Entering Super Fever!”

Collie’s voice trembled with both excitement and anxiety.

In his long teaching career, this was only the second time a student had reached this point.

The depth of enlightenment had nothing to do with magic level or age. Even so, Super Fever was a dangerous stage even for true practitioners.

“Th-that…”

The reflections of flames flickered in the students’ eyes.

Shirone and Fermi stood facing one another in a world made entirely of fire.

The collars of their clothes began to smolder little by little. A moment later, flames surged up around them, blazing fiercely as they swallowed their bodies.

“Ugh…!”

The students turned their heads away in horror.

The sight of three-meter-tall flames completely engulfing people was enough to make them feel the heat, even though they were not inside the Virtual Zone.

If ordinary pain traveled through the nerves, then fire stimulated the nerves directly, forcing pain to its threshold.

What made it even more terrifying was that this was a virtual world where the body was constantly reconstructed.

Roar! Roar!

The two burned like candles on a candlestick. Their expressions did not change, but their minds were screaming desperately.

This was not the same as transcending death. Human beings were not that strong.

Pain raced through the body, and fear meshed with the mind like interlocking gears.

This is bad. This is truly dangerous.

Shirone’s face began to twist little by little. Even with the spirit of Geumgangtae, it was difficult to maintain his composure.

What kind of life had Gaold lived to be able to unleash this sort of hell?

But even that thought could not last. It was swept away by the terrible pain surging up from within him.

The students watching through the vision no longer cheered.

It hurt just to watch. How much worse must it be for the people experiencing it?

If anyone shouted encouragement now, they would look like a psychopath.

“Kuh.”

Fermi caught sight of Shirone through the wavering flames.

Was he really daring to endure until the end against him?

What an arrogant brat.

Stage 6 was difficult even for Fermi. If he had known it would be this bad, he might have reconsidered his decision.

That’s why he was even more irritating.

By now, Fermi’s evaluation score was already high enough.

What mattered was money.

He had to put an end to Shirone here so that his business for the year could bear fruit without problems.

“You’re holding up pretty well. Does this sauna suit you?”

Shirone did not have the strength to answer.

If anything, he wished this place were real so his body would simply burn away.

But cruelly, his body was reconstructed in real time, and the threshold of his nerves was reset every instant.

“Why don’t you just give up? It’s only four weeks.”

Shirone’s face contorted.

At that moment, his mind had reached the height of irritation. He hated everything in the world. He was sick even of being alive.

So much resentment had piled up inside him that someone merely speaking to him was almost enough to knock him out of the Spirit Zone.

Fermi, who stood in the same space, had to know that.

That was precisely why he was getting on Shirone’s nerves.

If so, Shirone had to respond in kind.

“You…”

The students watching through the vision swallowed.

“Who the hell are you?”

Shirone’s question carried all the doubts he had had about Fermi and the graduating class.

“Well? Who knows?”

Fermi was not in a normal state either. But by using his own condition as a mirror, he irritated Shirone even further.

“Why did you attack Amy? Why aren’t you graduating? Why is someone as strong as you still in school?”

“I have no idea what you’re talking about. I’m just enduring well because my mental strength is better than other people’s.”

The students looked bewildered as they listened to the exchange.

They were discussing the graduating class while their bodies were on fire. It was impossible to think of them as anything but insane.

It’s coming soon.

Fermi instinctively sensed his limit.

He was pretending to be calm, but his inner self was running wild.

He could not afford to let arrogance make him frown. Customers would not come running if they saw the signboard suffering.

Publicity was important in business.

Fermi started a timer in his head and moved to give Shirone one final chance.

“Whatever you think, it isn’t true. Of course, I understand why you’d be suspicious. But in the graduating class, isn’t the guy who stands out always the one who gets beaten down first? That’s the natural order of competition.”

Shirone glared at Fermi with eyes burning in the flames.

I know. You’re using that.

Fermi concealed his crime within the students’ competitive psychology.

It was like a guard who beat a thief because he happened to be in a foul mood. On the surface, it would still look like punishment, and no one would know the real motive.

Shirone did not like it, but it was an excellent strategy.

“As a senior in the graduating class, I’ll give you one piece of advice. Don’t stand out too much. If you do, you’ll be eliminated at the very beginning.”

Shirone’s nose wrinkled. The flames pouring from his eyes and mouth seemed to embody his anger.

“Are you threatening me?”

“A threat? No, it’s advice. Anyway, twenty points is a big score. But is that more important than spending one more year in the graduating class?”

It was a threat.

He was saying that if Shirone did not give up here, he would make him fail the graduation exam, just as he had done to Amy.

Shirone knew what happened to people Fermi marked. Even Amy, of all people, had been eliminated at the very beginning, before she had even had time to come up with a countermeasure.

No matter what event came up, everyone would gang up on him. An entire year of effort would go to waste.

Conversely, if Shirone accepted the offer and gave up those twenty points, Fermi would surely let him pass.

After all, asking whether the points were more important than another year in the graduating class was the same as saying Shirone would not have to spend another year there.

Give up already, you bastard. Stop interfering with business and get out of school. I’ll let you graduate right away.

Shirone clenched his fists.

Heat leaked through his gritted teeth until it felt as though they would fuse together.

Pathetic Fermi.

Because of a guy like you…

Now Shirone could see how hard Amy had worked over the past year, and how much pain she had endured in order to become a wizard.

Fermi had destroyed that.

He had made her cry through the night.

“Eliminate me? Try it if you can. At least I will never lose to a human like you.”

Even amid the heat of hell, Fermi looked down at Shirone for a moment with corpse-like eyes drained of all vitality.

Negotiations had broken down.

And as long as Fermi remained at the school, Shirone would never become a wizard.

“Fine. Then look forward to it.”

With those words, Fermi turned around.

He had reached his limit.

“Wait. I’m not done—”

The moment Shirone opened his mouth, Fermi vanished.

His Spirit Zone had been released.

Fermi’s eyes opened inside the Virtual Zone. The pain of Super Fever was still vivid, and he could not lift so much as a finger.

Even in that state, the timer in his head continued to count down with perfect precision.

…Two seconds. One second. Now it begins.

A guide voice rang out.

—Survival program Level 7. Entering Air Coercion.

It’s a gift from me, Shirone.

Having experienced Stage 6, Fermi guessed that not even an outsider could survive Stage 7.

And if only one person in the history of Alpheas Magic School had ever passed it, there was no way Shirone would be safe.

If he gives up, that’s the end of it. But will he really…?

Collie headed for the engine unit to shut down the Virtual Zone. Even if a team had already won, an individual could still challenge a higher level for their own evaluation. But at the very least, this level was not meant to be conquered.

This isn’t a stage made because humans can clear it. It’s just the lid that marks the limit of the survival test. It has to end here.

Collie placed his hand on the Virtual Zone switch, then looked back at Alpheas and declared,

“We will shut down the Virtual Zone.”

Fermi, who had somehow managed to rise to his feet, pointed at the vision.

“No. I think he’s going to continue.”

“What?”

Collie’s head snapped around.

In the vision, Shirone stood in darkness where nothing could be seen.

Needless to say, it was the starting point of Air Coercion.

Despicable Fermi.

For the first time, Shirone regained his composure.

Everything he had endured until now had felt like an eternity, but the past was only an event that had already disappeared.

I will never bow to a human like you.

Shirone did not release his Spirit Zone.

Even though he had become the final survivor, he felt terrible. Fermi had played a lethal trick until the very end.

As of today, Fermi had marked Shirone as a target. It was clear that from now on, he would apply pressure whenever he could with his unique ability to agitate people.

Then I’ll pressure Fermi.

That was Shirone’s strategy against Fermi’s.

He would never give up the initiative. If the balance of power between them remained tight, Fermi would not be able to agitate him.

Survival Level 7 was certainly a risky challenge, but that only made it even more worth attempting.

By the way, where is this place?

Shirone looked around.

It was strange to say he could see darkness, but in truth, all he could see was darkness, aside from himself.

Rumble, rumble, rumble!

A vibration passed through the space.

It was as if the entire world were trembling, though he could not even tell from which direction it came.

“Huh? Uh…?”

With an ominous feeling, Shirone held out his hand.

The darkness pressed in from every side and began to crush him.

Is it compression this time?

Both of his arms bent toward his body.

Even then, the darkness continued to push inward.
