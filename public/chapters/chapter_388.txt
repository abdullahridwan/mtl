Magic Association (6)  
~*~

Shirone took a carriage to the Magic Association. His head was still tingling from all the knuckle raps Fleur had given him.

Fleur, who was cradling the metal box in her arms, pouted and sneered at him.

“What? Are you upset because I hit you? Then who told you to jump into the scene without permission? Do you have any idea how serious this is? Using magic without a wizard’s license is against the laws of the kingdom.”

“I’m sorry about that. But people almost died.”

Fleur couldn’t argue with that.

“Hmph. That’s that, and this is this. You said you were prepared to take responsibility anyway, didn’t you? Then stop grumbling.”

Since she was right, Shirone kept his mouth shut. More than anything, the terror he had witnessed today had completely changed the image of Baska he had carried until now.

“I’ve heard about Radum,” he said.

Fleur, who had been resting her chin on her hand as she looked out the window, turned her gaze toward him.

“My classmates from school used to live there. They said it was a terrible place.”

“Mm. Yes. The residents of Radum are banned from economic activity.”

“Why does the kingdom just leave a place like that alone? Terrorism is obviously wrong, but isn’t there a more peaceful solution?”

Fleur remained silent for a long moment before she answered.

“You… do you know what happens when a house has no trash can?”

“Yes? A trash can?”

“Eventually, the whole house becomes a trash can. In reality, a house stays clean because there’s somewhere to throw the garbage. Radum is that kind of place. It’s the capital’s trash can. The people there don’t produce anything, but their existence has value simply because they’re there. That’s why even the nobles can’t get rid of it.”

“But that’s…”

“I know what you want to say, but don’t force yourself to say it. Beliefs aren’t something you can impose on other people. If you have an ideal, then you have to achieve it yourself.”

Shirone lowered his head gloomily. In the world of professionals, it seemed there was no place for discussing ideals one had no power to put into practice.

“Still,” Fleur added, “today’s incident will make the politicians think a little differently.”

Shirone lifted his head again.

“Bat imitation is a unique ability of vampires. But there have been no academic reports of it manifesting in broad daylight. Something is changing inside Radum. Whether it’s species improvement, fusion, or something else…”

It was certainly possible.

Radum was home to countless subspecies that had fled in from all over the continent. If someone wanted to conduct experiments on living organisms, there was no better place.

Shirone turned his head toward the window. The scenery of the capital was as splendid as it had been when he first arrived. But now a shadow lay over it in his eyes.

Light and darkness. Perhaps that, too, was a law in which each existed only by referring to the other.

But Shirone did not know which had come first.

* * *

After they passed through the main gate of the Magic Association, Fleur looked back at Shirone.

“I’m going to report, so you go to the engine room and deliver that to the maintenance team. And stay put! Don’t wander off anywhere else!”

Aside from running errands, Shirone had never gone anywhere else. Still, he felt as if things were different now, so he moved toward the engine room with great caution, afraid the item might be damaged.

Fortunately, after inspecting it, the maintenance team determined there was nothing wrong with the box.

Shirone let out a sigh of relief and stepped out of the engine room. Fleur soon arrived in front of him. Perhaps because she had already given her report to her superior, her expression was colder than before.

“Um, is the report over…?”

“Ugh, seriously!”

Fleur smacked Shirone on the head.

“Ouch! Why do you keep hitting me on the head?”

Shirone had endured enough, and this time he glared at her angrily.

“You’re going to be the death of me! Do you know how hard I had to work to keep this incident under control? Even if you don’t have a license, at least learn to have some self-restraint!”

“I’m not trying to brag, but people were about to die. What was I supposed to do?”

“Shut up! More importantly, rumors are already spreading that you came to the Association! This is a serious problem. I think the president of the Association intended to meet you quietly, but who knows anymore!”

Shirone felt sorry about that as well.

“If it’s already happened, there’s nothing we can do now.”

“Oh? You think it’ll be over if you just go back? You’re really determined to get me killed, aren’t you? Follow me!”

Fleur grabbed Shirone by the ear and dragged him all the way to the inn. She flung open the door, shoved him inside as if throwing him into a prison cell, then pointed at him and shouted.

“Don’t take a single step outside! Stay dead quiet until everything calms down.”

Shirone rubbed his stinging ear and frowned.

“Isn’t this too much? You were the one who told me to run errands.”

“And you still have the nerve to talk back…!”

Just as Fleur was about to shout again, Gando’s voice came from the hallway.

“Fleur, what’s going on?”

Fleur turned her head, still full of resentment.

“Gando-sama, this little brat…”

Her voice caught, and her body froze.

Two people were following behind Gando as he approached with a worried expression.

Kang Nan, the chief secretary of the Magic Association.

And the man with a black fur coat draped over his shoulders was Mikea Gaold.

The moment Fleur saw Gaold, her heart skipped a beat.

She knew he was returning today, but she had never expected to see him here. Since Fleur had joined the Association, the number of times the president had come up to the third floor could be counted on one hand.

‘Why is he here…?’

If he had given an order, she would have brought Shirone herself. Yet upon returning, he had started searching for him from the third floor.

Above all, this was before she had fixed the mistake Shirone had made. There was no excuse for allowing rumors of Shirone’s arrival to spread through the Magic Association.

Fleur’s body reacted before her mind could think. She snapped upright and shouted.

“Unit! Attention!”

Gando rubbed his forehead and shook his head. Gaold, on the other hand, raised the corner of his mouth in amusement.

“Heh. Am I the only one who can’t see them? What unit are you calling to attention when no one’s here?”

“Ah… I’m sorry! I was nervous!”

Kang Nan looked away from Gaold and asked, “Are you all right? I heard on the way that there was an incident a little while ago.”

Fleur’s eyes widened. The moment she had dreaded had finally come.

“Ah! I have something to report. At the site of the recent terror incident, Shirone accidentally…”

“Doesn’t matter.”

Gaold cut her off in irritation and walked past her. Fleur, still locked in an immobile posture, stiffened like a stone statue.

Shirone looked up at Gaold and swallowed.

He was taller than Shirone had imagined. This was the strongest wizard in the kingdom, the man at the center of so many rumors, the person who had only stirred Shirone’s curiosity further since he had come here. Perhaps it was an illusion, but Gaold seemed enormous, as if his body might reach the ceiling.

Gaold raised one corner of his mouth and said, “Are you Shirone?”

Episode 3. The Strongest Wizard

Shirone finally boarded the Magic Association elevator he had been waiting to ride. But the atmosphere was strange, so he couldn’t openly show his excitement. Someone had to press a button for them to go anywhere, yet no one moved.

Gaold stood with his hands behind his back, looking at the panel that displayed the floor numbers. Kang Nan was meditating in the corner, and Gando could not hide his discomfort at standing beside Gaold.

‘Gaold-nim…’

In Fleur’s lowered eyes, sadness and bitterness mingled.

Until now, she had done everything she could to avoid tarnishing the name of the Gaold Guards. But before the name Shirone, all her efforts seemed useless.

Shirone’s name had become known within the Association, and that fact would surely reach the ears of the wizards who were keeping Gaold in check. Above all, this project was the once-in-a-lifetime undertaking Gaold had prepared for twenty years.

And yet Gaold had said it did not matter.

Fleur felt endlessly pathetic. Since her school days, she had admired him. She had eventually joined the Association and spent two years working for him, but now she felt as if she was not even worth as much as Shirone, who was not yet an official wizard.

Kang Nan opened her eyes and walked toward the panel.

“Then we’ll go down.”

Shirone looked puzzled.

‘Down?’

They were on the third floor. If they were going down, there were only the first and second floors. Surely they had not gathered all these people just to take the elevator to the second floor.

Kang Nan took out a key, opened the panel, and placed her fingerprint on a glass plate. A thin light scanned her finger, and the internal mechanism slid out. It was a hidden panel with thirty-six buttons arranged six across and six down.

Kang Nan took a deep breath and entered twenty-four inputs.

Once approval was granted, a woman’s guidance voice came from the ceiling.

[Proceeding to the tenth basement floor. This is a Level 1 security area.]

Gando and Fleur glanced at Gaold. Though they had once ridden the lift with him before, it was entirely up to Gaold to decide how far they could accompany him.

“Hmm.”

Gaold paid no attention to that and looked at Shirone instead. Then, noticing that Shirone’s eyes were fixed on the panel, he burst out laughing.

“Curious?”

“Yes? Ah, yes. It’s my first time riding an elevator. I didn’t know there was a password lock.”

“Heh. I bet you memorized it already.”

“Oh, no!”

Shirone hurriedly waved his hands, but after a moment, his face reddened and he confessed.

“Yes. Actually…”

“Ha ha ha! You’re thorough. Well, a wizard should be like that. But it won’t do you any good. The password changes every day. Besides, the important thing is that no one in the Association knows the password.”

Shirone tilted his head.

“No one knows? But just now, Ms. Kang Nan…”

“Yes, she entered the pattern correctly. But even Kang Nan doesn’t know what she pressed.”

Was that even possible? Shirone asked himself.

“How can that be?”

“You store the pattern in the unconscious, not in memory. Think of an instrument and it’s easy to understand. If you practice the same song every day, your hands move before you think. It’s a similar principle. Kang Nan goes to a random password generator every day and looks at the pattern for about an hour. The important thing is not to think about anything. Then, when the time comes, she simply presses wherever her hand leads her.”

Gaold mimed pressing buttons in the air.

“Of course, it’s different from playing an instrument, so it requires training. But once you master the technique, it’s more secure than any other encryption system. The harder you try to recall it consciously, the harder it becomes to remember.”

Shirone stuck out his tongue at a security system that went beyond common sense. The fact that an encryption system he could never have learned from books was already being used by the Association showed just how advanced its technology was.

‘Look at him, getting all excited. Can’t he tell the president is going out of his way for him?’

Fleur, who had been listening to their conversation, glared at Shirone fiercely.

Even she herself had never exchanged more than a few words with Gaold. Yet Shirone had not only been brought into a Level 1 security area, Gaold was even kindly explaining the secrets of the encryption system to him.

The elevator passed below the first floor and sank into complete darkness. It would have been a mistake to assume the tenth basement floor was only as far down as ten ordinary stories. The elevator continued descending for about a minute before the door finally opened.

It was Fleur’s first time here, and even Gando’s, so they were naturally tense. After waiting for Gaold and Kang Nan to step out first, Fleur looked around in astonishment.

“This place is…”

It was an enormous space. The ceiling soared so high it seemed to touch the sky, and the interior, without so much as a single pillar, spread out in a way that had nothing to do with the area of the Magic Association building above.

Even Shirone, who had been to places beyond common sense, could not help but be surprised by the sheer scale of the underground space. The temples and celestial buildings he had seen in the spacetime of the labyrinth had been magnificent, but the emptiness here gave off a different kind of pressure.

‘But why did he bring me here?’

It was far too vast a place for a simple interview.

When Shirone looked back at Kang Nan, she adjusted her glasses and explained.

“This is the largest area in the Magic Association. It may be empty, but because it is used as a bunker, its durability is beyond imagination. It’s also one of the few places free from the magic control device. It was built for multiple purposes, but when it isn’t being used for anything else, it’s mostly used for confidential magic demonstrations.”

“I see.”

The Magic Association was the place where all magic in the kingdom was analyzed, so it made sense that at least one space like this had to exist.

Then Shirone realized what she meant and looked back at Gaold in surprise.

“You’re going to demonstrate magic?”

Gaold looked back at him with a smile, and Shirone’s shoulders trembled.

The energy radiating from him heated the air inside the bunker. He was a completely different person from the man Shirone had seen in the elevator.
