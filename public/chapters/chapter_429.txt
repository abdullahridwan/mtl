Chapter 429  
The Rapidly Changing Situation (3)  
~*~

[Bang!]

Shirone slammed the door open, then shut it with equal force, causing him to stumble into his desk. The neatly arranged notes scattered across the floor. He flipped through them until he found a blank page, then sat down and picked up his pen.

‘The main point. I have to start from the core. So, what does “core” mean here? Ah, right. From the core…’

His mind was razor-sharp, yet he couldn’t process too many thoughts at once. The pen in his hand trembled. It felt as though someone were trying to pry into his mind and steal his thoughts. Still, he forced himself to draft a large-scale guideline first. He felt a wave of relief; the immediate danger of mental collapse seemed to have passed.

His hands kept moving. He opened the notes he’d already taken and began grafting numerous ideas into the core concept. The pen nib moved so fast it seemed to slice through the paper. His handwriting kept pace with his racing thoughts, his eyes tracking every stroke without blinking. Once the theoretical framework was complete, all that remained was the calculations. Shirone verified the feasibility through a simplified physical system model. While precise calculations would require expert validation, he could determine basic viability with a straightforward formula. It was a manageable problem. When the final calculation was complete, he set the pen down with a decisive click.

On the final page, the last figure he’d written down far exceeded his target number. Shirone stared at the notebook, utterly stunned. It felt as though his soul had been sucked into the pages, his mouth going dry.

[Bang!]

He slammed both fists onto the desk like a hammer. Burying his face in his hands, he remained frozen for a long time, his body trembling.

“Haaaa…”

When he finally lifted his head, the initial euphoria of conceiving a new spell had vanished from his face.

“Really… it actually works.”

It wasn’t guaranteed yet, but it was theoretically possible. Given the right circumstances, despite the complex constraints, Shirone could potentially wipe Heaven off the map. A rush of fear crept in. The possibility was so slim—no, he had initially thought it impossible—that his mind had already wandered into territories he hadn’t even considered. Clearly, devising a countermeasure against Heaven, which was planning a final war, was a form of self-defense. Ji-sung had argued that there was no need to push to the absolute limit if it wasn’t necessary. But…

‘If I had known Eider’s tendencies in advance, would I still have followed the same strategy?’

That was a difficult question to answer.

If Eider hadn’t attacked Maya, but had instead exercised better judgment, would the outcome have been different? Magicians typically measured efficiency using two criteria: justification and practicality. If Fermi leaned toward efficiency, Shirone tended to prioritize justification. Practicality yielded immediate results, but justification shaped the broader flow and ultimately brought greater rewards. He couldn’t say which was objectively better. However, the strategy to destroy Heaven was undeniably biased toward practicality.

“I need to meet Ikael.”

Why had she passed on Ataraxia? Had she known that her choice would eventually come back like a boomerang, capable of destroying Heaven?

“I need to meet Ikael.”

That was one more reason to see her.

* * *

The graduating class had already passed the twelfth week of the schedule. Though students had been running on empty without a chance to catch their breath, the campus atmosphere had grown chaotic lately. Or rather, it wasn’t just the school—the entire kingdom was in an uproar. Students everywhere, whether in the cafeteria or the park, clutched newspapers and traded stories about the shocking incidents at the Magic Association. The headline blared: *Former Sorcerer’s Association President Mikea Gaold Wanted for Treason.*

Shirone read the headline several times before turning his attention to the article. It reported an assassination attempt on Tormia’s King Adolf XII ten days prior. The news was shocking because King Adolf was not a tyrant and remained highly popular in Baska. But what stunned people even more was that the mastermind behind the attempt was allegedly Mikea Gaold. Newspapers were racing to publish supporting evidence, pointing out that Gaold had gone into hiding just two days before the assassination attempt. Shirone overheard students in the park complaining as they walked by.

“I mean, treason? What did you even do that for?”

“Who wouldn’t be shocked? He used to be the pride of this school, and now he’s its greatest shame. If he’s guilty of treason, the school will suffer too. Even if we block the path for the juniors, they’ll still be crushed.”

Though Gaold wasn’t standing right in front of them, they certainly understood the weight of his actions. Shirone read the article again. Those who had vanished alongside Gaold included Gando, head of the Magic Association’s guard, and Plu, the manager of the third floor. At the bottom of the page, transcripts from yesterday’s emergency hearing on national security measures were printed. Most Magic Association staff cited circumstances that pointed to Gaold’s guilt. Only Isabella, a certified third-class magic library manager, had insisted on his innocence. In the end, she was arrested on the spot by the kingdom’s National Intelligence Service agents and remained imprisoned.

“Wow, it’s gotten complicated.”

Shirone folded the newspaper and looked up at the sky. Gaold had plotted treason. Was that even possible? Well, treason might be within the realm of possibility for someone with Gaold’s power. But the real question was: why the hell?

“Even a first-class archmage would flee if the state turned against him,” Nade said. “Anyway, how are you holding up? I’ve heard a lot of strange people coming and going at the school. Iruki, what do you know? You get letters from your father all the time.”

Iruki, an albino and leader of the Dragon Club, would surely have accurate information, but he shook his head.

“You just said it was too early to jump to conclusions. Besides, it’s not something you’d put in a letter. Those people we saw earlier were probably from the National Intelligence Service. Gaold will be looking for refuge wherever he can find it.”

Iruki turned to look at Shirone. He knew Shirone had visited the Magic Association during the break. He wasn’t certain, but he had a feeling Shirone was hiding something.

“Shirone, do you know anything?”

“…”

Shirone remained silent. In a situation like this, he had to be extremely careful with his words. If Isabella, a certified third-class mage, could be arrested on the spot, there was no guarantee that he or his friends wouldn’t face the same fate.

*What the hell is going on?* Had Gaold really tried to assassinate the king? Shirone had thought there was little chance of that. A project prepared for twenty years was only a few months away. Even if the king’s assassination had been part of the plan, it felt like a premature, desperate move.

*Looking at the article, Gaold often displayed a volatile temperament. It even says he cursed the king in front of a foreign diplomat. But he continued to hold the position of Association President. Somehow, an emotional equilibrium must have been maintained between the two powers. But suddenly, it broke. Why now?*

It felt too reckless and catastrophic to happen now, especially when Heaven’s preparations weren’t even complete yet.

*No… maybe that’s exactly why it was inevitable?* Shirone ran the scenario backward in his mind. *Gaold had been preparing this project for twenty years. It would be impossible to keep all the information perfectly contained. As the activation date approached, he must have started taking action to contain leaks or put out fires. That made the most sense.*

If his assumptions were correct, this was the worst-case scenario. Shirone was a member of Gaold’s project. If the NIS dug deep enough, they would inevitably uncover his name.

*Who moved against us? The Tormia Kingdom? Or someone else? How much do they know? No… how long can we keep this hidden? Then, what should I prepare for?*

“…Lone. Shirone.”

Shirone snapped out of his thoughts and looked back at Iruki.

“Uh, huh?”

“What are you thinking about? Are you okay? Your face is pale.” Nade looked at him with concern. “What? Is that so? Shirone, can you tell me what’s going on? I didn’t want to push because I was afraid it might get you in trouble, but if it’s something too heavy to carry alone, we’ll fight alongside you.”

Disclosing the truth was absolutely impossible. It would be like dragging his friends to their deaths. Of course, Iruki and Nade understood that. Because of that, they would respect his silence.

“Sorry. I’ll tell you everything later when I get a chance.”

Iruki didn’t look upset. Shirone wasn’t stupid. If it were something that could be solved by talking, he would have said so immediately. It just frustrated his friends that the situation wasn’t helping.

“Alright. We’ll pretend we don’t know. But don’t be stubborn. You’re the most important thing here. If you end up in danger, I’ll start taking action one way or another.”

Hearing those words from Iruki of the Mercodyne family brought Shirone a measure of relief. The albino had claimed he hadn’t written anything in his letters, but Iruki likely knew more than he let on. It was a family capable of that level of discretion.

“Thanks. But it’s really okay. I’m going back to my room first. I need to think.”

Shirone stood up with a cold demeanor, but his heart felt lighter. It was a relief that Iruki and Nade were there. At the very least, even Amy and Lian wouldn’t turn a blind eye. Even if they were wrong, they were people who would do everything in their power to protect their loved ones. Shirone could be satisfied with that.

* * *

As summer arrived, the rain fell more frequently. The morning might start sunny, but the unpredictable weather would dump heavy rain before anyone realized it. The rainy season had come early.

It was past midnight, and Shirone stood by the window, watching the downpour. It had been three days since Isabella’s arrest, yet there was still no word from Gaold. The newspapers had inflated the facts and published increasingly provocative articles, turning themselves into public enemies. The figures presumed to be NIS agents were no longer visible around campus, though it was likely just a temporary cover-up. The evaluation schedule proceeded normally, but student morale was noticeably dampened. It was only natural to feel skeptical after seeing the kingdom’s greatest wizard, the head of the Magic Association, reduced to such a miserable state.

*Will Isabella be okay?*

As far as Shirone knew, she hadn’t been part of Gaold’s personal guard. So, had Gaold defended her at the hearing purely out of personal affection? Or was there something he didn’t know? He remembered her beautiful face as she kindly explained the laser guidance algorithm. Just the other day, she had placed a Kubrick on his finger and shown him how to use it.

*Smart.*

Shirone’s heart sank at the sound of knocking. It was already past midnight. There was absolutely no reason for anyone to visit his room when even the faculty was asleep.

*Smart.*

Shirone shifted into his Spirit Zone. He kept the radius extremely narrow to avoid detection by whoever stood outside. As he approached the door, he heard muffled footsteps but no voice.

“…Who are you?”

No answer meant it wasn’t a friend. They would have sent a signal. *If they break down the door, I’ll escape via teleportation.* With only that single guideline in mind, he closed the distance. Shirone finally reached the door and peered through the peephole. His tense expression went blank for a moment, then he hurriedly grabbed the doorknob and turned it.

“Senior?”

A figure stood there, clutching his injured shoulder. He looked utterly exhausted. His face was pale, and his bluish lips trembled. At first glance, he was in severe pain. His clothes were tattered, and without proper undergarments, his skin was visible through the soaked cotton. The only thing that preserved his usual demeanor was the sharp, wizardly glint behind his glasses.

“Shirone…”

The senior’s face showed a flicker of relief, but he collapsed into Shirone’s arms before he could finish the sentence.

“Senior! Senior!”

Shirone caught him, carried him to the bed, checked the hallway carefully, and then silently closed the door. When Shirone returned to the bedside, the senior was already sitting up. He covered his chest with his arm, not out of shame, but out of consideration for Shirone. His expression remained resolute. The thought of brewing a warm cup of tea crossed Shirone’s mind, but he pushed it aside. The senior had fled from Basuka all the way to Kreas’s magic school. The fact that he had arrived here completely drained of power meant his situation was far worse than anticipated.

“Senior! What happened? I was so worried!”

There were a dozen things Shirone wanted to ask, but the words caught in his throat. The senior pondered, carefully choosing his words. After a moment, his face contorted with remorse.

“Gando-nim… no, Gando-nim…”

He slowly turned to face Shirone. A single tear traced down his cheek, falling from eyes that usually seemed sharp enough to pierce iron.

“Gando betrayed us.”
