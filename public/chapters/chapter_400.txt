Steel Gate (2)

~*~

“You’re here?”

The moment Nade entered, he tossed his bag aside and threw himself onto the sofa.

“Ugh, I’m exhausted. The road from the central region to the south is horrible.”

“I came from the capital,” Iruki said.

“Do I look like I arrived by private carriage? I came here starving, waiting around in stations and washrooms. If I’d had a Mercodyne family carriage, I would’ve been snacking the whole way in comfort.”

“Then you should’ve snacked in discomfort.”

“Enough!” Nade pushed himself up on one elbow. “Anyway, how have you been? Don’t tell me you betrayed us and spent the whole vacation doing special training.”

Iruki did not deny it.

“Well, we’ve come this far. I should at least graduate, shouldn’t I?”

Nade sprang up from the sofa.

“Seriously? Damn it, what am I supposed to do with you now? What kind of magic is it? Is it stronger than Cancellation?”

“It’s still only at the ‘omnipotent’ stage, so it isn’t developed enough to call it magic yet. In any case, I just need to complete it before the graduation exam. I’ll tell you once I get the results I want.”

Every senior had a plan to bring their condition to its peak on the day of the exam.

Amy was proof enough that, no matter what process led there, the result was everything.

“Tch. Fine. I played around too much during vacation anyway.”

Iruki looked at Nade with a bitter expression. Contrary to his words, Nade did not seem particularly regretful.

Even after becoming a senior, it was clear he still had little interest in becoming a wizard.

“But you…”

“Huh? What?”

Iruki shook his head and swallowed the rest of his words.

“No, nothing.”

“Tch! Don’t be so bland.”

The door, which had been left ajar, suddenly flew open more violently than before, and Shirone came in.

A smile spread across his face at the sight of his close friends.

“Hey! Guys, what is that smell?”

The good mood lasted only an instant. Shirone immediately pinched his nose and frowned.

The study group room floor was littered with trash. How on earth had these two put up with the stench?

Nade and Iruki glanced at each other and burst into giggles.

“Sorry,” Nade said. “It’s been a while since we sprayed perfume. Was it too much?”

“Shut up! Clean first!”

The three of them each grabbed cleaning tools and got to work.

By the time the room, which had looked like a massive garbage warehouse, was almost clean, the time for the event was approaching.

Nade lay sprawled on the sofa, exhausted, and stared up at the ceiling.

“What time is it?”

“One o’clock,” Iruki said. “We should get ready soon.”

Shirone suddenly realized something and shot up from the sofa.

“Right! I didn’t bring my formal uniform!”

“I went and picked it up this morning,” Iruki said. “Yours and Nade’s too.”

Nade gave him a thumbs-up.

“As thorough as ever. But do we really have to wear them? I seriously hate those things.”

The formal uniform was the official attire of the magic school. It was forbidden to wear it except during special events, but because the design was so ridiculous, students were reluctant to put it on even once a year.

“It’s not even the graduation ceremony. Why do we have to wear formal uniforms for an entrance event? I’d rather look in the mirror while applying ointment to hemorrhoids than look at myself wearing that.”

Shirone turned to Nade.

“You have hemorrhoids?”

“It’s an expression! Anyway, let’s get ready. Hemorrhoids—no, where’s my uniform?”

Iruki pointed to the corner. Three boxes were stacked there like a tower.

Each of them took the box with his name on it and unfolded the uniform inside. The white fabric, with lines running along the shoulders, fluttered like a ghost.

Once they put it on, they might at least look like wizards. But if they had to walk through a crowd wearing this, hiding their faces would probably be better for their mental health.

“Ugh, we have to meet the Class Two kids like this? They won’t be wearing these, right? Aren’t we just going to be laughed at?”

“We can’t help it,” Iruki said. “It’s the rule.”

Iruki did not seem to mind much. In his opinion, clothing only needed to cover the important parts of the body—such as the head, the eyes, or the heart.

After putting the uniforms on over their clothes, the three stood side by side in front of the mirror.

They looked like three limp, melted monsters.

And yet, unlike what they had expected, their expressions were solemn.

“Will we ever get another chance to wear these?”

There were few days in the busy senior schedule when they would wear formal uniforms. In other words, if they failed the graduation exam, today might be the last time they saw themselves dressed like this.

Iruki flapped sleeves that were longer than his arms.

“It won’t be easy. After all, we don’t have enough information.”

Nade looked back in surprise.

“Huh? I think you and Shirone can pass the exam easily.”

“That’s only your personal feeling. Mathematically, the probability is one in three. If the candidates are Shirone, Dante, and Amy, then two of them are eliminated. If Shirone passes, Dante and Amy can’t graduate.”

Nade could not even imagine those three being eliminated.

“Would it really happen like that? All three of them might pass.”

“You only think that because you know all three of them. Humans tend to believe the information they possess is the most accurate. But if that happens, then there’s no one left to fail. Even by a simple average, only 3.3 people pass from each class. And besides, we’re Class Three, the lowest class in the senior year.”

Nade fell silent.

They were at the very peak of the magic school. The gap in skill was narrower than in any other year, and every single student was someone no one would easily imagine being eliminated.

Shirone smoothed the wrinkles from his robe.

“If we want everyone to pass, probability doesn’t matter. Let’s go in with the mindset that we’re starting from zero.”

Shirone was right.

Everything they had built up while advancing from the lower classes to the upper classes would be reset. Ahead of the final gateway to becoming wizards, thirty people would now be fighting with real blades.

* * *

With twenty minutes left before the graduating class event, ten students walked toward Central Park from different paths.

Without exception, they wore formal uniforms, and their gazes were sharper than usual.

Shirone and the others, who arrived first, greeted the classmates they would be competing against for the next year.

They were the students ranked within the top ten overall, based on practical skill, theory, and performance evaluations.

It felt as if even the air had grown heavy under the pressure radiating from every direction.

Shirone, ranked third overall in the advanced class, looked toward the road to the east.

Canis, ranked sixth in the advanced class, and Arin, ranked seventh, were walking side by side.

Shirone had faced them when the Arcanes attacked, and they had fought together in Heaven. He knew better than anyone that their skills were not to be taken lightly.

Canis met Shirone’s eyes, but turned away without a word.

He already knew that there was no way all ten people gathered here would pass the exam together, arm in arm.

Arin approached in Canis’s place.

“Hello, Shirone.”

“Hi, Arin. Did you have a good vacation?”

“More or less…”

Arin smiled shyly.

She kept her mouth shut, remembering what Canis had told her: Shirone would be surprised if he learned what they had accomplished during vacation, but she must never reveal it.

“Wow, you’re already gathered? Aren’t you nervous?”

At the familiar voice, Shirone turned around.

On the southern road, Boyle, ranked eighth in the advanced class, and Pandora, ranked ninth, were walking side by side.

Pandora had once tried to curry favor with Dante’s group, but after eventually being pushed aside, she seemed to have returned to hanging around Boyle. Of course, they argued every time they met, but it seemed they could not completely ignore the affection built between them since childhood.

“Boyle of Summoning Magic, Pandora of Fragrance Magic.”

Boyle had lost to Dante before, but his summoning magic was not on a level anyone could dismiss.

And Pandora’s fragrance magic would be extremely troublesome to deal with tactically.

“Shirone, over there.”

Nade pointed toward the western path.

Dante, ranked first in the advanced class, came at the head of the group, followed by Closer and Sabina.

Seeing Dante roll up the sleeves of his formal uniform and pin them in place, Shirone burst out laughing.

He had felt it at the old castle site too, but Dante’s desire to stand out seemed to be innate.

Still, today, Shirone could not help paying more attention to Closer and Sabina.

He had heard that they had undergone hellish training under Vice Principal Olivia’s guidance during vacation, and their eyes were definitely different from before.

Closer could freely form rocks, and Sabina could scatter dozens of blow-type, razor-sharp winds per second.

At the very least, Shirone had to assume they were much stronger than they had been during the two thousand battles.

“What’s with those faces?” Dante said, raising a hand as he walked over. “It’s a good day. Smile a little.”

Boyle looked displeased.

“What are you wearing? Don’t you know formal uniforms have to be worn according to the rules?”

Before answering, Dante examined Boyle’s outfit. As always, Boyle’s hair was slicked down with oil and neatly parted, and his uniform had been ironed so sharply that the creases looked like blades.

“You…” Dante said. “Isn’t that a little excessive? If someone brushed against you, they’d bleed.”

“What’s wrong with this? It’s neat and proper. If you dress like that, your score might be lowered.”

“So what if I lose a few points? All I have to do is pass the graduation exam.”

While Dante and Boyle bickered over fashion, Closer and Sabina each headed toward their own targets.

The person Closer, ranked fourth overall in the advanced class, most wanted to grind his teeth at was undoubtedly Iruki, ranked second overall.

“It’s finally here. When will we meet again like this? You’d better stay on edge. Maybe every day from now on will feel like hell.”

“If you don’t understand, I don’t see the need to explain,” Iruki said. “Do you want me to teach you?”

“You little…!”

Killing intent filled Closer’s eyes. It was the first time in his life someone had made him want to throw a punch so badly.

Unlike the bloody atmosphere between those two, a subtler current flowed on the other side.

Sabina, ranked fifth in the advanced class, hesitated over and over before finally speaking to Nade, ranked tenth.

“Hi. Long time no see.”

“Uh, yeah.”

Nade’s nonchalant attitude upset Sabina. Or perhaps “upset” was not the right word.

She had dated countless men in Baska, the capital, but the thrill she felt now was on a completely different level.

“My heart must be broken. Or I’m a pervert.”

In any case, as long as she could not get Nade’s gaze out of her mind, she could not retreat like this.

“The formal uniform…” Sabina said. “It suits you.”

But all she received in return was Nade’s bitter smile.

To Nade, who had no intention of becoming a wizard, being told that the formal uniform suited him only hurt.

Pandora watched the scene from a distance and snorted as if accusing her.

In the past, she had followed Sabina around and flattered her, but after hard training, she was confident she was no longer inferior.

“Clever as always. She’s already making her move, isn’t she?”

Pandora had witnessed the scene when Nade overwhelmed Sabina, and from that moment she had guessed that he would be the biggest variable among the ten people here.

He was still in tenth place for now, but if he revealed his true ability, the rankings would surely fluctuate.

“It looks like everyone’s here,” Dante said. “Let’s get started. I want to finish quickly and take this thing off.”

Shirone was not the type to step forward, so Dante naturally led the group.

His position as first overall in the advanced class had played an important role in moving even his competitors.

Of course, from the moment they passed through the steel gate, the rankings of the advanced class would lose their meaning.

The students of Class Three headed toward the senior class building.

At the top of the rising slope, a magnificent steel door stood firm.

Below it, ten members of Class Two waited in a line.

“Hey, juniors! Hurry up and come on out! We’ll knock you all down!”

“Those formal uniforms look great on you! What, are you going to a school festival? Fuhahaha!”

The taunts meant to provoke Class Three erupted all at once.

It was a long tradition, and the pressure was far greater than the rumors had made it sound. However, not a single member of Class Three changed expression.

“Hmph! Seniors, my ass,” Boyle muttered through gritted teeth. He was trying to hypnotize himself. “Once we’re in the senior year, we’re all the same anyway.”

He rolled his tense shoulders and quickened his steps.

“Yeah, talk all you want, dropouts. I’ll cook you one by one from now on.”

Most of the students widened their eyes, trying not to be overwhelmed by the pressure, but Dante merely looked bored.

After observing his classmates’ reactions, he turned his head and looked at Shirone.

“You’re not nervous at all.”

The corner of Shirone’s mouth was slightly raised. His eyes sparkled with passion and excitement.

How could he not be thrilled?

This was a scene he had imagined so many times that it had appeared in his dreams.

The life of a wizard he had wanted so badly.

The final gateway now stood only ten steps away.

His heart pounded, and his consciousness grew hazy, as if he were flying into another world.

“Come in! Come in and see! Do you think you can graduate? You’re nothing but fertilizer for us!”

“Hell starts here, you brats! Your lives end at this gate!”

Shirone clenched his molars and tightened his fists.

Wizard.

Wizard.

He would become a wizard.

“Yes. I’ll gladly walk into hell or wherever else.”

With every step he took, the steel door—the symbol of intelligence—seemed to tremble.
