Second Purgatory (4)

“Shirone?”

Kanya’s voice trembled.

It was definitely Shirone. But how was he here?

No, that didn’t matter anymore.

It meant that if the figure on the screen was truly Shirone, he could bring new hope to the rebels drowning in despair.

-Unit 5 collapsed! Cover!

The radio chatter finally snapped Kanya out of her daze. She turned her head toward the Multivision display.

Most of the enemy aircraft were immobilized, but their weapon systems were still active. And every single one of them was locked onto Shirone.

“Wait, no!”

Kanya hurriedly pressed the radio button. “You can’t shoot! I am not an enemy!”

When there was no response, her tension only grew. She quickly checked on Shirone.

He was standing his ground, unflinching.

“This…!”

The emergency escape button was pressed. With a sharp bang, the tempered glass of the cockpit flew forward.

After ejecting the Piper from the Kuroi’s synchro machine, Kanya crawled out from behind the brush and raised her hands. “Pause! Stop shooting!”

The platoon leader’s voice crackled through the speaker of Unit 5, which had managed to escape.

– Open fire.

Tick! Tick! Tick!

Kanya tightly closed her eyes, but the only sound she heard from the remaining three Kuroi was the dull clatter of steel bouncing off armor.

“What? What?”

Looking around with a blank expression, Kanya realized the scenery was distorting. At the center of the distortion, a pair of gigantic cogwheels rotated in perfect mesh.

Sein’s Equilibrium had equalized the chemical fluctuations in the propellant gas.

“Misfire! It’s an unexploded round, Platoon Commander!”

“Me too. Check your spark plugs.”

“Nothing wrong! This…! Kuck!”

*Boom!*

Quan and Etela took down one Kuroi.

It took longer than expected given their skills, but it was meaningful that two specialists in anti-biological warfare had destroyed the machine using purely physical means.

The Kuroi’s tempered glass popped out. The pilot passed out, still clinging to the synchro machine, with Armand standing quietly beside him.

As long as the coordinates were certain, it was a technique unique to space-time wizards: the ability to penetrate even an enclosed space.

The other team members also destroyed their Kuroi targets with ease. The only surviving aircraft was the one the platoon commander was riding.

Gaold approached the Kuroi, stroking his chin with interest as if inspecting an unknown artifact. Then, instead of offering a handshake, he looked up at the aircraft’s purple faceplate and spoke.

“Get off. Unless you want to die.”

*Perong!*

As the tempered glass soared upward, the platoon leader leaped at Gaold, the Cigna already in hand.

“Die!”

“No, Platoon Commander!”

Kanya shouted, but the platoon leader’s Cigna was already descending toward Gaold’s head.

“Kanya?”

Gaold glanced back at Shirone. Then, as if acting on instinct, his fist swung quickly and connected with the platoon leader’s jaw.

“Aww!”

The leader fell in disgrace, rolling across the ground with his eyes wide open.

If Gaold hadn’t canceled the press magic, the platoon leader’s face would have been completely obliterated by that blow.

“Anyone you know, Shirone?”

Shirone blinked, looking closer. “Is it Kanya? You, Kanya, right?”

Kanya seemed to finally relax. She stripped off her Piper mid-stride, running bare-chested before collapsing into Shirone’s arms.

“Shirone, Shirone! Aaaaaang!”

Everyone was bewildered as a tender scene suddenly unfolded in the midst of a deadly standoff.

“Really… Shirone?”

Among the rebels, there was no one who didn’t know Shirone. He was the first Nephilim to stop Ilhwa’s drinking. The rebel organization had been born from the riots in Sharmaine District 73, and they honored Shirone by calling him ‘the Light of District 73’.

Kanya belatedly wiped away her tears and smiled shyly. “Sorry. I didn’t recognize you because you were wearing something strange.”

Shirone didn’t mind. Anyone would have reacted the same way if they’d encountered each other as enemies while hiding their identities.

“It’s fine. How is Lena?”

“She’s in the main unit. She’ll be thrilled to see you.”

“Haha! So, what about your parents?”

“Dad still corrects me.”

“… I see.”

Shirone didn’t ask about her mother. Even back then, her lifespan had been short, so it was easy to guess what had happened after Kanya fell silent. There was no good death in the world. Still, Shirone hoped she had passed peacefully, surrounded by her family.

“How long has it been since I went back?”

“Exactly 432 days.”

Kanya didn’t need to think. The day Shirone stopped Ilhwa’s drinking was a monumental event for the rebels; they hadn’t even needed to calculate it.

*432 days.*

It was a span of time not too different from what Shirone had experienced. There was no temporal distortion caused by distance because the Guffin’s door warped space. Even so, the reason time moved slightly faster here must be the influence of the planet’s gravitational field.

Shirone asked the most pressing question. “I heard you’re at war with Heaven, but I haven’t seen even Mara, let alone an angel, since I arrived. Why is that?”

Kanya shook her head. “We don’t know either. We fought fiercely—no, we almost retreated—but somehow, the angels just started disappearing overnight. Even on the day of the full moon, when I was always on patrol.”

Sein, who had been listening while linking mental magic, intervened, her eyes shining. “When exactly did this change happen?”

“Um, maybe 130 days?”

130 days.

Compared to the time zone of the original world, it was roughly three months.

It suddenly occurred to Shirone that this was exactly when he had lost contact with Gaold’s bodyguards who had infiltrated Heaven.

*Surely something happened in Heaven. What is it? Does it have anything to do with the maze? Or not…*

The platoon leader looked at the damaged Kuroi with a bitter expression. Shirone, the so-called Light of District 73, hadn’t fought for any particular reason, but the material loss was too great.

“It’s a big deal. The damage to the aircraft is serious.”

Shirone approached with an apologetic expression. The Kuroi was so damaged it couldn’t be repaired without replacing the cockpit glass.

“Sorry.”

“There is nothing to be sorry about. We were the ones who struck first.”

The platoon commander, a woman who bore some responsibility for the incident, appealed with an exasperated expression, holding up her palms. “But that man gave me a grotesque vision first. If you saw it, you’d understand, but anyone would have recognized it as an enemy.”

Flew was taken aback.

“Before that, you aimed at the arc first, didn’t you?”

“Before that, that man started the fight!”

“You misunderstood first before you even started a fight.”

The woman could not stand it and cried out. “Then how can you not misunderstand when you look like that! You approached me as if you were going to hit me!”

A moment of silence followed.

In the end, when the consensus that Gaold’s face was a sin emerged, the platoon commander didn’t want to argue anymore.

“It’s okay. Stop that and quickly collect the aircraft and join the main unit. The main unit is not in a relaxed situation right now.”

Sein asked, “Where is the main unit?”

“Plains of the Dead. We are currently carrying out an operation to wipe out the giants.”

The platoon commander walked toward her Kuroi. “This is the only equipment that can be deployed immediately. For now, I will lead, so follow me… yes?”

Gaold stood behind the platoon commander.

When she looked back at him in disbelief, one foot already on the aircraft, Gaold pointed to the side with his chin. “Move. I will ride.”

“What are you talking about? Are you riding this?”

Kang Nan sighed and approached. “Why the hell do you want to ride that? It’s linked to the Piper anyway, so it won’t move even if you just sit in it.”

Gaold’s solution was simple. “Give me the Piper, then.”

Kang Nan looked back at Sein.

It was an official inquiry to the team’s commander: was it okay to leave this human alone?

Sein nodded.

Heaven had been destroyed by Shirone, but Gaold would be in charge of handling the angels. He was the only one who could buy time against a swarm of them, so it was better to leave combat decisions to him.

“For now, let them do whatever they want.”

Five minutes later.

*Chunk! Chunk!*

Gaold, equipped with a Piper, boarded the Kuroi.

The platoon commander, stripped of everything, simply stared at the distant mountains.

Shirone looked at her anxiously and asked Flew. “Can I really do this? At best, the atmosphere was relaxed, but stealing equipment is a bit… ”

“What are you thinking? Aren’t you the kind of person who would stop working just because you’re interested?”

Shirone shut his mouth.

Flew also felt a pang of remorse but eventually accepted it. “Actually, I don’t even know. But everyone here is the president of the association, so they followed us all the way here. I just trust the president of the association.”

Gaold connected the Piper to the Synchro Machine.

As he closed his arms inside, the Kuroi mirrored the motion.

“Are you feeling okay?”

Since there was no tempered glass, Gaold monitored the surroundings with his eyes while maneuvering the tank.

“Hmm, how about this?”

When he turned the drive joint on the waist all the way, the Kuroi’s waist began to spin like a top.

“Kuroi. Kang Nan, look at this.”

When Gaold jokingly swung the Kuroi’s arm, Kang Nan easily dodged by leaning back and exclaimed. “Oh really! I’m afraid you’ll break it!”

These were the words of someone who had already destroyed four Kuroi, so Kanya pleaded with sincere eyes. “Please bear with that. The Kuroi is a really valuable piece of equipment.”

“Whoa, okay. I’ll die of stress because of that person.”

Only then did Gaold take a proper look around.

It wasn’t comparable to Spirit Zone’s synesthesia, but the Multivision provided an excellent field of view, and its firepower was powerful enough to rival an air gun.

“How many guns are there in the rebels?”

“That is confidential. I can’t tell you.”

The Kuroi’s gun barrel clicked as it pointed toward the platoon commander.

It was impossible to tell how much of it was a joke and how serious he was.

“It’s also confidential, but I don’t know. At most, it’s about the status of the battalion’s equipment. There’s no way a platoon commander knows the total production of the rebels.”

Gaold licked his lips and stopped the aircraft. He jumped off the Kuroi, tossed aside his Piper, and quietly rejoined Shirone’s group.

Kang Nan crossed her arms and glared at him. “Why? It acted like it was going to ride all day.”

“I get motion sickness. Worse than words.”

Kang Nan gasped and snorted.

On the other hand, Gaold’s mind was racing.

*I wouldn’t even have a chin on that one. At least 2,000? No, more than that.*

It was the calculated strength of a single Kuroi unit required to capture one ordinary angel.

* * *

As the sun went down, Shirone and the others left the forest.

When they arrived at the hill overlooking the Plains of the Dead, the scene of the giant procession and the 23rd Battalion of the Rebel 1st HQ engaged in local battle stretched all the way to the horizon.

The battalion possessed a total of 30 Kuroi.

However, because the group Kanya belonged to had been sent as reinforcements, only twenty-five Kuroi were currently in operation.

Divided into five groups, the Kuroi troops cooperated with the infantry to lure a handful of giants at least one kilometer away from the column, then unleashed concentrated fire.

Every time a giant collapsed into pieces, Shirone wore a grim expression.

Giants were also human.

If the Mecca members had once been subjects, they would know that fact better than anyone else, so Kanya’s face was similarly shaded with sorrow.

“It is a rule of thumb to eliminate giants as soon as they are discovered. When they arrive in Jotunheim and achieve unity of mind, they will use the power of giants.”

The platoon commander said it like an excuse, but it wasn’t wrong.

If the eight egos overlapped, the schematic of the schema would become powerful enough to rival an elite swordsman. By that point, a single battalion would be unable to cope with their strength.

After entering the plains, the group quickly returned to their main unit.

Seeing the party with only one Kuroi, the company commander looked at them in disbelief at first, then suddenly lost his temper.

“What happened! It was Kuroi! I sent five planes to deal with it quickly. I thought I sent them to handle it properly!”

“Sorry.”

The platoon commander repeated the same words.

Anyway, since he was in charge of the platoon, he had nothing to say even if he had ten mouths.

But Kanya was different.

Shirone was back. He had also brought a much stronger ally than last time.

This was good news that could not be compared with the loss of tanks.

“Squadron commander! I have a report for you!”

“What are you! I can’t see you’re looking for a platoon commander! Are you ignoring the chain of command!”

The Kuroi pilot was an infantry squad leader, but reporting directly in front of the platoon leader was a huge disrespect.

But Kanya couldn’t wait.

It was an issue that the chief executive must know about now that the giant’s invasion line was being narrowed through the sound.

“The Light of District 73!”

“What? What nonsense are you talking about?”

Kanya burst into a tantrum and shouted. “Shirone is here!”
