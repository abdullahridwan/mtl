Chapter 418
Battle Readiness (1)
~*~
For the first time in a while, Shirone and his friends gathered at the Supernatural Psychic Research Society’s club room to enjoy a quiet afternoon.

Between his personal training and Gaold’s ongoing mission, a single day wasn’t enough to cover everything. Still, the feeling of collapsing onto the sofa on a rare day off and staring blankly at the ceiling was indescribably peaceful. *Why did I ever complain about being bored back then?* he wondered. *I had no idea how precious moments like this actually were.*

Shirone’s thoughts drifted back to a conversation from a few days ago.

“By the way, Maya,” Nade had said. “You’re a commoner. I didn’t know that.”

“Neither did I,” Maya had replied. “I’ve been looking into it since that day, but to be precise, my family are outsiders.”

“Outsiders?”

“It’s a mysterious minority tribe from the south. About two hundred of them settled in Tormia and cultivate a clan-secret herb called capura for medicinal use. That’s why we’re in debt. The stigma around our lack of conventional pharmaceuticals makes it hard to integrate into mainstream society, so it must be difficult for them.”

“Right. Then Maya’s family must be under a lot of pressure too.”

“I suppose so. I heard there are seven brothers in my household. It seems the tribe is pooling money to support my education. If I become a wizard, my standing within the Mystic Tribe will improve.”

“But how did you get into the magic school? If I hadn’t come from the Ogent family, I wouldn’t have even passed the special screening.”

“Mr. Collie recommended me. I don’t know the exact circumstances, but there must have been some connection.”

In the senior class, Collie was known as a strict head teacher, but he was kind-hearted underneath. It was entirely possible he had pulled strings enough to secure Maya’s spot.

“There was some gossip at the time,” Nade had added. “But the grades were surprisingly unremarkable, so it all got buried.”

*Was that a stroke of luck?* Shirone mused. *If I hadn’t stood out so much in the entrance exam, I probably wouldn’t have been resented as much.*

“Anyway, that’s rough,” Iruki had chimed in. “The burden on her must be enormous. She can’t even graduate without carrying the weight of her tribesmen’s expectations. It was pitiful watching her stretch thin portions of meat with water just to make it look like more.”

Shirone could relate to that part.

“Yeah,” Iruki continued. “Especially since Maya is a girl. Why wouldn’t she want to look pretty? Honestly, I think she’d look great if she lost a little weight. I can’t help it, though; we’re all in our senior year now.”

“Want me to show you what Maya looked like before?” Nade asked.

“Before? How?”

“I quit the club later, but back then, Nade collected a ton of their materials. Remember? A picture book.”

Shirone suddenly remembered. “Oh, the one the Female Figure Research Society publishes?”

“Exactly!” Nade rose from the couch. “We’re in our graduating class now. Most of the repeat students are probably featured in it.”

Without waiting for a response, Nade ducked under the desk. “Iruki, try calling Hosu and Paige.”

“The most accurate one is the latest issue,” Iruki said. “Publication No. 48, page eight.”

“Isn’t issue 48 from last year? Even back then, Maya must have already gained weight.”

“You’ll see when you look. Just bring it over.”

Nade rummaged through a stack of picture books stored in the club’s safe. He had stopped paying attention to the club’s activities after Shirone joined, but the collector’s instinct was too strong to let go of his archive.

“Let’s see… No. 48, No. 48… Ah, here it is.”

Nade placed the picture book on the table. Shirone and Iruki shifted closer on the sofa, leaning in to get a better look.

On page eight, a portrait of Maya’s former self was featured.

“Wow…”

The illustration was drawn in a modest, almost reverent style typical of the Female Figure Research Society. But they were stunned for a different reason.

“Is this really Maya? She’s so pretty.”

She didn’t fit Tormia’s conventional urban beauty standards, but she had a fresh, radiant charm that anyone could admire.

Nade nodded. “I remember. I saw this picture once and wondered who it was.”

Iruki explained, “Maya started gaining weight in her second year of senior class. The research group probably only featured her from earlier.”

“That’s rare. Do you have a crush on Maya?” Nade teased.

“Maybe,” Iruki replied. “But wouldn’t you say the feeling leans more toward respect than romance?”

Shirone felt a spike of curiosity. “Who exactly runs the Female Figure Research Society?”

“I don’t know,” Nade said. “If the administration catches them, they’ll be expelled immediately. But since we’re all in our senior year now, they probably can’t feature us while we’re still in advanced classes.”

The thought was unsettling. In short, they were essentially documenting their classmates for a graduation archive while crossing paths with them every day.

“Let’s check out the others,” Nade said. “As of last year, there must be senior girls featured.”

“Pony’s on page fourteen, Suabi on sixteen, and Dorothy on the last page,” Iruki listed off. “All three are in the graduating class.”

Shirone glanced at Iruki. “You really remember all this because of the Servant, don’t you?”

Nade pointed at the next page. “Hey, look. It’s Pony.”

Pony’s appearance in the illustration matched her current look. A symmetrical part accentuated her high forehead, while hair like spun gold cascaded down to frame her face. She wore a dress that showcased her shoulders and collarbone, her chin tilted slightly upward in an air of quiet, regal elegance.

“Oh, Suabi’s here too.”

In the drawing, Suabi sat by a stream, her knees drawn up as she wrapped a blanket around herself. A small bird perched on her hands, which were cupped together as if offering shelter. She wore a gentle, radiant smile that Shirone rarely saw in the school halls.

“And this must be Dorothy. Seeing her like this is quite different.”

Dorothy was depicted pulling up the hem of her black wool knit, biting it in a defiant pout. A teardrop-shaped navel was visible at the narrow curve of her waist, and her lower half was clad only in underwear, highlighting her long, graceful legs. The defining detail was the tin doll she never let go of, now hugging its neck in the illustration.

“I’ve said it before, but the artist is really good,” Nade murmured. “It’s like they just trapped the real person in the pages.”

Nade sighed and closed the picture book. “Man, there are so many beautiful girls at this school. How come we don’t have any partners?”

“Shirone’s got one,” Iruki pointed out.

“Oh, right! What am I talking about…” Nade’s eyes twinkled as he turned to Shirone. “Come to think of it, you and Amy went on a trip. Did anything happen?”

“Anything? What do you mean?” Shirone’s face flushed instantly. Obviously, nothing had happened. But why was his heart racing?

“What? Are you really dodging the question? You’d better be honest!”

Shirone shifted his gaze left and right. Realizing he had no backup, he resorted to his last resort.

“Ah, I don’t know! I’m going to sleep! I’ve been so tired lately. Wake me up in an hour!”

As expected, sleep was the best way to escape the awkwardness.

* * *

When Shirone woke, he pushed himself up from the sofa. His friends had already returned to their lodgings.

“What time is it?” he muttered.

A note was left on the table:
*We’re heading out first. You’ve looked exhausted lately, so get some good rest. We’ll see you later at the restaurant. Also, the red book, number 24, in the safe under the desk is absolutely awesome—you should never look at it.*

Shirone yawned, crumpled the note, and stood up.

“Still, I feel much more refreshed after a nap like this.”

He gathered his toiletries and headed toward the senior building. He didn’t need to go back to the hostel; he planned to wash up at school before heading to the cafeteria.

As he finished brushing his teeth and splashing water on his face, the five o’clock bell rang.

“Ahhh.”

He yawned, shaking off the last remnants of drowsiness. As he walked down the hallway, he heard music drifting from somewhere.

“What?”

Someone was singing in the music room at the end of the corridor. It wasn’t a magical vocal performance, but a popular song.

“Wow, you’re really good.”

The singing was surprisingly skilled.

*Could there be a professional singer among us?*

When Shirone pushed open the music room door, he found Maya sitting at a piano lined up against the window, singing along with her playing.

It was a cheerful rhythm and blues tune. As soon as he stepped inside, a shiver ran up his spine at the raw, unfiltered sound of her voice.

*There is nothing more beautiful than you.*

It wasn’t just good singing. He didn’t know much about music theory, but the moment he heard it, he knew. Maya’s voice was something entirely different.
