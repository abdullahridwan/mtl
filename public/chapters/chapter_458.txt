Chapter 458

Definition of Creature (4)

Fleur couldn’t make sense of Shirone’s words.

“Have you ever been here?”

Babel was known as the Tomb of Angels—a place sealed away long before any of the current generation had been born. It was impossible for Shirone, only nineteen years old, to have set foot here before.

“Actually… I don’t know,” Shirone replied, his voice distant.

“Hmm. Is it like déjà vu?”

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t have warranted much thought. But combined with the fact that the steel golems had collapsed just before attacking him, it was no longer something they could ignore.

“Let’s move somewhere safer for now. It’s too dangerous here.”

“Wait,” Joon interjected, pointing at the crackling electricity coiled around the cone. “Are you really going to leave that behind?”

Even if it wasn’t an electric spirit, abandoning such a potent source of energy felt like a waste. Fleur agreed—but something about it unsettled her. Jo Won, though far from weak, had died instantly, his body shattered by a single blast. He hadn’t even had time to react.

“Shirone,” Fleur asked carefully, “how do you feel?”

Shirone stared at the glowing energy, his expression unfocused. It wasn’t a memory, exactly—more like a faint echo of something long buried.

*Did I see this before?*

His lips moved silently as he sifted through the fragments of his past.

“Ultima.”

“Hm? Shirone, what did you say?” Fleur leaned closer.

“…What?” Shirone blinked, startled.

“You just muttered ‘Ultima.’” Fleur confirmed, nodding.

Shirone frowned. “Did I?”

Fleur didn’t doubt herself—she was meticulous, and never wrong about details like this. But Shirone couldn’t recall saying it.

“I want to take a closer look,” he said.

“You know it could be dangerous, right?”

“That’s exactly why we need to investigate. The device controlling Babel’s traps should be in the cockpit.”

It made sense.

“Fine,” Fleur relented. “But I’ll do it.”

“Sir, that’s—”

“Listen,” Shirone cut in. “This kind of thing is supposed to be handled by the second-in-command.”

No matter the situation, Shirone’s safety came first. Fleur understood, but she still stepped forward, drawing the phoenix and gently applying it to the electricity.

A sharp crackling sound echoed, the energy flaring in irritation—but there was no backlash. No pain, no resistance.

“Hmm. Doesn’t seem dangerous,” Fleur murmured.

As if waiting for that exact moment, Jo Won lunged forward and grabbed the electricity with his bare hand.

“It’s mine!”

He was the man with the long philtrum, assigned by Reisis to keep watch over Shirone. Perhaps chosen for his calm demeanor, but now, faced with raw power, he was no different from any other human consumed by desire.

Electricity surged up his arm as he shouted, convinced he was forming a contract with a spirit.

“Spirit! Make a pact with me!”

Blue light flared across the cockpit, the energy flooding into his body. His eyes rolled back, overwhelmed by visions of unimaginable power.

“Oh… this is… incredible… exorbitant…”

His ecstatic muttering ended with a wet *pop*—his face exploded.

Shirone and Fleur stared in horror at the corpse, now headless.

“Idiot,” Shirone muttered. “No matter how desperate you are, you don’t just grab something without checking what it is.”

It wasn’t about patience. It was the Norse people’s obsession with spirits—so deep it overrode survival instinct. A mindset Shirone and Fleur, who could activate magic freely through the Spirit Zone, could never fully understand.

Fleur turned to the last surviving member of their group. He shook his head, raised his hands, and stepped back.

Life was more valuable than power. A wise choice.

“Shirone,” Fleur said, “are you really going to keep going?”

She wanted to stop. But Shirone, shaken by Jo Won’s death, was more determined than ever.

Babel was sealed with henna—a place that existed before the Subjects were born.

“Only angels or Nephilim could have come and gone from here,” Shirone said. “Immortality must be a basic function. The steel golems appeared because someone *other* than a Nephilim entered.”

Fleur had to admit, the logic was sound. But with two dead already, caution was necessary.

“Just… be careful. Emergencies happen.”

Shirone nodded and reached out toward the electricity.

No pain. No resistance. The current flowed up his arm like a living thing, curling around his fingers.

Then—

A rushing wind roared inside his skull. It felt like a drill boring into his brain.

**Ultima System – A cosmic integrated information system.**

**According to the number of laws: 1.**

Unique, singular concepts—ones that overlapped with nothing else in existence—flooded his mind at near-light speed.

“Huh—!”

Shirone understood, in that moment, why Jo Won’s head had exploded. His own mind felt like it was on the verge of shattering.

Then—the Immortal Function activated.

A wave of overwhelming liberation surged through him. The concepts of Ultima scattered, vanishing into an infinite passage.

His mind went blank.

Only a thin, blue thread of light remained, tethering him to the outside world.

The last crew member approached, swallowing hard.

“W-what *was* that spirit?”

Shirone turned, startled.

“Say that again.”

“I… I was wondering what attribute the spirit had.”

When Shirone heard the man’s voice—without the drone’s interpretation mode—he finally understood.

He couldn’t make out the pronunciation. But the *meaning* of the words seeped directly into his mind.

*Who the hell created this system?*

Language was irrelevant. Any symbol in existence could be converted into a single signal and deciphered.

Shirone pressed his hand fully into the cone. Electricity arced across his palm, and the mechanism activated.

The flow of information became as clear as blood vessels beneath skin.

“That must be the steering device,” Fleur said.

Shirone nodded and connected to the main circuit, searching for Babel’s core.

Instantly, a bird’s-eye view of a structure so vast it resembled a floating castle unfolded in his mind.

*Thud. Thud.*

The cockpit wall slid open, revealing a cylindrical glass tube. It spun rapidly, then fired a red beam of light.

“That’s the main elevator.”

Operating on the same principle as the one in Arabot, it transported the three of them 100 meters underground in an instant.

“Wow… that’s huge.”

The control room stretched so wide its boundaries were invisible. A massive screen, ten times larger than life, hovered in the air. Beside it stood another cone-shaped pillar, identical to the one in the cockpit.

Shirone approached and gently inserted his hand.

*Why do I feel like I’ve been here before? Maybe the answer is here.*

Using the Ultima System, he accessed the records. The screen lit up.

To Fleur, it looked like pages flipping at impossible speed, leaving afterimages in their wake. Shirone, however, read calmly, as if turning the pages of a book.

Then the screen froze.

Strange symbols—neither henna nor any known Subject language—appeared slowly.

Only Shirone’s eyes moved, tracking the text.

Through Ultima’s single signal, the meaning reached him clearly.

**Log: The Babel Project**

**Light Power Omega Year 133** – Gaia, the first human, overcomes all biological limitations—gender, disposition, personality—at birth, achieving an integrated mental system.

**Omega Year 187** – Gaia attempts to escape the photon world via the Ultima System, but fails due to the barrier of Ankera’s Akashic Records.

**Omega Year 201** – Gaia declares war on Heaven. Conflict between the Ultima System and the Akashic Records begins.

**Omega Year 387** – Ankera, 1st Reset Attempt. Attempt to stop Gaia using the Ultima System.

**Omega Year 412** – Ankera, 2nd Reset Attempt fails.

**Omega Year 666** – Gaia loses the final war. 90% decline in Gaian population.

**Omega Year 689** – First anti-war movement begins. Gaia successfully implements Halo by analyzing the angel’s infinite algorithm.

**Omega Year 717** – Gaia creates a strategic weapon of mass destruction to eliminate Ankera. Named Babel.

**Omega Year 738** – Mass production of Babel begins.

**Omega Year 777** – Gaia loses the second war.

**Omega Year 799** – Last remaining Gaia (RECORDS EXPUNGED) suggests possibility of a 2nd photon field exit. Authorization granted by Ultima System.

**Omega Year 892** – (RECORDS EXPUNGED). Talks with Ankera collapse.

**Omega Year 927** – Ankera dismantles the Ultima System under false pretenses.

The Gaians splinter into Kergo, Nor, and Mecca.

**Omega Year 987** – (RECORDS EXPUNGED). Ikael (RECORDS EXPUNGED) announces to the Subjects.

Heaven descends into chaos.

**Omega Year 999** – Last Gaian (RECORDS EXPUNGED) exits the photon world.

**Record end.**

Shirone stared, speechless.

Before the Subjects existed, Gaia had waged war against Heaven. Having achieved an integrated information system, they created Babel—an ancient weapon capable of killing Ankera.

But after losing the final war, the Gaians fractured into three races: Kergo, Nor, and Mecca.

The timeline was divided into percentages—impossible to convert into exact years—but one thing was clear.

The last Gaian, the one whose name had been erased from the records, was the one who left this message.

*There are two kinds of expungements. One hides the name of a Gaian. The other… hides what Ikael committed as a great sin.*

The screen reset.

A single line flickered—words carved by the last Gaian.

**Beyond infinity.**

As Shirone stared at the message, Fleur could no longer contain her frustration and stepped forward.

“What? Why are you staring at something you can’t even understand?”

Shirone shook his head. He couldn’t explain—not yet. Jo Won’s death had confirmed something vital about the Ultima System.

“I’ll tell you later. There’s something I need to check first. Follow me.”

When the control device activated, the iron wall slid open, revealing a path.

At the end of that path lay Babel—the ultimate weapon forged by the first humans to kill God.

*What does it look like?*

They walked through the passage and emerged into a chamber shaped like a womb.

A single beam of light shone down like a spotlight. In the center, a figure sat with knees drawn to its chest.

It was a woman sculpted from sleek, black, glossy metal. Dozens of wires descended from the ceiling, connecting to her in thick clusters.

Joon’s face turned pale.

“Oh… you’re an angel…”

“No,” Shirone said. “That’s Babel.”

Fleur frowned. “Babel? I thought that was the name of this place.”

“It’s not. Babel is a weapon—an ancient construct designed to mimic the power of angels.”

“What are you talking about? You’ve been saying weird things since we got here. Explain properly!”

*Wheein!*

Electricity surged as the facility’s systems powered on.

Energy flowed through the wires into Babel, transmitted via the Ultima System.

The area was naturally charged—lightning struck constantly—but even so, the output was staggering.

Babel’s body began to vibrate violently.

It was like a fetus, absorbing every ounce of its mother’s energy, preparing for birth.

Her slender neck lifted. Her legs, once folded, unfolded as if weightless.

Over two meters tall, with a lithe frame and long blue wings, she rose. Her face—beautiful, serene—bore a striking resemblance to someone Shirone knew.

“Ikael?”

Red light ignited in Babel’s eyes. A halo of brilliant white light spun above her head. A high-frequency sound, almost a roar, filled the chamber.

“Sir Shirone,” Fleur whispered, gripping his shoulder. “We need to leave. *Now.*”

She felt it—the unimaginable power radiating from Babel. It wasn’t just strong. It was *angelic*.

*Did the Association President really intend to fight something like this?*

Babel floated one meter above the ground, her gaze locking onto the three intruders.

- Running Babel Program. Applying Kariel’s Algorithm.

- First Objective: Punish the intruders.

- **Dismissed.**

Her form dissolved into a tangle of electricity and vanished.

Then—a blade, sharp as a thought, stopped a hair’s breadth from Shirone’s face.

Only when he heard the faint whine of air at his fingertips did Shirone realize what had happened.

Frozen in space, he couldn’t move.

Babel’s face slowly emerged from the electric haze, inches from his own.

- **Warning! Warning!**

- Upper Algorithm determines non-hostility.

- Analysis: Gaia Code Number 2 detected. Ultima System application confirmed.

- Verification: Target is absolutely inviolable.

Babel locked eyes with Shirone. Then, silently, she sheathed her weapon and stepped back, metallic feet clinking against the floor.

- Reapplying Kariel Algorithm.

- Second Objective: Exterminate all Subjects.

“Hey—hey—*hey!*”

The halo spun faster. Babel’s body lifted effortlessly into the air.

The three of them looked up, paralyzed by dread.

Babel’s form twisted into lightning and shot upward, tearing through the ceiling with a thunderous roar.

“Huh!”

They stood in the center of the trembling chamber, staring at the perfectly circular hole now drilled through the roof.

**Black Angel Advent.**
