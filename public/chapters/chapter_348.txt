Chapter 348  
Ephemeral Rift (3)  
~*~

Shirone lay on his bed and enjoyed the silence.

Since he would be entering the senior class next semester, he could skip the performance evaluation. In another sense, however, this vacation was his last chance to polish his fundamentals. That was why Iruki and Ned had both refused to spend it playing around and had returned straight to their families.

“I need to rest first.”

Even as he said it aloud, Shirone laughed at how absurd it sounded. But right now, he truly did not want to do anything.

It was the first time he had ever felt this way, and he was embarrassed by it. Still, the fact that he did not feel impatient seemed to prove there was a reason.

“Now that I think about it, I really did work hard.”

Looking back over the past year, he could hardly believe he had survived it all. It felt like only yesterday that he had been last in Class 7, yet he was already at the top of Class 4.

“I suffered enough to earn a break.”

Every student at magic school worked hard, but Shirone had been involved in more incidents than most.

Through struggles where his life had been on the line, he had grown stronger. To be honest, he was confident that even if he entered the senior class, he would not be easily outmatched.

Shirone got up from the bed and approached [Demon Sword Armand]. It was satisfying just to see him standing upright without any support.

Thanks to rated control, there was no problem carrying him. But while attending school, Shirone would have no reason to use the sword.

When he drew Armand from his scabbard, a clear, high-frequency sound rang along the blade. Though shorter than a longsword, it was sharp enough for real combat, and its center of gravity was well balanced.

‘Why is he so quiet? Did he die or something?’

Since returning home, Shirone had sent mental waves to Armand every night, but the sword had remained silent. There had been so little response that Shirone wondered whether the contract had ended without him realizing it.

Ever since he had shown Armand to his friends, he had not activated Geumgang Armed Forces. There had not been any particular need to, but somehow it felt awkward.

Once something shared its thoughts with humans, it became difficult to treat it entirely like an object. If he activated Geumgang Armed Forces for no reason, wouldn’t Armand look at him strangely?

Of course, he knew there was no need to think that way. It was simply something he would have to grow used to.

‘Should I try it? It’s my sword anyway…’

It was frustrating to receive an S-class object and not even be able to use it. It was not that he wanted to fight anyone, but imagining himself playing an active role with Armand made his heart swell.

With a proud look, Shirone gazed at the sword and spoke the activation words.

“Geumgang Armed Forces.”

His fear that nothing might happen proved unfounded. The blade opened without hesitation and swallowed Shirone.

The change took place in an instant. A hood covered his face, and a cloak of fibers fluttered out like flames. A glass sphere emerged from the palm of the gauntlet, and finally the cybernetic brain began to whirl rapidly around Shirone.

“Whoa!”

Shirone felt wonderful.

His senses were clearer than ever. The focus point had deepened, giving him a sense of fullness, as though he could amplify any kind of magic.

Armand shared a brain with his user and adjusted his abilities according to the situation, but since there was no combat at the moment, no special change took place.

Shirone approached the full-length mirror and examined himself. Wearing a shabby robe that no longer looked new, with his face hidden beneath a hood, he looked like a fairly convincing wizard. On top of that, Armand’s form made him look more ominous and mysterious than any ordinary weapon could.

Even if it was not right away, the thought of one day becoming a professional mage and exploring the world like this made his heart pound.

Shirone clenched his fists and struck a pose as though gathering mana.

He was the wizard of Immortal Function. One day, he would become an infinite wizard and rise to the top of the world.

“Oohhh!”

Shirone shouted toward the ceiling—then his face stiffened when he saw the reflection in the mirror.

Vincent had opened the door and was standing there, staring at him with an incredulous expression.

“Oh, Dad!”

Flustered, Shirone stomped his feet.

He was grateful he had the hood on. If his face had been exposed, it might have exploded from embarrassment.

“Your mother says to come eat. Hurry downstairs.”

“Ah, okay.”

Vincent closed the door, leaving one remark behind as he went.

“Aren’t you a little old for that?”

Shirone’s face grew hot, and he lowered his head.

When he heard the door shut, he pulled off the hood and grabbed his head.

“Ugh! So embarrassing! Today was the first time I tried it!”

As expected, doing something that did not suit him only brought disaster. Shirone canceled Armand. The fibers and glass sphere were sucked back into the demonic sword.

Using rated control, Shirone returned Armand to his scabbard, left him in the corner, and went down to the first floor.

The smell of soup on the table made his stomach tighten with sudden hunger. Shirone sat down and picked up his spoon first. Olina smiled and poured soup into his bowl.

“You must be hungry. Go on, eat.”

“Yes. Thank you for the meal!”

The food at magic school was carefully prepared to provide the nutrients mages needed, but as expected, home-cooked food was the best.

Olina happily watched her son finish a bowl of soup in a rush. Then she poured him more and asked, “How are you going to spend this vacation? Do you have plans?”

“I’m going to rest for a bit, then start training seriously. But before that, there’s something I want to do.”

“What do you want to do?”

“Every winter, there’s a local festival in the Les Mountains. May I go?”

Vincent cut in.

“Oh, you mean the ruins of that old castle. You said you wanted to go there before.”

“Really? I don’t remember.”

“You were very young. It must have stayed in your heart without you knowing. Go ahead. Back then, things were difficult, so we couldn’t even show you around properly.”

Olina propped her chin on her hand and smiled knowingly.

No matter how much her son pretended otherwise, he could not fool his mother’s eyes.

“So, who are you going with?”

Shirone felt as if he had been ambushed. He had no particular reason to feel guilty, but his face burned.

“Well… Amy. I haven’t asked her yet.”

“Oh, so it’s a date.”

“No! I just thought she might be feeling down, so I wanted to help her change her mood… Uh, she might refuse anyway!”

Olina knew that Amy had failed the graduation exam. She did not mind. Amy, who had gone through Kazura with them, was a considerate and upright girl.

Besides, she had heard that the Karmis family treated Shirone well too. Hadn’t they gone to the island together in the summer?

“All right, go. It takes about three days to properly look around the old castle site. There are plenty of places to stay, so don’t worry, but you need to take good care of Amy. Don’t say you’re taking her there to comfort her and then try to get comforted yourself.”

“…Yes.”

Olina’s last words carried a strangely sharp nuance, but Shirone answered obediently. Animal instinct told him it would be dangerous to dig too deeply.

After clearing the dishes, Shirone went up to his room and packed his things. It would take quite a while to reach Creas, so he needed to leave before noon.

Teleporting would get him there faster than a carriage, but he did not want to break the rules over something trivial.

Wearing a wool scarf over his winter coat, Shirone put on his rucksack and headed for the door.

Just as he grabbed the doorknob, a pulse rang through his head. When he turned around, there was no doubt that Armand was sending a mental wave. He could not hear a voice, but it was clearly an echo of protest.

Shirone sighed.

“Sorry, but no. Commoners can’t wear swords. I don’t even have a weapon license.”

For a commoner to carry a weapon, they needed a weapon possession license issued by a specific guild. Once Shirone became a wizard, he would obtain one automatically, but as a student, even that was impossible. The S-class Armand was inconvenient in many ways.

Armand did not give up and continued sending mental waves.

This was the problem with an Ego. It had a will of its own. Shirone did not know what kind of owners Armand had passed through until now, but the sword seemed unable to understand why, after finally forming a contract, his master would not take him along.

Perhaps Armand had been in a state of resignation when he belonged to Zeon. Wasn’t that why he had sent out the light of temptation even while his previous owner was present?

‘Thinking of it that way makes me feel bad. I can’t be less merciful than his previous owner.’

As if he had no choice, Shirone approached Armand.

To be honest, it was true that Armand was reliable in any situation. Even if he were caught at a checkpoint, rated control would allow him to hide the sword somewhere else.

“Whew, fine. Let’s go.”

Shirone opened his coat and called Armand.

The sword flew like an arrow and settled at his waist. The fact that he was shorter than a longsword made him easier to conceal. Once Shirone covered him with his coat, only the tip of the scabbard was slightly exposed. At a glance, no one would even know a sword was there.

When Armand’s mental wave disappeared, Shirone shook his head and left the room. They said there were many demonic swords that could kill their masters, so he had no choice but to take this level of stubbornness as a charm.

* * *

Tormia Kingdom.

The Magic Association, located in the capital, Baska.

The Allog District was an area where major institutions were concentrated, even within the capital. Anyone passing through at noon would inevitably frown, because the many high-rise buildings reflected the sunlight.

In particular, the Magic Association, one of the tallest buildings, looked as if it had caught fire in gold.

At the main gate of the Magic Association, a neatly dressed woman had been standing for thirty minutes, waiting for someone while watching the main street.

She had dark skin, and her curly blond hair was tied back. Red horn-rimmed glasses rested on her face. Her nose was small but high, and her pink lips were pressed tightly together, revealing something of her personality. With the corners of her eyes slightly lowered, she gave a strong impression, yet overall she possessed an unusual charm rarely seen in Tormia.

She was the chief secretary of the Tormia Magic Association, Ahoya Kang Nan.

Located near the equator, the southern country of Iran was a confederation of numerous tribes. Kang Nan was the last descendant of the now-extinct wolf tribe and a warrior trained in the ancient martial art Ram Muay.

She was also exceptionally intelligent. Though she had begun studying late after coming to Tormia at the age of fifteen, she had overcome fierce competition and risen to the position of chief secretary of the Magic Association.

However, the old and lecherous nobles of Tormia did not see her that way.

To them, Kang Nan was merely an exotic-looking commoner woman with a perfectly proportioned body. Countless men had tried to fool around without understanding her personality, only to have their abdominal muscles torn by her knee kicks.

Among them had been high-ranking nobles, but the Magic Association had not fired her. It was a classic example of ability overshadowing personality.

Today, however, she intended to control the intensity. The person she was waiting for was the highest envoy from the neighboring Yakma Republic.

When she saw a carriage with splendid decorations appear at the end of the avenue, Kang Nan straightened her clothes again. Just as she checked her high heels for dust and adjusted her horn-rimmed glasses, the carriage arrived.

The iron-clad bodyguards opened the door, and a sullen-looking man emerged. His neck was thick with fat, his chin was buried, and his belly bulged like a cannonball. However, the eyes sunk beneath his eyelids shone sharply.

‘He is the country’s highest envoy. I cannot treat him carelessly.’

Kang Nan walked forward neatly, her heels clicking against the ground. Then she set the folder in her arms against one arm and bowed at a ninety-degree angle.

“Welcome. My name is Kang Nan, chief secretary of the Magic Association.”

“Aha? You look quite tough. Nice to meet you. I am Ordon.”

Ordon extended his hand for a handshake.

It was proper manners for the highest envoy to offer a handshake to the chief secretary first, but in truth, he had another intention.

The stories of Kang Nan, who had sent countless nobles crashing down, had even spread to social circles in other countries. When her exotic appearance was added to the titles of commoner and untouchable flower, Ordon could not help but be interested.

When Kang Nan shook his hand, Ordon gently rubbed the back of her smooth hand with his thumb.

Kang Nan’s expression did not change. She had heard rumors about Ordon as well, so she had prepared herself for a certain amount of provocation.

“The president of the association is waiting for you. I will guide you.”

Ordon entered the Magic Association while admiring Kang Nan’s narrow waist.

Seeing that she was still fine after beating Tormia’s nobles, it seemed the Magic Association was protecting her. But surely they would not dare disrespect the highest envoy of another country.

‘Let me pick that flower.’
