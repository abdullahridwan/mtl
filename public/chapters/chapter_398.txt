Chapter 398  
Special Training (5)

~*~

Flew rummaged through the box again and lifted out a heavy drone with both hands.

The drone flew by interacting with the training ground’s magnetic field. Because its trajectory could change violently and unpredictably, it was well suited for induction experiments.

“We’ll start with step one,” Flew said. “Get used to the sensation first.”

Shirone aimed a laser pointer at the drone. When Flew flipped the switch, the drone began to vibrate. She let go, and it shot into the air.

Shirone tracked the drone’s path with the laser pointer. At a certain point, eight Photon Cannons appeared at once.

They were slower than the standard Photon Cannon, but photon-type magic was still not a speed that could be dismissed.

Shirone watched the Homing Photon Cannons anxiously.

The drone wriggled along a path that skimmed past the wall. At that moment, all eight Photon Cannons curved simultaneously and struck the target.

A chain of explosions erupted. The drone burst out through the smoke and rolled across the floor.

“It worked! Success!”

Shirone could hardly believe this was magic he had cast.

Light had bent.

When the Photon Cannons made that sharp turn in front of the wall, he even felt a thrill run through him.

Flew did not think the result was bad either.

“A good start. But it’s not ready for actual combat yet. A Tier 1 drone can be hit with a normal Photon Cannon, so let’s set that as the acceptance standard. Your Homing Photon Cannon needs to hit drones that are difficult to catch with an ordinary Photon Cannon.”

“Yes! I’ll definitely make it work!”

After succeeding in the first experiment, Shirone’s determination burned hotter than ever.

The difficulty rose step by step, and at last, the day before he was scheduled to leave the Magic Association arrived.

Before the final evaluation, Shirone ran through his sequence faster than he ever had before.

Flew, waiting with the drone, looked just as nervous as he did. She had invested no small amount of passion into this training.

“Are you ready? It’s a Level 7 mod. Maximum trajectory limit, 27 degrees. This is the movement level of a professional wizard.”

“Yes. It has to be at least that good if I’m going to use it in real combat. I’m ready.”

Shirone launched the Homing Photon Cannons with far more skill than before. Without hesitation, eight laser points locked onto the drone.

The algorithm had been refined so that every aiming point now converged on a single spot.

“Here it goes.”

As soon as Flew hit the switch, she released the drone and stepped back.

The lump of iron, shaking the air with the force of its movement, flew lightly and flexibly through the training ground.

Its magnetically driven trajectory was certainly unfamiliar to human eyes accustomed to gravity. But the Homing Photon Cannons trembling around Shirone continued to track the drone to the very end, their red targeting points moving without pause.

‘Let’s go! I’m ending it here!’

The eight Homing Photon Cannons shot away from Shirone and chased the drone. Then, when the drone nearly reversed direction, they made a harsh curve and sprinted after it in the opposite direction.

A total of nine flying objects roamed all over the wide training ground, but Shirone only looked straight ahead.

The chase lasted three seconds.

In terms of pure speed, the Photon Cannons were faster. However, the drone evaded capture by using rapid drifts to shorten the effective pursuit distance and throw off their approach.

Even so, the seekers did not lose the drone until the end, and the distance between them finally began to close.

The leading Photon Cannon struck the drone, and the drone slammed down onto the floor with a bang.

Then the remaining seven Photon Cannons plunged after it, bombarding the rolling drone in succession.

Kwa-kwa-kwa! Kwak! Kwa-kwa-kwam!

Flew shouted, eyes wide.

“Wow! That was seriously—! No, that was amazing!”

The accuracy had been high enough to chase down a drone even after it had been thrown off course by the impact. Moreover, the power was strong enough to crush hard metal.

This was beyond merely being effective in real combat. If an opponent were struck without warning, it would be a massacre.

Shirone also found the result hard to believe.

‘Magic… really has no end.’

Only after the reality sank in did Shirone turn back to Flew, overcome with emotion.

For a moment, their eyes met. Then, without either of them saying who moved first, they ran to each other, hugged, and spun around in excitement.

“Success! We did it, Senior!”

“Great job! If you’d failed, I really would’ve been embarrassed! I’m finally free too!”

Their shouts, half-mad with joy, echoed through the training ground.

Only the dented drone remained on the floor, staring at them pitifully.

* * *

The last night at the Magic Association arrived.

After receiving Flew’s call, Shirone headed to the waiting room on the first floor.

The empty hallway felt lonely compared to the bustling atmosphere of daytime.

“But why the waiting room? She could have come to the dorm.”

At the end of the corridor behind the first-floor lobby, light leaked through a crack in the door. The darkness was silent, and he could not hear any voices.

“Hello?”

The moment Shirone opened the door and peeked inside, party poppers exploded.

He stepped back in surprise. The door opened wider, revealing four smiling faces.

“Congratulations, Shirone!”

“Uh, huh?”

A cake had been set in the middle of the waiting room, and Flew, Gando, Isabelle, and Kang Nan stood around the table.

Flew snapped her fingers to light the candle. Then she waved him in.

“What are you doing? Hurry up and blow it out.”

Shirone walked forward as if possessed.

“Wait, what day is it today?”

“It’s the day. The day you completed your field trip at the Magic Association. Now! Hurry up.”

Everyone clapped as Shirone blew out the candle.

The completion of his Magic Association field trip.

It sounded plausible enough when said aloud, but the more he thought about it, the hotter his face grew.

“Thank you. But it was just a visit. It’s nothing special.”

Isabelle ran a hand through her straight hair.

He had always seen her looking neat and composed at work, so with her hair down, she seemed far more alluring than usual.

“Even so, this is the Magic Association. We can’t simply send off the student who stayed here for the longest field trip period without doing anything. I heard your training ended well. Flew told me about it. She said you looked impressive. Congratulations.”

Flew added another meaning to the celebration.

“And you’re nineteen now too. You can think of this as a birthday party.”

“Oh, right!”

He had been so absorbed in training that he had not even realized the year had changed. The many things that had happened last year compressed together into one dense lump of emotion. It was far larger than anything he had felt in his life before.

Kang Nan, who had been standing a little apart, held out a small box past Isabelle’s shoulder.

“Congratulations, Shirone. This is a gift prepared by the Association.”

“Oh, thank you. But what is it?”

He had formed relationships with the people here, but there was no reason for the Association itself to take special care of a field trip student. Sensing Gaold’s influence behind it, Shirone wondered if there was some hidden meaning to the gift.

“It’s just a gift. Open it.”

When he opened the box, a silver signet ring gleamed inside. It was about three centimeters wide, with a magic circle engraved in its center.

“Huh? Isn’t this a Kubrick?”

At Flew’s words, Shirone turned his head.

“Kubrick?”

“A dimensional ring. Every Association employee has one. Do you know what a dimensional ring is?”

“No, I don’t.”

Since Shirone had advanced so quickly, he actually knew very little about magic tools.

“It’s a ring that stores objects using scale magic. They’re sold at the Alchemy Chamber too, but they’re quite expensive. On top of that, the ones issued by the Association have a much more precise encryption system than ordinary Kubricks, so they’re good quality.”

A ring that stored objects in subspace was naturally expensive.

But what Shirone liked most was the seal of the Tormia Magic Association engraved on the inside of the ring.

No matter how expensive a Kubrick was, the Association’s seal was not something money could buy.

Isabelle took the ring and slipped it onto Shirone’s ring finger.

“The principle is the same as other magic tools. It reacts to the Spirit Zone. Flew, the power controller…”

Flew went out into the hallway and shut off the magic control system in the waiting room.

The request had come from the head of the magic library, the captain of the guard had permitted it, and the chief secretary had tolerated it, so there was no problem.

When Flew returned, Shirone entered the Spirit Zone.

Through synesthesia, he sensed a unique magic he had never felt before. As he focused his attention on it, letters appeared in front of him.

[User registration? Yes or no?]

When Shirone looked around as if asking whether everyone else could see it too, they all smiled.

“Wait, this…”

Flew grinned broadly.

“Can you see the letters? Only you can see them. They’re projected onto your retina through brain waves.”

Shirone remembered the drone from heaven that had been given to Tess.

It must have used a similar technique, projecting the view from the sky directly onto her retinas.

When registration finished, a circle appeared before him, divided like a pie into seven equal slots. The slots were all the same size, and the edge of the top slot glowed red.

Knowing that no one else could see it, Shirone pointed to the slot.

“Do things go in here?”

Isabelle said, “That’s right. Based on gravity here, it can store up to forty-five kilograms in total. If you put forty-five kilograms into one slot, the remaining six slots won’t be able to hold anything.”

“Wow, forty-five kilograms?”

That was enough to store a decent amount of equipment. It could also reduce the burden of carrying food on long journeys, making it an extremely convenient tool.

“Let’s practice. First, choose something you want to store.”

“Oh, then this…”

Shirone took Armand from his waist.

Carrying a sword as a commoner had already bothered him, and as a wizard, moving around with a piece of metal attached to him was uncomfortable.

“Good. I’ll teach you how. No need to be nervous. It’s easy. As easy as the four-way method.”

Shirone almost laughed at hearing her call the four-way method easy, but he decided that must be the standard for a Class Three wizard and let it pass.

“Kubrick distinguishes objects based on perception within the mind. That’s the core concept. Kubrick recognizes the sword you’re holding as a completed object, so it separates that sword from the rest of the world. Therefore, you can’t store only part of the sword.”

“Because it doesn’t recognize that as a completed object.”

“Correct. Next is loading and unloading. It’s simple. Think of putting the ring over the object. When your thought changes, the nature of your Spirit Zone also changes minutely, and Kubrick works by detecting that minute difference. That’s why living creatures cannot be stored. Even the spirit of an insignificant creature changes the Spirit Zone. In other words, from Kubrick’s point of view, it’s a foreign substance.”

Shirone nodded, impressed by Kubrick’s sensitivity.

“Then I’ll try.”

He gripped Armand and imagined loading it. The sword disappeared as if by magic.

Inside Kubrick’s slot, he could see Armand stored with its three-dimensional form flattened away.

‘Now unload it.’

When Armand appeared in front of his palm, Shirone reflexively grabbed the handle.

Finding it fun, Shirone tested the function dozens of times. After he grew somewhat used to it, he understood clearly what kind of tool Kubrick was.

Flew added an explanation.

“You can upgrade the storage weight at any fairly large alchemy shop in the city. Up to a hundred kilograms of capacity, it costs about two hundred gold per kilogram. After that, the unit price rises much higher, and once the capacity exceeds four hundred kilograms, the price enters the hundred-million range.”

“Wow, that’s amazing.”

Both the price and the weight were beyond imagination. If it could hold four hundred kilograms, then with only a slight exaggeration, it would be like walking around with all of one’s household belongings on a finger.

“That means there’s a lot of demand. Of course, upgrading it is technically difficult. The current limit for storage capacity should be around eight hundred kilograms. There probably isn’t even one in the kingdom with that much capacity.”

Shirone was simply happy. Whether it could hold eight hundred kilograms or not, he liked the ring he was wearing best.

It was a Kubrick engraved with the seal of the Magic Association.

Gando said, “No matter how convenient it is, it’s best not to put important items inside unless you’re going on a trip.”

“Why? In case it gets lost…”

Kang Nan shook her head.

“No. Because it can be stolen. Of course, Kubricks produced by the Association have a complex encryption system, so the risk of extraction is low. But anyone can cut off a finger.”

Without realizing it, Shirone covered the ring with his other hand.

“Yes. I’ll be careful.”

“There’s no need to be that attached to it. If you’re in a dangerous situation, throw it away or hand it over. A Kubrick is only an object, and you can buy another on the market.”

It was advice that only Kang Nan, the second-in-command of the Magic Association, could give.

“In any case, that’s what it is. If you want to become a full-fledged wizard, you need to know how to handle one of these.”

In Kang Nan’s last words, Shirone heard Gaold’s voice.

If he went to heaven, a Kubrick would be essential. From the important to the trivial, this was unspoken pressure telling him to be thoroughly prepared.

“Yes. I will definitely become a wizard.”

Shirone raised the hand wearing the Kubrick.

Beyond his fingers, everyone was smiling at him.
