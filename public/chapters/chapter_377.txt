Chapter 377  
Prosecutor’s Belief (1)  
~*~

Shirone arrived in Baska, the capital of Tormia.

He could not take his eyes off the scenery beyond the carriage window. The roads were neatly paved, and the people passing through the streets wore colorful clothes that seemed to proclaim their individuality.

As the carriage entered the main avenue, tall buildings rose on either side, piercing the sky. Shirone read the names of institutions he had only ever encountered in books.

The Alchemy Foundation. The Prosecutors Association. The Supreme Court.

And at their final destination stood the imposing building of the prestigious Magic Association.

Calling the eighteen-story structure a building did not do it justice. It was a tower—an enormous one. Judging by the first floor alone, several noble mansions could have fit inside.

Shirone tilted his head back to find the top, only to squeeze his eyes shut against the sunlight reflected from the glossy glass front. It was too bright to look at directly.

“Let’s go in. This is the Magic Association.”

Kang Nan paid the driver, then walked past Shirone as she spoke.

Shirone followed her to the entrance, where the guards turned toward Kang Nan and bowed at a perfect ninety degrees. They were merely gatekeepers of the Magic Association, but even they carried themselves with the discipline of elite bodyguards.

Shirone knew the truth: everyone who worked here was a wizard.

No matter how low the position, only those recognized as the best in their field could enter this sacred place of the magic world.

Seeing a revolving door for the first time, Shirone stared at it curiously, then stepped in a little too quickly. The glass panel pushed him along, and he stumbled into the hall as though he had been swept inside. At the central reception desk, attendants smiled as they guided nobles to the appropriate departments.

Kang Nan headed for a platform not far from the entrance. Capsule-shaped elevators moved constantly up and down inside glass shafts.

‘They move by air pressure. How convenient.’

Kang Nan glanced at the floor indicator, decided the elevator was not in a useful position, and turned toward the stairs instead. Shirone licked his lips in regret, but he had no choice but to follow.

The staircase was wide enough for twenty people to walk side by side. It curved upward in a spiral, with multiple landings between floors.

When they arrived on the third floor, a plain, monotonous view of corridors and office doors spread before them.

A young woman was standing beside an office marked Foreign Management Department.

She was Ravid Fleur, the third-floor manager of the Magic Association Security Department.

She was shorter than average, with blue twin tails that reached her waist. Her pale skin and red lips made her look somewhat intimidating from a distance, but up close, her youthful face was by no means unpleasant.

She held a magic wand behind her with both hands, resting it against her hips. The shaft was thin, and its tip was decorated with an ornate phoenix design, with a large crystal sphere embedded at the end.

Fleur approached Kang Nan first.

“Hello, Chief Secretary. Did the dispatch go well?”

“So-so. Where is the president of the association?”

“He left this morning. He is currently out of the capital.”

Kang Nan narrowed one eye in frustration.

“Where did he go?”

“I don’t know either. I was told he would be gone for about three days.”

“Ah, of all days…”

Kang Nan’s mission had not been given a fixed deadline, so the timing could not be helped. Still, they could not keep Shirone waiting indefinitely after bringing him all the way from the provinces.

While Kang Nan was thinking, Fleur pointed at Shirone and asked, “But this boy is…?”

“Ah, he’s the president’s guest. This has become troublesome. We have nowhere for Shirone to stay.”

“A guest?”

Fleur looked at Shirone with disbelief.

In the two years she had worked at the Association, very few people had ever visited so secretly as Gaold’s guests. Most had been the highest-ranking envoys from foreign nations or powerful politicians whose names alone made people tremble. Whenever such visitors came, the entire Association had to be cleaned, and even a dedicated chef would be brought in.

‘Could it be?’

A thought passed through Fleur’s mind.

Perhaps he was someone involved in the project. Even when Kang Nan asked where Gaold was, Fleur had sensed that she was trying to avoid drawing the attention of other departments.

‘No, that can’t be. How could such a young child be involved in the project?’

Even Fleur, who considered herself part of the Gaold Guard—a tiny faction within the Association—had only been allowed to assist at the outermost edge of this project.

Under those circumstances, there was no way a boy younger than herself could be helping Gaold directly.

‘Who is he? A relative of the president?’

In any case, Shirone’s first impression on Fleur was not particularly good.

Those who followed the eccentric Gaold all had one thing in common: they wanted to be acknowledged by him. And now, a boy had appeared as the president’s guest, something Fleur herself had never managed. She felt an irrational sense of rivalry.

Having settled on a plan, Kang Nan said to Shirone, “Shirone, why don’t you spend some time here for now? I have to go out again immediately. Fleur, can you look after Shirone until the president returns?”

“Oh, of course…”

Before Fleur could finish, Shirone turned to Kang Nan.

“Um, then can I go somewhere for a while?”

“Hm? Where?”

“I have friends who live in the capital. I’ll stay with them and come back afterward.”

Kang Nan crossed her arms and thought for a moment.

It would be best for Shirone to stay at the Association, but there was no particular harm in having him remain outside. In fact, it was a good way to keep him out of sight of the other departments.

“Fine. Go ahead, then. But keep this matter confidential.”

“Yes, don’t worry about that.”

Fleur, listening to their conversation, grew even more suspicious.

Kang Nan had a calm personality except when dealing with Gaold, but when it came to work, she was more thorough than anyone. If someone had come to meet the president of the Magic Association in secret, he could not possibly be ordinary. Yet Kang Nan was trusting a boy not even twenty years old to handle his own security and secrecy.

‘Is he royalty? What kind of person asks the Chief Secretary of the Magic Association for personal favors like this?’

Contrary to Fleur’s expectations, Kang Nan did not seem especially concerned. Anything known by more than two people was not truly a secret anyway. She had brought Shirone here according to instructions; from this point on, it was Gaold’s responsibility.

“Then come back here when your business is finished. Make sure it’s within three days. When you return to the Association, ask for Fleur.”

Kang Nan then said to Fleur, “Fleur, as soon as Shirone arrives, guide him to the president. Somewhere quiet, if possible. I’ll take care of it if I’m here, but there may be times when I’m away.”

Fleur bowed politely.

“Understood.”

Then her head lifted slightly.

‘Shirone? Then he isn’t royalty. Where have I heard that name? Where was it?’

Something tugged at the edge of her memory, but it remained vague.

Kang Nan placed a hand on Shirone’s back and turned.

“Come on, let’s go. I’ll take you outside.”

Shirone looked back and forth between Kang Nan and Fleur, then hurriedly lowered his head.

“Ah, yes. Then, goodbye.”

Fleur gave a small nod and watched Shirone with sharp eyes.

She definitely knew that name. She simply could not remember where she had heard it.

‘Well, if I look it up, I’ll find it.’

Fleur waited in the hallway until Kang Nan disappeared, then quickly turned around.

* * *

After leaving the Magic Association through the revolving door, Shirone truly felt he had come to Baska when he saw the streets overflowing with wealth and brilliance.

Kang Nan checked the time and clicked her tongue softly.

“I’m sorry, but I have other work to handle. Do you know where your friends live? Can you get there on your own?”

“Yes. I know the address. I can ask around. If I can’t find it, I’ll take a carriage.”

“Good. Then I’ll see you in three days.”

Kang Nan turned away without hesitation.

In truth, she was not worried. It made no sense to think that the kingdom’s most promising aspiring wizard would be unable to find his way around.

Kang Nan stepped onto the main road and raised her hand, and a luxurious carriage stopped at once. When she opened the door, Shirone saw a mustached gentleman and an elderly lady already seated inside. Kang Nan climbed in without hesitation, and the carriage immediately departed.

Shirone watched it go, then raised his hand when the next carriage arrived. A carriage with a blue signal light stopped.

When he opened the door and stepped in, a girl with dimples turned toward him and smiled.

“Uh… hello.”

Shirone grew flustered.

Sharing a carriage with strangers was apparently normal in Baska, but being in such a small space with someone he had never met was unfamiliar to him—especially when that someone was a girl around his age.

“Come in. Where are you headed?”

“Ah, toward the palace.”

“Good. We won’t have to take a detour.”

Still feeling self-conscious, Shirone climbed into the carriage and sat across from her. After giving the coachman the address, he turned back, only for the girl to cover her mouth and smile.

“Why are you laughing?”

“You’re not from the capital, are you?”

“Ah, no. Did it show?”

“People from Baska never look so awkward when they share a carriage. Don’t misunderstand me. I’m not saying you’re rustic or anything. If anything, you’re quite charming.”

Shirone’s face turned red, and he lowered his head. It had been the same with Marsha—he simply could not get used to this kind of thing.

The girl, on the other hand, led the conversation skillfully, as though this were part of her ordinary day.

She was twenty years old and studying for the Diplomatic Examination. She said she had two boyfriends: one younger than her, and the other a twenty-eight-year-old businessman.

Once Shirone calmed down, he listened to her story.

At one point, she subtly asked whether they might meet separately sometime. But when Shirone failed to respond to the nuance, she smiled easily and did not bring it up again.

“Then, bye. It was nice meeting you.”

“Yes. Goodbye.”

After the carriage left, Shirone remained at the roadside, looking haggard after barely thirty minutes.

It was not merely that she had talked a lot. The culture shock had been so intense that he could hardly process it. Of course, not everyone in the capital would have the same personality, but he finally understood where Dante’s free-spirited way of thinking had come from.

‘Whew, my head already hurts. The capital really is different.’

Shirone entered a luxury residential district.

Reina’s mansion stood in a place where the Leth Mountains flowed like a vast wave in the distance, and the spire of the royal castle could be seen. It was much smaller than the Ogent family’s main residence, but it was still extravagant.

“Well, Reina noona is a court musician.”

When he arrived at the main gate, the attendants who had accompanied him to Kazura Castle recognized him.

“What? Isn’t that Master Shirone? What brings you here?”

“How have you been? I came to see Lian. The swordsmanship academy should be on break now.”

“It is, but…”

The attendant trailed off suspiciously.

The attendants exchanged glances, and one of them turned away as if unsure what to do.

“Please wait a moment. I’ll be right back.”

Since Shirone was a guest of the Ogent family, it was disrespectful to leave him standing at the door. Shirone did not feel offended, but he could not shake the strange atmosphere.

Butler Lewis soon came out of the mansion. His sharp, intelligent gaze was the same as ever, but unlike before, he greeted Shirone with a warm smile.

“Welcome. This is the first time I’ve seen you since Kazura.”

“Yes. I was very grateful back then. But are you here often?”

“Since the eldest young lady works here, I come and go with the family fairly often. Please, come in.”

Shirone followed Lewis and asked, “Did something happen? The atmosphere feels strange.”

“That is… you will understand once you enter.”

Reina’s mansion was much larger on the inside than it appeared from the outside.

Two women stood in front of Klumph’s room. One was Reina, and the other was someone Shirone had not expected.

“What?”

“Shirone!”

Tess, who had looked as though she might cry at any moment, lit up and ran toward him. Then she pounded both fists against Shirone’s shoulder and shouted.

“What is this? What is this? What is this? How did you get here?”

After greeting Reina first, Shirone belatedly turned back to Tess.

“Haha! I had some business in the capital, so I stopped by to see Lian. Did you finish school safely?”

“About that…”

At this point, Shirone also began to feel anxious.

“Why are you acting like that? Did something really happen?”

Reina spoke with a serious expression.

“Lian dropped out of school.”

“What? He dropped out?”
