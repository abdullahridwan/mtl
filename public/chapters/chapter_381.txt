Chapter 381  
Swordsman’s Belief (5)  
~*~

Everyone’s eyes turned toward the empty air above them.

The black sword spun as it fell, briefly blotting out the sun, then plunged into the ground behind Rai, burying itself more than halfway to the hilt.

Rai did not look back.

His gaze remained fixed on Lian.

Lian stood frozen with his greatsword still aimed forward, unable to move even a finger. The recoil from Divine Transcendence sent terrible pain coursing through his entire body.

A long silence stretched between them.

At last, Rai clicked his tongue and turned around.

“I lost. Let’s stop.”

Lian’s eyes widened.

It was the first time in eighteen years that Rai had declared defeat.

Yet Rai’s face showed no emotion. He simply pulled the black sword from the ground, returned it to its sheath, and walked over to Clump. There, he lowered his head.

“I lost. I concede.”

“Hah. You’ve finally had your face dragged through the dirt,” Clump said. “Still, unlike you, it was a heated fight. I enjoyed that.”

Rai licked his lips and muttered, “This is why you shouldn’t spend time with fools.”

“Even so, are you all right?” Clump asked. “Don’t you feel a little relieved?”

After thinking for a moment, Rai walked past Clump and muttered quietly, “…He seems to have become a bit more like a swordsman.”

Reina hurried after him.

“Wait. Come here. Are your hands all right? You said you have to be dispatched tomorrow.”

“It’s nothing. My hand cramped, so I lost my grip on the sword.”

“Who are you trying to fool? Since when do swordsmen drop their swords just because their hands cramp? Give me your hand.”

She grabbed Rai’s wrist and examined his hand.

Just from the calluses, now so thick they seemed no different from his skin, she could tell he had not reached his current level on talent alone.

“I knew it.”

When Reina saw the back of his hand, her eyebrows drew together.

The left hand served as a shock absorber when gripping a sword. Yet the nails of Rai’s index, middle, and ring fingers were all gone.

“Don’t make a fuss,” Rai said. “Nails fall off all the time.”

“That’s the first time I’ve ever heard such nonsense! Ugh, you won’t even be able to cut a steak like this. Come on. At least let me put pressure on them.”

Reina took a bandage from the gymnasium’s first-aid kit. Then she wrapped Rai’s index, middle, and ring fingers tightly and pressed down to stop the bleeding.

As the tension of the duel finally eased, even those who had only been watching began to feel the fatigue.

Quan, however, was still staring at Lian with grave eyes.

‘Divine Transcendence…’

The swordsmanship Lian had displayed in actual combat had been astonishing. It was also the sort of performance that could never be properly evaluated in a swordsmanship school, where each technique was graded step by step.

‘But if it had been me, I would have avoided it.’

Divine Transcendence was dangerous. It was not merely a matter of increasing destructive power.

The final movement that had caught Rai had possessed a bizarre quality, one difficult to reproduce even with external gravity. Even so, Rai had answered Lian’s steel sword with his own steel sword.

Was it pride? Lack of experience? Or had there been some other variable on the battlefield that Quan himself had failed to read?

“A swordsman is still a swordsman.”

“General.”

Before Quan realized it, Clump had come to stand behind him.

“But in the end, a sword is a tool for killing,” Clump said. “That is a truth we cannot ignore. So why do we continue to pursue the sword?”

Quan could not answer.

“You can never change the world with a single sword. But the sword you carry in your heart is different. An exceptional swordsman may win a war, but a knight with conviction changes the world. Rai was burned by Lian’s conviction.”

“The sword in the heart…”

“Perhaps Divine Transcendence is the manifestation of belief.”

Quan remembered something from long ago.

He pictured himself before his Achilles tendon had been severed. Back then, his spirit had been strong enough to reject even Fengzhang’s offer and choose death instead. Now, that same spirit felt distant, like a fading delusion.

“I was once there, too.”

Clump looked at Quan with kind eyes. As both a soldier and a swordsman, Quan was a junior he could not help but feel regret over.

“How long do you plan to keep wandering? Come back to the army. My direct adjutant is being discharged this time. I think you would be a great help if you took the position.”

“I’ll think about it. Thank you for your concern.”

Quan meant the thanks sincerely, but he had no intention of returning to the army.

Today’s match had made it clear to him.

He was already a broken sword. Without finding a new sword to carry in his heart, he would never reach Divine Transcendence.

“Grandfather, can I drop out now?”

Lian strode over. His face was soaked with cold sweat from enduring the pain. Since he had proved himself through results rather than words, Clump had no excuse to stop him.

“Yes, you won. Whether you drop out or go work as a knight, do whatever you want.”

“You should have said that from the start. We fought for nothing.”

Rai, now with his fingers bandaged, spoke while changing clothes.

“Don’t become a corpse somewhere. If you die, I’ll have to go looking for you.”

Contrary to everyone’s expectations, Lian did not get angry. They had both fought with everything they had, so he had no intention of looking back on the duel with resentment.

But one question remained.

“Hey, let me ask you something.”

Rai buttoned his clothes and looked back.

“That technique that exploded the ground—was that an application of sword energy?”

“So what?”

“Why didn’t you use that sword energy on me? With your skill, you could have broken my sword and ended the fight right there.”

Rai looked at him in disbelief.

There was a limit to being shameless. Was Lian mocking him now?

But Lian’s expression held no trace of provocation.

‘Is this bastard serious?’

Rai turned to Clump.

“What happened? You didn’t tell Lian?”

“What was I supposed to do?” Clump said. “I picked him up like a stray cat. I haven’t even decided on a successor. And all that talk about him opening his Schema was pure nonsense.”

Rai snorted at the blatant lie.

“You weren’t planning to give it to me anyway, were you?”

“Ah, not that I particularly need it.”

“Tch!”

Lian cut in as the two exchanged words only they understood.

“What are you talking about? Answer me already. Why didn’t you use the sword energy?”

As though he found the entire conversation bothersome, Rai turned away and headed for the gymnasium exit.

“Don’t ask me. Ask your grandfather. What a mess. He didn’t even know what he was fighting against.”

Reina followed him.

“Are you really leaving today?”

“No, I’m going to sleep. I’m tired. Bring dinner to my room.”

At Rai’s careless attitude, Lian raised his brows and pointed at him.

“Hey! Where do you think you’re going? Why didn’t you use it? Stop embarrassing me and tell me!”

‘There’s no medicine for stupidity.’

Ignoring Lian’s shouts behind him, Rai fell into thought.

In the Ogent family, black hair and blue hair had appeared at the same time.

Why had Grandfather not given him the greatsword?

When he was young, Rai had thought Clump protected Lian simply because they shared the same blue hair. But after today’s duel, he felt he could vaguely understand the reason.

At the very least, the true owner of that sword was Lian.

* * *

Shirone and Tess decided to spend the night at Reina’s mansion.

The three of them gathered in Lian’s room and caught up on the six years they had spent apart. The biggest topic was Amy’s failure in the graduation exam.

“By the way, how is Amy?” Tess asked. “She must have been devastated.”

They had heard the news of Amy’s rejection through the Spirit Journal. The paper had covered the shocking story of the Karmis family’s genius being eliminated at the very beginning of the graduation exam.

“Yeah,” Shirone said. “She was depressed for a while, but she’s all right now. I’m sure she’ll get a different result in next year’s exam.”

“Of course. If it’s Amy, she’ll overcome it. I want to see her. It was really fun back then.”

Lian also remembered what had happened in heaven. Realizing Divine Transcendence while fighting Ymir had been an important turning point in his life.

“Amy is more than qualified to be a wizard,” Lian said. “Tell her I’m rooting for her. For the next graduation exam, I’ll go in person and give her plenty of energy.”

A year later, Shirone would also be taking the graduation exam, so Lian had no intention of missing it.

“I envy you,” Tess said. “I can’t go. Swordsmanship school ends later than magic school. Should I just quit too? Then I can train with Lian.”

Lian waved his hand.

“What would be the point of a cadet competing for first place at a swordsmanship academy dropping out? You should graduate proudly.”

“But without you there, it feels so empty. I really want to follow you.”

Tess looked as though she might cry at any moment, so Lian hurriedly searched for something to change the subject. His eyes landed on the sword Shirone had propped beside the desk.

“By the way, Shirone, are you carrying a sword now?”

“Oh, that? I received it as a gift.”

Shirone told them what had happened in Kazura. Since Lian and Tess had also experienced going to heaven, it was easy for them to understand what kind of object it was.

Seeing their interest, Shirone demonstrated it himself.

First, he performed a feat with Rated Control, then activated Geumgang Armament. As the sword enveloped Shirone, an organic robe wrapped around him in an instant.

Even the bold Lian could not keep his mouth closed at the sight of that eerie yet powerful form. Tess pointed at the object floating in the air and asked, “By the way, what is that marble?”

“It’s a magic power calculator. Since it takes over the technique of time-division, calculations become much faster. And the crystal in the gauntlet is a magic amplifying device. By using both, I can expand the range of my magic operations.”

“I see. That’s fascinating. I didn’t know there were objects with such complex functions.”

Armand was a weapon that could tempt any swordsman. But Lian and Tess showed nothing beyond simple curiosity. For those who carried the sword of conviction in their hearts, no object could ever be more than a tool.

‘It would be nice if Lian could handle Armand.’

Training as a knight was on a completely different level from classes at a swordsmanship school.

The world would not present Lian with neatly arranged levels of difficulty. He could defeat any number of third-rate gangsters or robbers, but among criminals, there were always people skilled enough to trouble even a kingdom.

In that sense, Armand, which strengthened the user’s physical functions and provided self-defense, would certainly be helpful.

However, unlike with Dante, the demonic sword showed no response to Lian.

Armand could provide an optimal combat environment for any user, but achieving one hundred percent efficiency required a high level of tactical understanding and complex system control.

Given Lian’s straightforward and chaotic nature, it would be difficult for him to handle Armand’s functions perfectly. Armand seemed to understand that fact as well.

‘For a sword, it really does have a sharp eye.’

But Lian had never dreamed of relying on anything in the first place. After his fight with Rai, his confidence had soared.

“No need to worry!” Lian said. “I’m only just getting started! From today on, I’ll achieve an undefeated record!”

Seeing Lian proudly beat his chest, Shirone smiled warmly.

Lian was fire.

One day, he would return as a great blaze capable of burning the world.

That night, Lian curled up beneath his blankets and moaned.

The aftermath of Divine Transcendence had not ended with the first wave. Rather, the most excruciating pain came at night, when the body began recovering its functions.

“Hngh…! Hoo…!”

A groan escaped his throat, but he bit down on his pillow and forced himself to endure.

He could not let Shirone, who was sleeping beside him, notice his pain. More than anything, the fear that he would never be able to endure it again if he let his will falter weighed heavily on him.

His face was drenched in cold sweat, and his eyes could not focus. Even so, Lian glared fiercely into the darkness and endured the pain.

‘Thank goodness. I’m really glad I won.’

If he had lost to Rai in front of Shirone, he would have fallen into the abyss and been unable to do anything for a while.

Rai was clearly strong. In terms of technical perfection alone, he could be called a genius.

But he was by no means an impassable wall.

‘The gap has narrowed. It’s definitely narrowed.’

If they fought again, Lian could not expect the same result. Still, there had certainly been aspects where he surpassed Rai. That much, at least, he had accomplished with his own strength.

‘I can do it. I didn’t choose the wrong path.’

Simply knowing that the distance was not unreachable made Lian happy. Even as his body trembled with pain, laughter leaked from his throat.

“Hehehe… hehehehe.”

A night of pain and joy passed slowly by.
