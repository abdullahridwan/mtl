Task Force (2)  
~*~

If you travel by carriage for about two hours from the city of Creas, you reach the small port town of San Ferros.

Galliant is a world-class resort, but outside the holiday season, this area was little more than a secluded fishing village.

Arriving around lunchtime, Shirone bought a handful of fish fillets from a street stall.

The fish pho, said to be at its best when slightly spoiled, was chewy like jelly and carried the deep, briny taste of the sea.

There was no liner available, so he figured he would have to hire a captain.

The fare was expensive, but the money he had saved so far wouldn't be enough.

When he asked the villagers, they said the boats that left early in the morning would return with their catch in about an hour.

With nowhere else to go, Shirone walked to the breakwater and gazed out at the distant sea.

It wasn’t his first trip, and his teammates were waiting for him on the island, yet he felt utterly abandoned in the world.

‘School must be at lunchtime by now.’

Shirone didn’t dwell on it.

There was nowhere to go back to.

“Are you Arian Shirone?”

A woman in attire completely unlike that of the Tormia Kingdom stood behind him.

Even in the warm weather, her sleeves reached her wrists, and the hood draped down her back was thicker than winter garments.

Her appearance was striking, but her eyes were deep and mysterious, creating an aura that made it impossible to look at her with anything but respect.

Shirone turned his attention to the strange creature floating above the woman’s head.

It was a soft, transparent monster with a sphere inside it rotating and generating an electric field.

An autoprism that scattered light to create a transparent effect within a specific range.

Although it had almost no combat power, it was a monster listed in the fourth tier of summoning magic due to its great utility.

Shirone gave a wary look.

In the worst-case scenario, he would draw Armand and fight, but if she was a wizard capable of summoning a Tier 4 monster, he would have to acknowledge her superior ability.

“Who are you? How did you get my name…?”

“My name is Zulu. Gaold told me to bring you.”

“Zulu? Everything?”

For a moment, bewildered by the bizarre phrasing, Shirone opened his eyes wide and looked directly at the woman’s face.

“Zulu?”

There was no way he didn’t know the name of the world’s only summoning wizard who could match a Tier 1 lich.

In disbelief, Shirone pointed at her and checked again.

“…Zulu?”

“Yes, Zulu.”

Zulu nodded nonchalantly.

“I don’t have much time. I need to cross the border before dark, so follow me.”

“Where is the president of the association?”

“They have already arrived at Galliant and are preparing for the Gangran. Are you going or not?”

“I’ll go.”

When he gave up his exhausting search and approached Zulu, she disarmed the autoprism and cast a mass teleport.

The landscape seemed to be pulled into a single point, then expanded into a new vista.

They were halfway up the mountain behind the port.

The breakwater where they had been was now a small speck at the far end of the field of vision, and at the bottom of the mountain lay the fishing village.

Zulu summoned the grotesque Kaidra from there.

Its smooth, hairless skin featured a long snout like an awl, and its huge claws, which supported its massive body, seemed capable of holding up a mountain.

Contrary to its hideous appearance, the Kaidra rubbed its beak against Zulu like a child, then knelt and let one wing hang down.

Shirone climbed onto the wing, following Zulu.

The Kaidra’s backbone protruded from the skin like a shark’s fin, blocking air resistance and creating a hollow for riders to sit in, which provided a good sense of stability.

“Be careful. I will fly high.”

The Kaidra flapped its wings and pushed off the ground with its two legs, shooting straight up as soon as it gained altitude.

Shirone, his face pale from the sudden ascent, stared at the fast-approaching sky.

When they reached a normal cruising altitude, the ground was merely a distant dot.

“A lift-based rogue named Kaidra. They don’t need as much oxygen, so they can fly at higher altitudes than strength-based creatures. In this way, the probability of avoiding the search system Joner’s algorithm increases.”

Searching the sky was not too difficult for zoners who possessed a spirit zone with a basic diameter of two kilometers.

However, as the altitude increased, the shape of the spirit zone had to be transformed into a target type or sniper mode, so it was natural that the search range would decrease.

Shirone finally got a sense of reality.

The person in front of him was really Zulu.

“Thank you for coming to pick me up. To be honest, I never thought that such a great person would come to greet me.”

“Gaold said the Association would move sooner or later. It looks like there is stillness inside. I fixed it as soon as possible and took the lead to heaven. So I was in charge of the transport. Right now, I am on my way to pick up a woman named Shiina.”

“Oh, I see.”

“Of course, I would have come even if the matter was not urgent. Sein said he would use anything that could be used to produce results. I’ll be in charge of utilities on the team, so you can tell me anything you need for the mission.”

Zulu’s words made sense.

From now on, they needed to move organically as a team. Regardless of seniority, they must do their best in their respective duties.

“Yes! I wish you well!”

Shirone bowed his head courageously.

* * *

Kentra city.

The meat produced in Kentra, Tormia’s famous pastoral area, was designated as a royal tribute because of its high quality.

The catering business was also thriving, and an entire street was filled with restaurants.

People wearing cowboy hats often passed by, leather clothes were popular, and whips sold like hot cakes.

However, the reason Shiina decided to meet here wasn’t because the meat was delicious or to buy Kentra specialties.

It was because this was exactly the halfway point when traveling from Creas and Baska.

Shiina checked the restaurant sign and opened the door.

Compared to Creas, the atmosphere was more conventional, and although reservations had been made, the seats were sparsely occupied.

After emptying the glass of water the waiter poured out, she anxiously waited for the person she would meet today.

There were about twenty minutes left until the appointment time, but it was still unsure if he would show up.

No, if he were sane, there was no way he would come out.

After seeing the match, he hadn’t contacted her once. She had only received a certified 5th grade congratulatory letter, so she had made a lunch appointment.

That too via rapid telegram.

There was no one who received a telegram at dawn, prepared it in a hurry, and went all the way to Kentra City to eat meat.

‘Because it’s worth the bottom line.’

The door opened with a bell, and a man entered.

Parka Quan, an instructor at the Kaizen Swordsmanship School.

The cold eyes that scanned the atmosphere in the hall were still the same, but his outfit was different from their last meeting, and he seemed to be quite careful.

Quan’s icy stare only softened a little when he checked Shiina sitting at the table.

As he moved his body with a limp, all eyes of the nobles focused on him.

He never tried to hide the fact that his legs were uncomfortable, but he always felt like a loser in the crowd.

Especially when people’s eyes shifted from Quan to Shiina, he felt the urge to cut down everyone in this place.

Shiina stood up and raised her hand.

As per his personality, he couldn’t even give a friendly smile, but he had an attitude that didn’t care about people’s gazes.

There was also Armin, who had sacrificed both eyes for her.

The fact that Quan’s legs were uncomfortable didn’t bother Shiina at all.

Being conscious of prejudice was not something that could be hidden, so Quan could feel Shiina’s sincerity.

Maybe that was why he didn’t even know the topic and didn’t know if he had come this far.

“You came. Sorry for the rude request.”

“As this is an urgent telegram, I thought there must be a reason. For now, please sit down.”

The food was served, but the two were still silent. They were just concentrating on cutting the meat and putting it in their mouths.

Whoever it was, there was no immunity in this position.

Still, Quan, a man, mustered up the courage to speak to her for the first time.

“Congratulations. Certified 5th class wizard. You are really cool at such a young age.”

Shiina couldn’t put it off any longer.

She didn’t have much time, and if Quan refused, she had to leave immediately for Galliant.

“I sent you a telegram because I have a request for you.”

It was a strange thing to say in return for congratulations, so Quan blinked while holding the tableware.

Then he nodded his head and said, “Yes, you say.”

Shiina explained the project.

Information that could put the team in danger was blocked, but the world’s notice would end tomorrow, so the content was core and nothing harsh.

“…So, I wanted to have at least one hitman in the team. I think Mr. Quan is the right person.”

Quan, who had remained motionless until the end of the story, dropped the tableware onto the plate with a rattling noise. He gulped down the water as if he had lost his appetite.

Shiina calmly waited.

It was no different from saying that he was going to die. It would leave a stigma in history.

Persuasion was impossible anyway.

From now on, it was entirely up to him to decide.

Quan turned his gaze to the dining room floor, lost in thought. Then he looked back at Shiina and said, “Can I just ask you one question?”

“Anything. Any information that can be revealed.”

“Why me?”

Shiina’s eyes fell coldly.

“As you can see, my leg is not intact. And for me, the legs are an important test. Why does it have to be me?”

“That’s Quan-san’s skills… … .”

Shiina swallowed the compliment in her throat.

There was no way he could not know how Quan had come to this place. Because no man would drive all the way to lunch with a woman he didn’t even like.

Armin had sent him off with his heart, but he thought that he shouldn’t use Quan’s heart.

He just wanted to reveal the truth and wait for her decision.

“Quan-san, it doesn’t matter when you die.”

Shiina said with a determined expression.

“I heard that there are no parents, no siblings, no connections. Even if Mr. Quan dies, there is no force that can move. I thought there was no need to deal with it even if things went wrong.”

Quan looked at Shiina with blank eyes.

No matter how bluntly she said it to his face.

Somehow, from the moment he received the urgent telegram, he had thought things were going well.

“But never underestimate your skills. Quan-san’s swordsmanship… … .”

“Kuk-kuk. Heh heh heh!”

Quan lowered his head, shook his shoulders and smiled, and Shiina stopped talking with a nervous expression.

“Even when I die, I don’t have to take care of it? If you really thought so… … .”

Quan opened his eyes and bit his lips.

“You’ve come to the right place.”

Now he understood. Why was this woman.

She showed neither pity nor prejudice.

To him, who was fed up with the pretentious life of a human being, she offered a stark, unvarnished truth.

Quan withdrew his earlier thoughts.

Still, it seemed that God was still watching over her life.

It was heaven.

Could there be a more suitable place for a sword demon’s tomb?

Quan rose from his seat.

“Let’s go. It seems urgent. There is nothing to do other than prepare. The personal affairs were already done 10 years ago.”

“Thank you for agreeing, but at least a message… … .”

“Do not worry. It’s a decision I made as a single man. It will be too late to prepare yourself at the moment of death. There is no need to look back on the way to the battlefield.”

It was a bit of an extreme resolution, but it was hard to find a more suitable person for the role of a hitman.

Regardless of their motive, a suicide commando who takes on the future of mankind and plunges into the fire.

Shiina sincerely respected his resolve.

“Thank you.”

Quan, as a swordsman, also lowered his head as if swearing by a sword.

“I accept.”
