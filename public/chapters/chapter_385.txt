Chapter 385
Magic Association (3)
~*~
*I don’t think this situation is as rare as I expected. What should I do? Should I go?*

Terrorism against civilians was a serious crime, and the kingdom would respond immediately. Even if Shirone arrived at the scene, there was little he could do, but he couldn’t bring himself to ignore it and simply return to the Association.

Crowds of people fleeing for the second time rushed past him on the side of the main road. The shouts of the passing crowd gave Shirone a clearer picture of what was happening.

“It’s the Black Revolution! They’re holding hostages and barricading themselves in the Gold Tower!”

“Damn. They’re just crazy people who don’t care about anything.”

“What’s the anti-terrorism unit doing? Haven’t they been contacted yet?”

The noise was overwhelming, yet no one was heading toward the Gold Tower. It was only natural. Ordinary people had no chance against terrorists, so it was better to leave it to the professionals.

*What should I do?*

A magic academy student wasn’t an expert, but he wasn’t so unprofessional that he could just stand by and do nothing while people died.

Voices drifted from the crowd gathered on the sidewalk, watching the smoke rise in the distance.

“Serious business. This time, they’re probably trying to get their comrades released. If they aren’t subdued quickly, they’ll kill all the hostages. Three people have already died.”

Shirone’s heart sank.

*I’ll have to go and assess the situation.*

He could protect himself, so it was only right to investigate. As someone already near the scene, he was in a position to respond quickly. Even as a student, he had enough combat experience to handle himself.

Once he made up his mind, Shirone ran toward the rising smoke. Citizens on the sidewalk shouted as they saw him running against the flow of traffic.

“Hey, kid! Don’t go there! It’s a terror zone!”

Shirone didn’t stop. He ignored their warnings. The case he was carrying rattled against his hip, slowing him down. He could have thrown it away to run faster, but he refused to abandon the Association’s equipment, even for a simple reconnaissance mission.

“Not my problem!”

With his decision made, Shirone cast a teleportation spell. A flash of light erupted, and he shot through the air. All the bystanders looked up, their eyes wide with shock.

“What? What was that? Is he a wizard?”

* * *

The terrorist incident quickly drew the attention of the kingdom’s key authorities. The Magic Association was no exception. When news spread that the Black Revolutionary Corps had blown up the Gold Tower, the Association mobilized immediately. An official letter of cooperation arrived from the Kingdom Security Office, and the Association promptly replied that they would provide all possible support.

However, the information did not reach the certified 8th-level wizard directly. Flew was still in the engine room on the third floor, overseeing the maintenance of the horsepower control unit. She noticed the unusual commotion outside.

“What’s going on? You’re all slacking off.”

When she opened the engine room door and stepped into the corridor, the guards had stopped their work and huddled in a corner. Puzzled, Flew tilted her head and walked toward them.

“Why are you all gathered here? Where did the incident happen?”

“Ah, Boss. They’re saying the Black Revolutionary Corps launched a terrorist attack in the city.”

Flew’s expression darkened at the mention of the Black Revolutionary Corps.

Alongside ordinary criminals, numerous sub-species defeated during the war of conquest lived in Radum, the shadow of Basuka. However, due to capital city policies, economic activities for non-human races were strictly prohibited. This constant suppression bred deep resentment, which eventually festered into radical terrorist groups like the Black Revolution.

Their demands had always been the same: the release of their imprisoned comrades, including Vice-Captain Kanimar, who were held in the castle’s basement.

Naturally, the kingdom refused to comply. The more the Black Revolution resorted to increasingly radical and bold terrorist activities, the more the capital was thrown into chaos.

“Filth. What are they thinking, targeting civilians?”

Flew felt a surge of anger, wanting to rush to the scene immediately and pay a muk bowl. But she restrained herself. If she started abusing her authority, it would only cause trouble for the Association.

Calmly, she asked again, “So, where are they heading this time?”

“Ah, the Gold Tower. You guessed right.”

“Oh my. Did they breach the Gold Tower?”

The Gold Tower served as a foreign exchange bank where currencies from other nations were traded. While the kingdom’s treasury held a certain amount of foreign currency, any temporary paralysis of transactions could trigger severe trade losses.

*That’s why security inspections are so strict. How the hell did they get in?*

Flew’s shoulders tensed.

“Wait. The Gold Tower?”

“Yes. Why? Do you have a deposit there…?”

Flew’s hair seemed to stand on end.

The Gold Tower was only one block away from the Achim Alchemy Company. If that was true, there was no way Shirone hadn’t noticed the incident.

*Let’s see… what time is it? In terms of travel time…*

Even accounting for variables, it was definitely time for him to be on site.

Her heart began to pound. The thought that terrified her most was that her guest, who was supposed to meet Old, might be caught in the crossfire.

“Ah! Seriously!”

Flew stopped thinking and sprinted down the hallway. A guard passing by turned, his expression surprised.

“Boss, where are you going?”

“I’m off duty! I’ll be right back!”

Flew bounded down the stairs. When she emerged through the main gate, citizens were staring toward a single point with anxious expressions. Noticing the hazy smoke rising in the distance, Flew immediately cast a photonization spell. Her body transformed into light, and she shot across the firmament.

* * *

“Stay calm! I’ll kill anyone who talks nonsense!”

Eight members of the Black Revolutionary Corps had blown up the Gold Tower. All wore black hoods and carried grotesque weapons. Armed with chained scythes, twin axes, flails, and long swords, they herded the twenty hostages around the building.

“You are victims of the revolution from this moment on. We must be willing to give our lives to free our comrades! Those who refuse will suffer pains far worse than death!”

“Waaah! Mom, I’m scared!”

A four-year-old boy burst into tears in his mother’s arms. His cries cut through the tense silence, louder than the distant sirens.

“Shh! Quiet. You can’t cry.”

The mother tried to soothe him with a low, steady voice, but the boy’s sobbing only intensified.

Terrorists didn’t operate with a sane mind. The woman was irritated that even a child’s crying was disrupting the already volatile situation.

One of the Black Revolutionary Corps approached the mother and child and shouted, “Don’t stop crying! If you keep crying, I’ll kill you first!”

“Aaaagh! Mommy!”

The mother clutched her child tightly and bowed her head. “I’m sorry! I’m sorry! He’ll be quiet soon. It’ll be over soon.”

The terrorist showed no mercy. All the hostages gathered here were destined to die bravely to further the revolution.

“Shut up! Does the revolution amuse you?”

The terrorist raised his foot and kicked the child. The boy was thrown several meters to the side and crashed to the ground.

Gasping for air, he lay there unable to breathe. A woman rushed over, quickly scooped him up, and held him close.

“Why are you hitting the kid! He’s just scared, let him cry!”

A member of Jungki, wielding a scythe and spinning the chain, approached them.

The woman’s blonde hair was pulled back, though strands fell across her face. Her outfit and accessories were elegant and high-class.

Her name was Aria. She was the same woman who had shared a carriage with Shirone a few days prior. She had stopped by to exchange currency before leaving for diplomatic practice the following week, but she had been caught by the Black Revolutionary Corps.

“Shut up. Nothing is more important than accomplishing the cause of the revolution.”

“Huh! Revolution? If that’s so, don’t you need money? You can just take all the money here!”

“Money?”

Daewon shrugged and laughed in a muffled voice.

To the Black Revolutionary Corps, money was worthless—nothing more than toilet paper. Even if they had it, they couldn’t conduct any economic activity in Basuka.

The kingdom had failed to eradicate the terrorists that naturally bred in the capital’s gutters. Instead, they focused on crushing their power through economic pressure. Ironically, this only strengthened the terrorists’ resolve.

Being free from money was often romanticized as purity. The Black Revolutionary Corps branded the kingdom as a social evil and used that narrative to recruit more sub-species under a banner of sacred belief.

“Kyaaak!”

The terrorist grabbed Aria, wrenched her arms behind her back, and pressed his scythe against her throat.

The sharp blade glinted eerily in front of her eyes.

“Queue, is it because she’s the daughter of a rich family? Does she smell so sweet? How does it feel? Since you’ve been eating well and growing up well, shall we make an example of you in front of everyone?”

A thick, bluish tongue slithered out from the darkness of the hood. It was twice the length of a human tongue, and its saliva reeked of a foul stench. Aria frowned and turned her head away.

“I don’t have time to play around. The main squad will arrive soon. Let’s start with a demonstration.”

One of the two men stationed at the Achim Alchemy Chamber window called out from inside. Daewon, who had no particular interest in human women, nodded and followed the order.

“Cheuk, then let’s make this woman our first target. The psychological impact will be perfect. Too bad for you, miss. But she’ll live happily ever after in the afterlife.”

The Black Revolutionary Corps would do anything to fulfill their demands. Their sole goal was the release of their comrades, and killing a hostage as a warning was a fear tactic they relied on heavily.

“Help me! Help me!”

Aria realized her fate and struggled desperately.

But none of the hostages moved to help her. The mother with the crying child simply held her son tight and squeezed her eyes shut.

The terrorist with the scythe grabbed Aria and dragged her out through the shattered door of the Gold Tower.

The streets, evacuated by all the citizens, were completely desolate. Only nearby merchants watched the scene from behind their shop windows.

“Listen carefully! We are the Black Revolution! Release Vice-Captain Kanimar of the Black Revolution right now, and free your imprisoned comrades! Otherwise, I will kill one person every ten minutes! To prove our resolve is not empty, I will kill this woman right here and now!”

Aria’s face went pale as a corpse.

“Kyaaak! Help me! Please, help me!”

The watching merchants turned their heads one by one, refusing to meet her eyes.

The Black Revolutionary Corps were capable of anything. Unfortunately for the poor girl, today would be her last day.

“Don’t move. If you want to hold a grudge, resent the kingdom.”

The scythe’s edge pressed against Aria’s nape.

She didn’t intend to die easily. If only half her throat was severed, she would slowly bleed out in agony.

“Die for the Revolution!”

Daewon’s voice was so sincere it convinced her that her life was truly ending.

Her legs gave out before she could even scream. The paralyzing fear of death stripped away her reason, and her body convulsed uncontrollably.

“Wait! Just a moment!”

Just as the scythe was about to slit Aria’s throat, a voice rang out from the deserted street.

“What?”

The terrorists inside the Gold Tower peered through the window to find the source of the voice. A boy under twenty stood there, holding a metal box and catching his breath.

“Who are you?”
