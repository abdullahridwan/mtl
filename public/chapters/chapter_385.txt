‘This doesn’t seem like a minor incident. What should I do? Should I go?’

Terrorism against civilians was a serious crime, so the kingdom would respond at once. Even if Shirone went to the scene, there might be nothing he could do. Still, the thought of ignoring it and returning to the Association left a bitter taste in his mouth.

Another wave of people came rushing in from the main road, fleeing the area. From the shouts that passed between them, Shirone was able to grasp a little more of the situation.

“It’s the Black Revolution! They’ve taken hostages and occupied the Gold Tower!”

“Damn it. Of all people, it had to be those lunatics.”

“What is the anti-terrorism unit doing? Haven’t they been contacted yet?”

Voices rose from every direction, but no one headed toward the Gold Tower.

It was only natural. There was nothing ordinary citizens could do against terrorists. The best thing they could do was leave it to the experts.

‘What should I do?’

A student from a magic school was not an expert. But Shirone was not so helpless that he could simply stand by while people died.

Among the people standing on the sidewalk and staring at the smoke, someone muttered grimly, “Those bastards are serious. They’re probably doing this to get their comrades released again. If they aren’t subdued quickly, they’ll kill all the hostages. Three people died last time.”

Shirone’s heart gradually tipped in one direction.

‘I should at least go and assess the situation.’

He could protect himself. Since he was already near the scene, he might be able to respond faster than others. And although he was still a student, he had experienced enough real combat to be considered a capable fighter in his own right.

Having made up his mind, Shirone ran toward the rising smoke. The people on the sidewalk, realizing that he was going the wrong way, shouted after him.

“Hey, kid! Don’t go there! That’s a terrorist zone!”

Shirone stopped. But it was not because he had listened to their warning.

The metal box he was carrying rattled loudly, making it difficult to run. He could throw it away and go, but he could not simply abandon Association property when he was only going to check the situation.

“Ah, forget it!”

Shirone made up his mind and cast Teleportation. His body turned into a flash of light and shot through the air, causing everyone who had been watching him to look up in shock.

“What the—? Is he a mage?”

* * *

News of the terrorist incident quickly reached the kingdom’s key authorities.

The Magic Association was no exception. When word spread that the Black Revolutionary Corps had blown up the Gold Tower, the Association moved quickly as well.

An official request for cooperation arrived from the Kingdom Security Office, and the Association sent a reply stating that it would provide all possible support.

However, the information had not yet been delivered directly to the certified Level 8 mage. Flew was still in the engine room on the third floor, observing maintenance on the magic power control unit. But the noise outside was beginning to bother her.

“What’s going on? I only stepped away for a moment.”

When she opened the engine room door and stepped into the hallway, she saw the guards had stopped working and gathered in one corner. More puzzled than before, Flew tilted her head and walked toward them.

“What are you all doing? Did an accident happen somewhere?”

“Ah, boss. They say the Black Revolutionary Corps launched a terrorist attack in the city.”

Flew’s expression twisted at the words Black Revolutionary Corps.

In Radum, the shadow of Basuka, countless criminals lived alongside the many sub-species who had been defeated in the war of conquest. Due to the capital’s policies, non-human races were prohibited from engaging in economic activity. Dissatisfaction had always simmered beneath the surface, and once that resentment accumulated, radical terrorist organizations like the Black Revolutionary Corps were born.

Their demands were always the same: the release of their comrades imprisoned beneath the royal castle, including the Black Revolution’s vice-captain, Kanimar.

Of course, the kingdom could not accept those demands. And the more the kingdom refused, the more radical and daring the Black Revolution’s terrorist acts became, throwing the capital into chaos again and again.

“Filthy bastards. What are they thinking, targeting civilians?”

Flew wanted to run to the scene immediately and teach them a lesson. But if certified mages abandoned their posts and exercised their authority whenever they pleased, eventually no one would remain to run the Association.

After forcing herself to calm down, Flew asked again, “So where did they strike this time?”

“Ah, the Gold Tower. They really picked their target.”

“Oh my. They broke into the Gold Tower?”

The Gold Tower was a foreign exchange bank where the currencies of other nations were traded. Naturally, the kingdom’s treasury held a certain amount of foreign currency, but if transactions were paralyzed even temporarily, it could lead to serious losses in trade.

‘That’s why inspections and searches there are so strict. How in the world did they get in?’

Then Flew’s shoulders twitched.

“Wait. The Gold Tower?”

“Yes. Why? Do you have money deposited there…?”

Flew grabbed at her hair in frustration.

The Gold Tower was only one block away from the Achim Alchemy Company. If that was the case, there was no way Shirone had not noticed the incident.

‘Wait. What time is it? Considering the time…’

Even accounting for variables, Shirone should definitely have been in the area.

Her heart began to pound. What frightened her most was the possibility that the guest she was supposed to bring to Gaold had been caught up in an accident.

“Ah! Seriously!”

Flew stopped thinking and ran down the hallway. A guard walking toward her looked startled and asked, “Boss, where are you going?”

“I’m stepping out! I’ll be right back!”

Flew bounded down the stairs.

When she came out through the main gate, the citizens were all staring anxiously in one direction. Spotting the hazy smoke rising in the distance, Flew immediately cast a photonization spell. Her body transformed into light and roared across the sky.

* * *

“Stay calm! Anyone who runs their mouth dies!”

There were eight members of the Black Revolutionary Corps who had blown up the Gold Tower. All of them wore black hoods and carried weapons hideous to behold.

Armed with chained scythes, twin axes, flails, and long swords, they moved through the Gold Tower while twenty hostages trembled like aspen leaves.

“From this moment on, you are victims of the revolution. You must willingly give your lives to free our comrades! Anyone who refuses will suffer pain more terrible than death!”
