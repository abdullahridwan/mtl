Shirone was too out of breath to answer right away. He had been watching from a distance, but the moment the situation turned urgent, he had sprinted with all his strength.

“What are you going to do after you kill someone?” he shouted. “Even if I don’t know what’s going on, this can be solved through conversation!”

“What? Kill someone?” the terrorist snapped. “How dare you reduce the cause of the revolution to murder!”

Arya recognized Shirone’s face. He was the boy she had taken an interest in during the wagon ride a few days earlier. But there was no time to dwell on that now.

With tears and snot running down her face, she clung to the only hope she had.

“Help me! Please save me!”

“Shut up! The revolution has already begun!”

The terrorist pressed the scythe against Arya’s neck. Desperate, Shirone held out the iron box strapped at his side and shouted the first idea that came to mind.

“Wait! Take me hostage instead!”

The blade stopped just before it pierced Arya’s throat.

The terrorist froze. He had trained for three years to fulfill the cause, but this was a situation he had never imagined. Who would offer up his own life for a stranger?

“Take you hostage?”
