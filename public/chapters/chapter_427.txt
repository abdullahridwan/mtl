The Rapidly Changing Situation (1)  
~*~

The shouts of the medical personnel echoed through the air, but the students barely heard them. Eider’s miserable state on the stretcher spoke volumes.

As the first student to challenge Shirone, his condition was a stark warning. Fear began to take root in the hearts of those who measured Shirone against Fermi.

Eider’s departure from the front line meant the Screamer team was disqualified for capturing the hill, yet Shirone’s expression remained grim.

No matter how justified the outcome, people naturally lean toward compassion. I want to believe that.

However, Eider had activated the AT algorithm at the last possible moment.

It wasn’t a dramatic lightning strike or a divine intervention that saved him. It was a calculated tactical override.

Are humans truly infinitely free? Or is this, too, just another variable within a larger system beyond our cognition?

“Victory for the Shirone team. Twenty points awarded. This concludes the evaluation for the hill occupation.”

Shirone walked over to his friends and offered a quiet apology, gently placing a trembling Maya onto his shoulder.

“Sorry. For putting you all through such a brutal fight.”

“I’m okay. I’m more worried about you than that.”

Shirone was determined and cool-headed enough to completely sever Eider’s standing in his senior year. But this wasn’t born from emotional strength; it was the cold manifestation of insight, taking the shortest path to the objective.

‘It must be difficult for you, Shirone…’

The truth was, he didn’t want to fight anyone. All trouble stemmed from that reluctance.

What was an extremely straightforward choice for others became the most complicated problem in the world for Shirone.

Eider was rushed to emergency treatment outside the battlefield.

When the medical officer checking for a heartbeat shouted, a medic beside him immediately sprinted off.

The Electric Monster Licorn, who had witnessed everything, turned to Fermi and spoke.

“Eider succeeded. I got a clear read on Shirone’s capabilities.”

Fermi shook his head.

“If he were just a guy who moved on pure emotion, he wouldn’t even be worth playing against. Though our dispositions are completely different, Shirone is cut from the same cloth as me.”

“Rather, it’s a case where emotion actually sharpened his coolness. If we hadn’t stepped on Eider, a second and third Eider would have appeared later. I calculated it was better to crush the bud in advance.”

“Anyway, the strategy worked. I’ve seen all of Shirone’s techniques. From now on, we just need to gather the magic that can win the battle and counterattack. Wasn’t that the plan from the start?”

“Of course, but…”

Fermi rested his chin, lost in thought.

With a total of twenty points up for grabs, everyone would push their limits. He had assumed Shirone wanted to conserve at least the laser guidance algorithm.

In that respect, Eider had met the trading conditions exactly. So why did this lingering sense of incongruity haunt him?

“I get it.”

Licorn looked back at Fermi.

“You were hiding it? No, he didn’t hide anything from the start. Optical wing, shotgun movement, laser guidance algorithm. Even without Eider, I would have played that card the moment it was necessary. So why did I misjudge him? It’s because Shirone’s growth rate is far faster than anyone else’s.”

He already possessed three skills he hadn’t been able to use before entering his senior year.

Even if Fermi knew everything about Shirone now, it was impossible to predict how much he would evolve by the graduation exam.

‘In just three months, he’s acquired two intermediate and one advanced spell. At this rate, his growth matches the accelerated training programs that drain my budget.’

Fermi spoke to his colleagues without looking back.

“It’s a special sale.”

“Yes?”

“Tell all the suppliers we’re giving them a twenty percent discount. And from now on, I only invest in advanced magic. Buy whatever comes your way before the graduation exams.”

Hercy approached with a tearful expression.

“But Fermi, then this year’s revenue…”

“What did I say?”

Hercy’s face, pierced by Fermi’s gaze, turned pale. Fermi, the perfect manager, was just as terrifying as Fermi the strategist.

“If you don’t eliminate minor variables, they become unmanageable ones when you encounter them.”

Hercy spat out a clear acknowledgment, and Fermi turned away coldly.

“Go.”

“Ah, okay.”

When Fermi left the hall, the remaining four followed in his wake.

The area around the battlefield was still chaotic. Several students were desperately trying to restrain the Screamer.

They hated meddling in other people’s affairs, but if they left it like this, Eider might actually die.

“Let go! Son of a bitch, I’m going to kill you! Let go of this!”

“Screamer! Calm down! Eider is seriously injured! If you hit him, you’ll really kill him!”

There would be no place to beat a patient with whole-body fractures, but if the Screamer kept going, the outcome was unpredictable.

“I’ll beat you to death! A cheeky bastard dares to overturn the board I designed?”

The team had been disqualified due to a strategy they hadn’t agreed upon. On top of that, since he had colluded with Fermi, he couldn’t help but feel sensitive to any perceived betrayal of back-alley loyalty.

“Aww! It’s so annoying!”

The Screamer kicked the ground and threw dirt over Eider. The medics, already overwhelmed, glared at him as if they wanted to kill him and pointed a finger.

“You! Stay more than ten meters away from the patient! Otherwise, I will report you to the instructor!”

“Cadet! Shit!”

Having regained his reason, the Screamer shook off the hands of the surrounding students and turned around. There was nothing he could do right now, but once Eider recovered from his injuries, he would have to prepare for half a lifetime of regret.

The Screamer walked over to the drinking fountain, popped the lid off a water bottle, and downed it in one gulp. Then, as Shirone and the others approached, he pointed at the bottle and said,

“Don’t get proud just because you won. If it weren’t for Eider, I wouldn’t have known the outcome.”

Nade snorted.

“You’re laughing? Was it even a game we caught anyway? And would you have kept your promise? I want to apologize to Maya.”

The Screamer ground his teeth. He had to apologize for his words, but he couldn’t help himself.

There were unfortunate aspects to the situation. How could he possibly win a game against a rare idiot like Eider?

“Sheesh! What is an apology? Hey, drink some water.”

The Screamer handed over a fresh water bottle as if to end the conversation there. However, the reaction he received was unexpected.

As Maya, still unable to escape the trauma, shook with every breath, a heavy sigh escaped the Screamer’s lips.

‘Ah, if you get scared by a few hits. What kind of wizard would that be…’

Maya had no talent for magic. No, apart from her lack of talent, her way of thinking wasn’t like a wizard’s at all.

After all, a person’s nature might be pure, but in reality, what matters is who can push through it.

Her face was swollen, her eyes wide like a frightened rabbit’s, and her hands were folded tightly in fear.

“Maya, you…”

Just don’t become a wizard.

The Screamer swallowed the rest of his words. It was her life anyway, and it was a path she had to choose for herself.

Also, when he looked at her without the expectation that she was a wizard, he sympathized a little with the way she’d been bitten by a mad dog and gone mad with fear.

“Yeah, I’m sorry.”

The Screamer frowned and placed the water bottle in Maya’s hand.

“I’m sorry I called you stubborn. You sing well, so keep trying. No need to worry about Eider. As soon as I get back, I’ll make sure you’re sent to the hospital.”

“Screamer…”

The Screamer was a difficult person for Maya to be around. He was always angry, and of course, they often ended up in the same group, making her heart flutter at the mere sight of him. Hearing an apology from someone like that caused the sadness she’d been holding back to surge forward, and tears poured out.

“Ah, I really can’t get used to it. Hey, did you apologize? I’m going.”

The Screamer shook his head and left the training ground with another water bottle.

* * *

Two weeks after the hill occupation evaluation, on a holiday.

Shirone, Nade, and Iruki carried flowers and headed toward the advanced class building. They had heard that Shiina, who received an award at the royal castle for her thesis, had been promoted to an official fifth-class wizard.

In the magic schools under the Kingdom of Tormia, there was no institution with a certified fifth-class wizard as a regular teacher except for the Royal Magic School, so it was inevitable that he had become a sergeant at Alpheas Magic School.

“But is it really okay if we go? Seniors don’t usually cross the steel gate. I mean, it creates a weird impression for the juniors.”

“Still, I have to congratulate him. Just like during the presentation, he paid a lot of attention to us.”

The moment they entered the advanced class building while talking, all the students in the hallway widened their eyes.

A graduating senior, whose face was rarely seen, appeared. There were three of them.

“Wow! It’s Shirone-senpai!”

“Iruki-senpai and Nade-senpai are there too!”

In an instant, the corridors were blocked by students pouring out of the classrooms.

Seeing the bright, eager eyes of his juniors, Shirone recalled the memories of being equally astonished by Sanuel’s supersonic demonstration.

‘Well, I was like that once.’

It was unimaginable now that he competed every day with players on Sanuel’s level.

Maybe that was why the juniors he had taken classes with just a few months ago looked much younger than before.

“Hello, senior! My name is Orn, the best prospect in Class Four!”

A young boy stepped out of the crowd and bowed deeply.

It was a face he had never seen in the integrated class last year, so Shirone tilted his head.

“Orn?”

“Yes. I entered this semester. You wouldn’t know me.”

“Ah, so you’re a transfer student.”

Orn raised one corner of his mouth and shook his head.

“Nope. I also started magic late. It’s similar to Shirone-senpai.”

It was only then that Shirone took a closer look at Orn. He had heard that as soon as he entered the magic school, he scored quite impressive results on the Class Four entrance exam.

‘Hmm?’

When a dangerous energy suddenly spread, Shirone instinctively entered the Spirit Zone. Orn’s Spirit Zone pierced his chest like a sharp spear.

‘Hmm, that’s right.’

If you were a promising prospect in the advanced class, there was no way you hadn’t heard the anecdotes about Shirone.

Most of all, his followers, Mark and Maria, were holding out here with a sense of tradition.

Eider’s departure from his senior year must have had an impact as well. Depending on the graduation schedule, with the possibility that one person in Class Four might skip a grade, it was natural that they were highly motivated.

‘How is it? Even seniors have no choice but to admit this, right?’

Orn knew how formidable the senior class was. However, he didn’t think his own talent was inferior to Shirone, who was currently the most famous student at the magic school.

Even though Shirone entered the academy at eighteen and started in Class Seven, Orn was two years younger and had entered Class Four immediately.

Shirone just laughed.

This was exactly why seniors didn’t step out of the steel gate. If you dealt with juniors who didn’t know the world, you wouldn’t be able to do anything.

‘Certainly you have talent. But…’

Shirone suddenly opened his eyes and glared at Orn.

As his Spirit Zone shifted to an offensive type, no fewer than two hundred thorns shot toward Orn.

“Ahh!”

A frightened Orn sat down right where he stood. He planted his back against the ground and looked up at Shirone with a weary expression.

I thought I wouldn’t be pushed back as much as in the Spirit Zone, but the offensive type from before was on a completely different level.

‘Nonsense. Apparently Mark was the defensive type…’

Shirone squatted down to meet him at eye level.

“You. Are you specialized in attack types?”

“Yes? Ah, yes.”

I thought so. Judging by the personality he showed during their brief meeting, he was a junior full of energy, much like Amy.

“It’s not all about sharpening the mind. Offensive types require speed and agility. If you stay in the same state of mind over and over again, you’ll just get used to it.”

“Yeah, that’s right.”

Orn looked up at Shirone blankly. He was far more distant than he had vaguely thought.

‘Is this your senior year?’

As Shirone took a step forward, the students blocking the hallway parted to the left and right as if a waterway were splitting.

“He’s a really cheeky kid. Doesn’t he just remind you of the old Iruki?”

At Nade’s words, Shirone frowned, recalling his first meeting with Iruki.

“No, compared to Iruki, he’s really humble.”

“Because confidence is proportional to skill. In that sense, it’s only natural that he’s more humble than me.”

“Anyway, that bottomless confidence…”

Dozens of juniors watched the backs of the three as they exchanged conversations and walked away toward the end of the hallway.
