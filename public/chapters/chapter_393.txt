Chapter 393  
Worst Project (2)  
~*~

“The space-time of Miro’s labyrinth is balanced at the midpoint between Heaven and this world. For the sake of explanation, think of it as pressure. The labyrinth is being compressed from both sides by equal force. But what do you think would happen if the pressure on one side suddenly vanished?”

Shirone went cold at Gaold’s words.

“Pop. The labyrinth would shatter into pieces. That’s why we can’t bring Miro out. From the moment she created the dimensional wall, she trapped herself there.”

“But Heaven sees it differently.”

“Exactly. Heaven doesn’t care whether Miro lives or dies. As long as they can break through the dimensional wall and invade, that’s enough for them. The crack that opened in the labyrinth’s space-time this time is a perfect example.”

“Is that even possible? The pressure on that wall must be enormous.”

“It isn’t impossible if the archangels exert themselves. They wield powers from the moment the universe began—Amplification with Ikael, Birth with Kariel, Destruction with Uriel, and so on. But Miro is also a genius the human world may never see again. That’s why she became Guffin’s successor. In any case, after she closed the rift, she entered a state of sedation. If she can’t regain consciousness on her own, then the situation has become even worse. That’s why we need reinforcements on our side as well. And that reinforcement is you, Shirone.”

A heavy weight pressed down on Shirone’s chest.

There were many strong people in the world. He could not understand why Gaold would single him out while leaving all of them aside.

“What can I do?”

“In ancient times, the Nephilim led humans to Heaven. However, after Guffin’s erasure, it became clear that pure Nephilim no longer existed. What remains are the traces of Guffin. In other words, without Guffin’s remains, Heaven has no way to reach us either. So we…”

Gaold paused.

It was the hesitation that came when a secret known only to him and Kang Nan was about to be spoken aloud to a third person.

“We will hack the Akashic Records and change the coordinates of this world.”

“The Akashic Records… hacking?”

It was such an absurd statement that Shirone did not even have time to object.

Above all, the Akashic Records were the totality of law, the system that changed individual parts by altering the whole. Whether or not it was possible was one thing; if they mishandled it, the consequences would be catastrophic.

“Is that really all right? If you change the coordinates…”

If the meaning of even a single word changed, the world itself would become something entirely different. People spoke of Guffin’s erasure so easily, but no one knew what the world had been like before it happened.

“Of course, it could become a disaster for humanity. But that won’t happen. We aren’t trying to change the planet’s spatial coordinates.”

“Then…?”

Gaold raised his index finger.

“We will change the coordinates of the beginning.”

For a moment, Shirone could not answer. It took him time to understand what Gaold meant.

“You mean… the beginning of the universe?”

“Yes. If you add time to three-dimensional spatial coordinates, you get four-dimensional coordinates—though, to be precise, we’re dealing with eleven dimensions. If we change the coordinates of the beginning, the events that occurred after creation will not change. In the end, Heaven simply won’t be able to find us anymore.”

“Because the absolute value changes, even though the relative value remains the same.”

It was different from Guffin’s erasure. They were not resetting the world; they were changing its initial point.

Gaold nodded, as if Shirone had understood correctly.

“That’s right. Once the coordinates change, the pressure acting on Miro’s labyrinth will disappear as well. Then we escape with Miro. After that, we seal every trace of Guffin left around the world, and it’s over. We will be saying farewell to Heaven forever.”

For Shirone, the operation finally began to feel real.

Of course, whether it would succeed was still impossible to predict. But at least in theory, it could be done. And if something was theoretically possible, humanity would eventually reach it.

The final war can be avoided. No one has to die.

After organizing his thoughts, Shirone asked, “If I join the project, what role will I play?”

Gaold liked that Shirone had phrased it as an “if.” A child who naturally assumed the worst was prudent, and that prudence would benefit the team.

“The Akashic Records are in Ingris, in Zebul. But in Heaven, movement is only free from higher levels to lower ones. Therefore, if we want to enter Zebul without bloodshed, we have to pass through Araboth.”

Shirone recalled what he had experienced in Heaven. If Ikael had not invited him to Araboth, he would never have been able to approach Zebul, the resting place of angels.

“Then the key is how to break through Araboth. Most of Heaven’s security devices are designed as mecha systems. We will paralyze that mecha system and infiltrate Ingris.”

“Is that possible?”

Shirone had experienced firsthand just how sophisticated Heaven’s mecha system was, so he found it difficult to accept so readily.

“We’ll use the ancient weapon Targis. It generates powerful electromagnetic waves—EMP. If we strike with Targis, we can temporarily bring down the mecha system. There is only one uncertain factor.”

Shirone’s face tightened. It would be his role to remove that uncertainty.

“When abnormally terminated, the Akashic Records in Ingris reboot within two minutes through secondary storage. In other words, the time we are given to change the coordinates of the beginning is only two minutes.”

“What if we use Targis on the secondary storage as well?”

Gaold burst into laughter when he realized Shirone had reached the same thought he had.

“It’s not as if I didn’t consider that. However, according to our investigation, the auxiliary storage device is not part of the mecha system.”

The mecha system was a technology similar to magical engineering, based on electrical power, and it was optimized to systematically classify the Akashic Records.

“If it isn’t the mecha system, then what could possibly store the Akashic Records?”

Something flashed through Shirone’s mind.

“Don’t tell me…”

Gaold nodded with a grave expression.

“Yes. There is another Akashic Record in Heaven besides Ingris. Anke Ra.”

Shirone realized what Gaold was trying to say, and his heart began to pound.

“We estimate that Anke Ra may have been the first life-form to perceive the universe. And to achieve complete control, he created Ingris. As long as he remains intact, we will ultimately have to fight against time. But what if the situation were reversed? The success rate of the operation would rise dramatically.”

Shirone let out a deep sigh.

“Are you telling me to assassinate Anke Ra?”

Gaold waved his hand as if that were ridiculous.

“No, no. You’ve misunderstood completely. I’m telling you to blow up Heaven.”

Shirone’s eyes flashed as if he had been struck by lightning.

Destroy Heaven?

Leaving aside how absurd it sounded, it did not even make rational sense.

Even now, if he closed his eyes and thought back, he could vividly picture the enormity of Heaven. By what means was he supposed to bring it down?

“What could I do that even the president of the association cannot?”

It was a sharp question. But Gaold’s eyes were full of conviction.

“You can do it. No—Ataraxia can do it. The reason I blocked your attack wasn’t because Ataraxia was lacking. It was because your Photon Cannon was weak.”

Even Shirone could not deny that.

The original owner of Ataraxia was Archangel Ikael. And she had once amplified Galliant’s volcanic activity twenty thousand times over and annihilated the Kergos in an instant.

“But that is still the same problem. I’m the only one who can use Ataraxia, and increasing the power of the Photon Cannon in a short period of time is impossible.”

“That is why I’m asking you. If it’s you, you may find a way.”

Shirone felt frustrated. Gaold seemed so immersed in the project that he could not understand what Shirone was saying.

“Even if you say that, how am I supposed to…”

“Shirone.”

Gaold’s gaze pierced through him.

“You can do it. No, you must do it. Increasing the power of the Photon Cannon is probably one method. But if that’s impossible, find another way. Ataraxia is more powerful than any ancient weapon possessed by the temple. Understand everything about Ataraxia. Then find the method. A genius is not someone who runs quickly down a path others have already walked. A genius is someone who creates what does not yet exist. You have developed countless spells on your own. I am placing my hope in that genius.”

Gaold raised one finger.

“One year. Within the next year, develop magic capable of producing destructive power that exceeds Heaven’s durability. If possible, the best outcome would be to blow away all seven heavens. Even if you can’t, you must at least generate enough power to prevent the mecha system from recovering.”

Shirone was speechless. His head was so full of thoughts that it felt as if it might burst.

Gaold, on the other hand, returned to his carefree expression and reached for a cigar.

“That is everything I can tell you in exchange for your passing. If you succeed at this—no, even if you manage to grasp the slightest clue—you will join the advance squad one year from now.”

Shirone thought carefully.

Then he played the last card he had been holding back.

“Do you really think I would do that? Something that could lead to so many deaths?”

“Well. That is something you can think about over the next year. Make your judgment then. Either way, we are going to Heaven. I won’t blame you if you refuse. If you join the project, you become an enemy of the temple and the entire world. On the other hand, there is nothing I can give you.”

“I have one condition.”

At those words, which were close to half an agreement, Kang Nan looked back with a puzzled expression.

It was a task that risked life and death, and the mission itself was nearly impossible. If something went wrong, he would become a traitor to the world. Even if they succeeded, he would gain nothing.

Yet there was no trace of such calculation in Shirone’s answer.

This time, even Gaold could not help being surprised.

Is he saying he won’t be shaken by anything except his own convictions? Yes… perhaps he really can be my savior.

Gaold pretended to be indifferent and lit his cigar.

“Fine. Tell me. Anything that helps the project will be supported.”

“If I gain the power to destroy Heaven, and if I actually reach that point…”

“What if you reach it?”

“When I cast my magic, it has to be after I meet someone I want to meet. If that isn’t possible, I won’t do anything.”

“Hmm.”

Gaold held the cigar between his fingers and looked up at the ceiling. He had a vague idea of whom Shirone wanted to meet.

It must be Ikael.

The archangel who had taught Shirone Ataraxia.

Of course, Shirone had personal reasons to meet her, but now he also had another reason.

But was it truly possible? Taking that condition into account would greatly increase the difficulty of the operation.

Even so, refusing was not an option. If Shirone truly found a way to neutralize Anke Ra, the effect would be incomparable to the additional risk.

“I won’t ask who it is. But why do you want to meet that person? Is it worth making the whole world your enemy?”

Shirone answered with a question of his own.

“Why is the president of the association trying to save Miro while making the whole world his enemy?”

Cigar smoke burst from Gaold’s mouth.

“Ha, fine. I understand. I’m sure I don’t have to say it, but everything discussed today is confidential. Stay here for the time being. I’ll support you with anything that helps your work. I’ll also assist with your school life. You’ll want to graduate before you die.”

“Thank you.”

There was no need to read too much into it. Now that they were in the same boat, that level of support was natural.

When the conversation ended, Shirone rose from the sofa and headed for the door. With his hand on the doorknob, he was lost in thought for a long while before he spoke with difficulty.

“I met Miro.”

“…Is that so?”

The answer was surprisingly bland.

Only Alpheas and Shirone’s friends knew about the incident in which Miro had hijacked him in the Infinite Realm. But Gaold, who had been tracking Shirone’s movements, had vaguely anticipated it.

Did I bring up something unnecessary?

Shirone had mentioned it out of a sense of responsibility. But Gaold’s calmness, if anything, might mean he had been hurt beyond the range of understanding.

“Then I’ll go.”

Just as Shirone pulled the door open, Gaold said, “Well… how was she?”

Shirone recalled that time.

Even now, he could not say it had been a particularly pleasant meeting. Miro had tried to trap him in her labyrinth of time and space, and in the end, he had only been able to return to his original body after passing through a black hole.

Shirone looked back at Gaold and smiled.

“As I’d heard, she was an unpredictable person.”

“…”

Shirone left, and the door closed behind him.

Gaold remained deep in thought for a long time. Then he chuckled and crushed his cigar out in the ashtray.

“He’s a good kid.”

With a polite yet cold expression, Kang Nan said, “Yes. Unlike the president of the association.”
