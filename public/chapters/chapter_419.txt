Battle Readiness (2)

‘What is this feeling?’
Even after the song ended, Shirone remained in his seat, unable to calm his pounding heart.
“Uh? Shirone!”
Maya, who had only just noticed him, greeted him with a bright smile. “Come in. What are you doing here?”
“I thought I’d wash up a bit. I came because I heard the music.”
Shirone held up his toothbrush to dispel any suspicion of spying.
Maya shifted her weight off the piano bench and gestured to the seat beside her. “Sit here. There aren’t any other chairs. Oh, if you’re busy… … .”
Given that Shirone was currently fighting tooth and nail against the top players in the senior class, she had assumed he wouldn’t have time for leisurely chats.
“No, it’s okay. I had nowhere else to go anyway.”
The piano bench was narrow, and Maya’s presence filled the space. As their arms brushed, a strange, unfamiliar sensation traveled through their skin. Hers was soft, with a faint, cool chill.
The awkward Shirone broke the silence first. “Ahaha, you sing so well. Why didn’t you say anything? I don’t know much about music, but I’d say you’re better than most professionals.”
Noticing the sudden sadness in Maya’s eyes, Shirone realized his mistake. Even with such incredible talent, if you aren’t a professional, you’re bound to carry a story behind it.
“Oh, sorry. If I hurt your feelings… … .”
Maya smiled and shook her head. She couldn’t stand how seriously he took everything, which only made her feel more unsettled.
‘It’s been like this since the beginning.’
Maya had first seen Shirone on the roof during the presentation day of the Supernatural Psychic Research Conference. The way he had proven the invisible had shocked her. He was the brightest star in the school, and the situation that had swayed every student present left a deep impression on her.
“Song… … Shall I call you?”
“Yes. I want to hear it.”
When Maya placed her hands on the keys and began to sing, Shirone immediately fell into her music. He had thought it was merely sound, but she possessed the power to move people’s hearts with her voice.
‘Ah… … I see.’
To sing solely for the listener’s happiness, forgetting about graduation from magic school, the circumstances of her tribe, and her family’s poverty.
At this moment, he wasn’t a wizard.
Maya was an artist.
After the celestial song ended, a heavy silence followed. Shirone couldn’t even exhale until the final note faded soundlessly. A soft moan escaped his trembling lips.
“Haaaaaa.”
A diva. The goddess stood before him.
Shirone found himself unable to resist Maya’s gaze, which looked at him with a passionate intensity. A fire ignited in his chest. Her eyes seemed to strip away his defenses, leaving him completely exposed.
“… … .”
The sunset was setting outside the window.

* * *
The eighth week’s main skill enhancement evaluation had arrived.
It was an intense competition to gain an upper hand in the ranking standings, as the score distribution was autonomous and evaluated the achievement of twenty items related to each student’s major. Students submitted their challenge requests prior to the assessment. For example, if you challenged three items to level seven and failed to reach them, you would be awarded zero points for that week. Therefore, if you only needed a single point, it was better to choose a manageable difficulty, but if you aimed for a high score, you had to increase the difficulty even if it meant taking a risk.
Shirone sat side by side with his friends, watching the evaluations of their peers’ major skills. His eyes were sharper than usual because, starting the day after tomorrow, the confrontation with the Screamer team for high ground occupation would be held. While Shirone’s team lineup was already finalized, the Screamer team’s members had not yet been confirmed. It was crucial to analyze as many majors as possible today.
The main skill enhancement evaluation differed from other events in that actual magic was cast instead of simulated 2,000 presses. Various devices were installed in each testing area, and the impact force test, in particular, resembled the marble maze found in the Kergo ruins.
After seven students finished, it was Fishaw’s turn. The evaluation teacher checked the request form and looked up.
“Fishaw, what is your challenge?”
“I will challenge three items: Eruption, gigantism, and mutation. The difficulty levels are five, six, and five, respectively.”
At twenty-five years old, Fishaw was the oldest in his senior year. Although he didn’t stand out in typical evaluations, his Insect magic always excited Shirone whenever he witnessed it.
“Fishaw has gone up one notch from last week. It’s only been eight weeks, but you’re entering the intermediate level, right?”
“You are twenty-five,” Nade remarked. “You should consider that you’ve polished your major for at least five years longer than the others. I’ll probably pass easily this time too.”
“At this point, Shirone, there is something you must know,” Nade said with a serious expression. “That guy, Fishaw, is the president of the Ant Language Research Society.”
“Ant Language Research Society? Is there such a thing?”
“It’s a research society that operates in the shadows, much like us. They want to have an audience with the queen ant by analyzing the ant’s pheromone language. Perhaps they’re planning to negotiate a diplomatic agreement.”
After a moment of thought, Shirone asked, “But why should I know that?”
“Idiot! If they make a pact with the queen ant, they’ll target the Supernatural Psychic Research Group. It’s not a matter of subduing a few ants. All the ants under the queen’s control will become enemies!”
Shirone opened his mouth to respond but ultimately turned his head away without saying anything.
Iruki continued, “Insect magic is the most maniac category among majors. It’s good to watch while we still can.”
“Is it similar to summoning magic?”
“No. A summoner imitates a specific target so perfectly that it’s nearly indistinguishable from the real thing. Of course, insects are possible too, but you can’t implement hundreds of species that way.”
“Then how does Fishaw do it?”
Iruki leaned in, recalling an explanation he’d once heard from his father. “The core battery of the Insect series is the egg. It’s an aggregate of undifferentiated functions that can evolve into various forms. They claim it induces spontaneous generation using the theory of ‘ecosystem in the head,’ but they say it’s insanely difficult. You have to calculate everything from occurrence to evolution and mutation.”
“Hmm, that must be really difficult.”
“So there are many scholars in the Insect field. But once you become a wizard, you’re treated very well. Fishaw will be in his senior year by the time he’s twenty-five, but graduating now is absolutely no loss.”
When Fishaw signaled he was ready, the assessment teacher presented the first challenge.
“First of all, Eruption. The fifth level requires three hundred.”
Fishaw tuned his internal battery using the ecological theory in his mind. Once the food chain and predators were established, the eggs began to differentiate into the most suitable population for survival.
“Grasshopper.”
A swarm of three hundred locusts flew up from Fishaw’s feet, moving like black particles through the air.
The teacher wrote down three hundred and twelve on the record sheet. It wasn’t difficult to count the insects through synesthesia.
“It’s one point for passing the fifth stage. Next is gigantism.”
Fishaw closed his eyes again and concentrated.
It took approximately five minutes to differentiate the egg by increasing the oxygen saturation and adjusting the surrounding environment.
An ant as large as a person emerged, moving its antennae in all directions to examine its surroundings. Both female and male students frowned in discomfort. That explained why Fishaw was such a loner.
“Body surface area: 2.02 square meters. Passing gigantism. One point earned. Next, mutation.”
This time, it took a whopping twenty minutes to develop the organism. The results were shocking.
A centipede with the protective coloration of a ladybug crawled across the floor. Its scythe-like jaws opened, and deadly venom shot out.
“One type of shape change. One function change. Passed.”
“Thank you.”
It was an amazing demonstration and a shocking sight, but the students just wished they would never have to see it again.
That’s how Fishaw’s main skill enhancement concluded, and Maya stepped up for the next turn.
Nade blew a small horn and shouted, “Maya, fighting!”
Maya simply smiled, scratched her cheek, and walked into the evaluation room without showing any reaction.
“Huh? Why are you like that? Is something bad happening?” Embarrassed, Nade licked his lips.
But now that he looked closer, Shirone’s expression didn’t look particularly good either.
“What? What happened to you two?”
There was no way to know it was Shirone’s doing.
After the incident in the music room, Maya had deliberately distanced herself. She didn’t treat him coldly, but the warmth was gone.
Iruki spoke up. “Maybe we were too rude.”
“What are you talking about? What did we do to Maya?”
“Even if she was insulted by the Screamer, she should have made a choice. Because there are people she doesn’t want to fight with.”
Nade suddenly recalled the situation. He had been relieved when he shouted to bring Maya into the fight, but thinking about it again, he clearly crossed the line.
“Uh, so what? I made a loud noise. No, shouldn’t we apologize and fix things before they get worse?”
“Maya doesn’t get mad about that. And I’m not angry about that,” Shirone said bluntly.
According to Iruki, the tension should have already surfaced when they met in the music room. But Maya had been more affectionate than anyone else, singing her songs with all her heart.
‘But why are you avoiding it? Why?’
Maya’s evaluation ended quickly. All three categories—vocal volume, sound vibration, and sound—remained at the second stage, and she didn’t even pass that threshold.
“None of the sonic magics are below average. After that, it will be impossible to graduate this year.”
Nade groaned at Iruki’s words. “Shouldn’t graduation be the problem? Shouldn’t we be worried about the entire evaluation right now? You said that you could get a high score even with Maya as a team member. Wasn’t there some kind of strategy?”
“No, I just spit it out as it came out. You can’t say good things to the Screamers there, can you?”
“What? You then… … !”
“Don’t make a fuss. Just because I didn’t think it through doesn’t mean there isn’t a solution. That’s it. You can think about it from now on. Anyway, maybe the reason Maya is like this is because of her grades. Even I can see it doesn’t go well with sound magic.”
“Maybe it’s the other way around.”
Iruki looked back at Shirone. “What have you been talking about since before? You two, what really happened?”
Shirone told the story of what had happened in the music room: Maya’s incredible singing ability and the thrill that had run down his spine.
“Hmm, popular song. Definitely a different area. Because sound magic borrows vocal music to maximize the function of the voice.”
Iruki’s head turned quickly. He thought that maybe it could be a major variable in the occupation of this highland.
Next up was Fermi. Heading to the training ground, he glanced back at Shirone and laughed.
Fermi’s major was Air, and his main skill enhancement score was exactly one hundred points. All items had been cleared at level five.
Similar to the average speed of Class One, it seemed there was no chance of falling into Class Two even now.
The evaluation teacher changed the evaluation paper and asked, “Fermi, what is your challenge?”
Fermi said with a smile, “I will master it.”
The evaluation teacher raised his head as he was about to record. The students also started whispering.
Last year, he had mastered all of his main skill enhancements, so it wasn’t unusual, but the timing was too early.
Considering that they usually started to pick up speed after the twelfth week, it was a whopping four-week head start.
‘It must have been influenced by Shirone’s existence.’
After defeating Fermi in the survival test and even declaring war, it was a strategy to use this opportunity to dampen his spirits again.
“Good. Let’s measure from the first press item.”
Fermi held out his index and middle fingers and pointed at the impact-measuring device.
The air was compressed at the tip of his fingers, and an air gun fired with a bang.
The eyes of the students turned to the billboard beyond the glass screen.
1,123 press.
It was a figure that easily passed the master difficulty of 1,000 presses.
