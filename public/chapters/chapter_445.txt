Chapter 445  
Second Purgatory (2)  
~*~

Shirone and the others proceeded toward the mainland.

Traveling by Zulu’s summoned beast would have been faster, but it was too risky given the unknown conflict raging in Purgatory. In particular, since the skies of Heaven were patrolled by angels, Maras, and drones, Shirone wanted to avoid any situation where they could be captured beyond the range of Etela’s Spirit Zone.

Contrary to their expectations, however, the profane forest was eerily quiet. This surprised the group, who had imagined angels constantly patrolling the skies and hunting down heretics and rebels.

Kang Nan voiced the question on everyone’s mind. “How should we interpret this? An unexpected shift in the situation, or proof that the rebels have already been wiped out?”

“Either scenario is possible,” Sain replied.

Fleur tilted her head at Sain’s words. “Is it possible the forest is quiet without the rebels being destroyed?”

“By common sense, no. But if you think that way, you’d also have to accept that the maze simply vanished. Anything can happen here. We have to keep an open mind.”

Sein felt that same sense of incongruity she had experienced in the maze’s distorted space-time. In fact, the fact that Purgatory itself was defying expectations only deepened her anxiety.

*It’s something I’ve been meticulously planning for twenty years. Why is it unraveling so quickly?*

Could her current senses be deceiving her? Maybe this was just a coincidence. After all, anyone could look at the results and force a cause to fit. Sein forced herself to a simple conclusion.

*Don’t overthink it. I’ll calculate for both possibilities. I can handle it.*

“There’s someone ahead,” Sein said, stopping and glancing to the right. She hadn’t felt them nearby, but her Spirit Zone—a two-kilometer radius—was already flooding her mind with data. About twenty individuals were moving with unnatural agility. The clash of weapons and distant explosions echoed through her senses.

“I’d say we’re walking into a battle. Roughly eight hundred meters out.”

Fleur glanced sideways at Gaold. “Are you certain?”

The question was a request for permission to increase their speed. Given the unpredictable state of the forest, holding back was an option, but Gaold opted for a direct approach.

“Proceed.”

In an instant, the entire team blurred into streaks of light and vanished into the trees. Quan and Kang Nan rode on Zulu’s back, while the rest of the party—excluding Shirone—were seasoned veterans who navigated the dense terrain without issue. Shirone had also mastered the advanced movement techniques taught in the senior class, but the gap in raw experience was obvious. Gaold, Sein, Zulu, and Armin led the vanguard, their movements so fluid and precise they seemed to glide over the complex undergrowth like aircraft.

*How many years of training does it take to move like that?*

Upon reaching the source of the noise, the earlier silence was gone, replaced by the clamor of combat.

“Follow me! Aim for their hearts!”

“Kyaah!”

The Kergos, clad in black armor, pursued the Mechas with feral cries. Their mounted charges moved like a gale, but the Mechas’ speed was difficult to match.

*What is that?*

The Mechas’ mobility came from a mechanical exoskeleton strapped over their bodies. A skeletal frame ran from their shoulders down their arms, then followed their spine to their legs. As the wearer moved, the joints whirred with a crisp *ching-ching* sound. With every step, the device propelled them forward, adding five or six meters to each leap.

“Piper,” Sein murmured. “A strength-augmentation device. These are Mecha fighters.”

The Mechas bounded through the forest as if springs were attached to their boots. Yet the Kergos were exceptional hunters. One of them, having tracked a straggler, burst from the treeline and swung a blade.

“Hah!”

The targeted Mecha raised a holographic shield. The resulting shockwave staggered the attacker, but the Kergo’s raw physical strength was superior, and the Mecha stumbled to the ground.

Her squadmates quickly surrounded her, scanning their surroundings. Before they could react, Kergos warriors emerged from the shadows, cutting off their escape routes and closing in from all directions.

The short-haired woman leading the Mecha squad whispered into a wrist-mounted drone. “This is the Second Reconnaissance Team. We’re surrounded by Kergos. Requesting immediate support.”

Static was the only response. Though radio interference was likely, she couldn’t be sure the distress call hadn’t gone through. *Please… please…*

Twenty meters away, Shirone and the others observed the standoff. The Kergos prided themselves on their tracking skills, but they couldn’t afford to exert themselves in the presence of Sein’s *Equilibrium*. Under that technique, even the sound of breathing was muffled, and the five senses were rendered useless.

Kang Nan glanced at Gaold. “What’s our move? The Kergos or the Mechas?”

This was the first major junction they’d encountered since entering Heaven. The region’s inhabitants were divided into three races—the Kergos, the Mechas, and the Nors—each with distinct combat styles, cultures, and mindsets. Choosing which faction to engage first would drastically alter their path to the maze, so the decision required careful consideration.

A bush rustled. Kergos warriors patrolling the perimeter of the Mecha formation stepped into view. The lead warrior’s eyes widened in shock. It was unnatural to suddenly encounter ten strangers in a zone where even the Kergos’ sensory networks couldn’t detect intruders.

He turned to rally his comrades and opened his mouth to shout.

“Halt—!”

Gaold’s hand moved in a blur.

“Feather.”

With a wet *thwack*, the Kergo’s head detached and flew from his shoulders.

Shirone and Fleur stared blankly at the headless, twitching corpse before snapping back to attention. If restraining emotion and acting on pure rational judgment was the standard human response, Gaold’s reaction was profoundly mechanical.

“If you believe killing is necessary under any circumstance, do it without hesitation. I’ll handle the rear guard. Even if it’s the wrong call, I’ll accept it as a failure worse than losing a teammate.”

It was, in essence, Gaold’s license to kill. It was a brutally pragmatic approach to team survival, but it worked because Gaold possessed the authority and capability to enforce it. That ruthless decisiveness was precisely why he was chosen as commander.

Kang Nan looked at Gaold sideways. “Given that, we’re targeting the Mechas, then?”

While human values often prioritized life over death, the Kergos were an organization bound by divine law. Having already executed one of their own, the psychological barrier to negotiation was far higher than with the Mechas.

Gaold’s eyes narrowed in displeasure. “I’m going after the Mechas.”

The moment the words left his mouth, Quan launched himself forward. As a Hitman, he was perfectly suited for this kind of work.

“Squad Leader! If we stay here, we’ll be wiped out!”

The Mechas were barely holding their ground. The *Piper* exoskeleton compensated for muscle weakness, but it only enhanced large muscle groups. In a direct hand-to-hand brawl against Kergos, whose entire bodies were living weapons, they stood no chance.

“Fall back! I’ll hold them off!”

The Mecha squad leader leaped onto a tree branch. She knew exactly what a heresy hunter would do to a captured target, but she needed to buy time for reinforcements to arrive.

“Sever her limbs! Bring me her torso!”

The Kergo commander’s order made her teeth clench. She wanted to flee, but the warriors who had already vaulted over her head were swinging their blades to chop off her arms.

*Whoosh!*

Suddenly, a violent gust of wind erupted. A faint afterimage blurred past the Kergos.

“What…?!”

The woman’s eyes widened in shock. The Kergos’ heads detached from their necks and hit the ground like corks pulled from bottles.

*Seven left.*

Quan spun in midair, instantly mapping the positions of the remaining enemies. In a single, fluid motion, he dispatched six of them. No one noticed the twelve subtle shifts in his footwork that made the strike possible.

“Hostiles! Kill them!”

Only after losing half their number did the Kergos realize they were facing an ambush. They charged blindly.

Quan’s body moved in bizarre, unpredictable arcs, bound by threads of external gravity that stretched out in three different directions. He vibrated like a pendulum, then suddenly launched himself forward, leaving a trail of blood from the four Kergos he passed.

*Three more.*

He decapitated another warrior.

*Two.*

The enraged Kergo squad leader’s eyes bulged. Her people also utilized external gravity, but only as a secondary tool to enhance their slashes. Quan’s erratic, gravity-defying movements were something entirely new—and deeply unsettling.

“Don’t underestimate the Kergos!”

The vice-captain swung a crescent blade. Quan’s trajectory bent unnaturally, repelled by an invisible force like two matching magnetic poles, and he sailed past her strike.

“Hah! It worked!”

The Kergos relied on traps as the foundation of their tactics. A warrior who had been lying in wait lowered his sword to intercept the flying Quan.

“Huh?”

The blade felt strangely light in his grip. When he looked up, Quan was floating motionless in the air, well outside the sword’s effective range.

Quan shot the warrior an annoyed glare, then slowly turned his head. At that exact moment, his body spun like a top. An invisible thread of gravity wrapped around him, dragging him toward the squad leader.

“Captain! Look out!”

Before the leader could react, Quan’s sword slashed across her throat.

As the external gravity field collapsed, Quan landed and hurled his blade mid-spin. The sword flew faster than an arrow, piercing the ponytail-clad warrior’s stomach and embedding itself in a nearby tree.

With the Kergos annihilated, the forest fell silent once more.

It had been a dazzling display of combat, yet it had all unfolded in roughly five seconds. Only then did Shirone and the others step into the clearing.

Shirone was deeply impressed by Quan’s rumored prowess, but the man himself wore a face twisted in irritation.

*Damn it. How much of a has-been have I become?*

In the past, he would have cut down every last one of them before a single breath of external gravity could be drawn. Now, he had only managed to kill one, and his finishing move had been a desperate, temporary measure using a secret technique.

“Quan-san, are you alright? Even if you’re injured…”

As Sein approached, Quan quickly masked his expression. He had no intention of drawing her sympathy.

“Sorry. My balance isn’t quite perfect yet.”

Sein tilted her head. As a sorceress, she couldn’t possibly understand the martial arts intricacies Quan was referring to. After a moment’s thought, she offered a prepared compliment.

“It was incredible. I don’t see much swordsmanship these days, but you’ve certainly broadened my perspective.”

The praise only seemed to embarrass Quan further. He offered a bitter smile and moved past her, but Sein called out after him.

*Did I say something wrong?*

Meanwhile, the Mechas tensed as Gaold approached. Their intervention had saved their lives, but it was difficult to clearly identify the *Piper* users in the dense terrain of Purgatory. Above all, if a single swordsman had annihilated the entire Kergo unit, it was clear they were outclassed.

“Who are you? Are you heretics?”

Gaold looked back at Sein. Some of the team members had learned the language of Heaven, but more than half still needed an interpreter. When the woman’s words were finally translated through Sein’s Spirit World magic, Gaold took a step forward.

At the same time, the Mechas flinched back. The *Piper* exoskeletons attached to their bodies clicked as the joints tightened, narrowing their range of motion in preparation for combat.
