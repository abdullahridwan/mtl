Chapter 357  
Battle Mage’s Philosophy (3)  
~*~

“Hongan. So self-image memory corrects errors in the shortest time. That’s a really impressive ability. In terms of talent alone, it would be the best on the continent, even beyond Alpheas Magic School. That’s proven by the many geniuses from your family.”  

Amy couldn’t help but react.  

Even with her Karmis family background, or even compared to her older brother, she was already making a name for herself on the continent in her own field at a young age.  

“So what? I’ve never thought of leaning on my family until now! Are you saying I came here because of my talent? I’ve always tried to stand on my own.”  

“Yes. That’s what foolishness is.”  

Amy’s expression hardened.  

“I’d rather lean on my family. What you have, what your family has—you have to fight with everything. Are you saying that just because you want to try on your own, you throw away something you were born with, something others want but can’t have? That’s foolishness.”  

“I… that thought…”  

“Do you remember the kids I met at the skating rink? Did you say Zocre? Eins Magic School is honestly not prestigious. Their talents are far inferior to yours. Even so, they find their own way to live and become wizards. Did you drop out because you didn’t have enough luck? No, Amy. You’re competing in the top five most prestigious schools in the kingdom. People who are more disadvantaged than you are widespread in the world. If you want to become a wizard, if you want to become the best wizard, you have to fight and overcome whatever situation comes your way.”  

Amy lowered her head.  

Her anger faded after a while.  

No, it felt like everything had gone back to square one.  

Dante was right.  

She was attending Alpheas Magic School, where the best talents in the kingdom gathered, and she was among the top of her graduating class.  

A place where no excuses could be made.  

In a battleground where talented people who only aim for the best collide, talking about it was nothing more than the nonsense of someone with a full stomach.  

“Then what… do I do?”  

She couldn’t jump over the wall and was back at the starting line.  

The second time might be faster.  

But it was just an idea in her head.  

Now she had to overcome herself. And that was Amy’s first ordeal.  

‘Amy…’  

Shirone looked at Amy with pity.  

Dante’s straightforward way of speaking must have been a big shock to Amy. But Shirone couldn’t intervene.  

It wasn’t arrogance to think it would help Amy.  

Because Dante’s words were true.  

A silence fell over the table.  

They couldn’t hear the laughter of tourists clamoring from all sides.  

Dante looked over at Amy while drinking cold coffee.  

She didn’t have any special affection for Amy, but Amy was the one who stood out the most in the graduation exams.  

“Shall I tell you why you were eliminated?”  

Amy raised her head again.  

Shirone, lost in thought, also looked at Dante.  

If it was Dante’s analysis—her major was information processing—it was worth listening to, no matter the situation.  

“Of course, the only time I saw you using magic was during the graduation exam. And that was less than five minutes. But even in such a short time, there are things I just don’t understand.”  

“Are you saying I made a mistake?”  

“No. It’s a problem closer to the essence than that. To begin with, you have a fatal flaw. Especially if you aim to become a battle mage, you’re missing something you must have.”  

Amy never thought of herself as having a weakness. She swallowed hard and waited for him to continue.  

Dante raised her coffee cup and turned her head. She chose her words carefully, then pursed her lips and said,  

“For you… you have no style.”  

“Style?”  

Amy asked, surprised by the unexpected words. However, Shirone nodded slightly.  

No style.  

It was a word that implied the vague sense of incongruity Shirone had felt while being with Amy.  

Only Dante’s eyes—the best defense in the kingdom—could clearly see Amy’s weaknesses after watching only five minutes of battle.  

Shirone added,  

“To put it mildly, you’re omnipotent. I’ve felt it before, but you’re so great it’s like a mask.”  

Amy’s face flushed at Shirone’s words. After being shocked by Dante’s directness, she felt even more embarrassed.  

“Are you kidding me?”  

Dante shook her head.  

“No, there’s some truth in what Shirone said. From what I’ve seen, you tend to have complete control over the situation because you can.”  

“Why is that a problem?”  

“It’s not a very big problem. Your gimmick is sniper mode. Most mages direct the situation to an environment where their skills can specialize. Style arises from there. The reason Shirone likes the strategy of suppressing with brute force is because it has an immortal function. I like counters because I enjoy analyzing them. But you’re different. If sniper mode doesn’t work, you immediately find a second solution.”  

Amy was dazed, as if hit over the head with a hammer.  

“You know what’s really serious? In the end, you do it. You look for a second solution. You’re so clever. But how long can that method work?”  

Amy’s lips trembled.  

She was a stupid jerk.  

How could she have never thought about that?  

“Usually, you’ll hit a wall at the student level. But your talent was wasted all the way to your senior year. Then, at the most crucial moment, you hit that wall.”  

Dante opened her palms and clashed her fists.  

“It just so happened that all the contestants were aiming for you. It was the first time I experienced a situation that couldn’t be overcome by talent alone, facing a level of difficulty that soared sharply compared to the senior class. The difficulties Shirone and I experienced early on came too late for you. Fermi must have already known your fatal weakness. That’s why you were chosen as a sacrifice.”  

Dante recalled the graduation exam and gave an example.  

“The moment you sensed the sudden change in the charter, you gave up on Sanuel and went after Fermi. They must have judged that the schema would give them an edge in close combat. But I’ve been there for four months. The moment you feel uncomfortable, you should have opened the distance unconditionally. You had to somehow lead him to a situation where you could specialize in sniper mode. But you didn’t.”  

Shirone agreed.  

“Because I had confidence that I could solve it somehow.”  

Dante nodded.  

“It’s impossible to control every situation in melee. In battle, even one second longer to make a decision matters. If options are divided, there will inevitably be a delay. So style matters. Believe in your style and bet everything on it.”  

Amy recalled the situation in Kazura where she was trapped in Xenoger’s web.  

Even then, she had a lot on her mind.  

But if she had focused only on what she could do, she could have melted the web with Ignite and escaped.  

“Perhaps you analyzed everything in your senior year—combat power, judgment, habits, behavior, etc.—of all participants. You analyzed everything about them and decided you had the advantage, right?”  

Amy readily admitted,  

“Right. Exactly.”  

“But you didn’t think about style. It’s just as important as fighting power. You have to know how to avoid and wait if you don’t match. If I went back now and fought Shirone from back then, there’s probably a a 90% chance I’d win.”  

Shirone nodded obediently.  

There’s no ‘if’ in history, but if the past self and the current Dante faced each other, nine out of nine times it would be a losing battle.  

“Combat isn’t something where superiority can be measured simply by skill alone. Extremely speaking, it’s enough to destroy Shirone’s Ataraxia before it’s finished. But why didn’t I succeed? Because Shirone constantly induced the situation to his advantage. A clash of styles. That’s a wizard’s battle.”  

Amy kept her head down, unmoving.  

She was so occupied with her thoughts about magic that nothing else caught her eye.  

Dante said,  

“Well, I pretended to be proud, but if a similar situation came up, I might be eliminated too. But I’m not going to drop out early without trying anything, like you did. Anyway, this is the result of my analysis of you in the graduation exam.”  

Amy raised her head again. Her expression was still sad, but her excitement was gone.  

“Thank you for being honest.”  

For whatever reason, she was grateful that her competitor next year had analyzed her strengths and weaknesses so thoroughly.  

Dante, perhaps embarrassed by the solemn atmosphere, finished and stood up.  

“Looks like you brought up something stupid on a date. Anyway, I’ll stop here. You guys have fun too. See you later at school.”  

Shirone escorted her outside the door.  

Dante peered at Amy through the window outside the café.  

Seeing her sullen expression, he turned and scratched his head.  

“Sorry. The atmosphere seemed good, but I interrupted and ruined it.”  

Dante, who had been in love at the Royal Magic School, sensed a subtle current flowing between Shirone and Amy.  

The fact that Amy came along on a trip with just the two of them meant she too had her own feelings to some extent.  

If the atmosphere at the skating rink had continued, the tomorrows of Shirone and Amy might have been different.  

However, Dante’s advice for the day complicated Amy’s thoughts.  

It was natural for him to feel sorry for her as a man.  

But Shirone didn’t think so. Rather, he was grateful to Dante for giving Amy that advice.  

“It’s fine. If it wasn’t for you, Amy’s problems wouldn’t have been found. It really helped me a lot. Of course, Amy will have to find the solution herself.”  

Dante smiled bitterly.  

Shirone didn’t seem to ask anything about the state of the world.  

Well, in a sense, Amy belonged to the same department as Shirone, so it could be said they met properly.  

Dante had always been quick to fall in love. It suited her personality, so she had no complaints about it.  

But on the other hand, she thought it would be fun to match her feelings slowly, like a slug.  

“It wasn’t advice I gave specifically for Amy. After all, there will be Fermi and the others next year anyway. The more counterhorses you make, the better. Anyway, see you next time. Have fun and take care.”  

Dante waved and walked away.  

Seeing him leave as lightly as when he came, Shirone felt the composure and freedom of a traveler.  

‘I hope it will be a good trip for you too.’
