Chapter 469

With the activation of the artificial brain, Ataraxia’s arrival time accelerated to more than twice its original speed.

Shirone stared at the shimmering halo forming before her and raised her hand. “Photon Cannon!”

Photons compressed violently, trembling as if on the verge of detonation—when suddenly, Ikasa appeared directly in front of her.

“Kuk!”

A fist slammed into Shirone’s abdomen. Armand’s blade clenched instinctively, but the shockwave still tore through her body, rattling bones and organs.

*Equivalent exchange of cause and effect.*

The Valhalla Action didn’t create power—it reversed the order of potential and kinetic energy. For beings bound by time, such a reversal was nearly impossible to withstand.

“How dare you!” Shirone gasped, staggering back.

Ikasa launched forward in a blur, her movements accelerated by the Valhalla Action. Shirone swung Armand’s writhing tentacles to deflect the blows, flicking away each punch with desperate precision.

At point-blank range, she fired the Photon Cannon.

This time, Ikasa twisted aside in a dazzling evasion, the beam scorching the air where she had stood.

The surviving rebels watched in stunned silence. No one dared interfere in a battle where brute force and magic clashed at speeds beyond comprehension.

“That’s… a Nephilim,” someone whispered.

A human—once powerless—now standing equal to an angel.

Even a fallen one like Ikasa was an existence far beyond mortal reach. To see such a being challenged so openly shattered everything they believed.

*This can’t be real!*

Ikasa herself struggled to accept it.

A year ago, these humans couldn’t even lift a finger against her. Now, one stood before her, wielding power rivaling her own—power granted by the Great Full Moon.

“A mere worm!” Ikasa snarled.

She lunged, aiming a devastating combo at Shirone’s position. Afterimages of her fists formed a massive, rushing barrier, like a tidal wave of destruction.

Shirone dropped into an extreme defensive stance.

The Ultima System activated instantly. Armand’s robes coiled around her body, thickening, reinforcing. A fist-shaped pattern stamped itself across her skin—armor forged from pure will.

“Agh!”

The assault was too fast—too overwhelming. Defense was the only option.

But Shirone knew: after the storm passed, her chance would come.

*Just as I thought—there’s a limit when the target has a will. If we endure this… we can win.*

Even as Ikasa’s final blow sent her flying, bones cracking, Shirone endured. She channeled the pain, using it to accelerate Ataraxia’s accumulation.

Her prediction was correct.

Ikasa, having forced the result with Valhalla Action, now found herself frozen—unable to move as the system reset.

*But it’s close. Can I make it in time?*

It didn’t matter. She had to.

Given an angel’s durability, this was the only way—to finish it in a single, overwhelming strike.

Ikasa stared at the rapidly growing Ataraxia and felt the cold grip of a condemned prisoner.

*That’s dangerous.*

Archangel Ikael’s proprietary technique.

If Ataraxia completed before Valhalla Action recharged, Ikasa would be pacified—not by force, but by the sheer weight of the technique’s existence.

*Six seconds. Five seconds. Four seconds.*

Ataraxia versus Valhalla Action.

A race to see who could perfect angelic power first.

“It’s time. Bring it out.”

From the underground command center, Raysis issued the order.

Instantly, every living being on the battlefield froze.

*What?*

Fleur strained to lift her head, her vision swimming.

Above, the massive pupils of a colossal entity scanned the battlefield with terrifying speed.

Biological Weapon – Jin Akamai.

Its power inverted natural law.

Ikasa convulsed, as if bound by invisible ropes.

“Keugh! This… this…!”

Even for a fallen angel, the restraint was unbearable.

For a normal angel, merely receiving the power of the full moon was taxing. But this—this was annihilation.

Ikasa was immobilized. The others stood no chance.

Only Shirone moved—her body trembling under the strain of Mental Transcendence.

*What kind of power is this?*

Even Armand’s robe couldn’t withstand it. It unraveled, reversing its formation, and collapsed back into a demonic sword.

A researcher in the underground lab reported, “All targets have been arrested. What are your orders?”

Raysis tore off his commander’s mask.

The materials had been gathered. The rebels were now worthless.

“Take a team. Bring Shirone, Fleur, and the angel. Prepare the Anecdotal Sake immediately.”

His subordinates moved without hesitation.

While the Anecdotal Sake was being readied, Shirone, Fleur, and Ikasa were locked behind reinforced bars.

But the true prison wasn’t the cage—it was Jin Akamai, watching them from beyond the bars with unblinking eyes.

Fleur clenched her teeth.

She hadn’t expected Raysis to act while an angel was present.

*No—this weapon… how was it even created?*

Three former enemies now shared a cell.

Fleur glared at Ikasa—the one who had ruined everything.

But survival came first. She turned to Shirone.

“Raysis is enhancing himself through biological experiments. You’re the primary material. Garas and I are being used to mass-produce clones.”

Ikasa’s eyes widened. Even she found it absurd.

“A lowly race trying to become a god? How pathetic.”

The irony wasn’t lost on Shirone.

*They’re cloning me? How…?*

The cell door opened. Lacey stepped in.

“Whoops. Angels and Nephilim? What a delicious selection. No—make that *luxurious*.”

Ikasa spat, “Insignificant human! Do you even understand the weight of defying angels?”

Lacey smirked. “Knock-knock. Who’s the one trapped by a mere man-made creature?”

Without fear, Raysis stepped into the cage and sat before Ikasa, gently stroking her chin.

A tyrannical fallen angel—yet undeniably an angel in form.

“How beautiful,” he murmured. “Perfect for my new body.”

Shirone demanded, “Why are you doing this? Even if you gain strength through these experiments, you’ll only become a monster.”

“A monster…” Raysis echoed.

Memories surfaced—Satan’s words from long ago.

*Form is just function. Only weak humans assign aesthetic meaning to it. Strength… is beauty.*

His face twisted. Bones cracked and reshaped. His eyes slanted, his nose jutted forward like a beast’s, his lips splitting wide beneath his ears.

Flames flickered in his serpentine eyes, warping the air around him like a heat haze.

Then, a memory vision activated.

Shirone found herself standing in a laboratory.

Inside the central Physics Machine lay the preserved corpses of five legendary beings:

Akamai of Antithese,

Kuzen of Feeder,

Kenser of Infinite Cell Proliferator,

Galtomic of Jinmai Plant,

Ringer of Carapace Mineral.

“These,” Raysis said, “are the keys to my ascension.”

Shirone narrowed her eyes. “You think combining them makes you a god?”

“For living beings, mortality isn’t inevitable. Death is merely a chosen function. For a perfect individual, it can be erased. With the final two slots—*you* and the angel—I will merge them all. And I will become the god of this world.”

“You’re insane.”

Hatred burned in Fleur’s gaze. She had witnessed Raysis’s cloning empire from the beginning.

“Hmph. You’re nothing but waste—excrement that can’t even integrate into my body. Believe what you want. Just wait to die.”

With that, Raysis left.

Soon, researchers would extract Shirone and Ikasa and throw them into the Fuzix Machine.

The Anecdotal Sake, perfected by Ankera, had no flaws.

Even Ikasa’s angelic body, mixed with plasma, would dissolve into black liquid.

Fleur strained with all her strength, but Jin Akamai’s restraint was absolute. She couldn’t move a single finger.

“This is bad,” she whispered. “If we don’t act, we’re all dead.”

Ikasa laughed bitterly. “An ephemera speaking of death. How amusing.”

“Haha, is it?” Fleur shot back. “Tell me, do angels even fear death? I’d regret vanishing like this.”

Ikasa fell silent.

It was true. As long as the Akashic Records existed, true death wasn’t permanent. But *extinction*—erasure from existence—was one of the most dreaded fates, even for angels.

“There is a way,” Ikasa said suddenly.

“A way?” Shirone turned her head with difficulty.

The fact that Shirone could move at all under Jin Akamai’s restraint told Ikasa how powerful her incarnation truly was.

“Of course,” Ikasa said. “Do you really think a noble angel could be held by a mere artificial creature?”

Fleur pressed, “Then why suffer? What’s the method?”

Ikasa hesitated, then spoke.

“The Valhalla Action can destroy this creature. We reverse the process—draw the *result* of destruction first, then apply it.”

“That sounds strange,” Fleur said. “If you can do that, why are you trapped?”

Shirone answered, “Because the cost is too great.”

Ikasa didn’t deny it. “Correct. The binding force requires energy equivalent to *eight hours* of continuous output. If I destroy it, I’ll be incapacitated for eight hours.”

“Eight hours…” Fleur breathed.

Ikasa could level mountains in under thirty minutes. For her to pay such a price meant Jin Akamai was bound by something far beyond physical law.

Shirone understood Ikasa’s hesitation.

“I *will* save you,” she said firmly. “Just set us free.”

Ikasa scoffed. “And why should I believe a human?”

“Because if we stay here, we all die. You included.”

Ikasa’s expression twisted in irritation.

“And what changes if you escape? There are more of these creatures. You’ll just be caught again.”

“That’s where you’re wrong,” Fleur said. “Garas is being held in an underground lab. I’ll release him. To *them*, this place will become a garden of chaos.”

Ikasa paused, considering.

Garas—the King of Breeding. The most abhorrent creature she had ever known.

*Could we use him to eliminate Park Yo-jong? Not impossible… if we set him loose.*

“Ikasa.”

Shirone, still standing through sheer will, stepped closer and met Ikasa’s gaze.

“I *will* come back for you. Release us.”

The tenacity of human life—this was why Ankera had to control them. This was why Ikasa had fallen.

“Don’t compare me to an ephemera,” Ikasa snapped. “I’m not afraid of death. It just… annoys me.”

“But even for you…” Shirone began.

Ikasa cut her off. “Go. Just go.”

Shirone blinked in surprise.

Ikasa snorted. “Not because I trust you. There’s no other way. And… I’d find it satisfying to see a human who defied angels finally meet her end.”

“I *will* come back,” Shirone vowed. “Wait for me.”

Ikasa closed her eyes, offering no reply.

Outside, the planetary body expanded. The halo above began recharging the Valhalla Action.

*The binding force is stronger than I calculated.*

The system finalized its prediction: **9 hours and 43 seconds**.

That was the time it would take for Ikasa to destroy Jin Akamai using the full power of her incarnation.

*But catching a high-level angel afterward… that’ll be difficult.*

She almost laughed. If she hadn’t fallen, she wouldn’t be in this position.

*I never thought I’d have to rely on someone like *her*.*

Ikasa inhaled deeply.

Her eyes snapped open.

**Valhalla Action!**

“Kiiiii!”

Jin Akamai shrieked as its body convulsed.

The massive focal point shifted at light speed.

Its gelatinous form dried, cracked, and began to collapse.
