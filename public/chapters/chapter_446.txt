Chapter 446

Second Purgatory (3)

Gaold surged forward like a thief slipping between the gaps in the Mecha formation, his presence looming over them with cold arrogance. He stared down at the rebels and demanded, “Are you rebels? Where’s your base?”

One of the main reasons he’d come to the mainland was to locate the rebels’ central stronghold. If they’d just talk, it would save him a great deal of effort.

But the woman’s expression—previously pale with fear—shifted in an instant at the sound of his voice. She sprang to her feet, raised her arc weapon, and shouted, “It’s a military unit! Raise your arms, everyone! We die here today!”

At her command, the squad snapped into combat stances. Cigna and Xd stepped forward, their faces set with resolve, ready to fight to the death.

“Get out of here now!” the woman yelled. “Or you’ll become Ark’s next meal!”

A laser from Ark locked onto Gaold’s forehead—a Mecha ranged weapon capable of firing explosive orbs. But the woman already knew he wasn’t someone they could defeat.

*Since I’ve already risked everything,* she thought, *I might as well try blackmail.*

“Haha… kkkkkkkk…”

Gaold’s shoulders shook as a dry, wind-like laugh escaped him. Wrinkles creased his face, and when he lifted his head again, his eyes burned with a terrifying flame.

“You’re just babbling,” he said, voice low and venomous.

A wave of searing heat engulfed the Mecha squad. It didn’t matter if they were misunderstood or misjudged—Gaold wasn’t the kind of man who showed mercy to those foolish enough to challenge him.

The rebels collapsed into the inferno, their faces ashen, legs trembling, drool slipping from their lips. The sound of a demon’s scream tore through the air, so horrifying it made them want to claw out their own eardrums. This wasn’t fear a human mind could endure.

Even the woman, a seasoned squad leader who had faced death countless times, sat frozen, her chin quivering helplessly.

“Huh… stop… please…”

Only when the Mecha squad had completely lost their will to fight did Gaold release his grip on the oppressive aura.

The tear-streaked woman lifted her head and glared at him with bitter resentment. “Why are you attacking us? We’re fighting against Heaven. Aren’t you on the same side?”

“I don’t know what nonsense you’re spouting,” Gaold replied coldly. “And I don’t care whether you’re a military unit or not. But weren’t you the ones who called people like me ‘borderline scum’ and looked down on us?”

“That’s… that’s…”

Before she could retort, trees in the forest behind the Mecha squad began crashing down.

Everyone turned toward the sound.

Something massive was approaching at high speed—yet nothing could be seen.

– Release the magnetic field. Start the argon engine.

The forest shimmered, and an opaque boundary began to form. Then, from the distortion, a bipedal armored tank emerged—nearly four meters tall.

Its upper and lower body were balanced in a 1:1 ratio, giving it a solid, stable stance. Thick, powerful arms extended from short, reverse-jointed legs. Hydraulic cylinders pulsed in rhythmic cycles, synchronized with the deep growl of its engine.

– Enemy in sight. Preemptive strike authorized.

The machine lunged forward, palm outstretched.

Gaold stepped back, leaned forward, and slammed his fist into the ground. Soil erupted like an iron wall, blocking the charge.

The tank, struck by the wave of earth, staggered—its massive frame nearly toppling. But it quickly adjusted, digging its legs into the ground and scooping out nearly two tons of soil to regain balance.

Meanwhile, Shirone and the others spread out, maintaining optimal combat distances. Shirone activated his Diamond Arms and pulled his hood up, eyes scanning the battlefield.

*What is that thing?*

Every time the bipedal tank’s waist twisted smoothly, like oil on water, the upper cockpit remained locked forward. The cockpit itself was an oval glass sphere, glowing with a faint, electrified purple sheen—impossible to see inside.

“Guroi,” Kang Nan said, voice tight. “It’s the second-stage equipment of the Mecha combat system.”

Gaold narrowed his eyes. “Hmph. The real thing is far better than I expected.”

From documents alone, he’d assumed it was just a walking pile of scrap. But in actual combat, it was clearly a highly mobile tactical unit, especially in skilled hands.

The way it distorted magnetic fields to erase its presence was cutting-edge technology—something not even recent documents had detailed.

The Guroi’s upper body swiveled toward the Mecha squad.

“Is Squad 2 intact? What about Kergoin?”

The voice from the speaker was neutral, but the tone suggested a man.

“The Kergos were wiped out. But we’re still standing.”

The Guroi’s cockpit turned toward Gaold.

“Who are they?”

The woman hesitated, choosing her words carefully. The bearded man had clearly denied being part of any military unit. But the power he’d displayed was a form of intangible violence—something that could only come from blind hatred.

It was so terrifying she’d rather go mad than face that hell again.

“They attacked us,” she said simply.

The Guroi issued orders through internal channels.

– Sweep them out. Switch to strike mode. Increase argon engine output to level 5.

Chunk! Chunk! Chunk!

All five advancing Guroi units simultaneously transformed their hands. Fingers folded outward, revealing massive gun barrels lined with black holes.

– Open fire!

Can-can-can-can-can!

A deafening roar erupted as hundreds of kilogram-weight iron cylinders, each with a spherical core, were fired with tremendous force.

Heavy bullets rained down at three hundred rounds per minute, shattering rocks and snapping trees.

The Guroi units turned their fire toward Gaold.

The black bullets streaked toward him—stopping abruptly thirty centimeters in front of his face, as if hitting an invisible wall. They slowed, then hovered, unable to penetrate.

Puff! Fluff!

The bullets piled up, forming a dense barrier of black metal.

“Hmm. Iron balls,” Gaold murmured.

He waited until the bullets lost their spin, then plucked one from the air with his bare hand.

Inside the Guroi units, confusion rippled through the pilots.

– Captain! He caught the bullet with his hand!

– Keep firing! They’re borderline-class threats! Don’t underestimate them based on past experience!

Gaold casually weighed the bullet in his palm, then flicked it upward with his thumb.

“Feels like a toy air gun. Might sting a bit.”

Kang Nan, tears in his eyes, muttered, “If you’re going to do it, just do it. Or are you just showing off?”

Gaold caught the falling bullet.

“Here,” he said, tossing it to Kang Nan. “Try it yourself.”

Kang Nan clenched his teeth at the absurdity but didn’t dare defy his commander.

“Can you really take it without getting hurt?” he asked, turning to Shirone.

Shirone’s eyes began to glow. “Counting. About one minute.”

Kang Nan glanced at the battlefield, anxiety tightening his chest. One minute might be all it took for the Guroi units to be destroyed.

“Damn it! Who the hell are these people?”

The pilot of Guroi Unit 3 was about to die.

The AMOLED Vision in his cockpit displayed seven split screens covering a full 360-degree front view—but he couldn’t track the swordsman. The man seemed to leap between screens, defying perception.

*There are too many strange things in this world.*

Shirone slashed across the Guroi’s frame. Each strike split the iron plating like flesh, sending sparks flying.

*Is the armor thicker than it looks? Where did this guy even come from?*

When Shirone accidentally severed an engine artery, Unit 3 lost balance and stumbled.

“Keugh! You bastard!”

The pilot raised his arms, struggling to stabilize.

The Guroi, as the second stage of the Mecha combat system, could only operate when linked to the first stage—Piper. Since the Guroi’s control system connected directly to the pilot’s nervous system, only those with enhanced musculature could even move its limbs.

After a moment of struggle, the pilot regained stability and searched for Shirone.

But what he saw wasn’t Shirone—it was a beautiful woman with long brown hair and dark glasses.

“Cute bitch!”

Looks were deceiving. Every strike she landed sent violent tremors through the Guroi’s frame, echoing like thunder inside the cockpit.

“Alright! I’ll crush you to dust!”

The pilot roared and swung his fist.

The Guroi’s waist twisted, launching the thick gun barrel toward Etella.

Whoops!

The pilot’s blood ran cold—he realized too late his back was exposed.

He checked the multi-vision display just in time to see the woman blocking the gun barrel with her elbow, raised to her face.

“What? How? One arm alone generates 17 horsepower!”

“Whoa.”

Etella exhaled slowly, calming her muscles, then shifted her stance.

Yin-Yang Wave Fist – Wall Mount.

She struck the Guroi’s leg with a spear-like thrust. A wave of force rang through the metal, and a deafening roar exploded in the pilot’s ears.

“Aaaagh!”

Instinctively, he clutched his ear—causing the Guroi’s arm to follow, throwing it off balance. The machine collapsed forward.

– Damn it! What the hell is that big-breasted girl?! She’s not human! She’s a monster!

– Focus on the swordsman! He’s targeting the engine arteries! Prioritize defense!

“No, I’m asking—*who do I attack first?!*”

Kanya, spinning around the Guroi’s waist, shouted in frustration.

It didn’t matter. Bang! Bang! Bang!

A shockwave hit the machine, slamming Kanya’s one eye shut.

She forced the other open and saw it—a tentacle monster darting through the forest, its limbs embedded in trees.

“That damned monster!”

Can-can-can-can-can!

The Guroi’s bullets tore through the surrounding forest, obliterating every tree in the monster’s vicinity.

But the creature kept fighting, unleashing relentless beams of light.

Empty! Empty!

Each impact sent shockwaves through the multi-ton Guroi, fully transmitted to Kanya through the Piper link.

“This is unbearable!”

Wasting ammunition on a monster wasn’t the worst part. With rebel supplies dwindling due to munitions blockades, only the most efficient pilots were selected. That’s why Kanya—the Ark’s top sharpshooter—had earned her place in the Guroi.

“Why does this monster… have to look like *him*?”

What tormented her most was that the monster’s abilities reminded her of someone she missed.

The incident where Shirone stopped Ilhwa from drinking hadn’t just saved Kanya’s mother. The ripple of that moment had grown into a typhoon—becoming the foundational belief of the rebel movement.

Seeing those powers now, tied to such a precious memory, filled her with disgust.

“No matter how many bullets it takes—I *will* kill you!”

As the forest around Shirone was reduced to rubble, his room to maneuver shrank drastically.

Realizing a prolonged fight was hopeless, Shirone pulled back his robe and began casting magic in earnest.

Light gathered in the center of the Spirit Zone, coalescing like a retina, then fired as a single red beam—straight at the Guroi’s leg.

Destroying the torso would be ideal, but the leg’s drive joints were better targets during close combat.

– Warning! Heat detected inside unit! Activating cooling system! Output limit exceeded!

“What? What now?”

Kanya stared at the instrument panel in disbelief.

On the display showing the Guroi’s front view, the left leg flashed red.

– Excessive energy output! Energy overload!

The output needle climbed—240 percent… 370 percent… 600 percent.

With a loud crack, the knee-side drive joint shattered.

“Kyaaak!”

The Guroi’s body tilted violently. The boarding seat slammed into the ground, the impact rattling Kanya’s bones. But she clung to the control stick until the very end.

“Die, monster… yes?”

The distorted AMOLED vision cleared, and the targeting system automatically locked onto the muzzle’s aim point.

“What… what?”

Kanya’s breath caught.

The face of a boy who had haunted her dreams filled the screen—magnified, undeniable.
