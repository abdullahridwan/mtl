Chapter 446  
Second Purgatory (3)  
~*~

Gaold slipped between the Mecha squad members like a thief, looking down at them with an air of superiority. “Are you rebels? Where is your headquarters?”

Finding a key rebel base on the mainland was one of their primary objectives, so if he could just get them to talk, it would save them a great deal of trouble.

But unexpectedly, the woman’s expression shifted dramatically the moment she heard Gaold’s voice. The fear that had been on her face just seconds ago vanished. She jumped to her feet, raised her bow, and shouted, “This is a military community! Raise your arms, everyone! We die here today!”

At the squad leader’s command, the rest of the unit immediately fell into a combat stance. Cigna and Xd stood at the forefront, their faces etched with a resolve that bordered on suicidal.

“Get out of here right now! Otherwise, you’ll become Ark’s food.”

The woman’s laser rifle was already trained on Gaold’s forehead. She knew he was far beyond her squad’s capabilities.

‘Since I’ve already risked my life, I might as well try to blackmail him.’

“Hah. Kkkkkkkkk.”

Gaold threw his head back and laughed, his shoulders shaking as deep wrinkles carved across his face. When he finally raised his head again, his eyes burned with a terrifying, unyielding flame.

“You’re babbling annoyingly.”

A scorching, hellish heat suddenly engulfed the Mecha squad.

It didn’t matter if they were misunderstood or not. Gaold was no saint who would look kindly on those who dared to fight him. Every member of the squad who fell under his superheated pressure turned pale, their legs trembling as they drooled in sheer terror. The demon’s roar was so horrifying that it felt like it would tear their eardrums apart. It was a fear no ordinary human could withstand.

Even the woman, who had survived multiple life-and-death crises as a squad leader, could only sit helplessly, her chin shaking uncontrollably.

“Huh. Please… stop…”

It was only after the Mecha squad completely lost their will to fight that Gaold finally dropped his pressure.

The tear-stained woman lifted her head and glared at him with resentment. “Why are you bothering us? We’re fighting against Heaven. Aren’t you guys on the same side?”

“I don’t know what nonsense you’re spouting, but I don’t care if you’re a military community or not. And weren’t you the ones who called borderline people ‘mojo people’ and looked down on them long before this?”

“Th-that…”

Just as the woman opened her mouth to retort, a series of heavy crashes echoed from the forest behind the Mecha squad.

Everyone’s eyes snapped toward the sound.

Something massive was approaching at high speed, though it remained hidden in the shadows.

– Release the magnetic field. Start the argon engine.

As the forest landscape shimmered, an opaque boundary began to materialize. What finally emerged from the distortion was a bipedal armored tank standing nearly four meters tall. Its upper and lower body were in a perfect 1:1 ratio, giving it a stable, imposing silhouette. It possessed relatively long, thick arms attached to short, reverse-jointed legs. Hydraulic cylinders pumped in synchronized rhythm with the deep thrum of the engine.

– Enemy in sight. Preemptive strikes are allowed.

The mech surged forward, its four-fingered palm outstretched.

Gaold stepped back, leaned forward, and slammed his fist into the ground. The soil erupted upward, forming an iron wall. The mech, struck by the pouring earth, seemed too heavy to stand on its own, but it continued to drive its legs forward, scooping up roughly two tons of dirt to regain its center of gravity.

Meanwhile, Shirone and the others spread out, maintaining their respective combat distances. Shirone activated the Diamond Arms and pulled a hood over his head to keep a close eye on the situation.

‘What is that?’

Every time the bipedal mech’s waist twisted with fluid, oil-like grace, the cockpit ahead tilted forward. The cockpit was an oval glass dome with an electrified purple sheen, making it impossible to see inside.

“Guroi. It is the second stage equipment of the Mecha combat system.”

Gaold narrowed his eyes at Kang’s explanation.

“Oh, the real thing is definitely good.”

When he had only seen the specifications on paper, he had assumed it was just a walking lump of scrap metal. But in actual combat, it proved to be a highly mobile unit that could be used tactically depending on the pilot’s skill. In particular, the method of distorting the magnetic field to erase its presence was a state-of-the-art technology that hadn’t been documented until recently.

The upper section of the Kuroi spun, turning its attention toward the Mecha squad.

“Is everyone in Squad 2 all right? What about Kergoin?”

The voice transmitted through the speaker was neutral, but the tone suggested a male.

“The Kergos were annihilated. But not us.”

The Kuroi’s cockpit turned back toward Gaold.

“Who are they?”

The woman hesitated for a moment. The bearded man had clearly stated they weren’t a military community. Yet the sheer, intangible violence he had displayed was something she couldn’t ignore without blind hostility. It was to the point where she thought she’d rather go mad than return to that kind of hell.

“The enemy attacked us.”

The Kuroi issued commands through its internal channels.

– Sweep them out. Convert to strike mode. Raise the argon engine gear to level 5.

Chunk! Chunk! Chunk!

The five advancing Kuroi units simultaneously changed the shape of their hands. Four fingers folded outward, and a massive gun barrel with black holes protruded from within.

– Open fire!

Rat-tat-tat-tat-tat-tat!

A deafening roar erupted as hundreds of kilograms of iron cylinders swayed with tremendous force. Heavy bullets ricocheted at three hundred rounds per minute, shattering rocks and snapping trees in their path.

The Kuroi units shifted their aim toward Gaold.

The black bullets flew rapidly, stopping just thirty centimeters in front of him before slowing down as if buried in an invisible medium.

Puff! Fluffy!

Bullets that failed to penetrate the air shield gathered together, forming a dense black metal barrier.

“Hmm, it’s an iron ball.”

Gaold waited until the bullets lost their rotation before catching one in his hand.

The confusion of the Kuroi unit was transmitted through the shaking of the aircraft.

– Hey, Platoon Leader! I caught the bullet with my hand!

– Keep shooting! They are presumed to be borderline! Don’t judge your enemies by your experience so far!

Gaold weighed the bullet in his palm, gave a thumbs-up, and tossed it upward.

“Something like an air gun. It will hurt quite a bit.”

Kang said, tears welling in her eyes.

“If you’re going to do it, don’t do it. Or am I?”

Gaold caught the falling bullet.

“Take it. I’ll have to try it myself.”

Kang gritted her teeth at the nonsense, but she had no intention of overturning the commander’s decision.

“Is that so? Can you take it without damage?”

When Kang looked back at Sein and asked, her eyes narrowed sharply.

“It is counting. It will take about a minute.”

Kang glanced anxiously at the battlefield. It felt like one minute would be more than enough time to finish off all five units.

“Damn it! Who the hell are these people?”

The pilot of Kuroi Unit 3 was ready to die for the cause.

The AMOLED Vision installed in front of the cockpit featured seven split screens covering a 360-degree field, but it was difficult to track the man with the sword as if he were jumping between the screens.

‘There are many strange things in this world.’

Quan slashed through the Kuroi repeatedly.

Every time his sword struck, the iron plating split open like flesh, sending sparks flying.

‘Is it thicker than armor? Where did people hear it?’

When an engine artery was accidentally severed, Unit 3 lost its balance and stumbled.

“Keugh! This bastard!”

The pilot raised his arms to regain his footing.

The Kuroi, which corresponds to the second stage of the mecha combat system, can only operate when it meets the first stage. The first stage is none other than Piper, and since the Kuroi’s operating organs are connected to each part of the pilot’s body, those who have not strengthened their muscles cannot easily move even one arm.

The pilot struggled with his limbs until he finally found a sense of stability, then searched for Quan’s location.

But what caught his eye was not Quan, but a cute woman with long brown hair and dark glasses.

“Cute bitch!”

Contrary to her appearance, she was a martial artist. Every time she landed a blow, the Kuroi’s body shook and a roar echoed inside the cockpit.

“OK! I will crush you to the bone!”

The pilot gritted his teeth and swung his fist.

As the Kuroi’s waist accelerated, the thick gun barrel swung toward Etella.

Whoops!

The pilot turned pale as he realized he had left his back completely exposed.

When he checked the multi-vision display belatedly, he saw the woman blocking the Kuroi’s gun barrel with her elbow raised to her face.

“Uh, how? One arm alone has 17 horsepower.”

“Whoa.”

Taking a deep breath and calming her arm muscles, Etella turned toward the Guroi.

Yin-Yang Wave Fist – Wall Mount.

When the Kuroi’s leg was struck with a spear-like force, the iron rang out along with the shockwaves, and a deafening roar slammed into the pilot’s eardrums.

“Aaaagh!”

Unconsciously, he grabbed his ear. The Kuroi’s arms followed suit, collapsing forward.

– Damn it! What to do with that big-breasted girl! Not human! It’s a monster!

– This way first! Only the engine artery is being picked and cut! Take care of the inspection!

“No, I’m asking who to catch first!”

Kanya, who looked left and right while turning around the Kuroi’s waist, shouted with an annoyed expression.

Either way, it wasn’t a fuss.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

Kanya’s left eye closed from the shock transmitted to the aircraft.

Checking her vision with her right eye, she saw a tentacle monster flying through the forest, its tentacles embedded deep in the trees.

“That damned monster!”

Rat-tat-tat-tat-tat-tat!

The Kuroi’s bullets tore through all the trees in the area surrounding the tentacle monster.

In the meantime, the monster continued to fight, shooting beams of light without pause.

Empty! Empty!

Every time the light struck, dozens of tons of Kuroi shook, and the shock was fully transmitted to Kanya, who was connected to the Piper.

“That really sucks!”

Wasting ammunition to catch a monster wasn’t the only problem.

As the rebels’ weapons dwindled day by day due to the interference of the munitions community, pilots were selected only if they could perform strikes as efficiently as possible. It was the reason why she, the sharpshooter of the Ark, was able to board the Kuroi.

“Why such a monster… … !”

What annoyed her more than anything was that the monster’s ability closely resembled the magic of a person she missed.

The incident in which Shirone stopped Ilhwa from drinking did not end with simply saving Kanya’s mother.

The wind he left behind had now become a huge typhoon and had become the conviction of the first principle of the rebels.

It was terribly unpleasant to recall memories with such a precious person by seeing that monster.

“No matter how many bullets it costs, I will definitely kill you!”

As all the trees around it were shattered, Shirone’s range of movement became extremely narrow.

Realizing there was no chance of winning in a long-term battle, Shirone pulled back his robe and began casting magic in earnest.

Like a retina, the light gathered in the center along the Spirit Zone, condensed into a single red ray, and shot directly at the Kuroi’s leg.

Exploding the torso would be the most obvious target, but the drive joints on the legs were far more suitable for striking during engagements.

– Warning! Detect heat inside the aircraft! Cooling system on! Output limit exceeded!

“No, what are you talking about again?”

Kanya checked the instrument panel with wide eyes.

On the interface displaying the Kuroi’s front view, the left leg was flashing red.

– Excessive energy output! Energy overload!

The output reading on the instrument panel climbed endlessly.

At 240 percent, 370 percent, and finally 600 percent, the drive joint on the knee side cracked with a loud bang.

“Kyaaak!”

As the Kuroi’s body tilted, the boarding seat slammed to the ground.

The shock felt like falling from a building, making her bones tremble, but Kanya refused to let go of the control stick until the very end.

“Die, monster… … yes?”

As the noisy AMOLED Vision cleared, the focus automatically locked onto the target at the point where the muzzle was aimed.

“What, what?”

Kanya doubted her own eyes.

The face of a boy who had appeared even in her dreams was greatly magnified in the visor.
