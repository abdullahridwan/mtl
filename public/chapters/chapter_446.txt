Second Purgatory (3)

Gaold, having broken into the gap between the Mechas like a robber, looked down on them with open arrogance.

"Are you rebels? Where's your headquarters?"

One of the purposes of coming to the mainland was to locate a key rebel base, and if these people knew anything, it would save a great deal of trouble.

But instead of answering, the woman — whose face had been frozen with fear only a moment before — changed expression the instant she heard Gaold's voice. She sprang up, raised her Arc, and shouted.

"It's the military community! Everyone, raise your weapons! We die here today!"

At her command, the squad fell into fighting stances. Cigna and Xd stood at the front, their faces set with the resolve of people prepared to die.

"Get out of here now, or you'll become food for the Ark!"

The Ark's muzzle — a Mecha ranged weapon that fires explosive orbs — swung around to aim squarely at Gaold's forehead.

But the woman knew, even as she threatened him, that he was not an opponent she could actually defeat.

[Since I've already risked my life, all that's left is to try bluffing.]

"Ha. Kh-kh-kh-kh."

Gaold's shoulders shook, wrinkles spreading across his face as though a gust of wind had filled his lungs. When he lifted his head again, his eyes burned with a flame unlike anything the Mecha soldiers had ever seen.

"You dare babble at me."

A wave of scorching heat, like hell itself, swept over the Mecha unit. It made no difference to Gaold whether they'd misunderstood him or not — he was not the sort of man who looked kindly on people who dared raise arms against him without fear.

Caught in that superheated hell, every member of the unit went pale, legs trembling, drool slipping from their mouths as they heard something like a demon's scream tearing through their ears. It was not a kind of fear a human being was built to endure.

Even the squad leader — a woman who had faced death more than once in her career — ended up slumped on the ground, her chin shaking uncontrollably.

"Please... stop..."

Only once the Mecha soldiers had completely lost the will to fight did Gaold release his hold. The woman, tears streaking her face, lifted her head and glared at him with resentment.

"Why are you doing this to us? We're fighting against Heaven. Aren't you the same?"

"I don't know what nonsense you're spouting — military community, whatever. But weren't you people the ones calling the borderline folk 'mojo' and looking down on them?"

"That... that's..."

Before the woman could answer, trees in the forest behind the Mecha unit began crashing down. Every eye turned toward the sound. Something huge was closing in at high speed, though nothing could yet be seen.

— Release the magnetic field. Start the argon engine.

The air over the forest shimmered, and an indistinct boundary began to take shape. What finally emerged was a bipedal armored war machine nearly four meters tall. Its upper and lower body were built in roughly equal proportion, giving it a stable, grounded look, and its short, reverse-jointed legs supported long, thick arms. Hydraulic cylinders pumped in staggered rhythm with the growl of its engine.

— Enemy sighted. Preemptive strike authorized.

The machine charged forward, its four-fingered hand outstretched. Gaold stepped back, leaned in, and slammed his palm into the ground — soil erupted upward like a wall of iron. The machine, struck by the surge of earth, should have toppled given its shape and weight, but it kept its legs moving, scooping roughly two tons of soil aside to recover its balance.

Meanwhile, Shirone and the others spread out, each keeping to their own preferred fighting distance. Shirone activated his Diamond Arms and pulled up his hood, watching the situation unfold.

[What is that thing?]

Every time the bipedal machine's torso rotated — smooth as oil sliding across water — the cockpit mounted above it swiveled to track its surroundings. The cockpit itself was an oval globe of glass, glowing with an electrified purple sheen that made it impossible to see inside.

"That's Kuroi. The second-stage equipment of the Mecha combat system," Kang Nan explained.

Gaold narrowed his eyes.

"Well. The real thing is something else."

Reading the specs on paper, he'd assumed it was nothing more than a lumbering pile of scrap metal. But encountering it in actual combat, it proved to be a machine with impressive mobility, capable of real tactical use depending on the pilot's skill. Its trick of bending the surrounding magnetic field to erase its presence, in particular, was cutting-edge technology that hadn't appeared in any document until recently.

Kuroi's upper body spun to face the Mecha soldiers.

"Is Squad 2 all right? What about the Kergoin unit?"

The voice through the speaker was neutral in tone, but from its cadence, likely male.

"The Kergos were wiped out. We're still standing."

Kuroi's cockpit turned back toward Gaold.

"And who are you?"

The woman hesitated over her words. The bearded man had made it clear he wasn't from the military community. But the horror he had just unleashed on them was nothing less than violence with no need for open hostility behind it — to the point that she thought she'd rather go mad than be dragged back through that hell again.

"They attacked us."

Kuroi issued orders over an internal channel.

— Clear them out. Switch to strike mode. Raise argon engine output to level five.

Chunk! Chunk! Chunk!

All five approaching Kuroi units reshaped their hands in unison. Four fingers folded outward as a massive black-bored gun barrel extended from within each palm.

— Open fire!

Ka-ka-ka-ka-ka-ka-ka!

A deafening roar erupted as the barrels — hundreds of kilograms of iron acting as improvised cannon — shook with tremendous recoil. Heavy rounds tore into rock and snapped trees apart at a rate of three hundred per minute.

One group of Kuroi turned its fire on Gaold. The black bullets streaked toward him — then, thirty centimeters from his body, abruptly slowed as though sinking into something unseen.

Puff! Fuff!

Round after round, failing to punch through his air shield, piled up into a dense black metal wall.

"Hm. Looks like a pile of iron balls."

Gaold waited until the rounds had bled off their spin, then plucked one out of the air with his bare hand. A wave of confusion rippled through the Kuroi squadron, transmitted through the shaking of their frames.

— Sir! He caught the bullet with his hand!

— Keep firing! They're likely borderline types! Don't judge the enemy by past experience!

Gaold weighed the bullet in his palm, then tossed it upward with a thumbs-up.

"Something like an air gun. Would hurt quite a bit, I imagine."

Kang Nan spoke, looking near tears.

"If you're going to do something, do it properly. Or should I?"

Gaold caught the falling round again.

"Here. I'll have a go at it myself."

Kang Nan gritted his teeth at the sheer nonsense of it but had no intention of overruling the commander's decision.

"Is that so? Can you take one without damage?"

Kang Nan glanced back at Sein and asked. Sein's Cheoryun Eyes began to turn.

"Calculating. Give it about a minute."

Kang Nan eyed the battlefield uneasily — a minute seemed like more than enough time for the five machines to finish them off.

"Damn it! Who the hell are these people?"

The pilot of Kuroi Unit 3 wanted to scream. The AMOLED display mounted before the cockpit split into seven panels covering a full 360 degrees around the machine, but even so, it was almost impossible to track the swordsman who seemed to leap between screens.

[There really are strange things in this world.]

The sword tore through Kuroi's plating again and again. Every cut peeled the iron open like flesh, sparks of electricity spitting from the wound.

[Is it thicker than the armor plating? Where did people even hear about that?]

When the engine artery was severed by accident, Unit 3 lost its balance and staggered.

"Gah! You bastard!"

The pilot flung out his arms to steady himself. Since Kuroi — the second stage of the Mecha combat system — could only function in tandem with the first stage, and its control apparatus was wired directly into every part of the pilot's body, someone without heavily reinforced muscles couldn't so much as lift one of its arms unaided.

Struggling with his own limbs, the pilot finally regained a sense of balance and searched for the swordsman. But what caught his eye wasn't the swordsman — it was a woman with long brown hair and dark glasses, striking blow after blow with bare fists, each impact rocking the Kuroi frame and setting off a roar inside the cockpit.

"Cute little bitch!"

"Fine! I'll grind you down to the bone!"

The pilot gritted his teeth and swung a fist. Kuroi's torso accelerated, driving its thick gun barrel toward Etella. The pilot went pale as he realized, too late, that his back was now exposed with no way to turn in time.

Checking the multi-vision feed a beat too late, he saw the woman raise her elbow to guard her face, blocking Kuroi's gun barrel outright.

"How — ! One arm alone puts out seventeen horsepower!"

"Hah."

Etella exhaled, steadying the muscles of her arm, then pivoted toward Kuroi's flank.

Yin-Yang Wave Fist — Wall Strike.

The blow landed on Kuroi's leg like a spear, the iron ringing with the shockwave, the resulting roar slamming into the pilot's eardrums.

"Aaaargh!"

Without thinking, he clapped his hands to his ears — and Kuroi's arms mirrored the motion, sending the whole machine toppling forward.

— Damn it! What do we do about that girl with the huge chest?! She's not human! She's a monster!

— Focus here first! Something's picking apart the engine artery piece by piece! Somebody check on it!

"No, I'm asking who to deal with first!"

Kanya, spinning around Kuroi's waist as she scanned left and right, shouted in frustration. Either way, it was chaos.

Bang! Bang! Bang!

The shock transmitted through the machine forced one of Kanya's eyes shut. Checking her display with the other, she saw a tentacled shape whipping through the forest, tentacles anchoring into trees as it swung along.

"That damn monster!"

Ka-ka-ka-ka-ka-ka-ka!

Kuroi's rounds shredded every tree in the surrounding area as the tentacled creature moved. All the while, it kept firing bursts of light without pause.

Boom! Boom!

Every hit sent tens of tons of Kuroi shuddering, the shock traveling straight through the pilot's link to Kanya's own body.

"This really sucks!"

Wasting ammunition just to pin down one monster wasn't the only problem. With the rebels' weapons dwindling day by day thanks to interference from the munitions community, pilots were chosen strictly for their efficiency in combat — which was precisely why she, the Ark's sharpshooter, had been given a seat in Kuroi.

"Why does a monster like this even exist...!"

What bothered her more than anything was that this creature's power resembled the magic of someone she missed dearly. The incident where Shirone had stopped Ilhwa from drinking hadn't ended simply with saving Kanya's mother — the ripple he'd left behind had since grown into a typhoon, becoming something close to a founding principle for the rebels. Seeing a monster wielding magic that echoed his only made the memory of someone so precious to her unbearably painful.

"No matter how many rounds it costs, I will kill you!"

With every tree around it reduced to splinters, Shirone found his range of movement growing dangerously narrow. Realizing there was no chance of winning a drawn-out fight, he pulled back his robe and began casting in earnest. Light gathered at the center of his Spirit Zone like light collecting on a retina, condensing into a single red beam that struck Kuroi's leg — rather than the torso, which would have been the obvious target, since the drive joint in the leg was better suited to disable the machine mid-engagement.

— Warning! Heat detected inside the frame! Activating cooling system! Output limit exceeded!

"What? What's it saying now?"

Kanya's eyes went wide as she scanned the instrument panel. On the display showing Kuroi's forward view, the left leg was flashing red.

— Excessive energy output! Energy overload!

The output reading climbed without stopping — 240 percent, 370 percent, and finally 600 percent — before the drive joint at the knee cracked apart with a snap.

"Kyaaah!"

Kuroi's body tipped, and the pilot's seat slammed down toward the ground. The impact rattled her bones as if she'd fallen from a building, but Kanya never let go of the control stick.

"Die, you monster... huh?"

As the noisy AMOLED display sharpened into focus, the targeting reticle automatically locked onto whatever lay at the end of the barrel.

"What — what is this?"

Kanya doubted her own eyes. The face of the boy who had haunted even her dreams now filled her vision, magnified in front of her.
