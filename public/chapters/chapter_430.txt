Chapter 430  
The Rapidly Changing Situation (4)  
~*~

Kashan Empire.

The coronation ceremony of the emperor of the Kashan Empire, which controlled one-third of the human world's territory and had conquered the continent's northwestern region, was approaching in an hour.

The succession process, as always, was informal and clandestine.

In the spire on the twenty-third floor of the massive Aganos palace, only two Theraze shared the same memories, along with a handful of others privy to their private secrets.

The transfer of power from Theraze Mystra to Theraze Uorin.

To the outside world, it appeared to be a standard succession ceremony in which a parent passed their place to their child, but those in the know understood the truth. The only thing that had changed was the name.

Theraze was a proper noun for an entity that had lived through the ages, repeating itself endlessly.

“With this, the handover of the temple is complete. Well, if the crusade is over, it should be considered finished.”

Fourteen hours ago, Uorin had been returning from a visit to the World Chamber in the Central Continent. There, she had been introduced to the temple, and within an hour, she had ascended to the rank of the Three Emperors.

Theraze’s influence ran deep, even within the Holy Temple, the world’s strongest superpower. Beneath the Three Emperors sat the Chilwangseong, the kings of the seven most powerful nations on the continent, and King Lee, the head of the fairies and dragons. It was no exaggeration to say that these twelve monarchs who belonged to the Holy Temple stood at the absolute peak of the world.

In an instant, Uorin had risen from a mere princess of Kazura to a ruler of the world, yet her expression held no joy. After all, everything was merely a subset of the name Theraze.

“I don’t need any rubbish. I’ve already verified that there is no discrepancy between your memories and mine. But I have a question.”

Uorin stopped walking and looked back at Mystra.

“Why are you so fast? I thought it would take another four years for me to ascend the throne.”

Because the memories of mother and daughter were shared, they were two yet one. Therefore, the discrepancy in their thoughts was an issue that had to be addressed.

“Hmm, that’s the point…”

Mystra turned to face Uorin.

While Mystra had black hair and sharp eyes, Uorin possessed white hair and a translucent, ethereal gaze. Neither her husband, Orkamp, nor Mystra herself had gray hair. Hence, the mutation.

It was ‘herself’ infused with ‘Future Poetry,’ an ability extremely rare among the Theraze’s signature power, ‘History Search.’

Perhaps among the many daughters, it had always been an established fact that Uorin would become the emperor. However, today’s enthronement was an irregular undertaking that Mystra herself had not calculated.

“Do not worry about it. Extinction will surely see it through. I simply came to Aganos because there were matters I needed to pass on, drawn from the memories I held after you were born.”

Since Uorin had ascended to the Realm of the Three Emperors, there was no longer a reason for Mystra to exist in this world. Yet she still had work to finish.

This was precisely why the succession had been accelerated by several years, and why she had pushed events forward so rapidly, even going so far as to accuse Gaold of treason.

Mystra entered the room and retrieved a locked box from a desk drawer. She handed it to Uorin, who was waiting on the sofa, and sat across from her, crossing her legs.

“This box contains all the answers to your questions. You may handle it, but it holds a secret of the current era that no one else knows. It will be a powerful weapon if used well, but given the circumstances, I had no choice but to act first.”

“How do I open it? You won’t be able to break it.”

“The key is with me. I can’t show it to you yet. Before that, I have someone to introduce you to.”

A knock sounded just in time.

Only a handful of people in the world had the right to enter the twenty-third floor of the Aganos palace, and Mystra immediately granted them entry.

A tall man stepped inside. His thin eyes carried a sharp impression, and his purple hair was swept back in a severe style, with only a single strand falling forward.

Former Tormia Magic Association Guard Captain Gando.

He was the firstborn son of Theraze Mystra, making him biologically Uorin’s older brother. However, from a psychoanalytic perspective, he was simply a son. This was why bloodline genealogy held no meaning for the Theraze.

Gando bowed his head toward the ambiguous position of his two mothers.

“Did you call, Mother?”

“Come this way. You did well on this one.”

To Gando, who had been adopted into Tormia immediately after birth and raised by caretakers, the Tormia Magic Association had been his entire world.

But all of it had been a lie.

Even joining Gaold’s bodyguard detail had been a false life orchestrated by his mother.

In a life built on deception, the only sincerity Gando had ever found was his awe for Gaold.

In the end, however, he had helped frame Gaold for treason, fled, and now stood in his mother’s arms.

“Oh, what’s wrong with your expression? Could it be because of Gaold? Did you develop a private mind, of all things?”

“The only person dear to me is my mother. But… as a mage, I respect his accomplishments.”

“Sigh. Gando, you are a Theraze’s son. You don’t need to respect the president of the Magic Association.”

Gando shut his mouth.

A member of the Three Emperors would be fully qualified to say such a thing. But he, who had spent most of his life in Tormia, still couldn’t tell which was the truth.

Uorin looked at Gando and nodded.

“You are definitely different from Zion. I got it right this time.”

“Whoops, right? I didn’t expect you to be my son. Since you have talent, would you like to develop it into a wizard? You can keep it close and use it if you want.”

Gando lowered his head.

*Did I pull it out correctly? Is it growing well?*

To the Theraze, what kind of existence was he?

A human? A creature? Or if not, was he merely a cogwheel that must be turned for the rest of its life, stuck in the clock?

“Keep it close? Hmmm. I’m satisfied with that arrangement. Gando, come this way.”

Gando approached Uorin like a slave sold in a market. Every time her gaze swept over him, it felt as though his very soul was being stripped bare.

A chill ran down his spine.

Today, his biological mother, Mystra, would disappear. After being abandoned by Uorin, the only thing left for him was death.

“Yeah, what… Shall we take him in for a while? I have to keep an eye on Kashan for the time being anyway.”

“Thank you, Mother.”

Gando bowed deeply. His eyes wandered as he looked down at the floor.

*He will die.*

*If Uorin is Mystra’s incarnation, don’t believe any word that comes out of her mouth.*

“Instead, there are two conditions.”

Uorin burst into laughter. The fact that he was putting forward conditions to a Theraze, a member of the Three Emperors, meant he wanted to live that badly.

“Tell me. If you can listen and listen…”

“Please do not interfere with Kashan’s independence.”

It was meant to be recognized as an independent institution. If he survived and had to assist Uorin, it would have been a natural move.

“Good. Second is?”

“Do not force me to criticize my mother. I am just a warrior blindly fighting for her.”

It was a plea to be treated like a dog. Desperate, but with a will that refused to cross the line into total servility.

Perhaps it was also a cool-headed way to carve out a path as a mage, but Uorin didn’t care.

There was nothing wrong with raising a puppy.

“You’ve saved your life, Gando.”

“I am grateful to my mother for her kindness.”

Mystra waved her hand dismissively.

“Stop going out. Because there is work left.”

Perhaps this was Gando and Mystra’s last meeting, but he couldn’t sense any maternal warmth in her tone.

When Gando left the room, Mystra took the key from her bosom as if the time had finally come.

“The reason for inheriting the throne faster than planned is to create a variable. Gaold will go to heaven and do something. It will help us in the end.”

“Do you think he can make it? A twenty-year plan went awry at once.”

“It doesn’t matter whether he succeeds or not. In the end, it doesn’t matter that something changes. Humans gamble on low odds because the rewards of winning outweigh the frustration of losing. I just gave it to Gaold.”

“I still don’t understand. I don’t know what you’re thinking. How could this be?”

“Of course. So you have to open the box. There, the deja vu regarding the ‘underlying incident’ that only I know about is recorded. If you read it, you’ll know exactly what I’m thinking.”

The Theraze possessed all memories from the beginning to the present, able to quickly search through vast amounts of data with their unique ability, ‘History Search.’

At first, it was merely a simple data search.

However, as the Guffin Reset occurred, this ability underwent an explosive evolution. In other words, as a new history was created after the reset, a ‘base event’ was formed beneath the existing timeline.

It was a form of future foresight born when two different timelines sharing the same foundation were contrasted through deja vu.

If it was Uorin, who had added ‘Future Poetry’ to it, conquering heaven in her generation would not be just a dream.

“The reason I hid it until the end was because I thought subjective judgment would interfere with the History Search if we exchanged opinions. See for yourself and judge for yourself. After all, I am you, and you are me.”

Mystra walked to her desk. At the moment she handed over the key, her life and her mission were over.

Uorin asked, “What are you going to do with it?”

“It doesn’t matter whatsoever. Just neatly.”

Mystra held up the sword lying on the desk. The blade, drawn with a sharp hiss, scattered light across the room.

Uorin nodded and inserted the key into the box. Meanwhile, Mystra gripped the hilt, lifted her head, and placed the blade at the nape of her neck.

She blinked, staring at the ceiling, then suddenly remembered herself and turned to Uorin.

“Right, you said you were eating Shirone, right? Are your thoughts still the same?”

Uorin opened the box and shrugged.

“Ataraxia. You’re remarkably composed, aren’t you? The rest doesn’t seem particularly important. Why, is there even an element of anxiety?”

“No. Yes, then.”

Mystra raised her head again.

The angle was slightly off, but that hardly mattered. The long history of the Theraze was not weak enough to be swayed by a mere boy’s hesitation.

The moment Uorin opened the box, Mystra’s sword moved sharply, and a fountain of blood gushed out. After a moment, there was a heavy thud as Mystra collapsed.

Uorin didn’t even pay attention. Her only interest was the information inside the box.

The box contained documents on two agendas.

The title on the cover of the first document was ‘Guffin Expungement’.

Because the Reset was estimated to have occurred nineteen years ago, Uorin, who was born after that event, had a vaguer background than Mystra. However, it was not particularly important information, as it was currently an issue identified not only by the temple but also by major organizations in each country.

Uorin set down the first document, which had become a relic of the old era, and took out a new document lying beneath it.

*This is it…*

The hand that had reached for a coffee cup stopped, then returned to grasp the document with both hands. For a long time, she savored the title written in the center of the page.

Anchera Eradication

Uorin quickly turned the page. Memorization first, analysis second.

After memorizing all two hundred pages, down to the smallest detail, in five minutes, she took a candlestick and burned the document.

Uorin’s eyes, staring into the flames, were fiercer than before. She finally realized what Mystra had wanted to convey.

*You were right, Mystra.*

Gando knocked on the door and entered. He paused for a moment at the completely different scene from when he left, but soon returned to a calm expression and lowered his head.

“Mother, it is time to celebrate the coronation.”

“I get it. Don’t leave now.”

Gando turned his head toward Mystra, who had fallen to the floor.

She had cut her throat with a sword. One could tell without looking that it was a clean, undisturbed cut.

“What about the corpse?”

“Take it away.”

Uorin replied briefly, as if telling him not to interrupt her thoughts.

Gando quietly removed the body.

It was the mother who had given birth to him. But now, Mystra was Uorin. She was an existence that could not be treated with human common sense. That was the Theraze.

Uorin left her seat and strode out into the hallway.

At the end of the twenty-third floor tower, there was a balcony overlooking the square where hundreds of thousands of imperial citizens had gathered.

When she pulled back the curtains and stepped onto the balcony, a huge crowd stretched all the way to the horizon.

A massive shout erupted from the ground where they had gathered for the Theraze. The heavens and earth shook, and the heat rose to the skies.

“Long live Her Majesty! Long live Her Majesty the Empress! Long live Her Majesty!”

Uorin raised her hand. Then, the volume she thought could not possibly rise any higher doubled, paralyzing her hearing.

The whole world lay beneath her feet, but Uorin simply looked down with a cold gaze.

Humans were unique creatures, possessing as many personalities as there were individuals, yet they were also a species that, when gathered together, bound themselves under the name of humanity.

A huge monster that was not someone, but simply a human being, was roaring at her.

*Shirone, there is no time. Meet Kariel. You have to change. Otherwise…*

Uorin looked up at the sky. Only the sun was higher than her.

*Humanity will perish.*
