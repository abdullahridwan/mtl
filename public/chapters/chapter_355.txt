Chapter 355  
Battle Mage’s Philosophy (1)  
~*~

There were many couples at the skating rink.

Shirone and Amy joined the stream of skaters circling the outer edge of the ice. A crisp winter wind blew over the rink, refreshing and clean. As Amy glided along behind the person in front of her, she was able to forget her worries for a little while.

But her good mood vanished the moment she saw Zocre and his group enter the rink.

They did not join the flow of people. Instead, they cut straight to the center and began showing off skating tricks.

It was not against the rules, but Amy disliked even that. Perhaps it was simply because she had already taken a dislike to him.

“What? Did he follow me all the way here?”

“He probably came to skate,” Shirone said. “Don’t mind him.”

Amy did not want to get tangled up with Zocre’s group. Every time she saw them confidently enjoying their time after passing the graduation exam, the wound of her own elimination seemed to open again.

“It’s fine. Let’s just avoid them. I’ve cooled off anyway, and I want to look around somewhere else.”

“Is that so? All right.”

Shirone and Amy pushed off with their blades and headed toward the exit.

“Hey! Leaving already?”

When Shirone turned around, Zocre was skating toward them at full speed, arms swinging.

Shirone hurriedly pulled Amy aside, but Zocre swerved after them and came to an abrupt stop right in front of them. His skate blades scraped across the ice, spraying powder into the air. Shirone frowned as the cold flecks hit his face.

“What are you doing? You got that in my face.”

“Haha! Why are you so angry? You’re softer than I heard. It was just a joke.”

“Then why make that kind of joke?”

Amy pushed past Shirone and stepped forward.

“Why do you keep following me? If you have something to say, say it properly. Stop acting like a child.”

“It’s been a while since we last met. This is fate too, isn’t it? Let’s have some fun. We still have something to settle.”

“Settle? Settle what?”

When Amy thought of Zocre, the only thing that came to mind was that he had given up competing at a prestigious school and transferred to another as if he were running away.

Wait. No way…

A hazy scene flashed through her mind.

Before Zocre transferred, he had confessed his feelings to Amy.

At the time, it had not been particularly unusual. That was why the memory had remained as something almost laughably trivial.

But today, she was conscious of Shirone.

She might have spoken lightly, as if joking, but no woman in the world would take the word “dating” only as a joke. Amy was a woman, and she was not an idiot. If a man and a woman went on a two-night, three-day trip together, she understood what that meant and had come prepared for it.

That was why she did not want to embarrass Shirone with such a ridiculous piece of her past. It was not as if Shirone would feel bad simply because she had once received a confession, but having the person who had confessed standing right in front of them was a different matter.

“It’s fine. I don’t want to talk to you. Shirone, let’s go somewhere else. We don’t have to deal with these people.”

As Amy tried to leave, Zocre ignored Shirone and approached her.

“Wait a moment. About what we were talking—”

Zocre stopped and turned his head. Shirone had grabbed him by the shoulder.

“She said she doesn’t want to.”

“You. Take your hand off me. Our schools may be different, but I’m your senior. I’m a full wizard.”

“That’s that, and this is this.”

Zocre was taken aback by Shirone’s boldness.

“You got a little attention because people around you supported you despite your low status, and now you’re excited, aren’t you? Amy and I have known each other since we were thirteen. This is none of your business.”

“What does it matter when you met? If that’s how you’re going to argue, Amy and I met when we were twelve. Stop bothering someone who doesn’t want to deal with you and leave.”

“What? You met when you were twelve?”

Zocre was stunned by what he believed to be Shirone’s lie.

Thanks to the academic journals he had memorized, he knew about Shirone’s past as well. How could Shirone have met Amy at twelve when he had only been attending magic school for a year? On top of that, he was the son of a mountain guide, not even a noble.

“You’re surprisingly shameless. Do you think a lie like that will work? Do you know what happens when a senior catches you lying in this world? Especially someone like you, with no connections to cling to. Just get out of here.”

“I don’t want to enter a world where seniors make petty threats like that anyway.”

“You little bastard!”

Seeing Shirone answer back without yielding an inch, Zocre lost his temper and raised his fist.

But just as he was about to swing, a snowball flew from nowhere and struck the back of his head.

“Agh!”

It was a solid snowball, hard enough to make his skull ring. Worse, he was standing on slippery ice.

Unable to keep his balance, Zocre pitched forward and fell.

His bewilderment lasted only a moment before anger surged up inside him. What fearless fool would dare play such a prank on him?

“What the hell! Who was that?”

Zocre clutched the back of his head and turned around.

A handsome gray-haired boy stood outside the rink, tossing another snowball in one hand. In his other hand, he held an iced coffee, completely unsuited to the cold weather.

“No matter how unpopular you are with women, you shouldn’t bother a couple on a date.”

Zocre narrowed his eyes at the familiar face.

Vivian murmured in surprise, “That’s… Dante, right? Air Servant Dante.”

Dante stepped onto the rink without skates. Pushing off against the ice, he slid forward on one foot. Ludvans and Vivian unconsciously moved aside to let him pass.

Oh my gosh. Is that really Dante?

Vivian could hardly believe it even after seeing him with her own eyes.

From the time he had entered magic school, Dante had been everyone’s idol, never once relinquishing first place in the kingdom. That was why, when the news of his defeat by Shirone was published in an academic journal, every student in the kingdom had been shocked.

Even Zocre, during his school days, had once cut out articles about Dante and pasted them in his notebook to fuel his determination.

But how long could a person remain bound by memories of his student days?

Above all, being struck in the back of the head before he could even speak was the humiliation of a lifetime.

“Hey, you…”

Dante ignored Zocre and walked up to Shirone.

“It’s been a while, Shirone. Amy too.”

Amy crossed her arms and glanced at him.

“What? You’re calling me by name now instead of senior?”

“Haha! We’ll be in the same graduating class starting next semester anyway.”

Amy was not in a bad mood either. During the 2,000 matchups, Dante had treated her politely as a senior, so his friendly attitude felt a little awkward. But Dante’s personality was truly easygoing, as if those memories had already been erased from his mind.

In any case, even if they had once been rivals at school, meeting an alumnus outside brought a sense of gladness first.

Shirone smiled.

“I didn’t expect to see you in a place like this. What brings you here?”

“As you can see, I came to have fun. I travel every vacation, but this is my first time in Creas. Someone recommended the winter festival.”

“I see. Did you come alone? What about Closer and Sabina?”

“They started special training as soon as vacation began. By now, they should be learning what hell is. They’re exhausting people to hang around with anyway.”

Shirone burst out laughing as Dante shrugged.

Although he was enjoying himself, Shirone had been wondering whether it was all right to be so carefree. But seeing Dante so relaxed made him realize that perhaps the burden he had been carrying was not everything.

Dante really is amazing.

Sometimes, when Shirone recalled the 2,000 matchups, he was still amazed that he had beaten Dante. Leaving magic aside, Dante had star quality. The power to draw people’s attention was not something one could gain through effort alone.

He was well dressed, befitting someone from Vaska, the capital. He wore a blue coat and black skinny pants, with a purple muffler wrapped around him. Without being flashy, he seemed to shine. He even had a piercing in one ear, which could not help but surprise Shirone, who was unfamiliar with fashion.

But Zocre saw the situation differently.

He had been completely ignored.

And in front of Amy. No, in front of Shirone, Amy’s boyfriend.

What upset him most was that the friendship between Shirone, Amy, and Dante seemed so radiant.

Is this how geniuses play with other geniuses? Seeing them gathered together like this really gets on my nerves.

Zocre took a deep breath and spoke.

“Hey, Dante.”

“Yes?”

Dante bit down on his straw and looked back. He seemed to have completely forgotten what he had done a moment ago.

“If you hit someone, shouldn’t you apologize? Especially to a wizard who’s your senior.”

“Senior? Who are you?”

“Zocre, a graduate of Eins Magic School. I’m not someone who should be hearing that tone from a promising student like you.”

Dante blinked as he sucked through the straw.

The longer Dante remained silent, the more uneasy Zocre became.

Even if Dante looked down on him as unqualified, Zocre would have nothing to say. He could already imagine what Dante might be thinking.

Of course, Eins Magic School was an alma mater Zocre loved, and he felt no shame in having chosen to transfer there.

But Dante was from the Royal Magic School.

The most prestigious school in the kingdom.

Although he was currently attending Alpheas Magic School to avenge his defeat by Shirone, Alpheas was also one of the kingdom’s top five prestigious schools. Above all, since Dante had once been an apprentice of Olivia, a certified second-class wizard, there was no reason Zocre’s name would have caught his attention.

“Sorry,” Dante said. “I’ve never heard your name. And weren’t you the one bothering a couple who were having fun?”

“You…!”

Sparks flashed in Zocre’s eyes. The words “never heard” lodged in his heart like a thorn.

In minor circles, their names were fairly well known. Even Ludvans and Vivian, who had been expecting at least a little recognition, looked stiff.

“Dante, don’t misunderstand. Do you think you can do whatever you want? Yes, you’re a student. And you were horribly defeated by Shirone. Shirone is a genius. Even Amy, who was called a genius recognized by the world, is still only a student in the end.”

“You’re very long-winded.”

Dante’s expression did not change in the slightest.

“That’s the difference between you and us. We get published in academic journals, and you buy them to read about us.”

Shirone’s face grew hot instead.

Dante mercilessly trampled on an opponent’s weak points. If one enjoyed sharp words, it could seem refreshing, but from the target’s point of view, it was hell.

As expected, Zocre and his group’s faces twisted, and blood seemed to rush into their eyes. If Dante had been deliberately trying to provoke them, it would have made sense, but knowing that was not the case only made Shirone worry more.

Damn it! Damn it! Damn it!

Zocre was about to die from anger. Why did he have to be treated like this?

By common sense, their positions should have been reversed. The one who had succeeded in graduating should have been the arrogant one, while students still in school looked at him with jealousy and envy.

Hmph! Pathetic things. Do they think their school’s name will live their lives for them?

Zocre glared at Shirone and the others, trying to think of a way to put them in their place.

Wait. That’s…

Then he noticed something shining beneath Shirone’s coat. Looking closer, he saw it was a sword.

Why is he wearing a sword at a festival? No, more importantly, he’s a commoner, isn’t he? Is he allowed to carry a weapon?

Zocre pondered the reason. Then he realized something and burst out laughing.

“Puhahaha! I see, I see. In the end, you’re the same.”

At Zocre’s sudden change in attitude, Dante stepped back as if disturbed.

Ignoring him, Zocre pointed at Shirone.

“You act so confident, but you’re full of insecurity, aren’t you? Seriously, why would a lowly commoner wear a sword? Well, since your lover is the highest kind of noble, I suppose you wanted to look the part.”

Vivian looked over and seemed surprised.

“Oh, is that true? But isn’t that illegal?”

“Of course it’s illegal! Since when does a commoner get to wear a sword? This is enough to report, isn’t it?”

Zocre approached Shirone and pulled open his coat.

Since it was a serious crime for a commoner to wear a sword, his hands held no hesitation.

A button tore off, and the coat opened, revealing Armand’s silver scabbard at Shirone’s waist.

“As expected, I was right. How desperate. Did you think wearing this would make you a noble?”

Shirone’s eyes turned cold.

“Step back. And pay for my coat.”

Shirone desperately controlled his emotions. The monster inside him, sensing his anger, was about to come out again.

But Zocre, believing he had found the winning move, was busy saying whatever he wanted.

“Seeing how it has no decorations and looks so flimsy, it’s obviously cheap. Well, I suppose you wouldn’t know where to get an expensive sword anyway…”

Zocre reached for the handle.

Since the sword was short, the blade probably would not look impressive either. He intended to draw it out and repay his humiliation twice over.

But his thoughts ended there.

Zocre’s movement froze at a sound like rushing water. Then his arms slowly rose.

His eyes trembled in shock, and goosebumps spread over his skin from the chill.

“T-this…”

Ludvans and Vivian’s faces filled with astonishment.

Shirone’s sword had drawn itself and was now pressed deep beneath Zocre’s chin.

“W-what? How did the sword…”

Shirone was frowning.

Not because he was angry at Zocre. If he had been only a little slower to control his emotions, Zocre’s head would already have been rolling across the ice.

I was too complacent.

Rated Control was an excellent function, but the problem was that the synchronization was so perfect that it was influenced by the actions of the demons in the first level of Depth.

Shirone was a mage who had trained his mind so that he would not lose his cool in any situation. But that did not mean his emotions never changed.

The anger that had surged through a momentary gap had forced Armand out of its scabbard.

That disappointment in himself made Shirone even angrier.

Sensing its master’s anger, Armand trembled. The cry of the sword rang against Zocre’s eardrums.

Zocre gritted his teeth and glared at Shirone.

“You…!”

“This is your final warning. Leave. If you act rude one more time, I won’t hold back.”

Under the pressure of death, strength drained from Zocre’s legs, and his skates slid awkwardly across the ice.

At this rate, he would lose his balance and fall. Ludvans quickly came over and supported Zocre from behind.

“Let’s go back. We’re full wizards. If an incident happens here, we’re the ones who lose.”

It was meant to help Zocre save face, but realistically, it was the right thing to say.

“Hmph! Cheeky brats! Let’s see how far you get.”

Zocre cursed until the end.

However, because Ludvans had to pull and drag him away, he only looked more shabby.

Only after Zocre and the others left the skating rink did Shirone sheathe Armand.

“Hey, hey. No matter how angry you get, you can’t go drawing a knife on people.”

Shirone’s eyes widened at Dante’s words.

“You think this is funny? This is all because of you! You suddenly provoked him like that! And what about my coat? You’re paying for it!”

“It’s just a button. You can sew it back on. Anyway, what’s with that sword? Is that magic too?”

Amy looked around and said, “Let’s move for now. People are staring. The guards might come.”

For now, the bystanders did not know Shirone was a commoner, so they would probably treat it as just an incident. But if guards were dispatched to investigate, things would become much more complicated.

“Then shall we go to a cafe and get some coffee?” Dante said. “Of course, I helped you, so let’s say you two are buying.”

“Why should we buy you coffee? What exactly did you do that was so praiseworthy? And aren’t you already drinking coffee?”

“This is iced coffee. So let’s go get something warm.”

“You… Are you addicted to coffee?”

Dante raised his iced coffee and winked.

“No. I’m a coffee maniac.”

* * *

Shirone’s party found a cafe called Cozy Tea House in the shopping district and went inside.

As warmth spread through them, their cheeks flushed red.

Shirone shivered as if shaking the cold from his clothes, sat at a table, and took out a sewing kit.

Dante picked up the menu.

“When you go on a trip, you have to order the local specialty. Let’s see… Ice Queen’s hot coffee? Isn’t that name a little strange?”

Amy waved a hand in annoyance.

“Pass on the strange stuff. I just want orange juice.”

“Really? Then I’ll have the Ice Queen’s hot coffee. Shirone, what are you drinking?”

Shirone answered while concentrating on sewing the button back onto his coat.

“I want warm cocoa.”
