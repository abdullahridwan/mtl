Chapter 476  
Mental Armament (1)  
~*~

The two iron-ringed eyes interlock to form the world, and within that world, everything changes.

This is the Sun and Moon Halo.

It is the technique that made Shirone the strongest person in the Black Line mental system, allowing him to perfectly control the environment by equalizing horizontal values.

That means, for example, if the horizontal value is 100, he could increase an opponent’s force to 99 rather than reducing their speed to 1.

The transformation can be set in infinite ways, so the actual mechanism is not that intuitive, but depending on how it is handled, it holds formidable potential to defeat opponents who are superior to you.

– New program detected. Quest.

Babel’s halo immediately recognized the change in the world.

The biggest change was that Shirone’s speed was now exceeding her own.

It was not that Shirone had sped up or Babel had slowed down.

To be precise, it was the result of equalizing the relative speed of the two entities.

Babel’s deadly countermeasure calculation, which had climbed to 2.7 billion, plummeted to only 2 million.

It had been a long time since Babel had performed this many calculations for a single individual—and a human, at that.

Of course, there were still 2 million ways to instantly kill Shirone.

But what truly unsettled Babel was that this program was so utterly unruly that even that number gave her no sense of relief.

No carelessness, no impatience.

Babel simply repeated her existing calculations and dutifully pressured the world.

‘This is really tricky.’

By equalizing hundreds of environmental factors to gain an edge in battle, Shirone had leveled the playing field—but it came at a cost. His spirit zone, vast enough to rival Jonah’s as a rare spirit-type wizard, was now reduced to an area of only twenty square meters.

Babel closed the distance without delay.

With 830,000 deadly countermeasures left, she had no reason to hesitate.

‘What a monster. There’s not a single gap.’

Even Shirone, who had faced countless powerful opponents, could only click his tongue.

It was not a transcendent ability, but the combat power derived from Babel’s flawless algorithm was comparable to that of an angel.

*Clang! Clang!*

Every time Babel’s fist grazed Shirone’s cheek, a sharp metallic roar pierced his eardrums.

‘I’m already caught in a trap.’

He could feel it—Babel was beginning to be certain of something.

He couldn’t let this continue.

If things went on like this, Shirone’s head would be severed within the next ten seconds.

– Lethal countermeasures: 32,564.

A cause produces an effect, and that effect in turn becomes the cause of a new effect.

The countermeasure numbers dropped exponentially as every causal factor that had occurred since the start of the battle contributed to the current outcome.

And that could only mean one thing.

‘Die!’

At the very moment Shirone was convinced of his approaching death, the rebels around him could not even move.

Shirone alone was aware of the absolute end rushing toward him.

*BOOOOOOM!*

A huge steel pillar slammed down right in front of him.

Without even checking on Babel on the ground, Shirone raised his head.

The armored body, towering thirty meters high, was frozen in the posture of a massive arm strike.

It was a Titan—a third-level unit of the mecha combat system.

Even within the rebel headquarters, only three units of this equipment existed. Boasting a mass approaching 3,000 tons and the power of the heat-flash cannon created by immense physical force and magnetic pulse, it was the strongest of all mecha technologies.

Producing a single unit required hundreds of thousands of elixirs and thousands of tons of minerals, and even the programs that composed its control system were not designed by any single person.

If operating a Kuroi required a Piper, then a Titan could not be controlled without riding a Kuroi.

Moving just one of the Titan’s arms demanded ten tons of physical strength.

Because power was transmitted in the order of Piper, Kuroi, and Titan, the Titan was an extremely sensitive piece of equipment relative to its size, and its combat power varied greatly depending on the skill of the pilot.

The pilot aboard Titan Unit 1, which had just crushed Babel, was a genius pilot and the rebel commander—Crude.

He had not intervened because he sensed Shirone was in danger. He had acted because he was now confident he could definitely defeat Babel.

But that confidence shattered an instant later as tremendous force surged through his control panel.

“Aaargh!”

With the shriek of tearing iron, Babel lifted the Titan’s gigantic palm with both hands.

When Crude yanked the control stick, the plasma engine delivered power with a roar so violent it seemed about to explode.

The harder he pushed, the faster Babel’s halo spun.

Finally, it burst outward like a nova, shoving aside the absurd weight and pulling her body free.

*BO-BO-BO-BOOOOOM!*

The Titan’s palm crashed down again, rupturing the ground and sending pillars of earth tens of meters high into the air.

Shirone slid backward, retreating, and surveyed the battlefield.

Kuroi fire nets embroidered the sky, and the two remaining Titans fired their heat-flash cannons at the airborne Babel.

Among the rebels fighting desperately were Kanya and Lena, who had both lost their fathers.

“Die!”

Kanya burst into tears and pulled the trigger.

But hitting a target faster than a bullet was impossible.

– Babel Program: Wide-area combat system activated.

– Target: All.

– Battle pattern: Slaughter.

– Lethal countermeasures: 7.

Babel, applying a new algorithm, tore across the battlefield at terrifying speed.

Every Kuroi in the path of the silver flash exploded and collapsed.

*BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!*

Each time the three Titans struck the ground, vibrations traveled through the earth to the feet of fighters dozens of meters away.

‘We can’t defeat her with Kuroi. She’s already an enemy beyond comparison of raw force.’

Crude tracked Babel with his exquisite piloting skills.

His eyes could barely keep up with her speed, but the radar still traced her trajectory.

However, the radar only measured inertia; actually hitting the target was entirely up to the pilot.

‘There!’

*FWOOOOOOM!*

The Titan’s shoulders opened, and black, cavernous muzzles protruded, firing heat-flash cannons.

– Evasion chance: 0%. Entering defensive posture.

Babel stopped in mid-air and crossed her arms in an X. At the same moment, a terrifying flash of light slammed the metal body to the ground.

*KROOOOOOOM!*

Babel stood with her knees bent, wreathed in smoke, on ground that had turned dark red and melted like lava.

‘Dammit! What the hell is she made of?’

It was definitely not a pure mineral.

But Crude could not even begin to guess what alloy could produce such thermal durability.

‘Ten seconds.’

If the energy of the heat-flash cannon could be concentrated for more than ten seconds, even Babel would have no choice but to melt.

But having calculated that far, Crude smiled bitterly.

When you considered what Babel could do in ten seconds, it was only natural to despair.

As time passed, casualties mounted one after another.

Even though Shirone equalized every environment to Babel’s disadvantage, he was being pushed back.

‘Strange. It’s really strange.’

Only after stepping back from the thick of the battle did Shirone truly realize it.

That machine was by no means man-made.

It merely looked like a machine; the internal program far exceeded the limits of human invention.

An angel’s work? Or something greater?

That was the extent of Babel’s strangeness that Shirone could perceive.

– Battle system changed.

When the number of rebels fell below a set threshold, Babel finally stopped moving.

“Now! Concentrated fire!”

Crude gave the immediate order, and thousands of bullets struck her.

Babel’s body, spitting sparks, seemed like living flame itself.

– Activating map weapon. Codename: Babel.

Babel at last revealed her true reason for existence.

She bent her upper body back like a drawn bow, her neck detached and dropped behind her back, and then her chest opened wide.

A gun barrel tipped with a glass sphere was exposed. Her arms and legs embedded into the ground to serve as anchors, while her upper body folded down behind her legs.

*KRRRRRRRK!*

An electrical phenomenon erupted in front of Babel’s glass sphere as she aimed at the Titan.

“Heat-flash cannon! Fire directly!”

Sensing the catastrophe coming, Crude concentrated all six muzzles of his three Titans.

Firing a wide-area strike weapon directly at the ground was a combat action that never appeared in any manual, but he was in no position to worry about that now.

Six heat-flash cannons surged toward Babel. At the same instant, a massive photoelectric cannon erupted from Babel’s glass sphere.

As the two energies collided, the entire command center became as bright as daylight, and the world was filled with the roar of incineration.

Crude’s Titan, now with a hole pierced through one shoulder, staggered as if it would collapse at any moment.

“Guh… Dammit!”

– Babel test launch completed. Firing operation...

Light-particle cannons erupted from Babel’s muzzle in all directions, as if the previous salvo had meant nothing.

This was, in the truest sense, a map weapon.

The firepower was sufficient to devastate the entire headquarters within a single minute.

“Dammit! I can’t stop it!”

Shirone sprinted toward Babel and attempted a last resort.

He tried to project the equalization of the Sun and Moon Halo directly into Babel’s system—

—and his mind reeled as if he were dreaming.

‘What on earth is this…’

A labyrinthine, complex system, one into which even those who had disciplined their minds countless times would fall into a swamp of oblivion.

A single ray of blue light pierced through the center of those countless circuits.

* * *

“Haah.”

In the stillness of Niflheim, where everything was frozen, only the breathing of the guide could be heard.

Even he, who had made a contract with an Ice Spirit, did not seem able to hold out for more than a few more hours.

It was only natural; even the tier-two monster Salamander could no longer raise its flames.

They were not simply freezing to death—the shadow of death was creeping toward their ankles.

Kang Nan barely forced her frozen mouth open.

“Where the hell is Hel? You couldn’t have taken the wrong way?”

The guide answered in a dying voice.

“I only came as I was taught. But I… I don’t know anymore…”

The guide was paying a heavy price for his greed.

The dream of taking a mountain of black elixirs and becoming the ruler of the mainland was nothing but a mirage in the face of reality.

“This… no more…”

Just as the guide was about to utter his final words, Zulu’s steps came to a halt.

The severe cold was driving her body into a terrible state, but her face remained calm.

“Gaold, over there.”

A huge shadow was moving beneath the ice sheet where Zulu pointed.

Tiny shadows streamed in from all directions, merged, and then rose up in a convex, bell-like shape, soaring above the ice.

An enemy?

No matter what, it was the first change to appear since they had killed the group of Reapers.

And that was precisely the reason Gaold had patiently followed the guide all the way here.

For a moment, no one moved.

It was a perfectly natural sight in Niflheim, but everything changed as the rising darkness gradually revealed its reality.

*CRACK-CRACK-CRACK!*

As the ice shook, Kang Nan fought to keep her balance, glaring at the darkness.

Something ten meters tall, clad in a white robe over a black void, was rising into the sky.

No legs were visible, and the ends of the robe split into dozens of pieces, fluttering like tattered banners.

‘This… is tremendous!’

Kang Nan clenched her teeth and assumed a fighting stance.

The sun was growing dark.

*Tsssssss…*

She turned her head at the sound from below. The Salamander’s flames had been completely extinguished, and its body was starting to freeze over.

“Uh, uh…?”

The guide made a startled noise.

A strange excitement was rising from the tips of his toes. But by the time that ecstasy reached his head, he had already turned into a frozen corpse.

“That’s an immense amount of magical power.”

Zulu dismissed the Salamander’s frozen corpse and focused.

Which boss to cast would depend on the opponent’s alignment.

“I am Hell, the god of death.”

Gaold’s eyebrows twitched.

“Wanderers of Niflheim, I will harvest your lives.”

“Tch.”

Gaold strode forward without hesitation, even though this opponent was incomparable to the Reapers.

The Salamander was gone, so from now on, he would have to ignite the fire with his own body.

“So that’s how it is. It wasn’t just a local legend after all.”

The king of Niflheim, the land of the dead.

That was the true identity of Hel.
