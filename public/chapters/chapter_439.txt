Chapter 439  
Task Force (1)  
~*~

Magic Association.

Sakiri, an investigator belonging to the Inspection Division 3 who had previously interrogated Kanis and Arin, was on his way to the prison camp for special criminals under the Association’s jurisdiction. Beside him walked Blenner, the Third Deputy Director of the National Intelligence Service.

The NIS’s Third Division was the Daecheonguk Information Management Department, a covert organization composed entirely of black agents whose ranks were never disclosed to civilians. The fact that a department head personally visited the Association meant only one thing: he needed an item to stop Gaold’s plan.

Specifically, he needed the confession of Isabelle, the third class archmage.

“Anyway, it’s a real problem. Speaking of Gaold… he’s the kingdom’s shame. I never liked him from the start. He’s made everyone exhausted, and now he just walks away unscathed.”

Sakiri wasn’t fooled by Blenner’s affable demeanor. Everyone in the Inspection Department knew that Rufist’s political faction had actively courted Blenner while he served as the temporary head of the Magic Association.

“Isabelle, that woman is quite formidable. Pretty face, too. Tsk tsk. Mages really can’t be trusted.”

Hearing gossip about a mage from a non-magician was never a good sign. Especially when the subject was a certified third class archmage Sakiri usually respected. But Sakiri kept his face blank. He had long mastered the art of shutting off his emotions when necessary.

“I’ve heard you’re the best on this floor. Do whatever you need to do; I’ll handle the aftermath. I just need her to talk.”

“It won’t be easy. Even as a non-combatant, she’s a formidable mage. She’s definitely not the type to break under pain and commit perjury.”

“Hehe. Isn’t that why I called you? Sakiri of the Third Inspection Division, who claims no criminal can withstand interrogation once caught.”

“I’ll do my best.”

Sakiri replied in a dry tone, his mind racing.

Temporary President Rufist had long viewed Gaold as a thorn in his side. In his heart, Rufist would have used every ounce of the Association’s power to eliminate him.

‘But things didn’t go as planned.’

Rufist had seized the opportunity to fulfill his long-cherished wish, but Gaold’s reputation within the Association hadn’t deteriorated as expected. He was a madman, yes, but that very madness made him pure. His nasty temper was matched only by his overwhelming power.

More importantly, this was completely outside Rufist’s calculations.

The incident where Isabelle risked her life to protect Gaold during the National Emergency Hearing had caused significant embarrassment for the Association’s mages. An outsider—someone outside Gaold’s inner guard—had publicly vouched for his innocence. If that endorsement came from a certified third class archmage, the public’s standard of judgment would waver, regardless of the truth.

With the moral high ground slipping away, Rufist grew desperate. He needed a reason to mobilize the Association’s full strength. Since he could only secure the official presidency by removing Gaold, he turned to Blenner, the NIS’s Third Deputy Director.

‘This is my conclusion. But… … .’

A question lingered. Forcing perjury and torturing certified mages was dangerous, even for the NIS. How had this operation been approved?

‘Who’s really behind it?’

Sakiri’s subtle probing confirmed Blenner’s true intentions.

“How far can I get a confession? Isabelle claimed she was part of the Old Guard and was sheltered because of it. Is that enough?”

“Hmm. Shouldn’t we aim a bit higher? We need enough to prove she orchestrated the assassination plot against Her Highness. Whether she designed it or distributed the funds, she needs to confess to it herself.”

‘I’m thinking of letting him go completely.’

No one should know what happened underground today. Blenner wanted to prune every loose end.

This explained why Sakiri was handling the interrogation alone, with no observers.

“As long as I don’t die, I can do anything, so just make sure you get the confession. I’ve installed video recording devices. Now, get out of Obscura B and bring it to me. And you… you’re special.”

‘So that’s how it is.’

The final piece clicked into place in Sakiri’s mind.

The torture of the beautiful archmage would be recorded in its entirety. What better leverage could there be for old politicians navigating a decadent society?

That was the real purpose.

Outside, a king’s assassination attempt was unfolding, the head of the Magic Association was changing, and the world was on the verge of upheaval. Yet someone was simply indulging in a private hobby.

‘Perhaps this is also a very humane thing.’

Sakiri understood. If humans have the ability to do something, they will.

Upon arriving at the special offender’s prison cell, Sakiri fixed his eyes on the iron door. Blenner, seemingly uninterested in the circumstances behind the gate, didn’t press further.

“Then please. Handle it as soon as possible. You have to get the confession within forty-eight hours.”

Forty-eight hours.

“Don’t worry. I’m the right man for the job.”

Blenner patted Sakiri on the shoulder with confidence and turned away.

Sakiri didn’t touch the iron door’s security panel until the sound of Blenner’s footsteps faded down the hallway. Once inside the Spirit Zone, the lock reading the door’s personal information clicked and disengaged.

He pulled the half-open iron door aside and found Isabelle sitting on a bench bolted to the wall of the dark prison.

She had always been a model employee of the Magic Association, known for her immaculate attire and pride in her appearance. Now, her hair was disheveled, covering her face, and her wrists rested limply on her knees, shackled by mana-control cuffs. Two were on her left and right, and one was near the iron gate. Three video recording devices were mounted around the cell.

The one facing her front flashed a red light, having recorded her condition for several days.

“It feels strange to meet you in a place like this,” Sakiri said.

Isabelle didn’t respond. She just drew shallow, empty breaths, slumped like a corpse.

Sakiri didn’t care. He unbuttoned his sleeve and checked the recording devices.

“Do you know what’s the scariest thing I’ve ever felt in my life? It’s the realization that humans are endowed with unlimited freedom. It’s true. Humans can do anything, no matter how ridiculous.”

After confirming the left recording device was active, Sakiri moved to the wall on the right.

“Is it impossible to strangle a child who doesn’t get sick even if you put it in your eyes? Isn’t it foolish to think otherwise? No matter how much you love it, just do it. That is human freedom.”

After checking the second device, Sakiri looked into the lens of the final active device and spoke.

“Reset Obscura B to save records.”

He pressed the button, and the device powered down, ready to restart.

Only after all three recording devices were off did Isabelle lift her head. Her eyes, stripped of hope, locked onto Sakiri’s face.

“Come to think of it, it’s tomorrow. The first son’s death.”

Sakiri was now a father of two daughters, but he had once had a son. Two years after the boy’s birth, he contracted Tris disease, a condition that rotted the body from the inside out. The boy died two years later.

Sakiri had been a regular visitor to the hospital.

“Well, this is life. Shall we begin?”

When Sakiri flipped the switch on the wall, the incandescent lights overhead blazed to life, flooding the prison with daylight-bright illumination.

*Thud! Thud! Thud! Thud!*

Sections of the wall slid open in sequence, expanding the cell into a long corridor.

Every conceivable instrument of torture and interrogation equipment lined the space.

“Usually, torture chambers are dark, but I prefer the light. Torture is a kind of communion. It might make you feel a little dirty watching your body break down, but just give in.”

Sakiri took out a pair of scissors to cut away Isabelle’s prison uniform and tested the blade.

“What should I do first? What are you weak against? Pain? Foreign body sensation? Fear? Body transformation? Shame?”

Isabelle spoke in a calm voice.

“You’re kind, Sakiri.”

“I understand duality. There’s nothing good about stimulating them before we even start. We’d better begin with water torture. For the sake of the show, it’s better to have a clean body first.”

“If not… why are you conflicted?”

Sakiri’s fingers stopped snapping the scissors.

“Conflicted?”

“You would have known. No matter what you do to me, I will not retract my testimony at the hearing. However, your rule of thumb, ‘The Balance of Truth,’ is an exception. I don’t know the activation conditions, but there’s no need to waste time playing house like this, is there?”

Sakiri, who had been silently staring at the scissors, sighed and turned away.

“Haa. As long as this guy or that guy is alive.”

*Clink.*

The scissors fell back to their resting place.

Sakiri, who had been standing still, turned slowly and looked down at Isabelle.

“…Why did you do that?”

Isabelle tilted her head slightly, as if she didn’t understand the question.

“Why did you come? You’re not even a member of the Royal Guard. It’s the same as using Gaold, Lufist, or the Association for personal gain. Lufist took over the Association to seize power, and Gaold became its president for revenge. Why did you protect Gaold, anyway?”

Isabelle’s answer was simple.

“I don’t know either.”

“You don’t know?”

“Sakiri, I really don’t know. It wasn’t a thoughtful decision. Yes, like you said, both Gaold and Lufist are self-centered people. Still, the reason I chose Gaold…”

Isabelle thought for a moment and offered a bitter smile.

“I just liked Gaold a little better than Lufist. That’s all.”

“….”

Sakiri’s jaw tightened, clenching left and right.

He turned back to the shelf where the torture instruments were stored, his face contorted like a man in physical pain.

‘Damn it. There was such an easy way.’

Since when had the title “wizard” come to mean a politician’s dog?

Weren’t those who fought with iron intellects, pushing through what they believed to be right, the ones who built the Association?

“…Forty-eight hours.”

Sakiri picked up an instrument that resembled a twenty-centimeter-long awl.

“In the next forty-eight hours, Rufist will defy public opinion and move the Association. Of course, if you commit perjury, the time will be shorter.”

Isabelle hadn’t even considered that scenario.

“Forty-eight hours. It’s pretty tight.”

“That’s why Lufist is also impatient. If possible, he’d like to become president with the support of the majority of Association members. But as long as Gaold is alive, his current position is in jeopardy. In short, as long as you hold out, you can buy yourself more time.”

Sakiri tapped the awl with his fingertip, then looked back at Isabelle with a cold gaze.

“I will protect the last degree of dignity you have as a human being and as a woman. It’s not because I have good feelings for you. As a mage, I simply refuse to cater to the tastes of perverted old men. But the pain will be considerable.”

Isabelle didn’t care. She just stared up at the ceiling, lost in thought, oblivious to the terrible ordeal ahead.

‘Whoops, Gaold. Seriously, how many people are you bothering?’

Yeah, why?

Why had she defended Gaold, who had nothing to do with her?

‘Perhaps I, too, have been possessed by your madness.’

The light flickered on as the video recording device restarted.

After activating the remaining two devices, Sakiri stood at the center of the three focal points and announced the start of the official interrogation.

“From now on, I will begin interrogating Isabelle, the manager of the Magic Library of the Magic Association.”

Isabelle’s eyes lit up for the first time.

*Go, Gaold. Go beyond good and evil, transcend the future of mankind, and reach the ultimate realm that humans can attain.*

Sakiri grabbed Isabelle’s hair and yanked her head back violently.

His eyes widened as he stared at the soft nape of her neck.

*Forty-eight hours from now. Hold on.*

Screams echoed through the corridor of the prison cell.
