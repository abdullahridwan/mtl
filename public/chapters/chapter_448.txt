Chapter 448  
Second Purgatory (5)  
~*~

Shirone’s party navigated through the rain of bullets and made their way to the battalion commander’s headquarters barracks. Even though it served as the command center, its location right on the front lines meant voices had to be raised to be heard over the artillery. The noise only subsided once they crossed the threshold into the barracks.

A company commander, having finished his own report, was resting in the corner. Kanya stood by Shirone’s side, acting as an intermediary to ensure smooth communication. The battalion commander did not take his eyes off the strategic map, even as Shirone and his party entered.

“I heard about you.”

He was a stern-looking man with gray hair swept back and a thick, stubborn mustache.

“Are you a Nephilim?”

Shirone did not deny it. Regardless of the truth, leveraging his reputation would be more effective in dealing with the rebels.

The battalion commander seemed uninterested and moved to his next question. “I’ll ask directly. Why did you return?”

Shirone didn’t immediately understand the question.

“You’re a legend to the rebels. But legends are most beautiful when they remain legends. Some of my men see you as an irresponsible deserter. Why return to the battlefield after you’d already fled?”

Shirone had a hunch that the battalion commander was among those who viewed him as a fugitive.

“There’s no grand reason. Just as before, I’ll fight as a lone mage, standing by my convictions.”

“Convictions…”

The battalion commander stepped to the tent flap, surveying the battlefield. “The hope you ignited in District 73 is now the sun for seventy thousand rebels. I won’t extinguish it. If you’re here solely for your beliefs, then I’d prefer we pretend we never met.”

Shirone realized the commander would not budge. The coldness in the man’s eyes held no fighting spirit, only the grim conviction that Shirone had chosen the wrong path from the start.

“Even a small conviction can serve a greater purpose. I’d like to speak directly with the Commander. Please let me go to the rebel headquarters.”

“Denied.”

An unconcealable hostility flashed in the battalion commander’s eyes. “Let me be blunt. We’ve already lost this war. Or rather, we’re on the brink of total defeat. If Heaven hadn’t suddenly halted its offensive, the war would be over. The disparity was overwhelming. Countless mecha pilots have died. Our only remaining card is negotiation with Heaven. If you reignite this conflict, the fragile hope in District 73 will only become a liability.”

“The war is still ongoing, and Heaven is currently inactive. That’s an opportunity. If you can uncover the truth behind their pause, we might still turn this around.”

“Listen, ‘light of District 73.’ Don’t judge our situation with a human mindset. This rebellion was sparked by humans, but now it’s only the Mecca who are dying. Why did Heaven stop? Who can possibly know?”

“I can figure it out.”

Shirone’s jaw tightened, teeth grinding in frustration.

“Listen carefully. Do you think I don’t see your game? I may not know the truth, but I know you intend to use us regardless. They call you a legend, but to me, you’re a harbinger of disaster. At best, a myth incapable of defeating a Law Coordinator. Do you have any idea how fiercely we fought while you were gone? After abandoning us at our darkest hour, what do you think you can possibly change now?”

“I will meet Archangel Ikael.”

The commander let out a sharp breath, then burst into laughter. “Puhahaha! Fuhahaha! You’re delusional. Meet Ikael? The supreme arbiter of the Heavenly Laws? If it were that simple, hundreds of thousands of my soldiers wouldn’t be dead.”

“I know for certain now. You’re no legend. You’re a siren who lures countless souls to their deaths with sweet words. Company Commander! Arrest him! And keep this from the troops.”

Just as the company commander moved to obey, the tent flap flew open and an adjutant rushed in.

“Sir! Emergency! The giants are breaching the final defensive line!”

“What? Who the hell lured them here? Why?”

“Unconfirmed. After Kuroi Group Three lost contact, the line collapsed…”

The commander slammed his fist onto the tactical table. “Damn it. Why?”

Giant elimination operations rarely produced variables; mindless giants always reacted the same way. Yet the final line had fallen. He didn’t know how the Law had shifted, but his mind raced with grim possibilities.

The battalion commander grabbed Shirone by the collar. “You see? You’re the cause of this. You destroyed Kuroi, leaving a fatal gap in our defenses. Now my battalion will be annihilated!”

“Commander! That’s just speculation! Shirone…” Kanya shouted, ready to throw herself in front of him.

“Silence! What does a pilot who couldn’t even protect his own mech have to say?”

The adjutant pressed on. “Sir! Give us orders! If we stay, we won’t last thirty minutes!”

The commander ignored him, drawing a weapon labeled *Piper* from the wall. He tucked an item called *Ark* into his pocket, grabbed *Cigna* and *Xd*, and stormed out. He paused at the entrance, glaring at Shirone.

“Leave. Now. I don’t want to see you.”

“Captain! Let us help!” Kanya called out.

“Did you not hear me? Shirone is the one who broke the giants’ Law! Our duty is to fight, not hide behind fugitives!”

When the battalion commander left, Kanya had no choice but to follow. As a soldier, she couldn’t stand idle while her comrades died.

“Shirone, I’m sorry.” She offered a sad smile as she stepped out. “But it was nice to meet you.”

She didn’t know it was her final farewell. Yet Shirone still clung to a thread of hope. *A shift in the Law. They say the Law can be altered by the expression of will. Maybe it’s true…*

Kang Nan spoke up. “The situation is worse than expected. Can you really rally the rebels like this?”

Sein added, “We still have cards to play. Tagis is the same. The real issue is command. If the military command is covering up their location, we’ll likely be cut off from mainland intel. I need to act now.”

Zulu interjected, “The shift in the giants’ Law is crucial. I don’t understand why giants, whose sole directive is to march to Jotunheim, are acting collectively. Can anyone explain this?”

Silence followed. In Heaven, where the Akashic Records materialize, the Law is an artificial construct. But in Shirone’s world, it’s a fundamental principle of the universe. Questioning its sudden shift was like blaming a butterfly’s wings for a storm; countless variables were at play.

Gaold offered a solution. “Clear out the giants first. If you save my life, will you at least let me meet the commander?”

Shirone raised a hand. “Leave that to me.”

“No. It’s too dangerous. You have a special mission. Don’t treat your life as expendable.”

“It’s fine. I believe it ties directly to the Law.”

Sein pressed, “Do you have a theory?”

“Yes. But treat it as an experiment, not a guess. I need to verify something in Heaven.”

* * *

“All units! Attack! Hold the line!”

Seven giants seized a Kuroi mech, hurled it to the ground, and began stomping on it. The cockpit shattered. The pilot’s terrified scream was cut short after a second.

“Damn it! Aaaah!”

The battalion commander, arriving late, fired wildly in succession. It was a desperate, unaimed barrage. The giant didn’t even flinch. Its indifference only deepened the commander’s despair.

“Damn it! Damn it!”

This was never a fight humans could win. What was the point of existence? What did a fixed lifespan mean? Because of humanity’s sin against God, eternal life was lost. Now, the species faced extinction.

“Aaaah!”

The commander drove *Cigna* into the giant’s leg. The beast slowly turned its head, feeling the pain. When its dilated pupil locked onto him, the commander’s blood ran cold.

“Heresy… Punish it.”

His legs trembled. He couldn’t suppress the whimper escaping his teeth.

“Huh… uhhhh…”

As the giant advanced, terror washed over the commander’s face. He had witnessed countless deaths, but his own was a new, incomparable horror.

“Heresy… Punish it.”

“Oh, no. I am the heresy…”

The giant’s fist descended. The commander screamed silently and squeezed his eyes shut.

*Krrrr…*

A low, rumbling sound echoed. Slowly opening his eyes, he saw a creature he’d never witnessed before standing like a black barrier. It had sleek black fur, a lithe leopard-like frame, six legs, a flat face, and canine teeth over a meter long. Cracks spiderwebbed across the ground where its claws had dug in to absorb the giant’s blow.

“What… what is this?”

“A Tier 3 monster cougar.”

Gaold and the others had arrived. Zulu stepped forward, flicked a finger, and the cougar let out a soft, inquisitive chirp that belied its size.

“Go. Play.”

*Whoosh!*

The cougar launched into the air, the battlefield suddenly clear as a curtain lifted. With every swing of its claws, the giant’s armored body was cleaved apart. Its speed and agility were unmatched, befitting the strongest Tier 3 predator.

The battalion commander, previously transfixed by the slaughter, slowly turned his head. “Who are you?”

Gaold pursed his lips. “Human. Just like you.”

* * *

“Lena! Lena!”

“Sister!”

Kanya found Lena fighting amidst the giants and pulled her into a desperate embrace. A giant’s foot slammed down where they’d stood, the ground trembling like a rubber mat. Lena had never felt the Law’s pressure before, and terror gripped her. Seeing Kanya’s pale face, she instinctively checked her.

“Sister! Are you hurt? What about Kuroi?”

Tears welled in Kanya’s eyes before she could speak. *Poor little sister. Born into this world, she’s done nothing but suffer. It breaks my heart to leave her like this.*

Lena sensed her sister’s despair and offered a reassuring smile. “Sister, I’m fine.”

Kanya pressed her cheek against Lena’s. *She’s too smart. She knows. The comrades are already dead everywhere. This is our last moment.*

“Lena… Shirone…”

“Shirone brother?”

Lena looked up. Kanya followed her gaze, her eyes widening in shock.

Shirone soared through the sky, golden wings spread wide. *Is this a war in Heaven?* From above, the battlefield was a slaughterhouse. Giants crushing humans was like humans stomping ants. *But I can end this. If this works…*

Shirone cast *Shining Impact*. A blinding light, equal to sunlight, erupted, overwhelming everyone. In the gloom, the effect was devastating.

“Gaaaaa!”

“Damn it! What is this sudden light!”

As vision slowly returned, panic surged. The fear of being blinded by giants drove people to frenzy. Suddenly, a flash illuminated the sky. Rainbow-colored light spread across the night. Giants and humans alike looked up as the celestial glow painted the plains.

“I-that…”

Above them, Shirone had manifested a massive *Ataraxia*. His wings beat steadily as he gazed down. The Mecca people were stunned. Shirone’s appearance mirrored the very entity they feared.

“Ughhh… uhhhh…”

Hundreds of giants trembled, their knees buckling. They opened their mouths in awe, then began to weep. Giants, too, were bound by the Law. Even stripped of identity, Ikael’s magic circle resonated in their instincts.

“The Law… Coordinator…”

Kneeling wasn’t enough. They pressed their foreheads to the ground.

The humans, meanwhile, stared at each other in disbelief before looking up again.

“The Nephilim…”

The forgotten word slipped from the lips of the heretics.

“Sister… Shirone brother is here.”

In Kanya’s arms, Lena burst into tears. No matter how others cursed her as a fugitive, no matter the regret of defying God, she never doubted Shirone. It was a miracle.

“Yes, Lena. Shirone… He truly is a descendant of angels.”

Shirone returned with wings more radiant than any angel’s, a multicolored halo—the mark of an archangel—floating above his head. From the light of District 73 to the light of Heaven.

“Nephilim. Nephilim.”

“O Adjuster of the Law…”

Everyone fell to their knees at Shirone’s feet.
