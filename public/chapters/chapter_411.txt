Chapter 411  
The Meaning of Survival (3)

~*~

Once Collie activated the Virtual Zone, the thirty students were divided into red and blue teams.

Shirone took a deep breath and forced the tension from his body.

A survival evaluation was not a duel against another person, nor was it a strategic battle against monsters.

There was no defeating Mother Nature.

Pain was inevitable, and there were only ten seconds left before that pain arrived.

Perhaps that was why the faces of the students who had experienced this last year had already gone bloodless.

‘Damn it. If it weren’t for this, I could have stayed a senior for another ten years.’

The number one event upperclassmen wanted to avoid most—

The survival test.

—Transmitting information to the Virtual Zone. Five-second standby. Four seconds. Three seconds…

As the woman’s guiding voice rang out, the students revealed their different ways of coping. Some squeezed their eyes shut. Some loosened their shoulders and necks. Others stood calmly, steadying their breathing with composed expressions.

Shirone stared straight ahead and quieted his mind.

Then someone tapped him on the arm.

It was Iruki.

“See you alive, Shirone.”

—Data transmission complete. Virtual Zone activating Virtual Cold Program.

Beyond Iruki’s bitter smile, the scenery began to ripple.

The view of the Virtual Zone vanished in an instant, as if a page in a picture book had been turned, and a snow-covered summit appeared in its place.

Shirone looked around in surprise.

A frigid wind pierced his nose, and snow glittered everywhere, reflecting the sunlight. The sky felt close enough to touch. Snowy mountains surrounded them like walls, their rocky faces gleaming with vivid white as if condensed milk had been poured over them.

“Is this… the first stage of survival?”

Even for students in Class Two or higher, this situation was new. The survival program reset every time.

Iruki looked back and raised a finger.

“It starts over there.”

Shirone turned and his eyes widened.

A gigantic white cloud—something he had never seen before and would likely never see again—was slowly descending the mountain.

It was an avalanche.

“Damn it!”

Boyle, Pandora, and several other students immediately cast Teleportation and flew in the opposite direction.

But some, including Shirone, remained where they were.

The avalanche was approaching far more slowly than he had expected. It was pouring down from an enormous height. Yet the fact that it still looked so massive from this distance meant that an unimaginable amount of snow was coming.

‘This isn’t a situation they designed for us to avoid…’

Woooooo!

A mass of snow with an astronomical weight struck the summit, blasting the air outward.

A violent wind tore across Shirone’s cheeks, and in the next instant, a snowstorm three hundred meters high swept over the peak in less than a breath.

“Keugh!”

Shirone clenched his teeth and tried to regain his composure.

The avalanche, flowing faster than a raging torrent, mercilessly shoved him off the summit.

‘No! Stay conscious!’

Snow forced its way into his mouth, and Shirone pushed desperately through the mass around him.

“Puha!”

The snowdrift was larger than an iceberg, and it was falling endlessly, the ground nowhere in sight.

Near the surface of the rolling snow, Shirone finally thrust his face out.

“Puha! Ptoo!”

He spat everything from his mouth and opened his eyes. A round sun shone in the clear sky.

After launching himself upward, Shirone maintained Fly magic and scanned his surroundings.

One by one, other students who had made the same judgment were bursting out of the falling snow as well.

“Shirone!”

He turned at Iruki’s voice. Iruki, half-buried in snow from the face down, pointed below and shouted.

“Descend slowly…!”

Before he could finish, a waterfall of snow swallowed him.

Startled, Shirone looked up.

The clear sky had been completely blocked by white.

“You’ve got to be kidding me…!”

A white torrent crashed through the space where Shirone had just been.

“Aaaaaaaaaaaaa…!”

“It hurts…! Save me…”

Collie looked with pity at the students rolling around inside the Virtual Zone.

Starting with being swept away by an avalanche, all thirty of them had fallen into a half-conscious state without exception.

‘How painful must it be? They’re precious children.’

Victory or defeat would be decided by the final survivor, but for that very reason, none of the students would give up easily.

Their individual results, not their team results, would be disclosed to the association and scouts. They would try to endure with everything they had.

‘Perhaps it’s a situation they will never have to experience in their lives. But the world they’ll live in is even harsher than this. Once it becomes real, life turns into hell.’

“It looks like it’s already begun.”

Collie hurriedly straightened and turned around.

“Principal.”

Alpheas, accompanied by Vice Principal Olivia, was looking at the Virtual Zone.

“They’ve all entered a half-conscious state,” Alpheas said. “Well, that’s only natural.”

“Even professionals hate this, don’t they?” Collie said. “Schools with only a small number of graduation slots don’t even include it as an event.”

Survival evaluation was a subject the association had debated many times.

It was useful for assessing practical ability, but there was also a risk that students might break before they had even had the chance to sprout.

Even students who failed to graduate were still valuable talents who would eventually become members of society. The wizarding world could not function without wizards who opened magic shops, became teachers, joined the police force, carried out guild requests, and handled civil complaints for administrative agencies.

For that reason, the association had issued a guideline stating that only the five prestigious schools that received more than ten graduation tickets should conduct survival evaluations.

Because only the chosen took part, survival was an event that never failed to come up at alumni gatherings and became a favorite topic over drinks.

It would be horrifying to return to one’s school days and take the test again, but even that feeling could be called the pride of a prestigious school.

Olivia asked, “Is this the program the association provided this year? Is the current Stage One ‘Snow Play’?”

“Yes. Two minutes remain. To the students, it may feel like two hours.”

Olivia’s brow furrowed in displeasure.

“Still, there hasn’t been a single dropout. That means its ability to distinguish the lower-scoring students is reduced. In the future, I’ll have to suggest a higher difficulty.”

Alpheas shook his head with a wry smile.

The members of the teachers’ council always seemed impatient to give the students even more impressive ornaments to wear.

“Agh!”

Seeing a boulder hurtling toward him, Shirone curled his arms over his face.

A sharp pain shot through his arm bones, and his nose stung. A chunk of snow slammed heavily into the top of his head.

He had already been carried a long way down from the summit, so the amount of snow was no longer enough to crush a person outright. Even so, the impact was strong enough to rattle his bones.

‘My arm. My arm is broken.’

Although he had protected himself with an air shield, he had been falling too fast for it to absorb the full force.

When he raised his face from the rock beneath him, a sharp fragment of broken arm bone was protruding through his skin.

This was a virtual world, so it would reconstruct itself before long. But the pain transmitted through his nerves was even more vivid than reality.

“Whoa! Whoa! Whoa!”

He had landed on a slab of bedrock jutting out from the middle of a cliff.

This was better than being swept any farther. He hoped time would pass quickly and the first stage would end.

Crack. Crack.

As if mocking that thought, fissures spread through the rock wall, which began showing signs of collapse.

Shirone clutched his broken arm and trembled.

“Heh. Hehehe.”

He laughed because the whole thing was absurd.

‘This is a regular event? Are they trying to kill someone?’

He could imagine what the students would look like if survival was adopted for the graduation exam.

Once again, he felt respect for every graduate in the world.

“All right. Let’s see this through to the end.”

Shirone lifted his head, his eyes burning with defiance.

At the same moment the rock wall gave way, his body dropped toward the ground.

* * *

Splash!

‘What?’

Hearing the sound of himself falling into water, Shirone opened his eyes.

A cold so intense it felt as though it would shatter his mind enveloped him. One after another, students sank around him, bubbles rising from their mouths, regardless of whether they had fallen face-up or face-down.

‘Is this the second stage?’

There was definitely a transition process between Stage One and Stage Two, but those who had entered a half-conscious state could not perceive it.

The program allowed them no room to recover.

‘Why can’t I use magic?’

He couldn’t enter the Spirit Zone. Of course, because this world was virtual, his magic was not actually being blocked.

‘First, I need to get to the surface.’

Shirone struggled like a frog and headed upward. If he stayed in the water any longer, he felt as if he would freeze to death.

But the moment he stretched out his hand near the surface, his eyes trembled in shock.

He couldn’t get out.

What his hand touched was a cold, solid wall of ice.

‘What? Then how am I supposed to escape?’

A blurry shadow was visible above the ice sheet. Shirone rose a little farther and opened his eyes wide. A young boy was squatting on top of the ice.

‘I need to get out. I’m running out of breath.’

As if begging for help, Shirone pounded on the ice with his fist.

After staring at him for a while, the boy nodded as though he understood, then pointed somewhere.

‘There? I just have to go over there?’

Shirone pointed in that direction with his index finger, asking for confirmation.

The boy nodded again and began walking that way, as if guiding him.

Shirone pushed off the ice wall and followed the boy. All the while, the cold water constricted his body.

‘This is bad. I can’t hold out much longer.’

He wanted to arrive immediately and breathe in air to his heart’s content. But the boy’s steps were unbearably slow.

‘Hurry! Hurry! I can’t…’

The time when he could choose to hold his breath was over. From this point on, he was simply unable to inhale because there was no air.

His body shook from the cold and the fear of death.

At last, the boy stopped walking. He looked down at Shirone and pointed to a spot a couple of steps away.

‘There!’

Shirone kicked with all his might. Even though it was only three meters away, it felt impossibly far.

‘Yes! Finally…!’

Overwhelmed with joy, Shirone reached toward the exit.

Thud.

A solid ice wall blocked him again.

‘What…?’

His mind went blank.

Shirone looked up at the boy on the ice, demanding an explanation.

The boy clutched his stomach and giggled. Then he pressed his face close to Shirone’s and stuck out his tongue.

Gurgle!

A burst of air escaped Shirone’s mouth.

‘No. Stay calm.’

He hurriedly suppressed the rage burning through his mind.

He hated the boy taunting him, sticking his tongue out in a place filled with fresh air. But getting angry would not improve the situation.

‘Show me again. Where is the exit?’

With desperate eyes, Shirone tapped the ice wall.

Even with a person in mortal danger beneath his feet, the boy stroked his chin as if deep in thought.

Shirone’s head began to heat up again.

‘No. No. If I lose my composure, it’s over.’

After a long moment of thought, the boy seemed to reach a conclusion. Then he turned to Shirone and shook his head, as if to say he could not tell him.

‘Control your anger. I am a wizard.’

The light of life began fading from Shirone’s pupils. Without oxygen reaching his brain, his organs started to shut down.

‘Rage changes nothing. Until the moment I die… if I don’t let go of reason…’

Gurgle!

The air remaining inside him spilled out of his mouth in bubbles. As much air as he lost, cold water flowed into his stomach.

His cells seemed to freeze.

His pulse stopped, and the palms pressed against the ice wall slowly drifted away.

Through his sinking eyes, he saw the boy smiling.

The boy stared at Shirone’s face as he was pulled into the darkness of the abyss.

Then, as if he had made up his mind, the boy nodded and raised a trident that had appeared from nowhere.

Baaaaang!

The ice wall shattered in a single blow.

The sight reflected in Shirone’s lifeless eyes.

As his frozen nerves slowly transmitted that fact to his brain, his legs began to move again, and his body rose little by little.

“Puhaaaaa! Kkgh! Kkgh!”

Shirone burst through the surface and sucked in air without restraint. Right now, he could think of nothing else.

“Hah! Hah!”

Only after about a minute did he finally come back to his senses.

The ice field was peaceful, and before he knew it, the boy had disappeared.

“Puha! Ptoo!”

Students were surfacing one by one. Amy could be seen not far away as well.

‘She passed.’

Amy took deep breaths, wearing the expression of someone who had just faced death. Then, belatedly, she spotted Shirone and met his eyes.

When he greeted her with a smile, she blew out air and let her wet hair float around her.

“Haha!”

Shirone laughed.

It truly was hard just to stay alive.

And that was his final memory of the second stage, “Ice Prison.”
