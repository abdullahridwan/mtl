Chapter 454  
Second Command (3)  
~*~

After disrobing, Shirone walked through a long, narrow tunnel. Gas seeped from cracks in the walls, enveloping her body in a sterile mist.

Though wary of hidden dangers, she held her breath and pressed forward, quickly traversing the short passage. On the other side, she stepped into a vast cavern.

Sunlight filtered through countless holes in the ceiling, scattering like stars across the dim chamber. For a moment, Shirone gazed upward, captivated by the celestial illusion. Then she lowered her eyes and focused on the strange substance before her.

“What is this?”

A massive, gelatinous mass lay pooled on the floor, quivering like thick pudding.

As Shirone hesitated, unsure how to proceed, a voice echoed from above—dry, ancient, and authoritative.

“It’s part of the sterilization process. Break through it.”

“Hmm.”

She sent her drone forward first, scanning for traps. Satisfied, she took a deep breath and stepped into the gel.

It was slick and yielding, offering little resistance. Walking through it was easy—but the sensation was deeply unsettling. A faint blush crept across her cheeks as the gel brushed against sensitive areas of her bare skin.

When she emerged into the next chamber, a warm, crimson glow enveloped her. The gel coating her body dried instantly, hardening into a thin, translucent film.

“Aha. So that’s how it works.”

Sterilization—99.99% complete.

* * *

The final chamber stunned her.

Dozens of people moved about, bustling through a sprawling laboratory. More than ten glass tubes lined the walls, filled with liquids of every color, flowing through a labyrinth of hoses. On long tables, glassware cluttered the surface alongside dissected remains of creatures whose origins were impossible to guess. Blood had been mostly cleaned, but the faint metallic scent still lingered.

Scientists in white coats darted between stations, absorbed in their work. And standing near the entrance, arms crossed and face twisted in disdain, was the bald man who had greeted her earlier—now holding Shirone’s and Fleur’s clothes.

Commander Reisis approached, her crimson cloak flowing behind her like a trail of fire. Her smile was sharp, almost hypnotic.

Shirone, still naked beneath the dried film, felt a flicker of self-consciousness. But she suppressed it. Showing weakness now, in the middle of a mission, was unacceptable.

“Where is this?” she asked, keeping her voice steady.

Reisis ignored the question, her eyes crinkling with amusement. “How was the Norse sterilization system? Flawless, wasn’t it?”

It *had* been impressive. Shirone nodded. “Yes. Very systematic.”

Just then, the far door opened. Fleur stepped in.

“Ugh.”

Shirone barely stifled a gasp.

Fleur, however, didn’t flinch. He scanned the room with calm detachment, as if it were perfectly normal to be walking into a lab full of strangers while completely exposed. Then his eyes found Shirone, and he strode toward her.

“You made it quickly. Everything go smoothly?”

“Ah—yes.”

Fleur glanced down at her.

Even now, as a wizard, Kubrick remained loyal to him.

*Well, if you can’t handle this, you’re just a child.*

Shirone had flatly refused to remove her ring earlier. But Fleur hadn’t even checked his own hand.

Not that it mattered. But… she wanted to trust him.

Satisfied that Shirone was unharmed, Fleur turned his attention to the lab.

*Biology lab. What the hell are they researching here?*

The scene reminded him of the Magic Association’s underground chambers—scholars dissecting unknown organs, chasing forbidden knowledge. If the blood-scent in Lacey’s perfume had an origin, it was likely here.

And if all they needed was to measure Spirit Force, why go through all this? Why the sterilization? Why the blood?

*Hmm. Definitely suspicious.*

Reisis stepped toward Fleur, her smile unwavering.

“My apologies. Did this surprise you? I should’ve been more considerate of the ladies. But this is the only lab connected to the disinfection chamber. We need uncontaminated hair and blood samples.”

Fleur shifted his stance, one leg extended, hand resting on his hip. He knew she was lying. Knew she was enjoying this.

“Can’t argue with a physical exam. But you’re awfully thorough for someone claiming it’s routine.”

Lacey—Reisis—laughed, a deep, booming sound. “Ho-ho-ho! That’s just how our commander operates. Don’t worry. I’ll finish soon and get you both clothed.”

A woman approached with a syringe and drew blood from both Shirone and Fleur. Nearby, two assistants collected strands of their hair.

Reisis watched, pleased by their composure.

“I’ve studied your magic,” she said. “A fusion of Norse spiritualism and Meccan knowledge. But in the end… you’re closer to the Nord.”

Fleur, who had been observing a scientist dissect a hulking slab of flesh, turned to her. “On what basis do you say that?”

“Because you understand the body’s meaninglessness. The Norse are obsessed with the spirit. That’s why you see so many naked, dissected bodies here. The truth is, the human form is just a collection of functions. What matters is what lies *within*.” She tapped her temple.

“But the Meccans never grasped that. They played with iron, forged machines, built contraptions. Even in biology, they sought control. They called us barbaric for rejecting the flesh.”

Shirone understood now. This was where the Mecca-Nord feud began.

“Sampling complete,” a scientist announced.

The bald man returned with their clothes, his expression sour—perhaps resentful of playing servant.

Shirone seized the moment. “When do you measure Spirit Force?”

“Now.” Reisis gestured to a table where Norse scholars were mixing blood and hair samples into a glowing blue liquid.

Bubbles rose as the mixture shifted from blue to pale green, thickening into a viscous solution. Reisis pointed to a device beside it.

“This elixir reveals the biological substrate. When green, yellow, and white elixirs are combined in precise ratios and fused with organic cells, the result reacts to magical affinity. Neutralize it with distilled water, cast a spell, and you’ll see the true potential.”

“Fascinating,” Fleur murmured, stepping closer. He’d never seen biology used this way.

“No need to be nervous,” Reisis added. “Low affinity doesn’t mean low skill. Think of it as measuring raw magical potential.”

For Shirone and Fleur—still outsiders in the wizarding world—this was more than just a test. It was a glimpse into a system that didn’t exist in their reality. Curiosity burned beneath their mission focus.

“Can we try it now?” Shirone asked.

“No. Tradition demands new recruits be tested before the entire Norse assembly. Private measurements invite suspicion. Ranks are determined by percentage. Train hard, and you can rise.”

Shirone and Fleur followed the others outside.

The courtyard was packed. Nearly every member of the headquarters had gathered.

At the center stood two large drums filled with liquid. A glass tube, buried beneath the ground, connected them and rose vertically—ten meters into the air.

The bald man arrived, lugging two 20-liter containers. Inside sloshed the liquid containing Shirone’s and Fleur’s biological substrates.

He poured them into the drums. Instantly, the silver liquid in the underground tube surged upward—stopping precisely at the zero mark.

Reisis stepped onto a raised platform.

“Now,” she declared, “the tradition of welcoming new members begins!”

All eyes turned to her.

“I swear—there is no deception in this measurement. Your ranks will be set by today’s results. Now—affinity testing begins.”

“I’ll go first,” Fleur said.

He rolled up his sleeves and approached the device. The bald man stepped forward to explain.

“Place your hands in the drum. Focus your mind as if casting a spell. The pressure change will push the liquid up the tube.”

“Should I enter the Spirit Zone?”

The bald man smirked. “For reference—my affinity is 16%.”

“And?”

“That puts me in the top 20% of the Norse. It’s not about your current skill. It’s your *potential* that determines your rank. So do your best. Wouldn’t want you ending up under me.”

He leaned in, whispering. “Heh. I saw you earlier. Did you *kill* me?”

Fleur’s eyes narrowed.

*He enjoys the body’s meaninglessness.*

Fleur had never believed Reisis’s philosophy. There was always something off—something hidden beneath her words.

Even if you dissect a human, even if you reduce them to functions—humans are still *human*.

*There’s something here. Something they don’t want us to see.*

He pushed the thought aside and focused.

If his result was lower than this smug bald man’s—someone reliving some pathetic fantasy—Fleur would rather die.

“It begins.”

Fleur closed his eyes, entered the Spirit Zone.

The liquid bubbled. The water level in the glass tube began to rise—steadily, then faster.

“Whoa!”

A gasp rippled through the crowd.

All eyes locked onto the tube.

“T-two… twenty-eight…”

Affinity: 28%.

Murmurs exploded in every direction.

28%—a level reserved for the highest echelons of Nord Command. Even if he started learning ancient magic today, in a few years, he’d be unstoppable.

The bald man stood frozen, mouth agape. Slowly, he turned to Fleur.

Fleur, now wearing a faint, teasing smile, patted the man’s head.

“Then take good care of me from now on, *subordinate*.”

“N-no, this isn’t—”

“Don’t worry. I’m not interested in your bald head. Nothing to see there. Ho ho!”

Leaving the man red-faced and sputtering, Fleur returned to Shirone’s side.

She greeted him with a high five.

*As expected… Senior Fleur.*

A graduate of the Royal Magic School. A future archmage who reached 8th grade by age 22. In raw talent, he was among the kingdom’s elite.

“Now it’s your turn. Show them what you’ve got.”

Shirone stepped forward.

The murmurs died instantly.

The Light of District 73. The symbol of the rebellion.

This was the moment her true power would be revealed.

She took a breath, hands sinking into the liquid.

The tension in the air was suffocating. Her heart pounded. She clenched her jaw and plunged into the Spirit Zone.

**Perong!**

The liquid didn’t just rise—it *exploded*. A geyser erupted from the drum, spraying silver droplets in all directions.

“Wha—? Wha—?!”

The Norse stared, mouths hanging open.

The level in the tube had shot past four meters.

“Impossible…”

Affinity: 42%.

No one in Norse history had ever recorded such a number.

“What—what’s wrong with it? Is it broken?”

They all knew the answer.

Unlike Meccan machines, which could malfunction, this reaction was pure chemistry. It didn’t lie.

And yet—how could Shirone’s affinity surpass even Commander Reisis?

Reisis stood silent, her brow slightly furrowed.

*42%? Is that even possible?*

Without ancient magic, such a number was meaningless. But as a measure of raw magical talent? It was unprecedented.

The bald man roared. “This is wrong! 42%? That’s not possible!”

Others echoed him—Reisis’s loyalists, threatened by the shift in power.

“Exactly! Even if it’s true, Shirone doesn’t know ancient magic! Potential means nothing!”

“Our commander is Reisis!”

Shirone kept her hands submerged, lips parting in a quiet breath.

She hadn’t expected this. But she wouldn’t waste it.

She glanced back. Fleur met her gaze, smirked, and gave a thumbs-up.

*Crush it.*

Shirone turned forward.

And activated the Immortal Function.

The world within her spirit expanded—limitless, eternal.

The glass tube trembled.

70%. 80%. 90%.

“Wha—?! Wha—?!”

Every Norse stood rigid, necks craned upward.

Then—**Puong!**

The tube shattered. Silver liquid burst into the air like a glittering storm.

Just as Shirone had expected.

The Immortal Function—where spirit merged with the infinite—could not be measured by mere affinity.

“…Well,” Fleur said, breaking the silence. “I guess that’s that.”

No one spoke.

One thought echoed in every mind:

*Nephilim.*
