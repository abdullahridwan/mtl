Chapter 396  
Special Training (3)  
~*~

Serious training began.

Their practice centered on the shotgun movement, and during breaks Shirone studied the battery theory required for laser-guided technology. Thanks to Isabelle tutoring him for an hour each day, he was beginning to gain a rough understanding of radio waves.

When Fleur returned from work, she evaluated his progress for the day.

But no passing verdict ever came from her strict mouth.

“Too slow. Again.”

Shirone gritted his teeth and cast the shotgun movement. Each time, Fleur appeared in front of him and seized him by both shoulders.

He kept hoping luck would let him slip past her, but there seemed to be no room for error against a professional mage.

How is she finding me? No—can a human really react to this?

Her synesthesia was too precise to blame it all on Shirone’s lack of skill. Still, he had no reason to complain when an outstanding mage was guiding him. And so, day after day, his training piled up.

“Ahh!”

With a dull thud, Shirone rolled across the floor. His back arched, and his face twisted in pain.

He had been so focused on the photon output that he had misjudged the landing point of his teleportation.

Fleur looked down at him where he lay at her feet.

“Again.”

Flashes of light burst like firecrackers in the center of the training ground.

Photon output scattered in seven directions, and Shirone safely arrived at his destination. He had even landed in a place where he could not see Fleur’s face—the face he had grown tired of watching catch him over and over.

Done! I got it right this time!

He was certain he had shaken her off with the shotgun movement. As expected, Fleur had not moved from her original spot.

Then she closed her eyes and crossed her index fingers.

“Fail.”

Shirone gasped for breath and asked, “Why? The output was perfect, and even you couldn’t catch me.”

“Look where you’re standing.”

Shirone looked around his feet. He had chosen the right direction, and nothing seemed out of place.

“What’s wrong with here?”

“At a glance, you only moved about eight meters. Your teleportation compulsion is ten meters, isn’t it?”

“Ah…”

How could he have made such a mistake? It was the most basic of basics.

“Listen. Your teleportation compulsion must not waver under any circumstances. In real combat, hundreds of exchanges can pass in an instant, and you have to move through complex terrain at the same time. If your compulsion wavers, you die the moment it does. That’s why even high-ranking mages don’t change their compulsion during battle.”

“Yes. I’m sorry.”

At his dispirited answer, Fleur put a hand on her waist and snorted.

“The fact that your basics are weakening means you’ve become more technically proficient. But this is exactly when you can’t afford to get careless. Every application ultimately comes from solid fundamentals.”

Shirone clenched his fists and carved her words into his heart.

Basics. Basics were everything.

The next day, Shirone finally succeeded in the shotgun movement.

Fleur did not move, and his direction was correct. His teleportation distance was also a perfect ten meters.

“Ah… it finally worked.”

He was so tense that he could not even feel the joy of success.

But Fleur still did not smile. Instead, she approached him with a colder gaze than before, as if the real training started now.

“All right. Now let’s get to the main point—your own shotgun movement.”

Understanding what she meant, Shirone’s expression changed at once.

The shotgun movement itself was good magic, but the reason he had chosen it out of countless spellbooks was because it could be converted into attack magic.

Don’t be nervous. I’m just changing the photon output into Photon Cannon.

It had taken him three weeks to learn the shotgun movement. If he did not pass this step, he would lose several more days, and then he would have almost no time left to learn the advanced course: laser-guided magic.

“Begin. I’ll take it myself.”

Fleur’s eyes were completely different from when she had been evaluating the shotgun movement.

Unlike photon output, Photon Cannon was true offensive magic. Since this was the final evaluation, Fleur also had to face it seriously.

Shirone took a deep breath and measured the timing. He set the rhythm as though he would move one second later, then broke his opponent’s tempo by casting teleportation at the half-second mark.

As his body flashed, Photon Cannons burst out in every direction. At the same instant, Fleur moved.

Shirone arrived in the west.

The Photon Cannon that had been fired south flew toward Fleur, who had moved there.

The moment she sensed it through her Spirit Zone, Fleur’s head jerked back on instinct.

The Photon Cannon passed in front of her eyes and crashed into the wall, shaking the training ground with a heavy boom.

“Uh… how was it? Would that work in real combat?”

“…”

Fleur replayed what had just happened in her mind.

The pressure she had felt from the Photon Cannon had been far stronger than expected. Unlike most magic, its threat came from speed.

Quasi-light speed was a standard created to define light-speed phenomena realized through magic.

It was not the speed of light as it existed in the physical world, but to the eyes of living beings, it was indistinguishable from light. If someone shot an arrow with a luminous object attached to it at night, it would look like a flash.

However, the Photon Cannon she had just experienced was faster than she had expected. It was closer to a bullet fired from a gun. In other words, Shirone had properly matched its mass and output.

A greater mass than a magic bullet was being pushed forward at bullet speed. From the target’s perspective, that made it brutally solid.

The existing shotgun movement could only startle an opponent if it failed to catch them directly, but this version had real impact.

“Pass. This is enough for actual combat.”

Only then did life return to Shirone’s face.

“Whew, that’s a relief. Three weeks of hard work paid off. Thank you so much!”

There was nothing more gratifying for a mage than learning new magic, so Fleur, understanding Shirone’s feelings, erased her serious expression and smiled.

“You should hold on to that sensation for now. Do you want to keep practicing?”

“Of course. It’s important to remember the feeling of success. But don’t overdo it.”

From then on, Shirone practiced the shotgun movement until he was exhausted.

After two hours, there was no error in the power of his Photon Cannon no matter which of the eight directions he moved in.

“Aigo, my back.”

Fleur, who had been watching Shirone train, patted her waist after dodging another Photon Cannon.

If she had evaded with physical movement, it would have been one thing, but since she was using manipulation-type magic, the strain built up.

Once he masters something, he masters the intermediate level in two hours. Talent really is talent.

As she had read in an academic journal, Shirone’s gift was insight. He was a mage with an extremely strong tendency to dig into the core of a technique rather than scatter his attention across miscellaneous skills.

Perhaps that was what Gaold had pinned his hopes on.

“Whew.”

After training, Shirone looked up at the ceiling, his face covered in sweat.

The state of unity born from a sense of achievement was a miraculous interval in which one could improve in a short period of time.

Mages called it Fever.

“Are you finished?”

Shirone looked satisfied.

“Yes. I think I’ve gotten used to it to some extent.”

“Starting tomorrow, we’ll begin laser guidance technology. But before that, there’s something I need to point out.”

The joy of success vanished from Shirone’s eyes.

Whatever it was, he needed to hear it.

Living with the Association’s professional mages had naturally accustomed him to their way of thinking.

“After watching you for three weeks, I’ve realized your balance is badly off.”

“Yes? Balance?”

Fleur twirled her finger in the air, tracing the outline of Shirone’s Spirit Zone.

“The biggest problem is that your Spirit Zone is too large. It feels like it’s over eighty meters in diameter.”

“Is that a problem?”

“In real combat, the smallest details decide victory and defeat. You have to find the golden balance between density, volume, specific gravity, and durability. You have a tendency to overdo everything. How should I put it… you overpace yourself. If an opponent has a durability of one hundred, then one hundred power is enough. But you unconsciously pour in one hundred twenty or one hundred thirty.”

The Spirit Zone was the materialization of the mind, so it did not lie. Looking back on all the battles he had fought until now, Shirone thought she was probably right.

“It’s the same now. When your Spirit Zone overlaps with mine, your tension stabs into me. I’ve been wondering about it the whole time. Unless you’ve only been fighting monsters until now, it doesn’t make sense.”

Shirone could not deny it.

Looking back on the year since he had learned magic, there had not been many times when he had faced an equal opponent. At least by his standards, every enemy had been a monster.

When Shirone fell silent, Fleur understood as well. If he had grown through battles like that, then it made sense that his Spirit Zone had entered a constant state of display, like a wounded beast baring its fangs.

“In any case, it’s not good. I’ll help you, so let’s find your golden balance.”

Fleur went to the wall and flipped the Image Zone switch.

Shirone’s Spirit Zone became visible in bluish light. Then the lights in the training ground turned off, making the image even clearer.

Shirone simply watched the beautifully shining Spirit Zone.

At that moment, Fleur hugged him tightly from behind.

“Uh, um…”

“Stay still.”

Fleur cut off his words in a calm, subdued voice.

Then she opened her Spirit Zone, and through synesthesia, Shirone’s Spirit Zone came through to her clearly.

He really is tense. Almost like a mage on a battlefield.

Fleur closed her eyes, concentrated, and instructed him.

“Now close your eyes and take a deep breath. Reduce your Spirit Zone until you feel most comfortable.”

Shirone relaxed his body and mind.

His Spirit Zone, which had been inflated like a balloon, gradually shrank. As it did, he felt his head become lighter.

“Good… keep going…”

As the Spirit Zone shrank further, tension he had never noticed until now began to reveal itself.

“Yes… a little more…”

Fleur sensed Shirone with every nerve.

“More. More… That’s it. Yes, good.”

Fleur’s voice faded there.

Before he knew it, Shirone had lowered his head and was savoring an infinitely warm sensation. It was warm and peaceful, as if he were in his mother’s arms.

Fleur’s eyelids slowly lifted, as though she were waking from sleep.

“…How is it?”

“Yes. This feels good.”

Fleur took her hands from Shirone’s waist and slowly stepped back, softening her footsteps as she walked to the Image Zone device.

When she looked at the instrument panel, it showed a diameter of 62.8 meters.

The lights came on, and Shirone opened his eyes as well. His mind was clear, as if he had slept for a long time.

“Remember this feeling. Size, shape, sensation, density—everything. Everything contained within a sphere 62.8 meters in diameter is your current golden balance.”

Shirone understood what she meant. The higher the density, the clearer his surroundings became, and the stronger his durability was.

Above all, his thoughts flowed without obstruction, like water.

“It’s really comfortable. But at 62.8 meters, it hasn’t increased much compared to a year ago. Is that all right?”

“Yes. That’s more than enough. Unless you’re a sniper, you don’t need an overly large Spirit Zone. How often do you fight an enemy at a distance you can’t even see? Even if you only learn the Four Ways, your range will more than double.”

“That’s true, but…”

“Trust me. For a search-type zoner, the diameter of the Spirit Zone has to exceed at least one kilometer. A communication mage uses a detached Spirit Zone and needs to transmit information dozens of kilometers away. However, some battle mages have Spirit Zones only ten meters in diameter. Instead, they put everything into density and durability.”

In other words, what mattered was reaching a mental state that allowed one to draw out one hundred percent of the efficiency of their magic.

“Don’t be misled by vanity. It hardens your mind. Of course, it’s essential for a battle mage to estimate an opponent’s capabilities numerically. But it’s dangerous to imagine the outcome in advance. No trait has an absolute advantage or absolute disadvantage. Whether something is good or bad changes depending on the opponent, and victory or defeat depends on how well your style can respond to different situations.”

Shirone nodded with resolve.

“Yes. I’ll keep that in mind.”
