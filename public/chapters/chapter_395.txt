Special Training (2)

“Good. I’ve picked one for now.”

Almighty was far more difficult than the schema for Shotgun Movement. Even so, Shirone considered himself fortunate. Anything that required a strong Spirit Zone played to his strengths.

By his rough estimate, he could master it in about a month.

‘Then I can learn one more. Should I raise the difficulty for this one?’

Shirone headed toward the shelves where the newest magic books had been placed and scanned the advanced-difficulty section.

Books such as [Time Lock Using Quantum Transformation] and [Magic Circle Interference According to Wave Principle] were lined up side by side, their titles alone enough to make his skin crawl.

Most of them were related to information magic as well, which meant they were not techniques he needed immediately.

‘Pass. Pass. That one too.’

Shirone read through the titles one by one as he moved slowly down the aisle. Since they were all for advanced users, there was not a single thin volume among them.

“Oh? What about this?”

He stopped in front of the book in the final slot.

It was about as thick as the others, and it was not information magic. More than anything, however, the title caught his eye.

Laser Guidance Technology.

“Laser…”

Laser magic required an extremely high level of energy output, but Shirone had already reached that stage with ease through the use of God Particles. The ability to convert mass into energy was an enormous privilege.

For ordinary mages, however, laser magic was a field they could only conquer after years of practice. Whoever had developed laser guidance technology had to be quite an expert.

“Should I take a look?”

The book had the white cover of the photon series, but a red band wrapped around it, giving it a distinctly laser-like feel.

When Shirone checked the developer’s name beneath the title, his lips parted in surprise.

Louis Jacobin.

‘Ah, Jacobin…’

As the director of the particle laboratory under Tormia’s dragon, he was a mage anyone studying photon magic would know.

Shirone opened the book. Most of the pages were filled with complicated formulas.

He skipped over the detailed equations for now and looked at the outline of the magic. As he read, the calm expression on his face gradually heated with excitement.

“This is amazing.”

It was fusion magic that combined two principles of the photon system.

Magic manifested through concentration, so in principle, a mage had only one slot for thought. Advanced mages, however, combined two or even three attributes in order to bring out the strengths of several classes at once.

In short, the magic developed by Jacobin was photon magic equipped with a laser guidance device.

Ordinarily, bending light required an optical fiber or some kind of special medium. With laser guidance, however, the trajectory could be altered by directing the target with a laser.

Most importantly, it was possible to track a moving target as long as it remained within the guidance range.

Jacobin had likely developed the technique for the purpose of transmitting information, but in Shirone’s mind, the arcing trajectory of a Photon Cannon was already being drawn with perfect clarity.

‘This is what Flew was talking about. Magic that compensates for weaknesses and creates synergy.’

If he applied spin to a Photon Cannon containing mass, he could make it curve to some extent. But in terms of predictive shooting, the efficiency was too low, and the angle of the curve was not large.

‘But if I learn laser guidance technology…’

He would be able to hit an opponent no matter where they moved. The precision would vary depending on his level of mastery, but for Shirone, who specialized in explosive firepower over a short period of time, it was the perfect magic for reducing wasted mental power.

‘Great. This will be my first fusion magic.’

With the magic book in hand, Shirone went to find Flew. She was browsing in a section lined with flame-related books, one hand resting on a high shelf, when she turned her head.

“Hm? Did you pick everything?”

“Yes. These two.”

Flew looked back and forth between the two books and nodded. One of them was an advanced text, but it was not a bad choice, and there was no need for him to be intimidated beforehand.

“Good. I think they’re compatible with you, at least. Let’s talk to Isabelle and borrow them.”

The two of them brought the books to Isabelle, who processed the card.

Then, after looking at the magic book on laser guidance technology, Isabelle asked with a surprised expression, “This one is quite difficult. Will you be all right?”

“Yes. I can already use lasers. But is it that hard?”

“It’s a specialized field that isn’t covered at school. In particular, since it uses radio waves, you’ll probably find it unfamiliar.”

The laser Shirone used was different from that of ordinary mages.

God Particles had allowed Shirone to leap over barriers that should have taken years to climb, but because he lacked a precise understanding of the underlying schema, the range of his applications was limited.

“I’ll try. I definitely want to use this chance to learn lasers properly.”

If that was how he felt, Isabelle had no reason to stop him.

“Well, it’s rare for photons to be used as offensive magic, so I understand why you’d be eager. If there’s anything you don’t understand while studying, ask me. The president of the association instructed me to teach you personally if necessary.”

Shirone was grateful for Gaold’s consideration, but at the same time, it frightened him.

Gaold truly believed that Shirone could develop magic capable of destroying Heaven.

“Thank you. I’ll probably be back soon.”

“Haha! Yes. Come anytime.”

Having warned her in advance so she would not be surprised later, Shirone got on the elevator.

The iron door of the magic library closed before him.

He felt it every time he came here, but it was like being in another dimension.

* * *

With Flew’s help, Shirone obtained a private training ground. Strictly speaking, it had been arranged by Gaold’s order.

It was not as spacious as the underground bunker, but it was large enough for him to expand his Spirit Zone and run wild on his own.

A sleeping capsule had been installed in the corner, and the floor was made of the same material as the Two Thousand Training Ground. Once the simulation device was activated, it seemed he would be able to analyze his magic from various angles.

Shirone sat on the cold floor and opened the magic book for Shotgun Movement.

He quickly read it from beginning to end, then closed the book and organized his thoughts.

“Hmm. It looks easy, but it also looks difficult.”

The key was the linkage speed between passive magic and active magic. The instant he cast Teleport, he had to fire Photon Output in every direction. The principle itself was simple, but the technique only had value if the connection speed felt almost simultaneous.

“Should I try it once?”

Standing in the training ground, Shirone cast photonization magic. His body tingled with a familiar sensation, and the scenery in front of him crumpled.

He arrived ten meters ahead, looked up at the ceiling, and fell into thought.

He had meant to use Shotgun Movement, but it had not activated at all.

“Oh, I see.”

Once Teleport was triggered, the slot emptied. But the movement was so fast that there was no time to cast Photon Output afterward.

‘Does that mean I have to switch schemas much faster?’

If that was the case, Shirone had a talent he could rely on.

Time-sharing.

After deciding to approach it from that direction, Shirone attempted Shotgun Movement based on time-sharing.

By the time the sun set, he had slowly begun to get the hang of it. In the middle, he used the sleeping device for about an hour to recover his mental power, then pushed his concentration to the limit as if he were making a final spurt.

At last, flashes of light shot out in eight directions at the same time as he teleported.

Having moved north along the dazzling route of Shotgun Movement, Shirone looked back and could not hide his excitement.

“I did it! It worked!”

“What worked?”

Shirone turned toward the door.

Flew was leaning crookedly against the wall with her arms crossed. Her expression was stern, and she seemed to be in a bad mood.

“Huh? Senior, when did you get here?”

“Just now. More importantly, what did you just do?”

“What? Shotgun Movement, of course.”

“Hmm. Is that so?”

Flew stepped into the training ground.

She stopped about ten meters away from Shirone and looked around, as if checking the coordinates, then turned back to him.

“Try it again. I’ll evaluate you.”

Her firm tone made Shirone a little uneasy.

But no matter how he thought about it, the Shotgun Movement he had just performed matched the form written in the magic book exactly.

“Yes. Then I’ll start from the basic route…”

“No. You don’t have to. Take the intermediate course. Choose any of the eight directions and move where you want. Judging by what I saw a moment ago, you can manage that much.”

It was a compliment, yet strangely, it did not sound like one.

In any case, he was only just learning the technique, so Shirone did not feel much pressure about failing.

‘Let’s go southeast.’

As Shirone concentrated, Flew crossed her arms and waited for him to demonstrate the magic.

‘Shotgun Movement!’

At the same time Shirone’s body was dyed in golden light, flashes spread out like an explosion.

He arrived exactly ten meters to the southeast and clenched his fists.

‘Success. I succeeded.’

For a moment he was intoxicated with joy—then he shuddered in surprise and jerked his upper body back.

Flew had arrived in front of him. She reached out and grabbed Shirone by both shoulders.

Her eyes were half-closed, as calm as they had been when she subdued the terrorist.

Normally, she was a bothersome senior who nagged too much. But at moments like this, she was undeniably a certified Class 8 mage.

“Uh, um…”

“Now do you understand what your problem is?”

In terms of form, Shotgun Movement had succeeded. However, Flew had correctly identified Shirone’s true position among the eight flashes.

Not only that, she had moved faster than the caster himself and blocked his path.

“Are you saying the linkage is too slow?”

Only then did Flew let go of Shirone’s shoulders.

“Right. It isn’t something you can catch with your eyes, but you can feel it intuitively through the Spirit Zone. Especially for a manipulation-type mage like me, the delay is even clearer.”

“But I did my best.”

“This isn’t a matter of effort. In fact, your connection just now was excellent. I think you switched at a speed comparable to time-sharing…”

Flew thought for a moment, then spoke with certainty.

“But that is the problem.”

Shirone tilted his head.

Time-sharing was a method of thought in which two schemas were switched at high speed within a single slot.

The thought process itself could be slow, but there was no faster way to replace one schema with another.

“Let me explain the mechanism you used just now,” Flew said. “You equipped and cast Teleport, removed Teleport, then equipped and cast Photon Output.”

She represented the connection between the two magics by inserting and withdrawing both fists.

It meant that because the process went through at least three stages, there was a delay large enough for the synesthesia of the Spirit Zone to capture.

But that was the limit of what Shirone could currently do.

“If there’s still a delay even at time-sharing speed, then what am I supposed to do?”

“Crash them.”

“Yes?”

“Crash them. Don’t remove Teleport first. Strike it with Photon Output and blow it out. Then, at the same moment you blow it away, insert Photon Output into the slot.”

Flew clenched both fists and thrust them forward.

When she hit her left hand with her right, her left hand was knocked away and her right hand took its place.

It was a visual representation of what she had just explained.

“This way, removal and installation finish at the same time. That shortens the mechanism, doesn’t it? You need to be able to do that in the realm of thought. Strike an old thought with a new one. Mages call that ‘Force.’”

Flew showed him the process again. This time, to convey the actual feeling, she placed her fists almost touching and collided them quickly.

A sharp cracking sound came from her mouth.

“Do you understand? Skip the removal process. Just strike and enter. If you want to work in the field someday, you need to know how to use Force. Passive-active linkage is used regardless of class.”

Shirone listened blankly.

To knock one thought away with another.

Ordinary people never thought in such a way. But for a mage, it was an extremely efficient method of thinking.

“Force…”

“I’m not surprised you don’t know. This is a practical skill. They don’t teach it at school.”

“Is it dangerous?”

“No. It’s just less stable. When you crash thoughts together, the new one has to enter the slot exactly. It relies so heavily on instinct that it isn’t worth putting into the regular curriculum. Time-sharing is also a professional-level skill. The fact that you learned it on your own is amazing. You’ll probably learn Force quickly too.”

At Flew’s words, Shirone regained his confidence.

His first attempt at Shotgun Movement had ended in failure, but he had gained the chance to learn a new skill that could not be taught at school.

The key was Force.
