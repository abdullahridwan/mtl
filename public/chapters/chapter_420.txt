Chapter 420
Battle Readiness (3)
~*~
“Isn’t that amazing, Fermi? How much air did you compress for 1,000 presses?”
“Yeah. Even if it hits the armor, the impact will be considerable.”
Shirone wasn’t impressed. In the past, he might have at least clapped, but Fermi’s feat still paled in comparison to Gaold’s air gun.
Fermi had conquered the master difficulty step by step. Finally, applause erupted when he completed the tenth stage of the aerial acrobatics movement item. It was an acrobatic sequence impossible without mastering the basics of both the press and blow techniques.
“Fermi, all masters. That’s 100 points.”
It was the moment Fermi reclaimed the top spot in the senior class.
“Truly excellent.”
It was rare for Iruki to offer such praise.
This time, even Shirone had no grounds to argue. It was, after all, an All Master achievement.
“Yeah. I think his fundamentals are very solid.”
“No, that’s not what I mean. Even last year, Fermi was an all-master. It’s just that his major changed.”
Shirone’s head snapped toward him.
“You said your major was different?”
“Yes. I registered Earth Magic this year. The year before last, I achieved all-master status with fire magic.”
*Is it really possible?* Shirone thought. *It’s absurd to switch majors every year and still maintain all-master status.*
A high-ranking wizard would struggle with student-level difficulty, making Fermi’s feat even more nonsensical. Yet Fermi was merely a student.
Shirone stared blankly at Fermi. As he made a throat-slitting gesture with his thumb, the memories of their past survival assessment surfaced.
Fermi had achieved all-master status and cemented his position as the undisputed number one. It was an event so significant that even the students who had been closely weighing Shirone against Fermi had to shift their expectations.
*Even if you regret it, it’s too late, kid. I won’t let you graduate until you lick my shoes.*
Seeing the cold anger in Fermi’s eyes, Shirone stood up. He turned to his friends and headed toward the training ground.
“Iruki, Nade. I’ll go up first.”
Nade and Iruki exchanged a glance. They trained together daily, and both knew Shirone wasn’t yet ready to challenge for All Master status.
“What are you thinking? A direct confrontation won’t yield anything. It’ll only expose your power. Worse, if you fail, you’ll be completely trampled.”
“What are you talking about? Let’s trust Shirone for now.”
All eyes in the arena focused on Shirone.
Amy and Arin turned their heads mid-conversation, while Kang’s eyes lit up with anticipation. Dante, who had been sitting on the ground, rested his chin on his hand and watched quietly.
*Fermi just went all-master. How are you going to respond?*
The evaluation teacher held up a sheet and called out, “Shirone, your specialty is God Particles. What are your challenge parameters?”
“Power at level 7, projectile speed at level 8, projectile speed at level 7, defense at level 7…”
Like Fermi, Shirone listed his chosen items.
Until the seventh week, Shirone’s average skill evaluation level had been 3.6. The levels he was now stating were more than double that.
“Finally, movement control at level 10.”
The teacher’s eyes widened.
Level 10 denoted Master Difficulty. Furthermore, the standard value for the God Particles movement control item was significantly higher than that of the photon series.
It was clear that Fermi’s All Master status had influenced Shirone’s choices. However, it was also true that the comparison wasn’t entirely apples-to-apples.
In fact, Shirone had only selected seven items, with difficulty levels ranging from 7 to 8, excluding movement.
“What? If you’re going to do it, don’t you say it, right?”
“You’re cheating. This will inevitably spark a rivalry between Fermi and Shirone among the other students.”
“That’s a massive misunderstanding. Those levels wouldn’t even scratch Fermi. It’s a typical first-year mistake. After all, what can a freshman possibly do?”
The majority of Class Two agreed.
Dante remained neutral, analyzing the situation. He blinked rapidly, a faint smile touching his lips as he counted the parameters in his head.
*Hmm, that’s how it is…*
Meanwhile, Fermi and his group, watching from the hill, were already convinced of victory.
“Keukke, is this the final push? Doesn’t it look cute like this? Isn’t that right, Fermi?”
The Electric Monster Lichen turned to Fermi.
Fermi was laughing. But Lichen, who had stood by him for years, recognized the smile for what it was: the expression Fermi wore when he was most furious.
*That damn bastard…*
Shirone’s first evaluation was a single-attribute power item. Though it didn’t exist in the photon series, it was a newly established metric based on the fact that God Particles relied on mass.
When he closed his eyes and focused his mind, a sphere of light materialized, compressing at a terrifying speed as if pushing against space itself.
As the students watched the compressed vibrations, their eyes turned golden, and the photon cannon roared to life.
The flash of light embedded in the target sphere shattered into fragments, and the numbers on the instrument panel spun rapidly to calculate the power.
2,242 crashes.
It was a figure that far exceeded the 1,800 crashes required for level 7 single-attribute power.
After checking the score, Shirone glanced back at Fermi. The students suddenly realized the implication and began to murmur.
When converted to energy units, 2,242 crashes equaled approximately 8,200 presses. In other words, the destructive force generated by compressing the atmosphere with 8,200 presses translated to 2,242 crashes.
This was 7.3 times higher than Fermi’s recorded 1,123 presses, starkly revealing the raw power of God Particles.
Shirone’s evaluation continued.
In the projectile speed category, the photon cannon reached 100 meters per second, a result that far surpassed Fermi’s projectile velocity metrics.
Pfft-pfft-pfft-pfft!
Shirone fired the photon cannon at a target in front of him at blazing speed.
Even in broad daylight, the surroundings sparkled, and the students’ eyes were filled with the afterimages of golden lines.
“165 rounds per minute. It’s a level 7 pass.”
The standard for Fermi’s rate-of-fire master level was 160 rounds per minute.
Having already outperformed Fermi in three categories, the students finally understood Shirone’s strategy.
“I see. So that guy Shirone…”
The seven items Shirone had chosen as challenges were all applicable to the Air series as well.
“He’s surpassing all of Fermi’s criteria.”
When the students turned to look at Fermi, he was staring at Shirone with his palms covering his face. Only his closest colleagues knew that his eyes were burning with murderous intent.
Shirone hadn’t achieved All Master status.
Yet, even so, he was scoring higher in the same categories than Fermi, who had.
The students felt the same way. Air-type magic could be completely overpowered at the level 7 God Particles threshold.
“That bastard child…”
Fermi’s colleagues were startled. It was the first time his resentment had been so blatantly displayed.
Feeling the weight of countless stares, Fermi finally lowered his hands and forced a smile.
*A calculated smile. The businessman’s smile.*
Entrepreneurs had to stay relaxed. Even if a debt was strangling you and you were on the verge of having a knife plunged into your back, as long as you maintained confidence, a way out was always a business opportunity.
Of course, the crisis was real. The students were already halving Fermi’s reputation expectations and redirecting them to Shirone. The Air series was locked at level 7 God Particles. The strategy of inflating his standing by directly comparing numbers with Fermi was working.
“As expected, Shirone hyung is amazing.”
Eider approached from behind Iruki. His major was also Air, so it was only natural that he felt a sting of bitterness.
“It’s different from talent. How do you beat someone like that?”
“Talent?”
“Yes. The photon system’s advantage is that it has no mass. But Shirone hyung can even give it mass. No one can beat that.”
Iruki also acknowledged Shirone’s magical talent. But he didn’t believe Shirone had reached this point solely because of it.
Talent was merely a tool. The speed of a person digging with bare hands versus one using a shovel was inevitably different. If magic was the land, talent was the shovel.
However, no matter how much talent one held, they would never dig the ground unless they used their arms, waist, and legs.
That was why even a moment of breakthrough was merely the explosion of accumulated daily training.
Shirone had been working hard day by day, breaking through his limits every single day. Even if the results weren’t immediate, they eventually accumulated and manifested.
All success in the world began with the certainty that the future would always arrive.
“I don’t think anyone is born with bare hands.”
“Yes?”
Eider looked down at Iruki.
“Some are born with a shovel, some with a hammer, and some with a pen. The one with a shovel digs faster, but they can’t write well with it. Talent isn’t something handed to you; it’s something you discover and develop yourself. The Air series has capabilities that only it can utilize. If you can’t find your own path, don’t just rely on someone else’s. Talent is also hard work.”
Eider chewed over those words. Though spoken in response to the dismissal of Shirone’s efforts, they were advice worth more than gold.
“Yes. Thank you, Iruki.”
Iruki raised his eyebrows, then lowered them. His calm, almost grateful demeanor was very much like the youngest among them. It was precisely why Eider had no enemies.
“Finally, movement control. Master difficulty. The response time is less than 0.8 seconds.”
Shirone took a deep breath and cleared his mind. He had pushed himself to the limit across the previous six items and felt a wave of dizziness, but it was nothing compared to his practical combat experience.
The evaluation teacher activated the red-line certified evaluation magic: navigation.
A small sphere materialized, inside which an arrow spun rapidly, pointing in random directions like a compass that had lost its polarity.
Movement control was an item that required the wizard to accurately execute the movement suggested by the navigation system. The shape, length, and color of the arrow indicated direction, distance, and speed, respectively. The wizard had to complete the movement within the allotted reaction time.
The God Particles master difficulty required a 0.8-second reaction speed. It was 0.2 seconds faster than the photon series master difficulty of 1 second.
At the nerve level, 0.2 seconds was an enormous gap.
Nevertheless, the association had set the standard because they believed rapid acceleration and sudden stops using mass would be possible.
“A reaction time of 0.8 seconds?”
The students looked puzzled.
Of course, photon-type magicians differentiated time and built out a series of processes in advance. Patrol, rainbow drop, and other techniques were applications of that pre-planned approach.
However, movement control wasn’t an item that moved according to pre-set mental commands.
The navigation system would suggest a completely different direction, speed, and distance than expected, forcing the wizard to react immediately.
In such a situation, a 0.8-second reaction speed felt brutally harsh.
*No, the association’s judgment is correct.*
Shirone recalled Collie’s teachings. After all, it was a standard because someone had already achieved it.
He had heard recently that Etela’s movement control response speed was 0.1 seconds, a feat classified as superhuman. At that level, one could traverse a densely forested area simply by teleporting. He had also heard that you could evade attacks by weaving your upper body, feeding back instantaneous movements in place.
Of course, as a student, Shirone couldn’t take comfort in the fact that a famous continent-wide monk could do it.
However, he fully understood the association’s philosophy.
Do not judge God Particles by the standards of the photon series.
It was an ability unique to Shirone that no one else possessed.
*I conquer with my skill.*
A light rose from Shirone’s body like fire. The flames gradually expanded, taking the shape of a pair of giant wings before burning brightly.
“I-what is that?”
The students widened their eyes. It was a magic they had never seen or heard of before.
