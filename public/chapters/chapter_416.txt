Chapter 416  
One-Man Team (2)  
~*~

The graduating class schedule had already entered its sixth week.

The competition grew more intense by the day, and the rankings in each section shifted constantly. Yet not a single student had changed classes.

The teachers clicked their tongues, saying this was the first time it had happened in five years. The main reason was that Class 2, which usually had the most volatile rankings, had frozen in place.

Class 1 kept holding Class 2 down, while Class 3 chased desperately from below. In other words, the overall level of this graduating class was higher than in any previous year.

One factor was the unusual survival evaluation, where as many as fifteen students had scored 20 points. But most of those students belonged to Class 3, so it had not led to many changes.

Today, however, was exactly the kind of day when the fierce ranking competition could truly show its teeth.

A strategy-tactics evaluation awaited the senior students, and the groups would be formed according to their current rankings.

“Well, then I’ll be in Group 5.”

Shirone knew without even looking at the table. He had already memorized the rankings of all thirty students.

Group 5 consisted of 21st through 25th place. Naturally, 21st place would serve as the group leader, and Shirone, ranked 23rd, would be included in that group.

Since 21st place Dante and 22nd place Iruki had continued to earn the same number of points as Shirone, their rankings had not changed since the first week.

The 24th and 25th places were Canis and Closer. Judging by the members alone, it was a team capable of scoring highly.

On the other hand, there were groups that had every reason to weep over their rankings.

A typical example was Group 4.

As a group made up of students from the lower ranks of Class 2, its members were Screamer, Amy, Suabi, Maya, and Eider, in that order.

Most of them had managed to maintain their positions, but Screamer had fallen from 11th to 16th in just six weeks.

‘I’m so irritated I’m going crazy. When am I going to crawl out of this abyss?’

As a Bookmaker, Screamer’s easiest source of points was, of course, one-on-one combat.

But this year, the matchups had been terrible. The strongest students of Class 1 had been clustered in the early rounds, and because certain students’ draw strategies had worked against him, he had twice ended up with only 1 point out of 3.

Of course, the real cause of his collapse was the 20 points he had lost to the red team during the survival evaluation.

“Hey, everyone gather around. Let’s start by deciding positions.”

At Screamer’s gesture, the other four members of Group 4 gathered around him. Looking at them, Screamer let out a sigh.

‘This balance is seriously awful.’

There was a measure scouts used called “party contribution.”

In a five-person party, the total contribution score was set at 5, and points were distributed according to how much each member contributed. It could not perfectly measure individual ability, since it evaluated party formation and role performance, but there was no better indicator for determining who was carrying their weight.

If someone’s contribution was 1 point, they had done their share. If it was 2 points, it meant they had done the work of two people.

A scout would certainly pay attention to someone like that, but it also meant someone else had failed to pull even one person’s weight. In other words, the party balance was poor.

The ideal ratio was 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, with every member properly fulfilling their role. But Screamer’s current group could not even dream of that golden balance.

The cause was Maya.

In Screamer’s opinion, Maya’s contribution in Group 4 would be 0.5 at best.

That meant the other four members would each have to compensate for the missing half-point if they wanted the group to be competitive.

‘If Maya is 0.5, Eider is 0.8, Suabi is 1.1, and Amy is 1.2, then I’m… 1.4? You’ve got to be kidding me.’

A difference of 0.4 in party contribution was enormous.

It meant he would have to raise the combat power of a five-person party by 8 percent all by himself.

‘Hmm. Is it possible? We do have two of them.’

Although Suabi and Maya had different majors, both specialized in utility.

Normally, he would never put two utility casters in a five-person party. But if Screamer himself was included, the story changed.

A magician who possessed both magic and physical ability gained far greater returns from buffs than an ordinary magician did.

‘I’m glad we have Amy. I’ll stir them up, and Amy will pour firepower into them. In the end, I’ve got no choice but to drag this team along by the collar.’

Once he finished organizing his thoughts, Screamer clapped his hands with a forced cheerfulness that did not suit the mood. In any case, he was the leader of Group 4. He needed to unite the team.

“All right. I’m going to assign positions now, so listen carefully. If we build the strategy properly, we can get a high score.”

Screamer pointed at himself first.

“First, I’m the main dealer. That’s the core of the plan. You know what I mean?”

“The Bookmaker’s Deadly Strategy?”

Screamer nodded at Amy’s words.

“Exactly. So, Suabi, you stay on me and keep buffing me. Haste, Concentration, Healing—everything. Don’t hold anything back. Even if this is the sixth week, there won’t be anyone stronger than me once I’m fully buffed. We’ll settle things early. Got it?”

“Ah… okay. I’ll do my best.”

Suabi answered in a voice that sounded half-dead. There was no fighting spirit in him at all, but Screamer ignored that.

Even if Suabi was indecisive and had terrible interpersonal skills, he was still a genius who had earned perfect marks in utility by linking more than twenty types of buff magic. Once given a role, he would at least do his own work.

“Amy, you’re the assistant dealer. Well, with you, I don’t think I need to give detailed instructions. And Maya…”

Screamer looked at her.

“What magic can you use? I think it’d be better if you handled a wide-area buff.”

The advantage of sound magic was that it affected a wide radius.

“Huh? The one I’m most confident in is Aria on the Battlefield. It amplifies mana through resonance.”

“Hmm…”

Aria on the Battlefield was a spell that produced a considerable effect even in actual combat.

However, the story changed if Maya was the caster. Her buff efficiency was far below average.

Screamer made a slight adjustment to the Bookmaker’s Deadly Strategy.

“Then let’s go with a debuff instead. Do you have anything you can cast on enemies?”

“Rhapsody of Oscillation. It amplifies anxiety.”

“Good. Use that. It’ll be more effective to cast it on hundreds of enemies than on five allies.”

“Yes. I’ll do my best.”

Maya nodded deeply, her expression resolute.

“Brother, what should I do?”

Eider asked, supporting his chin in his hand.

Perhaps because he was younger and felt less pressure about graduation, he did not seem as tense as Maya.

“Multiplayer. I’m going to charge in aggressively, so you confirm the kills. Finish them off for sure. It’s not uncommon for the whole board to turn over because one enemy was left alive.”

“Okay. I can do that much.”

“All right! Let’s do this properly! Let’s show them what we’ve got.”

Amy, who knew Screamer’s personality, felt goosebumps rise at his uncharacteristic show of enthusiasm.

‘He must really want to get out of here.’

Still, she had doubts about whether the Bookmaker’s Deadly Strategy would actually work.

In the past, Screamer had been famous even among back-alley thugs for fists that made people tremble. She could not rule out the possibility that he planned to solve everything alone.

‘Well, my rank is low right now too. For now, let’s go with this.’

The other members could have voiced their opinions, but suggestions were only effective when they matched the leader’s tendencies.

Meanwhile, Shirone’s Group 5 was also waiting for the evaluation, but unlike Group 4, they did not come up with any special operation.

Dante stood with Closer, Shirone with Iruki, and only Canis kept to himself a short distance away.

Of course, he was not alone on the mental channel.

—It’s rather boring. So this is school.

It was Harvest, who had cut off communication with the outside world for a long six months while finishing his deciphering of the [Book of Light and Darkness].

However, since the ranking battle in the senior class was so fierce, he had not revealed himself yet.

—Be patient. If we endure for one year, we can do what we want.

—Is that so? Well, you are much stronger now. Graduation should be simple enough.

The [Book of Light and Darkness], obtained from the Akashic Records of Heaven, contained a theory that amplified the power of darkness using the power of incarnation.

In other words, its central idea was that light strengthened darkness.

Canis had not yet reached the level where he could draw out concrete magic from that realization, but that insight alone had made his mana far stronger than before.

—Graduation isn’t the problem. This time, I have to surpass Shirone and follow Master’s will.

—Keuk. That should be quite entertaining.

Once the preparations in the 2,000th training ground were complete, the instructor spoke.

“It is five minutes before the match begins. The mission for week six is ‘Eradication.’”

All thirty students entered the 2,000th training ground.

The evaluation would be conducted simultaneously, and rankings would be assigned in order of which group cleared its monsters first.

The enormous 2,000th training ground was divided into six sections, and Shirone entered Group 5’s evaluation area.

Once the members gathered, Dante spoke briefly, exactly as his personality suggested.

“Well, since we’ve ended up together, let’s work hard.”

Shirone asked, “What about strategy or tactics? You’re the leader, and your major is information processing, so I think it’d be fine if you decide.”

“Tactics? Hmm…”

Dante rested his chin on his hand and looked over the members.

Iruki of Explosion. Shirone of Flash. Closer of Assault. Canis of Darkness.

“Just kill them fast. No conditions.”

“Okay.”

At the same time, the five of them turned and moved toward the center of the area.

After three minutes of warm-up, the evaluation instructor announced, “We will now begin the strategy-tactics evaluation.”

By the sixth week, no further explanation was necessary.

Green, yellow, and red lights lit up in order on the display board, and a large number of monsters of the same types appeared in all six areas.

‘It’s starting.’

Shirone entered the Spirit Zone.

The first thing he needed to determine was the species and their numbers.

Bat Jargol flew through the sky. Like monsters, each one was about the size of an eagle.

On the ground were bipedal walking monsters: Warwolves.

And starting this week, one more species had been added—giant Earthworms that dug underground tunnels as they moved.

‘This week is Tier 9 too.’

Judging by species alone, they were worth fighting. The problem was that there were more than three hundred of them.

Awooooo!

The Warwolves burst into wolf howls and charged.

The Jargol swooped in while emitting ultrasonic cries, and the Earthworms spat acid from dozens of holes in the ground.

Everyone in Group 5 instinctively took position.

Shirone and Iruki cast magic at the Jargol. Photon Cannons and atomic explosions rapidly detonated among the bats.

The ground was handled by Dante and Closer working together.

Dante installed a space-movement magic circle ten meters in front of him, then scattered instant magic circles all over the area.

The Warwolves caught in the instant magic circles were automatically transported into the main magic circle at the center.

Once about twenty of them had gathered, Closer cast his magic.

“Eruption!”

The ground heated from friction, and the Warwolves looked down.

At the same time, a small volcanic eruption burst upward, blowing away the entire main magic circle.

“Ha ha ha! Send more, Dante! More!”

“Oh, yeah?”

As Dante laid even more magic circles across the ground, three enemies per second began pouring into the main magic circle.

Closer unleashed every kind of magic he had, like a fish in water.

If he did not have to worry about aiming, earth-type magic was among the strongest in terms of raw physical force. With Dante gathering the targets for him, the monsters could not withstand it.

Rock and Roll swelled like a snowball and rolled across the ground, smashing into the Warwolves.

Huge Heavy Stones dropped from the air and crushed the enemies.

Then, with three consecutive Eruptions—Eruption, Eruption, Eruption—all the monsters on the ground were devastated.

Canis stood with his hands in his pockets.

Earthworm acid flew at him from every direction, but it could not pierce his Dark Skin.

Of course, that did not mean he was doing nothing. His Spirit Zone was locating every monster underground.

At last, his right hand emerged from his pocket.

‘If the sky is a world of light…’

His index and middle fingers rose upright.

“Then underground is a world of darkness.”

The power of darkness, shaped like a saw blade—Canis’s signature technique—slashed through the Earthworms as they rushed through the soil.

Kieeek! Kieek!

All of the Earthworms standing vertically went limp and vomited green fluid.

—328 monsters have been annihilated. Completion time: 3 minutes 28 seconds.
