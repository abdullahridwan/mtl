Main Character of Life (1)

Shirone, having completed the Trinity, opened his eyes. At the same moment, through the Multi-Law Network Small World Creation, Armand began a reverse assault on the Ice Queen’s law.

The world trembled, and starlight flared. Unable to contain Shirone any longer, the Ice Queen retched violently and spat him out.

“Ugh! Whoa! Aaaaagh!”

Amy’s face went pale as she watched from the ground. The sight of that enormous face straining so hard its eyes seemed ready to burst was beyond grotesque.

“Quaaaaaagh!”

With one last surge of power, the Ice Queen expelled Shirone completely. The instant he was free, he spread his wings wide to stop himself in midair, then sucked in a breath.

“Whoa! Whoa!”

It was the first time he had ever realized that even air had a taste.

“Shirone! Shirone’s alive!”

Amy, who had looked as if she herself were dying only moments ago, finally brightened. Not even the Ice Queen’s hideous appearance could overcome her joy at seeing Shirone return safe and sound.

But Dante’s eyes remained grave.

More than anything, the Ice Queen’s condition was strange.

Her face twisted into bizarre expressions no human facial muscles could ever produce. Yet those grotesque changes revealed her current emotions more accurately than any words could have.

‘Infinite? Infinite? Infinite? Infinite? Infinite?’

The Ice Queen was confused.

‘What is that?’

As Armand built his firewall, the Ice Queen had also copied Shirone’s information.

But there was a problem.

Shirone possessed the Immortal Function, something so difficult to analyze that even Optrus’s vast information could not fully decipher it.

An immense elation surged through the Ice Queen, exceeding the scale of her own law, and she could no longer control her mouth as it opened on its own.

She was approaching infinity.

That was the only thing the Ice Queen could understand.

Her jaws split again and again, stretching beyond the range her joints should have allowed, and she began to swallow herself in reverse.

A new being emerged from within her throat, like the flesh of a grape being peeled from its skin.

‘I am infinite.’

A human face was attached to each of its three sides, and the arms protruding from its shoulders multiplied into dozens, spreading out like a peacock’s feathers.

Three-Faced Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin.

It was Optrus’s second devotion, now having absorbed the Immortal Function.

“Galgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgal, galgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgal!”

A sound like thousands of beetles chittering at once burst from the mouth of the laughing Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin.

“Kuh!”

Shirone, struck head-on by the sudden sound wave, adjusted his hearing through Armand and pulled back. Then the head of the Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin turned. A face with upturned eyes and a downturned mouth moved to the front.

The Law of Wrath exploded, and countless balls of fire flew in every direction.

“Danger!”

As Lyria pointed at the rain of fire pouring down, Dante spread a huge defensive magic circle over their heads. The moment the fireballs struck it, the barrier roared and cracked.

“Keugh! Damn it!”

Dante grimaced. In an isolated space, a mental body’s growth should have been limited. But after swallowing Shirone, this mental body had become a powerful law incomparable to the Ice Queen.

It had to be the Immortal Function.

For a being that absorbed laws, there could be no better nourishment than Shirone.

“Prepare Park Yo-jin now!”

Lyria looked up at the battle in the sky, her eyes wavering. Shirone was firing Photon Cannons in rapid succession, but not even a scratch appeared on the Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin’s body.

“Not yet. Park Yo-jin’s range is much narrower than Bar Margin’s. If we can’t bring it here, activating it now will only get us killed.”

Amy shouted toward the sky.

“Shirone! Push the monster this way!”

Armand copied Amy’s voice. Mixed with noise, it was transmitted directly into Shirone’s mind.

“Push it?”

That meant they had found some way to deal with it on the ground. But how was he supposed to push a monster that did not even move when hit by a Photon Cannon?

‘I have no choice.’

Shirone widened his eyes and focused on increasing the Photon Cannon’s power. Then, through Mental Transcendence, his mind sought a vast expansion.

The Immortal Function could borrow infinite mental power, but it could not increase power beyond the limits of the Spirit Zone.

Mental Transcendence, however, forcibly strengthened the Spirit Zone itself.

Of course, just like with Divine Transcendence, the mental storm that followed was a separate problem.

Just as Lian’s arm had exploded when he swung his sword at a speed beyond what his body could withstand, Shirone’s mind could be destroyed if he tried to exceed the value it could handle.

—Expand focus points. Activate Overdrive.

But Shirone did not hesitate. Feeling a tremendous power he had never experienced before, he stretched out his hand.

Dozens of flashes of light struck the torso of the Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin head-on.

“Galgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgal, galgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgalgal!”

The body of the Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin, which had remained fixed in space as if nailed there, began to move back little by little. It fired sound waves in every direction, but Shirone did not stop his assault.

No—his power was growing stronger with every passing moment.

Two hundred fifty meters to Park Yo-jin’s installation point.

Those watching the battle from the ground were entranced by the sight of dozens of Photon Cannons flashing across the sky.

“How can magic like that…?”

Lyria, as a mage herself, was the most shocked. The reason mental bodies were classified as demigods was because they freely wielded laws. A being of law had to be sealed with law.

And yet Shirone was pushing back a demigod with pure physical force.

“…I thought he was just an innocent kid.”

Dante smiled bitterly at Lyria’s misunderstanding. Anyone who judged Shirone by his appearance made that mistake. He was a plain boy with no interest in dressing himself up, the sort who would sit in a café and casually mend his clothes.

But he was also the only boy who had ever defeated Dante.

“Well, he does look coolest when he’s being a wizard.”

Lyria was conflicted. She could see that the distance the Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin moved with each barrage was gradually shortening. If the Photon Cannon did not grow even stronger, it would be impossible to drive the monster to Park Yo-jin’s installation point.

“Lyria. Deploy Park Yo-jin. Whether Shirone succeeds or fails, if we’re going to do anything, this is our last chance.”

‘I… What should I do?’

If Park Yo-jin failed, the Thousand-Handed Guanshi Yin would escape into the world. Even if the kingdom had made preparations, judging by the scale of its law, it could raze a small city to the ground in a matter of hours.

Lyria turned to Dante and said, “I’ll prepare Park Yo-jin.”

She had no choice but to bet on Shirone. The people here were just as important as the people outside. This was not a situation she could solve by refusing to kill the monster.

“Good. Then I’ll explain the plan. If Shirone can push it another fifty meters, I’ll cast a defensive magic circle and trap the monster. It may not be as effective as Bar Margin, but it will at least serve as a safety device. In the meantime, Amy, prepare magic to support Shirone’s firepower.”

Amy nodded.

“Okay. I’ll cast my strongest magic.”

Zocre watched them fight with everything they had. Anger surged in him as he compared their desperation to his own inability to do anything.

Why were they risking their lives?

They could just leave. He would have to pay for all his sins anyway, and dealing with the aftermath was the kingdom’s responsibility.

“Why are you fighting?”

Dante, who was guarding Lyria, turned his head.

“What are you talking about? If you intend to interfere, I’ll kill you first.”

“Why are you fighting? You’re innocent, so just run away! Don’t you want to live? You’re all so strong, so talented! Are you saying you’re willing to die in a place like this when you have such bright futures ahead of you?”

Dante stared at Zocre in bewilderment. Then he sighed, as if he understood.

“Did you learn magic because you wanted to be successful?”

“What?”

“You’ve got it backward, don’t you? You learn magic because you like magic. Success follows once you become good at it. If I ran away from here, I’d feel sick to my stomach. I’m just fighting with the magic I learned. So don’t get in our way. Just watch quietly.”

Amy, having completed her sequence, opened her eyes.

“It’s ready. I’ll start now.”

As her crimson eyes radiated a red glow, two pillars of fire rose from beneath her feet and merged into a gigantic fireball.

It was an Inferno several times larger than the one she had fired at Zero Radius.

Zocre stared blankly at the flames blazing like the sun. Then, realizing something, he lowered his head.

‘They’re all insane about magic…’
