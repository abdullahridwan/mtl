Chapter 441  
Task Force (3)  
~*~

A mysterious bird of prey circled Galliant Island before spotting a cargo ship cutting through the open sea and dipping lower. Meanwhile, Zulu released his hold, and the four of them—including Shiina and Quan—began plummeting toward the water at high speed.

Zulu and Shiina activated flight magic, while Shirone spread her wings to regulate their descent. Though the open sea was unfamiliar territory for Prosecutor Quan, he kept his eyes fixed on the ship’s position without blinking. His technique—offsetting gravitational acceleration by generating external gravitational force through precise muscle vibrations—was every bit as formidable as any wizard’s spell.

Shiina quietly admired the technique of the death magician she had only ever heard about in rumors. Ten years ago, he had been hailed as an unrivaled prodigy in swordsmanship and movement. Even now, having shed his past vulnerabilities, he moved with the same lethal grace. *Come to think of it,* Shiina thought, *they really do look alike.*

They launched themselves toward the ship, landing silently on the deserted aft deck. Instantly, Zulu summoned *Autoprism*, cloaking them in invisibility. *As expected,* Shiina mused, *this kind of infiltration is a breeze when you gather talented people.* How many could possibly board a vessel at sea and reach Galliant Island without raising an alarm? If Sein were present, he could have masked their presence entirely with his *Equilibrium* skill. *No matter how treacherous this mission turns out to be,* she thought, *a team like ours won’t fall easily.*

They moved to a corner stacked with sacks of grain and crouched behind them, backs pressed together. “Once we dock, *Autoprism* will fail,” Zulu explained. “The port’s detection array, *Jonah*, will catch it. Flu will be waiting at the mid-junction, so we need to slip out completely undetected.”

Shiina handed a cloak to Quan. Zulu could already conceal his face with his current attire, and Shirone would simply activate *Diamond Armament* to handle any close encounters.

An hour later, the ship docked. Commotion erupted outside the warehouse, and the heavy doors swung open. The four slipped out just as the sailors began unloading. The dock was swarming with island workers, mechanics, freight handlers, and traders, so they blended in easily. Among the crowd, Jess—a longtime acquaintance of Shirone’s—was already hard at work, darting between ships to solicit customers.

“Hey! Need something lifted? I’ll guide you straight to the Kergo Ruins, the pride of Galliant! I speak the local dialect!”

Shirone adjusted the hood of her cloak and cast a quiet glance toward Jess. *Same old Jess,* she thought. *I hope Yuna is doing well.* The area hadn’t been empty since the parrot thieves had been driven out; life here was simply moving forward in its own rhythm.

“Huh? Who’s there?” Jess turned at a sudden whisper. Shirone gave a slight nod and melted into the crowd. She wanted to greet her, but there was no time. *Be well, Jess.*

Jess stared blankly after the hooded figures, a strange chill running down her spine. “Ugh, why is business so slow today? Jess, what’s wrong? You look like you’ve seen a ghost.”

“I don’t know… it’s just those people.”

Her companion frowned at the retreating cloaked figures. Jess’s usual paranoia was flaring up again. “Hey, don’t waste your breath on strangers. Just staring won’t pay the bills. If you get involved with the wrong crowd, you won’t even be found.”

“I agree,” another dockworker chimed in. “Ten years of soliciting, and my gut tells me the same thing. Those people… they reek of danger.”

Jess watched them disappear into the throng, a heavy sense of nostalgia settling over her. *I wonder how you’re doing, really.*

“Hey! Jim here! Need a hand with your bags?” A tall passenger disembarked from a ferry, struggling with two heavy trunks. Jess snapped out of her reverie, waved enthusiastically, and sprinted toward him. “Over here! I’ve got you!”

* * *

Kergo Ruins.

Gaold, Kang Nan, Sein, Zulu, Flu, Etela, Shiina, Quan, and Shirone. The nine members of the task force assembled for the first time. There was no time for a formal briefing; urgency dictated their movements. Though several guards patrolled the outer ruins, they were all focused on the long line of applicants waiting outside. Naturally, discretion was paramount, so keeping their faces covered was mandatory. Yet Gaold, the operation’s key operative, simply flipped back his hood, stripped off his cloak, and let it drop to the ground.

Kang Nan gritted her teeth. “Put your hood up. We’re trying to be subtle.”

“Tch. Anyway, if we don’t move now, the opportunity is gone. We’re heading straight into the Kergo Autonomous Region.”

Sein stepped forward. “If anyone is waiting at the Gate of Heaven, I’ll assess them based on their abilities. We might need to recruit additional members on the spot.”

They approached the terraced altar on the east side of the central temple—the exact spot where Shirone had once activated the *Immortal Function* and passed the labyrinth trial. A Kergo native stood guard at the entrance, but he was not the same man Shirone remembered. Though Shirone was unaware, Chief Kadum had recently lost his life, and the tribe’s dynamics had shifted dramatically. A year ago, this guard had been a stern warrior who commanded terrifying respect. Now, he swallowed hard, his eyes darting nervously toward Gaold and his companions. Any seasoned Kergo warrior could feel the lethal aura radiating from the nine of them.

Gaold stepped forward and spoke in the local tongue. “Keltia, Lo Hoima. Akrasia, Widmia Benzene.” *Knock on my door, and it will open.* It was a password etched into Shirone’s memory. But the guard’s response was cold. He shook his head firmly and replied in Kergo. “Not today. Too many are waiting to enter the sacred grounds. Return in three days.”

Without warning, Gaold flicked his wrist. The guard was struck across the jaw and sent flying with a sickening crack.

Shirone stared at the unconscious guard. “Is Gaold angry about what the native said?” she asked Sein.

“Gaold doesn’t speak Kergo,” Sein replied flatly.

Shirone fell silent. Gaold then activated a small device, and the altar wall crumbled inward, revealing a hidden passage. Kang Nan muttered, “I hate the Kergo.”

Inside, a square spiral staircase wound downward into darkness. After twenty minutes of descent, the corridor opened onto an iron gate. Beyond this point lay the heart of Kergo territory, completely isolated from the outside world. The guards here wore their tribal tattoos openly and carried themselves with far more aggression than their counterparts above.

“Halt! How did you get in here? Starting today, the entrance to the sacred gate is closed…” One guard shouted, but Gaold’s backhand caught him squarely in the jaw. He crumpled to the floor. His companion lunged forward, eyes blazing, but received the same treatment.

Shirone watched the two unconscious guards, frowning. *This isn’t necessary,* she thought. *If we simply activate the Immortal Function in the Chamber of Achievement and Sacrifice, the Kergo would welcome us. There was no need to provoke them.*

“Can’t we just follow the proper procedure?” Shirone whispered. “I know a little about their customs, but if you trigger the Eight Orbs of the Angel…”

“Angel?” Gaold paused, turning his head slightly. “Shirone, that’s just a toy my classmate built.”

Shirone fell silent. She could feel a deep, simmering rage radiating from Gaold. It wasn’t just about the password. Now she understood why he despised the Kergo.

The iron door swung open to reveal an octagonal chamber. Three guards stood at the far end, and at their center stood a Kergo priest marked by a white tribal tattoo. Even Shirone could sense the difference in their combat prowess compared to the outer guards. Yet Gaold ignored them entirely, stepping into the chamber where eight floating orbs hovered in a maze-like pattern.

The priest stepped forward, face grim. “Who are you? A stranger who dares to enter the Holy Angel’s chamber…”

Before he could finish, Gaold lunged forward, snatched one of the orbs, and channeled energy into it. A simple *Air Press* technique—completely unrelated to magic—slammed into the mechanism. The chamber shook violently. The priest’s eyes darted around in panic before locking onto the orb. A brilliant white light flared to life, something Shirone had never been able to trigger even with all her resources a year ago.

She stared intently at the display. The number that appeared was startlingly low.

1,008,000.

“One million and eighty thousand points…” Gaold approached the trembling priest, locked eyes with him, and spoke two words. “Move.”

The priest’s legs obeyed before his mind could process the command, stepping aside to clear the path. It was a score that far exceeded the threshold required to pass the Divine Trial.

They soon reached the Kergo Autonomous District, where warriors had already been alerted to their approach. Spears bristled like a porcupine’s quills as the guards raised their arms, eyes sharp with suspicion.

“This is sacred Kergo land! Invade, and you will die, no questions asked!”

Gaold compressed a sphere of air over his palm and strode toward the spear wall. Compared to the elite guards of the inner sanctum, these warriors seemed almost gentle. They were devoted followers of Ankera, fiercely loyal, but their individual combat power paled in comparison to the Kergo’s inner circle. To Gaold, however, they were all just Kergo.

“Lower your weapons!”

Just as Gaold’s *Air Press* was about to detonate, a burly figure stepped through the ranks. His tribal tattoo, once black, now gleamed with a golden hue. Shirone recognized him immediately. Mahatu, a leading figure of the anti-angel faction and a longstanding rival of Chief Kadum, had ascended to chieftain. The warriors immediately dropped to one knee, weapons clattering to the ground.

“Chief! These are heretics who attacked our priests! Why are you suppressing our righteous fury!”

Mahatu exchanged a brief glance with Gaold before turning to the group. “It’s been a while, Mr. Shirone.”

Mahatu spoke in the continental language, a practical skill adopted to separate religious doctrine from civic administration and facilitate negotiations with the Galliant government.

“Mr. Mahatu. You’ve become chief.”

“I’m not sure I deserved it, but the circumstances unfolded as they did.”

The warriors could barely contain their outrage. Assaulting a priest was considered the ultimate blasphemy. “Chief! Explain yourself! Who are these people?!”

Mahatu turned to Shirone, his expression unreadable. “Why didn’t you activate the Angel’s Trial?”

“Ah… there were some complications.”

Mahatu nodded slowly. He had noticed the recent surge of outsiders flooding the Kergo Ruins. *What exactly is happening in the sacred grounds?* The sheer caliber of Shirone’s companions made the situation clear. *What kind of monsters are these?* Mahatu, Kergo’s greatest warrior, could feel it. Each of them had honed their unique combat styles to the absolute peak. Gaold, in particular, radiated an aura that was distinctly inhuman. Even Shirone, who prided herself on her unshakable fighting spirit, felt a chill just meeting his gaze.

Though negotiations were underway and open conflict was avoided, the tension between the warriors and Gaold remained razor-sharp.

“Arrest the heretics who attacked the priest! We will fight! We fear nothing!”

“Then come at us!”

Kang Nan sighed, rubbing her temples as Gaold stood his ground against a sea of hostile warriors who couldn’t understand his language. *As expected, I should have put someone else in command.* Still, she tolerated it. Sometimes Gaold’s brutal efficiency was necessary. Besides, wasn’t this the day Gaold had waited twenty years for? It was a moment that demanded absolute resolve.

“Silence!” Mahatu’s voice boomed across the square. “How dare you defy my order!”

“Chief! We are ready to die for you! But yielding to heresy is a betrayal of Kergo itself!”

“Fools. Do you even know who stands before you?”

The warriors’ eyes snapped to Shirone. They had been caged under Kadum’s iron rule for years and had never seen the boy’s face. Mahatu raised a hand.

“This is Shirone-sama, the Messenger of God, who dismantled the Kadum faction and liberated the half-angel faction.”
