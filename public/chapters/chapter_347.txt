Ephemeral Rift (2)  
~*~

Kariel wore a ferocious expression utterly unlike that of an archangel. As though he had suffered an unforgivable insult, his pupils flashed with murderous intent.

“How could the chief of the archangels do such a thing? For a human, it would be no different from mating with a dog! And you want me to forgive that? Could you?”

Uriel could not answer.

In a sense, Kariel was Uriel’s twin. Both of them had been born with the source knowledge of the first Archangel, Ikael.

But their personalities were as different as the original concepts they had been given. And above all else, Kariel truly loved Ikael.

Kariel withdrew his halo, returning it to the form of a celestial body. Then, as if his heart had already been shattered, he turned away coldly.

“If you will not help, then leave. I will break through Miro’s spacetime myself. I will destroy every arrogant, insolent human who has defied God’s will.”

Uriel looked at Kariel’s back with pity.

How petty.

The wings of light, the symbol of an archangel, spread behind Kariel in splendid radiance. Yet in some ways, he had a side that resembled a human being.

Just like Ikael.

I am sorry, Ikael. But we—no, Kariel—still need you.

Uriel placed a hand on Kariel’s shoulder.

“I understand. I misspoke. I will not interfere with what you intend to do. So, is it enough if I break this Cheonpok or whatever it is?”

Kariel turned back as though reluctantly giving in, but his expression soon brightened as he began to explain.

“Yes. But it truly will not be easy. Even for you, it will take your full power to destroy it. I brought forth a material far harder than the hardest substance in the world.”

Uriel nodded, passed Kariel, and approached the Cheonpok like a streak of lightning.

At close range, the black barrier filled his entire field of vision. Examining its molecular structure, he could tell it was not a substance created through heat and pressure. Perhaps it had been born through an explosion. In any case, this would not be simple.

“You remember, don’t you, Kariel? It has been a very long time.”

Uriel transformed his celestial body into a halo.

As information accumulated within the halo, which was four meters in diameter, a club made of light appeared beside him. It was four meters long, but the shaft itself was only one meter; triangular cones at both ends made up the remaining length. It was the heavenly sword known as the Geon of Paradise.

“There is nothing you have made that I have not destroyed.”

Uriel took a horse stance and exerted force. The Geon of Paradise began rotating rapidly, leaving behind the afterimage of a wheel. As its rotational speed surpassed Mach, the air shrieked with a metallic grinding sound.

The Law of Destruction of Paradise—Cheonbo-ryun.

“Ooooh!”

Archangel Uriel was the angel of destruction. His source concept was symmetrical to Kariel’s, the angel of birth.

In the beginning, the world had been born through the amplification of something. Then, as birth and destruction interlocked, all things were created.

Kariel constantly built, and Uriel destroyed. That was the fate of the two brothers born from the amplifying archangel Ikael.

“Gaaaaah!”

Uriel twisted his upper body and crossed his arms. The Cheonbo-ryun, now expanded to ten meters in diameter, moved along his forearm. Then he sprang forward and swung his fist, sending a powerful wheel of light flying into the Cheonpok.

Wooooooong!

A massive echo filled the Great Hall.

Yet the Cheonpok did not even budge. Only the Geon of Paradise rebounded, flew to the far end of the Great Hall, and returned.

“Hah! Taha! Ooooh!”

Uriel continued driving the endlessly spinning Cheonbo-ryun into the Cheonpok.

Kariel, who understood that power better than anyone, frowned each time a blow landed.

Tsk. How crude…

After dozens of attempts, Uriel finally stopped throwing the returning Geon of Paradise. No matter how hard he struck, the Cheonpok showed no sign of breaking.

“Hm. This one is certainly quite well made.”

Kariel smiled as if to console him.

“You do not have to push yourself too hard. Even this much impact should be enormous against Miro’s spacetime. It is a pity I cannot see the woman’s frightened face.”

The words sounded pleasant enough, but they only provoked Uriel further.

Uriel swept his wings forward and retreated far into the distance. After measuring the space for a moment, he spoke.

“Stand back. This time will be different.”

Kariel raised both hands with a shrug, as if telling him to do his best, and flew toward the ceiling.

“Whew.”

Uriel took the stance of a heavy infantryman about to charge and exhaled deeply.

As the halo expanded to twenty meters in diameter, information of destruction began to accumulate. Golden sparks rose around the halo, and the Great Hall trembled faintly.

Even Kariel, unable to joke this time, looked around his laboratory with concern.

“Ooooooh!”

Uriel charged toward the Cheonpok.

A Cheonbo-ryun twenty-five meters in diameter followed him, increasing its rotational speed. The atmosphere inside the Great Hall was sucked into the center of the wheel, and a gale raged around it.

When Uriel was ten meters from the Cheonpok, he swung his upper body with all his might.

The Cheonbo-ryun flew in a curve and collided with the Cheonpok. For an instant, the afterimage of the disk vanished, leaving the Geon of Paradise clearly embedded in the surface.

The entire inertia of the rotation had been concentrated into the Geon of Paradise. The enormous Cheonpok shook violently, and white cracks began spreading across the black wall.

Aaaaaang!

A powerful explosion erupted, and the Cheonpok shattered.

Although he had lost the contest this time, Kariel’s face was filled with joy. It was the fate of their source concepts for Uriel to destroy what Kariel had worked so hard to create. But Kariel merely applied that fate in a constructive direction.

What mattered was that it had broken.

And according to his calculations, that power was enough to destroy Miro’s spacetime.

* * *

The labyrinth was Miro’s construction.

Miro had built a temple in a meaningless space whose end could not be defined. It was an act of gathering an immense void and squeezing a single meaning out of it.

She existed because the temple existed, and using her existence as its anchor, she had built a vast barrier between Heaven and humanity.

Miro was lonely inside the enormous temple.

Sitting cross-legged in meditation, her biological clock had long since stopped. In a new time and a new space, she had inherited the fate of maintaining an endless dimensional wall.

What are you planning, Kariel?

There had been attempts to circumvent Miro’s spacetime for a long time, but recently, the approach had become blatant. She felt as though she were being slowly cornered.

If Heaven attacked, Miro blocked it. Until now, there had been no problem. But now she had an ominous sense that they were aiming for something else.

What are you thinking?

A powerful shock struck Miro’s spacetime. Electromagnetic waves rippled out as the spacetime barrier stiffened. Miro’s eyes flashed open.

Then came the second and third shocks.

As her spacetime shook, shockwaves of the same strength rushed into Miro’s mind.

They are striking spacetime directly. Is it a new technology?

No matter how far humanity had developed, it had never caught up to Heaven’s technology. If it was Heaven, then they might have found a way to break down the dimensional barrier through a non-magical mecha system.

Miro hurriedly knelt and formed a complicated hand sign. She crossed the index finger and thumb of her left hand, while pressing the middle and ring fingers of her right hand together to form a cross.

As she strengthened the dimension with Scale magic, the walls of space and time began to thicken.

This power… Is it Uriel?

Miro’s expression twisted. Cold sweat broke out across her skin, and her crossed arms began to tremble.

She did not know how much time passed. Then, abruptly, the shocks disappeared.

Breathing heavily, she collapsed to the floor. Even though she had reinforced the dimensional walls, she would not be able to endure in this state for long. She needed a new method.

Boom!

As Cheonbo-ryun struck the Cheonpok, an unprecedented shockwave surged into Miro’s spacetime.

The moment the impact arrived, Miro was thrown dozens of meters as if she had taken the blow directly.

Even as she slid across the floor, she clenched her teeth and endured. Her eyes trembled with confusion.

That blow had caused a crack in Miro’s spacetime.

At this rate, it will be breached. No—it has already been pierced.

Miro decided to use her last resort.

She sat cross-legged, placed her fists on her palms, and gently closed her eyes. There was no time to hesitate. If the dimensional gap remained open for even ten more seconds, Heaven’s army would pour through before she could mount any defense.

Enter samadhi.

Once she entered samadhi, she could not come out on her own. It was a state of single-mindedness so complete that even thought disappeared.

As the sole master of this space and time, she was supposed to remain awake at all times. But now, she had to fall into a deep sleep.

She concentrated. Then she focused again on that concentration. She repeated the process endlessly, until her ego faded and, at last, no thoughts remained. Only the inertia of descending into the abyss was left.

What she gained in exchange was enormous mental energy. The rift disappeared as the dimensional walls grew infinitely thicker. This time, even Uriel would never be able to break through.

The shaking of the temple subsided, but Miro had become as still as stone and did not move.

Lost in infinite focus, she forgot her own existence.

And unless someone came to wake her, she would remain that way forever.

* * *

“How did it go?”

At the top of the central control system, Kariel and Uriel analyzed the results of the experiment. Kariel watched the screen with an intrigued expression.

A crack had appeared in Miro’s spacetime. However, it had been restored almost immediately, before Heaven had time to do anything. It showed just how agile Miro’s response had been.

More importantly, the restored spacetime had become incomparably stronger than before. The durability value transmitted through the screen was enough to send a thrill through even Archangel Kariel.

“You are still getting stronger. What did you do? Is this truly a figure that a single human can produce?”

Uriel summarized the complicated result in simple terms.

“So we failed?”

Kariel gave a bitter smile.

“Still, we obtained some results. Roughly three seconds by terrestrial time. If she had been just three seconds later, it would have broken completely.”

“I see.”

Uriel did not seem disappointed. In truth, even he did not know how he felt. Perhaps he was relieved.

“The Cheonpok technology itself can be considered a success. However, I underestimated Miro too much. As expected of the Nephilim who represents the people of the land. How did she reinforce it? Now, an ordinary Cheonpok will not even scratch it.”

“Humans are strange. They look infinitely weak, yet they can become infinitely strong. Guffin was like that. Miro is also a formidable human. Perhaps that is why Ra blocked access.”

“Hmph. In the end, she is only human.”

Kariel did not acknowledge humans.

They were unclear beings. Chaos itself. That was why Ra controlled their lifespans in the name of eternal life.

“What will you do now? The strategy to destroy the dimensional wall has failed. It will be even harder to attack in the future.”

Kariel was not worried.

“There will be another way. We simply have to find it. More importantly, the fact that Miro’s spacetime cracked at all matters. Interesting things will happen from now on. This will make for a good experiment.”

Kariel pointed to the monitor. The process of the Cheonpok experiment had been rendered as a three-dimensional image.

A reference line was drawn at a specific point on a large sphere, and the durability value appeared as a number. Cheonbo-ryun’s impact was applied, and cracks could be seen forming.

No living creature—not even a soldier of Heaven—could pass through a rift of that size. But that only applied to things that could be classified as living creatures.

On the outskirts of Heaven, there were all sorts of strange beings that could not be defined by human standards. And at that very moment, the system detected something entering the terrestrial world through the gap in the crack.

“What in the world is that…?”

Kariel could not answer.

It was not because his knowledge was lacking. Rather, it was because the thing that had crossed into the terrestrial world existed in an [unknown state].

Looking back, a full year had already passed since he first entered the school. A year could be called short, but so many incidents had happened in that time.
