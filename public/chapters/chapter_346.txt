Ephemeral Rift (1)  
~*~

Heaven’s sixth thousand, Jebul.

There were two mecha systems in Jebul, the city of angels.

One was Ingris, where the Akashic Records were located. The other was the Great World War, Archangel Kariel’s laboratory.

The Great World War, once devastated by Shirone’s Photon Cannon, had been completely restored.

The enormous pillar-shaped central control system was the tallest structure in the Great World War. Beside the pillar, which emitted five-colored light, the galactic mirror used to observe the movement of the stars was running smoothly.

The Great World War had returned to its former state.

Heaven, however, had not.

After Shirone left, winds of change began to blow through the first thousand, Sharmain. The subjects no longer believed without question that Ilhwa alone was right, and radicals had even begun to appear among them.

Because of that, the angels mobilized every ability they possessed in order to break through Miro’s space-time.

At the forefront of that effort stood the archangel Kariel.

He was the archangel of birth who had once tried to cast the Art of Life on Amy, Tess, and Arin, who had been captured by the fallen angel Ikasa.

The most intelligent of all angels, Kariel analyzed the Meta Gate Shirone had left behind when he returned to the original world.

At last, he found the coordinates to the terrestrial kingdom. However, in order to reach it, he still had to pierce Miro’s space-time.

“Even if we know the coordinates, we still have no choice but to drill through. But this is the truly ambiguous part.”

“Then was there any point in recovering the coordinates?”

Kariel turned toward the majestic voice.

When he realized who it belonged to, he smiled. Another archangel, Uriel, floated in the air wearing heavy white gloves.

Angels were taller than humans, but Uriel was tall even among angels, reaching three meters in height. He was as broad as he was tall, and his breastplate rose all the way to the tip of his chin.

With a single flap of his wings of light, Kariel flew to the top of the central computing system. A large screen had been installed forty meters up, and countless numbers moved across it.

“Not necessarily. By now, the population of Nephilim in the terrestrial kingdom has grown considerably. If we destroy Miro’s space-time by force, we’ll still have to hit the firewall. But with the coordinates, we can ignore that.”

“You’re planning to create a space-time that links to Miro’s? Wasn’t that strategy dismissed long ago?”

Ankera was the only being who could reject an archangel’s proposal. In fact, after the Final War was blocked by Miro, Ankera had forbidden any investigation into the terrestrial kingdom.

The archangels did not know why. But they could not disobey the will of Ankera, who governed the Akashic Records.

Kariel, however, had not given up.

The decisive factor had been Shirone’s appearance. His existence had changed the subjects. And as time passed, that change was accelerating.

“I have no intention of going against Ra’s will either. This is a precaution, nothing more. The terrestrial kingdom will have to be conquered one day. If we wait until then to start preparing, it may be too late, don’t you think?”

“Then... this experiment is still difficult to justify.”

One corner of Kariel’s mouth lifted.

There had never been a time when he lacked confidence. However, each time he made an attempt, Miro reacted swiftly and blocked him. It felt as if he were playing her game while hiding behind her back.

But today, she would have no choice but to yield.

Miro’s space-time would be pierced.

Ankera did not want it, but what could be done?

That, too, was fate.

Kariel flew across the Milky Way toward the western wall. Uriel watched for a moment, then caught up to him in an instant, stirring a fearsome gale.

“And now, this is the work I wanted to introduce to you today.”

“...”

Uriel could not find anything to say.

Kariel’s masterpiece was enormous, large enough to occupy a quarter of the vast Great World War. It was a rectangular wall forty meters wide and seventy meters high.

Only a moment ago, Uriel’s size had seemed like the second coming of a giant. Now, in front of that wall, even he looked small.

“It’s quite big.”

When Uriel acknowledged that much, Kariel became even more elated.

“I’ve been thinking about why we can’t break through Miro’s space-time. It’s because a dimensional wall is, by nature, something that cannot be broken. But with this canvas, it becomes possible. It’s like freezing liquid so you can shatter it. This will deliver a direct blow to Miro’s space-time.”

“A direct blow. In short, this canvas is Miro’s space-time?”

If one examined the details, that statement was quite wrong. But simply speaking, it was close enough.

Well, what did it matter? Kariel had called Uriel here today because he needed his arms, not his head.

“If the canvas is destroyed, Miro’s space-time will be destroyed as well. I wanted to add an amplification circuit, but without Ikael’s help, that’s impossible.”

Uriel did not answer.

Ikael would not help. It was true that she had been imprisoned for committing an irreparable crime, but Shirone’s incident had made it even more certain that she stood on the side of humans.

Kariel tapped the canvas and said, “How about a challenge?”

Uriel wondered whether he should destroy it.

If the canvas was broken, Miro’s space-time would also break. And that would be against Ankera’s will.

No. No one can go against Ra’s will. Not because he is strong, but because he is whole.

No matter what future came, Ra was simply Ra. Kariel knew that, which was why he could do something so reckless.

Uriel decided to be honest with himself. From the very beginning, what had troubled him was not Ra.

It was Ikael.

Why... did Ikael abandon us?

Since ancient times, angels had been responsible for conveying the will of the gods to the terrestrial kingdoms—and for destroying those kingdoms when necessary. Among them, Uriel was an archangel who had accomplished great destruction. His power overturned the earth and split the sea in two.

However, when Uriel, who had slaughtered humans without hesitation, heard rumors that Ikael might have sided with them, he began to question whether he had been right.

Ikael was always right.

No, it was not a matter of right or wrong. Even if she was not right, he wanted to follow her. If that was the nature given to him, then even if it was wrong, he would devote himself to it.

Perhaps Kariel had lived with that same thought his entire life.

Angels had no concept of a mother. But if Uriel had to name someone as his mother, it would be Ikael. She was the archangel among archangels, the one who had cared for them since they were born.

Uriel turned his head slightly and looked at Kariel. Seeing his innocent smile reminded him of something from long ago.

Back then, Kariel had not been like this. He was still a smirking eccentric, but he had not been obsessed with a single purpose, nor had he created things for that purpose alone. When he created amusing, whimsical objects and gave them to Ikael, she would laugh happily and pat Kariel on the head.

“Kariel, if it’s me, I can destroy it. I’m sure you know that. Do you truly want this?”

“Ha ha! It won’t be easy this time. No, to be honest, you have to destroy it. I want to destroy the terrestrial kingdom as quickly as possible. If we break Miro’s space-time, today will be the day.”

“Why are you in such a hurry? He has not said anything yet. Ymir still hasn’t awakened. Is this truly necessary?”

Kariel looked beyond the flames. The riot that had begun in the first thousand, Sharmain, had spread to the second thousand, Rakia, and the battle between the subjects and the fallen angels was now in full swing.

“Shirone changed Heaven. And this is only the beginning. We must break through Miro’s space-time as soon as possible and eliminate the humans on the ground.”

Uriel did not entirely fail to understand Kariel’s concerns.

However, it made no sense for an archangel who had destroyed countless terrestrial kingdoms to grow impatient over a riot among the subjects.

“Do you think Ikael will return if we destroy the humans?”

Kariel’s face hardened at once.

“...What do you mean by that?”

“Ikael’s sins have already been erased. The event itself no longer exists. In exchange, the Guffin disappeared as well. Isn’t that enough for us to forgive her, at least between the two of us?”

Kariel’s planetary body expanded at terrifying speed.

As a halo more than forty meters in diameter unfolded, the mecha system of the Great World War sounded an emergency warning. It seemed to reflect Kariel’s current state of mind.

“Can guilt be erased? Then what are these filthy memories still left in us? I don’t care about the Guffin! Ikael is simply disgusting! Never say that again!”

“Are you truly going to fight her? Kariel, I’ll ask you again. Do you truly want to fight her?”

Kariel’s expression held both sorrow and anger.

“She gave birth to a human child!”

His halo sent out a wave-like ripple. As the mecha system repeatedly turned on and off, the Great World War flickered.
