Chapter 373  
Divine Transcendence (1)  
~*~

At the Kaizen Swordsmanship Academy in Basuka, the capital, Quan was fighting a nightmare from the past—one he could never return to, and yet could never escape.

He was currently a certified 6th-grade swordsman. Before his Achilles tendon had been severed, he had once been called the Battlefield Mage for the deathly, almost magical way he wielded a sword.

Quan had been born into a military family, but when he was six years old, his entire family died on the battlefield. His uncle took him in afterward, only to seize his inheritance and send him away to a swordsmanship school in another country.

Even so, Quan had never resented anyone.

There had been no one for him to rely on, but he had possessed genius enough to become a certified 6th-grade swordsman at the age of only twenty.

His nightmare always began with an incident from ten years ago.

At twenty-one, Quan had been assigned as the commander of the 2nd Company in a search battalion tasked with capturing Cointra, the founder of the Dark Church.

The men had not welcomed him. It was only natural. A twenty-one-year-old kid had been transferred into a veteran unit whose average age was thirty-eight.

Quan had not cared either. To him, as their commander, soldiers were consumables necessary to win a war.

Without even taking time to greet his men properly, Quan immediately led the company into the Haunted Forest.

Numerous search teams had entered the forest before them, but every single one had lost contact without exception. The previous company commander, terrified, had returned to his homeland on sick leave, and Quan had been sent in as an emergency replacement.

But Quan had been confident.

Even if ghosts truly lived in that forest, there was nothing in the world his sword could not cut.

‘Just don’t let them be ghosts.’

The first day passed without incident.

But something happened at dawn, while the 2nd Company was breaking camp and performing maintenance.

“Attack! We’re under attack!”

The sentry’s cry was cut off after a single blow. Realizing the enemy had already broken through their perimeter, Quan grabbed his sword and stepped out of his barracks.

The others armed themselves and formed up, watching for enemies approaching from beyond the dim twilight of dawn.

A gale blew.

No—there was no actual wind. It only rang in their ears like one. Quan reflexively cut through the darkness, but his blade caught nothing.

“Form ranks!”

The forty men gathered into a circle. In a situation where they could not identify the enemy, the most important thing was to avoid being picked off one by one.

At last, Quan saw them.

Twenty meters away, a black curtain shimmered. One by one, white masks turned toward them and emerged from the darkness.

“What…?”

The figures wore owl-like masks. They swirled around the clearing, then rose into a triangular formation.

Shrrrng!

Thirty swords were drawn at the exact same time, yet they made only one sound. They stood in identical stances, their blades aimed along the same line of sight. The sight brought to mind certain information Quan had heard during commander training.

“No way…”

The strongest swordsman corps on earth.

‘Why is Fengzhang here?’

Fengzhang were the escort knights of Theraze, the empress of the Kashan Empire. If they had appeared here, there could be only one reason.

Cointra, the leader of the Dark Church, was under Theraze’s protection.

“What is Kashan planning?”

“If you go to the underworld…”

Fengzhang’s signature declaration—a collective notice of murder—rang out.

“You will find out.”

“Aaaagh!”

The men of the 2nd Company screamed. Even to Quan, the killing intent was overwhelming. No matter how veteran his soldiers were, this was not an enemy ordinary men could withstand.

“Damn it!”

Quan kicked off the ground and charged. Fengzhang scattered like wind, then gathered again. Dozens of swords produced thousands of afterimages, and Quan’s body moved as though it ignored gravity itself.

‘Damn. How many points of external gravity are they using?’

Their skill was astonishing. They were people who implemented fluid mechanics with the human body. It felt as if direction itself had vanished, and they were exchanging sword strikes in the middle of zero gravity.

But the ones who were truly surprised were Fengzhang.

‘He’s lasting too long. How many times have we swung?’

‘Approximately twenty-three thousand.’

There were very few people in the world who could withstand Fengzhang’s swordsmanship for more than a thousand strikes. In other words, Fengzhang could usually grind any opponent to pieces in a single second.

‘How unfair God is.’

If the world were one hundred percent perfect, all living things would have been happy as microbes. But nature always created a 0.1 percent mutation. The existence of that 0.1 percent made the remaining 99.9 percent seem insignificant.

‘Born by chance… yet specially chosen.’

That was the impression Fengzhang had of Quan.

Technique, refinement, physical balance, the ability to control gravity, and the instinct to create new routes of attack.

‘This man is a genius.’

The moment that thought passed through them, the members of Fengzhang spread out like wings. Then they became a gale once more and coiled around Quan like a snake.

‘Kill the genius.’

Fengzhang’s swords swallowed every inch of space. They were so fast that they almost seemed to move slowly.

“Uwaaa! Charge! Charge!”

At that moment, the company members rushed in.

The instant they entered Fengzhang’s radius, their flesh was ground apart like meat caught in machinery. Quan escaped the storm of blades almost by instinct.

‘These lunatics…!’

Kobe, the oldest man in the company, passed Quan last. There was no time for farewell, but his smiling eyes said a thousand words.

‘Live, Company Commander.’

As he watched his men slaughtered in an instant, Quan realized why they had chosen to die.

‘I see. At least one person has to get out.’

Kashan’s involvement had to be reported to their superiors. Otherwise, more search parties would fall into Fengzhang’s trap.

‘This is the best option. If someone has to live, I have the highest chance.’

Driven back by external gravity, Quan stared expressionlessly at the dark storm raging in the gloom. Every member of his company—men he had known for less than a day, some whose eyes he had never even met—was dead.

‘Why…?’

Something hot rose inside him. A great flame burned in a place he had thought long extinguished, leaving only ashes behind.

“Why the hell…? Damn it!”

Quan shot forward, exploding with external gravity. In response, the scattered members of Fengzhang gathered like feathers into a triangular formation twenty meters away.

“You…”

Quan glared at them.

“Don’t think you’re leaving here alive.”

Fengzhang did not answer. Faced with an unexpected situation, their minds were racing.

‘Mission failed.’

No—it had very nearly failed.

If Quan had chosen to run, Fengzhang would not have been able to catch him. The lives of forty insignificant soldiers had created an irreversible precedent for the world’s strongest swordsman corps.

The Fengzhang member standing at the lead slowly raised a hand and removed her mask.

Quan’s brow twitched.

Beneath the owl mask was a woman’s face. Her eyes were long and sharply slanted; her brows were so pale and thin they seemed almost nonexistent.

“This is the first time a living person has seen the face of Fengzhang.”

“So what? Do you want me to be moved?”

“Join Fengzhang. We will guide you.”

It was an exceptional offer, even for Quan, whose ability had been acknowledged by the world. Above all, Fengzhang answered directly to Theraze, the third emperor. If he accepted, he would rise vertically to the highest possible position a swordsman could attain.

Quan finally lowered his sword and rested it on his shoulder.

“I refuse.”

The woman tilted her head slightly. What swordsman in the world would refuse Fengzhang’s invitation? It was clear to her that the deaths of his subordinates had made him lose touch with reality.

“The deaths of your subordinates are regrettable. But a new future awaits you. Do not bury your talent in the past.”

Quan snorted. No matter how great Fengzhang was, there could not be two “strongest” beneath the heavens.

“Don’t misunderstand. My men only fulfilled their duty as soldiers. The fool here is the commander who can’t control his emotions.”

“Then why refuse?”

Quan aimed his sword at the woman’s forehead again.

“I’ve been curious for a while now. Aren’t you embarrassed? What do you call yourselves, gathering in a group to cut people down and then claiming to be the strongest?”

“…This is a breakdown.”

The woman put the owl mask back on.

At the same time, the thirty members of Fengzhang scattered like wind and flew toward Quan.

‘Wait for me. I’ll bring at least one of them with me.’

How could he face his men in the afterlife if he betrayed their expectations and did not even take a companion to the other side?

Quan plunged into the storm of Fengzhang, spreading external gravity in every direction. The movements created by masters of external gravity surpassed combat and approached art.

‘Right. The answer was surprisingly simple.’

Quan crossed a wall.

It was a level he reached precisely because he no longer cared whether he lived. This time, even Fengzhang could not help but feel nervous. The Haunted Forest trembled from the deafening wail of dozens of swords.

‘A sword… can it really be this beautiful?’

At the end of that joy, Quan’s sword cut through the trajectory.

One member of Fengzhang had her neck severed from her body, and at the same instant, his blade pierced another through the heart.

‘Two. That’s enough to pay for my meal.’

Whoooooosh!

The moment Fengzhang realized their comrades were dead, the flow of wind exploded into a raging current. Killing two of them at once was an extremely rare event in the entire history of Fengzhang.

Quan calmly watched their anger whirl into a frenzy.

But they did not kill him.

Instead, they sliced away his Achilles tendon as cleanly as carving flesh from a fish.

“Aaaaaaa!”

Quan screamed.

It was not because of the pain.

Everything that made him who he was—including the realization he had just reached—vanished along with those thirty grams of flesh.

The nickname Battlefield Mage had come from his magical swordsmanship, which could decapitate an opponent anytime and anywhere. But that had only been possible because of his genius-level footwork and mobility.

There was no genius without an Achilles heel.

He was no longer the Battlefield Mage.

“Guh…!”

Quan could not stand. He could not get up. Even so, he stretched out his arms and crawled up the hillside.

The deafening wail of wind circled him. The words spoken by twenty-eight people, one syllable at a time, would remain unforgettable for the rest of his life.

—You have become a mere insignificant thing.

Quan continued to crawl.

He was not afraid of death. He had lost everything. But he could not abandon his forty men and throw away his own life.

—Struggling like a bug.

“Aaaagh! Shut up!”

—Bark like a dog.

“Kill me! I said, kill me!”

—Report to your master. Tell him Fengzhang spared your life. Wag your tail while you say it.

As Fengzhang’s wind surged, it sounded like fireworks. To Quan, it was as if the whole world were burning.

He collapsed face-first onto the ground and clenched his fists.

He could not endure any more.

He could not stop the conviction to which he had dedicated his life from falling into the abyss.

“Aaaaaaaaaaa!”

Quan’s scream echoed through the Haunted Forest after Fengzhang disappeared.

“…”

Quan opened his eyes.

His face remained expressionless, but cold sweat had broken out across his forehead.

“Damn it.”

He could not lift a single finger. It felt as though his body were being pinned down by sleep paralysis. It was always like this after he dreamed of Fengzhang. For ten minutes, he would lie as stiff as a corpse.

Just like that day ten years ago, when his Achilles tendon had been cut.

Quan had almost escaped the Haunted Forest before losing consciousness. After his return, he was urgently evacuated to Tormia.

He reported that Theraze was protecting the Dark Church, but as expected, his testimony did not affect the situation.

The search battalion continued a sluggish local war while surrounding the mountain range for about a month, then was disbanded.

It seemed some kind of agreement had been reached with the Kashan Empire, but from that point on, it was a matter for politicians. Quan did not interfere.

Quan could not become a hero.

The incident from that day disappeared into history without even the name Fengzhang being mentioned.

The army offered him the position of battalion commander in the cavalry. It came with a special two-rank promotion, but there was no way Quan could fail to understand that it was payment for his silence.

Quan chose to be discharged instead.

His mind had already been destroyed to the point that he could no longer hold a sword.

For a while, he lived like an idiot. Out of consideration for his men, he never dreamed of committing suicide, but he also had no suitable help.

Then one day, at the suggestion of his only friend, Kiyora Ellis, he was assigned to the Kaizen Swordsmanship Academy.

‘It’s moving now.’

After getting out of bed, Quan limped on his right leg and walked to the window. When he drew back the curtains, morning light spilled over his old desk.

Countless papers lay scattered across it.

The titles differed—[Psychoanalytic Opinion on the Phenomenon of Psychological Transcendence], [Personalization], [Correlation Between Will and Phenomenon]—but the theme was always the same.

In the corner of the desk were memo pads on which he had recorded his thoughts.

Quan looked down at them with dry eyes, then swept his hand across the desk and sent them flying.

He had gathered every piece of data he could on transcending the mind, but he had still failed to reach it.

Suppose a person moved his body through sheer willpower.

Then what, exactly, was will?

Even now, Quan limped. No matter how desperately he tried to walk normally, he simply could not.

‘Ogent Lian.’

Who could have predicted it?

A boy in whom no one could find any talent had accomplished something Quan, a recognized genius, could not.

‘Was I just an idiot all along?’

Quan shook his head.

He knew the truth.

He had already broken his sword. The moment his Achilles tendon was cut away, the sword god had turned its gaze from him.

‘Another year is over.’

Today was the final day of the second half of the swordsmanship school year.

The evaluation subject for the cadets under his charge was bakuto—more specifically, wrestling.

Even if Lian could use divine transcendence, he would not be able to withstand the enhanced physical strength of schema users.

Perhaps today would be another miserable day for him.

Even so, Quan was curious about the results of the past half year.

* * *

Tess entered the locker room with the female cadets in her group.

Wrestling was an evaluation item that required close physical contact, but in a swordsmanship school, there was no meaning in separating men and women. During the evaluation, everyone had to remove their tops.

Women, however, were allowed to wear tank tops.

Tess struggled to put hers on. Her frame was already large to begin with, and because her chest was also well developed, she had to squeeze herself tightly into the garment.
