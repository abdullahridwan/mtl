Chapter 423  
Capture the Highlands (1)  
~*~

It was the day of the highland occupation evaluation. After thirty students gathered at the training ground, their first instinct was to size up the opposing teams. Shirone did the same, focusing on the Screamer squad. The Screamer team had formed a strategic alliance with Pony, while Suabi and Luman were the linchpins of their infamous "Bookmaker" strategy. With the reliable Eider anchoring the backline, they had built a formidable lineup.

With all six teams assembled, the evaluation instructor stepped forward to explain the rules.

“Today’s exercise is the highland occupation. The opposing teams already know the stakes. All six teams will compete simultaneously with no time limit. The first team to score five points wins, earning twenty points each. The losing team will have twenty points deducted. The battlefield is the main combat zone for Hill 2,000.”

Shirone surveyed the arena. It was a rectangular space, two hundred meters long and fifty meters wide, enclosed by a twenty-meter-high glass barrier. Inside, two ten-meter-high hills sat at opposite ends, each with a flag planted at the summit.

The match was divided into rounds. Pulling a flag would immediately end the current round, triggering a brief pause before the next one began.

“To encourage strategic depth, space movement is prohibited once the match starts. Flight is also restricted to twenty meters below the barrier; exceeding that altitude results in disqualification. If you hold the flag, your personal magic control device will disable your spells. Do not complain when you’re vulnerable—remember that.”

Holding the flag left a player defenseless, making them a prime target. Therefore, securing the flag required at least two people to coordinate on the hill.

The instructor continued. “Upon entering the arena, both teams will be divided by the central one-hundred-meter line. There are four positions: forward, winger, linker, and guard. The forward starts ten meters from the center line on the friendly side and rushes toward the enemy flag. Wingers patrol the left and right flanks, usually positioning themselves twenty meters back for defense, though some prioritize speed over caution. The linker operates fifty meters from the center line, balancing offense and defense. Depending on the situation, a linker can reinforce a flank, push with the forward, or retreat to help the guard block the enemy. Finally, the guard defends the flag on the hill. Since the flag-bearer cannot cast magic, the guard must provide all defensive coverage.”

Shirone’s team had finalized their positions the night before. Shirone, with his superior mobility, took the forward role. Nade and Iruki covered the left and right wingers. Amy, versatile and multi-talented, served as the linker. Maya, whose raw attack power was weaker, naturally took the guard position.

On the opposing side, the Screamer team’s namesake forward led the charge. Luman (crowd control) and Pony (water power) took the wingers. Suabi (utility) was the linker, and Eider guarded the flag.

The two teams avoided eye contact. Regardless of past relationships, they were competitors now.

The Screamer forward lifted his chin and walked toward Shirone. He understood the importance of psychological warfare before the match even began.

“Hey, you ready? Prepare to get crushed.”

After the survival test, Shirone’s reputation had skyrocketed, and the Screamer forward’s excitement only grew with it. The Screamer team’s lineup, anchored by Maya, was notoriously unbalanced. Defeating Shirone in this opening clash would solidify their standing in the senior class.

“I’ll be nervous. There’s a glaring hole in your team.”

“Aren’t you ashamed to call yourself the biggest hole?”

To Shirone, Maya was no longer just a senior classmate. He couldn’t let the Screamer forward insult her.

“Tch. I’ll finish this 5 to 0. Watch closely.”

At the instructor’s signal, the six teams moved to their designated battlefields.

Arin, trailing behind Canis, suddenly paused and glanced back at the Screamer team.

“….”

A massive, arrogant snake coiled in the air above them. It was a grotesque sight, but today it felt even more oppressive.

“Canis, shouldn’t we warn Shirone?”

“It’s fine. Shirone is a senior competitor. He’s not allied with us.”

Arin didn’t press the issue. She had no energy to worry about others when her own life hung in the balance during the highland occupation.

As they crossed the battlefield threshold, Canis added, “Just remember, I’m not the type to be underestimated.”

* * *

Team Shirone and Team Screamer entered their respective arenas and quickly reviewed their strategies. If the Screamer team had a primary concern, it was Ataraxia.

“Listen up,” the Screamer forward instructed. “When Shirone uses the magic circle that defeated Dante, he’s always charging. Your only job is to stop him. The casting time is over a minute, so we have a window.”

Pony asked, “What if it fails? If they switch to Guardian Force, we might get hit from the other side.”

Guardian Force was an extreme defensive tactic where all members protected the flag. It was Shirone’s likely fallback when casting Ataraxia.

“Then we retreat unconditionally,” Eider said. “Dig trenches on the hill and hide. If Luman uses crowd control, it’ll be over instantly.”

“So we’re just handing them the first point?”

“It doesn’t matter if we give up one point. I even passed out after using it once. You can’t spam giant magic every round. On the contrary, we benefit from draining Shirone’s mental stamina.”

Even with immense power, a one-minute cast time left plenty of room for counterplay. If they couldn’t stop it, hiding was enough.

“Now! We’re here to eat those twenty points raw. Like I said, let’s crush their first fighter.”

The Screamer team linked hands and shouted, “Fight!”

Meanwhile, Team Shirone finished their briefing. Iruki gathered everyone and spoke quietly.

“You’ll need to use Ataraxia while monitoring the battlefield. They’re going to sacrifice utility to counter us anyway. We should take an early lead with the Sidewinder.”

The Sidewinder was a strategy where the wingers pushed both flanks wide, forcing the enemy to split their focus away from the center line.

When the announcement voice declared the start of the first round, the ten players dispersed to their positions via space movement. Of course, activating the 2000 System would void their space movement.

Shirone waited ten meters from the center line.

The battlefield map was symmetrical, with various cover structures blocking direct lines of sight. All Shirone could see was the Screamer forward standing twenty meters away.

He wore a tight-fitting tracksuit and was warming up, shifting his weight and loosening his joints. Despite his calm demeanor, his tension was palpable.

‘Iruki will clash with Luman, and Nade will collide with Pony. Should I reinforce Iruki’s line?’

The teams were evenly matched at five versus five, but strategy would inevitably create localized 2-on-1 or 3-on-1 advantages. The key was to control the flow of local engagements through selection and concentration.

-10 seconds until Round 1 begins.

The arena lights flared, and the hum of engines echoed.

The 2000 System bracelet on the Screamer forward’s wrist lit up. He stepped lightly, shifting his body diagonally. His feet blurred as he bounced in place, seeming to defy gravity.

“Great! Let’s play properly!”

Four seconds. Three seconds. As the countdown dropped, his legs moved faster.

From a distance, he appeared to tremble, but Shirone, watching from twenty meters away, recognized the terrifying speed of the Schema.

Could human legs really move that fast? Like a compressed steel spring, he had the momentum to launch instantly.

“Oohhh! Oooooooo!”

The Screamer forward’s limbs shook, his body blurring. At the same moment, the 2000 System activated, channeling all power into the Spirit Zone.

-Start Round 1 of Hill Occupation.

Pop! Puff! Pop! Pop!

Colorful fireworks exploded above his head. Suabi’s twenty utility magic combat packages—muscle strength, agility, nervous system enhancement, concentration, and muscle endurance—rained down without mercy.

This high-speed force, a hallmark of utility mages, accelerated the Screamer forward’s movement to inhuman levels. The moment his limbs became invisible, a tearing groan ripped from his throat.

“Kyaaaaaaaaaaaaa!”

Screamer Martial Arts – Biryonggak.

Stretching his arms wide as if his shoulders were tearing, he launched forward with his right leg extended like a cannon barrel.

‘Spear to spear?’

A tactic that overwhelmed the entire line by directly attacking the enemy forward.

Seeing him fly like a living siege weapon, Shirone reflexively cast his spell.

‘Broad!’

At the exact moment a curtain of light pulsed, the Screamer forward’s flesh bullet pierced the mass barrier and struck Shirone.

“Keugh!”

Shirone, who had crossed his arms to block the impact, was sent flying five meters before crashing to the ground.

Even though he had absorbed the brunt of the blow with Broad, the force penetrated his guard and knocked the wind out of him.

‘This is the Bookmaker’s deadly strategy.’

A creature’s strength couldn’t be measured by raw numbers.

As power and speed increased, the twenty buffs coordinated to create a synergistic effect that transcended simple statistics.

Puff! Boom! Pupperpuppung!

Suabi applied consecutive buffs. Her active buffs averaged two seconds in duration. The key was rotating through all twenty species at precise intervals.

Amy intercepted the Screamer forward. In a spear-to-spear clash, it was standard for linkers to reinforce the center line.

“Come on, come on, come on!”

The sight of the Screamer forward flying through the air was terrifying. With every flash of magic, his posture shifted like a spinning wheel.

“Come! Come!”

The Screamer forward’s movements were too fast to track. He quickly cut his fist, and a side kick loaded with his full weight knocked Amy out of her defensive stance.

“Kyaaaaaaaaaaaaa!”

The Screamer forward clenched his fists, trembling. His torn shirt revealed corded muscles as his upper body shuddered.

The moment he crossed the center line, he leaped onto the hill without hesitation. Maya stepped forward to intercept, but before she could even begin her spell, he grabbed her arm behind her back and threw her aside.

“Hey, let go of this!”

“Cheuk. Why? Are you new to these muscles? Pathetic.”

The Screamer forward grabbed the flag. The Spirit Zone faded, but the Schema’s enhancements made overpowering Maya trivial.

As the pole was yanked free, the 2000 System deactivated.

– End of Round 1. Round 2 begins in 5 minutes.

Descending the hill, the Screamer forward planted the flag at its base and shouted toward Team Shirone.

“Hey, wasn’t that too easy? How can a guard be so weak they can’t properly defend the flag?”

The Bookmaker’s killer strategy was far stronger than Shirone had anticipated. The Screamer forward’s skill was excellent, but Suabi was the real problem.

She was a skilled mage capable of cycling through twenty buffs at blinding speed. Shirone would have a hard time securing a win without neutralizing her.

Shirone glanced back at Maya. She was the key to countering the Bookmaker’s strategy.

Maya nodded vigorously. Though being the team’s focal point was burdensome, she was willing to do anything for Shirone.

‘I’ll do my best, Shirone!’

Round 2 began, and the Screamer forward immediately pushed forward with another spear-to-spear charge.

As Shirone retreated toward his camp, the Screamer forward’s confidence swelled. Was the great Shirone afraid of him?

“Ha ha ha! There’s no ambition… … !”

Aaaaaaaaaa!

The Screamer forward’s steps faltered. When Maya’s sonic magic exploded, it felt like an earthquake had struck his skull.

“Huh!”

Suabi frowned.

Maya’s Rhythm of Death was a spell designed to shatter enemy concentration. Yet, she shouldn’t have been able to produce such power with skills that only reached the second stage of her primary ability.

‘Isn’t the voice different?’

Unlike her previous vocal techniques, Maya was performing the Rhythm of Death as if singing a full song. Though her radius was short, the sheer violence of the effect was enormous.

The Screamer forward’s unbuffed power had significantly weakened. Team Shirone, having secured the center line early, defended firmly.

As the clash devolved into a melee, the Screamer forward retreated to his camp.

Maya’s amplification was powerful, but her radius wasn’t wide enough. The Screamer forward decided to cut off Shirone from his teammates.

“Suabi! Buff!”

After crossing the center line, Suabi’s buffs reactivated.

With his power restored, the Screamer forward sprang forward with tremendous acceleration.

He quickly closed the distance to Shirone, who was sixty meters behind enemy lines.

“Kyaaaaaa!”

The Screamer forward struck with Biryonggak, pressing the attack.

Amy shouted, twisting her body to intercept and knock him off course.

“Shirone! Back!”

“It’s already too late!”

At that moment, Shirone’s body glowed pale and erupted outward in eight directions.

Shotgun Movement!

“Gagging!”

The moment a flash of light struck his abdomen, an anti-magic force of immense strength slammed into him. It was a shock that even the Schema’s strength-enhancing build couldn’t withstand.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!

Photon cannons sprayed across the battlefield every time the Shotgun Movement detonated.

It wasn’t just a movement skill. It was a powerful crowd-control attack that tore through the Screamer team’s formation.

“Keugh!”

Eider barely managed to dodge the barrage of photon cannons. With Amy covering the retreat and cutting off the path to the flag, Shirone effortlessly grabbed the pole and yanked it free.

– End of Round 2. Round 3 begins in 5 minutes.

By convention, the flag should be left at its original spot, but Shirone carried it down the hill. After planting it directly in front of the Screamer forward’s feet, he sneered and returned to his camp via space movement.

“That kid… … !”

The Shotgun Movement, which combined mobility, attack, and crowd control, was a magic only Shirone could wield.

‘What? Particles of God?’

Was there only room for gods and no demons in the world of magic? What the hell were they doing without capturing someone like that?

Pony approached, eyeing the high ground of Team Shirone.

“Don’t get excited. How did it go? Wasn’t Maya’s sonic magic too strong?”

They had lost the second round, but he had no idea why her magic had amplified so drastically in such a short time.

Seeing Maya laughing with her teammates reminded him of their strategy, and his frustration flared.

‘Damn it! What the hell did you do? That talentless kid… … .’

The atmosphere within Team Shirone was warm and friendly. It was clear the counter to the Bookmaker’s deadly strategy had worked.

“Maya! That was really cool! You won this round.”

“I just did as I was told… … .”

Iruki’s advice to abandon vocal techniques and focus purely on amplification had been right. For Maya, who possessed a natural talent for singing, it was a strategy that sacrificed versatility for overwhelming power.

Of course, this approach had limitations.

Sound mages learned vocal music because they were technically specialized in handling the voice. In the end, fighting with pure amplification was said to only work at the student level.

However, Maya gladly accepted Iruki’s plan.

She wanted to bring victory to those who had fought the Screamer forward for her sake.

Team Shirone was not a one-man team.
