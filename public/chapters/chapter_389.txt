“Understood.”

Gaold waved them off as though shooing flies, and Gando awkwardly turned toward the lift. As the one in charge of Gaold’s security, he had no choice but to obey.

But Fleur remained where she was.

I’ve always been by his side. I came here as part of the association president’s guard.

She knew Gaold was losing his memory, but knowing that did not make it hurt any less. After all, Shirone, whom Gaold had met barely ten minutes ago, still seemed to remain clearly in his mind.

“Fleur, what are you doing?” Gando asked anxiously. “Let’s go up.”

Fleur refused to move.

For more than two years, she had served as a member of Gaold’s guard and carried out countless missions. She could not accept being dismissed like this.

“Please let me stay here.”

Gaold, who had been walking toward Shirone, stopped abruptly. Slowly, he turned his head. A strange, silent abnormality hovered in his eyes.

“Just now… what did you say?”

Fleur drew in a breath and shouted louder.

“Please let me stay here! We are also your subordinates, President Gaold!”

Gando’s face went pale, and he grabbed Fleur by the shoulder.

“What’s wrong with you, Fleur? Are you crazy?”

It was not as if Fleur was calmly standing her ground. Her heart pounded against her ribs, and her terrified eyes had lifted toward the ceiling to avoid Gaold’s gaze.

“Kheh, khh, khh.”

Gaold bent his knees and crouched low, like a beast watching its prey. Heat shimmered above his shoulders and head, as if the very air around him were burning.

“Fleur.”

“Y-yes!”

“Since I became president of the Magic Association, I have never once been involved in an assault case. Do you know why?”

Fleur slowly lowered her gaze to Gaold. The moment she saw his face, the corners of his mouth twisted into a grotesque grin, fear struck her so hard it felt as if her heart had stopped.

“Because if I hit someone once, they all die.”

Gando flinched and stepped back.

Damn it. It’s started.

Fleur’s vision darkened, and a hellish landscape of sulfurous flames spread before her eyes. The horrific screams of starving ghosts gnawed at her brain until she wanted to tear off her own ears.

“Uh… ah…”

Fleur’s pupils rolled upward on their own. Her knees trembled like a dog being jabbed with a stick, and the tears streaming down her face felt as hot as blood.

I’m scared… I’m so scared!

She felt as though she would do anything to escape this moment. But running would mean betraying Gaold. Everything she had built within herself would collapse in an instant.

“Hah! Hoo!”

Fleur squeezed her eyes shut and shouted with all her strength.

“I respect you!”

At that instant, the scenery of hell vanished.

But her soul, which had flown far away in terror, still showed no sign of returning. Half in a trance, Fleur kept speaking before she could stop herself.

“I respect you! I’ve admired you ever since I first started learning magic! I can do anything for the president of the association. So please, let me stay here!”
