Chapter 390  
Magic Association (8)  
~*~

All the mages in the bunker began walking toward the lift.

Shirone could not understand what had just happened. Why had he been judged unsuitable? Even if Gaold had blocked the Photon Cannon, that only proved how extraordinary Gaold was. It did not prove that Shirone was weak.

‘Was that still not enough?’

No—what level had Gaold been demanding from him from the very beginning?

“President of the Association.”

Everyone waiting for the elevator turned to look at Shirone.

“Give me one more chance.”

Flew snorted in disbelief. To ask a first-class archmage for a second chance—he was not merely bold. It was as if he had no respect for the Association at all.

But this time, she did not have to step in. Gaold was already walking toward Shirone, his gaze cold.

His thoughts were no different from Flew’s. A toy that had lost its value as a toy was nothing but trash.

“Are you misunderstanding something?” Gaold said. “This is not a place for you to prove yourself. I came here to see whether you had the quality to become my tool. And you failed even that.”

Shirone accepted that he had made a mistake.

“I won’t ask you to judge me under the same conditions as before. If you give me another chance, I don’t mind if it’s a real match. Please let me demonstrate one more time.”

All emotion vanished from Gaold’s face. What remained was only cold killing intent.

Gaold was not a man obsessed with authority. But neither was he generous enough to indulge a child who did not know his place.

“You want permission to attack me?” he asked. “Are you saying you want to fight me?”

“That’s not what I—”

“Shut up.”

The moment Gaold’s killing intent erupted, Shirone’s vision was swallowed.

A curtain of flame swept around him, and in the next instant, an endless sea of fire spread in every direction.

Even knowing it was an illusion, the heat felt as if it were burning his flesh. Cries of pain, screams, and wails of despair filled his mind until thought itself became difficult.

Realizing what Shirone was experiencing, Flew’s face went pale.

‘This is bad. He really intends to kill him.’

Those caught in Gaold’s Self-Denial, Great Heat Hell, were crushed beneath unimaginable fear until their minds shattered. Given enough time, it was a terrifying ability that could suffocate and kill a person through sheer emotion alone.

But Shirone was not consumed by fear.

He did not shed tears. His knees did not tremble. He simply stood there, calmly gazing at the blazing hell before him.

“H-how…?”

Flew could not accept Shirone’s composure. Could this really be Great Heat Hell? But Gaold’s monstrous expression was no different from when he had once terrorized her.

‘So this is what Flew suffered.’

Shirone even turned his head and looked around. Then, deciding he had seen enough of hell, he faced forward again.

His gaze pierced straight ahead. He could not see it with his eyes, but in real space, Gaold had to be standing there.

‘Mika Gaold…’

When Shirone had first heard about him from Flew, he had thought Gaold was simply an exceptionally powerful mage. But looking at the hell spread out before him now, Shirone could sense what kind of life Gaold had lived—and how far he had fallen.

Shirone closed his eyes and gathered his thoughts.

No matter what kind of hell unfolded before him, the incarnation defined by perfect law would not be shaken.

His eyes snapped open.

The sea of fire split to the left and right, and in an instant, hell vanished.

Gaold’s face appeared in front of him, the whites of his eyes exposed as he laughed like a monster.

“Keh, keh, keh.”

Gaold let out a laugh that scraped at his throat, his shoulders shaking. Then he looked at Shirone with burning eyes and said,

“Beyond the mind.”

Now he understood why Kang Nan, who had been pessimistic about this operation, had brought Shirone here. Gaold did not yet know how useful Shirone would be in practical combat, but his talent was certainly material that could reach the first class.

“Mental transcendence? And you’re only eighteen?”

Kang Nan muttered with a shocked expression.

Flew was just as dumbfounded. She knew what mental transcendence was. But the deeper she had dug into it, the less it had resembled enlightenment and the more it had become an indistinct, unreachable concept.

“Hmm.”

Gaold stroked his chin in thought.

The reason he had considered including Shirone in the project was not because Shirone was strong. At the moment, there were plenty of people in the world stronger than him. But Shirone’s ideas were unmistakably those of a genius.

An Unlocker with the unique ability of mass endowment. A battery. And from there, countless practical applications of magic.

Shirone was a piece of timber that could add genius to the team Gaold intended to assemble. His talent for finding a breakthrough in any situation could become a key code capable of creating variables even under the worst circumstances.

In that sense, overturning the mental world of a first-class archmage was also a talent.

“You want a practical match?” Gaold asked. “Do you understand what that means?”

“Yes. I know you’re not the type to go easy on anyone. I don’t mind dying.”

Kang Nan frowned at Shirone’s reckless words. A mage could speak easily about death. But when placed in a situation where death was truly possible, no mage would say such a thing lightly.

“Fine. But first, let me ask you one thing.”

Gaold’s eyes widened.

“Why do you want to die?”

Shirone closed his mouth.

Perhaps he was the only one in this place who saw even the slightest possibility of survival. That fact alone made his body tremble as if struck by the chill of a winter night.

Gaold changed the question slightly.

“What I mean is… what exactly are you imagining? What conclusion did you reach? Do you know why I called you here? You know nothing, and yet you think there’s something behind me important enough to risk your life for?”

“I don’t know anything,” Shirone said. “But I can guess.”

He remembered the face of Archangel Ikael.

She had been warm and kind. And yet she had also once been an archangel powerful enough to destroy the Kergo Ruins.

Why had she entrusted the future to him?

“There is someone I must meet.”

It was the first thing he had thought after the incident in the Kazura Kingdom—more precisely, after Guffin’s security device had been released.

Behemoth, who knew the answer, had disappeared along with mental transcendence. But the question carved into Shirone’s mind still tormented him. That was why he had to meet Ikael.

Shirone’s answer did not fit any of the possibilities Gaold had expected. He found the boy interesting, but he also felt that he could not allow the conversation to drift any further. Gaold disliked people who talked too much.

“Are you saying you intend to use the Association for your personal affairs, kid?”

Shirone did not answer. Gaold’s question was emotional, and because of that, it missed the point. There was no need to answer a question that did not need an answer.

Gaold turned away, as if that was enough.

“Fine. I’ll give you one last chance. We’ll take a one-hour break.”

After casting Ataraxia, Shirone’s mental strength had to be greatly depleted.

But Shirone shook his head. If Gaold was willing to offer him a concession, there was something more useful than time to recover.

“It’s all right. Instead, I have another condition.”

Shirone drew Armand from his waist.

“Please allow me to use the Geumgang Armed Forces. I think I should be allowed at least that much of an advantage.”

Gaold looked at the sword in Shirone’s hand. The object known as [Demonic Sword Armand] had already been famous even before it fell into Shirone’s possession.

“I thought you were just a reckless brat, but you do seem to understand your position.”

Doing his best even while knowing he could not win meant Shirone was truly wagering his life this time. It was not a bad proposal for Gaold either, since it would save time.

“However, one item won’t change anything. So let’s set the rules.”

Gaold compressed the air above his palm. A transparent sphere flickered as its density increased.

“As you can see, what I use is air magic—something anyone can learn. Specifically, the Press series. I’m not good with complicated magic, so I don’t know anything fancy. I’m only here because I’m a little stronger than other people.”

A bitter smile touched Shirone’s lips. If a mage who could block Ataraxia with one hand was only “a little stronger,” then every other mage in the world might as well already be dead.

Gaold clenched his fist, and the bubble of air burst with a pop.

“Anyway, here are the rules. I’ll receive Ataraxia once more with Bakum Press. The difference from before is that I will cast attack magic at you only once.”

Gaold pointed his index and middle fingers at Shirone. A tiny bubble of air formed at his fingertips.

“As you can see, the magic that will attack you is an air gun. The principle is simple. Compress the air and release it. Would this be enough to count as 10,000 Press? I don’t know.”

Gaold’s arm snapped upward.

The air bubble, compressed with tremendous force, exploded, and a roar loud enough to shatter eardrums ripped through the bunker.

The air gun passed by Shirone and struck the far end of the bunker.

Until that moment, Shirone had not even realized something had gone past him. His ears only rang. Then he felt the vibration and slowly looked back.

At the point of impact, a huge circular dent had been gouged into the wall, with cracks spreading out in every direction.

The cracked area was roughly twenty meters in diameter. And though he could not confirm it, the air bullet itself had probably been no more than two centimeters across.

Shirone stared back blankly, unable even to blink.

Gaold waved the hand that had fired the air gun and laughed.

“Well, it’s child’s play as far as magic goes, but it’ll sting a little if it hits you.”

Sting?

Even in the best defensive posture of the Geumgang Armed Forces, he would be killed instantly the moment that struck him. Yet because the words had come from the mouth of a first-class archmage, they did not sound like a joke.

“Anyway,” Gaold said. “Do your best.”

Shirone’s lips trembled as he smiled.

Gaold was so powerful that it was frightening. But that was precisely why Shirone wanted to challenge him.

If he fired the Photon Cannon while combining Armand’s four core abilities—magic amplification, immortal function, mental transcendence, and Ataraxia—how far could its power rise?

“Geumgang Armed Forces.”

Armand rose upright and swallowed Shirone, completing the transformation in an instant. His cybernetic body whirred into place and settled over his shoulders.

“Let’s begin.”

Even after seeing Shirone’s transformed appearance, Gaold remained relaxed. He wiggled his fingers while maintaining the shape of the air gun.

As if pulled forward, Shirone attacked.

A Halo had already been drawn where he had been standing. Thanks to time-sharing, Ataraxia began to accumulate faster than before.

‘A division operation. And a fairly fast one too. At this rate, he’ll cut it down to fifty seconds, won’t he?’

Shirone formed a blade of bone from his gauntlet and swung it. But Gaold kept his eyes on Ataraxia and avoided every attack with only slight movements of his upper body.

‘Brute force won’t work either. His practical experience is on another level.’

Shirone pressed him desperately. The bunker was so wide that extending tentacles would only be a disadvantage. But in exchange, Armand’s physical enhancement ability could be used to its fullest.

‘What is he doing?’

Flew tilted her head.

Both of them were mages, yet neither was casting magic. Gaold could only attempt the air gun once, so that much made sense. But it was strange for Shirone to insist on hand-to-hand combat.

“Why doesn’t he use his specialty?” Flew asked. “If he’s using time-sharing, he should have one slot left, right? If he continuously fires attack magic, he might have a chance. The president of the Association can’t even use defensive magic.”

Kang Nan answered without taking her eyes off the battlefield.

“Because they’re both serious.”

“Yes?”

Caught in the tension of the match, Kang Nan had no time to explain further.

In any case, the movements they were exchanging now had no real meaning. The match would eventually be decided in a single instant.

‘Or perhaps… something completely unexpected might happen.’
