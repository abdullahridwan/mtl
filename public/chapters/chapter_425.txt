Chapter 425  
Capture the Highlands (3)  
~*~

The fourth round belonged to Shirone’s team. The shotgun-style push had been a calculated maneuver, and Iruki’s Cancellation had completely neutralized Suabi’s advance.

The overall score stood at 3 to 1.

It was a clear display of Shirone’s superiority, and the momentum seemed set to continue unless new variables emerged.

“What? Are we already done with round four?”

Students poured out of the third battlefield. Kanis and Arin rejoined their group.

Their opponents had been Fermi’s team, and the result was a decisive 5 to 1 victory for Fermi’s squad.

The loss was disappointing for Kanis and Arin, but such evaluations were the price they had to pay for clinging to their deeply individualistic, anti-cooperation philosophies. Besides, facing Fermi’s team meant no outcome was guaranteed, even with perfect synergy, so their expressions showed no sign of discouragement.

From a vantage point overlooking the second arena, Fermi checked the scoreboard.

“Hmm. Three to one.”

“Shirone’s team performed better than expected. According to the initial projections, it should have been a closer match.”

Fermi shared that assessment. However, tournament outcomes were always subject to probabilistic variables, and the possibility of unexpected shifts remained open.

“Insights that create variables. That’s Shirone for you. If I secure just one more point, this match will likely end here.”

With that, Fermi’s gaze shifted to Eider’s team.

*Of course. It’s never going to end like this.*

The corners of Fermi’s mouth curled into a vicious smile.

* * *

The fourth round had concluded, but Suabi remained fixed on Iruki from the sidelines of Shirone’s team.

*Mercodyne Iruki…*

If not for the overwhelming power of Cancellation—the highest-tier magic tied to Servant Syndrome—no student should have been able to frustrate her so thoroughly.

Iruki stood with his hands clasped behind his back, calmly returning her glare.

*It seems rather unfortunate.*

He hadn’t intended to provoke her. In terms of raw calculation speed, every member of the graduating class was still beneath him.

To defeat a Servant, one relied on intuition, not calculation. Yet Suabi’s intuition had reached a level comparable to a Servant’s.

“Suabi, let’s go.”

At Eider’s call, Suabi finally turned away.

A fierce fire burned within her. She knew there were many formidable seniors in her class, and she had already failed the graduation exam last year. But having her primary combat discipline disabled was a completely different kind of misery.

Eider gathered his teammates.

“Let’s push together. I’ll break through the center line in the shortest possible distance. Pony will be aggressive from here on out. If we lose this round, a comeback is nearly impossible.”

If the score reached 4 to 1, defeat was almost certain. Shirone’s team always possessed Ataraxia, a skill that guaranteed at least one point.

“No. Pony will keep refreshing.”

Suabi removed her glasses. “I’ll handle it correctly this time.”

Eider tilted his head.

They had known each other for over a year, but this was the first time Suabi had shown such unwavering determination, aside from the graduation exam.

*Hmm. Is she completely pissed off?*

There was nothing wrong with Eider, the team leader. He couldn’t have predicted the Cancellation law, but Suabi, who had directly experienced it, had given her approval. That was enough to trust her judgment.

“Alright. Let’s assume this round is ours. I’ll show you what I’ve got once I see it properly.”

At the start of the fifth round, Eider’s team assembled along the center line, excluding the designated guards.

It was a high-risk, high-reward rush tactic with a strong track record for comebacks.

The moment the Combat Package buff activated on Eider, Iruki’s eyes twitched. He immediately fired a Breakaway Spirit Zone.

*If it’s just Cancellation, then…*

[Crackling energy surged through the air.]

The buff effect detonated in rapid succession, transferring Combat Packages to every team member except the guards.

Iruki blinked, momentarily caught off guard, but Suabi simply gritted her teeth and glared harder.

*How am I supposed to do this?*

She had 137 mana batteries reserved in her mind. That was the cumulative total while consuming eight per second.

[A rhythmic pulse echoed in her skull.]

With every strike of that unique internal rhythm, the mana batteries in her mind scattered and rebounded.

“Ha ha ha! Good! This is it!”

Fueled by raw aggression, Eider leaped into the heart of Shirone’s formation without hesitation, striking all four of them. Just like in the first round, he assumed a bold finishing stance.

“Kyaaaaaaaaaaaaa!”

The morale of Eider’s team skyrocketed. The moment Suabi’s fighting will was felt, the wingers also began to deploy their reserved cards.

*All I have to do is shut down Shirone.*

Luman thought as he slowly raised his hands, as if drawing energy from the soles of his feet.

*Everyone hates me.*

But he didn’t care.

For humans, harmony was merely a byproduct of mistrust and fear. If you could control it, other people’s feelings were irrelevant.

*Yes. I am the number one (self-proclaimed) crowd controller.*

Luman’s cheeks trembled, his lips pulled back to reveal his teeth, and the corners of his mouth stretched wide.

“I am the king of the battlefield!”

His secret weapon, ‘Spiral Hunter,’ was unleashed.

[The ground swirled into a vortex, softening into quicksand that dragged Shirone downward. Then, it hardened into jagged rock, clamping onto his waist and refusing to let go.]

“Now!”

As Eider charged, Suabi’s concentration spiked to a record high.

Countless mana batteries detonated through her mind like a gale.

This was the realm of intuition, beyond mere calculation. With every mana cell colliding, her tongue snapped with precise rhythm.

[A rapid, staccato beat filled her mind.]

372 reserve batteries.

The corners of Iruki’s mouth lifted.

*It’s a good thing I entered the senior class.*

He had never cared about defeating others in his life. But now, the feeling was becoming unmistakably clear.

He hated losing even more.

*It’s wonderful, Suabi.*

Like stars, many talents recognized their own.

*He must have practiced every single day. He must have been diligent, biting his knuckles in the dark just to maintain his distant focus.*

Iruki lowered his stance and clenched his fists. It was his answer to Suabi’s sincerity.

*If I do this, my lifespan will shorten, but…*

It had been six years since he entered magic school, and only two times had he engaged in a bloody, rain-soaked battle like this: once against Nade, and a year ago when Canis’s provocation in the infirmary hallway had ignited his bloodlust.

Brain cells would be destroyed, but did it matter? Beyond the confrontation itself, the pride of Shirone’s faction was on the line.

*You will become a great wizard. But not now. Because there are only two people in this world who can stop me. One is Shirone, and the other…*

Real sparks flew in Iruki’s pupils. It was a physiological phenomenon where electricity generated in the cerebral cortex projected onto the retina.

*…That’s me. Mercodyne Iruki!*

In the virtual space of Iruki’s mind, data streams reaching one hundred million lines per second soared like a tornado.

When the volume of information required to process every event in the city simultaneously was fully integrated, Double Spirit Zone shot through Eider’s team at an astonishing velocity, canceling every single buff.

“Nonsense…”

Suabi was devastated. The calculation speed was so overwhelming that even her will to fight evaporated.

[Shirone, who had broken free from the frenzied hunter, led a swift counterattack with his teammates.]

Stripped of their buffs, Eider’s team rapidly weakened and was forced onto the defensive.

“Haa. I’m pushed, after all.”

Eider sighed.

The fatal flaw of his all-in strategy was the complete lack of a contingency plan. If this round was lost, there would be no chance for a comeback.

The youthful energy drained from Eider’s face. He pulled out a chip patterned in black and white and swallowed it.

*It’s embarrassing, but does it matter?*

He could even abandon graduation if it meant enjoying the fight. No, graduation couldn’t be abandoned. He had to enjoy being a wizard more than anything.

A mana battery was installed in his head. According to Eider, it was the advanced Air-family technology known as ‘Ghost Movement.’

The reason he could cast magic without understanding its inner principles was similar to Shirone’s Ataraxia.

“Ah… ah… ah…”

Eider’s eyelids trembled.

The days he had endured, waiting to finally taste what it meant to be human, were over. He no longer had to hold back. After making his deal, his graduation was already secured.

“Ha ha ha ha ha!”

Eider tore off his mask and flew straight into the enemy camp. It was classified as a reverse-position tactic, but there had been no agreement with his team.

While his allies stared in bewilderment, Shirone’s team intercepted him with their own magic.

The first to arrive was a Photon Cannon. However, just before impact, Eider’s movements twisted in an unnatural way. His trajectory swayed like a willow branch, slipping past every cannon.

“What? Ghost Movement?”

Dorothy’s eyes shone as she watched from the spectator stands.

It wasn’t magic that should be usable at the student level. Yet Eider’s movements couldn’t be explained without the AT algorithm.

*Is it linked to Fermi?*

It was hard to believe he had learned it in just a few months. The AT algorithm, which automatically avoided incoming objects by manipulating air turbulence, was a mana battery that even professionals struggled to master.

Nothing could stop Eider. Reaching the hill, he sprinted toward the flag.

However, it was Maya who broke everyone’s expectations as she aimed to intercept him.

[A powerful kick connected, snapping Maya’s jaw.]

“What…!”

Eider landed on Maya’s stomach. The muscles in his face twitched as he finally vented a year’s worth of pent-up desire.

“Sister, it’s going to hurt a little. Are you still patient?”

Eider’s fist pounded into Maya. A muffled laugh escaped his lips.

Seeing her terrified expression, a surge of pleasure substance flooded his mind.

“Aww! It’s fun! I’m going crazy!”

Like a gorilla, Eider raised both hands and began grinding down, his movements losing all human restraint. The whites of his eyes were visible, and saliva dripped from his open mouth.

The brutality was so extreme that not only his own team members, but also the students watching from outside the arena, were left speechless.

“You asked me a question during our game of Truth or Dare.”

Arin’s voice cut through the tension.

“‘Who’s the ugliest person?’ I was puzzled at first. I can’t distinguish physical shapes well. At the time, I chose Luman, but in reality, the most intense psychological profile belonged to Eider. According to the mental system classification, he corresponds to ‘evil’.”

Kanis listened in silence.

“Only those who understand evil can truly play the part of good. That is the law of morality. All he possesses in his head is the desire to torment others from beginning to end.”

Kanis didn’t think much of it. In Radum, all humans were essentially Eiders.

“The reason I asked the question was to test you. I acted a bit more aggressively after you denied the truth.”

“Right. I thought I could use it as leverage. But today, his psychological profile was even more blatant. Probably because he joined the Fermi organization.”

“Your existence must have had an impact. You probably thought you couldn’t act freely for a year now that someone with supernatural ability had entered the mix. Anyway, one card we had is gone.”

Kanis lightly set aside his lingering thoughts.

There were dozens of cards left anyway. Rather, it was good to know how much Arin’s psychological profiling could pressure the senior class.

“Maya!”

Shirone’s eyes widened. His face paled as he saw Maya crouched on the ground, face covered, being beaten.

“Hehehe! Sick? How much does it hurt? Aren’t you mad? Will you hate me to death?”

Eider swung his fist, his eyes wide open.

The idea of Shirone’s team surrounding him never crossed his mind. As long as he had Ghost Movement, no one could touch him.

[Dismissed!]

Blue electricity flashed in front of Eider’s eyes.

The AT algorithm kicked in belatedly, shoving his body over ten meters backward. Nade’s face, as he slashed through the air, lingered on Eider’s retina. When he came to his senses, he had already tumbled down the hill.

Eider’s face, previously lost in bliss, was now mixed with fear.

*What? What did I just see?*

He thought he had seen Nade’s face, but he couldn’t process what it meant. It would only be recognized later that one of his legs was shaking uncontrollably.

*Am I scared? Me?*

Nade, who had arrived first, stepped in front of Maya. The memory of his face, twisted with killing intent, fractured into fragments and seeped into Eider’s mind.

“Screamer. The flag.”

“Ah…”

Eider snapped out of his trance at Suabi’s voice. He hurriedly scrambled up and yanked the flag from the ground. Nobody cared about the rest.

Shirone approached Maya. Seeing her still trembling in a curled-up ball, his heart ached.

“Maya…”

Maya was by no means suited to be a wizard. Her talent, sufficient to enter the Spirit Zone, should have been spent elsewhere. She was an artist who moved people’s hearts, a genius singer.

“Maya, wake up.”

Shirone’s voice was cold.

Maya had a family to support and a tribal honor to defend. If she collapsed here, she would never cross the threshold of a wizard.

“Wake up. You are still being evaluated. If you get disqualified here, we all lose twenty points.”

Maya’s shoulders twitched.

Twenty points for her. But everyone in the senior class was risking their lives for those same twenty points.

*I… I’m so scared, Shirone. Everyone here… their coldness is so terrifying.*

She had wanted to give up. She had endured for her tribe and fought for her family, but the moment she saw Eider’s eyes, she realized the truth.

This wasn’t the place for her.

*Shirone…*

Maya slowly pushed herself up.

She had fallen in love with Shirone. She was even more desperate for her wizard license than anyone else. Everyone in her senior year would laugh at her, but Maya was just that kind of person.

“I’m fine… We can fight more.”

Seeing Maya try to hold back her tears, Shirone’s own eyes welled up.

He looked back at Eider, who was still taunting them from the enemy camp. Terrible anger filled his eyes.
