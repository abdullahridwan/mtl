“Photonic shaping. More advanced than fractal,” Canis muttered, a nervous expression on his face. Creating wings out of light was proof that plastic surgery had transcended the realm of photons. Shirone was already aware of the incarnation.

“How much do you know?” Harvest’s voice echoed through the mental channel.

*If you’re at a stage where you can utilize certain magics, you should have considered it ahead of us. It seems Immortal Function wasn’t the only reason he passed the cowardice.*

If that were true, the sense of incongruity Canis had felt back then could finally be explained.

“It’s clearer,” Canis said, turning to Arin.

“What are you talking about?”

“Shirone’s wings have become clearer.”

Canis tilted her head. It wasn’t common for her to focus on the shape of a specific object. *Come to think of it…* On Galliant Island, Arin had asked if Shirone was blonde. She hadn’t thought much of it at the time, but after hearing Harvest’s words, she couldn’t ignore it now.

“What exactly does Shirone look like? Can you explain?”

“Shirone.”

“Yes?”

“Shirone is Shirone. I vaguely suspected it before, but now I know for certain: I was watching Shirone’s incarnation.”

Arin couldn’t analyze the shape reflected in the incarnation. However, the form of the incarnation born from the Law’s numbers never changed.

“It’s not the incarnation. Shirone *is* the incarnation.”

That was the conclusion of Arin’s mental transcendence.

“Hmm.”

Canis looked back at Shirone with a serious expression. There had to be a distinction between the incarnation and the true form. She couldn’t imagine what would happen if they collided head-on.

“What do you mean your wings are sharper?”

“They were there back then, on Shirone’s back.”

“What?”

When they met at Galliant, Shirone didn’t possess the skill for wide wings. So how had she seen them?

“Is it like a feeling?”

Arin shook her head. “There’s no feeling in the incarnation. Just the form. But it wasn’t as clear as it is now. It appeared as a transparent shape, as if woven by the wind. Now, it’s colored golden.”

Arin wasn’t a foresight. That meant it was highly likely the wings she saw were manifestations of an incarnation. *The incarnation has wings?* If Shirone further strengthened the power of the incarnation in the future, would wings really sprout from his shoulders? There was probably no one in the world who could answer that.

Shirone bent his knees, watching the navigation magic fixed in his peripheral vision. *One, two…* The arrow finally pointed to the sky. *Three!* At the same time, his light wings flapped, and his body soared. The acceleration generated by driving his mass downward at sub-light speed was tremendous, and everyone below raised their heads in surprise. Judging direction, distance, and speed within 0.8 seconds to cast teleportation was the realm of intuition. The trickiest part was that the navigation’s suggestions changed mid-teleportation. In the end, he could only judge the outcome after completing the turn, which cost him about 0.2 seconds. That 0.2-second gap was exactly what the God Particles proposed by the Association were meant to eliminate. *This is the beginning!* Gwangik met that standard precisely. *Pop!* During the teleportation, his wings flapped, bending the direction of the flash vertically. *Pop! Pop! Pop!* As explosive sounds rang out one after another, Shirone executed the navigation’s suggestions without a single error.

“Aww!”

The gravitational pressure from the sudden directional changes fell entirely on Shirone. Every time he bent his trajectory, blood pooled to one side of his veins, and his muscles strained to the point of tearing. Armand’s diamond armament could have mitigated the risk on its own, but at present, Shirone was fighting unarmored. The stronger the pressure, the more intensely his wings flapped. Even if his body broke, he couldn’t stop. This was a flight meant to forge a true wizard.

“Wow, is that for real?”

The 0.2-second difference was obvious to the students on the ground. It was a completely different trajectory from standard teleportation. Knowing how difficult it was to turn while casting, they felt a chill of terror.

“Isn’t that guy breaking from the sky? He must be enduring several times the normal gravity.”

“But if he can hold on, it’s on a completely different level from regular teleportation.”

The rhythm of his movement was unlike anything they’d seen. As long as he maintained the beat, there was a high chance his opponents would perceive it as a brand-new tactic. Numerically, it was a massive advantage. In a hundred-turn battle, it meant he could reach his target coordinates twenty seconds faster than his opponent. That was the true value of the 0.2 seconds Shirone was currently mastering.

*When will it end?*

The navigation system persistently pushed Shirone forward as if it possessed a mind of its own. Reversing direction was basic, but it now demanded vertical ascents immediately after vertical descents. How far had he already flown? The magic school campus looked small enough to fit inside a circle made by his fingers. No standard movement evaluation required such a vast space. *What kind of alternative Association is this?* *No, this is right!* Shirone trusted it blindly. The God Particles were his own, but the standards calculated by the Association’s experts would contain no errors. Finally, the arrow pointed directly toward the magic school. The navigation suggested an average speed of over 300 kilometers per hour. The distance was 5.4 kilometers. *I’ll reach it in 65 seconds?* That was a distance typically covered by chaining 540 teleportations. The problem was that he couldn’t maintain acceleration because inertia reset with each jump. This wasn’t a photon-series test from the start. What the Association actually demanded was mass mechanics—movement that inherently contained acceleration.

“Whoa!”

Shirone initiated the sequence with a teleportation, then swung his light wings at full speed. Every time his wings struck the air, a gong-like sound echoed toward the school. 180 kilometers per hour. 220. 280. At the 2.4-kilometer mark, his speed finally exceeded 300 kilometers per hour. However, because he had lost momentum during the early acceleration phase, he needed to fly faster to meet the average.

“Mmmmm!”

The field of view was so narrow that only the navigation magic was visible, a sensation not unlike falling off a cliff. Shirone focused solely on speed. There was no way a navigation system would deliberately drive a student into a rock wall. 340 kilometers per hour. The surface area of his light wings doubled. Power equivalent to a photon cannon rippled through them, and his acceleration soared. 440 kilometers per hour. He heard the roar of wind tearing through his eardrums. The magic school was approaching at breakneck speed. *Wait a second…* A thought struck him at that moment. *Who guarantees the navigation won’t send me into a rock wall?* That was just the desperate hope of someone backed into a corner, not a rational judgment. The only reliable fact was that the navigation was simply pointing to the school’s coordinates. *I’m really going crazy!* Shirone pulled his light wings tight against his body. Acceleration was already locked in as it should be. But if he lost speed, he’d be eliminated. The only thing that could save his life was air resistance. *Kwa-kwa-kwa-kwa-kwa-kwa!* Shirone tore through the dense air, feeling violent vibrations. For the first time, he realized just how solid air could be.

The students who spotted Shirone jumped to their feet. “Damn! Falling here is the end!” But where could he go? Since the exact landing point was unpredictable, his upper body swayed left and right as he fought for control. The navigation system only issued its final command upon reaching the evaluation site. *Dot.* It meant it was time to stop.

“Huh!”

Shirone’s eyes widened, and he swung his wide wings forward. *Whoa!* A sound tore through the air, threatening to rupture eardrums. Dust from the training ground was blasted outward, creating a thick haze. The students turned their heads, wincing at the deafening roar. But soon, fear replaced their discomfort as they stared at the settling dust. Imagining Shirone’s bloodied corpse, they wished the dust hadn’t cleared.

“Shirone?”

Maya stepped forward with a pale face, but her body stiffened, unable to move. “Shirone! Shirone!” Amy and her friends rushed past her toward the evaluation site.

*Flutter!*

A heavy, slow sound blew dust in all directions. Nade, who had taken a mouthful of dirt, spat it out and looked ahead. Shirone, floating five meters in the air, slowly flapped his wings as he descended to the ground.

“Shirone, are you okay?”

Shirone responded with a smile and turned his gaze to Maya. A wave of exhilaration washed over her, and she slumped forward, her face a mask of relief. It was a contradictory feeling.

“I’m okay. Let’s finish the evaluation first.”

As his light wings folded and extinguished, golden fragments flared brilliantly for an instant before vanishing. The assessment teacher began recording.

“Shirone, you have passed the Movement Control Master difficulty. With this, your score for today is 30 points.”

It fell short of Fermi’s 100 points, but the minds of everyone in the senior class were bound to grow complicated once more. *All-Master Fermi.* Even though Shirone wasn’t an All-Master yet, he had already surpassed Fermi’s capabilities. Still, the rivalry between the two was as cold as ice. *This makes things more difficult. Isn’t this really going to break Fermi?* Aside from Fermi and a few others, conflict was inevitable among those risking their lives in this graduation exam.

Electric Monster Lichen spoke with cold eyes. “This is the first time I’ve seen such a relentless guy. Fermi, take it slow…”

Lichen’s face froze as she turned her head. Fermi’s pupils were no longer normal. His right pupil had rolled upward, and his left pupil was pushed to the side. It was the true monster hidden behind his businessman’s mask. *Damn it. The only thing we can do is commit suicide together.* Regular eaters. No matter how cool-headed he appeared, the thing inside him was a twisted monster. It was unlikely, but if Fermi lost his emotional control, the fallout would be catastrophic. *How much do you earn per month?* I thought it might be better to just send Shirone.

“Hey, Fermi. You…”

“Call an emergency meeting. All executives, gather around the golden ring.”

Fermi gave a brief instruction and walked away. As if he’d seen a ghost whose features had vanished, Lichen couldn’t quite remember Fermi’s face from a moment ago.

* * *

The evaluation for occupying the high ground was approaching in two days. Students were gathering secretly for strategy meetings, and even those who hadn’t finalized their teams had to make a decision by now. As befitting a mock graduation exam, everything from team composition to the competition format was autonomous. Students could nominate their desired teammates, and if nominations were tied or absent, marbles would be drawn. Until now, the only team with all members publicly revealed was the Shirone team. On the other hand, the screamer team was still shrouded in mystery. At least, that was true from the Shirone team’s perspective.

*Is she in the music room today?*

Shirone entered the graduating class building. At Iruki’s request, he had come to find Maya earlier than the scheduled strategy meeting time. As expected, the sound of singing drifted from the music room. The feeling of a setting sun washed over him, and his heart began to beat faster. As he slowly pushed the door open and stepped inside, Maya turned her head with a bright smile.

“Oh, Shirone! What are you doing here? We were supposed to meet later.”

Shirone didn’t even know why he was here. Even though a sense of distance always lingered between them, she was somehow more approachable than anyone else in moments like this.

“Iruki asked me to come a little early. He said he remembered the Screamer team’s custom strategy.”

“Oh, I see. Where would you like to see it?”

After closing the piano, Maya stood up and walked past Shirone toward the door. She carried a faint, milky scent, reminiscent of a child.
