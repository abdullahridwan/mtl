Second Purgatory (1)  
~*~

The Arun Mountains, located in the northeast of the Tormia Kingdom, were steeped in a gloomy aura dating back to ancient times. Long ago, Belkyr’s seal had been buried deep within this place. Just as magic existed long before humanity understood it, it was the mages who understood the ancient laws who had sealed the demons away. But now, thousands of years had passed. The will of the righteous had faded, and Belkyr’s evil had seeped through the cracks, slowly encroaching upon the seal. This was the beginning of Belkyr’s resurrection.

The majesty of Belkyr, as it shattered through twenty meters of underground bedrock and emerged into the mountain’s hollow, was overwhelming. Its fifteen-meter-tall, thirty-two-ton body resembled a grotesque octopus, its skin dark and necrotic. Its two arms appeared human at a glance, but a closer look revealed they were woven from writhing, multicolored snakes. Its reptilian, ferocious face bore only pupilless eye sockets, within which red flames burned like dying embers. With those flames flickering, Belkyr undulated across its twenty-four octopus-like legs like a tidal wave.

*“I will destroy the world! I will subjugate all creatures beneath my feet!”*

That was the only law that defined Belkyr.

Yet, the ancient demon soon felt the crushing weight of time. Humanity had long since built civilizations and was no longer so fragile that a single demon’s appearance could bring them to their knees. The Mage Sect, which had long tracked demonic signatures using ancient texts, immediately dispatched an official letter of cooperation to the Kingdom’s Ministry of Defense. With the King’s approval, a Cage-level party was assembled, and twenty-one mages descended into the Arun Mountains’ caves.

Eight hours and thirty-seven minutes had passed since then.

Cage Team B, composed of official 4th to 6th class mages, had already severed all of Belkyr’s legs and was now launching a final offensive to ensure its complete extinction.

“Quaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa!”

Belkyr dragged its mangled body through the cave, its snake-like arms thrashing in agony. Though it was a demon god possessing myriad regenerative abilities, the combined power of twenty-one human mages was far beyond what it could withstand, even at the height of its ancient power.

“Insignificant humans! How dare you defy me! Aaaaagh!”

Belkyr’s torso caved inward, forming a deep X-shaped gash. As the flesh split, thick, slimy ichor spurted out. A fat, bald man leaped directly into the wound, swinging his twin knives with blinding speed.

“Is the letter X a decoration? Huh? A decoration? Huh? A decoration? Huh?”

Twenty-eight super-tier buffs were currently active on the man. His arm showed no sign of fatigue, endlessly carving the flesh into an X-shape as he drove deeper toward the demon’s heart. A flush of excitement colored his face when he spotted a pulsating, light-purple muscle mass. He sank his teeth into the demon’s heart.

“Aaaaaagh! Delicious! So delicious!”

He tore the heart free with his teeth, sliced through the connecting tissue with a razor blade, and let the flesh fall away. The moment it slid down his throat, the intense, raw flavor exploded across his palate, sending electric shocks through his nervous system.

“Oooh! Oohhhh!”

**Meat Addict Benifis**

**Certified 5th Class Battle Mage**

**Passive: Amplifies magical power through the joy of consuming game meat**

**Special Note: Former butcher shop owner**

“Make it stop…!”

Belkyr clung to life until the very end. Any ordinary living creature would have died from shock long ago. It was a stark reminder of how tenacious and obsessive the very concept of a demon truly was.

“I-I can’t disappear like this…!”

“Sorry. I’m so sorry.”

A woman with pale skin and waist-length gray hair wept as she embraced Belkyr. Every time her hand stroked the demon’s writhing flesh, the spasms ceased.

“How much does it hurt? How painful must that be? Hehehe! Sorry. I can’t help it either… Hee hee hee!”

An unbearable, manic laugh bubbled up between her sobs. The contrast between her tear-filled eyes and the grotesquely twisted corners of her mouth was chilling.

**Saint Moriac**

**Certified 6th Class Healing Mage**

**Active: Wide-area healing magic with a 100-meter diameter and prolonged duration**

**Peculiarity: Manic Severe**

In the human world, she was so feared she was practically called a demon herself—a position equivalent to a two-legged Mara in the heavens. Yet now, she was merely a demon on the verge of destruction, brought low by a mere twenty-one humans. Belkyr finally realized the grim truth: he had been trapped. The mages’ abilities had systematically sealed his powers and worn him down for eight straight hours.

As the demon neared its end, Rose, the team leader of Cage Team B, turned to the main caster. As expected, he was preoccupied, smoking a cigarette in the corner and goofing around.

“Hey! Can’t you get in here soon? Do you have any idea how much this party costs per hour? Does the Association pay you to just dig holes in the ground?”

“Ah, that witch.”

Tarvan flicked his cigarette ash aside and turned his gaze toward Belkyr. The demon was already crushed and ready to be finished off.

*Stop messing around, you lazy bastard.*

Tarvan rushed forward, spreading his hands. A high-frequency sound pierced the air from his palms.

*Kaiser Blast.*

At the mention of the skill’s name, the other mages scattered in all directions. As Tarvan thrust his hands forward, Belkyr’s massive body began to vibrate violently.

“Remember, humans! I…!”

*Woooooooooooooooooooooooooh!*

Simultaneously with the explosion, Belkyr’s form vanished without a trace. Flesh splattered across the cavern, but even the remains melted away in the intense heat.

“Mission complete.”

The clock read exactly eight hours and forty-nine minutes since Cage Team B had entered the cave.

At that moment, the eyes of the communications mage, who had been dozing with his back against the cave wall, snapped open. Dense magical notation scrolled across the crystal ball hovering before him. It took roughly four hundred lines of complex script to convey a single sentence.

“Captain. Captain.”

As Rose turned around, the communications mage floated the crystal ball over her palm.

“It’s a call from the Association. Gather all of Team B in the cage.”

**Magic Association.**

The hallway on the eighteenth floor, where the president’s office was located, was crowded with mages. Team B’s members stood scattered about, their faces etched with dissatisfaction as they waited for their leader. Cage Team B was assembled by selecting only the absolute best performers in every magical discipline. It was no exaggeration to say they were the busiest mages in the kingdom, so it was only natural that they were unhappy to be summoned immediately after completing a mission.

“What are you so anxious about? I need to get home soon to look after my baby. Are you sure you don’t intend to do anything else here?”

“By the way, why isn’t the team leader coming out yet? Do you know anything, old man?”

Tarvan asked Horkin. As the eldest member of Team B and the officer in charge of discipline, Horkin should have known something, but he merely offered a dubious smile.

When the door to the president’s office finally opened and Rose stepped out, the mages immediately rushed toward her. As expected, the news was not good, and her expression reflected it.

“Team leader, what did the Association president say?”

Currently, Rufist served as the temporary president of the Association. Those outside the Association didn’t care who held the title, as long as the settlements were handled properly.

“It’s an urgent mission. I’m afraid you’ll need to run one more time.”

No one spoke, but the hallway grew tense as cold glares shot between them.

Saint Moriac broke the silence. “Why us? There’s also Cage Team A, right?”

There was almost no difference in skill between Cage Team A and Cage Team B. Regardless of how top-tier each field was, Tormia’s defensive power was not so weak that it lacked forty such talented individuals. However, while Team A had operated together for a long time, Team B was an improvised unit assembled based on immediate circumstances. Because of that minor difference, they were always the second choice. If the Association had to deploy a team this time, it would have been Team A, not them.

“I suggested we step aside, but currently, Cage Team A is temporarily unavailable,” Horkin explained.

“It’s because of Gaold,” Rose added.

“Right. Currently, Team A is tied up with a person presumed to be Gaold’s bodyguard. Until the truth is uncovered, we’re the only Cage-level party available in the kingdom,” Tarvan said.

“Still, it can’t be done. Isn’t the team leader the one who requested the contract from the start? And this friend of Roche’s has a daughter who can’t even crawl yet. She’s going to be beaten to death by Jesu.”

“That’s right, Chief. Please pull me out of this. Even if I have to pay a penalty…”

Rose’s eyes narrowed. “Don’t mistake this for a guild contract. Settlement terms are merely guidelines at the Association level. The Kingdom has currently declared a first-class mobilization order. Failure to comply will result in a Red Line penalty and punishment under Kingdom law. Do you understand the current atmosphere in the Kingdom? You’d better know how to crawl.”

The mages fell silent. Regardless of the Red Line, they had no choice but to comply.

Rose’s expression softened slightly. “As your team leader, I apologize. However, upon completion of this mission, the Association has promised a substantial reward. You will receive three times the original down payment and reputation points equivalent to a special promotion.”

Money was one thing, but the mention of reputation points immediately caught the mages’ attention. From the official 6th class onward, the points required for promotion were astronomical. Success in this mission would secure their share of the promotion points.

Horkin asked, “Therefore… what exactly are we supposed to do?”

Rose remained quiet for a moment, as if weighing the difficulty. Wasn’t the target’s level too high, regardless of being a Cage-level party?

“This target… is human.”

“Human? Who is the human?”

Rose’s eyes flashed sharply. “The former president of the Tormia Magic Association, Mikea Gaold, is to be eliminated.”

**Purgatory outside Heaven. Forest of the Vulnerable.**

After emerging from the tunnel where light flowed like a torrent, a vast expanse opened before Shirone’s eyes. Beyond the sea of clouds, the concentric circles of Heaven were visible, and swarms of creatures clustered like gnats in a specific sector. Having experienced this phenomenon before, Shirone took a deep breath and prepared to land.

As expected, the gravitational pull shifted sharply, sending him into a vertical descent. The sea of clouds vanished, replaced by a dense forest rushing past. He kept his eyes wide open until the ground was directly beneath him, then twisted his body to absorb the impact.

“Whoa.”

It was a much more stable landing than before, but no one offered congratulations. The nine people who had passed through the Guffin Gate first were scattered, gazing in different directions, lost in thought.

Sein spoke up. “This is the Forest of the Profane. It is the habitat of the monopods.”

“Single ears? It’s a quiet place. Shall we move out?”

*A secluded place.*

Shirone recalled the situation where he had been surrounded by poisonous ears. Thanks to that encounter, he had met Kanya and Lena and gained entry to Heaven, but it had never been a safe environment.

*Well, it might be considered secluded for these people…*

Sein continued explaining as they walked.

Purgatory was broadly divided into three zones: the habitat of heretics, the mainland, and the borderlands. The borderlands were a general term for areas inhabited by non-subjects, and their residents were specially designated as “borderline people.” Sein’s current destination was the mainland. Since it was the only neutral zone where heretics and vigilantes gathered, it was a necessary stop for gathering information.

As they passed through the heart of the forest, the ground suddenly began to shake as if an earthquake had struck. The party, instantly on alert, waited for a new threat to emerge. As the tremors gradually subsided, they exchanged glances and pressed onward.

“Oh my god…”

Hundreds of giants formed a long column across the plain below the mountain, marching toward a distant destination.

A giant created through Anecdotal Sake.

At the time, they had been too shocked by the giants’ existence to process it, but according to Sein’s explanation, they were currently en route to Jotunheim, the land of giants. There, they would regain their true identities and return to Heaven to uphold the law. If they failed, they would be denied entry to Heaven and banished to Muspel, the land of fire.

“Giants are a power that cannot be ignored. But they are just monsters. I’d rather ignore them and keep moving.”

The faces of the ten people who had been lingering in the underbrush vanished without a sound.
