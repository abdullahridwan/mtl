One-Man Team (1)
~*~
Assessment of mental activity at week 5.
The mental activity assessment, which measured changes in the shape of the spirit zone, had grown significantly more difficult since Week 5. The advanced technique of ‘joint’ was now applied to the four-directional method.
Because mental activity was the direct opposite of durability, Shirone approached the ceremony with a different mindset than usual. His thoughts were quick and logical, but he knew it was easy to fail if he treated it as a test of ordinary human limits. Wizards who specialized in mental activity possessed unmatched mental fluidity. To them, belief meant letting go of fixed convictions. Their spirits readily assimilated with others, even monsters.
“Arian Shirone. Next.”
At the teacher’s call, Shirone stepped into the Image Zone.
The standard diameter for the spirit zone evaluation was twenty meters. As the floor turned transparent, a spherical spirit zone materialized around him. A beep sounded, and the first test appeared: a figure-eight filter with two circles stacked vertically.
Week 5’s challenge required using the ‘joint’ technique on specific mental sections to form a symmetrical spirit zone.
*It’s harder than it looks,* Shirone thought. *I thought I’d have plenty of time after watching the others, but I was wrong.*
He channeled the ‘joint’ technique into his spirit zone with all his strength. Meanwhile, the filter closed in relentlessly.
Ten meters. Five meters.
With every meter that closed, his heart pounded. At the final meter, he tightened the core of his spirit zone, and the filter passed straight through. A green light flashed ahead: Pass.
*Whoa, that was dangerously close.*
The subsequent tests were tilted figure-eights. Unlike the first round, Shirone handled the spirit zone with growing ease, smoothly passing each filter. Though his mental fluidity wasn’t exceptional, he possessed another weapon: sharp insight. Once he grasped the core concept, his application was unmatched.
Shirone passed all the tests and scored five points, but his expression as he stepped out of the Image Zone was far from bright.
They had progressed to Week 5, yet not a single student had scored below five. The advanced level of the students played a role, but mental activity was also a skill that improved with training. Since Class Two had already taken the Week 30 evaluation once, they should have easily mastered Week 5. Still, there was no room for complacency. Class Three had exceptional talents.
“Next up, Arin. Please step into the Image Zone.”
The chatting students fell silent. It was rare to see a magician who wielded both a weapon called menarche and mental magic.
The lights in the Image Zone dimmed, and Arin’s spirit zone materialized with a soft blue glow. Among the five patterns prepared for Week 5, the same figure-eight filter Shirone had faced appeared again.
However, Arin did not move.
Even as the filter closed to within five meters, she simply stared at it.
*What? Why isn’t she doing anything?* Shirone wondered.
The moment the filter reached her nose, Arin’s spirit zone rippled like water, and its core tightened instantly.
“Wow…”
Gasps echoed from Class One. Even Shirone, who knew Arin’s capabilities well, couldn’t help but speak.
*This is absurd. How can the human mind shift like that?*
To Arin, the world was nothing but raw emotion. The turbid desires spewed out by humanity would drive anyone mad, burying them in anger, lust, and self-loathing. If her thoughts weren’t as fluid as water, she would have already broken down. That was why Arcane had once called Arin the greatest spirit talent he had ever seen.
After the stunt-like demonstration, the instrument panel displayed a filter volume ratio of 99.9%. It meant her spirit zone had perfectly matched the proposed shape without a single error.
*Graduating class really isn’t easy,* Shirone thought.
With that, all student evaluations concluded. As expected, the discrimination power up to Week 5 wasn’t high, so almost everyone scored five. Only Eider missed two questions.
The first four-pointer in mental activity this year. Shirone didn’t view it as a disgrace.
*He’s got a similar disposition to me,* he mused. Even at a young age, Eider had demonstrated formidable durability, reaching Week 5 in the survival evaluation. It was a similar tendency to Shirone’s own. The only difference was that Eider lacked Shirone’s sharp insight.
*He’s still young. If he trains for another year or two, who knows? He might even graduate.*
Eider stepped down from the Image Zone looking sullen. He glanced at Shirone and offered a pitiful smile.
“Man, that was brutal.”
“You wasted time. I wish you’d applied the ‘joint’ technique a little faster,” Shirone said.
Eider sighed. “If it were that easy, I would’ve. I’m not an absolute genius like you.”
There was a chance he could become a moderately impressive genius, thanks to his quick wit. Besides, he wasn’t unattractive either.
“What are you talking about? I’m the youngest graduating student. It just proves you’re standing shoulder to shoulder with us. Besides, mental activity is an assessment where proficiency increases with training.”
“Honestly, I thought the same thing until last year. But after seeing Class Three this time, those thoughts vanished. Like Arin, they’re all incredibly talented.”
Even in Shirone’s opinion, the current Class Three squad was a force to be reckoned with, rivaling any prestigious family in the kingdom.
“Besides, my major is Air. It’s the most common attribute. There are even rumors that untalented students choose it.”
Attribute affinity was closely tied to omnipotence, but nothing was absolute.
“That’s not true. How many strong players are there in the Air attribute? Sabina is strong, and…”
Shirone cut himself off. Citing Gaold, the head of the Magic Association, would have been perfect to cheer Eider up, but he didn’t want to risk leaking any secrets.
“Hey, Eider! What are you doing over there? Can’t you come out quickly?”
A voice shouted from the distance. Eider was already annoyed about losing twenty points, and Shirone’s teasing had only made him more irritable.
“Ah, I’m going. As you know, it’s the youngest’s survival strategy.”
“Haha! Yes, go quickly.”
Eider turned and ran. Though his skills were still immature, he made up for it with his knack for human relationships. In a way, he came across as modest, and Shirone didn’t mind his honest self-awareness.
*Work hard, Eider.*
* * *
After the evaluations, the students headed to the cafeteria. The senior class had flexible meal times according to their schedules, so the food was served buffet-style.
The friends sat across from and beside Shirone, continuing the conversation they’d started when Nade joined them.
“Then I got a cramp in my calf. I was in so much pain I crouched down, but they said I farted because I exerted too much force.”
“Kick-kick, that must have been embarrassing. So how did it go?”
“My legs are sore, but my mouth is bursting with laughter. That’s why you laughed and then cried! Fuhahaha!”
Shirone held his stomach and stamped his feet in laughter.
“I think I know what you mean. Oh, in that situation, tears come to my eyes.”
“Ooh, hey! Are you really talking about that while eating?”
Despite the mock disgust, a smile played on Amy’s lips. She had found more peace of mind this year than last, now that she was in the senior class with Shirone and the others.
“Hi? If it’s okay, can I join you?”
Maya from Class Two approached with a tray.
“Ah, of course.”
When Nade pulled out a chair for her, Maya bowed and sat down.
“Thanks. It looked so interesting, so I thought I’d listen in.”
“You better not listen,” Iruki warned. “Nade’s laugh is far from the dining table.”
“Ho-ho-ho! Are you okay? My stomach’s pretty good too. What? What?”
Shirone watched Maya, who naturally bonded with her friends through her unique charm. She had uncommon brown skin, and aside from always ranking last in the graduation exams, Shirone knew little about her.
Sensing his gaze, Maya turned her head.
“What? Why are you staring at an extramarital woman? Surely there’s a lover sitting next to her?”
“Uh? No, I just…”
Before Shirone could finish, Amy snorted.
“Hey, what about a lover?”
Maya playfully glanced at Amy, cut a large piece of meat, and shoved it into her mouth. She followed it with a gulp of water from the glass beside her.
Shirone and the others stopped cutting their food and stared. She hadn’t even tasted it; she’d just forced it down.
Realizing everyone was watching, Maya hurriedly spoke.
“Oh, sorry. Didn’t that look a bit bad?”
Shirone shook his head.
“No, not at all. It’s just a bit unique. If you eat meat and drink water together, you won’t taste anything.”
Maya nodded bitterly.
“Right. But I can’t help it. I have to eat to maintain this body. I have a constitution that doesn’t gain weight easily.”
Nade asked, “Do you have a reason to gain weight?”
“Of course. My major is sound magic. It’s also a buff-type attribute. The higher the volume, the wider the range of effect and the higher the efficiency.”
Iruki, who was chewing vegetables with his front teeth, asked, “You don’t necessarily get bigger just because you gain weight, do you?”
“It could be. But in my case, it seemed to work to some extent. In fact, I have no talent. That’s why I’m desperately trying to grow my body.”
Understanding the reason, Shirone reached for the salad on his plate and said, “I think you’ll look pretty when you lose weight.”
This time, everyone’s attention snapped to him.
Seeing the shocked expressions on his friends’ faces, Shirone realized his mistake and hastily clarified.
“Ah, no, of course you’re pretty now, but I thought you’d get even prettier if you lost weight… That’s what I meant.”
Maya, who had been staring at him blankly, soon changed her expression and smiled happily.
“Thanks. Shirone is really kind. I wish I were like Amy.”
“Heh, that’s good. Sooner or later, you’ll find out too. What a bursting personality.”
Maya smiled and cut another piece of meat.
“Whoops, is it? I’m good…”
Between Maya and Amy’s teasing jabs, the three boys simply buried their faces in their trays.
