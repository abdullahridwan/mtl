Chapter 472  
The Day of the Full Moon (6)  
~*~

20 minutes ago.

After escaping the containment chamber’s glass tube, Garas attempted to mate with every creature inside. The incident unfolded exactly as Fleur had intended, but one thing had been overlooked. Even she, who knew what a terrifying creature Garas was, could not have gauged the full extent of its fertility.

Garas, confined in that narrow glass coffin for days, had been driven to the most agitated state of its life. The moment it found freedom, its urge to reproduce surged to two hundred thousand times that of a human. Normally, the genetic information Garas injected into another species took an average of fourteen hours to grow from embryo to adult. But now, seized by a desperate instinct to propagate before it was too late, the gestation period collapsed to a mere three seconds.

“Giaaaang! KIA AANG!”

The giant creature let out a cry of labor, and dozens of Garas burst from its belly. The offspring, writhing as fetuses, grew to full adulthood within ten seconds and immediately attacked other organisms. It was exponential. In every corner of the containment chamber, new Garas were born, and the room reached saturation.

Bang! The iron door burst open, and a flood of creatures poured into the hallway like water from a shattered dam. The torrent of bodies collided at the intersection, then spread throughout the command center, driven by sheer momentum.

* * *

“Hoo-wook! Whoop!”

Only gruesome traces of victims remained in the corridor where Garas had swept through. A bald man who had been attacked leaned against the wall, breathing raggedly. His stomach was grotesquely swollen with the growing creature, the bloating more than doubling every second.

“Huh! Huh!”

A trembling hand grasped a dagger from the floor. The man’s face was a mask of terror as he raised both hands, reversed the blade, and aimed it at his own stomach.

“Aww!”

I have to stab it. It’s the only choice.

But in the end, his face twisted, and he hurled the dagger away.

“Aaaaaaa!”

His stomach exploded. A black monster, curled into a tight ball, spilled out. As its bent spine straightened, its body stretched to a full meter in length. It rested on the floor with two skinny arms and reverse-jointed legs, a long tongue lolling out. Its face was distorted as if smashed by a hammer, eyeless, with its brain exposed atop its head.

“Key, Kikiki!”

The newborn Garas prowled like a cheetah, its keen sense of smell detecting living prey.

“I… am… the King of Species… to be.”

Pak! Four cracks split the floor, and the Garas vanished.

* * *

Shirone and Fleur ran down the corridor, unable to look back. Everywhere they turned, there were Garas; they could feel the space to escape narrowing with every step.

“Senior! What do we do? We’re going to be surrounded!”

A pack of Garas appeared ahead. Fleur stopped, swung her Phoenix, and a whirlwind of fire roared out.

“Kiaaa! Kiaaa!”

The heat melted the Garas’ skin like porridge. But the wounds began regenerating—Kenser’s ability.

“Tch! Is this a joke?”

Garas was not inherently a powerful creature. But its bizarre fertility—offspring within ten seconds—combined with a laboratory environment where all manner of organisms were isolated, had spawned an absurd species. As the population exploded in the confined space, some mutants even began reproducing with their own kind, and with each generation, the Garas grew stronger.

Shirone gritted his teeth, exhaustion plain on his face. By now, he could identify most of the creatures Raysis had collected just by looking at the forms the Garas took.

“I’ll handle this.”

A dark sphere materialized before Shirone’s eyes and crushed the charging Garas. He had learned that the dark sphere was perfectly suited to countering Kenser’s traits, but this was still just luck. Every living thing has natural enemies, and with Garas currently active in an environment of infinite variation, time was against them.

“Kiririk! Kyririk!”

Slime dripped from the ceiling, hanging like dozens of pouches. The center of each pouch split horizontally, and protruding pupils activated an antithesis, freezing the two in place.

“Keugh!”

At the same moment, a Garas that had absorbed the power of Hororos, the invisible ear, bit into Fleur’s calf. Seeing the teeth marks clearly embedded in her white skin, Shirone raised his mental transcendence and counterattacked. He crushed the snake-shaped transparent Garas with his tentacles, then fired his photon cannon at the ceiling. The wall collapsed, blocking the exit.

Without a moment to think about what came next, Shirone looked at Fleur. If Hororos had been mixed with the traits of a poisonous organism, it would be disastrous.

“Senior, are you okay?”

Fleur looked at her leg, blood streaming down. She could still move. But the moment she saw the Garas growing more powerful with every passing second, she had already made up her mind.

“I’ll lure them away. You find Raysis. If she isn’t stopped, the mission fails.”

Shirone couldn’t answer. He knew it was inevitable, but the thought of leaving Fleur as food for the Garas was unbearable.

“Senior, I…”

“Hurry! You promised me! I won’t hesitate when I have to make a choice!”

Shirone clenched his fists and shouted. “How can I leave you, Senior! I’ll—”

Smack! Shirone’s head snapped to the side. Fleur’s cold eyes captured his face.

“Go. I can handle this. You know that too, right?”

Shirone felt no physical pain. The ache in his chest was far worse than the slap.

“Kill Raysis. Then come back and save me.”

Both of them knew that would never happen. That fact saddened Shirone more than anything.

“The more you hesitate, the less time you have to rescue me. Could you bear it if I died five seconds before you got back?”

“Senior…”

“Two seconds have passed.”

Shirone closed his eyes and straightened. For the first time in his life, he lied about someone’s life.

“I will definitely come back.”

He kicked off the ground and ran around the corner. At almost the same instant, a horde of Garas came rushing from the other direction.

Fleur was no longer afraid. On the contrary, her expression was calmer than ever, filled with relief that she had sent Shirone away.

‘Thank you. You’re a good kid.’

A dry smile touched her lips.

“Goodbye, Shirone.”

Fleur’s expression as she raised her Phoenix was as fierce as ever. Smoke billowed from her body as if she were about to explode. She had amplified her mana with omnipotence to the point where she no longer cared if she burned herself alive. She thrust her Phoenix forward.

This was the strongest magic possessed by the certified 8th-class wizard Fleur.

Hellfire!

Explosive flames, rising like clouds, filled the hallway.

* * *

Tears would not stop falling from Shirone’s eyes. But the grief that threatened to break his heart turned to cold fury the moment he arrived at the laboratory housing the Fuzix Machine. Glaring at the door with eyes that could cut metal, he slowly pushed it open.

Silence. Only the sound of boiling bubbles. The first thing he saw was a researcher’s corpse. Creatures were contained in the glass spheres of the Fuzix Machine that Raysis had opened with memory vision. Shirone stopped before one sphere. Inside was Ikasa’s body, cut into pieces as if chewed by teeth. Seeing her face turned upside down, bitter emotion flooded Shirone’s eyes.

‘Ikasa.’

He hadn’t been able to keep his promise. But thoughts of her would have to wait.

Without looking back, Shirone asked, “Are you satisfied now? Is this the result you wanted?”

Raysis’s blood-red lips curled upward as she walked forward calmly. “You’ve trapped yourself. That’s why you shouldn’t have touched Garas.”

Shirone slowly turned. “It’s the same for you. Give up now. Headquarters will be shut down.”

“Knock-knock! Are you really that naive? Do you think I did this because I was weak, or because I longed for strength? Don’t make me laugh.”

Crack! Crack!

Raysis’s bones twisted, and her muscles began to swell like balloons.

“I am already the strongest creature.”

Her height surged past two meters, then three, until she nearly touched the ceiling. Her entire red skin was tangled with bundles of gut-like tails, and organs—eyes, noses, mouths, teeth of unknown creatures—clung to her muscular body. Indescribable grotesqueness. It was a form with no regard for function, an ugly body filled with nothing but absolute malice.

“Shirone, become one with me.”

The photon cannon extended automatically, reflexively, the moment Raysis’s Neutral Bass resonated. But Shirone’s target was not Raysis—it was the Fuzix Machine.

Bang! Kwak kwa kwa kwam!

Raysis moved faster than the photon cannon and blocked the machine. Even under the powerful impact, her solid body did not budge.

Satan’s law – evil incarnate.

For a human, such power would be divine transcendence to the point of bodily collapse, but Raysis’s durability, combined with all manner of creatures, was not inferior to that of angels.

“Kuaaaaa!”

Raysis vomited powerful flames. Shirone evaded by teleportation and retaliated with Armand’s tentacles and photon cannons. The lab quickly descended into chaos. Raysis never left the front of the Fuzix Machine, but Shirone was still being pushed back. When flames and lightning mixed to the point of no escape, Raysis lunged, seized Shirone by the neck, and slammed him against the wall.

“Keugh!”

If not for Armand, his neck would already have snapped. Tentacles extended from his robe and attacked, but they couldn’t even break the skin.

“Hehehe, did you think you could win? Because you’re the Nephilim? Because you’re the light of Area 73? Did you think that meant you could defeat me?”

Raysis’s eyes glowed golden.

“I am Satan, the embodiment of evil!”

A new consciousness flooded Shirone’s mind. In a space of soaring flames, the incarnations of Satan and Shirone manifested, mirroring reality. Shirone’s golden wings swayed as if about to break. Satan raised his hideous face.

“Poor lamb. The Nephilim are no match for me.”

“Great!”

Shirone maximized the power of his incarnation with the power of transcending the mind. Satan’s face twisted for a moment, but then he focused his strength on his arms as if it were nothing.

“Ugh!”

Shocked, Shirone’s back bent to the breaking point, his legs struggling. The only thing that could stop the incarnation of Satan, standing at the extreme of evil, was the extreme of good. That was a level unattainable for a human like Shirone.

‘Is this how I die?’

As the incarnation’s presence gradually faded, he felt his strength draining. Perhaps his real body was already bent to the point of snapping his neck.

‘Senior Fleur…’

What was she doing now, besieged by Garas?

‘It can’t end like this!’

Shirone gritted his teeth and performed the incarnation again. He had to fight to the end—for his senior, who had thrown herself into that terrible hell.

“How dare you!”

As Shirone’s resistance intensified, Satan’s arm muscles bulged.

Flash!

At that moment, Shirone’s body was enveloped in pure white brilliance, and another incarnation overlaid the first. It was Armand, integrated into the Ultima system. As mind and body rushed into the incarnation as a single signal, it led Shirone to a state he had never reached before.

Transcendence of the mind—everything mindful.

“Kuaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa!”

A scream of beasts erupted from Satan’s mouth as Shirone’s body turned into light and exploded.
