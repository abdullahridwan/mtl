Chapter 412  
The Meaning of Survival (4)  
~*~

Level 3 was the Poison Sea.

A swamp stretched all the way to the horizon.

At first, the students used flight magic and stayed in the air. But one by one, they inhaled the poisonous gas rising from the swamp and fell.

That was when the true trial began.

The students who plunged into the swamp screamed, their faces twisting so badly they no longer looked like themselves. Shirone could not know exactly what they were feeling, but it was obvious that the pain was horrific.

Those still floating in the sky gritted their teeth and endured.

But everyone knew they would have to go down there eventually.

The Association was conveying something through the survival program.

The power of Mother Nature could not be defeated with magic. The strength to survive until the end was not magic, but a wizard’s will.

The students in the air began vomiting. The ones who had fallen into the swamp were splattered by it, but they were already in such agony that they could not even register what was falling on them.

“Haa.”

Shirone heard Nade sigh. Nade slowly tilted his head, looked at him, and said,

“See you later, Shirone.”

Starting with Nade, the students began dropping one after another.

Shirone could not endure it any longer. A muffled voice escaped his mouth.

“Yeah… Nade.”

* * *

The students eliminated from survival crawled out of the Virtual Zone one by one.

They sat away from the device with ice towels pressed to their heads, trying to recover.

Collie checked the Virtual Zone’s instrument panel and said,

“Level 3, Poison Sea, has ended. There are currently eight survivors on the red team and seven on the blue team.”

Olivia said,

“Hmm. Surprisingly many of them survived. This year’s graduating class is certainly strong.”

“There are exceptional students in Class Three, and Fermi and his group are still in there. Of course, Class Two doesn’t have any weak students either.”

Alpheas said,

“Stage 4… Jeolsa. This is where the balance of the match will shift.”

The Magic Association set Level 4 as the standard difficulty for certified wizards.

Because from this point on, the students did not simply experience pain.

They experienced real death.

Over and over again.

* * *

Whoooooosh!

Listening to the wind roar up from the abyss, Shirone was in the middle of an endless fall.

Until now, the program had reproduced extreme natural environments. But the fourth stage was a mechanical space.

He was inside an enormous chimney whose depth could not be measured.

Hundreds of thousands of blades spun fiercely, leaving no gap to escape through as they tore the students to pieces.

Death was an incompatible sensation.

Every time a terrible, tragic impact struck the mind, the Spirit Zone collapsed, and more students were eliminated.

Shirone clenched his teeth.

How many times had his body been dismembered and reconstructed now? Ten? More?

The program seemed to be asking him, Can you still endure this?

A massive blade waited below, spinning so quickly it left an afterimage like a solid tray. There was likely no way to pass through it alive.

Hold on! Hold on!

Shirone pierced through the afterimage of the blade in an instant.

But his body had already been sliced into three pieces.

How could he describe the feeling of seeing his own legs falling in front of his face?

People died, but no one had ever returned from death to explain it. Human language could never contain the sensation accurately.

The other students were no different.

One student was impaled on a blade protruding from the wall, stopping in midair for a moment before the blade withdrew and sent him on to his next death.
