Chapter 412
The Meaning of Survival (4)
~*~
Stage 3.
The landscape stretched into a vast, swampy expanse that reached the horizon. Initially, the students hovered in the air using flight magic, but one by one, they succumbed to the poisonous miasma rising from the swamp below and plummeted into the muck. The trial had truly begun.

Those who fell into the swamp screamed with contorted faces, their original features unrecognizable. It was clearly excruciating pain. The students still airborne gritted their teeth and endured. Yet everyone knew they would eventually have to descend. The Magic Association had designed this survival program to test more than just magical prowess. Mother Nature’s power could not be overcome with spells alone. True survival depended not on magic, but on a wizard’s sheer will.

Students in the air began to vomit from the fumes. Shirone watched in disbelief as others started falling.

“Sigh.”

He heard Nade’s heavy breath. Nade slowly tilted his head, turned toward Shirone, and spoke.

“See you later, Shirone.”

Following Nade’s descent, the rest of the students began to drop one after another. Shirone couldn’t stand it any longer. A muffled voice escaped his lips.

“Wait… Nade.”

* * *

Eliminated students crawled out of the Virtual Zone one by one. They placed cooling towels on their heads, sat away from the control consoles, and tried to rest. Collie, monitoring the Virtual Zone’s instrument panel, reported, “Stage 3, severe cold environment, has concluded. There are currently eight survivors on the Red Team and seven on the Blue Team.”

Olivia nodded. “Hmm. Surprisingly, a lot of people made it through. This year’s graduating class is certainly at a high level.”

“There are outstanding students in Class Three, and Fermi and his friends are still going strong. Besides, Class Two doesn’t have any slouches,” Alpheas added.

“Stage 4… Is it the cutoff? The weight of this exam will shift here.”

The Magic Association set Stage 4 as the official benchmark for certified wizards. It was the first stage where students experienced actual death, not just simulated pain. They would face it dozens of times.

* * *

The wind howled from the abyss as Shirone fell endlessly. Having already endured extreme natural environments, he now faced a purely mechanical hellscape. He plummeted through a colossal, bottomless chimney. Hundreds of thousands of blades rotated in tight, unbroken patterns, leaving no gap for escape. They sliced through the falling students with ruthless efficiency. Death here was absolute and inescapable. Every time a wave of terror and agony crashed against his mind, his consciousness in the Spirit Zone shattered, and more students dropped out one after another.

Shirone clenched his teeth. Had he already been dismembered and reconstructed ten times? It felt like the simulation was mocking him, as if to ask, *Are you really going to hold on like this?*

A massive blade loomed ahead, leaving a trailing afterimage. There was no way to pass through it unscathed.

*Hold on! Hold on!*

Shirone forced himself through the blade’s afterimage in an instant. His body was already severed into three pieces. What could possibly describe the sensation of watching your own legs fall away just below your face? Those who had never truly died could never comprehend it; human language failed to capture it.

Other students fared no better. One was impaled by a blade protruding from the wall, pausing her descent for a moment before being torn apart and sent into her next respawn.

“Hah… hah…”

With his body fully reconstituted, Shirone gasped for air as if drawing his first breath.

*Ugh! Just kill me!*

Ahead, dozens of drills rotated like a floor of grinding teeth.

*Crunch!*

Shirone fell directly into them.

*Squelch! Grind!*

In an instant, his body was pulverized and drained away like juice.

* * *

“Cadet! Damn it!”

Nade jolted awake in the Virtual Zone, thoroughly annoyed. Iruki, Amy, and even Fermi and the others couldn’t bear it anymore; they ripped off their headsets and collapsed onto the floor. Nade’s head felt like it would split open, and he was already nauseous. Vomiting and gasping for air were common reactions.

“It’s driving me crazy. Why do I always get stuck at Stage 4?”

Amy wiped her lips, her expression grim. Maintaining the Spirit Zone after repeated deaths required an obsession that transcended the fear of life and death. Seekers, religious zealots, killers—whatever their background, from this point forward, only those whose mental states bordered on the unhinged could survive. It wasn’t surprising that Iruki had been eliminated.

*You die when you die. What’s next?*

There was no logical reason to push yourself to death. The stage defied the rational mind, which abhorred uncertainty. Stage 4 clearly met the Association’s brutal expectations. Many students dropped out here, crawling out of the Virtual Zone with phantom wounds that felt all too real.

“Ugh, I’m done for. Hey, are you okay?”

Nade approached Iruki, rubbing the back of his neck. Iruki looked back, his eyes wide with disbelief.

“No way. Did you fall too?”

“Yeah. My head’s pounding, and I feel like I’m dying.”

After a pause, Iruki asked, “Why?”

“Why? What do you mean?”

Iruki had assumed Nade would breeze through the cutoff. But as the thought settled, he nodded in agreement. *Maybe this is just a natural result.* Nade never dwelled on death. Perhaps it was a deliberate mental block. If his mind ever truly crossed that threshold, he wouldn’t be a normal human anymore.

“No matter. Let’s just go. I’m already dead anyway.”

Collie turned to Alpheas. “In thirty seconds, they will enter Stage 5: King of Kings. Shall we open the vision feed?”

“Hmm. King of Kings…”

This was a realm that could no longer be explained by theory. Having passed the ‘death scene’ benchmark for professional wizards, it was essential to open the vision to monitor the students’ mental states. Prolonged exposure would cause fatal psychological damage.

“I’ll open the vision.”

“Understood. I’ll activate the master device.”

As Collie manipulated the controls, a large, concave, curved hologram materialized above the Virtual Zone. While the graduation exam had displayed a massive hologram on the ceiling from the start, this focused vision allowed the observers to clearly monitor the simulation’s interior.

*[System] Entering virtual simulation program, Stage 5: King of Kings.*

“Ugh! What is that!”

When the King of Kings scene materialized on the hologram, the eliminated students gathered before the Virtual Zone recoiled in horror. A swarming mass of vile insects covered everything. Hundreds of millions of bugs moved in a continuous, undulating wave, as if the ground itself were alive. The survivors were likely being buried alive somewhere in that mass.

Stage 5: King of Kings.

It was a place of pure fear, loathing, and agony—a realm where death seemed preferable.

* * *

The sight of writhing insects triggered a primal human fear, but this simulation went far beyond mere observation. The bugs penetrated skin, nerves, and intestines, delivering visceral, real-life sensations. The agony of being hollowed out and consumed didn’t just trigger a death respawn; it shattered minds. As students who couldn’t endure it continued to drop out, the mound of insects began to churn violently.

A figure, completely coated in crawling worms, suddenly scrambled to his feet, screaming and thrashing.

“Aaaaaaa!”

Eider, who had collapsed in the Virtual Zone, mirrored the screams erupting from the simulation.

“...Aaaaagh!”

He shook violently, rolling across the floor, clawing at his limbs, and trying to crawl away from the phantom sensations.

“Ouch! Aww! Aww!”

No one rushed to help him. Approaching him in such a severe state of shock would only worsen his trauma. Still, Eider’s innate talent allowed him to quickly regain his composure.

“Hah! Hah!”

At the threshold between the virtual and the real, he looked around frantically, tears streaming down his face.

“Oh, this is really scary.”

“...”

How could it not be?

The eliminated students watched Eider’s breakdown and felt a grim sense of relief that they had only reached Stage 4. On the other hand, the observers’ expressions were grim. Eider had consistently scored well in survival evaluations since last year, earning him a spot on the Blue Team. Yet, as expected, he couldn’t survive Stage 5. Meanwhile, Shirone—the student they were most wary of—was still fighting in the King of Kings.

*Shirone is enduring what Eider couldn’t?*

Nade was thinking the exact same thing. With an ice pack pressed to his neck, he turned to Iruki.

“Eider made it to Stage 5, right? He’s young and talented.”

“Regardless of his skills, he’s currently the youngest in our graduating class. You can be sure he has exceptional talent.”

The difficulty of King of Kings was nothing like the cutoff stage. Starting with Eider, students began dropping out in rapid succession.

Canis frowned, straightening her posture. After a moment, Arin slowly opened her eyes. Unlike Eider, they calmly managed their mental states.

“Canis, are you okay?”

“So-so. It’s much harder than expected. After Stage 4, the Spirit Zone becomes far too sensitive.”

“There are too many bugs secreting toxic substances. It’s hard to keep your mind still when they burrow into your gut and shoot toxins through your nerves.”

*You’re talking about mental immobility. These guys are crazy.*

Arin’s assessment, coming from a Spirit Zone expert, was harsh, but the eliminated students’ reactions were almost absurd. With worms crawling through their intestines, it was natural they looked unhinged. They were experiencing toxic overload and forced mental paralysis.

But that was exactly what was required. It was a sensation that could never be understood without reaching a depth of mental fortitude that numbers couldn’t measure.

*Everyone is amazing.*

While the eliminated students gathered near the Virtual Zone, Maya remained at the recovery area. Though she had been the first to drop out, her recovery was the slowest, and no one paid her any mind. The cutthroat nature of the competition was understandable, but it saddened her that she wasn’t even considered a competitor. She approached Shirone’s friends. They were the only seniors in her year who had shown her any courtesy.

“Amy, are you okay? How are you feeling?”

Hearing Maya’s voice, Amy turned her head.

“Yeah. I think we’re fine. You?”

“I’m a bit more comfortable now. Sorry. I was eliminated in Stage 2.”

Maya lowered her head, ashamed. She felt terrible for failing to meet Shirone’s expectations when he had accepted her into his team.

“It’s fine. Honestly, I was pretty shaken up too. Don’t worry about it.”

Amy offered a comforting smile. She knew exactly what Stage 2 pain felt like, so she couldn’t blame her.

“By the way… Will Shirone be okay? He hasn’t come out yet.”

Amy glanced toward the Virtual Zone. All the other students had been eliminated in Stage 5, leaving only two figures lying side by side. Shirone from the Red Team, and Fermi from the Blue Team.

“Anyway, the balance held until the very end. Whoever survives over there wins.”

Maya turned her attention to the holographic vision floating in the air. The sight was repulsive even from a distance, but watching it up close made her stomach churn again.

*How do you survive in a place like that?*

She had barely survived Stage 2’s Ice Jade. Yet someone was still struggling in such a nightmare.

*If it were me, I would have just died.*

Maya couldn’t bear to look, but she kept her eyes fixed on the screen. She was watching Shirone, the one person who had believed in her.

“Look—over there…!”

Boyle pointed at the vision, his eyes wide with astonishment.

The swarming bugs parted slightly, and Shirone slowly pushed himself to his feet.

“Oh, my God…”

Maya’s face drained of color.

The other students were equally shocked, their mouths agape, unable to look away. Shirone’s condition was horrifying. In some places, flesh was completely stripped away, exposing bone; in others, his skin was swollen and rotting. Maya couldn’t fathom how the human mind could withstand such agony.

Shirone’s body hung limp, his arms drooping and his head bowed.

A faint smile touched Alpheas’s lips at the sight of the bugs continuing to swarm even as they tore through Shirone’s skin.

“It’s remarkable. Complete mental immobility.”

It wasn’t that he felt no pain or disgust. It was a dehumanized state of absolute focus, maintaining concentration regardless of the circumstances.
