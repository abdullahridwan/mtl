Chapter 424  
Capture the Highlands (2)  
~*~

In the third round, the Screamer team abandoned their direct spear-to-spear tactics in favor of more advanced strategies. Pony, acting as a winger, crossed over to the center line to coordinate with Suabi. *I’m switching positions. As expected, this isn’t easy,* he thought. With three mages positioned along the center line, the left flank was effectively abandoned. True to form, Luman had completely retreated to the hilltop, covering all three lines. As Suabi stepped into the radius of the *Rhythm of Death*, his concentration wavered. Seizing the opening, Pony cast a spell.

*Refresh.*

A wave of revitalization flooded Suabi’s mind, completely nullifying the *Rhythm of Death*’s effects. Though it offered no physical defense, it was the premier rare magic for blocking most crowd control effects. It wasn’t an exaggeration to say that *Refresh* alone justified the inclusion of a water mage in the party.

“Ha ha ha! How’s that? Can you stop this now?”

Now fully equipped with a combat package, the Screamer transformed back into a combat-ready force and swept across the battlefield. Maya maintained her magical support until the very end. She couldn’t directly intercept Suabi, but she was doing far more than merely playing guard; she was anchoring Pony’s presence to a single *Refresh* cast. *As expected, Suabi is the key. It was the same last year… That kid is truly remarkable.*

The twenty buffs granted by the combat package each had different durations. The sight-enhancing buff lasted 1.13 seconds, while the muscular endurance buff lasted 1.78 seconds. Initially, they cycled in a strict order, but after roughly twenty cycles, the shorter-duration buffs would overlap, inserting themselves into the sequence. This rotation demanded the highest concentration of any wizarding technique, as it required precise calculation of timing intervals.

*Tick-tock. Tok. Tactic tick. Tactic tick.*

Utility wizards employed a unique technique called *Cross Force*, distinct from standard force applications. By establishing a cross-shaped intersection and mounting a battery at the center, they would channel reserved batteries inward from four directions simultaneously. The reason the twenty buff cycles could be executed at the exact right moment was thanks to a specific rhythmic cadence: *tick toc tak tick*. *Tick* for vertical strikes, *tok* for horizontal strikes, and *tak* for repeated strikes in the same direction. This rhythm allowed them to perceive and correct timing errors of mere fractions of a second.

*Tick tick tick tick tock tick tick tick tock tick tick tock.*

*Puff. Boom boom. Boom. Puff puff.*

Iruki carefully monitored the explosion effects erupting above the Screamer’s head, tracking them against the unique rhythm. *0.11 seconds. 0.23 seconds. 0.38 seconds. 0.49 seconds…* It was a machine-accurate cycle. The rapid replacement of batteries was truly impressive. *I’ll end it here.*

However, that assessment existed only within Iruki’s *Servant Calculation*.

*Cancellation.*

As the Screamer’s strength-enhancing buff was abruptly nullified, Suabi, who had been maintaining the *tick-tock* rhythm, frowned. As the sight-enhancing and flexibility-enhancing buffs vanished, the rhythm of the *Cross Force* began to tangle. *No matter how fast a Servant is, it breaks the rules.* *Cancellation* was a difficult spell because it required perfectly reversing the spell’s vector. Yet, that was a different challenge from reading and intercepting passing batteries at a rate of four times per second. In short, even while flawlessly analyzing the battery output and vector of omnipotence, Iruki’s thoughts raced faster than Suabi’s.

“What’s wrong, Suabi!” the Screamer shouted, looking back. He had never faltered in his *Force* execution, so this situation was unacceptable. “Where are you looking during the match?”

The Screamer hastily turned his head. At that exact moment, Nade thrust both hands forward, triggering a lightning shock that erupted.

“Kgh! You bastard!”

The Screamer’s body locked up in a stun state.

“What? A grunt like a butchered pig?” Nade raised his fist, stepping in for Shirone. “Cry out like one!”

A straight punch connected with the Screamer’s jaw, snapping his head to the side as if it might break.

Meanwhile, on the Screamer team’s side, Luman was doing everything he could to halt Shirone and Amy. He deployed blinding traps, slowing fields, air pressure presses, and various other crowd control techniques to buy time, though these forward-link combinations were proving frustrating.

“Damn! Damn Hong An!”

Amy’s crimson eyes were the bane of crowd control. No matter what effect landed on her, it was impossible to maintain control because her self-image memory reset her mind. *I can’t keep up. I’ll cut Shirone.* Shirone’s durability was formidable, but at least he was more likely to be caught in crowd control than Amy.

*The guy uglier than me is getting all the attention.* Luman was driven by personal spite, fueled by his belief that he was the best. To him, who considered himself devastatingly handsome if he just lost a little weight, Shirone was a political rival worth punishing.

“You must have even kissed Amy!”

“What are you talking about, you idiot!”

As Luman’s mountain-range aura erupted, a massive ten-meter-high barrier slammed down between them. *Amy’s been neutralized. Air pressure here!* The crushing atmospheric weight forced Shirone to a standstill. Seizing the opening, Luman kicked off the ground and launched himself forward.

After clearing the barrier, Amy’s eyes widened. Luman’s torso was drawn back like a bowstring, his stomach pushed out and belly button exposed. His molars were clenched so tightly his face was crumpled with exertion.

“Shirone! Harm!”

It was obvious that Luman’s long-term skill was the *Spirit Hammer*. A non-elemental, anti-magic crowd control technique that collided spirit zones to induce stun. Often compared to a headbutt, the *Spirit Hammer* also worked because the victim absorbed the impact energy. *I’ll smash him!* Luman intended to strike with everything he had.

As proven in the survival test, it was no exaggeration to say Shirone possessed the strongest durability in the graduating class. However, the harder the *Spirit Hammer* struck, the greater the backlash on the attacker. Betting on the pride of a crowd control wizard, embracing the pride of the most handsome graduate, and aiming to destroy Shirone in one shot.

“Gooooaaaah!”

Seeing Luman’s hideous, strained expression, Shirone instinctively activated a mental defense state. At the exact moment his *Spirit Zone* shifted to a defensive configuration, Luman retracted his stomach and swung his forehead forward.

*Whoa!*

“Agh!” Shirone frowned and stepped back.

It hadn’t been a direct head-on collision, but it felt as though someone had struck his skull with a hammer.

“Shirone! Are you okay?” Amy hurried over. Even from a distance, the *Spirit Hammer* had landed cleanly. *But why is there no follow-up?*

When Amy glanced back, Luman was standing there, panting. Suddenly, blood trickled from his nose. He stumbled through his sluggish body and collapsed onto his backside. Sitting with both hands braced on the ground, Luman’s eyes were wide open.

Although the anti-magic system was originally a balanced seesaw, the *Spirit Hammer* granted the attacker an unconditional advantage. Even so, casting it had been a shock enough to rattle his own mind. *Is this what it feels like to hit your head against an adamantine stone? Oh, Mr. Shirone… What are you going to do if you’re this hard?*

The recoil had stunned him in reverse, leaving him without the strength to stand. By the end of the third round, his spirit would hopefully begin to recover.

“Now! Let’s go!” Shirone sprinted up the hill, passing Luman. Amy, who had been monitoring the friendly camp, was also covered by Nade’s intervention. After confirming the Screamer’s jaw was properly dislocated, she followed. Eider struggled to fire an air gun but was forced to retreat out of range due to Shirone’s wide attack radius. Meanwhile, Amy secured the flag.

– End of round 3. Round 4 begins in 5 minutes.

Everyone’s movements ceased, and the tension eased. However, the Screamer’s eyes still burned with resentment. Though he had developed a short temper since entering magic school, he couldn’t stomach the shame of being punched by a wizard.

“Are you crazy? How dare you hit me?”

Nade snorted. “Why? Can you do anything about it? If I did the right thing, it’s right. Are you going to get angry over that?”

The Screamer suddenly closed the distance and shoved his face forward. “Yeah? Shall we settle this outside? If you’re a wizard, use magic. Don’t even think about punching me in front of everyone.”

Nade kept his mouth shut. A fight between a victor and a defeated opponent didn’t excite him. If it were a life-or-death duel, could he even be provoked so easily?

“Hah! You coward!”

When the Screamer stormed off, Nade turned back to his friends with a bright expression, as if nothing had happened.

“Cheer up! Reverse! We’ll keep going like this!” Shirone said.

“What’s the matter? What did the Screamer say?” Nade asked.

“Yeah? I don’t know, I forgot. Anyway, Maya! How was I?”

Maya quickly pieced together why Nade had struck the Screamer. It was a blow delivered on her behalf during her confrontation.

“Yeah! I felt relieved! Thanks, Nade.”

It must have been bothering her that she had kept her distance lately. Actually, she had been cold because of Shirone, but she felt sorry and thankful that they interpreted it that way.

“I’m glad Maya is here. Even though you’re playing guard, you apply debuffs, so the enemies are constantly wandering around.”

“Yeah! I’ll work hard in the next round too!”

Nade and Iruki exchanged glances as Maya’s face flushed red. It was a complete turnaround from just a few days ago. *I was praised by Shirone…* Maya no longer wanted to hide her feelings. There was a chance to hold back, but she was willing to jump into hell. If so, wouldn’t it be alright to enjoy at least this small pleasure?

Meanwhile, the Screamer frowned at Luman, who sat on the ground like a defeated soldier. Rumor had it he had struck Shirone with a *Spirit Hammer* and ended up like this. No matter how much he lived for his own tastes, he couldn’t help but make an absurd judgment.

“Why did you do that? You know Shirone’s durability, right?”

“Then what about Hong An? Pouring water into a bottomless jar. Wouldn’t it be better to break Shirone? Agh, but that guy really has a stone head. I’m dying of pain.” The Screamer sighed. “How are you anyway? Will you recover?”

Pony asked.

“I cast *Refresh*. But until the next round, I think I’ll have to rest myself.” Luman turned to Pony.

“Don’t do that. Let me rub your hair. It still throbs.”

A vein pulsed at Pony’s temple. *How dare you demand a massage as a member of the royal family.* In particular, Luman was the most hated man in the senior class, aside from Fermi and his friends. *You can’t ruin teamwork. I don’t want to hear that we lost because of you.* Enduring the urge to kill him three times over, Pony began rubbing Luman’s hair from behind. A deep groan escaped Luman’s lips whenever her thin fingers massaged his scalp.

“Agh, that’s cool. Anyway, Screamer. After this confrontation, I’ll introduce you to a pretty woman, right?”

Pony’s fingers tightened. She couldn’t understand Luman’s way of thinking, risking his life for personal pride. But after all, this was a magical world. Luman knew his own personality. If he possessed the skills, no matter how eccentric he was, he was the type of wizard who would be recognized.

“To be precise, if you win. So please recover as soon as possible. What’s the shame in losing?”

“Hah! Just charge up this round. Then push straight to round 5 and you’re done.”

At present, Luman’s strategy was the most realistic. Besides, it was clear that the Shirone team would at least survive this round thanks to Maya. The guard position had little to do except defend, but Maya had already been casting magic nonstop during the third round to interfere with Suabi. *Because my base stats are low, I must be exhausted,* the Screamer thought as he made the decision.

“Okay, let’s take some time. But that doesn’t mean giving up the round. When the opportunity arises, let’s strike immediately and fall back.”
