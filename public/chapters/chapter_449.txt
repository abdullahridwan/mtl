In Heaven (1)  
~*~

The sixth thousand jebul of Heaven. The Great World War.

There was an angel whose face was as transparent and beautiful as light itself. He was the pinnacle of intelligence, born with a slender two-meter-thirty frame and an innate understanding of the world’s fundamental principles.

Of the many angels, only eight were archangels. Among them stood Kariel, the Archangel of Birth, renowned for his unparalleled beauty.

Yet now, his face was drawn and pale, like a sick man’s. His eyes were sunken, his expression tense. The benevolent, sharply defined features that usually radiated wisdom had been distorted by recent years of strain. In truth, he was on the verge of collapse.

If an angel’s power stemmed from a pure concept, his current combat readiness had plummeted to a mere ten percent of its usual peak. All of this was the price he had paid to bring one human across.

“Kariel, are you really alright?”

Uriel, the Archangel of Destruction, watched Kariel’s fading presence with concern. Standing three meters tall—the tallest of the archangels—Uriel wore a breastplate thick enough to graze his chin. A golden halo spun above his head, flickering with agitation.

“I can still hold on. I have to endure. This is only the beginning.”

In human terms, the exhaustion Kariel felt was equivalent to going forty years without sleep. Any mortal creature struck by such fatigue would have seen every cell in its body wither and dry up. But Kariel’s eyes still burned with resolve.

Uriel followed Kariel’s gaze downward.

Beneath the galactic mirror that governed the world’s operations, a woman sat cross-legged. By human standards, she was stunningly beautiful.

Adrias Miro.

Could there be a more heinous notion for a heavenly being to harbor? She was humanity’s last bastion, the one who prevented even Ankera—the incarnation of the Akashic Records—from conquering the universe by erecting an impenetrable barrier.

Yet even she had fallen to Kariel’s relentless persistence.

“You did it, in the end.”

Just before the rebels were on the verge of annihilation, Ankera had abruptly called off the war and banned all investigations into humanity. All the angels had obeyed the decree, but Kariel had refused to give up. He had steadily pushed forward with his plan to destroy humanity.

What he had obtained in this way was the ‘body’ of the labyrinth, right before him.

“How did you manage it? Didn’t I tell you that the labyrinth’s space-time is indestructible?”

A faint, weary smile touched Kariel’s lips. “When I attacked the labyrinth’s space-time with a cloth bomb, Optrus passed through the rift. The electromagnetic patterns carried embedded data about the labyrinth’s time-space. I analyzed it.”

“But the labyrinth’s time-space immediately healed itself. Couldn’t you have just shattered her dimensional barrier and pulled her back?”

“The dimensional barrier is, in fact, insubstantial. The reason Heaven’s army cannot cross into the earthly realm is simply that the underlying protocols differ from this place. Earth is an independent world, lacking even quantum entanglement with Heaven.”

Uriel made no effort to understand. Those born for destruction were not obligated to comprehend what they were meant to destroy.

“Therefore?”

“I deciphered the protocol using the data I extracted from Optrus. Then, through quantum superposition, I transported the labyrinth into the Great World War.”

“Hmm. So?”

Kariel realized Uriel still didn’t grasp it. “Simply put, I reached into the barrel and kept pulling until I retrieved the item I wanted. It’s no exaggeration to say that barrel is the size of the entire universe.”

Uriel finally understood why Kariel was so weakened. “So you searched the entire universe and kidnapped her.”

“...That’s right.”

Kariel decided to move on. “Hmm.”

Uriel looked at Miro, who was lost in deep concentration. Right before the final war broke out, she had worn the same youthful expression as when she had single-handedly opened a dimensional wall against Heaven’s army.

“The human spirit is formidable, in a way.”

Currently, her mind—which formed the space-time of the labyrinth—was being endlessly drained into the dimension of cowardice. It was a mental state and conceptual framework completely incomprehensible to an angel who had been born perfect in every way.

“Hmph. That is merely proof of an imperfect being. Do not grow sentimental over insignificant humans.”

Uriel did not answer. For him, it was enough to destroy the incomprehensible.

But would Kariel do the same?

Birth was a concept established on the premise that everything is understood. The fact that Kariel, who wielded the art of life, could not comprehend humanity was perhaps the greatest contradiction in this universe.

“Humans are very strange creatures. Even now, isn’t a single human blocking all of Heaven’s power?”

Kariel’s expression crumpled. *Human. Human.*

He still couldn’t figure out why everyone, starting with Ankera, applied such different standards exclusively to humans.

“Nothing like that. I will destroy it.”

Uriel turned away. He did not wish to offend his twin, born on the same day but embodying a different concept.

“Fine. What’s the next move? Even if you kill Miro, the labyrinth’s space-time won’t just vanish. Why bring her here while torturing her mind to the brink of extinction?”

“Hmph. Of course it’s to destroy the labyrinth’s space-time. I’m going to drag the labyrinth out of the fear dimension. If I kill her once she’s back in her senses, the labyrinth’s space-time will collapse.”

It was the correct approach. No, the only approach.

“But we cannot comprehend human imperfection. I doubt any of our subjects can understand Miro’s spirit. The Nephilim disappeared after the Guffin was erased.”

“It is not in Heaven. It is in the kingdom of Earth.”

Uriel tilted his head.

Of course, in the earthly realm, there were those who spontaneously awakened as Nephilim. But as long as the labyrinth’s space-time blocked the way, it was impossible to retrieve anyone from there.

“Are you going to search the entire universe again? Then it might disappear completely.”

“I don’t have to. I don’t even have the strength left.”

Kariel ascended to the pillar-shaped central system and accessed the Akashic Records.

Uriel followed, hovering behind him and watching the screen.

“I was browsing the Akashic Records and found something interesting.”

As Kariel stepped aside, a human’s name appeared on the display.

Uriel read the explanation below and nodded. “Certainly. Interesting.”

Coincidentally, he was the one who understood the human spirit best.

No, it was only right that he saw it shift into its current form, given his deep understanding of the human mind.

“I will summon Lee to the Great World War.”

When Kariel opened his hand, a celestial body flashed, and a code of laws materialized on his palm.

The law code, containing the principles of the universe, was a high-strength metallic hardcover. Pages made of light began to turn.

As the central computing system fully activated, bluish electricity flowed across the floor like waves, converging into the arched mechanism.

Uriel watched the spectacle for a moment before turning his gaze back to the central processing unit’s screen.

*A grave robber…*

The name of someone who existed only as a concept blinked at him.

Morrigan Arius

* * *

Rebel First Command Headquarters.

Shirone’s party was met with enthusiastic cheers from the rebels. The heroic tale of Shirone subjugating hundreds of giants with the power of an archangel had quickly spread through the rebel drones, quickly becoming a new myth among the resistance.

Amidst the roaring white noise of celebration, Shirone’s expression was somber.

It was strange that the number “1” was attached to the headquarters, but the sheer scale of the rebel base—far larger than expected—dispelled any lingering doubts. Dozens of buildings, presumably munitions warehouses, surrounded the site, and a massive structure over thirty meters tall stood prominently in the forest.

*Incredible power. If they mobilize…*

Gaold thought differently. *They won’t even leave seeds behind.*

Of course, the headquarters’ power was formidable. Yet there was no doubt in the battalion commander’s words: they were on the brink of annihilation.

This wasn’t a matter of power asymmetry. From the beginning, beings of Heaven were fundamentally different from humans. If thousands of ants bit a human, the human would die. But just as a human would never be treated like an ant in the first place, so too were the angels.

“Uh? There’s Shirone! Hey, Shirone! It’s me, me!”

A familiar face popped out from the middle of the procession.

It was Clove and Gadlock, who had guided Shirone and his friends to Nord’s shelter a year ago in Heaven. The memory of Clove ignoring Amy still lingered, but it was a relief to see a familiar face in a moment like this.

But Shirone couldn’t bring himself to approach. The massive crowd, having heard Clove’s shout, was surging forward.

“Wow! It’s real! The real Shirone is here!”

Clove and Gadlock were quickly buried in the crowd, completely obscured. It was a natural reaction; very few people outside District 73 knew what Shirone looked like.

Shirone widened the distance with a cold expression. It was a situation where people could easily get hurt if they got any closer.

Watching Shirone walk away, Clove tore at his hair and lamented. “Oh, I never thought that kid would become such an important figure. If I’d known it would be like this, I would have gotten to know him in advance.”

Gadlock thumped the disciple on the head. “Is that even the time for regrets? This is a matter of whether we get annihilated by the angels or not.”

Clove said with a pained expression, “But Master said so too. When Shirone comes, we’ll be treated well here as well.”

Gadlock fell silent.

Shirone was back. And at the perfect timing.

The suspension of Heaven’s attack was certainly a boon to the rebels, but it couldn’t bridge the widening gap between Mecca and the Norse faction, which had deepened after a long losing streak. The command structure, unable to reach a consensus, had finally split into the First and Second Headquarters. There had been no actual battle, but the defeat was already profound.

At the point where everyone was waiting for their own self-destruction, the light of Area 73 began to shine on them once more.

*Why now? Did he come back knowing this?*

A series of events connected by an invisible thread made it impossible to dismiss Shirone’s return as mere luck.

“Hehe, it’s so nice to walk with you like this.”

Meanwhile, Lena, who had Shirone’s arm linked with hers, didn’t let go for a second as they headed toward the command center.

Fleur, watching Shirone’s stiff back, clicked her tongue. “What is that doing? Did you come to Heaven to fall in love?”

Gaold didn’t care. “Hey, leave it alone. Something like that will help with morale during the parade.”

A miracle called Shirone had arrived for the rebels on the brink of annihilation, so a little theatricality was acceptable.

However, Kanya was embarrassed by her sister’s bluntness. Unbearably so, she approached Lena and whispered in her ear. “What are you doing? Let go quickly. Don’t you know what Shirone means to the rebels? What if people misunderstand?”

“What misunderstanding? Shirone oppa and I aren’t even close. We met for the first time in a year. Can’t we even link arms?”

Lena turned to Shirone. “Brother and sister, please link arms next to you.”

Shirone couldn’t find the words to respond.

His heart was still pounding, but how was he supposed to keep walking with two women on his arms?

Kanya’s face flushed crimson. “You… what do you mean by that? Why am I arm-in-arm with Shirone?”

“Oh, right. My sister is too weak for this. We have to look close to Shirone oppa so we can work at headquarters. My sister’s Kuroi was also damaged. I need to get a new Kuroi here so she can become a pilot again.”

Kanya drank from her honey bottle and fell completely mute.

Although young, she thought of her younger brother more closely.

No, should I call it clever in this case?

Shirone reassured her. “Kanya, don’t worry too much. I destroyed the Kuroi. I’ll be sure to tell you when I see the Commander.”

Smiling at Shirone, Kanya opened her eyes again and looked back at Lena.

*Aww, that fox…*

Lena wrapped her arms around Shirone, whether or not she knew her sister’s true feelings, and simply smiled happily.
