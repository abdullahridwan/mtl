Second Outing (2)  
~*~

Arriving at Amy’s room, Shirone stopped in front of her door and caught his breath.

He listened for a moment, but nothing unusual came from inside. Only when he knocked on the door with his fist did he hear the rustle of blankets.

Clearing his throat, Shirone said, “It’s me, Shirone. I’m coming in.”

The sound of the blanket stopped as if it had never been there.

He waited for a while, but when no answer came, he spoke again.

“If you don’t open the door, I’ll open it myself.”

Something thudded to the floor, followed by the fierce sound of footsteps. The door flew open, revealing Amy with her face flushed bright red.

“What? What is it? Why are you suddenly making such a fuss?”

“I was worried because they said you were sick. But you don’t look sick.”

Amy let out a sigh, as if relieved.

She had realized long ago that half-hearted lies did not work on Shirone.

She stepped aside to let him in, then turned back toward her bed.

“I think there’s been a misunderstanding. I’m not avoiding people. I just want to rest. I’m trying to sort out my thoughts.”

“Yeah, I know.”

Shirone smiled and closed the door behind him.

Downstairs, Shakora, who had been reading the newspaper, raised the corner of his mouth when he heard Amy’s door shut.

“He’s passed the first gate.”

Isis, surprised as well, went up to the second floor with tea. Normally, she would have stayed in Amy’s room and chatted with Shirone, but this time she simply set down the refreshments and left.

It meant she was entrusting everything to Shirone.

Sitting across from Amy at the table, Shirone burst out laughing when he saw the teacup.

He had remembered how, half a year earlier, Amy had sprayed tea all over his face.

Amy seemed to be thinking the same thing. However, it was a dark chapter in her history, so she forcibly erased it from her mind and lifted the teacup.

“So, why are you here today? You’re not planning to drag me off to some strange place again like last time, are you? I want to rest this vacation.”

“Yeah. That’s why I came. To help you rest.”

Amy looked at him in confusion.

“You’re going to help me rest? How?”

“Go on a date with me.”

“Pffffft!”

The tea in Amy’s mouth sprayed out like mist, drenching Shirone’s face.

It should have stopped after a moment, but the baptism continued until every last drop in her mouth had been expelled.

Shirone slowly opened his eyes and spoke in a low voice.

“This… makes it the second time.”

Amy shot to her feet, her face red, and wiped her lips.

“W-what are you saying, you idiot!”

She grabbed a tissue from the cabinet, wiped her mouth, then pulled out a few more and threw them at Shirone.

As Shirone wiped his face, he said, “Every winter, they hold a festival in the Leth Mountains. I’ve always wanted to go, and since I had the idea, I thought we could go together.”

It was certainly a surprise. Shirone, the model student recognized by everyone, wanted to go out and have fun.

“You mean the old castle? The Ice Queen’s ruins? I know the place. I’ve never been because I was too busy.”

“Yeah. Let’s go for two nights and three days and change our mood. To be honest, I haven’t had much motivation lately either, so I need to recharge. Nothing dangerous will happen this time, so let’s just go and enjoy ourselves.”

Amy rested her chin on her hand and fell into thought. The part that bothered her most was the two-night, three-day trip.

“Just you and me? Without any friends? Where are we going to sleep?”

“We can find an inn. And we can have discussions at night.”

“Aha…”

Amy was at a loss for words.

In short, it sounded like he was saying they would share a room. But because Shirone said it so confidently, the atmosphere made it impossible to suspect anything improper.

“Are you…”

Amy started to say something, then shook her head.

He was probably being sincere. If she brought up something strange for no reason, she would only be the one embarrassing herself.

“Why? Is something bothering you? In my opinion…”

“No, it’s fine. Wait outside. I’ll get ready and come down. If I tell Dad where we’re going, he’ll have a carriage prepared.”

“Okay. I’ll wait downstairs.”

When Shirone came out sooner than expected, Shakora and Isis looked at each other in question.

But once they heard what Shirone had to say, they immediately understood.

“Oh, the Ice Queen’s ruins…”

Shakora thought back to his youth.

When he had been dating Isis, he had taken her to the Ice Queen’s ruins as well.

“It’s a beautiful place. Romantic.”

“Yeah, I heard that. But they say it takes some time to look around.”

Shakora chuckled.

“That’s part of its charm. You can’t come back in a single day. All right, go. I’ll have the carriage prepared. If you leave now, you should arrive tomorrow morning.”

Shirone let out a sigh of relief.

From a parent’s perspective, it would be natural to worry about sending their daughter off alone with a man. Yet Shakora went even further and offered advice.

“When you reach the old castle, look for an inn called the Snow Crystal. My wife and I stayed there too. You’ll be able to make good memories.”

Isis turned to her husband with a bewildered expression.

Her lips twitched as if she wanted to say something, but she missed her chance as Amy came out of her room.

“Shirone, let’s go. Dad, I’m going to the winter festival.”

Amy came down the stairs wearing a red winter coat with rabbit fur trim.

She must have curled her hair in a hurry, because steam still seemed to rise from the waves of it.

Isis, who knew her daughter usually did not dress up, was quite surprised by the transformation.

Shirone, too, stared for a moment at Amy, who looked far more mature than she did at school, before coming back to his senses and bowing to her parents.

“Then we’ll be going.”

“Yes. Be careful.”

It would take more than ten hours to reach the Leth Mountains, so they had to set out diligently if they wanted to arrive by morning.

After Shirone and Amy left the mansion, Isis narrowed her eyes and asked her husband, “Snow Crystal?”

“Why? What about it?”

“Are you seriously asking me that? Have you forgotten how we ended up getting married? Back then, you lured me there and—!”

“Haha! Did I? Anyway, you enjoyed it too.”

Isis put her hands on her waist and snorted. Apparently, Shakora did not grasp the seriousness of the situation.

“What exactly are you thinking?”

“If we’re going to use shock therapy, we might as well do it properly. Once the blood rushes to her head and then settles back down, she’ll wake up.”

“And if it doesn’t settle back down?”

“That’s for Amy to decide. She’s eighteen. She’s an adult. Besides, if it’s a matter of her own body, she should decide for herself.”

Isis shook her head.

The red bloodline that characterized the Karmis family produced exceptional talent in whatever field its members chose. Because of that, the family had, for generations, built a tradition that valued human free will over restraint and discipline.

If their talents were allowed to flow freely, they would succeed no matter what path they chose.

The greatest strength of the Karmis family was its broad spectrum. Members of the family could be found in nearly every occupation.

The moment discipline and oppression entered, talent was blocked.

Shakora knew that better than anyone. He had let Amy run free since she was a child, to the point that she had even gone astray for a time.

However, if this was part of the Karmis way, Isis had no choice but to accept it.

“Are you sure you can handle it? If you start regretting it later or say anything to Amy, I’ll be really angry.”

“Regret it? Why would I do that?”

Shakora opened his newspaper and resumed reading. Thoughts of his daughter had already vanished from his mind.

“Shirone will handle it.”

* * *

Heaven’s northern hemisphere.

The permafrost tundra.

In Heaven, it was called Optrus.

It was a pattern of electromagnetic nets that had drifted on the cold northern winds for eons.

The fact that its pattern of information exchange approached infinity meant that, in a sense, it was a divine being.

But Optrus could not become a god.

For Optrus, which had arisen in the tundra, Heaven’s environment was a dull, monotonous space without stimulation.

Without stimulation, there could be no learning.

It was the same logic by which a baby abandoned in the mountains would become a beast if he never left the mountains for the rest of his life.

Thus, for an incredibly long time, Optrus had lived by repeating meaningless, simple exchanges of information.

Then, one day, a crack opened in the Labyrinth’s space-time.

It was a tiny change, one that living creatures could not even perceive.

But to Optrus, it was a tremendous stimulus, as thrilling as birth and extinction.

An aurora spread over the tundra from the enormous magnetic field emitted by Optrus.

The crack in the Labyrinth’s space-time lasted three seconds.

Optrus broke through that gap and escaped into a new world.

Its first destination was the Leth Mountains.

The electronic network that composed it absorbed and learned from the unfamiliar environment with the speed of a newborn child’s brain.

The place where Optrus could gather the most information was the castle site halfway up the Leth Mountains.

Countless humans moved about, each one scattering information like an explosion.

At a level even they could not perceive, Optrus instinctively sucked in all of it.

Its electromagnetic networks blazed in infinite patterns, opening up a new frontier.

* * *

Around dawn, Shirone and Amy arrived at the Leth Mountains. After eating at a food stall, they made their way to the ticket booth at the old castle ruins.

The entrance was already crowded with tourists.

After waiting in line and buying tickets, Shirone widened his eyes at the spectacle before him.

“Wow…”

The old castle ruins had been completely restored with ice instead of stone or earth.

As sunlight scattered across the surface, everything in sight glittered like diamonds.

Even Amy, who had been acting high-spirited, turned red at the sight of the scenery.

Shirone was satisfied.

When he had taken her to the Galliant Ruins, things had gone wrong from the very beginning, leaving him embarrassed. As if fighting the Parrot Mercenaries had not been enough, they had even ended up going to Heaven and undergoing a trial.

To make up for the hardship she had gone through back then, he had sworn that this trip would be enjoyable.

“Let’s go. This is going to be fun.”

Shirone and Amy walked across the snowy field.

The rainbow bridge connecting the towers, the railings along the battlements, and the stairways inside the castle were all made of ice.

So this is why they call it a winter festival.

Since the structure would collapse as soon as the temperature rose, the site had to close by early spring.

Eventually, when summer came, the ice would melt and disappear, leaving behind only the dreary remnants of the old castle.

As if the memory of a winter day had been nothing more than a midsummer night’s dream.

“Shirone, let’s try that.”

Amy pointed to a food stall selling ice treats, one of the winter festival’s specialties.

Shirone put the cold snack in his mouth and chewed. Strawberry syrup had been mixed into white snow and frozen.

“All right, we bought something to eat. Where should we go first?”

“Obviously, we should go see the Ice Queen.”

Amy took Shirone and headed toward the castle.

The main castle was the least damaged part of the historic site. It had a hall of roughly one hundred and twenty pyeong, with staircases on both sides, but the left staircase had long since collapsed and been repaired with carved ice.

Perhaps it was strange to call a place warm when it was cold enough that the ice would not melt, but compared to outside, it was definitely warmer where people had gathered.

Shirone and Amy entered the corridor on the mezzanine level that connected the staircases on both sides.

At the point where the central balustrade jutted out into the hall stood the Ice Queen.

Shirone approached her.

Inside a huge block of ice stood a beautiful woman with her eyes closed.
