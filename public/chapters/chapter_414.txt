Chapter 414
The Meaning of Survival (6)
~*~
Tension was evident in the students' eyes. The seventh stage, a trial so obscure that rumors of it had barely reached them, was unfolding before their eyes.

“What is it? Are you still pushing yourself? You can’t possibly die from being crushed against a wall like that, right?”

“That won’t be it. If it stops there, there’s no difference from the previous stages.”

Collie’s expression turned serious.

*Cowardice is not the same as pressure. It was a terrifying trial that could end his life as a wizard.*

*We must respect the students' opinions, even though they are not official evaluators. But Shirone has already won. He doesn’t need to push himself this far to become a wizard. If he goes too far, everything will be lost.*

Unable to ignore her conscience, Collie, despite being an evaluation teacher, requested an unusual reconsideration.

“Principal, are we going to proceed like this? If we miss the timing, he may not be able to escape the Virtual Zone even if we cancel the program.”

The students whispered at Collie’s words. *Is it possible that even if the device is turned off, there’s no escape?*

Amy asked, her eyes wide with surprise. “You can’t get out? Even if that’s the case, shouldn’t you have told us in advance?”

“I didn’t say he couldn’t get out. I said he *might* not be able to. And information about survival programs is top secret. Our school isn’t the only one using this program.”

Currently, survival assessments were being conducted by the same program across all countries, synchronized to universal standard time. Even if he had known in advance, it was information he couldn’t share. But to be honest, he hadn’t really expected Shirone to get this far.

*It’s been nineteen years. Watching this again…*

In any case, since the evaluation teacher had exercised her right to intervene, what remained was Alfeas’ judgment.

“Where is the decision? The student will do it himself. Shouldn’t he already know how to get out of the Virtual Zone?”

“That’s right. But the coercion…”

“The dimensions are different. But isn’t that exactly why there are stages? It would be murder to trap any student and lock them up at the seventh stage, but Shirone has beaten all the difficulties to reach it. Isn’t that enough to qualify for evaluation? How can you become a wizard if you can’t even judge your own life?”

At first glance, the stance on education seemed harsh, but the students, who had already overcome countless dangers to reach this point, silently agreed.

“Aww!”

In the vision, Shirone was pushing back the darkness with all his might. But in the end, he couldn’t withstand it, and his body was crushed flat.

Collie let out a sigh of resignation.

*Am I going to do it in the end?*

This was the beginning of the cowardice.

* * *

*What? What happened to me?*

Shirone was compressed into a two-dimensional square. Lost in time and space, he couldn’t lift a finger.

*What the hell are you doing here…?*

The edges of the square folded inward like paper. Then, the pattern of folding from left to right, top to bottom, and right to left repeated endlessly.

It was shrinking.

It was shrinking infinitely into a one-dimensional point.

It was impossible to infinitely fold real materials, but the Magic Association had implemented the concept of cowardice through a virtual program.

*Ahhhhhh…*

A massive shock hit his head. Even in Shirone’s world, the Spirit Zone was on the brink of collapse.

Whether he was getting smaller or the world was getting bigger, the scale gap widened transcendentally. Pressure built in his head, and his eyes rolled back.

Each time a cycle completed, it was reduced to one-sixteenth of its previous size. Infinitely smaller. Smaller than molecules, smaller than atoms.

*No. I can’t stand this.*

The students wore blank expressions. They couldn’t fathom what kind of pain he was enduring.

Since their focus was locked on Shirone, all they could see was the pattern of folding.

However, even if he realized the mechanism of the cowardice, the feeling of accepting it would not have changed.

Until you realize it, you will never realize it. That was the colossal true intention hidden within the cowardice.

“Shirone!”

Amy shouted, her face pale. Shirone’s body was convulsing as he collapsed in the Virtual Zone.

Collie’s fists clenched tightly.

*Shirone, give up! If you give up now, you can live! Release the Spirit Zone!*

The coercive program struck his mind with the maximum gap that could be expressed in units of space.

Humans who had only ever lived on a single planet did not even possess the sensory organs to feel fear of this nature.

If he progressed further, even if they turned off the device, his mind would continue to be sucked toward the first dimension, and he would eventually become vegetative, unable to wake up forever.

After slamming his back against the floor, Shirone’s waist arched like a bow before he forced himself back up. The fact that his body, which had withstood the initial fever, was shaking so severely meant he was in a critical state.

“Move! We have to save Shirone!”

As Amy ran toward the Virtual Zone, Nade hurriedly grabbed her arm.

“Wait! If you interfere, either you or Shirone will fail!”

“I don’t care! You can take the test again next year! If Shirone goes wrong here, I’ll never forgive myself for the rest of my life!”

Nade looked into Amy’s burning eyes. She was sincere.

After a moment of hesitation, he let go and turned around.

“You’re right. Let’s save him together.”

Then, this time, Iruki stepped in to stop the two of them.

“Don’t be quick to judge. It’s Shirone.”

“What are you talking about! If Shirone…!”

“There is no change at all.”

Amy immediately fell silent. She then turned her head toward the vision screen.

Still, only the same pattern of folding was repeated.

“A change? What is it?”

“The folding is slowing down. Although subtly.”

Amy peered through her crimson eyes.

Clearly, the progress had been slower than before. And… it was getting slower.

*Aww!*

Shirone had entered a state of immortal function.

He was attempting to escape the ever-deepening pit of cowardice by extending his mind into the realm of infinity.

However, if he did not expand his mind faster than the speed of the shrinking, he would not be able to escape the bondage of eternity.

*It’s not enough. A bigger expansion…*

The bonds of his mind began to loosen. If he were an ordinary Unlocker, he would already be on the verge of losing himself and dissolving into everything.

But Shirone never lost himself.

Mental transcendence.

When the fiction of the scale was broken and the immortal function moved beyond the realm of fear, the folding phenomenon stopped in the vision.

“Stop. Stop.”

The students buzzed with excitement. Then, in the reverse order of the folding process, Shirone began to unfold.

“what? Why, why are you doing that?”

An even more surprising thing happened in the Virtual Zone.

The students slowly backed away. The fallen Shirone stumbled and stood up.

“How is he moving? Is he in a semi-liquid state?”

“Look over there! The vision!”

The students’ eyes turned back to the screen. Shirone on the screen was standing in the exact same posture as Shirone in the Virtual Zone.

With his head lowered and motionless, he raised his arms and began pushing against the walls of darkness to his left and right.

“I-that…!”

Collie shuddered in disbelief.

The Shirone in reality and the Shirone in the vision were performing the exact same movements, perfectly synchronized.

Shirone slowly raised his head and opened his eyes. At the same time, the darkness of the cowardice vanished from the vision, replaced by the scenery of reality.

Same place. Same people.

The two Shirones, real and virtual, were staring fiercely ahead with their arms outstretched.

*A coward… I woke up on my own…*

*[Stage 7, air coercion program ended. Disable the virtual zone virtual cold system.]*

When the machine’s voice fell silent, Shirone slowly lowered his arms and stepped out of the Virtual Zone.

The students flinched and parted to let him through.

Even the students who normally looked down on Shirone knew instinctively that they should not touch him at this moment.

It was none other than the seventh stage of the survival assessment.

While everyone’s eyes tracked Shirone’s movements, Shirone stopped in front of Fermi.

“There’s something I couldn’t tell you because you ran away.”

Fermi’s eyebrows rose.

“I will definitely become a wizard. I don’t care what you do, but if you get in the way of my dreams, I’ll drop everything and destroy you first.”

The faces of the students hardened.

It was a declaration of war against Fermi, and a message to everyone present.

*Don’t disturb my dreams.*

Faced with this life-and-death resolution to single out and defeat one person, everyone’s thoughts grew complicated.

*Damn it. Just Fermi alone is a headache…*

Anyone in the senior year knew that Fermi held some kind of control over them. The reason they couldn’t fight back, even though they knew it, was because the first person to be stamped would fail the graduation exam.

Of course, even now, Fermi’s influence was considerable, but with Shirone’s arrival, the atmosphere had begun to shift subtly.

*The second survival assessment master in school history.*

In other words, if the survival test counted toward the graduation exam, it was possible to pass with a hundred percent certainty.

*So far, I’ve been the one stepping on the guy Fermi targeted. What do we do? Am I the only one taking the fall because I was instigated for nothing?*

All eyes were now on Fermi. Depending on how he reacted, they would have to reconsider their positions.

Fermi gave a forced, toothy smile. Whatever Shirone said, it was just the ramblings of a loser.

*I’ll step back today.*

The students were shocked when Fermi simply turned away without saying a word.

It was the first time anyone had seen him exercise his right to remain silent, a man who always twisted situations to his favor.

*Damn you, son of a bitch. From now on, I will consider you my number one enemy. You will regret it for the rest of your life for making me think this way.*

As he walked away, Fermi’s face displayed an expression of anger that no one in his senior class had ever seen.

“The final survivor, Arian Shirone. Twenty points are added to all members of the red team, and twenty points are deducted from all members of the blue team.”

When Collie’s declaration fell, the students’ focus returned to team versus team, rather than Shirone and Fermi.

“Done! We won! That’s twenty points!”

“That’s the score I got by beating Fermi! It feels amazing! After all, my judgment was correct!”

Amy and Nade embraced each other and ran around in celebration, and Shirone also celebrated the victory by exchanging high fives with Iruki.

“Wow…”

An exclamation of admiration erupted from Maya’s mouth as she watched the scene from afar.

He was a veteran of the senior year, but he had been the first to go head-to-head with Fermi.

But soon, his eyes sank with loneliness.

*I hope… the strong man…*

He had never even dreamed of going against Fermi. Of course, he wouldn’t mind if he messed around.

To him, who was trying to solve the poverty of his tribe by becoming a magician even after getting caught, Shirone was a person living in another world.

“Shirone! It was amazing! I never thought I would pass the seventh level!”

“As expected, my prediction was correct! No matter how great Fermi is, he can’t beat the Unlocker!”

*Shirone, Shirone, Shirone.*

Even the students, who were normally distant, praised Shirone’s performance as part of the team today.

Shirone had led the team to victory by advancing at least two more steps than the other participants. Thanks to that, fifteen people earned twenty points each, so treating them coldly on a day like this would have a negative impact on interpersonal relationships.

After comforting the blue team members as they left the training ground, Alfeas finally looked back at Shirone. It was a complicated feeling.

*Is this really the boy I used to know?*

The boy who had climbed over the wall at the age of twelve and asked him to teach magic had now become a student who had broken through fear.

He was happy with the student’s growth, but on the other hand, he was concerned about the too rapid development.

It was no coincidence that memories from long ago overlapped in his head.

Looking at Shirone, Olivia spoke.

“Mind transcendence, or self-denial equivalent to it. Except in two cases, it is impossible to escape the air terror on your own.”

Alfeas let out a bitter laugh.

“The vastness of the universe may be only the size of dust to beings outside. So what do we mean in this universe? That terrifying truth is the true intention that Gong-Gyeol wants to convey. Shirone took the first step toward the truth.”

The two did not speak for a long time. He knew that the sights that came to his mind were no different.

“How close was that child to the truth?”

Nineteen years ago, for the first time in school history, a student had passed the seventh stage of survival.

It was an Unlocker who had transcended the essence of humanity, who had realized the truth of scale.

“Adrias Miro. While building the school, I hoped that at least one genius would surpass me. But the emotion I felt when I saw the maze was fear. Perhaps that child was a human who should not have been born into this world.”

Alfeas had also been a genius recognized by the kingdom when he was young, but Miro was different.

He was a student so eccentric and idealistic that even the word genius could not fully capture him.

“Shortly after the survival test, there were twenty judges. I can’t guarantee that it didn’t affect him. Everyone wants to be a genius, but it must have been a curse to Miro.”

Olivia’s eyes softened.

“Me… Will you be resentful?”

“It was a difficult problem. We all lived because of Miro’s sacrifice. And after nineteen years, Shirone is standing here.”

That was why he was anxious.

History did not repeat itself just because some parts were similar, but the judgment of the twenty people was the original sin of mankind, which should never have occurred.

“Don’t leap. The ‘Holy War’ doesn’t move just because he broke through the cowardice. At that time, Miro was already a completed scale wizard. Even if he had been sent out to society as he was, he would have been at the highest level in the world.”

“I guess so. But in the end, the level leads to the state.”

There was no limit yet. Then there was no guarantee that the feared event would not happen.

The appearance of Shirone laughing at the compliments of his friends resembled the sunny Miro from nineteen years ago.

*Miro put the lives of all mankind in his bowl. Shirone, what are you going to put in that huge vessel?*

On the final holiday of Week Four, the survival assessments were completed.
