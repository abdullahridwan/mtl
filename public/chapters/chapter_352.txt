Chapter 352  
Second Outing (3)

~*~

“This is the Ice Queen. She really is as beautiful as the rumors say.”

“Eight hundred years ago, wizards were denounced as witches. In a sense, she was an unfortunate woman.”

Amy gave Shirone a brief account of the Ice Queen.

The Ice Queen had originally been a wizard.

At the time, she lived under the rule of the priesthood and had to hide her identity, but there was no hiding her extraordinary beauty. When word of that beauty reached the royal castle, the aging king summoned her in person. He fell in love with her at first sight and took her as his queen.

But darkness had already taken root in the heart of a woman who had spent her entire life hunted by the Holy Church.

Hundreds of innocent people were tortured and killed in the name of experiments in sorcery.

As her people died, the queen’s magic grew stronger. And as her magical power grew, the queen went mad.

Insane wizards were extremely rare. Magic itself was a discipline that strengthened the mind. However, if a wizard’s mind was destroyed for some reason, the result was far more dangerous than ordinary mental illness.

As the mind and magic assimilated, magical power became impossible to control.

Wizards called this phenomenon magic match.

Consumed by madness, the queen froze everything around her.

Her room, strewn with icicles, became like an ice cave in the Arctic. The bed, dressing table, and other furniture were frozen solid, as were the maids and servants who had once attended her.

When the situation grew impossible to manage, the king built a small castle in the Leth Mountains and sent the queen there. Though she was still called queen, it was effectively exile.

But even there, her madness did not stop.

The first subjects of her experiments were dozens of attendants. When they had all died, she began capturing commoners and continuing her cruel research.

After a year, no one remained in the small castle in the Leth Mountains.

But the queen was satisfied. After long experimentation, she had finally reached the state she desired.

“I will sleep in a place where even time is frozen,” she said. “On the day I return, the world will kneel at my feet.”

The queen cast her final spell, sealing herself inside a giant block of ice.

That was the anecdote behind the final appearance of the Ice Queen now before Shirone.

“Hmm. I see.”

Shirone moved a little closer to the Ice Queen.

After hearing the story, she felt strangely real. With her eyes closed as if in hibernation, she truly looked like a person from eight hundred years ago who had fallen asleep.

Amy finished the tale.

“Maybe even after her mind broke, she was still tormented by guilt. I think that’s why she devoted herself to magic that could even freeze herself.”

“That could be. If it’s true, that’s incredible.”

A wizard could not be killed by magic he cast himself.

It was not theoretically impossible, but casting self-harming magic required willpower that bordered on the absolute.

“If the Spirit Zone wavers, the magic won’t activate. It’s something an ordinary wizard couldn’t even attempt.”

“Hmm. Maybe it’s closely related to the magic match phenomenon?”

Shirone stroked his chin and sank into thought.

Amy burst out laughing at his unnecessarily serious expression.

“Why are you taking it so seriously? It’s just a legend. We don’t even know whether the Ice Queen was a real person. Some say she was modeled after a tyrannical queen from that era. Now she’s only a wax doll trapped in ice.”

Shirone knew the winter festival was not based on actual history.

“Yeah. But she looks so real.”

Even Amy had to acknowledge the craftsmanship of the doll. Since the Ice Queen was the main attraction of the festival, it was only natural that great care had gone into making her.

Shirone and Amy stepped closer to examine the queen sealed in the ice.

At that moment, the Ice Queen’s eyes snapped open.

“Aaah! What the—!”

Shirone and Amy screamed at the same time, clung to each other, and stumbled backward.

The tourists turned at the sound. Once they realized what had happened, they burst into laughter.

Amy, realizing she was in Shirone’s arms, hurriedly pushed herself away.

Her face flushed bright red with embarrassment, and she stomped on the hard floor as she shouted.

“Y-you idiot! There’s a device on the floor. If you step on it, the doll opens its eyes.”

“How was I supposed to know? And you were surprised too.”

“Who was surprised? I only reacted because you screamed so loudly. Honestly, men are such cowards.”

Shirone pointed at Amy’s pale face.

“Haha! Then why is your face so white?”

“…Oh, is that so? Should I make your face even whiter? If I punch you hard enough to make you throw up, you’ll look as pale as soup.”

As Amy approached while cracking her fist, Shirone smoothly avoided her gaze, looked down at the floor, and muttered.

“If you’re surprised, you can just say you’re surprised. It’s not like I’m such a bully…”

“What? A bully? Fine, I’m a bully. What are you going to do about it?”

Amy sharpened her fist and jabbed Shirone in the side. Shirone, his ribs throbbing, fled down the corridor while flailing his arms.

“Hahaha! Stop! It tickles!”

“Where do you think you’re running? Get back here!”

Their voices echoed through the hall.

The tourists paid them no mind.

This was a festival ground, and a famous date spot for lovers.

However, after spotting Shirone and Amy, three students from Eins Magic School could not take their eyes off them.

To be precise, they were graduates who had passed their graduation exam this year.

Eins Magic School, one of the two magic schools in Creas, was not as prestigious as Alpheas Magic School, so its name was not widely known.

Above all, even after graduating, students from Eins could not automatically acquire a magician’s license the way graduates of prestigious schools could.

Of course, in practice, most guilds issued a wizard license if a graduate submitted a diploma. Still, compared to the privileges enjoyed by graduates of prestigious schools, it was true that their pride was wounded.

“That couple over there… aren’t they pretty famous?”

Ludvans pointed toward the railing with his chin.

He was tall, had curly hair, and wore elongated tinted glasses that made him look far more mature than his age.

“Oh, you’re right.”

Vivian recognized Shirone and Amy at a glance as well. Perhaps because she had a complex about the corners of her eyes drooping slightly, she drew her eyeliner more sharply than most.

Like most magic school students, they read the Spirit Journal without fail.

Most of its pages were filled with introductions to prestigious schools or their students, but academic journals actually sold far better at non-prestigious schools.

Students at prestigious schools had little need to buy magazines to read articles about their own school or the rivals they competed against.

“Arian Shirone and Karmis Amy. Wait, Zocre, don’t you know Amy?”

“Of course I know her. Very well.”

Zocre, the leader of the group, pressed his lips together.

He was of average height, with bushy hair, narrow eyes, and thin lips that revealed a stubborn personality.

For Zocre, Alpheas Magic School was a painful memory.

He had taken classes there until he was thirteen, then transferred to Eins Magic School.

The fact that he had confessed to Amy right before transferring and been rejected was a secret even Ludvans and Vivian did not know.

Of course, he had not transferred simply because a girl had rejected him.

It would not be an exaggeration to say that the best talents in the kingdom gathered at the five or so prestigious schools, such as Alpheas Magic School.

Moderate talent existed only to fertilize the soil for geniuses.

Zocre had decided he could not win in that competition and had sought another path by transferring.

In truth, most students who entered prestigious schools stayed there until they were old enough to graduate.

From that perspective, Zocre’s judgment could be called excellent.

However, in reality, it was extremely rare for anyone to transfer from a prestigious school to a non-prestigious one.

That was because prestigious families had their own pride.

From the students’ point of view, carving out a different path to avoid competition looked like running away.

Before entering magic school, all of them had been talented children praised as geniuses, so their sky-high self-esteem must have played a part as well.

‘Hmph! Prestigious name or famous signboard, it’s all just bluster. What’s the point of pride if you can’t produce results?’

Zocre had always thought that way.

However, if he asked himself whether he truly had no regrets about leaving a prestigious school, the answer did not come easily.

“Oh my gosh. I never thought I’d see them here.”

Vivian had memorized everything about Shirone and Amy. Confined to school life, she was confident she knew more about them than even their families did.

It was common among students of non-prestigious schools. The students featured in the Spirit Journal were the goals they aspired to and the idols they wanted to become.

“That boy is Shirone. He’s the one who defeated Dante, who boasted an undefeated record. And the red-haired girl is Karmis Amy. I heard the two of them were lovers, and it looks like the rumors were true.”

The word lovers drove a nail into Zocre’s heart. The fact that his heart still throbbed when he saw Amy, even after five years had passed, made him angrier.

“In any case, they’re still students. Clinging to a prestigious school doesn’t mean they passed the graduation exam. Don’t be intimidated. We’re proper wizards.”

After saying that, Zocre muttered to himself.

‘Though I haven’t gotten my license yet…’

Still, he was no longer the sniveling Zocre he had been at thirteen. He had proudly graduated from magic school and was now going through the formal procedures to become a wizard.

Amy, on the other hand, had failed her graduation exam rather spectacularly. When else would he get a chance to look down on her?

“Let’s go. Since it’s been a while, shouldn’t we say hello?”

Following behind Zocre, Ludvans added in a flat, courteous tone.

“As their seniors, of course?”

Unaware that Zocre and his party were approaching, Shirone had been cornered at the end of the mezzanine corridor and was being relentlessly attacked by Amy.

“Ahaha! Fine! I was wrong! Stop already!”

“You’re laughing? This is just the beginning!”

Her mischievous streak fully awakened, Amy jabbed Shirone’s ribs even more persistently.

Shirone’s laughter exploded like a bomb.

“Hm? Where have I heard that voice?”

A handsome boy looking around the history exhibition hall on the third floor heard them and turned his head toward the railing.

From above, he could see Shirone and Amy fooling around on the mezzanine where the Ice Queen was displayed.

“Are they here on a date?”

Apparently, Shirone had brought Amy here to comfort her after she failed the graduation exam.

The boy watched up to that point, then turned away without much interest.

‘Well, it has nothing to do with me. I’ll stop paying attention.’

Not only was he the type who did not care about other people’s business, but interfering in matters between a man and a woman was especially unacceptable.

‘Where is the antique art exhibition?’

The boy sucked on the straw of the iced coffee in his hand and disappeared deeper into the exhibition hall.

After their scuffle ended, Shirone and Amy went up to the second floor.

At the end of a collapsed wall, a rainbow bridge made of ice stretched across to the spire on the other side.

“Wow, it’s pretty.”

The railings were decorated with exquisite craftsmanship, and from the center of the arch, the ice garden spread across the castle grounds could be seen all at once.

After crossing the rainbow bridge to the spire on the other side, they found a mini-game room set up inside.

The game involved knocking down soldier targets standing on the watchtower opposite them, and the projectiles were snowballs made from the snow scattered everywhere.

“Now! Test your skills! Ten silver for ten snowballs. Knock down the soldiers, and you’ll receive an Ice Queen plush!”

After hearing the attendant’s explanation, Shirone turned his gaze to the Ice Queen doll displayed among the prizes.

It was a small, three-heads-tall plush that could be held in one’s arms, and it smiled brightly with large, sparkling eyes.

It gave off a completely different impression from the witch in the legend Amy had told him.
