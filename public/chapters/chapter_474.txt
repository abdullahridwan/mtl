The Day of the Full Moon (8)  
~*~

“Kikiki! Humans are funny. Emotions show in your eyes.”

Garas, who had subdued Flew, flicked out his tongue. The human before him was the perfect prey for a creature who had all his breeding stock stolen by his brothers simply because he was born late. As Garas had noted, Flew’s eyes were locked in fear. But it wasn’t fear of some terrible thing to come. In truth, she had already guessed.

The persistent headaches, the thoughts that never quite connected, as if something was missing—she was certain. Maybe, no, even if it was her own body, she would have made the same judgment. Death is a profoundly personal thing. The fear of extinction strikes both the original and the clone equally. That was why Flew could smile dryly in the face of death: the satisfaction of fulfilling her duty as a single wizard.

“Eat this.”

Garas didn’t understand what it meant when a human raised her middle finger. In that brief moment of confusion, Flew’s eyes slowly closed.

‘Shirone, be sure to become a great wizard.’

Perhaps the documents Shirone had seen contained evidence that she was a clone.

‘Thank you for saying that.’

He had said he would come to rescue her. The fact that she was recognized as Flew, not just a cloned creature, made her happy until the very end.

A clone’s lifespan is only one week. It had been a wonderful life nonetheless.

Flew’s hand fell limply to the floor. Only then did Garas realize that the creature beneath him had become a mere object. Reproduction, after all, is the desire to preserve the species. He felt no interest in beings that could not give birth to a second generation. However, the emotional plummet from the peak of excitement to the bottom of emptiness was unbearably irritating.

“Kuaaaaa!”

Grasping Flew by the neck and hurling her against the wall, Garas straightened up and let out a roaring cry. Creatures! He needed creatures! His sense of smell, which had undergone extreme evolution in matters of reproduction, detected another creature in the distance.

“This time, I won’t miss it!”

The afterimage of Garas, chasing the scent of a creature, stretched along the corridor like a black wind.

* * *

Reisis struggled with all her might. But as her muscle fibers were properly disassembled into plasma, her limbs moved only in directions unrelated to her will.

‘No! I have to get it back!’

Currently trapped in glass sphere #1, Shirone was a Type B equipped with the Ultima System. Moreover, the main type had been set, so if the Ilhwa's sake continued as it was, all organic substances would combine around Shirone.

‘How the hell did he survive?’

Even seeing Flew with her own eyes, Reisis couldn’t believe the situation. Could she escape the glass sphere? Why had the situation turned out this way? Was the woman in front of her really Flew? She thought she had to think, but only a few questions fell into a swamp of a loop that kept spinning.

Flew, having modified the Fuzix Machine, slowly turned around. Almost at the same time, the plasma filled up to the end and covered Reisis. She approached the glass sphere with a calm gait. Chuck! And Reisis’s palm was stamped on the inside.

Reisis pressed her face against the glass. Her eyelids had disappeared, and she wore a look of surprise, with unanswered questions still lingering in her eyes.

Flew held up the ring finger of her right hand. The spot where Kubrick should have been worn was much lighter than the surrounding skin.

‘Then what about the body? But since when...’

The moment Flew had removed Kubrick was a week ago, when she had spied on the underground laboratory while Shirone was away. Looking at the clone containment room, she had intuitively sensed what Reisis intended by accepting Shirone into the headquarters. To mass-produce Shirone’s clones, she needed a Flew clone with the same world’s genes. Furthermore, with the addition of Garas traits, the clones could be rapidly grown to adulthood.

After examining the Fuzix Machine, the memory transfer device, and the traits of various living things, Flew had come up with an idea. She would transfer the main body’s memories to the clone trapped in the containment room. She cut out only the last memory, handed over the drone and Kubrick to the clone, and then entered the containment room herself to wait for a chance to counterattack. It was physically and psychologically heavy work, but the effect was powerful. The most perfect way to defeat an opponent is to insert a knife from behind. It was a tactic that exploited the psychological blind spot of all members of the headquarters—as the saying went, a blind man can kill even a god.

‘No way, she was hiding in the containment room?’

Reisis stared at Flew with a blank expression. How had she never doubted it? A Kubrick always worn during the physical examination. The thought that interpretation was impossible without a drone had been buried in her unconscious, leaving no room to accept a new situation.

“Stop making that distant expression and disappear, you demon.”

Reisis, submerged in the liquid, couldn’t hear Flew properly. But an emotion surged within her far more powerful than any words she might have heard.

‘How dare you...!’

Reisis gritted her teeth. The teeth, as soft as rubber, were crushed in an instant.

“Even if I do, nothing will change! It’s all just coming together anyway! I’m going to have perfect beauty! I won!”

Leaving behind the cries of the drowning Reisis, Flew ran to the number 1 glass sphere containing Shirone and knocked on the wall.

“Shirone, wake up! Shirone!”

When she had arrived here after escaping the containment room, the Ilhwa's sake had already progressed considerably. Her first thought was to cancel it, but that was too emotional. If she canceled it, and Shirone was already more than half disassembled into plasma, there would be nothing to gain. It would be better to proceed like this but change the main type to Shirone. Then, even if he became a monster, the project could continue. Shirone would also understand—because it was magic.

“Shirone! Shirone!”

Yet in her heart, she was earnestly praying that Shirone would wake up right now and do something.

The moment Flew’s voice was transmitted through the liquid medium, Shirone’s stopped heart flinched. Unlike Reisis, who was 70% melted, Shirone’s body, protected by Armand, was still intact. Armand, who was fused with Shirone through the Ultima System, attempted a last resort using the fundamental nature of life. It delivered a shock so powerful that Shirone’s heart burst. His body convulsed violently in the liquid. Then a second shock struck, compressing his heart again, and his eyes flew open as air escaped his mouth.

“Ouch! Gagging!”

With the shock of returning from the dead, Shirone thrashed his limbs and struggled. A dull impact sound came from the glass wall, and he could faintly hear Flew’s voice.

‘Senior?’

She was alive. The moment that fact registered, his sanity quickly returned. The speed of his mental recovery would have been impossible without having experienced the second stage of the survival test.

‘Cold. In the coldest state of mind.’

Finally, he felt Armand melting away. That meant he was trapped in a glass sphere, and if it continued like this, he would be absorbed by Reisis.

‘By casting light width...?’

The moment he considered breaking the glass sphere and escaping, the memories from just before his death connected, and he hastily reversed his judgment. Reisis was an unheard-of Square Mara. If he escaped now, having already lost one battle, he would only become prey for the even more strengthened Reisis.

Although the process was different, Shirone reached the same conclusion as Flew. He took the Spirit Zone and reinforced Armand instead.

‘Let’s hold on. I’m going to hold on!’

He didn’t know what kind of spirit would be the subject when the anecdote was over, but if it was Armand equipped with the Ultima system, it was worth betting against the Square Mara.

Armand’s organic matter regenerated to the limit, protecting Shirone’s body from the plasma. And finally, after holding out for a minute, the plasma began to escape through the bottom of the glass sphere.

“Big!”

Shirone shattered his shell, pulled Armand down as if he were metamorphosing, and rose to the surface. His life was saved, but the outcome was unpredictable. If Armand lost the initiative to Reisis, what would be created in the central glass coffin would not be the demonic sword, but the devil itself.

Woo woo woo!

The sound of burning air caused Reisis to open her eyes. Everything was clear to her in her liquid state. The oscillating fluidity, the electrical stimulation that triggers life, Ikasa’s Valhalla action, Jinmaisikjong Galtomic’s magical power, and Ringer’s hard armor—everything was integrated and rushed at once.

Everything in the world is one. Didn’t everything beautiful and ugly originally start from one thing? As blue light descended from far away and colored the whole world in silver, Reisis smiled happily. In the reflected light, her image before meeting Satan was reflected. Pure white skin, slightly more developed breasts than others, firm thighs...

‘Whoops. But the feet are still ugly.’

Reisis shed tears. It was the most beautiful figure in the world—the one she had so desperately wanted.

‘Now... it’s okay.’

A blue light burned like a flash, and everything returned to one.

“Cool! Cool!”

With Flew’s help, Shirone escaped from the glass sphere and vomited up all the cloudy liquid. The pain was excruciating, as if his stomach had melted.

“Shirone, are you okay?”

“No. It hurts so much. But how did you...?”

At that moment, the glass tube in the center flashed, and the Ilhwa's sake ended. The glass tube rattled open as Shirone and Flew, who had stopped talking, looked at it nervously. Armand, shining in all natural colors, was showing off its form.

“Success, success.”

It was clear that Armand’s Ultima system had worked as the main type and incorporated the other traits. Without time to rejoice, Shirone moved. He needed to activate the Geumgang Armament as quickly as possible because his stomach was damaged.

Aaaaaang!

The laboratory door smashed open and all kinds of glass shards rushed in. Since there were no more breeding subjects left in the underground laboratory, it was natural for everyone to flow here.

“Kikik! Key profit!”

Hundreds of Garas, consumed by frustration, were excited at the peak of their lives. The moment Shirone grabbed Armand, Flew activated the Phoenix. Even without the Phoenix itself, the power of the Phoenix Pavilion was immense, but the flaming Garas charged at it, even risking death.

“Kikiki! Found.”

Flew, who had been fighting against the Garas with all her might, turned her gaze to the door. All the Garas stopped attacking and opened the way for the one Garas that had just arrived.

As Flew’s upper body was tilted by the Dawkins Algorithm, a tremendous load was transferred. All she could see was a dark afterimage passing before her eyes. In terms of speed alone, the Garas was supposed to be slower than her.

“Kikiki! This is fun too!”

The excitement that had subsided when he saw a human with the same shape as the one who had died with her middle finger raised a moment ago surged even more than before and activated his cells. Evolution, which had been progressing in real time, finally reached its final stage by transcending the gap of the missing link. The curved spine straightened out, and his face became close to human, resembling that of a bald man.

Sensing the air shimmer around her, Flew deployed an air shield, causing the air to expand like an explosion and push her away.

‘Kuu! Spirit Force...!’

It was a Justice affinity unique to the Norse people. Though Garas was born from a bald man, his power far surpassed that.

“You’d better have your limbs torn off!”

As Garas turned into a black afterimage and charged forward, Flew bit her molars and braced herself for the impact. She could have avoided it with the Dawkins Algorithm, but breaking her spine was something she had to be prepared for.

“Quaaaaagh!”

At that moment, Garas stopped. Flew hurriedly looked behind her as she straightened up, her shoulders completely narrowed as if bound by ropes, and groaned.

Shirone, who had activated the Geumgang Armament, was glaring at Garas with fearsome eyes.

“Antithesis?”

It was a completely different form of Geumgang Armament from what she had known so far.
