Steel Gate (1)  
~*~

It was the opening day of magic school.

Shirone woke early, folded his blanket, and packed his belongings.

Kubrick was surprisingly useful in everyday life as well. He divided his miscellaneous items among six slots and placed Armand in a separate slot, just in case of an emergency.

When he left his room and went downstairs, Olina greeted him with a smile.

“Are you ready? Wait, where’s your bag?”

“I have it. It’s right here.”

Shirone held out his palm and showed her the ring.

Olina stared at her son’s mischievous face for a moment, then turned away without asking any further. After the Kazura incident, she had come to understand that her son truly was a wizard.

“Hurry up and eat. You’ll be late.”

Shirone gazed at Olina’s back for a long moment. She was his mother, the only one in the world.

“I love you, Mom. And thank you.”

When Shirone hugged her around the waist, Olina’s hands stopped.

Of course, he was her only child, and at home he had always been affectionate. But a mother’s instinct sensed the heavy fate hidden in her son’s voice.

‘What is it this time…? God, please protect this child.’

Olina recovered her smile, turned around, and flicked Shirone on the forehead.

“You.”

“Ouch!”

“You’re all grown up, and you’re still acting like a child. And don’t go touching a lady’s belly.”

Vincent, who had just washed his face, wiped it with a towel and spoke solemnly.

“Exactly! Shirone, I can never yield your mother to you. So you should hurry off to school too, to Amy—”

“Stop fooling around and eat.”

“Yes, ma’am.”

Vincent sat down at the table with the disciplined steps of a soldier. Shirone’s laughter brightened the kitchen.

He wished this happiness could last forever.

* * *

When Shirone arrived at school in the Ogent family’s carriage, the front gate was crowded with people.

It was the new semester, and parents were bending down to nag their immature children at eye level.

When a voice announced that the entrance ceremony was about to begin, countless students hurried past Shirone.

But Shirone was relaxed. The senior class event was not until two in the afternoon at the Steel Gate, and he had already moved into the dormitory. He planned to stop by the study group and spend some time with his friends.

“Hi.”

At the sound of Amy’s voice, Shirone smiled and turned his head. But his happy expression soon vanished, and his eyes widened.

“H-Huh?”

“Why are you so surprised? You’re looking at me like you’ve never seen a red-haired girl before.”

Amy’s red hair had been cut above her shoulders. The ends brushed lightly against her cheeks, and her asymmetrically trimmed bangs made her look unfamiliar—almost like a completely different person.

Amy pressed her lips together as if holding back a laugh, then ran a hand through her hair.

“How do I look?”

“Huh? Well… pretty?”

Embarrassed, Amy stamped her feet, slapped Shirone on the shoulder, and laughed.

“What is that? Don’t you feel anything different besides my appearance? Like my resolve has changed?”

“Oh, yes. How should I put it… You seem a little sharper?”

“Hmm. Do I?”

Amy seemed satisfied as she fell briefly into her own thoughts, then returned to the conversation and asked,

“Anyway, how was it? Did everything go well at the Association?”

“Yes. I asked about heaven and did some field training.”

Shirone evaded the question without lying. He could not reveal a secret that even his best friend in the world would not be able to bear.

Amy smiled faintly but did not ask anything more.

After two months without seeing her, Shirone could tell that her confidence had clearly grown.

“It suits you. But why did you cut your hair?”

“Just because. It gets annoying when I train, and I wanted to make a fresh start.”

Amy was a promising graduation candidate who had risen to fifth place in the graduating class the previous year.

Since the key now was to break through the second-year jinx, it seemed she had chosen to reset her mindset from the beginning.

“Anyway, you’re here early. The event starts at two. Are you going to meet the stupid duo?”

“Yes. I’m going to meet Iruki and Nade, then we’ll go up together. What about you?”

Amy pointed with her thumb toward the uphill road in the distance.

“I’m going to the Steel Gate. I have to get ready for the meeting. Oh, right. Later, just say ‘me.’”

“Me?”

When Shirone pointed to himself and asked, Amy shook her head.

“No, not you. Me. Understand? If it’s me, it’ll be fine no matter what.”

“What does that mean?”

“You’ll find out when you see the event. You’ll be very grateful to me. Then I’ll see you at the Steel Gate at two.”

Amy wrinkled her nose, smiled, and turned around. Her hairstyle had changed, and her personality seemed brighter than before as well, but Shirone still had not quite gotten used to it.

With her hands clasped behind her back, Amy turned around once more and said,

“Welcome to hell.”

“Ha ha! Yes, thank you very much.”

Amy giggled as if she found everything exciting, then walked away.

Shirone shook his head, unable to help himself, and a smile spread across his lips.

‘That’s right. I’m finally standing in the same place.’

He thought he understood why Amy was in such a good mood.

* * *

Supernatural Psychic Research Society.

Iruki stared at the blackboard, which was filled with formulas, and sank into thought.

He had finally entered the graduating class. But to be honest, he had only happened to come this far.

‘Am I going to become a wizard? Me?’

This was a place he could have reached whenever he wished. At least, that was how Iruki saw it.

What had made him remain stagnant was fear—fear of how his monstrous brain might be used in the world.

‘In the end, I followed Shirone all the way here.’

It was not an unpleasant feeling. The reason he could remain calm even with the graduation exam only a year away was probably because a massive control device named Shirone had been installed in his mind.

‘Well, Shirone is practically a wizard by nature. Is he preparing well?’

To be honest, there was nothing to worry about when it came to Shirone. As long as he had a firm goal, he would never stop.

‘On the other hand, Nade…’

There was a very good chance Nade had done nothing this time either.

He had probably spent all day shut up in some storage room, making strange devices.

It was unfortunate, but there was nothing to be done. Nade did not want to become a wizard.

‘Has it already been six years?’

Six years ago, Iruki had been expelled from the Royal Magic School and transferred to Alpheas Magic School.

Of course, his personality had still been eccentric back then, so no one had wanted to hang around him.

Iruki had no interest in friendship either. The only person who caught his attention was Nade.

He was a boy who always sat in the back of the classroom and stared at the blackboard with bloodshot eyes.

‘Hmm. He’s unusual.’

After observing Nade for several days, Iruki began asking his classmates about him with his usual shamelessness.

“Nade? I don’t know either. He doesn’t talk. His grades seem decent enough.”

“I don’t like Nade. How should I put it? He’s gloomy. He seems like a pervert.”

“Why are you curious about Nade? You’d better not mess with him. His eyes are terrifying.”

Iruki could roughly guess what Nade’s school life was like.

Then one day, after several days had passed, Nade came to Iruki’s seat. His eyes were as cold as ice.

Iruki calmly raised a hand.

“Hi. What’s the matter?”

“Are you the one digging around about me?”

If you let your tail drag too long, someone would step on it. Rumors had spread that Iruki was asking about Nade, and the person himself had come directly.

“Digging around? Why would I dig around your backside?”

A snicker burst from somewhere in the classroom.

At the same time, Nade’s fist struck Iruki’s chin.

Iruki fell over with his chair and hit the floor, unable to move for a moment.

“I’ll ask again. Are you the one digging around about me?”

“Ptoo.”

Iruki spat out bloody saliva and slowly stood.

But it was a feint. The instant he rose, he rushed in and thrust two fingers toward Nade’s eyes.

“Oh!”

Iruki’s arm shot forward. Nade leaned back, and Iruki’s fingers stopped just barely in front of his eyes.

Even then, Nade’s expression did not change.

“…”

Iruki drew his arms back, took a pose like a snake, and prepared to fight.

The atmosphere cooled rapidly, and the younger students’ faces turned pale.

“Whew.”

Nade let out a long breath as he looked at Iruki’s ridiculous stance.

He did not know how such an idiot had managed to enter magic school, but it was best not to deal with him.

“I’m warning you. Don’t pay attention to me again. If you do, I really won’t let you go.”

“Nope. Why should I listen to you?”

A ferocious energy flashed in Nade’s eyes.

“You… really are going to die.”

“I know. I’m human too, so I’ll die someday. Are you stupid?”

Nade felt his reason snap.

The only reason he did not throw another punch right then was because he might kill Iruki.

“Hey. See me after class. Come to the place I mentioned.”

“Oho, is this the after-school duel I’ve only heard about? But are you sure? I’m pretty strong.”

Nade turned around without even answering.

If Iruki got scared and did not show up, that would be the end of it.

But if he did show up…

Iruki would not be coming to school starting tomorrow.

“Ah, my fist feels spicy.”

Iruki set his chair upright, sat down, and licked his lips.

Class began, but Nade did not come back.

The weather was gloomy, and by the time class ended, gray clouds had covered the sky.

Iruki stroked his swollen cheek and looked out the window.

‘It’s going to rain.’

Lightning flashed, and a delayed clap of thunder tore the world apart.

In the blinding rain, electric shocks and explosions chased one another without giving him time to breathe.

“Haah.”

Iruki lowered his chin and breathed roughly. The raindrops beating down on his head soon felt hot, and water streamed from his soaked hair.

Only his eyes were still alive. His gaze pierced sharply through the darkness and the rain.

Nade’s silhouette was visible beyond the heavy curtain of water.

Every time lightning writhed like an eel, parts of that silhouette recovered their color, but the monster-like expression Iruki had seen at first never appeared again.

‘This is a bit serious.’

Iruki had to admit his calculations had been wrong.

He had thought Nade was no ordinary person, and he had been right. But this was beyond abnormal. It had gone too far.

Iruki’s voice cut through the rain.

“You… How the hell did you become like that?”

Nade approached, splashing through the water. The terrible expression from before had vanished, leaving behind only a look of helpless resignation.

“What the hell are you talking about? Why are you so interested in me?”

“It’s not interest. It’s exploration. I’ve been looking for something lately.”

“Looking? For what?”

“As you can see, I’m a genius. A truly incredible genius. Depending on how I use my abilities, it could turn into a disaster. I might kill a lot of people. That’s why I need someone who can control me.”

Nade neither laughed nor took it lightly.

“Kill a lot of people?”

“If it’s you, you should understand. With my abilities, it’s more than possible.”

“That’s ridiculous.”

Nade spoke with cold eyes.

“You. Have you ever killed anyone?”

A tremendous clap of thunder shook the world.

Iruki turned away from the blackboard with a bitter smile.

The opponent he had fought to the brink of death was now an irreplaceable friend.

Of course, Nade’s personality had become milder after that incident and after a little more time had passed.

But at the very least, it was from then on that he accepted Iruki as a friend.

“Hey, comrade. How long has it been since our reunion?”

Nade kicked the door open, straightened his posture, and saluted.

Remembering the events of six years ago, Iruki burst into laughter.
