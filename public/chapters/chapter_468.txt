Chapter 468  
The Day of the Full Moon (2)  
~*~

At the Rebel 1st Command, Commander Crude stepped outside and gazed up at the full moon.

Memories surged through him—of comrades vanishing one by one on nights like this, their fates swallowed by the silence. A vengeful spirit stirred in his chest.

*Even for the sake of my dead brothers, I will win.*

“You seem troubled,” a voice said.

Crude turned. Sein approached, his silhouette sharp under the moonlight. Without breaking eye contact with the sky, Crude replied, “Not really. No angel patrols have been reported. It looks like this Great Full Moon Day will pass quietly.”

Sein followed his gaze. “Yes.”

Crude finally turned fully. “How are you? Is your study finished?”

Sein had been observing the motion of the planets day and night, calculating the precise celestial coordinates needed for God’s Punishment.

“To some extent.”

“Astronomy,” Crude mused. “What does it mean to a soldier? Especially in war?”

“It still doesn’t make sense. But there will come a time when it’s the only thing that matters.”

Crude raised an eyebrow. “I don’t know what you’re talking about.”

“I can’t say more yet. But don’t worry—it’s not bad for the rebels. At least you won’t be crushed without a fight.”

*Is it some kind of suicide tactic?* Crude wondered.

That was the limit of what he could guess from planetary movements. Even Gaold and Sein had been stunned when they first heard Shirone’s plan.

“Commander!”

Kanya emerged from the barracks, saluting. Lena stood beside her, along with her father—the Witcher.

The Witcher had once served in Heaven but left after his wife’s death. Now, he trained Mecha crews in maintenance and repair.

Crude returned the salute. “What is it at this hour?”

Kanya hesitated. “It’s just… staying in the barracks feels suffocating.”

Night walks weren’t encouraged for Kuroi Unit members, but Crude didn’t reprimand them. On a night like this, every rebel had the right to feel uneasy.

Kanya turned to Sein. “Any word from Shirone?”

“Guard Rock was visited a few days ago. Things seemed to be going smoothly.”

The story of how Shirone had overthrown the 2nd Command using the Nephilim’s power was a closely guarded secret—known only to a few in the 1st Command. Like the others, Crude had been displeased when Shirone was transferred to the 2nd Command, but he hadn’t voiced it.

It wasn’t the real issue.

“I’ll say it now,” Kanya added. “I don’t think Reisis ever truly accepted Shirone.”

Sein turned to her. “You mean the feud? The disagreement over biological experiments?”

“On the surface, yes. But we didn’t lose half our strength just from wartime experiments. What she did… it went against Norse common sense. It wasn’t just unethical—it was *unthinkable*.”

“Are you saying she crossed a line? What exactly did she do?”

Crude’s face twisted. “Laysis…”

***

***

A sudden rupture tore through the sky, cutting off his words.

“Ah!”

The shockwave slammed into them like a physical blow. Crude grimaced, while Kanya and Lena clutched their heads and dropped to their knees.

The Witcher, shielding both daughters, looked up at the dome-shaped sky crackling with electricity.

“It’s an ultrasound wave. It collided with the magnetic field.”

Sein lifted his head, his iron-gray eyes flashing with cold calculation.

Something reflected in the moonlight—flying fast from across the sky.

– *Termination of the search algorithm.*

Babel deactivated its scanning function and transmitted the results of its sweep across Purgatory directly to Kariel via wireless communication.

– *Magnetic field detection. 92.7% target probability.*

To the naked eye, only dense forest lay below. But the program had detected that 0.3% of the terrain shared an identical structural signature with the rest—artificial, like a patch of light copied and pasted.

The rebel command center.

– *Switch to attack mode.*

Babel’s eyes burned red.

– *Blow approved.*

With a sonic boom, the barbell-shaped projectile shot straight toward the command center.

Sein reacted instantly—casting a mass teleportation spell.

***

***

A deafening explosion ripped through the air. A crater thirty meters wide erupted where the projectile struck.

Emergency sirens wailed across the base. Barracks lights flickered on in clusters.

“Damn! What the hell was that?!”

Crude gritted his teeth and stepped toward the crater—just as a tall woman in inky black armor landed atop the rim with a metallic *clank*.

Kanya’s voice trembled. “That… that’s a machine, isn’t it?”

– *Punish the subjects.*

Babel’s command echoed through the air.

***

***

**Infinite Justice Halo: Valhalla Action**

Ikasa’s unique ability, *Valhalla Action*, reversed cause and effect.

If she desired an outcome, it would manifest instantly—provided she later paid the required time and effort as “cause.”

For example, if she wished to move from point A to point B, she could arrive instantly, even if the journey normally took ten seconds. But if an obstacle blocked her path, she had to account for the effort needed to bypass or destroy it.

If breaking through required five minutes, then upon arrival, she would be incapacitated for five minutes and ten seconds—repaying the causal debt.

Calculating this causal rate was the core of *Valhalla*.

Like pressing one side of a balloon, the effect inflated as the cause compressed. Set the desire, and the result followed—automatically.

But desires beyond *Valhalla*’s computational limits simply failed to manifest.

For instance, “I want to go inside that rock” was impossible—because there was no space within solid stone.

However, if a cavity *was* created—by any means—then the desire *could* be fulfilled, even if Ikasa didn’t know about it.

This meant *Valhalla*’s system didn’t rely on pure logic, but on the Akashic Records—the hidden blueprint of all possibilities.

Similarly, “I want to decapitate that human” couldn’t be realized. *Valhalla* couldn’t calculate the infinite variables of human action, so it couldn’t define the necessary cause.

But one desire was clear.

*Only Shirone needs to be removed.*

Ikasa stared down at the rising dust clouds, her eyes cold with murderous intent.

To her, Shirone was more than a personal enemy. He was the final obstacle to her reinstatement as a true angel.

And though she knew Kariel was making dangerous moves within Heaven, she also knew—Shirone was the only one who could stop her.

*He’ll be back soon.*

22 minutes and 42 seconds.

Under normal causality, that was how long it would take Ikasa to level a mountain with her bare fists.

Even on a full moon night, such power was divine.

While those 22 minutes and 42 seconds ticked away, the rebels scrambled to clear the dust.

The collective magic *Mahagard* blew the haze aside, but the cloud from a mountain’s collapse didn’t dissipate easily.

Shirone and Fleur had escaped before the cliff gave way. They hadn’t fled far—just far enough to watch.

Fleur summoned a phoenix from Kubrick and activated the *Dawkins Algorithm* in the spirit zone—a defense that automatically triggered when an object breached it at high speed.

When Shirone drew Armand and activated *Diamond Arms*, Fleur immediately sensed the change.

The ragged robe was now pristine, glowing with a deep blue hue—identical to the *Ultima System* he’d seen in Babel.

“What happened?” Fleur asked.

“I integrated it with the Ultima System.”

Fleur hesitated, then let go of his doubts. That didn’t matter now.

“Are there any combat functions?”

“I’m not sure. The connection changed from shared consciousness to full integration. But how it works… I don’t know yet.”

Finally, the dust cleared. The full moon shone down again, illuminating the devastation.

The headquarters cliff was shattered—no intact pillars remained. Norse corpses lay buried beneath the rubble.

All from a single surprise attack.

This was why the subjects feared the angels.

“That…!”

Shirone’s eyes widened as he saw the figure hovering in the sky. A memory surged—unwanted, painful.

*Ikasa.*

The fallen angel who had stood by helplessly as Amy, Tess, and Arin were taken—right before his eyes.

Her movements had been too fast to follow.

“Shirone, what’s wrong?” Fleur asked.

“I’ve seen her before. She’s a fallen angel.”

She was far stronger than the average angel, but Shirone didn’t feel his odds had improved.

Ikasa crossed her arms and descended.

An oppressive vibration pulsed through the air, making every rebel’s heart race.

“Huh. You should’ve run when you had the chance. Did you really think you could defy me? You’re worse than insects.”

Her voice resonated directly into their minds. One surviving executive roared, “Focus on her! Use collective magic!”

He began channeling *Aruope*, a spell famed for its unparalleled cutting power, drawing on the energy of the wind.

Ikasa watched as dozens of mages linked their spirit force to the executive. Then she laughed.

“Fools.”

She moved.

Her form flickered—like electrons existing as probability waves in space.

An afterimage appeared before each rebel. One by one, she struck—her delicate wrists delivering punches that knocked out wizards and officers alike.

From low-level mages to high-ranking executives, the entire command structure collapsed in seconds.

“Ghk!”

An executive doubled over as Ikasa’s fist plunged into his abdomen. Blood sprayed from his mouth.

“Knock, knock! Killing humans… it’s *fun*!”

Ikasa was strong. She could level a mountain in 22 minutes. But even without *Valhalla Action*, she had wiped out dozens of mages effortlessly.

Yet Shirone saw the truth.

He saw the *function* of her halo.

*Equivalent exchange of cause and effect.*

The *Ultima System* detected it perfectly.

When Ikasa turned, her eyes blazing with vengeance, a chill ran down Shirone and Fleur’s spines.

“Shirone,” she said, “do you know how long I’ve waited for this? Back then, you ran like a rat.”

“I haven’t forgotten what you did.”

“Ho ho! No, I think *you* have. You couldn’t even lift a finger. You watched as those women were taken. Will it be different this time?”

Shirone narrowed his eyes and lowered his stance.

“Be careful,” he warned Fleur. “That angel’s halo can reverse cause and effect.”

Fleur had seen the massacre with his own eyes. He understood instantly.

“Let’s go defensive. Buy time. There are still rebels alive underground.”

Ikasa puffed her cheeks and laughed.

“Pooh! Do you know why I hate humans? Because they’re deluded. They believe anyone can achieve greatness—just because they’re *human 1*, *human 2*.”

She kicked off the ground, launching a punch straight at Fleur’s face. The air itself shuddered.

Fleur bent backward like a willow branch—activating the *Dawkins Algorithm*.

The evasion worked, but the strain nearly shattered his spine. And even that wouldn’t last long.

“Keugh!”

Ikasa’s left hook exploded forward. Fleur twisted away, barely avoiding it. At the last moment, he used the phoenix to cushion the shock—but the impact still sent him flying like laundry on a line.

“Ho-ho-ho! Struggling is just what bugs do!”

As she laughed, a massive tentacle erupted from the ground, snaking up and clamping around her face.

Then, with brutal force, it arced into the sky and slammed her vertically into the earth.

***

***

“Aaaaaang!”

Ikasa lay sprawled, her face buried in the writhing tentacles.

*What… was that?*

A jolt ran through her skull. She hadn’t even seen it coming.

Not just a surprise attack—this was a strike too fast, too heavy to ignore.

“Jackanapes…!”

She slashed through the tentacles with hidden blades and lunged at Shirone like a coiled spring.

“Dangerous!” Fleur shouted.

But what happened next defied expectation.

Shirone swayed—just slightly—and evaded Ikasa’s relentless punch.

*I see it! I can see her attacks!*

If Armand had once enhanced his body, now Armand *was* his body.

Every motion, every diamond-reinforced limb, moved with the ease of a finger twitch.

“You think *you’re* human?” Ikasa snarled. “I am the body of an *angel*!”

She pushed forward, accelerating her conversion rate.

The moment her fist reached him, Shirone spun.

A heavy tentacle burst from his robe and slammed into Ikasa’s cheek, knocking her sideways and sending her flying.

***

***

“Aaaaaang!”

Fleur stared, stunned, as Ikasa crashed into the rubble of the command center.

Before Shirone, *Ataraxia* began accumulating at a rate far faster than before.
