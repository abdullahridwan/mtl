Main Character of Life (3)

Kang Nan took off her glasses and slipped them into her inner pocket. Then, like a wolf lowering itself before a pounce, she raised her fists to her face, set her left foot forward, and began to sway lightly in rhythm.

Shirone and the others stared at her in confusion. Even if they knew nothing about Ram Muay, they could tell she had taken some sort of Baktu stance. But in front of the enormous Thousand Arms Guanshiyin, it looked almost comical.

Unable to stand it any longer, Lyria stepped toward her with a frown.

“Hey! What are you—”

At that instant, Kang Nan’s body blurred.

A high kick flashed out too quickly for the eye to follow. Her foot sliced a threadlike arc through the dust, and her instep smashed into the face of the Thousand Arms Guanshiyin, snapping its head sideways at a sharp angle.

The Thousand Arms Guanshiyin’s expression changed to one of bewilderment. Its eyes rolled for a moment, and the countless hands that had been moving slowed to a stop. Then, as if sinking into sleep, it was sucked down into the abyss of the Jade King Bak Yojin.

“What… was that?”

The shock that struck Shirone and the others was beyond words. The Thousand Arms Guanshiyin had already been trapped inside the Jade King Bak Yojin, but watching Kang Nan break its neck with a single kick still made their hearts leap into their throats.

Shirone’s gaze dropped to her legs beneath her suit skirt. At the moment of impact, the stocking over her instep had scorched and curled away, exposing her right leg. For an instant, the muscles beneath her skin split and flared like cracked stone. Then the soft layer beneath the skin returned, and her leg became smooth again as if nothing had happened.

‘Who in the world is she?’

Shirone wondered if she was some kind of government official. If a foreign envoy had arrived in the kingdom, it made sense that the relevant departments would have anticipated the situation and taken measures.

“Whew.”

Kang Nan let out a small breath. Then she took her glasses from her inner pocket and put them back on.

After watching her for a moment, Shirone exchanged glances with his friends and approached.

“Excuse me, who are you? Are you perhaps from the kingdom—ugh!”

Kang Nan abruptly lifted the hem of her skirt, and Shirone’s face went bright red. Paying no attention to the awkward glances of the boys, she peeled off the ruined stocking and took out a fresh one.

As she changed her stockings, she said, “The enemy you were dealing with is a cerebral body code-named ‘The Lawyer,’ which inhabits the region beyond the northern limits of Heaven.”

“Heaven?”

Dante tilted his head. It occurred to him that there really were a lot of strange people in the world.

But Shirone and Amy could not dismiss her words. If she knew about Heaven, then this woman was no ordinary person.

“Strictly speaking, the Lawyer isn’t that dangerous,” Kang Nan continued. “It’s a simple phenomenon, like an aurora. But once its environment changes, it becomes troublesome. The golden time is seventy-two hours from the start of its manifestation. After that, it evolves exponentially. You dealt with it just in time.”

Kang Nan pulled the new stocking up to her waist, then smoothed down the folds of her skirt.

“Your judgment was sound, so I’ll overlook the fact that you acted on your own. If the Ruler of Law had expanded its range of activity, the army would have had to be deployed. You saved many lives.”

With that, Kang Nan turned, put her high heels back on, and straightened her clothes with a light kick of her foot.

“By the way,” Shirone asked, “can you tell me who you are?”

When Shirone pulled back his hood and stepped closer, Kang Nan’s eyes narrowed slightly as she confirmed his face.

‘He’s even younger in person. And he really defeated the law…’

Even in its initial stage, an awakened Ruler was classified as Phenomenon Grade B by Valkyrie. Since ordinary angels were Class A, that meant Shirone had fought an enemy comparable to a fallen angel.

‘But how did he break the law?’

The [Demonic Sword Armand], a gift from Princess Uorin, could have defended him against the law to some extent. But Kang Nan was certain that he could never have defeated the Lawyer with that alone.

‘Don’t tell me…’

There was only one possibility Kang Nan could think of.

‘Mental transcendence? No, that’s impossible. It isn’t something an eighteen-year-old can reach.’

According to Kang Nan, all living things eventually moved toward realizing incarnation. However, the time required was so long that most died without ever finding themselves within their allotted lifespan.

If there were exceptions, they belonged to Heaven.

There were humans who lived for hundreds of years, and plants that lived for hundreds of thousands.

That was why incarnation was called an art of Heaven.

The time required to reach enlightenment differed by creature, but on average, it took humans 220 years, cats 400 years, monkeys 600 years, snakes 700 years, and foxes 1,000 years.

In other words, if Shirone had truly reached the state of mental transcendence, he had leapt over roughly two hundred years of time.

‘It seems the Association’s evaluation wasn’t exaggerated.’

Kang Nan was not an idle person. She was one of the most trusted figures in the Association.

If the order had not come from her direct superior, Gaold, she would never have come to meet a magic school student merely for an interview.

Kang Nan recalled what had happened at the time.

“If it’s Shirone… are you talking about Arian Shirone from Alpheas Magic School?”

“Yes. The most troublesome junior from my alma mater. I don’t know why that school hasn’t gone under yet.”

Gaold grumbled, but Kang Nan saw through her superior’s intention and cut straight to the point.

“You’re not thinking of bringing him into this project, are you?”

As expected, Gaold did not answer.

“I’m against it,” Kang Nan said. “It’s too risky. This isn’t something we can decide based on Ataraxia alone. Are you planning to waste twenty years of preparation?”

Gaold, whose expression had grown serious without her noticing, finally opened his mouth.

“I hear he grows quickly.”

Kang Nan snorted. When it came to Shirone, no one in the Association knew more than she did.

“He is certainly one of the rising talents who may represent the kingdom someday,” she said. “He’s an Unlocker who uses Battery to give mass to light. If he steadily builds achievements, he’ll probably reach sixth grade by the time he’s twenty. Any organization would be short on excellent personnel like that.”

Kang Nan narrowed her eyes, watching Gaold’s reaction. When she saw that he understood her point and still remained silent, she continued.

“Are you serious? In the end, he’s sixth grade. Romi Etelado and Olifer Shiina, who are teaching Shirone, also received official sixth grade at the age of twenty. And even they weren’t the youngest. There are a handful of geniuses like that all over the kingdom. Unless you’re planning to wait another ten years, it’s right to give up on Shirone.”

“I don’t know about youngest or whatever,” Gaold said. “I wasn’t a genius either.”

He put a cigar in his mouth, leaned his neck against the backrest, and looked up at Kang Nan.

“But am I not the best right now?”

That was impossible to deny. The Gaold she knew had not been an exceptional student. Yet now, both in name and reality, he was the greatest wizard in the Kingdom of Tormia.

“That is an exceptional case—”

Gaold raised a hand to stop her.

“Enough. Just listen. It doesn’t matter whether someone becomes sixth grade at twenty or sixth grade at forty.”

“No. The reality is that many scouts measure achievement over time as a standard of talent. If that isn’t important, then what is?”

Holding the cigar between his fingers, Gaold exhaled a rough cloud of smoke and thought for a moment.

“In the end… what matters is what you do.”

Kang Nan tried to argue, then closed her mouth. Gaold was an absurd man, but there were times when he left even her speechless.

Gaold lifted his chin arrogantly. In truth, he was the only one who could call the last descendant of the wolf tribe a mutt.

“Listen, mutt. Now that I’ve climbed to the pinnacle of magic, I can tell you this. Being the best doesn’t mean being ranked number one. It means stepping completely outside the group called rankings. Of course, if quickly following a path someone else has already paved counts as talent, then that’s talent too. But look at the world. The people we call the best are the ones who shine a light into unexplored territory, where mankind has devoted its entire history and still found no answer. First place, second place—this isn’t about that level. There isn’t even anything to compare them against. That is an entirely different matter from following an existing path.”

Kang Nan turned Gaold’s words over in her mind several times.

“Do you think Shirone can become the best?”

“I can’t say for certain. However, his growth rate is fast enough to destroy common sense, and he can use Ataraxia. If those two things mesh at the right timing, under specific circumstances, he may be able to reach that state instantaneously. Then perhaps we can create at least one more variable.”

The project had been prepared for twenty years, but its difficulty still amounted to challenging the absolutely impossible. In that situation, even a single variable was worth obtaining at any cost.

But to Kang Nan, that argument was unacceptable. In a matter where the fate of the entire human race was at stake, they could not place groundless trust in an uncertain element.

“I understand what you’re saying. But you know that, don’t you? ‘The right timing,’ ‘specific circumstances,’ ‘instantaneously,’ ‘perhaps.’ Almost everything you just said is hypothetical.”

Gaold’s mouth curved upward, as if he had finally relaxed.

“I know. That’s why I’m telling you to meet him. See him with your own eyes. If we can raise the probability of success by even 0.1 percent, shouldn’t we use everything available to us?”

Kang Nan returned from her memory and spoke to Shirone.

“I am Ahoya Kang Nan, chief secretary of the Magic Association.”

“The chief secretary of the Magic Association?”

Shirone’s eyes widened. His friends, watching from behind him, could not hide their surprise either. The chief secretary was effectively the second-highest-ranking person in the Magic Association. Someone whom most wizards would never meet in their entire lives was standing right in front of them.

Shirone quickly bowed his head.

“Ah, hello. I’m—”

Kang Nan had no interest in formalities and got straight to the point.

“Shirone, this is a national emergency. There is someone who wants to meet you. Come with me to the capital.”

Shirone stared blankly at her.

She called it a national emergency, but it was strange that a mere student like him would be involved in such a situation.

“Who wants to meet me?”

Kang Nan replied without hesitation.

“The president of the Tormia Magic Association, Mikea Gaold.”

“Yes? Mikea Gaold?”

Shirone’s heart began to pound. It was the name he had heard more often than any other, despite never having met the man.

And it was not only Shirone. In the Kingdom of Tormia, there was no one connected to magic who did not know the name Mikea Gaold.

Dante asked with interest, “Why would someone that important be looking for Shirone?”

Shirone was just as curious. But at the same time, he had a guess.

“Is it related to the Ice Queen?”

“That’s right. The Ice Queen is a being of Heaven. Do you understand what it means for such an existence to descend into this world?”

Shirone’s eyes widened. When he heard that a being of Heaven had descended, the first thing that came to mind was the final war.

“Don’t tell me…”

“Yes. A crack appeared in Miro’s space-time.”

If the only barrier blocking the army of Heaven had cracked, then Kang Nan’s words about a national emergency made sense.

Lyria quietly stepped forward.

“Wait. By Miro’s space-time, do you mean the barrier of law mentioned in the prophetic books?”

Kang Nan looked Lyria up and down and asked, “A priestess?”

“Achaeans.”

“I see.”

Kang Nan nodded and continued her explanation.

“For now, it isn’t a serious problem. The crack was small, and it has already been restored. But a rift is always the beginning of collapse. Things cannot become more stable than they are now. We have to prepare for what comes next.”

“Then Miro is now…”

Kang Nan raised a hand, cutting Shirone off.

“This is all I can tell you. For the rest, hear it directly from my superior. Come with me to the capital.”

Shirone fell into deep thought, his expression grave.
