Chapter 403  
Senior Class Information Session (3)

~*~

After The Courage of Truth ended, an elderly teacher walked out from the graduating class building.

It was Collie, the head teacher of the senior class.

“Is the event over?”

It was rare for advanced-class students to see a senior-class teacher, but Collie was a familiar face to Class Three. When Alfeas had resigned from the principalship for six months because of the Arcane incident, Collie had served briefly as acting principal until Olivia took over.

His official rank was Grade 6, but he had been recognized for decades of achievement in the teaching profession. In terms of pure skill, he might not compare to younger teachers like Shiina, Etela, or Sade, but that was no reason to underestimate him.

In any organization, there were people who rose to executive positions at a young age, and there were also managers and department heads who reached such posts only later in life.

None of the students let their guard down. When it came to evaluating ability, Collie was likely superior to the younger teachers.

“Nice to meet you. My name is Collie, and I am the head teacher of the senior class.”

“Hello!”

The students of Class Two answered more energetically than Class Three had.

“From now on, we will hold the senior class information session. Class Three will follow me. Class Two, take a short break. I do not need to explain what will happen next, do I?”

The faces of the Class Two students turned pale. After watching Class Three go through it first, they understood perfectly. That, too, was the privilege of those who had suffered ahead of them.

Collie led Class Three to a meeting room on the first floor of the building.

The room was empty and a little cold.

Once the ten students had seated themselves around the long table, Collie stepped up to the podium at the far end.

“From this point on, there will be no time for you to learn anything at school.”

At that one sentence, all conversation died.

“Your basic education ended in the advanced class. The senior class exists only as a link between the school and society. What matters most here is whether your skills are effective in real situations.”

We have to get through this.

Everyone was thinking the same thing.

“You were born with talents different from ordinary people. For that reason, the school has not treated you as children who must be protected. This may sound inhumane, but if you die during class, fifty percent of the responsibility lies with you. If putting it in numbers feels too vague, I will say it plainly. In the event of your death, the school provides compensation, but it does not accept responsibility.”

They had heard something similar at the magic school entrance briefing. But Collie’s words were far more direct.

“And the senior year is different. From now on, you are one hundred percent responsible for your own lives. This place will no longer function as a school. As a cooperative institution of the Kingdom Magic Association, it will only determine whether you can be deployed immediately to the field as a meaningful force.”

Collie held up a card.

“What you see here is an evaluation teacher certificate issued by the Graduation Examination Registrar. Starting today, every evaluation I record will be converted into official data and submitted to the association. In addition, every organization under the World Red Line will have exclusive access to that information.”

An indescribable chill settled over the students. What they did in the advanced class would no longer remain merely as memories within the school.

“Now, I will explain the senior class schedule.”

Collie pulled a golden cord, and a curtain descended. On it was a schematic list of the graduation-year evaluation items.

“What is written here is the senior class schedule. It runs for a total of thirty weeks, and each week, six events are evaluated in rotation.”

Pandora raised her hand.

“May we take notes?”

“No. The evaluation on the first day is person-to-person combat. The thirty senior students will change opponents every week. A win is worth three points, a draw one point, and a loss zero points. In other words, over twenty-nine weeks, you will fight everyone once. There will be no person-to-person combat in the final, thirtieth week, as it may negatively affect the graduation exam.”

Boyle raised his hand.

“I have a question. You said a draw is worth one point. Can a match actually end in a draw?”

“The person whose mental strength gauge reaches zero loses. However, if both gauges reach zero at the same time, or if there is no winner within the ten-minute time limit, the match is treated as a draw, and both participants receive one point.”

As expected, there is a time limit.

There were also senior-class majors that were not specialized for combat, so Shirone had expected some adjustment in person-to-person battles.

If someone built a strategy around defense, it was possible to earn a point by holding out for ten minutes.

“The second-day evaluation is mental activity. It takes place at the Image Zone training ground. A geometric filter made of holograms will approach once every three seconds. You must transform your Spirit Zone so it passes through the filter without touching the hologram. The difficulty increases every week, and scores are awarded from five to zero depending on the number of successful attempts.”

Ugh. That sounds incredibly difficult.

Shirone had once rescued hostages in Baska’s Gold City by transforming his Spirit Zone. But his strength was durability, and he knew very well how difficult fine transformations could be.

Arin, on the other hand, looked relaxed. As a spirit-type mage, her mental fluidity was incomparable. For her, this event would not be especially difficult.

Knowing that, Canis leaned toward her and whispered, “You’ll have to get a perfect score on that.”

Arin nodded obediently.

“Day three, tactical strategy. This is a group evaluation. Six groups are formed according to rankings from first to thirtieth, with five members in each group. Each group will face simulated monsters at Training Ground 2000. The first group to complete the task earns five points, and the last group earns zero points.”

Even from that brief explanation, the students understood the severity of the situation.

There was a rule that guaranteed someone would always receive zero points.

And the responsibility was shared.

If students were divided according to rank, their overall levels would be similar. Even so, within the same group, blame would inevitably be assigned. Conflict between members was bound to arise.

Naturally, the group that received zero points would be the most likely to fall into that conflict.

“Day four, strengthening the main skill. Twenty evaluation items are prepared according to major. Each item is divided into ten levels, and one point is awarded per level. For reference, the tenth level of the ‘heat amount’ item in the flame series is one thousand degrees Celsius.”

Master difficulty is one thousand degrees Celsius?

That was not a student-level difficulty.

Of course, Amy had reached Thousand City during vacation, but that was thanks to the ability of her Red Eyes to remove entropy.

So it really is a professional-level achievement.

“Duplicate acquisition is not possible for strengthening the main skill. When you reach a certain level, the score from the previous level is erased and replaced. You will register your major during tomorrow’s personal interview, so choose carefully.”

I see…

If someone passed Master difficulty and obtained ten points, they could not gain any more points from that item. However, because they could adjust their score deliberately and earn high points even early on, this event would become a major variable in the competition.

“Day five, cognition and judgment. The location is the Virtual Zone. You should all have experienced it during the entrance ceremony test.”

Shirone had also been tested in the Virtual Zone. He had counted balls and answered questions about sensations like cold and heat.

“But the senior year is on a different level. You will experience extreme conditions. The key is how long you survive, depending on your judgment. This, too, is scored from five to zero according to level.”

Shirone rubbed his face. The other students, who had passed through the steel gate with such confidence, also looked grim.

“Day six, Omniscience Implementation. This is also done in the Virtual Zone. For example, if you are placed in an environment of minus twenty-seven degrees Celsius and given the task of raising the temperature to three degrees Celsius, you must solve it using one of the thirty-six types of magic designated by the association.”

Omniscience Implementation was also difficult for Shirone.

Of course, he had scored over eighty points in every subject on the theory test. But so had the other students. He could not call that a competitive advantage.

“These are the six evaluation items that will be completed in one week. You will earn points by repeating this rotation for thirty weeks, and the rankings will be rearranged daily according to overall score.”

Thud! Thud!

As soon as Collie finished speaking, several students dropped their foreheads onto the table.

Shirone also folded his arms and lowered his head.

He just wanted to sleep.

Is this really only the first week’s evaluation? Amy, how did you survive a year here? No, how did you even decide to do this again?

Collie smiled.

“If you want to rest, rest. You are semi-professionals now. But if this is already too much for you, you will not be able to graduate.”

The students slowly lifted their upper bodies again.

Fine. Let’s hear what else there is.

That was the look in their eyes.

“There is also something called an overall evaluation. It is held on the final holiday of every four-week period. The items are almost identical to the graduation exam: person-to-person combat, occupying the hill, management operation, special move, survival, and tactical strategy.”

It was essentially a mock exam for the graduation exam.

“For the overall evaluation, you may form groups freely. You can team up with whomever you want. If you win the overall evaluation, you gain twenty points. Conversely, the loser loses twenty points.”

The students stirred at the enormous point value.

If one side gained twenty points and their opponent lost twenty, the gap between them would widen by forty points.

This is… too cruel.

Amy had not been exaggerating when she said, “Welcome to hell.”

“Assuming perfect scores in all subjects through the thirtieth week, the highest score an individual can obtain is eighty-seven points in person-to-person combat, one hundred fifty points in mental activity, one hundred fifty points in tactical strategy, one hundred fifty points in cognition and judgment, two hundred points in strengthening the main skill, and three hundred points in Omniscience Implementation, for a total of one thousand thirty-seven points.”

Numbers began spinning through the students’ heads.

“In addition, if you win all six overall evaluations, another one hundred twenty points are added, making the perfect score one thousand one hundred fifty-seven points. According to statistics, the average score needed for graduation is one thousand points.”

“One thousand points…”

Shirone’s mouth fell open. That meant they had to achieve 86.4 percent of the total possible score.

“Because evaluations are conducted every day except holidays, the rankings will also change every day according to score. At first, the difficulty is low, so there will not be much fluctuation. But as the level rises, it is common for rankings to leap up and down. This is the one-year journey you are about to undertake.”

A solemn silence filled the meeting room.

One thousand points? This is worrying. It really won’t be easy.

It was not simply a matter of fighting well or possessing extraordinary talent.

Everything a wizard needed would be evaluated.

“Then I will tell you the most important thing last.”

The students’ eyes returned to Collie.

“In two days, the graduating class schedule will begin, and evaluations will be conducted for thirty weeks. And you…”

Collie set down his baton and clasped his hands behind his back.

“…have the right to refuse all of these evaluations.”

For a moment, the meeting room seemed to buzz with the sound of their hearts.

“You mean we can give up on being evaluated?”

“Yes. Strictly speaking, the scores obtained through evaluations do not affect whether you pass or fail the graduation exam. Even if you are not evaluated, you graduate as long as you pass the test.”

Sabina asked, “Are there any restrictions if we refuse the evaluations?”

“There are no specific restrictions, but students who refuse evaluation are barred from entering the training grounds.”

“Hmm… So they cannot spectate.”

They would be unable to see their opponents’ abilities with their own eyes.

However, cliques had formed even during the previous graduation exam. As long as one member of a group participated in the evaluations, the others could use that information to devise strategies. It was not a severe restriction.

Nade asked, “Then what are the advantages of being evaluated?”

“You seem to misunderstand something. I said you are semi-professionals. This is not homework. There are no advantages or disadvantages in the school’s eyes. The decision is yours.”

Collie looked over the room from the podium.

“On the day of the graduation exam, scouts from Red Line associations around the world are dispatched. Since this is one of the five prestigious schools, you should assume that scouts from dozens of countries will be watching.”

The students’ eyes shone intensely.

“Under Red Line regulations, the school is obligated to provide your profiles. From a scout’s perspective, evaluation scores add depth to their judgment. But if your scores are a mess, they will become poison. That is the nature of this issue. It is a choice about how each of you will live your life, not about the school.”

I see. From now on, graduation isn’t everything.

The Red Line operated a trade system to prevent affiliated wizards from leaving the line.

Of course, the association held the first negotiation rights. But if negotiations broke down, another organization could pay the transfer fee and take the wizard.

What major field did they want to work in? What did they ultimately want to accomplish in life?

From the moment they entered the senior year, their lives as wizards had already begun.

“This concludes the presentation. There will be personal interviews tomorrow, so think carefully about your major today.”

Collie left the conference room, and everyone stood up. The door closed behind him. The students looked at one another, but no one said a word.

The competition had begun the moment they learned the rules of the senior class.

After a while, Dante’s group left first, followed by Boyle and Pandora, then Arin and Canis.

Shirone and the others, unfortunately, remained until the end, unable to find any reason to leave the conference room.

Shirone’s legs gave out, and he collapsed into a chair.

“This is really serious. I never imagined it would be this hard.”

“Yeah. We won’t be able to do anything except evaluations. How are we supposed to run the study group like this?”

Shirone looked back at Nade.

“You don’t have anything to do anyway, do you?”

Iruki said, “He said we have the right to give up the evaluations. But no one will actually give them up. Nobody enters the senior year planning to get a low score.”

“But if we focus too much on scores, there’s a risk that our abilities will be exposed. Then the chance of being checked or eliminated in the graduation exam goes up.”

The obvious example was Amy. Until the final week of the thirtieth week, she had been ranked fifth in the graduating class. But during the exam, she had been heavily checked and eliminated early.

Regardless of Fermi’s collusion, the fact that she was a top graduation candidate must have influenced how easily the students were incited against her.

Nade said, “Hmm. In the end, is it about evaluation scores or the graduation test? Process or result? That means we have to judge carefully.”

“If the scouts are going to check the scores, I want to do my best. But even then, it’s meaningless if I don’t pass the graduation exam.”

Iruki added, “The mind games with the other students are going to be no joke.”

Shirone rested his chin on his hand and twisted his lips.

“Still, I think it would be good to stay in the top ten. I don’t want to go into the exam room with low confidence.”

Nade said, “Person-to-person combat has the lowest score value, but it creates the clearest difference. It’ll be important not to lose points there.”

“We’ll also have to keep an eye on tactical strategy. If your rank drops, you’ll end up in a lower group. The score difference can be as much as five points. It’s bleak when you think about that accumulating over thirty weeks.”

Iruki said, “We can adjust our rankings to some extent through strengthening the main skill. But it’s dangerous to go too far from the beginning. It would be best to adjust while watching the other students’ scores.”

Once their opinions were gathered, a rough outline began to form. It was a long journey of one year, and they needed to decide their course of action as carefully as possible.

“For now, let’s wait until tomorrow’s meeting. We’ll think about it after we see the person-to-person combat schedule and the items for strengthening the main skill.”
