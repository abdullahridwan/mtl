Chapter 383  
Magic Association (1)  
~*~

After leaving Reina’s mansion, Shirone caught a carriage to the Magic Association.

Because Lian had withdrawn from the knight training program, Shirone’s schedule had been moved up sooner than expected. He could have stayed at Reina’s mansion for another day, but he chose to go straight to the Association.

The fire Lian had left behind seemed to have kindled something in everyone’s hearts. Tess had gone home, saying she would begin training immediately, and Shirone, too, wanted to immerse himself in the atmosphere of the Magic Association, the heart of the world of magic.

When he arrived at the Association building, Shirone asked for Fleur at the information desk. The receptionist made a call through the internal line, and five minutes later, Fleur came down.

The moment she saw Shirone, she frowned. Kang Nan happened to be away from her post, so there was no one around to intervene.

“Why are you back already? You acted so busy, saying you had to handle everything yourself.”

“The work ended earlier than expected. May I stay here?”

Fleur stared at Shirone without blinking. He did not even look embarrassed as he asked her to give him a place to sleep.

For all its grand name, the Magic Association was not an inn for children with nowhere to go. However, since Kang Nan had personally instructed her to take care of him, Fleur could not refuse outright. With too many eyes on them in the lobby, she took Shirone up to the third floor, the restricted area.

“The chief secretary specifically asked me to look after you, so from now on, you will follow my instructions. If you disobey me or wander around on your own, I’ll throw you out. Understood?”

“Ah, yes.”

Fleur’s harsh attitude puzzled Shirone from the start. He had done nothing to offend her, yet she had not looked kindly on him even when they had first met.

“All right. You can stay here.”

Fleur led him to a small lodging room used by staff who worked overtime. It was a square room with bunk beds pushed against both walls.

Shirone cautiously unpacked his luggage. Before he even had time to change clothes, however, Fleur called out to him again.

“Follow me. I’ll show you around.”

Fleur held her magic wand behind her back and strode down the corridor with her back straight and her chin lifted proudly.

“First, let me introduce myself. I am Fleur Rabid. Twenty-two years old. I graduated at the top of my class from the Royal Magic School and am currently a certified 8th-class wizard. Of course, within two years, I will be 6th class. My current position is third-floor manager of the Security Department of the Magic Association.”

“Oh, I see. I’m—”

Just as Shirone was about to introduce himself, she stopped and turned to him coldly.

“I know. Arian Shirone. Student at Alpheas Magic School. Capable of using Ataraxia, the power of the archangel, and specializing in light magic. An Unlocker whose unique ability grants mass.”

Shirone was dumbfounded. She had not even known his face the day before, yet in one day she had learned all of that about him. The strangest thing was still her openly hostile attitude.

“There’s a student named Dante at your school, isn’t there? He transferred there not long ago.”

“Yes. Do you know Dante?”

“Why would I know Dante?”

“Ah, I just meant that he went to the Royal Magic School too, and you—”

Seeing Fleur’s sharp gaze, Shirone naturally shrank back. He had no idea what he was supposed to say to please her.

“When I was in my senior year, Dante was a junior far below me. I know you defeated him. But if you’re going to let something like that make your shoulders stiff with pride, you’d better stop now. If I had been only three years younger, the number one in the kingdom would have been me, not you.”

‘I’ve never been proud about it…’

Shirone swallowed the words that rose to his throat. Whatever he said, he had the feeling he would not receive a favorable response.

Fleur patrolled the third floor while explaining the departments whenever she had the chance. Even guards with gray in their hair bowed their heads when they saw her. Each time, she acknowledged them with only a brief, dignified nod.

Fleur was trying to show him what a successful wizard looked like. In the end, what she called a tour was really a process of disciplining Shirone by making him recognize her position.

Just then, a sweaty, disheveled man appeared at the end of the corridor with an armful of documents. For a brief moment, tension flashed across Fleur’s confident face.

“Hello, Mr. Sakiri. Good morning.”

Shirone looked closely at the face of the man called Sakiri.

‘So that person is Sakiri.’

After the Arcane incident had ended, Alpheas and Canis had been summoned to the Association, and the investigator in charge of them had been named Sakiri.

He looked like a tired middle-aged man, but his eyes were sharper than those of anyone Shirone had ever met.

“Yes. But who is that beside you? I don’t think I’ve seen him before.”

“Oh, he’s here for a field trip. It’s part of a special activity.”

Fleur covered for Shirone with a lie.

Since the Magic Association was a core institution of the Kingdom’s wizard society, countless students applied to tour it. Perhaps because of that, Sakiri simply nodded and walked away.

Fleur let out a small breath, then glanced at Shirone. The tense atmosphere she had maintained until now wavered, and for a moment an awkward silence hung between them.

“That was Mr. Sakiri, a certified 5th-class wizard. He’s a famous figure in the Inspection Department. No criminal he catches gets away intact.”

“Ah, yes. I’ve heard of him.”

“You have? Hmm.”

For the first time, Fleur smiled with satisfaction. The fact that she worked alongside someone even an ordinary magic school student knew seemed to restore some of the pride Shirone had bruised earlier.

‘I think I’m starting to understand how I should deal with her.’

As Shirone was thinking that, a man stepped off the platform at the back of the corridor.

When he saw Fleur and approached, his eyes lit with interest as soon as they landed on Shirone.

“Fleur, is that Shirone?”

Fleur’s expression brightened immediately.

“Ah, Mr. Gando. Hello.”

The man’s name was Klein Gando. Like Fleur, he was a member of the Guard.

A battle wizard skilled in electric magic, he was known by the nickname Gando of Thunder. His title was Guard Captain of the Magic Association, and his rank was certified 4th class.

He was tall and wore tight leather pants and studded boots. His purple hair was swept back, with only a single strand falling loose. He looked like he must have been quite popular when he was younger, and even now he had handsome, almost angelic features.

Fleur led Shirone over to Gando.

“The chief secretary asked me to look after him. Hey, say hello.”

Gando extended his hand first.

“I see. Nice to meet you. I’m Gando.”

“Ah? Hello.”

Shirone had been about to bow, but when Gando offered his hand, he hurriedly shook it instead. Since he ended up bowing and shaking hands at the same time, Gando smiled at the awkward posture.

“You’re much younger than I expected. I thought you’d have a more intimidating look.”

‘Is that how people imagine me?’

It was something Shirone, who had rarely left Tormia, had never known. But when he thought about what had been published in academic journals, he supposed such a misunderstanding could happen.

“Are you on regular patrol?” Fleur asked.

“Yes. I’m making my way down from the top floor. How is the third floor? Everything all right?”

“Yes. As always. Ho-ho-ho!”

Fleur covered her mouth and laughed, full of pride as the manager of the third floor.

“Good. Kang Nan seems to have gone after receiving a call from the president of the Association. She’ll probably return with him by tomorrow or so. Keep that in mind and relax.”

“Thank you.”

Seeing Gando’s kind impression and consideration, Shirone desperately wanted to tell him, “This older sister is bullying me.” But knowing it would only hurt him in the end, he kept it to himself.

When Gando headed back toward the elevator, Fleur’s smiling expression changed in an instant. She returned to her sharp demeanor and began lecturing Shirone again.

“He is the one who oversees security for the entire Magic Association. In other words, he is my direct superior. Gando of Thunder. You’ve heard the name, haven’t you?”

Since Shirone had known Sakiri, she assumed he would naturally know Gando as well.

“Ah, yes. I’ve heard of him.”

In truth, it was the first time Shirone had ever heard the name. If the major incident at Alpheas Magic School had not happened, he would not even have known Sakiri’s name. But in this situation, it seemed better to say he knew regardless.

Fleur seemed satisfied with Shirone’s obedient answer.

Thinking she had disciplined him enough, she slowly brought up the subject she had been keeping in mind.

“I know why the president of the Association wants to meet you. It has something to do with Heaven, doesn’t it?”

“…”

Shirone’s expression grew serious, and he kept his mouth shut. Even if Kang Nan trusted Fleur enough to entrust him to her, he still had to maintain confidentiality.

When his gaze sharpened, even Fleur could not treat him carelessly this time. She had already sensed he would not be easy to handle, but realizing that his meek behavior until now might have been restraint rather than weakness irritated her.

“In any case, my role is to assist the president of the Association. I will definitely go to Heaven. So don’t get carried away just because you were invited.”

Shirone had never been conceited, nor had he even thought of being so. But with those words, he could roughly guess where Fleur’s hostility toward him came from.

“You seem to respect the president of the Association.”

Fleur’s expression stirred like a fish freshly pulled from the sea.

“Of course! Mr. Sakiri and Mr. Gando, whom you just met, are great people. But to be honest, compared to Mikea Gaold, they’re nothing.”

“I suppose so. He is a certified 1st-class archmage.”

“Ho-ho-ho!”

Fleur laughed mockingly, almost to the point of tears.

“You really don’t know anything. Listen carefully. At present, there are four 1st-class archmages in the Kingdom of Tormia. However, the position of president of the Magic Association is not one you can hold simply because you are strong. It requires dealing with high-ranking officials, so political ability and competence are necessary too. Then why was Lord Gaold chosen as president of the Association?”

Shirone thought about it. However, because he could not even grasp what level a 1st-class archmage truly stood on, no answer came to him right away.

“Well… because he is capable in many ways?”

Fleur shook her head.

“No. Rather, the opposite. It’s not that he is merely strong. He is too strong.”

“Too… strong?”

“Yes. Because he possesses overwhelming strength that sets him apart even from other 1st-class archmages, he can keep his position as president of the Association despite carrying the stigma of being a madman. Power comes first! As a battle mage, he is my idol and my ideal. So how could I not respect him?”

Hearing Fleur speak, Shirone realized Gaold was a far greater person than he had vaguely imagined. The more he understood that, the more he wondered why such a person wanted to meet him.

Fleur turned around as if she had said all she needed to say.

“Anyway, I have work to do, so go back to your room until dinner. The tour is over, so don’t wander around without my permission.”

“Ah! Um…”

When Shirone called out urgently, Fleur turned back to look at him.

“Before I go in… could we take the elevator just once?”

Fleur looked at Shirone and smiled. But soon her expression returned to its usual coldness, and she answered curtly with a twist of her lips.

“No.”

Shirone stared blankly after her as she walked away.

“What did I do wrong?”

* * *

The next morning dawned bright after a boring day that had been no different from prison life. Shirone overslept. It was not as if he could take a single step outside until Fleur came to work anyway.

As he kept his eyes closed a little longer, he heard a commotion outside. While he was still half-asleep, Fleur burst through the door and shouted.

“Hey, newcomer! Are you still sleeping?”

Shirone opened his eyes and sat up.

“Oh, hello…”

“You’re completely out of it! Do you think this is your bedroom? Get up right now!”

By now, Shirone was starting to get annoyed.

“What am I supposed to do when I wake up?”

“What do you think? Did you think you could eat and sleep here for free? Today, you’re following me and assisting my work. I’ll give you ten minutes, so wash up quickly and come out!”

Shirone felt as if a bomb had gone off while he was sleeping. Faced with a morning he could barely process, his upper body slowly tilted to the side, and he collapsed back onto the bed.

But in the end, he could not hold out for even ten seconds. He curled up, groaned, and dragged himself out of bed.

“Ugh, this is too much.”
