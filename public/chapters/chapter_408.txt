Entering the Competition System (3)  
~*~

Iruki landed in the training ground. Then, with a grin on his face, he walked toward Binder.

“Kuk!”

Even when Binder retreated, Iruki showed no sign of impatience. For Binder, it was already over.

“Your Battery is certainly clean. It was like reading a textbook.”

Cancellation analyzed changes in the opponent’s Spirit Zone and returned every vector quantity in reverse order.

The effect of completely resetting an opponent’s magic was enormous, but the humiliation it inflicted could not be ignored either.

“Don’t be ridiculous! My Battery is perfect!”

As if unable to accept defeat, Binder cast the spell again. But before he could even properly activate it, his magic was initialized.

“I know. Not just clean—perfect.”

Binder’s upper lip curled like a gorilla’s. Then, all of a sudden, his gaze dropped, his expression turning bewildered.

A detonation reaction was taking place beneath him.

‘Damn it!’

“It’s fine. It’s only 10 kilobusters.”

The explosion went off the moment Iruki turned away.

Under the two-thousand-times system, Binder did not go flying, since there was no “jungle” judgment in a person-to-person battle. However, his mental strength gauge was driven all the way down to zero.

He could have endured it if he had cast defensive magic, but in front of Cancellation, there was nothing he could do.

Iruki came down from the training ground, flashing a V-sign in victory.

At one time, he might have been booed for being disrespectful to a defeated opponent, but no one in the senior year laughed at him now.

“From the very first day… Cancellation.”

Their expressions were serious. Iruki had revealed the strongest weapon among all the magic he could use.

‘Is he confident he’ll never lose? Or does he have some other magic prepared?’

Because he had so boldly shown his special move, everyone’s thoughts grew complicated.

“Hmm…”

Fermi watched the battle with his hands in his pockets. Then he smiled and asked the student beside him.

“How long?”

“Two minutes and eleven seconds.”

“Two minutes and eleven seconds to put the broken glass back together.”

No matter how textbook Binder’s magic was, analyzing a graduating class student’s Spirit Zone in under three minutes was impossible unless one had a Servant’s brain.

‘If the graduation exam includes person-to-person battles, that’s going to be rather tricky.’

Business calculations began running rapidly through Fermi’s mind.

* * *

The dormitory door closed with a thud.

After eleven at night, Shirone returned to his room and collapsed face-first onto his bed with a groan.

“Oh, this is hard. It’s too hard.”

The graduating class schedule was already in its fourth week.

Except under special circumstances, most evaluations ended around lunchtime, but even so, there was no time to rest.

Senior year was, quite literally, a wilderness.

No one taught you anything. If you wanted something, you had to learn it yourself.

After evaluations, Shirone and his friends continued training at the training ground all day.

He was still climbing in person-to-person combat, and the cognition and judgment evaluations were also favorable to him.

Even when faced with terrifying situations, his mental endurance did not waver. With judgment based on his excellent insight, he accurately found the path that led to survival.

However, the other students also possessed professional-level skill in at least one field.

Binder, who was aiming for Dragon Lightning, received a perfect score in Battery implementation, while Arin, Joner, and Dorothy drew attention in mental activity.

Students with unique majors, like Fisho, maintained their rankings by smoothly gaining points in strengthening their primary skills.

As a result, even though the six events had already rotated four times, there had still been little meaningful change in the rankings.

“It’s a lot harder than I thought.”

Considering the thirty-week schedule, this was still only the early stage of the race.

But the fatigue Shirone felt was beyond imagination.

Every day, he had to take an evaluation where losing even a single point could drop his ranking. The tension from that carried straight into his personal training in the afternoon, and by the time he entered the dormitory, he was completely exhausted.

“I want to sleep. I want to sleep. I just want to sleep for one hour.”

Contrary to his muttering, Shirone pushed himself up from the bed.

The other students might have gone to sleep by now, but he had another task.

He had to fulfill Gaold’s conditions.

The reason he subjected himself to such brutal training every day was not only to raise his rank, but also to avoid dying like a dog if he was suddenly dragged to Heaven.

Become strong.

And develop it.

Magic powerful enough to destroy Heaven.

“Me?”

Was Gaold really talking about him, someone who was still struggling just to survive the senior year evaluations?

“Haha!”

Shirone burst out laughing, then sat at his desk and opened his notebook.

It was filled with the ideas he had come up with over the past month.

However, none of them reached the heart of the problem. They were still nothing more than scribbles.

‘Let’s approach it conceptually today. Magic that destroys Heaven. What would that be?’

For now, it had no choice but to be long-distance magic.

If he cast wide-area destructive magic at close range, the wizard himself would die in the aftermath.

‘No, depending on the situation, maybe I have to consider that too…’

At the end of his notes, Shirone wrote, “Possibility of caster death.”

‘I can die. If I accept that, will more options open up?’

As he pondered, Shirone clutched his head.

“Ugh, I really don’t know.”

The key was using Ataraxia’s amplifying power.

Only that much was certain.

However, with a Photon Cannon that contained mass, reaching the subluminal speed range of the light system was extremely difficult.

Someday, it might exceed the speed of sound, but even then, it would not be powerful enough to blow Heaven apart.

“I’m just running in circles.”

Shirone wrote “Ataraxia,” then added “Trigger” beside it.

Ataraxia’s strength was, of course, its tremendous amplification, but when he thought about it, it had other strengths as well.

First, it was especially threatening to heavenly beings.

Humans with sensitive senses were also shocked by Halo, but Maras, born from the concept of angels, felt instinctive fear.

“Well, maybe it can delay them.”

Shirone put parentheses around Ataraxia and wrote, “Delay of about one to two seconds.”

“The most important thing is that it’s a trigger.”

Magic that passed through Ataraxia was no longer controlled by the magician’s Spirit Zone, but by Ataraxia itself.

That was why Shirone defined Ataraxia as siege magic.

“Amplification. Delay. Trigger.”

These were the three assets Ataraxia currently possessed.

“Then the magic I can use is…”

Shirone wrote them down one by one.

For offensive magic, there were Photon Cannon, Homing Photon Cannon, and Laser.

For defensive magic, there were Wide Range and Dark Ball.

For control techniques, there were Shining Impact, Shining Chain, and Slow.

For movement techniques, there were Teleportation, Space Movement, Mass Teleport, Shot Movement, and, in addition, the movement techniques using Optical Wing.

“That’s a lot. I’ve worked pretty hard, haven’t I?”

For a moment, pride swelled in his chest. Then his expression darkened.

No matter how diverse his magic was, none of it had the power to blow away the whole kingdom of Heaven.

No, from the very beginning, it was impossible.

How could he, who was not even a formal wizard yet, destroy Heaven?

“No! This won’t work, that won’t work—nothing works!”

Shirone drew a large X over his notebook and slammed his face onto the desk.

All he had gained from a month of study was a feeling that tickled at his brain.

Even if he learned new magic, there would be a limit to what a single series could achieve within one year.

‘Amplification. Delay. Trigger. Timeshare. Force. Fusion. And all the magic I’ve learned so far.’

He had to graft all of them together and create something completely new.

He could be wrong. But at the same time, a strange feeling that this might be the right answer flowed along the path of his insight.

‘Let’s find it. Let’s find it. Let’s find a way.’

Repeating the only thought left in his head, Shirone fell asleep.
