Chapter 378  
Swordsman’s Belief (2)  
~*~

Clump sat in the seat of honor with his arms crossed, his expression grave. Beside him was Quan, who had rushed over from the swordsmanship academy after receiving Clump’s summons.

Lian stood before them, straight-backed. He met Clump’s eyes without flinching and said, “This is something I’ve decided for myself. I know it’s strange to ask permission after making up my mind, but I ask you to accept it.”

Clump let out a deep sigh.

Those of the blue-haired bloodline were free-spirited and hot-blooded by nature. He knew better than anyone how hard Lian had worked, and he understood the frustration Lian felt over being unable to use schema. But it had only been a year. It was far too soon to decide whether the academy had been a success or a failure for him.

“…Are you running away?” Clump asked. “What do you plan to do after dropping out? Return home and train in private?”

“No. I’m leaving to train as a knight.”

Clump’s beard twitched as he clenched his teeth. Anger and, strangely, a trace of joy welled up together. How could the boy think exactly as he himself had when he was young?

Even so, he had to oppose him.

When Clump was young, there had been many knights who set out on training journeys. The world had been chaotic then, and the dream of unifying the world with a single sword had not been entirely impossible.

But times had changed.

A sword was a skill meant to kill people. In that sense, there was probably no better training than experiencing real combat. But it was also a double-edged sword. One mistake could end your life.

“I won’t call you a child,” Clump said. “You’ll be nineteen soon. But can you truly say there’s nothing more for you to learn at the academy? Is school really so worthless to you?”

“I’ve never thought that. I just want to become stronger.”

This time, anger flashed clearly across Clump’s face.

Being pleased that his grandson resembled him and allowing that grandson to blindly follow the path of their ancestors were two entirely different things. Above all, he could not push his beloved grandson into danger with his own hands.

“You want to become stronger, so you’re abandoning your training? Do you think that kind of foolishness will work after you’ve come this far?”

“This isn’t foolishness! I’m sincerely prepared to risk my life—!”

Clump cut him off firmly.

“If that’s all it is, then no. You can gain real experience anytime. What comes first is building enough skill to survive when you step out into the world.”

“No. I’ve already submitted my withdrawal request. My decision has been made.”

“I canceled it. This old man in his seventies lowered his head to your junior here and begged him to take it back.”

“Grandfather!”

Lian raised his voice.

It was one thing for his withdrawal request to be rejected. But the thought of Clump humiliating himself for his sake was unbearable.

Just as Lian was about to speak, emotions burning hot in his chest, a knock came at the door.

Clump frowned in irritation.

“What is it? We’re in the middle of something. Come back later.”

“Elder, Shirone has come to visit.”

Lian turned toward the door in surprise. His heart suddenly began to pound.

Shirone was his best friend and the lord to whom he had sworn his knightly oath. Whatever the reason, Shirone was the one person Lian had never wanted to see him like this.

Clump knew exactly what Shirone meant to Lian. He stroked his beard for a moment, lost in thought, then made his decision.

“Let him in.”

Lian was stubborn and strong-willed; his family’s words barely reached him. But Shirone might be able to change the situation.

The door opened, and Shirone entered. Tess poked her head in to gauge the atmosphere inside, but Louis promptly shut the door before she could step in.

Without realizing it, Lian avoided Shirone’s gaze. Shirone did not press him. Instead, he greeted Clump first.

“Hello, Elder.”

“Yes. I heard everything from Rayna. You went through a great deal.”

Clump already knew what had happened in Kazura. After the ordeal he had endured, Shirone’s presence had grown noticeably stronger.

But at the moment, Clump had no room to focus on that change. Shirone knew why Clump had called him here, and he wasted no time getting to the point.

“Lian is dropping out of school.”

Clump scratched his head with an exaggeratedly troubled expression.

“Yes. See if you can talk some sense into him. Can you believe this foolishness?”

“I’m in favor.”

Clump’s hand stopped mid-scratch. Not only Lian, but even Quan, who did not know Shirone, looked at him in surprise.

“In favor?” Clump asked. “You mean you think Lian should drop out?”

“Yes. I don’t know the circumstances, and I haven’t heard his reasons. But if Lian made that decision, then I believe it is the right path for him.”

Clump struggled to keep his composure.

He had hoped Shirone’s appearance would become a turning point, but if Shirone intended to act as Lian’s advocate, then this could not be more troublesome. He was going to be a very difficult advocate indeed.

“Yes,” Clump said. “As Lian’s lord, your opinion is worth considering. But if that’s what you think, then surely you can give me a proper reason?”

Shirone turned to Lian. Lian quietly lowered his gaze, as if he still could not bear to face him. Shirone looked back at Clump and continued.

“There is no proper reason.”

“What?”

“I don’t know which choice would be more efficient for Lian. But there is one thing I can guarantee.”

Shirone spoke with certainty.

“The Lian I know is not someone who runs away, no matter the circumstances.”

Lian clenched his fists. Those words gave him more strength than a thousand arguments.

“It may be a foolish decision,” Shirone continued. “But he would never make it to avoid something. If that’s true, then I believe it’s our duty as his friends to support him.”

Clump burst into laughter, the feeling of defeat tickling deep in his chest.

It was a beautiful friendship. And it was human nature to want to protect beautiful things.

‘You wicked brat. You already know exactly what I’m weak to.’

This was different from dealing with Bischoff, the head of the household. If Shirone came at him like this, Clump, a man who valued loyalty above all else, could not simply crush his argument.

But as Shirone already knew, Clump was not a man who handled matters on emotion alone.

“I understand how you feel,” Clump said. “And it is persuasive. But Shirone, that is only the justice between you and Lian. You still don’t know the world. I have lived as a swordsman for decades. Do you think school is nothing more than school? You’re mistaken. One day, today’s dropout record will grab Lian by the ankle.”

Quan raised a hand.

“I have something to say on that matter.”

Everyone’s eyes turned to him.

It was Shirone’s first time seeing Quan, but the aura around him was enough to tell him that he was no ordinary person.

“To be honest,” Quan said, “as a teacher, I did not come here simply because the general summoned me for a conversation. I felt I had to deliver this to you.”

Quan took an envelope from his inner pocket.

It was Lian’s withdrawal request.

“I respect the general and wish to help him. However, withdrawing the resignation is unacceptable. Teaching a student who has already given up on the academy goes against my educational philosophy.”

“Grunt.”

For Clump, it was one obstacle after another.

Quan’s stubbornness was well known even within the swordsmanship academy. Even if his opponent was a general and a certified third-class swordsman, that did not change.

“Can’t you do this old man one favor?” Clump asked. “Just this once?”

‘Grandfather…’

Lian felt as if he might suffocate. Seeing Clump, a general, lower himself and plead with a much younger man for his grandson’s sake tore at his chest.

But Quan was truly not an easy opponent.

“This may be irresponsible for a teacher to say, but the truth is that Lian has no future at the academy. Of course, there are still things he can learn in terms of technique. However, the evaluation items are ultimately based on schema. There is an opinion that brain enhancement also falls under the category of schema, but even so, it is not something that can be taught in school. As you know, education and practical training are very different. Practical training and real combat are also completely different systems. A wrestling match is not a sword fight. Even if a cadet wins in wrestling, you cannot count that as a swordsmanship score.”

Quan’s words did not sound especially harsh. As the person who had watched Lian most closely at the academy, he was simply giving his honest opinion.

Silence settled over the room. No one spoke.

Then Louis, the butler, spoke again from outside the door.

“Elder, the second young master has arrived.”

Lian’s face hardened.

Ogent Rai.

Of all times, his lifelong rival had come home now. In a different way from Shirone, Rai was also someone Lian did not want to see.

When Rai entered with his usual confident stride, Shirone hurriedly stepped aside. He had seen Rai only once, back when he worked as a librarian for the Ogent family, but the impression had remained vivid in his mind.

Black hair. A cold face. That cool atmosphere had not changed, but his aura had grown far stronger than before, strong enough to be felt on the skin.

When Rayna followed him in, Louis could not close the door this time.

She came up behind Rai, glaring sharply, and shouted, “Hey! What have you been so busy with that you couldn’t even show your face? Did you know I’ve been home for a month?”

Even the cold-hearted Rai frowned at his sister’s nagging.

“I was busy. It couldn’t be helped. I’m being dispatched soon, so I came to pack my things. I’m hungry, so make me something to eat.”

Rayna glared fiercely at the back of Rai’s head, then whispered to Louis to prepare a meal. He was still her younger brother, home after a long absence. At the very least, she had to feed him something warm.

Rai approached Clump and bowed at a proper angle. Now that he, too, was a soldier, he could not treat Clump carelessly even at home.

“Grandfather.”

“Hmm. You seem to be doing well.” Clump then gestured to the man beside him. “Ah, say hello. This is Quan, a teacher at the swordsmanship academy.”

Rai’s eyes changed.

‘Quan? Quan, the Magician of Death?’

It was a story he had heard after passing the official exam and enlisting in the military. Of course, it was unconfirmed, but the name of the only swordsman who had faced Fengzhang and returned alive was definitely Quan.

Rai turned to Quan and bowed.

“Hello. My name is Ogent Rai.”

“Yes, I’ve heard about you,” Quan said. “You have a fine presence, just as the rumors say.”

Quan returned the greeting with as much courtesy as possible. His attitude was different from when he dealt with Lian.

‘That boy. The genius swordsman of the Ogent family.’

Rai had received official tenth class only last year, yet he had already become an eighth-class sergeant. His aura was cold and sharp, as if Quan were seeing himself in his youth.

Rai stood at attention until Quan’s brief assessment was complete, then turned back to Clump.

“By the way, why is someone from the swordsmanship academy here?”

Rayna crossed her arms.

“Lian dropped out of school. Grandfather called him here to change his mind.”

“He dropped out?”

Rai turned his head toward Lian as if to confirm it. Lian glared right back at him. He might have been unable to face Shirone properly, but he had no intention of losing to Rai in a staring contest.

But Rai merely snorted, uninterested.

“What’s the big deal? If he wants to quit, he can quit.”

Rayna clicked her tongue.

“How can you be so indifferent? Aren’t you worried about your younger brother’s future?”

Rai walked past Lian and headed for the door.

“He was hopeless from the beginning. Better to give up now. Hurry up and bring me food. I’m hungry.”

Lian followed him with his eyes, then said, “Wait a moment.”

When Rai turned around, Lian pointed a finger at his forehead.

“You didn’t go to school either, did you?”

“That’s different from you,” Rai said. “I didn’t need to go. Even if you did, it would be meaningless.”

A grinding sound came from Lian’s teeth.
