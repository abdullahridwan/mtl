Chapter 361  
Midwinter Night (4)  
~*~

Shirone held Amy’s hand tightly.  
“Amy, you are stronger than anyone I know. I will definitely find a solution. It has always been that way.”  

Amy looked back at Shirone’s face. The moment their eyes met, she felt a jolt of electricity run through her body. The moonlight, the sound filtering through the walls, and the warmth of Shirone’s touch through their joined hands made her heart race.  

As if the fear she had felt just moments ago was a lie, a surge of courage welled up in both of them.  

‘Shirone…’  

Amy had to make a choice.  

Another hellish year lay ahead. For someone who had already experienced defeat, all she cared about was her only magical achievement. Yet, this was the first time in her life she wanted to rely on someone, to lean on them like this. She lacked the strength and confidence to overcome it alone.  

Even in this moment of silence, Shirone’s unwavering gaze was fixed on her.  

She had to cross a river of no return. If she looked away now, Shirone would leave her side. She had to accept it to avoid losing him.  

Amy thought her resolve was firm, but new hesitation flooded her mind—selfish thoughts.  

Even if she didn’t choose anything, even if she coldly pretended not to know, was it greed that she wanted Shirone to stay by her side like before?  

‘Just one more year… Can’t you give me one more year? Can’t you stay like this until then so I can make a careful decision?’  

Amy slowly withdrew her hand. If this disappointed Shirone, it was a hand he would never have let go of.  

Her heart was filled with self-ridicule.  

‘What? Are you done with your commitment? Liar.’  

She knew the truth. Shirone would wait. He couldn’t help it, even if she cursed him for being selfish. Graduation exams and Shirone—both were things Amy could not give up.  

“Thank you, Shirone. I’m glad you’re here.”  

Amy spoke the words she wanted to hide somewhere. To say she had chosen and made a decision felt like such an insincere answer.  

But Shirone smiled kindly, just as Amy had expected.  

“That goes for me too. Let’s work hard.”  

Shirone gave Amy a firm grip on her shoulders, then returned to his seat and laid out his sleeping bag on the floor.  

Amy felt relieved, as if she had returned to a past where nothing had happened. However, Shirone’s crimson eyes from earlier reminded her clearly that this was reality.  

How long could he wait for her?  

Contempt for herself and guilt toward Shirone tangled in Amy’s eyes.  

* * *  

Past midnight.  

Zocre and the others were drunk. Still unsatisfied, Zocre bought a large bottle of whiskey and snacks from a nearby store and settled in an empty lot near the fortress.  

He opened the bottle and poured whiskey into glasses in front of Ludvans and Vivian. After filling his own glass, he passed it without even a toast.  

“Wow, that’s strong. What are you doing? Aren’t you going to drink?” Ludvans asked anxiously.  

“Aren’t you drinking too much? What about tomorrow?”  

“Tch, if you feel sick, stay at the dorm,” Vivian said, dipping her lips into her glass as she looked up at the night sky.  

“Sigh, there’s nothing wrong with graduating. Before graduation, I thought I could really have a lot of fun. Not even men are twisted.”  

Zocre pointed at himself with his thumb.  

“We are men too.”  

“Oops, Arthur. Are you going to hit me with that face?”  

“Ha! Vivian, you’re not pretty either.”  

Ludvans agreed.  

“Yes, pretty should be as good as that Amy.”  

Zocre’s expression crumpled.  

“Damn it! I remembered again! How dare you ignore us? They haven’t even graduated yet!”  

“By the way, what happened between you and Amy? You didn’t have to chase her to the rink. Such trivial actions are looked down upon by them.”  

Vivian waited for an answer, clearly curious.  

Certainly, at the skating rink, Zocre had been more excited than usual. Joe Kre intended to keep what he did when he was thirteen a secret forever, but alcohol loosened his tongue.  

In fact, it wasn’t a big deal for others to hear, but it was an unbearable humiliation for him.  

“She confessed to Amy right before she transferred to Eins.”  

Vivian made a fuss at the unexpected words.  

“Oh, really? So? How did it go?”  

“I don’t like her.”  

“No, no?”  

“Yes! She said she hates me! Her heart is grateful, but it wasn’t words that started with ‘you’re a good kid, but…’ It wasn’t even a lie that there was a guy she liked! She just hates me!”  

“Oh my, that’s really mean!”  

“You’re arrogant.”  

Vivian and Ludvans threw one word at a time.  

Jocre clenched his fists as he recalled the moment.  

“You know what annoys me the most? That’s what I answered at the time. ‘Yeah, I’m sorry.’ I will never forget the humiliation of that moment for the rest of my life.”  

Vivian clicked her tongue.  

“Anyway, smooth-faced kids only know themselves? Hey, hey! Rather good. How big is the kid? We became wizards. Zocre! You won! Now! Drink.”  

Zocre passed the bottle Vivian was pouring. His throat felt like it was burning, and a fire welled up in his stomach.  

But he felt fine. Still, the only people he could rely on were his friends.  

What if it’s not a prestigious school?  

He met people he could relate to, so he had no regrets or remorse.  

Zocre and his party, serving as a side dish for Zocre’s gruesome past, continued to drink.  

Then the world started spinning.  

The winter wind howled and passed over the snowfield.  

Suddenly, no one spoke.  

Everyone knew that no matter how comforting the words, the truth would not change in the end.  

Zocre couldn’t beat Amy.  

Vivian bent over and touched the ground. Her pupils, looking up at the sky, were already unfocused.  

She let out a breath full of heat.  

“Actually, you know? Alpheas Magic School is one of the five most prestigious schools in the kingdom, and once you graduate, you can at least get a job at an authorized institution.”  

Zocre and Ludvans silently agreed.  

Becoming a wizard did not mean everyone got a job under the same conditions. The rank and connections of the aristocracy couldn’t be ignored, but since it was a merit-oriented society, treatment varied starkly according to origin.  

Anyone working in the wizarding community knew there was a difference in competitiveness between prestigious schools and regular schools.  

So it was only natural that treatment differed.  

Ludvans said,  

“Anyway, we graduated from magic school and became wizards. That’s great too. Wizards who didn’t even graduate from school and just learned a single spell are all over the world.”  

Whether you graduated from school or joined a guild, the certificate you received was unofficial, so there was no difference.  

Even so, the reason students risked everything for school was to have the school’s name engraved on their license.  

In fact, the level of wizards whose guild name was engraved in their place of origin had fallen to the point that Zocre and the others could make fun of them.  

But that fact was no consolation.  

On top of those from regular schools, there were those from prestigious schools. Their ground task was a certified wizard license.  

Guild wizards were also treated as pros because they made money with magic, but the only way to be recognized as a wizard in the kingdom was to obtain an official license.  

However, not everyone could obtain a certified wizard license.  

Because it was one of the five major exams in the kingdom, the competition was enormous, and all talented wizards gathered from across the country.  

Admission to a prestigious magic school, promotion every year, passing the graduation exam, taking the certified wizard exam and passing—it was a course to be treated as an elite in wizarding society.  

So, from the start, it was impossible to compare themselves to others like Zocre and his party, who were only above guild wizards.  

Jocre shouted as he took a drink straight from the bottle.  

“Damn it! We don’t care if others get licenses from the guild or not. After all, everyone at work is a wizard. Besides, our boss must have graduated from a prestigious school. Look at Dante. He said he’s been breaking professional wizards ever since he was in the Royal Magic School.”  

“But isn’t that just gossip in the journal submissions section?”  

Zocre’s tongue gradually twisted.  

“What do you know? It’s the same anyway. We have to compete on resumes with the top five kids in the kingdom. Would you get votes in the association’s main body? Besides, we’re not even the highest aristocrats. In the end, you become 9th grade while chasing others, work like a dog and become 8th grade, and after about 10 years like that? All the graduates of prestigious schools have already reached 6th grade and will be sitting in the manager’s seat.”  

It was a drunken rant, but it wasn’t a lie. Eventually accepting reality, Vivian nodded.  

“But prestigious schools aren’t the end. Even there, the kids who graduate with the highest grades rise to 6th grade within 2-3 years. What are you saying to us?”  

Zocre said, pointing his finger.  

“You know what annoys me more? Such geniuses eventually become teachers and are assigned to prestigious schools. It’s the inheritance of talent. Because genius teachers teach, geniuses come. In short, we cannot even dare to intervene.”  

Little by little, the alcohol mixed with Zocre’s words, but they weren’t entirely wrong.  

Of course, some ordinary school graduates rose to the top of wizarding society.  

However, such exceptions lost their meaning in the face of the average magic.  

Those who graduated from ordinary schools were inferior to those from prestigious schools.  

This was the only orthodoxy recognized by the world.  

The pessimistic stories that sprang from the mouths of Zocre and his party continued to hover around the drinking party, not fading even in the wind.  

When he was in school, he thought it wouldn’t matter if he became a wizard. However, once he became a wizard, his earnest heart disappeared like a mirage, and new worries arose.  

Vivian looked up at the stars and said,  

“Have you ever thought about this? It’s as if this world is a play. We live our lives, and someone is watching us.”  

Zocre and Ludvans turned to Vivian.  

“If that were the case, I would never be the main character. The audience would never want my success. They probably wouldn’t even be interested. Get out of here, no, maybe they want me to die.”  

Zocre looked at Vivian pitifully.  

From an early age, he was a sensitive friend. But what was truly heartbreaking was that these remarks were not just drunken ramblings.  

Maybe she was right about what she said.  

“What are you worrying about? Life is not a play. No one knows, and no one cares about you.”  

“But it is. Shirone, Dante, and Amy. Do they know how much we wanted to be like them? I don’t know. They just get along with each other. Maybe we didn’t have a place to intervene in the first place. Like extras in a play.”  

The atmosphere at the drinking party became extremely depressing.  

In particular, Jocre, who couldn’t refute Vivian’s words, felt pathetic.  

“Damn it! What! It’s a graduation celebration trip! We passed! You became a wizard! I mean, we won! All in all, meet the unlucky children!”  

Zocre put the spout of the bottle in his mouth and blew on the nabal. The strong drink flowed down his throat.
