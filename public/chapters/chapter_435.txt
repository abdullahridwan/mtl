Final Decision (5)  
~*~

“Get out of the way. It’s still mechanical.”

Shirone pushed Gaold’s chest aside and stepped up to inspect the instrument panel.

Seeing the former head of the Magic Association being pushed around by someone was an extremely unfamiliar sight.

However, Shirone’s gaze soon softened with nostalgia.

*Oh, I see.*

Nade and Iruki.

If Shirone ever found herself trapped in a maze-like situation, what would the two of them look like decades from now?

Gaold, Miro, and Shirone—the founding members of the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Society—had been caught up in the most terrible incident in the world. Even though twenty years had passed and their paths had completely diverged, their time seemed to stand still.

Gaold raised his middle finger toward the back of Shirone’s head.

It seemed inevitable that even a world-renowned archmage would revert to childish behavior when old acquaintances reunited.

Unable to let go of his lingering feelings, Gaold glanced at the instrument panel over Shirone’s shoulder. To him, however, a machine was just an unknown object that worked when irons were attached and certain words were chanted. Then he let out a wicked laugh, as if a mischievous idea had just struck him.

“Hey, you know what?” Shirone said, his fingers quickly manipulating the instrument panel. “Putting mind magic on a national intelligence agent carries the death penalty. If you want to live a long time, you’d better be eager for me to get back to work.”

“What do you know about the laws of the Red Line, you idiot?” Ethella frowned. “I can’t remember what happened here as long as I’m mentally controlled. It looks pretty uneasy. Should I just bury them alive?”

As the Bishop of the Karsis Monastery, it was only natural that she could not tolerate a Black Line wizard who was muddying the world. Yet she took no action. She believed in Alpheas. Even if it was because of a cherished disciple’s earnest request, she was not the one to ask for a mission that would shatter the Karsis Order’s beliefs.

*He must have meant something.*

When she heard the story of the upper levels, her first thought was that nothing should be judged. It was not a matter so trivial that it could be evaluated based on individual beliefs. The only thing that stirred her was a sense of mission: she wanted to see with her own eyes the full story of a massive current that must have begun twenty years ago, or perhaps even longer.

Shirone entered the master mode of Istas. When green letters flashed across the panel, he quickly input information by combining several symbols. Shirone, who had been waiting patiently, asked without suppressing his curiosity.

“By the way, did the Upper Istas really exist? I know that even the intelligence agents of the NIS returned without finding anything.”

“The upper level exists,” Gaold replied. “There’s just no way to get in. It was designed that way from the beginning. Even I, who analyzed all the patterns of Istas, made it inaccessible.”

Shirone looked around at the warehouses scattered everywhere. *How could an inaccessible room exist when all the warehouses had already been individually dismantled?*

“How is that possible? In fact, you can enter any warehouse now. That’s why I also made the master key for Istas.”

Gaold came here to take a closer look at Shirone for the first time. He knew Shirone belonged to the project from the moment Gaold introduced him with special care, but to Gaold, Shirone still looked like just a soft kid.

“How do you know the master key?” Shirone asked.

“I am a member of the Supernatural Psychic Research Society,” Gaold said. “Among my friends, there is a Servant, and that friend was like that. The Master Equation of Istas could not be made more beautiful no matter who touches it.”

Shirone didn’t laugh. But the explanation was more detailed.

“Perhaps. It is an equation that equates a complex four-dimensional diagram to a three-dimensional one.”

The mathematical elimination of one dimension could be compared to the difficulty of making ants understand human language. That was why only a select few in the research society could grasp the master equation. It was a map that possessed a three-dimensional shape but actually located four-dimensional coordinates.

“The upper floors are not limited to space,” Shirone nodded.

Shirone’s insight was quite good, but if he hadn’t achieved this level, he wouldn’t have been qualified to participate in Gaold’s project in the first place.

“Yes. In three dimensions, Istas is just a warehouse made up of eighty-nine buildings. But the upper level exists on a higher plane than that. That’s why the agents couldn’t find it.”

“In addition to a specific space, do you mean that a specific time has to coincide?”

“That’s right. Taking the 333 cube as an example, when the cube at coordinates 1, 1, 1 is moved to coordinates 3, 1, 3, what changes is time in addition to space. No matter how fast you turn it, it takes 0.1 seconds. This is called space-time. I came up with this idea and found a way to keep anyone from approaching the maze: I hid the space-time of the maze at the origin of the cube.”

Suddenly, everyone was listening to Shirone.

Even Alpheas, who had once been Shirone’s teacher, was now in a position to learn from him after twenty years.

“No matter how the cube is rotated—in other words, no matter how the pattern of Istas is changed—the cube’s space-time origin never moves. That’s how the upper layers remain completely concealed. What you created to calculate this is the Master Equation of Istas, which you are currently using.”

Saliva ran down Shirone’s throat. He hadn’t expected that there was such a serious reason for the Master Equation, which he had previously thought was nothing more than a prank by a shadowy study group.

Shirone said.

“But aren’t we gathered today to go to the upper levels? If no one can get in, what are you waiting for here right now?”

“Another master key.”

As if that word had become a keyword, the air crackled with the sound of exploding electricity. It was a noise peculiar to flicker magic, which even Shirone was familiar with.

A man and a woman appeared through the scale magician’s proprietary patent.

The woman had a cute impression with her short, permed hair, and the man was tall, with blonde hair hanging down to his waist.

The important thing was that both of them were old acquaintances of Shirone. If no two people could be the same in the world, then the blonde man with bandages over his eyes couldn’t be anyone else.

The Eternal Reflector, Armin of Gwangan, stepped forward.

“Oh, brother?”

Shirone couldn’t believe what he was seeing. Why was Armin, who should be wandering the world and capturing landscapes on canvas, at the center of the worst human situation?

“Are you really Armin’s brother?”

Shirone approached, but Armin coldly turned away from him and moved on.

His wife, Keira, also headed for Istas without even giving a glance.

Shirone froze in place, shocked. His mind was reeling, and he couldn’t think of anything.

“You arrived just on time. Really, was that rude to the world’s greatest time mage?”

World’s greatest time mage.

Being a magician who had conquered the speed of light, Shirone did not find the title awkward at all. However, Shirone, who knew nothing about him, only deepened his embarrassment.

“A time mage. What the hell are you talking about, brother?”

Armin remained silent.

Shirone, deciding that confusion was not good ahead of the event, introduced Armin. It was ironic to Shirone, who thought he knew Armin best.

“In order to reach the upper levels of Istas, you will definitely need the help of a powerful time mage. Armin of Gwangan couldn’t be more qualified for this.”

Shirone shook his head. The more he listened to the explanation, the more confused he became.

“Keira, how the hell did this happen! Are you kidding me?”

Keira, who always had a lively smile and sometimes said nasty things, didn’t laugh this time. She raised her voice high-handedly, lowering her eyes so coldly that Shirone wondered if it was the same person.

“You’d better be careful with your words, Mr. Shirone. We are Armin and Keira, wizards of the Ivory Tower Human Security Enforcement Department, belonging to the White Line.”

“The White Line…”

Shirone, who knew Armin’s identity, couldn’t help but be surprised this time.

If there were associations on the Red Line and factions on the Black Line, the White Line was a completely independent organization that operated solely out of the Ivory Tower.

Magic that is not abused in the world.

Purity not swayed by a ruler’s beliefs.

The wizards of the Ivory Tower, whose motto embodied these two ideals, only pursued the extremes of pure magic.

It was said that even their ordinary staff were equivalent to a country’s great wizards, and their military power was not at a level that any single kingdom could surpass.

They were the absolute monarchs of the sorcerer society, where even the royal family trembled when they presented their certificate of belonging to the Ivory Tower.

But Armin was here as a member of the Ivory Tower.

As a wizard of the Human Security Enforcement Department.

Ivory Tower wizards did not discriminate between departments, but it was true that from the perspective of the Red Line, the Human Security Enforcement Department—which had little to do with society—was the most noticeable.

In fact, it was a famous anecdote that thirty-two years ago, when Igor, the dictator of the Kingdom of Korn, reached out to necromancy, a wizard from the Human Security Enforcement Department had come in alone and forced him to kneel and crawl to the floor.

Keira walked up to Gaold and got in his face. Armin had no choice but to come because he was stubborn, but if he had already done his job, shouldn’t he have taken care of his business?

Armin was a person far removed from real life.

“Since you brought Armin, you should also keep your promise. First, the Ivory Tower has the right to record and disseminate information obtained from the construction of the maze. Second, it forbids Shirone from going to the upper levels. You know that Armin came here because of the second clause, right?”

“Of course. But let’s make one thing clear. It was only if Shirone agreed. I really hate convincing people. Especially if you are a woman.”

“Well, it’s okay. Because Armin knows that. But later you’ll have to tell Armin that I definitely imprinted the second point on you.”

“Hehe, you live a really difficult life too.”

When Keira said it, she frowned as if asking what she was doing. Stubborn Armin.

In fact, the Ivory Tower had yet to make any judgments about the upper levels. This was because it was still seen as a problem that could be sufficiently solved within human society.

But Armin had fallen for Gaold’s trick. In order to open the upper floors of Istas, the highest-level time mage was needed, and Gaold, who tried to recapture the labyrinth, could not have been unaware of his existence.

The result obtained after investigating Armin’s whereabouts was the same reason why the Ivory Tower was able to capture him: Shirone. A person so precious to Armin that he could even exchange his life for it.

That’s why Keira hated Shirone even more.

*Sheesh, is that guy that good? He’s a guy who doesn’t have any charms.*

When Shirone released the mind control, the ten agents collapsed to the ground like puppets with their strings cut. It was to make sure that there were no variables when activating his thoughts right before the gigantic event.

“Finish it before the agents come to their senses. I will go right in.”

Shirone pressed the button on the instrument panel simultaneously.

The eighty-nine warehouses began to mesh together in a specific form.

For this moment, Shirone put aside his thoughts of Armin and looked at Istas.

A time mage belonging to the White Line Ivory Tower, a first-class great mage of the Red Line, and a top-notch spirit-type mage of the Black Line.

At the gathering of the three great wizards who had reached their peak in different fields, the upper floors of Istas were about to be opened for the first time in twenty years.
