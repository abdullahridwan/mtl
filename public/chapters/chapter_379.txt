Chapter 379  
Prosecutor’s Belief (3)  
~*~

“Do you really think you’re ready to wear a rank badge already? Whatever my grades were, it’s not as if I spent all this time playing around. When it comes to the sword, I’m confident I won’t lose to anyone.”

“Do you think divine transcendence is such a big deal?”

Reina looked at Rai in surprise.

She had once told him about Lian’s divine transcendence. Considering Rai’s usual lack of interest in anything that did not concern him, it was strange that he had remembered.

“Transcending the body? How impressive. Should I applaud?”

Sparks flew in Lian’s eyes.

“What? You little—!”

“If you rely on that, your body will break first.”

Lian’s mouth snapped shut.

“Yes, divine transcendence is a difficult stage to reach. But that doesn’t make it great. There are plenty of difficult, useless things in the world. It would probably be harder to move a mountain with a shovel, but no one does it. What are you going to accomplish by transcending your body? You’re a human being made of flesh and blood. Divine transcendence? It might make you a little stronger than other people, but that’s all.”

It was cruel, but it was also true.

Even Shirone, who had reached a similar realm to Lian, could not refute him this time. Mental transcendence allowed one to surpass the limits of magical power through the power of incarnation, but once one surpassed feedback itself, the mind would eventually collapse.

Lian gritted his teeth and forced the words out.

“Shut up. What do you know? You’ve never experienced it anyway, have you?”

“Right, I haven’t. Amazing. Don’t expect me to cheer.”

Rai turned away again. Before leaving the room, he added, “Congratulations. For the first time, you can do something I can’t.”

Lian heard the thread of his reason snap. His heart pounded so hard he could not control it, and his body trembled against his will.

No one knew how effective divine transcendence would be in a real fight. But after ten years of training, it was the one hope that had miraculously come to him.

“…Wait.”

Rai kept moving, as if he had not heard him.

“I’m busy. It’s your problem, so handle it yourself.”

“I told you to wait!”

Lian’s shout rang through the room. No one was startled; everyone had expected him to explode sooner or later.

When Rai turned back with a trace of annoyance, Lian strode up and faced him.

“Ogent Rai, I challenge you to a duel. Let’s fight with real swords.”

Reina rubbed her forehead as if she had known this would happen.

“Lian, you always—”

“No, I’m serious. If I lose to Rai, I’ll go back to school. How about that? Is that enough for you?”

Rai laughed.

“I don’t care whether you go back to school or not. If you want to quit, quit on your own. Don’t make it my problem.”

“Ha! What, then? Are you running away because you’re scared?”

Rai’s face twisted subtly.

“Me, afraid of you? That’s absurd.”

“Oh, then you’re busy? You were acting so proud a moment ago, but your excuses are weak. Haven’t you always bragged that you could handle someone like me with just your sword? Or what? When you talk about swordsmen, do you mean men whose mouths are the only things worthy of a rank?”

Rai sighed.

He rubbed his face tiredly, then nodded as if indulging a child.

“Fine. I’ll duel you. I’ll wait at the training ground. Get ready and come out.”

He turned coldly toward the door, then looked back at Lian as though he had just remembered something.

“And you. Don’t try to provoke me again. You’re terrible at it.”

“That little—!”

Lian tried to rush after him at once, but Shirone grabbed his arm and stopped him.

“Calm down, Lian. If anything, he provoked you.”

“Ah.”

Lian realized it belatedly and forced himself to settle down.

His anger, however, did not vanish. He had tried so hard to use a tongue that did not suit him, only for Rai to turn it around with a single remark. He hated him for it. Of course, Shirone simply thought Lian was far too honest.

Tess, who had been listening outside the door, came in with a worried look.

“Lian, are you really going to do this? If you lose, you have to go back to the swordsmanship school.”

“Hey, have you ever seen me go back on my word?”

“That’s not what I mean…”

Tess quietly turned her gaze to Quan. Since Quan had already stated that he would not accept Lian’s resignation letter, even if Lian changed his mind, that would cause its own problems.

But Lian did not care.

If he lost to Rai, he would go back to school even if he had to kneel and beg. If they refused to accept him, he was prepared to stand at the school gate until they did.

“I’ll go. A promise is a promise. But I’m not going back. I challenged him because I’m determined to win.”

Lian turned to Clump.

“Grandpa, do you agree?”

Clump crossed his arms and fell into serious thought. At last, he opened his eyes.

“Let’s go to the training ground.”

* * *

The training ground was behind Reina’s mansion. Reina, Clump, and Rai all lived there, but Rai was constantly away on dispatch, and Clump spent most of his time in the general’s barracks, so the place was not well maintained.

At the center of the small, half-neglected lot, Rai was waiting for everyone with a sword at his waist, having changed into clothes suitable for a duel. While Lian prepared, Reina approached Rai with concern on her face.

“Hey, are you really going to do this? I don’t like it. Brothers don’t need to fight each other with real swords.”

“Let him do what he wants. Do you think that fool will listen otherwise?”

“And what if there’s an accident?”

“We’ll think about it when it happens. Isn’t this good for you anyway? That fool will go back to school.”

If the duel ended safely and Lian quietly gave up, it would be a relief for Reina as well. But because the two of them mixed as poorly as oil and water, her concern as their older sister did not disappear.

“Fine, but be gentle and end it quickly.”

“I’m busy too. I’ll do that even if you don’t tell me.”

Reina shrugged. Although Rai was self-centered, he understood situations clearly. Unlike Lian, he had been a younger brother who needed no nagging since childhood.

Well, there’s no real need to worry about him.

Lian stood at one corner of the training ground, staring at Rai and taking deep breaths.

Tess came over and vigorously kneaded Lian’s shoulders and arms. Even for the fearless Lian, this duel seemed to carry weight; his muscles were stiff with tension.

“Come on! You can do this! Relax!”

Tess did her best to encourage him. Of course, she did not want Lian to drop out of school, but she hated seeing him humiliated even more.

Lian remained silent and focused on clearing his mind. His family was watching, and so were Instructor Quan and Shirone. This was a fight he could not afford to lose.

Reina came over and placed a hand on Lian’s shoulder.

“For today’s match, I’m neutral. So keep it moderate. Don’t expect me to step in.”

“Hey, you’d better worry about Rai. I’m not the same as I used to be.”

Reina said nothing.

Rai was not thinking of losing, and neither was Reina. Having once wielded a sword herself, she knew better than anyone the difference in their skill.

There’s almost no chance Lian will win. And Rai isn’t the type to go easy on him just because he’s his younger brother.

When Clump and Quan entered the training ground, Lian picked up the greatsword he had received from his grandfather.

As Lian walked toward the battlefield, Shirone spoke from behind him.

“Lian, you have to win.”

Lian turned his head and smiled. There was not a trace of nervousness on his face.

“No problem. Leave it to me.”

When Lian reached the center of the training ground, Rai drew the black sword from his waist. It was longer and thinner than an ordinary longsword, but it was a straight sword tailored to Ogent swordsmanship, and since it was made of black iron, its hardness was considerable.

Rai lightly swung the black sword in an X, then raised the blade toward the sky and examined it.

“Let’s start quickly. I want to rest.”

“You’re not going to rest. You’re going to be lying down. I don’t know what dispatch you’re talking about, but you’d better cancel it.”

“Whatever you’re doing, just do it quickly.”

Rai did not even bother responding properly to Lian’s words.

Lian hated that about him. Rai learned any skill in an instant, and to him, Lian, who had been born without any talent, must not even look like a member of the same family.

The ridicule Lian had suffered when he was young suddenly flashed through his mind.

“Anyway, I should thank you for accepting the match. My bad blood with you ends today.”

Lian raised the greatsword with one hand and took aim. Just seeing its precarious center of gravity, which exceeded normal human limits, was intimidating.

Rai lowered the black sword diagonally. Even held that way, the sword was long enough for its tip to reach the ground like a pole.

The reach of the greatsword and the black sword was surprisingly close. However, the black sword was lighter, so Rai should be faster.

Either way, I’m behind in technique.

Lian planned out the fight. His strategy was as simple as his personality.

I’ll crush him with power!

The instant he clenched his molars, both his eyes flashed. He kicked off the ground with tremendous force and launched himself forward.

“Here I come!”

Lian raised his greatsword and charged. Even without being struck, one could imagine the force of that heavy blade falling from nearly three meters high.

At the same time, flashes of black light burst from Rai’s body, slipping into every opening in Lian’s stance. Lian could not even complete half of his swing before he had to switch to defense.

Rai’s swift, sharp attacks struck the surface of the greatsword like a blacksmith’s hammer.

“Keugh!”

Lian’s body jolted from the unexpectedly heavy impact.

It felt different from Tess’s sword. Rai’s swordsmanship was fast and precise, but it still carried the unique power of the Ogent family.

Once he gave up the initiative, Lian was driven back without pause. The process was so one-sided that it seemed impossible for him to take control again.

Rai moved as if he were dancing a sword dance alone in an empty lot.

When Lian was pushed to the edge of the arena, exclamations escaped from the mouths of the onlookers. It was swordsmanship so impressive that even Tess, who had been cheering for Lian, was stunned.

“Yaaaaa!”

Lian’s sword swept horizontally. The air roared and screamed.

Lian barely managed to create space and stepped forward. But against Rai’s lightning-fast attacks, he could do nothing and was forced back again.

Rai did not stop attacking. It was as if Lian had been trapped and beaten inside a cage.

Lian counterattacked less and less, until even blocking seemed beyond him.

Tsk. He’s improved, but there’s no development.

Rai had no intention of wasting more time on such a bland match.

After battering Lian’s sword again and again to throw him off balance, Rai reversed his grip on the black sword and drove it into the ground. The moment the blade pierced the earth, a sudden vibration surged up through the soles of Lian’s feet.

“What…!”

Lian looked down in shock. The ground swelled as if wind were rising beneath it, then exploded with a bang.

“Keugh!”

A shock wave burst from the ground and struck both of Lian’s feet, making his calf muscles convulse.

The name of the technique was Jipok.

It was a blade technique driven into the earth, one Rai had mastered when he was only twelve years old.

Lian’s knees shook, and he could not keep his balance. He sank lower instead and raised the greatsword using only the strength of his upper body.

Rai’s black sword pierced forward with a crash, and vibrations rushed through the greatsword.

Lian gritted his teeth and suppressed a scream.

It felt as though the muscles in his arms were splitting apart in chunks. The shock wave that had run through his body radiated outward, making his upper garment burst apart with a popping sound. His clothes were left in tatters, as if they had been slashed by dozens of knives.

Lian frowned deeply and glared at Rai.

Rai’s expression, as he still held the black sword, was indifferent.

Everyone watching sensed that the duel was over. Since the shock wave had torn through his body, even if Lian looked fine on the outside, his muscles would no longer have the strength to move properly.

The second son of the Ogent family, Ogent Rai.

His skill was real.
