Chapter 436  
Final Decision (6)  
~*~

Curleung. Thump.

The buildings of Istas stacked and merged into a colossal, mass-like structure, resembling a giant with two thick legs planted firmly in the ground.

Sein, who opened a chamber on the giant’s right leg, spoke to Alfeas.

“If we go in, please activate the Istas again.”

Only those who can reach Heaven are qualified to navigate the labyrinth’s space-time.

At least, Alfeas thought so, and he intended to do what he could.

“Let’s do it.”

Gaold’s usual mischief vanished from his face. We were finally going to meet Miro.

It was fortunate she had fallen into samadhi. The anger I’d harbored for twenty years might finally find release.

After everyone entered the chamber, Alfeas restarted the Istas.

Following Sein’s lead, the cube-like structures began to shift, separating and recombining in an intricate pattern.

*Thud. Coo-kung. Thud.*

I could hear Istas’s heartbeat.

With each step he took, Sein’s amber irises flicked. The left iris flicked left and right at a fifteen-degree angle, while the right iris rotated at varying speeds depending on the section. Sein adjusted his stride length rather than his walking speed. It was a method of controlling time by manipulating space.

“Come this way.”

When Sein opened a door at a right angle from the center of the chamber, another chamber slid into place, aligning its doorway with the passage. The moment they crossed the threshold, the chamber they had just left collapsed, replaced by another block that sealed off their previous position.

This process repeated countless times.

*Tremendous. I couldn’t even imitate him unless I were a Servant.*

The chambers moved, the world moved, and time moved. It felt as though the very concept of space Shirone knew was collapsing. He was truly walking through four-dimensional time and space.

As Sein’s steps halted before their final destination, Shirone’s eyes filled with vague, lingering emotions.

A nostalgic sign hung above the door: *Supernatural Psychic Research Society*. The signboard was crooked, as if it constantly tried to right itself but failed, tilting on its own axis.

When the door was opened, traces of the occupants’ recent activity were still visible. The equations Iruki had written on the blackboard were smudged and partially erased, and the picture books Nade had been reluctant to discard lay torn to shreds.

Sein walked to the sofa.

“The Supernatural Psychic Research Society will arrive at this coordinate in twelve seconds. At that exact space-time point, the upper layer of Istas will manifest. From here on, Armin, it’s your turn.”

Armin stepped past Sein.

As a scale wizard who manipulated time and space, Armin had already deduced the nature of Istas’s upper layer without needing an explanation.

“Seven seconds. Six. Five.”

As Sein began counting down, a fierce wind roared, as if they were caught in a storm current. It was the phenomenon of the upper layer’s space-time coordinates converging with theirs.

The furniture and artifacts of the research society melted and blurred in the dense, howling wind. On the table before the sofa, a stone gate resembling the Guffin’s door Shirone had seen in Galliant began to overlap as a ghostly afterimage.

*Woo woo woo!*

“Four seconds. Three.”

As the countdown continued, the surrounding scenery grew paler, while the stone gate’s colors grew vividly sharp. The objects flickered and then turned transparent as their space-time coordinates drifted away.

“Two seconds. One second.”

The moment all scenery vanished, leaving only the stone gate at one hundred percent materialization.

“Now.”

Armin cast a spell. *Stop.*

“Oh my god… … .”

Shirone’s eyes lit up. The sense of acceptance was no different between Etel and Shirone.

The upper layer of Istas, hidden within a specific space and time, was caught by the stop magic and revealed itself.

Twenty years ago, a genius girl had shocked the world. Humanity had imprisoned her at a specific coordinate in time and space, one no one could find. This was the truth behind the upper echelons of Istas, a secret only rumored about at Alfeas Magic School.

*It must have been something like this.*

Shirone finally understood what Sein had meant. The origin of the cube. A place countless agents had searched for but never found.

Armin said, turning away from the stone gate.

“As long as time is stopped, the door will remain. But you’d better move quickly. If the magic wears off before you’re through, you’ll be trapped in the labyrinth of time and space.”

Such a scenario seemed unlikely, but given the life-threatening stakes, caution was warranted. Shirone had escaped the maze’s space-time before, but only because he was an Unlocker. Besides, he couldn’t ask Miro for help; she was currently in a state of samadhi.

Gaold wasn’t thinking about the consequences. The renowned first-class archmage’s face was pale, his heart pounding, his cheeks flushed more than ever.

“Let’s go, Sein.”

“Understood.”

Sein’s irises also rotated in the opposite direction. It was a phenomenon that occurred when coolness and excitement overlapped. This perfect separation of emotion and reason was precisely why he had risen to the ranks of the strongest anti-mages.

“Did you say Shirone? I hope you’re ready.”

The door to the upper floors was meant to be unsealed via the Immortal Function, just like the Guffin’s door. There were two Unlockers on the team, but it was better for Armin to focus on maintaining the stop magic. Opening the door fell to Shirone.

When Shirone activated the Immortal Function, the runes on the stone gate glowed, then disintegrated into tiny cubes that expanded outward in spherical waves. As the material melted away, a massive black hole formed.

“There… … Who goes in first?”

Shirone didn’t know why, but he felt compelled to establish an order. It didn’t matter right now, but it could be interpreted as a historically significant event in the future.

“It would be fitting for the one who broke the seal to enter first. Shirone, you go first.”

Armin gave Shirone a friendly nudge.

Shirone’s mind grew complicated as he watched the scene. He felt as though he couldn’t even remember who Armin was.

“Then I’ll go in.”

For a moment, his vision darkened. Then, the view from the center of the colossal temple unfolded before him.

The scenery, dominated by two-kilometer-high pillars supporting the ceiling, was undoubtedly the labyrinth of time and space.

*I never thought I’d come here again.*

One by one, the others emerged from the black hole. Just as Shirone had been when he first arrived, everyone stood at a loss for words, overwhelmed by the temple’s scale, which defied human common sense.

“What? By the way… … .”

Shirone hurriedly turned around, scanning the group to see if they had the same realization.

“What happened?”

The maze was gone.

“Shirone, maybe here… … .”

“Nope. It’s just the framework of a maze. It’s no different from when I came a year ago.”

Gaold cast fly magic. With a sharp *pop*, his body shot rapidly from one end of the temple to the other. It was a ‘jet’-type magic that propelled him by exploding air pressure rather than generating lift. Gaold quickly surveyed the vast temple. His flight resembled Shirone’s wings, but the speed and agility were on a completely different level.

Returning to the group, Gaold’s face began to contort terribly. It seemed as though a superheated hell would erupt at any moment.

“These damn bastards… … .”

“Calm down. Nothing’s happened yet. It just means there’s no actual maze within the maze’s space-time.”

There was no labyrinth in the space-time of the labyrinth. It was true there wasn’t, but it was something that should never have happened. First, no existence could enter and exit the maze’s space-time. Furthermore, if someone was in a state of samadhi, escaping on their own was impossible. No, if that were possible, there would have been no reason for Gaold to wait twenty years.

“It’s a kidnapping.”

“Perhaps they kidnapped Miro from Heaven.”

Gaold asked.

“Is that possible? Not even a single molecule has ever crossed over from Heaven.”

“No, there is.”

Armin bent down to pick up debris from the floor. It was a chunk of stone that had fallen from the ceiling when an impact struck the maze’s space-time. Rubbing the material between his fingers to inspect it, he said,

“I heard there was a crack in the labyrinth’s time-space not too long ago. The Ruler of the Law also came to the Tormia Kingdom. I know it was handled and reported by the mage of the Akeanis Shrine.”

“But it’s only a mental body. If they wanted to kidnap an entire human being, a crack wouldn’t be enough.”

“The Ivory Tower shares that opinion. However, that’s human logic. Heaven, especially the archangels, may have found a special way.”

Sein rested his chin on his hand and asked himself, “Why take Miro with you?”

It seemed like an odd question at first glance. Heaven was planning a final war and had consistently expressed its intention to infiltrate ever since the labyrinth’s space-time was created.

Shirone asked, “Of course they did it to invade this world, right?”

Etel said, “No. Maybe that’s impossible.”

Sein agreed. “Just taking Miro has no effect. Even if you kill her, the maze’s space-time won’t be destroyed.”

Shirone asked with a puzzled expression. “Indestructible?”

“The space-time of the maze is basically a dimension based on cowardice. It keeps creating new dimensions within dimensions. Using that infinity, it creates a boundary between space and time. And Miro further increased the speed of the attack through Sammaegyeong. The theory is that even if Miro dies, the inertia of samadhi-gyeong continues to strengthen the space-time of the maze. Of course I can’t understand it.”

As one moves into the mental world after death, control over consciousness weakens, and one becomes subject to the most intense inertia of their life. This was why, even if the Virtual Zone was deactivated, failure to escape fear would result in being buried in the mind forever.

“Nope. I think I know.”

Sein didn’t even scoff. “It’s just an illusion. The illusion of a beginner who believes they’ve grasped their true intentions by understanding the context.”

How many people in this world truly understood the space-time of the maze? Even if they didn’t, it certainly didn’t apply to a kid who attended a magic school.

Shirone said, “Shirone has passed the cowardice.”

Sein’s eyebrow muscles twitched. It was the greatest surprise he could express. At Alfeas Magic School, the only person who had passed the cowardice was Miro. Even back then, the memory of the uproar surrounding the arrival of a world-class genius was vivid in his mind.

*Hmm. Indeed, Gaold deserves it.*

Gaold put it simply. “Anyway, they can’t kill Miro. Even so, taking her must have required a way. We don’t have much time. As soon as the team is ready, we’ll go to Heaven.”

Sein did not disagree. He was the one who had walked the black line for twenty years to become who he was today.

*But… … Is this normal?*

The feeling was strange. It was literally just a feeling. Was today’s outcome, which had just occurred, merely an unfortunate situation that could happen to anyone, with tiny coincidences stacking up? If so, what was the source of this unpleasant feeling of incongruity haunting his mind?

An extremely irrational thought nagged at him: if things were destined to turn out this way, shouldn’t they have been twisted somehow over the course of nineteen years?

*Why now? No, why is this happening? Is it normal to think like this? Anyway, the world doesn’t always go the way you want it to. Attributing transcendent meaning to chance is no different from superstition.*

He wanted to draw that conclusion. But Sein couldn’t shake it off in the end. It wasn’t an illogical thought. Rather, it was a subtle intrusion in the realm of intuition, transcending logic.

*For sure… … .*

Something was out of place.
