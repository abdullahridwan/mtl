Chapter 382  
Swordsman’s Belief (6)  
~*~

After spending the night at the mansion, Shirone headed to the dining room with Lian and Tess.

Klumph was seated at the head of the table, reading the newspaper, while Raina bustled about preparing breakfast.

Shirone rubbed his sleepy eyes and nodded in greeting.

“Good morning.”

“Mm. Sit down and eat.”

Lian dropped into a chair, rubbing his empty stomach, and asked Raina, “Where’s Rai?”

“He left early this morning. Did you think he was an idle, unemployed layabout like you?”

As always, the jab came in the form of a joke, but this time there was a barb in it. As his older sister, Raina could not help being bothered by the fact that her youngest brother was leaving to train as a knight.

“Sheesh, it’s only been one day since I dropped out! Besides, I’m leaving as soon as I eat.”

Raina set a plate of chicken stew in front of him.

“Rest for a few days. Shirone came all this way too.”

Shirone, carrying his own plate, shook his head.

“No. I have to go back soon too.”

He had an appointment with Kang Nan, so he could not explain the details. More than anything, Shirone himself was curious as to why the president of the association wanted to see him.

Since this was the day Lian would leave, the atmosphere at breakfast was unusually solemn. Lian, however, was Lian. He devoured three plates of chicken stew and then hurriedly prepared to go.

His luggage was simple: a change of clothes and a backpack with enough food for a day. After strapping on the leather scabbard and fixing the greatsword to his back, he approached Klumph in the living room.

“Grandfather.”

“All ready?”

There was only one thing left to say before the preparations were truly complete.

The fact that Lian had defeated Rai would give him great courage on the path ahead. But there was one thing he still needed to know.

“What did Rai mean yesterday?”

Klumph lowered the cup from his lips and looked up at Lian.

“When he said he didn’t use this sword. You know the reason, don’t you, Grandfather?”

“Ah, that.”

Klumph smacked his lips and set down the teacup.

He had put off telling Lian, worried that it would sound hollow. But now that Lian was leaving to train as a knight, there was no reason to keep it from him.

“The sword you’re carrying isn’t an ordinary sword. Have you ever heard of an object?”

“An object?”

Lian’s eyes widened. He pulled out the greatsword. His heart thumped as he remembered the abilities Shirone had demonstrated the night before—Rated Control and Diamond Armament.

Hearing the conversation, Shirone and Tess also came into the living room and turned their attention to the blade. Apart from its rough, chaotic shape, it did not appear to have any special characteristics.

Lian held the greatsword upright and stared up at it.

“You mean this is an object? Is that why it didn’t break? What ability does it have?”

Klumph crossed his arms and legs.

“The official name of that sword is [Idea]. Its name is as grand as its ability. To put it simply, [Idea] is, under any circumstances…”

“Under any circumstances?”

Klumph’s eyes sharpened, and he spoke with absolute clarity.

“Indestructible.”

Silence fell over the living room.

“And?”

“Hm? What do you mean, and? It doesn’t break. Whether you drop it into lava, cut it with a saw, or beat it with a hammer, it won’t lose so much as a fingernail’s worth. Isn’t that amazing?”

In a sense, it was every swordsman’s dream. However, compared to Armand, which possessed dozens of functions, it was admittedly so simple that it almost felt disappointing.

“Uh, well… that is amazing. Incredibly amazing. But it’s still just a sword. If a sword breaks, can’t you just buy a new one?”

“Idiot. Do you know how important a weapon’s durability is in real combat? Even in yesterday’s duel, if it hadn’t been for [Idea], the fight would have been far more difficult.”

Even Lian had to admit that much.

No, looking back, [Idea] had already saved his life before.

It had been when he blocked Ymir’s straight punch.

Even Cigna and Xd, made of steel, had crumpled like paper, yet Lian’s greatsword had not broken. If not for [Idea], Ymir’s fist would have punched through the blade and shattered Lian’s body.

“In any case, that’s what kind of sword it is. It’s not that Rai never tried to use it. It simply didn’t work for him. When he was young, Rai wanted it very badly.”

Lian pursed his lips. He could now say with some confidence that he had become a decent swordsman, but even when they were children, Rai had been the pride of the family.

“Sheesh, then you should have given it to Rai. Why did you keep it until now?”

“He hates bulky things. It didn’t suit him.”

Lian had asked with a little expectation, but the answer was not what he had hoped for.

When Lian’s expression turned even more sour, Klumph opened his mouth wide and laughed.

“Hey, what did you expect? Did you think I had prepared it especially for you?”

“Who said anything? Don’t go reading minds when you can’t even do it properly.”

Klumph played it off, but Shirone could guess the truth.

Rai had probably truly wanted [Idea]. Regardless of its ability, inheriting the sword of the previous family head was a great honor. And yet, in the end, that sword had been passed down to Lian.

Even so, Klumph turned it into a joke because he did not want to burden his grandson.

“Since we’re on the subject, shall I tell you an anecdote related to [Idea]?”

“Huh? There’s an anecdote?”

Lian’s eyes shone with interest. For all his grumbling, he did not seem to dislike [Idea]’s unique ability.

“Of course. When I was young, I went out to train in swordsmanship, just like you. At that time, I single-handedly subdued a group of bandits. They were quite strong. I wiped them out, but before they died, I also suffered a fatal wound. With that injured body, I came down the mountain. Then I found a strange village that wasn’t even marked on the map. It didn’t look much different from other villages, but for some reason, the people were quiet and gloomy.”

Shirone and the others held their breath, drawn into Klumph’s story.

It was the story of the fateful encounter between the man who would one day become a general of Tormia and an object.

“There, I met an old man and his granddaughter. They saved my life when I was at death’s door. When I came to, the sword wound had completely healed. I still don’t know how they treated me. All that remained in my hazy memory were words like a spell, repeated countless times. Smille, Smile, and so on.”

Lian muttered the words under his breath, then asked, “Smille? What does that mean?”

“I don’t know. It might have been some kind of spell, or perhaps magic. In any case, the old man seemed to chant those words all night long. After I regained consciousness, I looked around the village. There was an antique shop there, full of strange items. The most memorable thing was that they were selling glass bottles filled with smoke. I asked what they were for, but the owner wouldn’t tell me.”

As if he had finally reached the important part, Klumph took a sip of tea to wet his throat.

“Then I saw a greatsword hanging above the counter. That was [Idea], the sword you now carry. I knew at a glance that it was no ordinary sword. Then, as if possessed by something, I approached the owner.”

Lian swallowed, his expression tense.

“And then?”

Klumph blinked and replied flatly, “What do you mean, and then? I paid for it and bought it, of course.”

As all the tension collapsed, one word slipped out of Lian’s mouth.

“Are you kidding me?”

Klumph furrowed one eyebrow, displeased.

“What, you brat? You said you wanted to hear an anecdote.”

Lian scratched his head.

His grandfather had gotten him again. Was it really that much fun to tease an eighteen-year-old grandson?

“You made it sound so tense. I thought you’d gone through some mysterious experience that transcended life and death again.”

“Puhahaha! Do I look crazy enough to risk my life over a sword? Lian, remember this. A true sword is grasped with the heart, not the hand. Anyway, I made a big decision and bought it. Treat it with respect.”

“Sheesh. That sounds ridiculous. By the way, if it was such a big decision, how much did you pay? Since it’s an object, I’m guessing around two million gold?”

“That? Eighty million gold.”

Clang!

Lian dropped the greatsword to the floor.

“Damn it! Are you insane? You paid eighty million just to buy a sword that doesn’t break? With that money, you could buy hundreds of famous swords!”

“It’s an object, you fool! And I bought it with my own money, so why are you meddling? If you’re going to treat it like that, give it back! Hand over eighty million gold if you want to take it!”

“No way! You said you were giving it to me, so it’s mine now. Don’t come asking for payment after the fact!”

Lian hurriedly picked up the greatsword and hugged it to his chest.

“What kind of swordsman throws his sword on the floor? That sword is wasted on you! Hang it in my room this instant!”

“Why? Are you worried it might break? Didn’t someone just say a true sword is held with the heart?”

Shirone and Tess watched the argument with bewildered expressions.

“…They really are alike.”

“Yeah.”

After bickering for a while, the two finally fell silent, as if both had exhausted themselves.

In the end, Lian was the winner. He returned the greatsword to its scabbard and tightened the leather strap more firmly than usual, as though making sure Klumph saw it.

A moment later, Klumph thought of something and smiled.

This was the moment he had been waiting for for eighteen years. Seeing it with his own eyes made the old man’s heart ache.

‘The more I look at him, the more he resembles me.’

Though Lian had almost no talent for swordsmanship, Klumph had decided from the beginning that the owner of [Idea] would be Lian.

He had not paid eighty million gold because the sword’s function was impressive.

A sword that would never break.

Wasn’t that the belief most suitable for the Ogent family?

And at this moment, all of Klumph’s philosophy was being conveyed to Lian through [Idea].

‘Still, it really was strange. I paid eighty million gold and returned later, but the village had vanished without a trace.’

Of course, if eighty million gold had entered a mountain village, the entire village might well have moved away in search of a better place to live.

However, there was also a subtle feeling he could not tell Lian about.

Perhaps he had simply collapsed in the mountains with a fatal wound, and the month he remembered spending traveling back and forth between the village and home had been nothing more than a dream he had wandered through in a trance.

In any case, it was one of the most incomprehensible events in Klumph’s life. And with the realization he gained that day, he went on to win battle after battle, eventually rising to the position of general—a certified third-class swordsman.

“Grandfather, I’ll be back.”

Before anyone knew it, Lian’s expression had changed. He said his farewell with eyes burning with resolve.

“Yes. The most important thing is to protect yourself. Remember that victory is not always something you obtain by fighting.”

It meant that if a fight could be avoided, it was better to avoid it.

Taking his grandfather’s advice to heart, Lian bowed sincerely.

‘Thank you, Grandfather. Thank you for trusting your troublesome grandson until the very end.’

Lian left the mansion, seen off by his family and friends. A carriage was waiting at the front gate.

He looked at the carriage for a moment, thought about his destination, then made up his mind and turned to Shirone.

“Shirone, work hard. Even though I dropped out, just think of it as me graduating first.”

“Yeah. You take care of yourself too. Write often.”

Lian then looked back at Tess, whose eyes were filled with tears. She had endured until now, but when the moment to leave truly arrived, she could no longer contain the emotions pounding inside her chest.

Lian smiled warmly.

Unlike a swordsman, Tess was deeply affectionate, and Lian liked that about her. One day, the day would come when he could stand face-to-face with her.

“Tess, I’ll be back.”

“You idiot. You didn’t even discuss it with me…”

“Sorry. I was short-tempered.”

Tess understood Lian’s choice. He was someone she, more than anyone, needed to understand. Yet at the same time, she was angry with herself for being unable to cruelly reject such an indifferent man.

“I’ll put up with it this time. But if you treat me this coldly again when you come back, then…”

Lian pulled Tess into his broad chest.

“I’ll come back stronger.”

Tess pressed her lips together, holding back the tears that threatened to spill.

‘You don’t have to be strong. As long as you come back alive…’

Then she gently stroked Lian’s back and said, “Yes. Make sure you come back stronger.”

After saying goodbye to everyone, Lian climbed into the carriage. True to his nature, he did not look back. He only faced forward and ran toward what lay ahead.

Once the carriage began to move, those left behind felt as if the space where Lian had stood had suddenly gone empty.

That was how large his presence had been.

Shirone watched the carriage disappear into the distance and prayed.

‘Come back as a great swordsman, Lian. I’ll work hard too.’

May Lian’s future be prosperous.
