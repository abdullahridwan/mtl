Chapter 358  
Midwinter Night (1)  
~*~

It was winter, so the sun set early. The old castle ruins closed at 6 o’clock, so Dante stopped exploring and headed back to his lodgings.  

‘What’s the point of going in now? Maybe I should just have a beer.’  

At eighteen, he was old enough to buy alcohol outside. Strong liquor wasn’t his preference, but Dante liked to sip beer while traveling.  

The shopping district was crowded with people escaping the tourist spots. As he walked down the street looking for a place to drink comfortably, he noticed a young woman protesting in front of a bar entrance on the third floor.  

Her striking outfit caught Dante’s attention. She wore a loose dress with sleeves down to her wrists, a white sash fastened around her waist. Her robe reached her ankles, and her skirt was red.  

Judging by the large backpack that didn’t suit her slim figure, she had either traveled a long time or come from far away. Maybe mid-twenties?  

She had a beautiful appearance, with pure white skin that seemed never to have seen sunlight. Her black hair was tied high on her head and hung down to her waist.  

Even now, her outfit looked like it would freeze her to death, but it was a delightful sight for the drunken crowd.  

However, she preached her message without caring how women looked at her.  

“The end of the world is coming. It’s dangerous here. Everyone, leave the castle quickly.”  

‘The end? What is she talking about all of a sudden?’  

Dante didn’t take her seriously.  

There were people who gave up everything, even their lives, because they were obsessed with pseudo-religions. The legend of the Ice Queen itself was a festival tale, so it made no sense that it was dangerous.  

Not a single person seemed to listen seriously, as if the tourists shared the same skepticism.  

“If it’s the end anyway, isn’t it the same wherever you go? Where would you go if you left here? Surely you’re not asking me to die in the arms of some sickly wife waiting at home? Fuhahaha!”  

“I’ve heard and seen it. That’s never going to happen. Miss, if you want attention, why don’t you stop talking nonsense and dance?”  

The woman looked them straight in the face without changing expression, perhaps used to this kind of mockery, and confronted them.  

“It’s not a lie. This is an oracle from the goddess Achaeans, who controls the melody of time.”  

“Achaeans? I’ve never heard of that religion.”  

“You have to believe me! Sooner or later, the dawn of the apocalypse will come here. Everyone will die!”  

“Okay, okay. I’ll trust you, so let’s go inside. Let’s talk while drinking.”  

As a tall man hugged her by the shoulders, she shouted, pushing him away with both hands.  

“Don’t put your ungodly hands on me!”  

“Ugh! This lady really—don’t do that. Come in. What kind of bad person am I?”  

The woman reached into a cylinder slung across the bottom of her backpack, pulled out something, and thrust it into the man’s neck.  

At first glance, it looked like a quiver, but inside was a thin iron rod about fifty centimeters long. The tip was sharp like an awl, and inside the ring on the other end was a small bell that jingled.  

Feeling the real stabbing force, the giant hurriedly removed his hand and stepped back.  

“Oops, my temper.”  

As the scene unfolded, tourists who had previously ignored her began to gather, interested.  

Dante heard the men behind them exchanging trivial jokes.  

“She’s crazy. But she looks fine. How about we play with her today?”  

“Arthur, you could bleed dealing with a woman like that. Who knows what might happen?”  

“It’s a pity, though. She’s a rare beauty.”  

Dante studied the woman carefully.  

“Achaeans…”  

Dante, who had a strong interest in psychics, had never heard that name before.  

Well, there were tens of thousands of religions in the world, so it was impossible to know them all.  

The woman repeated her message like a parrot.  

“The end is coming. Hurry and leave the old castle ruins! Otherwise, everyone will die. What I say is true!”  

“Will leaving solve anything?”  

People’s eyes turned to Dante.  

She gained more attention because he was the first to take her seriously.  

Stepping forward, Dante said,  

“If the apocalypse really comes, wouldn’t there have to be a solution? I don’t think running away will end it.”  

The woman confirmed that Dante was younger than her, then turned to the crowd with a disappointed expression.  

“I am Totemist Lyria, a mage of the Achaeans Shrine. An edict will be attempted here tonight. Failure could be dangerous. So please leave as soon as possible.”  

Dante paid more attention to her words about being a mage than to her feelings of being ignored.  

Mages worked within the Order of the Spirit, and the magic circles they used had many similarities to magic circles.  

That’s why Dante, who majored in information magic, also studied spirits, even if only superficially.  

If a magician materializes a phenomenon through an equal exchange, the mage strengthens one aspect of reason with the power of will.  

The two were similar but different.  

If fire needed to be extinguished, a wizard would create water. But a mage suppresses the will of fire by strengthening the will of water.  

This governs the balance of the source.  

Mages called this the law.  

It was an ability to encompass a broader and wider range of phenomena, though it lacked the sharpness of magical transformation.  

Nevertheless, the reason it didn’t receive attention in the western world was that the power of the law itself was not logical.  

Mages—specifically white mages—were those who fought to destroy evil in the world.  

However, good and evil were nothing more than artificial distinctions made after humans were born.  

But how could mages seal evil?  

Due to the ambiguity of this proof, people disparaged the law as an imitation of magic, or alternative magic.  

“There is no time! Get out before the day ends. Your life is at stake!”  

Lyria appealed desperately, but no one listened.  

Dante looked around, and when no one stepped forward, he approached her again.  

“Can I help you?”  

Lyria sighed softly without looking back at Dante.  

‘Kids these days,’ she thought.  

It wasn’t the first time she’d met a boy pestering her while she went about her rites.  

Being a magician itself was a job that sparked quite a bit of curiosity among young people.  

Looking at the boy’s appearance—handsome face, good style, even ear piercings—she thought, ‘But this time, you picked the wrong opponent, kid.’  

Lyria glared at Dante with cold eyes.  

“It’s not for a kid like you to intervene. You should go home soon, too, if you don’t want to offend your parents.”  

Dante was about to reply when one of the onlookers said,  

“Uh? That kid… isn’t that Dante? Air Servant Dante, the most promising wizard in the kingdom.”  

The middle-aged man recognized Dante’s face thanks to his work at the top of the magic material supply chain.  

Even if they weren’t wizards, most people involved in magic-related work read academic journals.  

There were hundreds of types of magical weapons developed each year, and it was necessary to analyze students’ needs to dominate popular products.  

“Yes? That boy’s a wizard? Doesn’t look like it.”  

“No, he’s still a student. But from what I’ve heard, his skills are ones even pros can’t handle carelessly.”  

Magicians were mysterious beings to common people.  

Furthermore, the title of the kingdom’s best in any field wasn’t given lightly, so people’s attention was focused.  

Lyria finally softened her expression and looked at Dante.  

“The best student in the kingdom?”  

Dante licked his lips and shrugged.  

“No, what… Are you saying I’m number two now?”  

The man at the magic guild also knew that a boy named Shirone had defeated Dante.  

But nostalgia for a champion was not easily forgotten.  

Dante had boasted an undefeated record since childhood, so his popularity hadn’t cooled.  

Lyria didn’t care whether Dante was number one or two. As a mage with some expertise, what mattered was that she was taken seriously for the first time.  

Dante pointed to the third-floor tavern and said,  

“This place is a bit different. Shall we go in and have a drink?”  

Lyria looked at Dante suspiciously.  

Even if he was an excellent wizard, flirting like this was a different story.  

“If you’re thinking about making a deal, find someone else.”  

“What are you talking about? Work?”  

“Then why are you trying to help me?”  

“My major is magic circles. Will that deal with the sealed margins, too? I’m interested. It might be useful for my major.”  

“A magic circle?”  

If the boy’s words were true, he could surely help her work.  

Besides, if he was the kingdom’s best prospect, at least at the student level, didn’t that mean he was the best at handling magic circles?  

“Great. Let’s go in. But I won’t drink.”  

“As you like. I just want a beer.”  

Lyria brushed the snow from her hair piled on her head and followed Dante into the tavern.  

“This… was taken away by a kid who doesn’t even have blood on his head.”  

The old castle site was not only a dating spot for lovers but also a place for lonely singles to find relationships.  

Numerous young men who had tried to stake their chances on Lyria looked at the two with envious expressions.  

* * *  

8 o’clock in the evening.  

The old castle site was so quiet.  

Moonlight shimmered down on the statue made of ice.  

Even the laughter echoing from the distant shopping district could be heard reverently here.  

Breaking the silence of the night, two figures swiftly jumped over a collapsed wall and entered the deserted castle grounds.  

Dante’s expression was serious.  

If the story at the tavern was true, the old castle was never safe.  

She said, ‘You see the future? Is that really possible?’  

According to Lyria, the head of the Achaeans was a 70-year-old woman named Poriter.  

From birth, she had the ability to imagine what would happen in the future, like déjà vu.  

Rumors circulated that she was mentally ill when young. Some even suspected her of megalomania.  

However, what was the probability that a future scene that suddenly came to mind would coincide with reality by mere coincidence?  

One day, at eighteen, Poriter realized she had seen the village burned down by bandits exactly as she had imagined.  

She had the ability to see the future.  

She couldn’t explain how she foresaw events, but it wasn’t foresight in the usual sense.  

Rather, just as a person walking into the future recalls the past, images like old memories suddenly arose.  

“Did someone like that say there would be a disaster at the old castle ruins?”  

Lyria nodded, her eyes full of trust.  

“A disaster is not just a disaster. She said there would be a being who could bring about the end of the world.”  

“It’s the end of the world. If you’re up against someone like that, there’s nothing we can do, right?”  

“No. That’s why Professor Poriter is great. The future can change. I don’t know what kind of disaster it is, but we just need to deal with it before it manifests. You remove the egg before it hatches.”  

“Right. Have you thought of a way?”  

“I’m going to use necromantic sorcery. His energy permeates this place now. After materializing it, I will immediately lock it in the barracks.”  

“But will it be easy? What if it’s stronger than expected?”  

Lyria put her hand into the barrel she wore behind her waist, took out her totem, and shook her wrist to ring the bell.  

“That’s why I, the totemist, was dispatched. This totem has a birch wick embedded in it. If you strengthen the power of the law with this, it will be caught.”  

Lyria slammed her totem into the ground, then moved away and pulled out another totem.  

“It’s true there was a shortage of manpower because this was a sudden oracle. There is much more evil in the world than people think. Not only me, but most ancestral spirits are wandering the world without even being able to enter their homes.”  

Lyria drew a huge circle twenty meters in diameter using twenty totems and walked toward the center.  

Dante asked, following her,  

“I’ve been curious before—how do you do it?”  

“Yes? What?”  

“If it’s a magic circle that grants physical restraint, I can design it too. But isn’t that what the sealed margins are like? They only react to evil. When something enters the sealed margin, how do you tell if it’s good or bad? What is the definition of evil?”
