Chapter 392  
Worst Project (1)  
~*~

Shirone opened his eyes. His head throbbed for a moment, then a refreshing clarity washed through him.

He was lying in a recovery room at the Magic Association. The capsule was large enough to hold one adult, with a glass canopy overhead and soft cushions beneath him.

Shirone blinked a few times. The glass canopy slid aside, and Flew’s haggard face appeared in his narrow field of vision.

“How do you feel? Are you all right?”

“Ah, yes. What happened?”

“You lost consciousness in the bunker. You forced your magic too hard. Next time, reduce the size of your Spirit Zone if you have to and increase its density instead. That will help prevent your brain from overloading.”

Flew’s voice was gentler than before. It felt a little awkward to Shirone, but he said nothing. He could see the sadness in her eyes.

“Thank you.”

Shirone sat up.

Capsules identical to the one he had been lying in were lined up around the room. Most of their glass canopies were open, but someone was still asleep in one corner.

Flew glanced that way and said, “That’s one of the duty officers. Sleep capsules enhance mental activity, but don’t rely on them too much. They aren’t omnipotent.”

Shirone climbed out of the capsule and stretched. His head felt as clear as if he had just woken from a long, deep sleep.

“How much time passed?”

“Two hours. Your recovery was surprisingly fast. I expected you to be out for about six.”

Confident in his mental endurance, Shirone loosened up with satisfaction. Then his gaze slowly turned to Flew. Unlike a few hours ago, there was now a strange distance between them.

“Um…”

“Follow me. The Association President wants to see you.”

Before he could speak, Flew turned and walked toward the exit. Shirone followed her to the lift platform. While they waited, she checked the floor number.

“I’m telling you in case you didn’t hear. You passed.”

Shirone had lost consciousness immediately after casting the Photon Cannon, so he had not had time to hear Gaold’s decision. Still, from Flew’s subdued expression, he had expected as much.

“Thank you.”

“You don’t need to thank me. You’ll be going to Heaven with the Association President.”

Flew told him what she knew.

In any case, from this point on, Shirone would be hearing secrets of the highest level, things even she would never be allowed to access. There was no longer any need for her to watch her tongue.

“And the rest of us will be the follow-up team. Of course that includes me, but even Kang Nan won’t be able to directly assist Gaold. That’s how high-level this operation must be.”

Flew turned to Shirone.

“I don’t know exactly what he intends to do. But Gaold said it’s a project he has devoted his entire life to. If he has helpers, they’ll be the best wizards available, and there will only be a few of them. An elite unit. And you’re one of them.”

Realizing what Flew meant, Shirone felt apologetic. Being able to take part in something alongside someone as powerful as Gaold was one of the highest honors a wizard could receive.

“I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to upset you.”

Ataraxia was a secret technique Shirone had risked his life to acquire. But there were countless wizards in the world who would gladly throw their lives away if it meant raising their level. That was why he could not simply act proud of having been chosen.

Flew smiled and shook her head.

To be honest, she had looked down on Shirone for the same reason. It had wounded her pride that he had been chosen by Gaold because of a spell he had obtained from someone else.

But after watching him fight Gaold in the bunker, her thoughts had changed. Flew, too, would have died for Gaold, but she did not have the courage to risk her life the way Shirone had.

“Don’t think that way. You deserve the Association President’s approval.”

Hearing those words from Flew was different from hearing them from anyone else. It had only been a short time, but the moments they had spent together as senior and junior flashed through Shirone’s mind like a kaleidoscope.

Sensing the warmth in Shirone’s eyes, Flew stepped back with a disgusted expression. As if hastily returning to her old self, she pointed a finger at him like she was driving back an enemy force that had breached her defenses.

“But you still have a long way to go! I acknowledge Ataraxia’s power, but you need to refine it further and compensate for the risks. A real wizard has to be able to respond to countless unexpected variables.”

Shirone did not argue. Flew’s ability to subdue the vampires in Gold City had been more than enough to prove she deserved the Magic Association employee card hanging around her neck.

“Yes. I’ll work hard, senior.”

“Hmph!”

The elevator doors opened with a clear electronic chime.

Shirone arrived on the eighteenth floor. Any employee could come this far, but passing through the door to the Association President’s office was another matter entirely.

As Shirone stood before the door and took a deep breath, Flew said, “Listen. Beyond this door is the Association President.”

Shirone looked back at her.

“Nothing you hear in there must ever leak out. It’s different from what I know. I won’t ask you anything afterward, either. So don’t let your guard down.”

Shirone could feel Flew’s resolve. He, too, had risked his life to reach this point. He wondered what Gaold was about to tell him, and at the same time, he hoped he would be able to bear it.

Flew knocked on the door in Shirone’s stead.

“Shirone is here.”

The door opened, and Kang Nan showed herself. After first checking the hallway, she turned to Shirone.

“You’re here. You recovered quickly.”

“Yes. Thank you for your concern.”

“Come in. Flew, go back down and handle your work. Good job.”

Flew was not disappointed. From this point on, it was not an area where she could intervene.

“I only did what I had to do. Then, excuse me.”

After Flew left, Kang Nan opened the door wide.

Gaold, freshly groomed, was buried in an old sofa inside the Association President’s office. The wallpaper carried the acrid smell of cigars.

“You’re here? Sit.”

After Shirone sat across from Gaold, Kang Nan served tea.

No one spoke. All three of them knew who had to begin.

“Miro…”

Gaold finally opened his mouth.

“She was my schoolmate.”

“Yes, I know.”

“She was a strange kid. No, maybe stupid is the better word. One day she said she could create life. So I told her to try it, and she said she needed a cadaver.”

Gaold began with a story about Miro. Shirone perked up as well, since he was as curious about Miro as he was about Gaold.

“So I snuck into the anatomy lab and brought out a cadaver. It was one used for harvesting tissue. Of course, if I’d been caught, I would’ve been expelled, but she didn’t care at all. Then I took it to her laboratory, and there was a fish tank on the table. Strange devices were set up around it, hoses tangled everywhere and shoved into it…”

Gaold waved his hands vaguely, as if describing the mess was too bothersome.

“Yes, I think I understand the scene.”

“She put all kinds of bizarre things into the tank. It was disgusting to watch. I said I was leaving, but she grabbed me and wouldn’t let go. Said she still had something for me to do. In the end, I got stuck there.”

It was hard to believe that there had been a time when Gaold, who now seemed capable of overwhelming the world with a glance, had been dragged around by Miro. Just imagining it made Shirone smile.

“So everything was ready. Then Miro handed me a little cup and told me to collect a semen sample.”

“Cough! Cough!”

Unfortunately, Shirone had been drinking tea at that exact moment, and he choked.

“S-so… did you give it to her?”

“Am I insane? Of course I yelled no and ran away—no, I left. But the next day, somehow, she had gotten some. She said she bought it. The experiment resumed. When she boiled everything together, the water became truly vile. Miro called it primordial soup. I made a bet with her. If anything came out of that tank, I’d run around the school in nothing but my underwear.”

Shirone smiled. It seemed school life had not changed much then or now.

“A month after the bet, I went back to the lab. Miro was completely worked up and out of her mind with excitement. I couldn’t understand it. Did she really think something would come out of that? To me, she was just an eccentric kid, and I’d only been humoring her.”

“Haha. I see.”

At that moment, all emotion vanished from Gaold’s face.

“But there was something.”

“…Yes?”

“The disgusting liquid had already dried up, and the tank was covered in sticky slime. And inside it were living creatures I had never seen before.”

“Couldn’t she have brought them from somewhere else…?”

Gaold shook his head.

“No. I thought the same thing at first. But no matter what creature a human imagines, patterns based on existing knowledge will appear. Those things were completely different. They were creatures born in a truly unique environment.”

Shirone could not believe it.

But Gaold’s eyes were sincere. Even now, after he had become a first-class archmage, his eyes trembled with the shock of recalling that moment.

“If you had been there, I’m sure you would have felt the same thing I did. Not something you understand with your head, but a sense of alienness felt by instinct. Those creatures were completely different from us.”

“How did Ms. Miro… create them?”

Was it possible for a human to create life? At present, the furthest mankind had reached was the Harvest, a magical creation capable of movement. No one called a Harvest a living creature.

“Miro never explained it. She told me that what happened that day must never become known. But now I have a vague idea. Miro is the greatest scale wizard in the world. In a specific space she created, she could probably turn time back hundreds of millions of years in an instant.”

“Are you saying primitive life evolved there?”

“Possibly. Anyway, that isn’t what matters. It also doesn’t matter that I had to run through school in my underwear. Thanks to that, I was branded a pervert.”

Those things really don’t seem to matter.

Shirone barely swallowed the words that rose to his throat.

Understanding how Shirone felt, Gaold smiled. Of course, that did not mean what he had just said was a joke.

“Miro knew those creatures wouldn’t live long. In the end, they died a week later. Miro cried a lot that day. You’d think, if she was going to cry like that, why create them in the first place? But she was that kind of weirdo. You could never get a handle on her.”

Shirone nodded. Just from Gaold’s anecdote, he could tell Miro had not been an ordinary person.

“It wasn’t just living creatures. She was a creative genius. She made all sorts of inventions no one expected. When I asked how she could do it, she said she could see it. If she heard the name of an object, the principle behind it came to mind.”

“The name… if she heard it?”

One thing immediately came to Shirone’s mind.

“Yes. She may have reached the Akashic Records. In any case, Miro quickly became prominent in the wizarding world. She opened the Immortal Function early, and she believed she could twist space-time with magnetic force. But she wasn’t interested in success. She was a woman whose head was full of curiosity. That was how the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Society was formed.”

“Yes. I belong to it too.”

Shirone felt proud to be part of the same society.

“Heh. Congratulations on ruining your life. There were three founding members: me, Miro, and another fool named Sein.”

“Sein has Servant Syndrome, right? He made the master key of Estas.”

“Well… I’m not especially fond of him.”

Gaold skipped the explanation entirely. Shirone found it regrettable, since he had wanted to know where Sein was now and what he was doing.

“And…”

Gaold’s expression hardened.

“From here on, everything you hear is top secret.”

Shirone swallowed.

“As a result, Miro’s genius brought about the worst possible situation. The twelve monarchs who hold the greatest power in the world—the ‘Holy War’—began to keep an eye on Miro. They had long been watching the affairs of Heaven. That was how the Valkyries of the Temple Army were created.”

Shirone’s expression darkened. The reason Orkamp, the king of Khazra, had summoned him was to use Ataraxia to exert influence within the Valkyries.

“The Crusade realized the final war was imminent and sought an heir to Guffin. In that process, they obtained Miro. A girl with a bright future ahead of her quickly became humanity’s breakwater. Can you imagine it? The feeling of being trapped forever in a world of your own, carrying the fate of never growing old and never dying?”

Shirone did not dare answer.

“At last, the trial began in the presence of renowned leaders from around the world. It was the day called the Twenty Judges. The Empire’s first-class envoys, the Pope of Lamie, the person called the Living Saint, the head of a human rights organization, and so on. To maintain balance, Miro’s teacher, Alpheas, and Olivia, the auditor of the Tormia Kingdom Teachers’ Association, were also given voting rights. The result was sixteen in favor, one opposed, and three abstentions. In the end, it was decided that Miro would be sent away.”

“How could they do that…?”

For Miro, it was a matter of weighing all of humanity’s lives against her own. Even if the decision had been voluntary, it made no sense for only twenty people to gather and decide the end of one person’s life when the truth itself was still uncertain.

“Yes. The whole process was too complicated for me, but to the world, it seemed to flow far too easily. No one objected to the proposition that all humanity could be saved by sacrificing one person. After many hectic days, the day of departure finally came. And Miro…”

Gaold’s voice abruptly quieted, then disappeared. His gaze fixed on empty space, and he sank into thought.

His breathing grew rough. Blood vessels rose in his eyes. Tears, terribly out of place on his fierce face, welled in his unblinking eyes.

—Please save Miro! I have lived my whole life as God’s servant without shame! I can offer you my soul! So please, Miro alone…!

—Are you Gaold?

—Please…! Bless this humble servant! God’s mercy…!

—God will forgive us as well.

Shirone looked around in alarm. The equipment in the room rattled and trembled as if an earthquake had struck.

What is this?

When Shirone turned his head toward Gaold, his breath caught. Perhaps his tears had already dried, but Gaold’s eyes were now filled with an indescribable storm of emotion.

The scenery changed, and a colossal blaze roared up.

It was a pressure unlike anything Shirone had felt in the bunker. The difference was that this time, Gaold himself was inside that hell.

Gaold’s eyes rolled back, showing the whites. His face twisted completely, his teeth bared through lips pulled into a grotesque snarl.

Ugh… This is too much.

Shirone held on with the power of incarnation, but this was not an attack on his mind. Real physical force was at work. If this continued, the eighteenth floor itself might explode.

Through the flames, Kang Nan’s low, sharp voice rang out.

“Association President.”

As if it had been a lie, the hellish scenery vanished. Gaold narrowed his eyes as if he had returned to his senses.

Only then could Shirone breathe. He had not even realized his entire body was drenched in cold sweat.

Gaold leaned back against the sofa as if nothing had happened. But Shirone knew he would not be able to hear more about the Twenty Judges from him.

“Anyway, that’s how Miro was sent away. Since that day, I have been planning a project. Now only its execution remains. That is why I asked to see you.”

Shirone calmed his pounding heart and prepared himself to listen.

Gaold watched him for a moment, then suddenly leaned forward as if springing up and thrust his face toward Shirone.

“I will destroy Miro’s space-time.”

“…”

Shirone took Gaold’s words into his mind and analyzed their meaning.

Once he understood, the feeling was far more chilling than when he had first heard it.

“Miro’s space-time… destroy it?”

It meant bringing Miro back. From Gaold’s point of view, that was only natural.

But if that happened…

This world would be pushed to the brink of destruction.

“If Miro’s space-time disappears…”

“Yes. Heaven’s army will attack. Eight archangels, dozens of ordinary angels, hundreds of fallen angels, thousands of Maras, giants and fairies, and troops armed with the firepower of ancient magic, schema, and mecha weapons. Whichever side wins, the world we live in will be devastated. That is what I’m telling you.”

Gaold did not hide the truth. His resolve, unchanged even once over the past twenty years, would not waver now.

Shirone rubbed his face with both hands. He was frustrated that his unease had proven true. No—it was a far greater problem than he had imagined from the beginning.

This was rescuing one woman while holding every life in the world as collateral.

He did not panic or make a fuss. When he faced a problem too enormous for him to handle, his mind instead became cold.

“Is there a way?”

If Miro could have been brought back easily, Gaold would have done it long ago. In the end, Shirone could only assume there was some condition involved that would be extremely difficult to fulfill.

“Of course there is.”

Gaold smiled pleasantly. Seeing Shirone cut straight to the heart of the matter in any situation made him trust the boy even more than before.
