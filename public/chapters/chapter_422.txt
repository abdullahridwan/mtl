Shirone did not look back. No matter how much he turned the situation over in his mind, he couldn’t figure out why Maya was acting this way.

“Maya.”

“Can we have a meeting while eating delicious food? I’ve wanted to do it for a long time.”

“Maya.”

At the second call, Maya finally turned around.

“Yes?”

Shirone closed his mouth. The moment their eyes met, the words he had prepared scattered in his head. He couldn’t say anything that wasn’t clear, but the problem was that he didn’t know what was clear anymore.

“If you’re fighting the Screamers because of us, you don’t have to. I’ve been worried about you lately.”

He knew it sounded like he was borrowing Iruki’s words, but if this simple conversation could resolve things, there was no need to overcomplicate it.

“Ah, no. It’s a full evaluation anyway. Rather, I feel sorry for being a burden.”

The moment Maya turned the doorknob to leave, Shirone realized he couldn’t stand the distance between them any longer.

“Then why are you avoiding me?”

Despite the sharpness in his voice, Maya wasn’t surprised. She just offered an awkward smile and scratched her cheek, just as she always did.

“No matter how much I think about it, I don’t know. Why are you avoiding me these days? I mean, I can feel it.”

“I… Did I do something wrong?”

“It’s not like that.”

“Then why should I be nice to you?”

Maya’s steps halted. The words felt like a sharp blade hooking into her chest.

“What do you want from me? What would you like me to do? Do I always have to smile while looking only at you?”

Shirone still couldn’t understand why Maya was pushing him away. If there was a misunderstanding, he wanted to clear it up. But this wasn’t a misunderstanding.

“The way you treat me is your freedom, but…”

Maya smiled bitterly. Amy’s earlier words about dying inside suddenly made a little more sense. Yet, she had no intention of taking back what she had said that day.

“Then, Shirone… can you love me?”

Shirone’s expression hardened.

At his expected reaction, Maya lowered her gaze, her eyes clouded with sadness. It was the same feeling as the wind that had blown through the music room that day, carrying unspoken words.

*Shirone, I’m different from Amy. I’m not cool, and I’m not strong. If I can’t hold onto you, every day will feel like hell. You’re the only one I want. If you can’t love me, then I’ll just have to let it go.*

Shirone’s trembling eyes gradually steadied. His face, which had been startled like a rabbit’s, slowly calmed.

Maya steeled herself. Even so, the anticipation made her heart race. What would Shirone answer? *I love you?* Or maybe, *Actually, I liked you too?*

All sorts of hopeful words drifted through her mind, but she already knew the outcome.

*Sorry.*

She already felt terrible. She knew she wouldn’t be able to shake this off for a while. She would spend the night in tears.

*But that’s it. It ends here.*

Having made her resolution, Maya lifted her head. However, the moment she saw the determined look on Shirone’s face, her heart plummeted.

“Maya, I…”

“Ah, hahahaha!”

Shirone’s face flushed with embarrassment. He looked genuinely flustered, even tearing up slightly, and Maya stepped forward, wiping the corner of his eye with her index finger.

“Just kidding, kidding! Ugh, you’re so naive.”

“Are you kidding?”

“It was like that at the graduation ceremony, and it’s happening again. If you keep reacting like this, will it ever reach Amy?”

Shirone was at a loss. He felt like he was standing in the middle of an unreal world.

“But you haven’t been talking to me lately…”

“Ah, my head was so complicated that I didn’t have time to think about anything else. I have high expectations at home, and my grades keep dropping. Sorry. Never again in the future! I won’t pretend like I don’t know.”

Shirone’s pulse went numb. He felt he needed to organize his thoughts, but Maya didn’t give him a second to spare.

“Now! Let’s hurry! I’ll wait for Iruki. Occupy the Highlands! Are you sure you’ll win?”

“Yes? Uh, that’s how it should be.”

Maya gave Shirone a firm push on the back and left the music room. She crossed the threshold, her eyes clenched shut, her teeth gritted.

*Haa, I came in and threw it all away. What should I do now?*

It felt like stepping across the threshold of hell.

* * *

In the mountains northeast of Alpheas Magic School, a cave descended twenty meters underground. Beyond the complex labyrinth within lay the base of the Golden Ring, with Fermi as its chairman.

It was a spacious, splendid chamber that could easily pass for a mansion. Delicacies lined the tables, and waiters in tuxedos circulated with wine, refilling empty glasses. In the center, beautiful dancers moved gracefully, while seven musicians performed a calm, elegant piece.

The Golden Ring employed forty top-notch staff members. Labor costs alone exceeded seven hundred gold per day, and all of this luxury was reserved for just five members.

Fermi lounged on a sofa, savoring a glass of wine. On the carpet beside him, a white-furred tiger slept peacefully on a leash. On the four sofas arranged around him, the members of the so-called Fermi Party sat in silence.

“Finally, the monthly profit exceeded seven million gold. Breaking ten million is also within sight.”

It was an unimaginable sum for a student, but Fermi was simply bored.

The quick-witted members kept their mouths shut. On a day like this, when an emergency meeting was called, they knew better than to speak out of turn.

“Arian Shirone…”

As soon as Fermi brought him up, the members began reciting prepared responses, as if they had been waiting for this exact moment.

“Do I need to care? I’ll have to step on him someday, but why don’t you focus more on business?”

“I think the same. He’s just a kid who doesn’t know the world. Can’t we just graduate and leave him behind?”

You didn’t tear down a house just to catch a fly. Nor was there any guarantee that ignoring him wouldn’t backfire, especially since Fermi had already shown his displeasure.

“That’s exactly why you shouldn’t let it go any further.”

“Fermi, aren’t you stressing yourself out? Provocations can be ignored.”

“Provocations can be ignored. But it’s a risk. The danger of the Golden Ring’s continued existence.”

They followed Fermi’s lead, but they couldn’t sympathize with his classification of Shirone as a threat.

“Of course, Shirone is a great rookie. But you know what? We’re not just in this for the money. We’re here for the skills. I have enough control over the graduation exam.”

Fermi twirled his wine glass, his eyes cold.

“Do you know why I can’t make more money? It’s because you don’t realize that the problem we’re facing has been brewing for a long time. Or maybe you know it and are ignoring it. A risk today becomes an unmanageable disaster tomorrow. If we don’t prepare now, there will be no solution when it arrives.”

The members fell silent. Again, Fermi was right.

“From now on, categorize Shirone as the highest level of threat. I’ll handle the analysis myself. I heard you’re going up against the Screamers. What about their teammates?”

Hercy reported the results of their surveillance.

“Screamer, Pony, Luman, Suabi, and Eider. The team was finalized yesterday.”

“Hmm. Luman and Suabi. A two-support, magician-killer combination.”

As expected, the Screamers had mobilized all their networks to form this team. It was surprising that Dorothy was left out, but it was likely she had declined. She was an incredibly sharp woman.

“With pride as strong as theirs, they’ll want to prove themselves. Their strategy will be completely different from standard tactics. It won’t be easy for Shirone to defeat a team like the Screamers, even with Maya.”

In a direct confrontation outside of a teamwork-oriented mission like the annihilation exercise, the Screamers’ deadly strategy was quite powerful. In a previous engagement, Shirone had annihilated over three hundred monsters in three minutes and twenty-eight seconds, but in another, the Screamers’ carry had killed over one hundred and fifty monsters entirely on his own.

They hyper-focused on a single powerhouse, causing a collapse in balance.

This strategy would shine even more in a five-on-five matchup, and Shirone’s team would need to establish perfect balance to win. In such a situation, Maya’s presence could easily become a liability.

“That’s not enough. I’m going to use this opportunity to uncover all of Shirone’s strengths. I’ll scrape away even what’s hidden beneath the surface.”

“What are you going to do? Anyway, the overall evaluation carries a high score, so the participants are already pushing themselves. The Screamer team is fully prepared.”

Fermi tapped the sofa a couple of times. A bluish aura rippled from his body, and dark objects beneath his raised hand made a gurgling noise, bouncing back and forth rapidly.

*Ting!*

A casino chip spun and bounced with a sharp sound. Fermi caught the vertically falling chip horizontally and tossed it back to Hercy.

A whirlpool pattern was etched into the center of the chip, bordered by alternating black and white segments.

“This…”

Hercy looked at Fermi in surprise.

It was a fairly rare chip, rarely used for casual purposes. Even converted to market value, it was worth over four hundred thousand gold.

*He’s serious about this.*

Shirone would undoubtedly become a major threat in the future. Fermi would never produce a chip like this unless he was absolutely certain.

“Place a bet with it. The terms are simple: Shirone must push himself to the limit during the Occupy the Highlands mission. If he holds back even slightly, it will reveal his true capabilities.”

“Understood. Anything else?”

After a moment’s thought, the corners of Fermi’s lips curled into a vicious smile.

“Don’t hold back. Push them hard.”
