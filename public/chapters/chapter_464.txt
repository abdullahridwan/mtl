Chapter 464  
Opportunity for Revenge (6)  
~*~

Isgermont’s plasma was poured into one of the eight receptacles of the Fuzix Machine.

Seven slots remained.

Reisis planned to enhance Isgermont’s antithesis ability by combining it with the traits of three other creatures: Volthoom, Grabber, and Liston.

Volthoom, resembling a mosquito magnified a thousandfold, possessed a unique trait called *blood adhesion*—an affinity for the organism from which it had drawn blood. This was believed to be a psychological adaptation evolved to suppress hemolytic reactions. In the lab, it had been exploited as a tool for subjugating living beings through this very trait.

“I will now introduce Volthoom’s plasma,” Reisis announced.

As the plasmas of Grabber—known for levitation—and Liston—whose essence heightened biological activity—were added, the four distinct lifeforms swirled within the Fuzix Machine’s glass sphere, awaiting fusion.

Unlike anecdotal accounts of haphazard mixing, the machine required a dominant base type to manifest a coherent new being. Isgermont was designated the primary, with the other three serving as subtypes to amplify its antithesis power.

“Then let’s begin.”

Reisis pressed the activation button. The four half-filled plasmas were violently sucked into the central glass tube. Electricity surged through the vacuum-sealed chamber, and the liquid began to boil.

“Ohhh!”

A collective gasp filled the room.

Researchers who had once recoiled at the inhumanity of such biological experiments now found themselves transfixed—entranced by the ecstasy of creation, of forging life with their own hands.

A blinding white light erupted from the glass tube as electrical discharges crackled through it. Everyone flinched—except Lacey. Her eyes gleamed with rapture as she stared unblinkingly into the core.

With a sharp *crack*, the glass tube split into four pieces and shattered onto the floor.

“Success!”

The researchers stepped back in awe. The result defied all predictions.

Before them floated a massive eyeball, one meter in diameter, its surface covered by a thin, translucent epidermis that functioned as a lid. From the neural cluster at its rear, dark red tentacles unfurled and writhed like serpentine tails.

**Biological Weapon – Akamai (Prototype)**

A cold shiver ran through the laboratory.

Could this truly be called a living creature?

Staring at this organic construct—designed solely for human purpose, utterly divorced from natural environmental pressures—they wondered if there was anything in the world that *shouldn’t* be done.

“Haaaaa…”

Reisis’s red lips parted in a soft sigh.

*What a beautiful form.*

God had granted life the potential for infinite transformation.

“Huh!”

Her expression twisted—ecstasy giving way to sudden distortion.

Her red cloak flapped violently as something writhed beneath it.

A biologist approached, face pale with fear.

“Commander… we need to establish the contract now…”

Reisis quickly composed herself. The internal spasms—like her insides were being torn outward—were agonizing, but they subsided as quickly as they had come.

“Yes. I almost forgot.”

She slashed her wrist with a sharp nail and let her blood drip onto Akamai’s surface.

*Thud. Thud.*

Akamai’s massive iris contracted as the blood was absorbed through its epidermis.

Then, as if in recognition, it turned toward Lacey and blinked twice.

*Blood adhesion complete.*

“Is it a success?” someone asked.

“Of course,” Reisis replied. “It’s now my loyal servant. Now, let’s begin in earnest. Bring me Shirone’s clone.”

At her command, a researcher contacted the lower level. The floor split open, and an elevator rose, carrying Shirone’s clone.

The clone’s brain was empty—devoid of memory or higher cognition. It existed only on instinct, like a feral animal driven by base needs: hunger, survival, reproduction.

The researchers had implanted a device resembling an EEG into its eyes. When light was shone into the retinas, fragmented memories were transmitted—similar to the joint research once conducted by Arcane and Alfeas.

But the flaw was the same: clones infused with foreign memories collapsed mentally and died shortly after.

Not that it mattered to Reisis.

If Shirone could absorb all traits, he could simply consume them whole.

***Kwa-kwa-kwa-kwa-kwa-kwa-kwak!***

The *Berserk* spell detonated on the bed where the clone lay.

The shockwave threw researchers aside. The clone’s body erupted upward like a wild beast, instinctively seeking cover.

*What? Where am I?*

Shirone’s eyes darted left and right, scanning.

*Laboratory. Lacey. Strange device. Never seen this before.*

He couldn’t grasp the situation.

Just moments ago, he’d been lying beside Flu, undergoing an EEG. Now, he was here.

“What have you done to me?”

“Um… well…” Lacey tapped her lips, amused.

“I’d love to explain, but I won’t. I have a feeling I’ll be saying that a lot in the future.”

Reisis flicked a finger.

Akamai, floating in midair, activated its antithesis ability.

“Keugh!”

Shirone froze. It was as if the air itself had solidified into iron—his limbs locked, unable to move a single muscle.

“This… this is… ?”

“Apologies,” Reisis said coolly. “But you need intelligence to wield Nephilim power. This won’t last long.”

The *Immortal Function* began with insight—something impossible for a clone with only animal instincts.

Shirone clenched his teeth.

*You’re smiling while saying sorry?*

Trapped in Akamai’s restraining field, Shirone was carried and placed inside the Fuzix Machine’s glass sphere.

Unlike previous cadaver experiments, this required monitoring mental transformation. The method—inserting the subject whole and melting it directly into the plasma—was the most effective.

“Commander, what should we combine it with?”

A Nephilim with 100% affinity could theoretically assimilate any creature’s traits. To verify this, a being with advanced capabilities was ideal.

“Synthesize Akamai.”

“Yes, but… it’s only a prototype…”

“Doesn’t matter. This is the perfect opportunity to test Shirone’s clone.”

The biologist obeyed without further protest.

Once preparations were complete, plasma flooded the glass spheres containing both Akamai and Shirone.

Black liquid oozed down Shirone’s neck, covering his face. After a moment, the fluid level dropped, draining into the central glass tube. Shirone vanished—fully dissolved.

The researchers watched, breathless, as the central tube pulsed with blinding light.

*Main type: Shirone. Subtype: Akamai.*

Two organisms melted completely, merging into one.

“What… what?”

The glass tube split open.

Shirone stood within—eyes closed.

Even with Shirone as the core, combining two vastly different lifeforms should have caused visible change.

The biologist adjusted her glasses, frowning.

“Is it… just a human? Did the experiment fail?”

Then—Shirone’s eyes snapped open.

A flash of light burst from his left pupil, a color unlike his natural eye.

“Aaaagh!”

Researchers screamed, collapsing in agony as if their bones were being twisted.

Reisis leapt back, eyes wide with shock.

*Did he absorb only the functions?*

The changes that occurred in the plasma state were known only to those who had experienced it.

Reisis was one of the few in the world who had undergone plasma transformation multiple times.

In that state, thought ceased. But within the liquid—now a unified substance—countless signals flowed like neural currents.

*It’s an Ultima System.*

If one could control the unification signal during the plasma phase, it became possible to selectively integrate traits and forms.

*This is dangerous.*

No—*this is magnificent.*

The ability to possess the traits of any living creature in existence.

This was nothing short of reaching godhood.

“What have you done to me?” Shirone whispered.

Lacey’s lips trembled.

Shirone had taken full control of his consciousness. Akamai had been completely subsumed.

*I want more. That power.*

Lacey spread her arms and stepped forward.

“Shirone… let’s become one.”

Shirone activated the antithesis.

A crushing force slammed into Reisis, halting her advance.

Yet, she smiled.

This power… it was on an entirely different level from Isgermont’s antithesis.

Shirone unleashed a photon cannon.

Reisis was hurled into the wall. The ceiling cracked. Dust billowed with each flash of energy.

“Sigh… sigh…”

Shirone exhaled heavily, eyes wide with shock as he stared ahead.

From the smoke, Reisis emerged—half her face grotesquely transformed.

Her eyes bulged as if trying to burst from her skull. Her pupils narrowed to reptilian slits. Her mouth stretched wide, lined with triple the number of teeth a human should have—jagged fangs protruding past her lower jaw.

“Kiyi… Shi… lone…”

The sound scraped like metal on stone, sending chills down Shirone’s spine.

He raised his palm—reaching for Armand.

*Armand…?*

Only then did he realize—Kubrick was gone.

Impossible. No one could have taken it. He hadn’t lost consciousness since the EEG.

*Could it be… I’m not the real Shirone?*

He retraced his steps.

He hadn’t blacked out—but the moment after the EEG, everything was a blank.

The liquid in the anecdotal sake. The sensation of dissolving, then being reconstructed through the Ultima System.

*It’s a clone.*

That was it. The original body wasn’t here. Kubrick remained with the true Shirone.

And Reisis was using *this* body for her experiments.

*A biological bond, like the anecdotal sake…*

A sudden, searing insight pierced his mind.

*Armand! Armand!*

Something lashed out from behind Reisis’s cloak.

In an instant—Shirone’s head was severed from his body.

“Whoa.”

Reisis casually discarded the detached head.

The sight was grotesque, but the researchers were too terrified to react.

Just like Shirone, the moment they acknowledged their own bodies, parts began flying off.

“Double clone production from now on,” the biologist urged, voice trembling. “We need to analyze the changes immediately.”

“It’s too dangerous,” another protested. “Shirone controlled the biotic bond. If the commander is consumed in return, no creature can stop him.”

Reisis wasn’t pleased with the hesitation.

“For now, use only Type A clones—the ones before Shirone went to Babel. We can proceed without the Ultima System.”

“But ultimately, the main body is Type B. The risk remains.”

“Doesn’t matter. If I set myself as the main type, even the Ultima System won’t be able to override me.”

According to myth, Ilhwa’s liquor was created to destroy the Ultima System—but how much of that was true remained unknown.

Reisis smiled bitterly.

“Even if I’m eaten.”

“Yes?”

“If Shirone and I become one… the result won’t be me, nor him. It will be something entirely new. That’s what I want. I won’t stop. Continue as planned.”

No matter the base type, the end result would always be a completely new being.

To her, identity didn’t matter—only evolution.

But the biologist hesitated.

Reisis believed fusion was enough.

But the Ultima System didn’t just combine—it *controlled* the biological integration.

And they had no understanding of how.

*Yes… Shirone can’t reverse it. At best, merging with the commander is the limit.*

Yet her anxiety stemmed from years of experience in this very lab.

To assume nothing would go wrong was to admit their knowledge was confined to a narrow scope.

Variables always existed beyond understanding.

And when something moved in that darkness…

*Is it truly safe to proceed?*

Bioshock happened.
