Chapter 363  
Wave of Rhythm (1)  
~*~

The next morning, Shirone and Amy toured the prison in the old castle.  

Time had worn it down, eroding the structure until it was half-exposed above the ground. Although magic had a long history, its academic definition was relatively recent. Eight hundred years ago, research on batteries was insufficient, so primitive magic was the main focus.  

At that time, the power of the Holy Church was so strong that it laid the foundation for the current Holy Magic. As a result, wizards were persecuted. Any ability that did not invoke the name of God was considered the power of the devil, and many wizards were abandoned by their families.  

The two were taken to a prison where wizards had been captured and tortured. In front of an apparatus that bound limbs and stretched the spine, Shirone shuddered with a chill.  

“Ugh, how do you even come up with a tool like this? It must have been unbearably painful.”  

“Wizards must have suffered a lot too. Magic isn’t something you can just choose. It’s a gifted talent now, but back then, it must have felt like a curse.”  

Shirone and Amy left the dungeon. When they climbed the stairs leading to the surface, everyone was staring at the sky. A huge dark cloud was rolling in from the opposite horizon.  

Winters in the Leth Mountains were famous for clear skies, as westerly winds rose through the mountains and created high pressure. Occasionally, localized cyclones would drop snow, but even visitors to the annual winter festival had never seen such thick, dark clouds.  

“What? It doesn’t look like it’s going to rain.”  

The wind wasn’t strong. Still, the dark clouds approached at high speed like smoke, covering the sky. The temperature began to drop rapidly.  

As the cold wave hit, even those bundled up thickly felt the chill, and anxiety finally showed on the faces of the tourists.  

“What, what? Is the weather going crazy?”  

Shirone and Amy tightened their collars and watched the situation. This was an abnormal temperature drop that couldn’t be explained as a simple weather change.  

“Amy, something’s wrong. In nature, the temperature can’t drop this quickly.”  

“Yeah. It’s like magic, right?”  

A thunderclap erupted from the Ice Queen’s castle. Then, a powerful wave surged forth, pushing the dark clouds away as it galloped off. The folds of the atmosphere rippled visibly along the grain.  

“Dangerous!”  

Shirone stepped in front of Amy. There wasn’t even time to dodge as the waves rushed in at high speed, sweeping through the outskirts of the crowd.  

“Move!”  

A woman in uniform stopped Shirone and Amy. She was the sorceress Lyria.  

She quickly moved her fingers, forming a sign. The barriers of the law spread as the three basic rites—prohibition, antiban, and suppression—connected in succession like flowing water.  

The moment the powerful wave swept through them, a shockwave exploded before Lyria’s eyes.  

“Wow!”  

Lyria’s body flew back like a cannonball.  

All the tourists stared at her. The wave had passed through, but she was the only one who actually took the shock.  

“What, what? What’s wrong?”  

Even after falling to the floor, Lyria rolled over. She lowered her stance as Dante appeared across from her and caught her against his chest.  

“Are you okay?”  

Leaning on Dante, Lyria bit her lip. She had issued numerous edicts before, but this was the first time she had ever used such a powerful law.  

Shirone and Amy spotted Dante and ran over. They had a hunch that the woman with Dante had protected them from something dangerous.  

“Dante, what is this? What’s going on?”  

Dante hesitated before answering and helped Lyria to her feet. Unlike magic, law had no physical impact. Whether Shirone and Amy were normal was something Lyria had to explain.  

“How are you? Did you succeed?”  

“It was close. How about you?”  

The wave of Yul spread quickly, and it was impossible for Lyria to defend both places. No matter how brilliant she was, she couldn’t stop the law if she couldn’t control the will of gold, half, and chain.  

Dante pulled a totem from her neck.  

“Thanks to this.”  

A pendant shaped like a monster’s face hung from a twisted birch bark collar. Though it looked ferocious, it was a guardian deity that chased away evil spirits.  

There was a diagonal crack along the forehead of the guardian deity, caused by exposure to the waves of the law.  

When Dante placed it in her palm, it snapped in two with a sharp sound.  

She held out the broken totem with a shy expression.  

“Didn’t I say there was only one? Sorry.”  

Lyria shook her head vigorously.  

“You don’t have to be sorry. Totems are like that. It means the law was so strong it split the Rakshasa. I’m just glad I stopped it.”  

Shirone couldn’t fully understand the conversation between the two, but the situation seemed serious, so she intervened.  

“Dante, what happened?”  

Lyria answered, “I was lucky. You’ll be fine.”  

Amy asked with a frustrated expression, “So, what the hell is going on?”  

Lyria looked between Shirone and Amy.  

It was already expected that the cerebral body that had gained self-awareness would alter the laws around it to survive. In such a situation, there were only two or three people Lyria could instantly save from the law.  

Currently, hundreds of people were gathered here. Among them, some must have had the abilities necessary for this ritual.  

Was it really the right choice?  

She couldn’t foresee the consequences of giving up on all of them to protect a couple who weren’t even twenty years old.  

“Dante, who are these kids? I didn’t follow your orders to save your friends.”  

Dante reflected on the Ice Queen’s true nature and said, “You said second place.”  

“Second place? Second place in what?”  

Dante squinted one eye at her. She had learned this after spending the night but hadn’t noticed Lyria’s confusion. She didn’t want to explain at length. Even if she did, what would it change?  

“He is number one. First place in the kingdom.”  

Lyria looked back at Shirone with a surprised expression. Though she wasn’t a pro yet, she was allied with Dante because he was recognized and highly skilled in her kingdom.  

Since mages didn’t have a competitive system like wizards, she wasn’t particularly impressed when she heard someone was ranked second. How many people were in the kingdom? What did it matter if they were first or second? If you’re just second, you’re a very good student.  

But here it was.  

It shocked her to find both first and second place in one place in this vast kingdom.  

She thought, *Did this kid really beat Dante?*  

She had a good impression. Aside from her lively eyes, Shirone carried the aura of the number one anywhere in the country.  

Dante’s skills had been personally witnessed just yesterday morning. Her ability to respond to actual battles, her judgment, and outstanding skills were comparable to those of the pros.  

To be honest, as a mage, Lyria couldn’t imagine how Shirone’s skills surpassed Dante’s.  

Dante pointed to the Ice Queen’s true nature.  

“Come. Shirone and Amy are ready too. You probably don’t understand, but you better relax.”  

A woman wearing the same dress as the Ice Queen flew across the sky.  

“Um, isn’t that the Ice Queen?”  

The Ice Queen arrived above them and slowly descended to the ground. A flash of light flickered far away, then a curved teleportation flash flew in, and she landed beside them.  

Amy’s eyes widened.  

“Zocre? Why are you…?”  

Zocre laughed and prostrated himself behind the Ice Queen. Then Ludvans and Vivian landed.  

No one understood the situation.  

At least Shirone and Amy felt a sense of crisis, but the tourists seemed relaxed and smiled.  

“Haha! Are you the real Ice Queen? What kind of event is this?”  

“Wow, are they really similar? How did you find the same person among wizards?”  

It was natural to assume this was part of the festival. Otherwise, the Ice Queen couldn’t actually wander right in front of their eyes.  

If Shirone hadn’t seen the shockwave erupt right before Lyria’s eyes, she would have thought the organizers had arranged the most spectacular concert.  

Tourists clapped and cheered as the Ice Queen stepped into the crowd.  

Winter festivals were held every year, but this year’s special event was even more novel than anyone expected.  

Thinking of resurrecting the legendary Ice Queen?  

Even the wax figure of the Ice Queen had many fans who admired her beauty. However, the woman playing her role was so beautiful that she couldn’t be compared to a wax figure.  

She possessed a brilliant ability granted by the power of life.  

“Whee! Ice Queen! Please hold my hand just once!”  

A pot-bellied man smiled broadly and extended his hand to her, while the person next to him stopped as if he were Arthur.  

“Oh, how rude. Then she might freeze you.”  

People burst into laughter. No one realized this was the real situation.  

Even amid the jokes, the Ice Queen moved forward with a nonchalant expression.  

Lyria kept a close eye on the Ice Queen’s every move while holding hands.  

*What? What law did you touch?*  

The wave of the law had changed the laws of everyone except Shirone and the others. Nothing special had happened yet, but judging from the power of the wave, the range of change would be enormous.
