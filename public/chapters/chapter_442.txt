Chapter 442  
Task Force (4)  
~*~

Shirone and the others followed Mahatu through a winding, labyrinthine corridor. Upon learning that they were messengers of God, the local warriors knelt in unison, and even after entering together, they were warmly welcomed by the tribesmen. Unlike Kadum, who paid a massive tribute to secure the messenger of God’s favor for eternal life, Mahatu managed Kergo’s resources with complete transparency. The tribesmen’s lives had improved, and the faces of passersby were bright with vitality. Although Shirone hadn’t fought for Kergo, he was their savior—the one who had opened the Guffin’s door and ascended to heaven.

“I’d like to throw a festival at least, but it seems the situation is urgent,” Mahatu said.

“Yes. I have to reach heaven as soon as possible.”

Mahatu did not press for details about heaven’s current state. Kergo still worshipped Anchera and revered angels, but Mahatu’s foremost concern was the survival and well-being of his people.

Sein asked, “How many people are on the waiting list? I heard earlier that it’s saturated.”

Anyone could enter heaven if an Unlocker opened the Guffin’s door, but to prevent indiscriminate passage, Maze had established a trial of achievement and sacrifice, allowing only selected warriors to pass. If the list was saturated, it meant many were waiting for an Unlocker to arrive.

Mahatu licked his lips thoughtfully. “Actually, not that many. About seven.”

“Strange. Then why block entry?”

“That’s… well, it’s not something I can discuss here. You’ll see for yourself.”

Gaold didn’t care. The only purpose of visiting Kergo was the Guffin’s Gate. If there were talented warriors waiting to ascend, he could recruit them, but their team’s power level was already so high that matching them would be difficult.

“It’s fine. Let’s go see it, and get it over with quickly.”

As if eager to show his sincerity, Mahatu quickened his pace.

They arrived at a massive iron gate engraved with a giant sculpture.

“This is the Warriors’ Chamber. Those waiting to ascend to heaven are gathered here.”

When Mahatu swung the iron door open, Gaold stepped inside first, with the rest of the party following. The seven warriors Mahatu had mentioned were scattered around the room, all watching Gaold with sharp, wary eyes.

The swordsman, seated with a longsword resting across his lap, spoke up. “A warrior or an angel?”

Gaold didn’t answer. He scanned the faces of the seven, then turned to Sein.

“They need to be at least Level 6 Certified. Beyond that, they’re not worth evaluating.”

The wizard in the corner frowned. “You talk as if you’re going to be a Level 6 Certified yourself.”

“What if I am?”

“Hah! Is that why you’re so arrogant? I’ve killed countless Certified wizards. Judging skill by rank is anachronistic. It’s about how much hell you’ve endured, how life and death have been divided by your experience.”

The wizard turned away, revealing five rings on his right hand.

The swordsman spoke up nervously. “Hey, I asked first, you know?”

“Does it matter? As long as you have the skill, anyone can go. Wasn’t that the rule?”

The wizard waved his right hand, gesturing for Gaold to approach. “I’ll show you the state of those who’ve escaped the water supply.”

Gaold’s index finger snapped forward.

The moment the air gun detonated with a deafening crack, the magician’s body was launched backward into the wall. He spat blood, crashed to the floor, and lay motionless, his head bowed too deeply to move.

“Next.”

Shirone frowned. He hadn’t intended to kill him like he had in the association’s underground bunker, but he knew Gaold’s air gun better than anyone.

“I also agree with what he said earlier,” Shirone muttered.

An elderly wizard stepped to the center, rolling his neck. “There is a level beyond certification numbers. But for those not backed by actual skill, theory is just delusion. I’d be a little different…!”

Before he could finish, the back of Gaold’s hand—having just cast teleportation—struck the wizard’s face. The impact sent the old man’s head snapping left and right like a bobblehead before he collapsed like a felled log.

“Next.”

In an instant, the five remaining observers froze.

This wasn’t a group of dreamers hoping to strike it rich on the outskirts of heaven. They were clearly a task force with business to conduct inside.

When no one else stepped forward, Gaold pointed at the swordsman who had been guarding his blade. “Hey, you. Didn’t you order something earlier?”

The swordsman kept his mouth shut, glaring at Gaold. Then, his expression suddenly relaxed, and he raised both hands in surrender. “Whoops, I give up. Recognizing your skill is also…! Aww!”

Gaold lunged forward and slapped his fist against the swordsman’s chin. The press magic detonated on impact, sending the man’s face bouncing backward as he slid across the floor.

“Anyway, all of them are only alive because of their snouts.”

The atmosphere in the room plummeted.

Gaold glanced back at the four remaining candidates, who stared at him blankly, then turned toward the door. “All of you, get out. Unless you want to get lost.”

No one needed to be told twice. The four of them scrambled out the door, tripping over their own limbs in their haste to flee.

With only the candidates who had passed and Shirone’s party remaining, a chill hung over the Warriors’ Chamber.

Gaold shook out his hands and turned back to the door. “It’s fine. It’s neat.”

“There’s still one more left,” Mahatu said as he re-entered the chamber.

Certainly, his skills would be a step above those who had just been dismissed, but in Gaold’s opinion, ascending to heaven was still too much of a stretch.

“What? Are you challenging me too?”

“No. The fact is…” Mahatu hesitated, glancing toward the doorway. “There’s one more messenger of God. He asked me not to let any more warriors in…”

“Hm?”

As Mahatu stepped aside, a familiar figure walked through the door.

It was Armin of Gwangan, the Eternal Reflector.

“Oh my.”

To catch a big fish, you need good bait. As expected, Shiina was perfectly suited to handle Armin.

Shiina’s eyes turned cold. *How long do you have to be treated like a child before your intuition is finally resolved?*

However, when he saw Armin’s face, his tone lacked the harshness he had intended. “Why is my brother here? Did you forget what I asked for?”

“No, I remember. I didn’t come here with the intention of interfering in your life. I’m here to keep a promise I made to myself.”

Gaold interjected. “Sorry, but that won’t work. I don’t play by the white line. I can’t figure it out.”

“The ivory tower has withdrawn. Isn’t that what happened?”

Shiina’s eyes widened. If Armin had abandoned the magical utopia that only the world’s best wizards could enter, it meant his resolve was no longer normal.

“Uh, why? And Keira…?”

Armin kept quiet about the events inside. A heavy look passed over his face. “Shiina, nothing in this world is more important than you. Heaven is a dangerous place. You can never go somewhere like that alone. Not even for the sake of the Olipher School who raised me.”

“Shiina-san is a Certified 5th Grade wizard.”

“Mr. Armand…”

Shiina was surprised that the taciturn Armand had spoken.

“I know roughly what kind of relationship it is, but I feel like I’m ignoring it too much. And you don’t have to worry about Shiina-san’s life or death. No one will die until I die.”

As a swordsman, the fact that they tended to die first regardless of skill hurt their pride.

*Is it Armand…?*

A demon of the sword who had cut down two members of the continent’s strongest swordsman group, Fengzhang. It seemed such a figure was looking out for Shiina.

It was a comforting fact in a situation where he had to follow the madman Gaold, but as an older brother, he couldn’t help but feel a pang of sadness.

*Well, with what qualifications do I…?*

Except for Shiina, everyone seemed to welcome Armin’s joining. He was a master of time magic who had conquered the speed of light. In particular, his stop magic, which halted time itself, was the world’s strongest crowd control.

“Alright. If it has nothing to do with the white line, I accept him. But anyway, I’m in charge. If you disobey, I will remove you from the team, so keep that in mind.”

Armin had no complaints. Since Shiina was effectively held hostage, fighting for the team was the best course of action.

With the flight to heaven just around the corner, a final team of ten members was formed to recapture the labyrinth.

*Wow, it’s really amazing when you put it all together.*

Shirone couldn’t believe he belonged to the team. Regardless of the white, red, or black factions, this was a task force with the absolute best people gathered.

* * *

Mahatu assigned the remaining rooms. The ten members, each given their own private quarters, had dinner and then gathered in the largest room, which served as a conference hall.

The opening time for the Guffin’s door was 6:00 AM the next day.

Considering the association’s pursuit, leaving immediately would be the logical choice, but even if they traveled by day, they would need to sleep first. After entering heaven, even taking a break could turn into a war, so Shirone preferred to spend the night here.

Gaold unfolded the map Kang Nan had given him on the table.

It was a spray chart overlaid on a map of heaven, marked with colored dots.

“Heaven is currently going through a cataclysmic period. A revolution has broken out among the subjects, and rebels are active. What you see now is a real-time threat map with Level 2 or higher alert zones displayed. Oh, I must leave out the word real-time. Because it was three months old.”

Gaold’s Royal Guard, who had been on a spy mission in heaven, had made no further contact since sending this map.

Sein spoke up. “As a result of confirming Eastas, it has become necessary to revise our existing strategy. Checking the maze’s condition is the first priority. In some cases, changing the coordinates of the Akashic Records may not make sense.”

Ethella added, “If Miro was kidnapped from heaven, shouldn’t the enemy’s status be reset? It means they are already preparing themselves.”

“Right. A preemptive strike through stealth is difficult. I’ll have to look at the situation, but I’ll use the strategy of infiltrating after causing as much disturbance as possible,” Armin said.

“Creating a disturbance in heaven? There won’t be many ways. Are you planning to use the rebels?”

“That, too, is clearly in my head. But the rebels won’t move easily. So what you need is the ancient weapon Tagis. If we can neutralize Heaven’s interception system, ‘Aegis’, they will change their minds.”

Armin shook his head. “No. Even if they could break through the Aegis, there is little chance that the rebels will launch an all-out war. As most of them were subjects, they must know the power of heaven better than anyone else. If Mecca and Norr were united, it would be a force that could not be overlooked even by Heaven, but the problem is fear. Imprinted fear is not easily forgotten.”

“Fear can be eliminated. Anyway, the trend of heaven will be far away from our expectations. You have to deal with it from time to time. After all, we have the Masterpiece.”

Shiina asked, “What does it mean? Masterpiece?”

Sein looked back at Shirone. “It is you, Shirone. If you succeed, you can create an effect equivalent to moving the entire rebel army. So let’s listen now. How the hell are you going to blow up heaven?”

When everyone’s eyes suddenly focused on him, Shirone gulped down his saliva with a nervous expression.

It was a part that had to be explained before leaving for heaven, but the atmosphere was different from when he told Gaold. From now on, it was a real battle where every word was a strategy.

Gaold shoved his chin forward as if vouching for him. “Hehe, there is no need to hesitate, Shirone. In my opinion, it’s really crazy magic.”

*That’s why I’m more anxious…*
