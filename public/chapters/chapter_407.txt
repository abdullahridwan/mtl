Chapter 407  
Entering the Competition System (2)  
~*~

The students’ reactions were mixed. Class Three, in particular, did not even seem to understand what had just happened.

“Shirone wins. Shirone receives 3 points. Dorothy receives 0 points.”

Despite the evaluation teacher’s stern announcement, Dorothy merely nodded in acceptance and returned to her seat.

She already knew there were not many students in the senior class who could fight Shirone on equal terms. That was the conclusion she had reached after watching his battle with Dante at Training Ground No. 2000 half a year ago.

If she went all out, she might be able to endure for about ten minutes. But it would be foolish to sacrifice something more important for the sake of a single point.

Dorothy was a manipulation-type mage. In particular, mages who controlled real physical objects lost most of their combat power when those objects were damaged.

‘I can’t afford to spend an entire week repairing my doll.’

If she fought for ten minutes against Shirone while he fired mass-filled photons at her without pause, her doll would be reduced to scrap metal.

In any case, since there would be an interpersonal battle next week, the decision she had made was the best one.

Shirone came down from the training ground with a dazed expression. He had gained 3 points, but he still looked bewildered.

Iruki and Nade approached him, their faces full of curiosity.

“What was that? What happened?”

“Well, you see…”

Shirone told them about the proposal Dorothy had made before the match began.

His friends could only stare at him in disbelief. None of them had imagined that there was a way to earn points like that.

Nade quickly checked the schedule.

“When do I fight Dorothy?”

Iruki’s palm struck the back of Nade’s head.

“Ow! Why did you hit me? I was just joking…”

“I detected ten percent sincerity in that joke. Anyway, Dorothy won’t use the same strategy again. She must have made that decision after taking into account Shirone’s personality and his situation with Amy.”

Shirone looked toward Dorothy. She was still sitting alone, her expression unreadable.

“It felt too much like she was trying to use the information she got from the Courage of Truth. That’s why I turned her down. I guess she knew I would react that way.”

“I thought she was naïve, but she’s quite calculating. The same will be true for the other students. Their real value will show once the practical matches begin.”

Nade rubbed the back of his head and pouted.

“Isn’t she just a weird kid? I still don’t get it. You’re not taking her side just because she confessed to you, are you?”

Iruki raised his eyebrows.

He had been the one to hear, during the Courage of Truth, that Dorothy loved him. Of course, it had been a light remark, nothing worth taking seriously. Even so, it was also true that it had been the first confession he had ever received from a woman.

“Stop talking nonsense. Just remember that, starting from Class Two, the level is fairly high. We need to stay alert.”

Nade still looked suspicious.

“Sounds like you’re taking her side…”

Iruki changed the subject.

“Anyway, you’re up next. Your opponent is Fermi. Do you have a strategy?”

As his turn approached, Nade also began to look tense.

“Well. For now, I’ll fight the way I usually do. If he shows me something, I’ll shift gears.”

“You don’t have to push yourself too hard. It’s only the first week. Besides, Fermi is likely to take first place in the senior class this year anyway. In other words, today’s points aren’t that important to you.”

If Fermi became first overall, then whether Nade won or lost here would not make a huge difference in relative terms.

But Nade thought differently.

Fermi was expected to become Shirone and Iruki’s greatest enemy. Nade had no intention of treating this match lightly.

“I know. But if I draw out Fermi’s abilities, it’ll be easier for you two to prepare when you fight him. First, I’ll focus on winning. And even if I can’t win, I’ll at least find out how strong he is.”

Another match ended at Training Ground No. 2000, and the evaluation teacher called Nade and Fermi by name.

Nade raised his thumb to his friends and headed for the training ground.

He reached the center first. Fermi followed at a leisurely pace, admiring the scenery around him as if he were an office worker taking a stroll after lunch.

Feeling as though he was being ignored, Nade glared at him coldly.

Fermi’s smile did not change.

“Whoa, those eyes are fierce. It’s only the first day, junior. Let’s take it easy.”

Nade did not even snort.

Whether or not the graduation examination was fixed, he had a debt to repay.

Last year, Shirone had been trapped in the labyrinth’s space-time and fallen into a near-death state.

At that time, it was Fermi who had led the student council in acknowledging Shirone’s death and beginning the funeral procedures.

‘Do you have any idea how much I hated you for that? I can finally let that out now.’

The two met each other’s eyes, then stepped back. Fermi turned first, and Nade followed, walking to his starting position.

As they warmed up in their respective positions, the electric sign changed from green to yellow, then to red.

The moment Training Ground No. 2000 activated, Nade shot forward with electric shocks crackling from both hands.

‘I’ll uncover everything about you in these ten minutes!’

Three minutes later—

“Fermi wins! He receives 3 victory points. Nade receives 0 points.”

Fermi fanned his face with one hand.

“Phew, that was dangerous.”

Despite his words, not a single drop of sweat was visible on his face as he walked down from the training ground.

Nade remained kneeling in the center of Training Ground No. 2000 like a puppet whose strings had been cut.

Thanks to the 80 percent synchro rate, he had not taken enough shock to lose consciousness, but the psychological blow seemed considerable.

He stared blankly at the ground.

When he showed no sign of moving for a long while, Shirone and Iruki hurried over.

“Hey, Nade, are you okay? Get a hold of yourself.”

“He’s completely out of it. Let’s just carry him.”

The two grabbed Nade by the arms and dragged him out of Training Ground No. 2000 like a sack.

As the students burst into laughter, Amy sighed and shook her head.

‘Honestly, those idiots…’

Although Nade was being laughed at, Amy could understand how he felt.

At first glance, the battle between the two had seemed intense. But Fermi’s mental strength gauge had never dropped below 90 percent. That meant he had allowed almost no effective hits.

In fact, Fermi had driven Nade into a groggy state using only thirty-six prescribed spells, without revealing any special major magic at all.

Like clothes getting soaked through in a drizzle, Nade probably could not even pinpoint exactly what had happened to him.

Shirone shook Nade worriedly.

“Nade, snap out of it. Are you okay?”

Nade suddenly stood up as if nothing had happened.

“Of course I’m okay. Did you really think I’d collapse like that?”

Iruki asked, “What happened? You said you’d hold out for ten minutes no matter what.”

Shirone could not hide his regret.

“Your reactions were too passive. You should have pushed a little harder. If you had, you could have landed a decent hit.”

Nade turned his head without answering. Then he stared at Fermi’s back as he walked to the other side of the training ground.

“Shirone, be careful.”

“Huh?”

“That guy… is really strong.”

Shirone let out a soft sigh.

From what he had seen, it had been a match where Nade could have had a real chance if he had pressed just a little more.

To Shirone, it had only looked like Nade had been careless.

“Of course he’s strong. He beat you.”

Fire lit in Nade’s heated eyes.

“I’m serious! Do I look like I just lost because I was careless?”

“Fine. Either way, don’t dwell too much on your first loss. You can’t fight the same opponent twice anyway. Forget it quickly and do better in tomorrow’s evaluation.”

Iruki understood how Shirone felt.

Even in his eyes, it had looked like a match where Nade should have attacked more aggressively.

But Nade had not.

‘That’s rare. Since when does he call someone strong?’

It was the first time in six years that Iruki had heard Nade say such a thing.

But if Nade said it, then they needed to be wary. When it came to battle, he had the instincts of a beast.

‘I didn’t sense it. Does that mean it was outside my range of perception?’

That was the only explanation if Fermi had managed to hide his true ability so thoroughly in front of the entire senior class.

In any case, Shirone, who had no way of knowing what Nade had felt, was simply upset over his friend’s defeat.

Thinking that Nade’s first loss should not affect Iruki’s performance, Shirone quickly changed the mood.

“Iruki, don’t be careless either. Binder has never stood out, but that’s exactly how unexpected ambushes usually are.”

As always, Iruki remained at his own pace.

“There is no carelessness in my dictionary. I’ll finish it in three minutes.”

‘That’s what carelessness is…’

But Shirone’s thought did not reach Iruki, who had already left.

Iruki faced Binder in the middle of Training Ground No. 2000.

True to his usual dislike of showmanship, Iruki only made brief eye contact with Binder before turning and walking away.

“Iruki, it’s an honor to meet you like this.”

Binder spoke to Iruki’s back.

It was not a simple greeting. His eyes shone, and the corners of his mouth lifted in a mocking smile.

“An honor?”

“You are Mercody Albino’s son. The heir to the Dragon Mine.”

Iruki turned fully around, his hands clasped behind his back.

“People like that come to my house sometimes. So what?”

Binder pointed to himself.

“Do you know what my theory score is in the advanced class? One hundred points. It is one of the best records in the history of Alpheas Magic School. And no one can break my record. Why? Because it’s already perfect.”

Iruki scratched his head.

“So what are you trying to say?”

“I will enter the Dragon Mine. No, someday, I will definitely become the head of the Dragon Mine.”

For three hundred years, the position of head of the Dragon Mine had been held by the Mercodyne family.

Naturally, the family regarded Iruki as the next head.

Binder was not simply challenging Iruki. He was formally challenging the Mercodyne family itself.

“Listen, Iruki. The greatest intellect in the kingdom will be me, Binder—not you.”

Iruki, who had been lost in thought, strode toward him.

Binder, who had been brimming with confidence, flinched and stepped back when Iruki approached without warning.

“W-what? The match starts after Training Ground No. 2000 activates…”

Iruki quickly reached out with both hands and gripped Binder’s arms tightly.

“I’m begging you.”

“…What?”

“Please do it. I’ve been hearing that since the day I was born, and I’m sick of it. Bring my father down and become the head of the Dragon Mine. I’ll cheer you on. Go for it.”

After saying that, Iruki turned away.

Binder stared blankly at his back as he walked off.

Then, slowly, his eyes burned with anger.

‘What? He’s been hearing it since the day he was born? He’s sick of it? Does he have any idea how many scholars shed blood and sweat just to cross the threshold of the Dragon Mine?’

Binder’s fist trembled.

He had not intended to reveal his strength in the first week. But Iruki was different. Binder decided to crush the future rival standing before him.

The moment the light came on at Training Ground No. 2000, Binder closed the distance with Teleportation.

With his perfect score in theory, he could use every basic spell with near-perfect precision.

Fireball, Wind Cutter, and Ice Spear flew in alternating patterns, crossing over one another.

In response to the frantic attack, Iruki opened the distance and activated Bomblet Barrier.

Hundreds of miniature bombs spread out, forming a spherical curtain. As Binder’s spells struck the bombs, a series of explosions erupted.

“Hmph! I’ve already analyzed that!”

Binder extended his index finger and fired Air Gun, the fundamental spell of the press series.

Shirone recognized the spell, but its power was far too weak to compare to Gaold’s.

However, its accuracy was impressive.

The spell threaded through the dense arrangement of the Bomblet Barrier, and Iruki instantly shot upward.

After switching to Fly magic and hovering in the air, Binder stretched out both hands, his eyes gleaming.

“I knew it!”

A glowing sphere of fire gathered between his palms.

‘Did you think theory was the only thing I was perfect at? There’s a limit to how much you can look down on people!’

The flames of Flame Strike burned in Binder’s hands.

It was difficult magic to use unless one majored in flames, but Binder’s perfect theoretical foundation allowed him to surpass beginner-level fusion magic with ease.

“This is it!”

Convinced of his victory, Binder cast the desperate spell, thrusting out his hand in the proper magical action.

“Huh?”

But the fireball that should have shot forward vanished right before his eyes.

‘No way… Cancellation?’

Goosebumps rose along the back of his neck.
