Chapter 380
Prosecutor’s Belief (4)
~*~
‘Is that really the skill of a swordsman who just turned twenty?’
Quan couldn’t hide his admiration for Rai’s swordsmanship. It was precise, yet exquisitely refined. If the Ogent family’s two lineages were to clash, this time, without a doubt, belonged to the black-haired branch.

“Whoa! Whoa!”
Lian gasped, his arms trembling as he struggled to support the massive greatsword. It had been a year since he’d last felt its true weight, aside from the brief moments he spent eating.

*Lift it. Lift it up.*
Rai’s eyes twitched. The greatsword, pinned beneath the pressure of Rai’s black blade, began to slowly rise.

*Do you still have strength left?*
Given the shockwaves that had already rippled through the examination building, Lian’s body should have been pushed to its absolute limit. Yet when Rai tried to press down with just a fraction more force, an overwhelming counter-pressure shoved him back. It was a force so immense that blocking it hadn’t even crossed Rai’s mind.

Sensing danger, Rai withdrew his sword and created distance.
At the same moment, Lian swung his blade upward and rose to his feet. He grabbed the torn fabric of his shirt and ripped it open, revealing tightly corded muscles.

Rai instantly recognized Lian’s defining trait.
*His resilience is monstrous.*
To elevate divine transcendence beyond the body’s natural limits and turn it into a tangible technique, ordinary self-torture was unthinkable. Rai could only imagine the grueling, painstaking training Lian had endured to maximize his physical feedback.

“I’ll admit you gave it your all,” Rai said. “But I am the one who leads this family.”
Using external gravity, Rai closed the distance and swung his black sword directly at Lian’s exposed right arm.

Under normal circumstances, Lian should have raised a defensive stance.
Instead, Lian dropped his ribs, raised the greatsword vertically, and prepared to strike.

*What?*
Rai’s mind raced for a split second.
*Trade flesh for bone? Or is offense the only defense?*
Either way, it made no sense. A greatsword’s movements were inherently slower than a sharp blade’s.

Yet, in that instant, Lian’s greatsword blurred into an afterimage and plummeted straight between Rai’s eyebrows. It was a burst of acceleration that defied common sense.

Rai’s muscles tensed as he braced himself. Riding the external gravity, his body slid out of the blade’s radius in a wide semicircle.
But before he could fully recover, Lian’s greatsword whistled through the air and caught up to him.

*Damn it! What is this?*
Rai had evaded by a hair’s breadth, and now he was sweating profusely. His neck was seconds from being severed.

Watching the clash from the sidelines, Rayna stomped her foot in restless agitation.
As their conflicting emotions collided, the mana in the air grew heavier. If this continued, one of the brothers would end up holding a funeral.

“I can’t just watch! We have to stop this.”
“I can’t do that. Neither Lian nor Rai would want it to end like this.”
Rayna turned to Tess, her expression heavy with sorrow.
“Tess, no matter how much you want to protect Lian, this isn’t the way. They’re fighting with the intent to kill. You should have stopped Lian from the very beginning.”

“I’m not fighting because I hate Rai oppa,” Tess replied quietly.
Tess remembered that day vividly. When she had visited Lian’s lodging at the swordsmanship school with late-night snacks, she had heard a terrible moan leaking through the door.
“Lian pushed himself beyond his limits every single day. And at night, he’d collapse, gasping for air. He’d stay up all night with a rag in his mouth just to endure the pain of breaking his own body.”

Rayna fell silent, unable to meet Tess’s tearful gaze.
Despite sharing blood, Rayna had never understood the depth of Lian’s suffering. Only those who had chosen the path of the sword, just like Lian, could truly comprehend it.

Rai’s kick slammed into Lian’s stomach. Lian was launched into the air, rolled across the floor, and forced onto his knees. He was seconds from being struck in a vital point.
The cold aura had completely vanished from Rai’s face.
As the fight dragged on, Lian’s defenses grew stronger, fueled by his raw emotions. It was grating.

“You idiot! Why don’t you know when you’re outmatched?! Even if you have to hold back—!”
Lian slowly rose to his feet. He raised his sword to the exact same position he’d held before he collapsed.
“Come on.”
“This kid is really… !”

Rage welled up in Rai’s eyes. A menacing aura radiated from his body, reaching even those watching from outside the training ground.
Rai’s strikes looked like a ghostly apparition. As Lian’s body accumulated more scars from Rai’s fast, precise swordsmanship, his movements gradually began to slow.

The skill gap was palpable. There was a talent chasm that no amount of effort could bridge. Yet, the clearer the gap became, the more enraged Rai grew.
*The family that pinned its hopes on such a pathetic bastard is stupid. Lian has no future. Swordsmanship without technique is nothing but brute force.*

“You foolish child!”
Rai deflected the greatsword with exquisite precision and followed up with a roundhouse kick. Lian’s head snapped back like a spinning top.
For a moment, Rai thought he’d broken his neck. But Lian’s body rotated with his head, floated upward, and crashed back down with a heavy thud.

Rai took a deep breath and glared at Lian. Even his posture looked pathetic. With that blow to the chin, he’d likely be unconscious until tomorrow.
“Haha.”
Lian’s lungs trembled. He slowly raised his head, his mouth torn and bleeding.
“That tickled. Can you actually kill me with something like that?”

Rai was taken aback by Lian’s tenacity, the man who rose like a zombie no matter how many times he was beaten. But then he looked down at his opponent with cold pity.
“Kill? Are you even worth killing?”
Lian’s eyes widened.
“I’d have to kill my grandfather’s precious youngest grandson to use that on you. There’s no need to kill someone like you. I just need to beat you until you lose your mind. Got it? You’re just the youngest member of a swordsman family with no skill or talent. A low-tier backbone, you bastard!”

“Yes!”
Lian screamed, crouching low.
“I am a low-tier retard!”

He charged forward, brandishing his sword. The attack carried such ferocity that the wind itself seemed to demand you approach it, unless you had a heart of steel.
Rai hastily raised his black sword. *Bang!* As the greatsword descended, the shockwave traveled straight into his knees.
Lian pressed his face directly into the blade.
Seeing his mouth torn all the way to his ear, Rai wondered if his brother had finally lost his mind.
*Can we keep going like this? Should I call a doctor right now?*

“Feel free to mock me. I’ve heard it my whole life. Call me pathetic. Call me inferior. Despise me, ignore me, curse me. Still, I… !”
Lian drew his greatsword back and assumed a full-swing stance.
“Never stop!”

Rai’s face went pale as a hallucination took hold: Lian’s greatsword seemed to grow dozens of times its original size. The brain, overwhelmed by the image of an unstoppable blade, scrambled the expected trajectory.
“Yaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa!”
At the exact moment Lian’s battle cry erupted, Rai’s eyes dropped.
*Ankle!*
Rai belatedly read the sword’s trajectory and leaped upward.

The greatsword dug through the ground and emerged on the other side, throwing up a wall of soil as wide as the blade’s radius.
A black line cut through the center of the dirt wall. It split in half with a sharp *pop*, and Rai burst through the soil, landing in a crouch.
But what awaited him was another attack from Lian, who had already raised his greatsword again.

Rai was stunned by the linked assault that completely ignored conventional mobility. As he stretched his upper body back like a drawn bow, a strong wind brushed the tip of his chin. Every hair on his body stood on end.
“I can’t hear anything! Even if everyone ridicules me, I can only move forward!”
Lian pushed Rai back, venting years of resentment.

When the clash began and Rai was pushed back for the first time, even the family elders and Quan showed signs of embarrassment.
*Lian… … .*
Rayna’s eyes softened with sorrow.
He was a child who had grown up in the shadow of his older brother, constantly compared to him since birth. She honestly doubted he could ever beat Rai, even with a sword in hand. Perhaps Rai himself had used his own sword as an excuse to pass the burden of the blue-haired lineage onto Lian.

“There are many problem children in the blue-haired branch.”
Rayna licked her lips and turned to Clump.
“The black-haired branch always plays by the rules. Reasonable. Cool-headed. On the other hand, the blue-haired branch is often unpredictable. They’re stubbornly single-minded. That’s why they say if you’re born with blue hair, you’re lucky.”

“What are you talking about all of a sudden, especially when your grandfather has blue hair?”
“The black-haired branch’s existence as the stable, trouble-free line is one of the reasons Ogent is limited to the second class. But looking back at the family’s history, it was the blue-haired branch that eventually earned Ogent’s reputation as a sword family.”

Clump watched Lian swing his heavy blade like a storm.
“There are no detours. Don’t look back. Even if you’re knocked down and broken, the will to push through it—that is the conviction of the blue-haired. I don’t know how far Lian can go. But when he finally looks back from wherever he arrives, the road will be straight and undisturbed.”

“Goaaaaaa!”
When Lian attacked with aggressive posture, Rai didn’t even dare to cross swords.
He didn’t feel like dealing with him. He couldn’t shake the feeling that something absolute was lurking behind Lian’s back.
*Divine transcendence? The power of the spirit?*
A spark ignited in Rai’s eyes.

“Don’t be ridiculous!”
Rai bounced off his back foot and countered Lian’s sword head-on. The greatsword and black sword swirled in the center of their line of sight.
Rai only came to his senses after a devastating shockwave traveled up his handle. He had been too agitated to realize it.
However, his senses had already slipped from his grasp.
*What is this? It’s fine to be ignorant.*

As soon as Rai shifted his external gravity to his back and backed up quickly, Lian lunged like a wolf and snapped at him. The motion of falling forward wasn’t normal inertia; it was as if the ball had reverse spin.
As the greatsword came right in front of him, Rai twisted his ankle completely and spun. At the same time, the sole of his opposite foot slammed down on Lian’s Achilles tendon.
“Keugh!”
Rai rose up as he rotated like a dragon, lowering his black sword toward the back of Lian, who was bent on one knee. The actions were performed in a trance. It was no longer a matter of life and death.

“Lian!”
At the exact moment Tess cried out, Lian twisted his upper body.
In an unnatural posture, he swung the greatsword with one hand. The black sword bounced off, and Rai’s arms were forced behind his back.
“Wow… … .”
An involuntary exclamation escaped Rai’s lips.
It was a posture that completely collapsed his center of gravity. Even so, the physical strength required to deflect a vertical slash carrying his full weight with one hand was staggering.
*No, it’s not just on that level.*
The law of action and reaction did not apply to Lian’s sword. Absolute power that defied the laws of physics was melted into every single strike.

“Whoa.”
Shirone let out the breath he’d been holding. Watching Lian push Rai back one-sidedly, his heart—more than his role as a prosecutor—burned hotly.
“Tess.”
Tess’s face turned white with tension as she turned to Shirone.
“Lian… you’ve become really strong.”
At Shirone’s acknowledgment, Tess finally burst into tears. She knew exactly for whom the penance Lian had endured was meant.
Her throat tightened, and she could only shake her head, unable to speak.

“Yaaaaa!”
Lian swung his sword even as his whole body felt like it was breaking. Now, even Rai stopped backing down and began repelling every one of Lian’s attacks.
*If it transcends the limits of the body, it won’t last long anyway. I just need to stop this once. No, if I block it one more time… … .*
Rai grew frustrated.
*Damn it! When will it end?*

At that moment, a thought flashed through his mind.
*Ah… … .*
Rai finally realized what kind of swamp he had fallen into.
*This guy… he’s an idiot.*

At the same time, Lian’s greatsword hooked upward, lifting Rai’s black sword.
The pain of twisted knuckles rushed through Rai as he heard a sharp *ding*. His muscles tightened, his hands opened, and the black sword spun up into the sky.
