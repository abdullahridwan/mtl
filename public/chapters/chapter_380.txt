Chapter 380  
Swordsman’s Conviction (4)  
~*~

‘Is that really the swordsmanship of someone who only just turned twenty?’

Quan was impressed by Rai’s skill.

His blade was not merely precise; it was exquisite. If the Ogent family had two lineages running through it, then there was no question which one defined this generation. It was the era of the black hair.

“Whoa! Whoa!”

Lian sucked in ragged breaths as he held up his greatsword with trembling arms. For the first time since he had begun carrying it every waking moment—except when eating—he felt the true weight of the blade.

‘Lift it. Lift it up.’

Rai’s eyes twitched. The greatsword he had been pressing down with his black sword was slowly rising.

‘He still has strength left?’

The shock from his previous strike had landed properly. Lian’s body should have been at its limit.

Rai tried to put more weight into the pressure, but an absurd force—one he had not expected to have to resist—began pushing him back instead.

A bad feeling crept over him. Rai pulled his sword away and opened the distance.

At the same moment, Lian swung his greatsword through and rose to his feet. He grabbed his tattered shirt and ripped it open, revealing muscles drawn tight like cables.

Rai understood Lian’s greatest trait at a glance.

‘His recovery is incredible.’

To elevate the body’s transcendence beyond its limits into something that could be called technique, he must have endured self-inflicted torment beyond ordinary imagination. Rai could guess how much grueling training Lian had gone through to increase that feedback.

“I’ll admit you’ve worked hard,” Rai said. “But I’m the one who will lead this family.”

Using external gravity, Rai slipped back to Lian’s side. He aimed for Lian’s undefended right arm and swung his black sword.

Under normal circumstances, Lian should have taken a defensive stance.

Instead, he left his flank open and raised his greatsword vertically.

‘What?’

For one fleeting instant, Rai’s thoughts raced.

Was Lian giving up flesh to take bone? Or did he believe attack was the best defense?

Either way, it made no sense. A greatsword’s movement should have been slower than a thin, sharp blade.

Then Lian’s greatsword blurred with an afterimage and dropped toward the space between Rai’s eyebrows.

It was a bizarre acceleration, impossible to understand through common sense.

Rai sprang back and triggered a vibration through his muscles. Following his external gravity, his body slid in a semicircle out of the sword’s range.

But before he knew it, Lian’s greatsword was roaring through the air, already catching up to him.

‘Damn it! What is this?’

Rai dodged by a hair’s breadth, cold sweat breaking out across his skin. His neck had nearly been taken off.

Reina, who had been watching the battle, stamped her feet in distress.

As the brothers’ emotions clashed, the force behind their techniques only grew sharper. If this continued, one of them might end up having a funeral held for him.

“This won’t do! We have to stop the match.”

“We can’t,” Tess said. “Neither Lian nor Rai would want that.”

Reina turned to Tess, pain in her eyes.

“Tess, I know you want to protect Lian, but this isn’t right. Those two are fighting like they mean to kill each other. You should have stopped Lian from the beginning.”

“Lian isn’t fighting because he hates Rai.”

Tess still remembered it vividly.

The night she had gone to Lian’s lodgings at the swordsmanship school with a late-night snack, she had heard a terrible groan leaking through the door.

“Lian trained beyond his limits every single day. And at night, he suffered as if he were dying. He put a rag in his mouth and stayed awake until morning, enduring the pain of his body breaking down.”

Reina could not say anything. Tess looked as though she might burst into tears.

Although Reina was bound to Lian by blood, she had not known the true depth of his pain. The only people who could understand Lian were those who had chosen the path of the sword, just as he had.

Rai’s kick slammed into Lian’s stomach.

Lian flew through the air, rolled across the ground, and caught his balance on one knee. He had barely avoided being struck in a vital point.

The cold composure had vanished from Rai’s face.

As time passed, he could feel Lian’s defenses growing stronger. Lian was being swept up in his emotions, and Rai found that unpleasant.

“You idiot! Why don’t you understand that you can’t win? At least know when to stop!”

Lian slowly stood.

Then, just as he had before collapsing, he raised his sword and aimed it at Rai.

“Come.”

“You little…!”

Killing intent welled up in Rai’s eyes. The ominous aura radiating from his body reached even the people standing outside the training ground.

When Rai moved with all his strength, it was like watching a ghost. His swordsmanship was fast and accurate, and as wounds multiplied across Lian’s body, Lian’s movements gradually began to slow.

The difference in skill was obvious.

There was a gap in talent that effort alone could not fill. Yet the more clearly that gap showed, the angrier Rai became.

The family was foolish for placing expectations on such a pathetic bastard. Lian had no future. Swordsmanship unsupported by technique was nothing more than a crude display of strength.

“You stupid brat!”

Rai deflected the greatsword with exquisite skill, then drove a roundhouse kick into Lian’s head.

Lian’s neck snapped around like a spinning top.

For an instant, it looked as though his neck had broken, but his body followed the rotation of his head and lifted into the air before crashing to the ground with a heavy thud.

Rai exhaled deeply and glared down at Lian. Even the way Lian lay sprawled on the floor looked pathetic. With a blow like that to the jaw, he probably would not regain consciousness until tomorrow.

“Ha… ha.”

At that moment, Lian’s lungs trembled.

He slowly raised his head. His mouth was split and bleeding, but he smiled.

“That attack barely tickled. You think you can kill me with something like that?”

Rai was shaken by Lian’s tenacity. No matter how badly he was beaten, he rose again like a zombie.

Then Rai looked down at his brother with pity and spoke coldly.

“Kill you? Are you worth killing?”

Lian’s eyes widened.

“I can’t very well kill Grandfather’s precious youngest grandson. There’s no need to kill someone like you. I just have to beat you until you lose consciousness. Understand? You’re just the youngest son of a swordsman family, with no skill and no talent. A slow-witted deadweight, you bastard.”
