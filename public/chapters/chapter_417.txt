Chapter 417  
One-Man Team (3)  
~*~

As the monsters across the sky, ground, and underground were wiped out almost simultaneously, a guide voice echoed from the observation deck at level 2,000.

“Whoa.”

Wiping sweat from her forehead, Shirone glanced back at the display board. “Wow! Did it turn out well?”

Group 5 had secured second place. First place belonged to Group 2, which included Fermi. Their completion time was three minutes and fifteen seconds. Losing by only thirteen seconds was disappointing, but considering Group 2 was essentially Fermi and a few others, it was still a solid result.

*Well, we’re an instant party,* Shirone thought. *The closer’s role was a weak point.*

“Sheesh, Fermi again? How do they keep climbing the ranks together?” Dante remarked.

“You have to admit he’s skilled at score management. Still, second place in our group isn’t bad. That puts us in the top ten overall. If we maintain a top-ten finish in every evaluation, we’ll eventually graduate.”

Dante was pleased that a trio who had never worked together had taken second place. Judging by their party contribution scores, everyone had earned a solid 1.0. Iruki was thinking the same thing, though his assessment was more precise.

*If this were a graduation exam, I’d rate Dante at 1.01, the closer at 0.99, Shirone at 1.0, Kanis at 1.02, and myself at 0.98.*

Kanis was the standout player in Group 5 by a narrow margin. It was impressive that he had single-handedly annihilated the underground monsters.

*To maximize party contribution, choosing the right targets is crucial. The gap between us and the top teams will only widen in the graduation exam.*

Meanwhile, Group 1 took third place. The time gap widened slightly to four minutes and eighteen seconds.

“Anyway, let’s head down. I want to check on the other groups.”

Dante led his crew down the two thousand steps. He immediately approached Zocre. “Shirone, good job. Iruki, Kanis as well.”

Shirone greeted him in high spirits. “You worked hard too. Let’s team up again if we get the chance.”

Fourth and fifth place were soon decided, with completion times of four minutes and twenty-four seconds, and four minutes and twenty-eight seconds, respectively.

“Wow, this week’s evaluations have been over quickly.”

Although Shirone’s group had only taken second place, they had been fortunate that the monsters hadn’t yet reached higher tiers. Given that, the other groups’ performances were still impressive.

Iruki noted, “This trend will likely continue until Tier 8. It’s difficult to handle them alone, but mages become incredibly powerful when they work together. Of course, that’s partly because we’re in a school.”

At the magic school, nearly everyone they met was a wizard. Even the thirty students in the graduating class were elite personnel who would be scattered across the world upon graduation. Since wizards accounted for less than 0.0001% of the population, at least the Royal Guard, Capital Guard, and Border Guard were required to maintain dedicated wizard units.

In fact, even third-rate wizards from various guilds could generate terrifying firepower if about twenty of them gathered. A simple calculation showed that if each cast one fireball every ten seconds, they would unleash 1,200 fireballs in just ten minutes. Even so, hiring that many wizards was expensive, so few missions could justify the cost. If such opportunities did arise, they would typically go to high-ranking wizards.

While there were exceptional cases of terrorism, they were powerless against the state’s official authority, known as the “Red Line.” The combat power of the Red Line—officially sanctioned wizard units—was on another level entirely. When a monster beyond the standard tiers, such as a demon, appeared, the Magic Association would organize a large, elite strike force under the king’s approval, known as a “Cage.” The name implied trapping the target and overwhelming it. It was widely known that when twenty of the best talents in their respective fields gathered, they could unleash enough power to be described as a walking army. Since third-rate wizards were already scarce across the entire continent, experts considered the Cage the ultimate strike force, barring a global catastrophe.

*If I enter society after graduation, I doubt I’ll ever join a party with so many different people,* Shirone thought. *I should learn as much as I can from this opportunity.*

The operation at level 2,000 came to a halt. “Article 4 evaluation complete. The fourth group to finish the mission will receive zero points.”

Shirone looked at Team 4 with a pang of sympathy. She had briefly worried that Amy’s group might finish last.

*As expected, balance is important,* she thought. *Even with a great dealer like Amy, we can’t win everything.*

Team 4 couldn’t hide their bitter expressions. It wasn’t a situation where anyone could point fingers and say they were entirely wrong. In fact, they had stormed the enemy camp with flashy, colorful attacks led by the Screamer. They had simply taken longer to annihilate the monsters than the other groups due to poor team balance.

*I should have taken the buff instead,* Amy regretted. The dealer’s deadly strategy could boost one person’s combat power to an unmanageable level, but the sixth week’s mission was pure annihilation. They had no choice but to expose their weaknesses in a mission that required precise coordination to eliminate enemies efficiently.

*I can’t do anything about it. I’ll aim for next week.* Amy coolly accepted the zero points. After all, the team leader was a stubborn Screamer, and she had no choice but to follow his lead. If you couldn’t adapt, you got left behind. That was the rule for the graduating class, with no room for excuses.

*Ugh. I’m going crazy with frustration.*

On the other hand, the Screamer couldn’t accept the result. *How did we even finish last?* Even in his previous senior year, he had never received a zero in strategy and tactics.

*It’s all because of you.* Unable to contain his rage, the Screamer shouted at Maya. “You idiot!”

Students throughout the graduating class turned their heads at the unprecedented outburst from the Magic School. When a team loses, individual capabilities shouldn’t matter, but it had already been weeks since her strategy and tactics evaluations had been ruined because of her. As the Screamer strode forward, Maya’s eyebrows furrowed and her expression hardened.

“Because of you, the monsters became more violent!”

Her magic induced anxiety and riotous behavior. It was strategically useful for crowd control, but for an annihilation mission, it only caused the enemies to rampage longer, costing them precious time.

“No, it wasn’t that they became violent, it was just a commotion…”

“Shut up! Talking about making pig-picking noises! You call that sonic magic? What are you going to do now?”

Maya forced a sad expression and lowered her head. “I’m sorry…”

No one intervened. Maya wasn’t particularly skilled in sound magic, and the Screamer was the second-ranked graduate in their class. For her, stepping up to argue could have severe consequences for her future standing.

“What the hell are you doing? If you’ve been in your senior year longer than me, shouldn’t you have some experience? Are you just going to stand there? If a Warwolf had appeared, would your answer have been correct?”

“Sorry. I’m sorry…” Maya could only repeat her apologies. There was nothing else she could do.

“Anyway, commoners aren’t allowed here. If you don’t have the talent, drop out. You’re just a nuisance to everyone else, holding us back while you try to claim your share as a wizard.”

Maya’s shoulders trembled. Tears dripped down her face, hidden by her hair. *She’s a commoner, Maya…*

Shirone glared at the Screamer. It was the first time he’d heard that Maya was a commoner, but that didn’t excuse his behavior. Demeaning someone’s specialty by calling it “pig-picking noises” was unacceptable. Maya’s voice was relatively pure compared to combat-oriented sound magic, which might have made it seem less effective, but this was a personal attack. It was a remark she would never have heard if she weren’t from a common background.

“I’m sorry…! I’m sorry…”

The Screamer grew even more irritated. *You’re a wizard, yet you’re the one cursing others?* This was the graduating class. It was an evaluation that affected everyone’s future. *Isn’t he making himself look like the villain?*

“Get out of here! And I’m begging you, if you don’t have the skills, drop out voluntarily. Wouldn’t you feel guilty holding back the rest of us from climbing the rankings?”

The Screamer turned coldly and walked away. Shirone called out after him. “Hey.”

The Screamer turned around and lifted his chin. “What?”

“I’m apologizing to Maya.”

The Screamer’s eyes widened in bewilderment. There was no reason for him to apologize, and interfering in this way was never wise in the senior class. *Look at this kid. Is he trying to challenge me?* Even when Shirone’s words implied a direct confrontation, the Screamer just snorted. *Mages and their pride. They act like they can crush anyone with a touch, yet they’re picking fights inside the school.* *No, is this a good thing?* The Screamer wasn’t stupid. Crushing Shirone, who had passed the seventh stage of the survival evaluation, would only boost his own reputation. *Should I just cut him down right now?* Steeling himself, the Screamer stepped toward Shirone.

“Come to think of it, you’re a commoner too, right? Or was it royalty? Ah, I thought you were royal, but turns out you’re just a commoner again. Hahahaha!”

It had been several months since Shirone’s background was clarified, and the students had already accepted it as fact that he was a commoner.

“It doesn’t matter if you’re a commoner or not. I’m taking back everything I said to Maya, and I sincerely apologize.”

“What? Why should you? I did my part. Finishing last is Maya’s fault.”

“It’s a joint responsibility. And if someone has to be held accountable, it’s you, as the leader.”

“What nonsense are you spouting? Don’t you know how party contribution works? Do you even know how many monsters I’ve killed?”

Nade stepped in to interrupt. “Isn’t it embarrassing to shout about this in public?”

The Screamer’s face flushed with anger. Shirone was bad enough, but now even the others were jumping in. Iruki chimed in. “He’s the main dealer, but there’s hardly any difference between him and the support dealer. Do you really think the dealer’s deadly strategy is suitable for an annihilation mission? The fact that we got zero points is ultimately the leader’s misjudgment.”

The Screamer frowned. “The third person talks too much. I was right. There was just a weak link in the team. I had to push forward since Maya was already on board. Who cares about getting 1 or 2 points? I still managed to raise my contribution to 1.4.”

“That’s your limit. The fact that you were already calculating your contribution before the results came out proves you don’t trust your teammates. Maya is a legitimate graduating class student. She undoubtedly had her own ways to contribute.”

“Hahahaha! So what? If you’d been in Group 4 instead of me, would you have said the same?”

“Absolutely. If I were leading, we’d have finished in the top three.”

“Sigh.” The Screamer let out a long sigh. *This is why I hate rookies. They talk big without any real experience.* *Maya is hopeless. Aside from her skills, she’s just not cut out to be a wizard.*

“Can you really get a high score with Maya on your team?” Amy spoke up. “Do you really think that’s possible?”

“It would definitely be higher than a team led by someone like you.”

The Screamer turned back. Amy crossed her arms and tapped her foot. “Are you talking to me?”

It was standard senior-class etiquette to avoid unnecessary conflicts with future competitors. There was no point in getting dragged into a war of attrition that only benefited others. Amy knew this well. In fact, she had been hesitant because of her elimination the previous year. But this year would be different. Unlike Seriel, who was wary of harming each other, Amy now had an ally she could trust and rely on completely.

Amy took a quick step forward. Iruki and Nade stepped up beside her with grim expressions. Their confidence in their comrades’ support had grown far beyond mere strategic alliances.

Amy pointed at the Screamer. “Then would you have done the same to me? Honestly, it’s true that the dealer’s killer strategy failed. I can understand that, but why are you taking your anger out on a teammate?”

The Screamer’s face went blank. *No matter how emotional he gets, he’s always watching the situation carefully.* On the other hand, Shirone and his group were pushing forward recklessly, without a second thought. *Are these kids crazy?* Focusing on emotion would only make them pawns. But the Screamer quickly found a way out. This was an opportunity. If he could secure 20 points while only risking a 5-point loss, it wouldn’t be a bad trade. *As long as Maya is on the opposing team, any group will lose.* Making up his mind, the Screamer made an offer.

“If you’re so confident, why don’t we test it out?”

“Test it?”

“You know there’s a full evaluation in two weeks, right? Highland Occupation. If you beat me with Maya on your team, I’ll admit defeat.”

Highland Occupation was a confrontation where flags were planted on both sides, and teams had to capture the opponent’s flag while managing environmental factors. In the graduation exam, the score was 15 to 15, but in group evaluations, it was a mini-game played to 5 points.

*As expected of the graduating class. He’s biting down hard.* Shirone immediately saw through the Screamer’s strategy. It was a plan to force Maya into his team, guaranteeing him a 20-point victory. When Shirone hesitated to answer, the Screamer sneered, his red tongue flashing. *That’s why you shouldn’t hesitate. Whether he refuses or accepts, he wins.*

“Why? Are you not confident? Did you just yell at me for nothing?”

Shirone finally spoke. “It’s true that Maya’s sonic magic is below average.”

“Hahahaha! So you’re confessing! What can you do if you know you’re going to lose in the end…?”

“But.” Shirone’s expression darkened. “It’s still way better than having a guy like you on the team.”

The Screamer snapped his mouth shut, his expression going completely blank. The eyes that once made thugs tremble were now fixed on Shirone. “That remark… Can I take that as a challenge?”

Shirone turned to his friends. “Guys.”

Iruki, Nade, and Amy stepped forward one by one, flanking Shirone on the left and right. Amy wagged a finger. “Alright, let’s do it. You’ll probably have to assemble your best team and go all out. We’ll crush you anyway.”

Amidst the commotion, it was Maya who felt the most embarrassed. She had ruined the evaluation and taken the brunt of the swearing. The situation had escalated far beyond what should have happened. She was grateful to Shirone and the others for standing up for her, but that only made her feel more guilty. *I can’t cause any more trouble.*

“Wait a minute, I don’t think…”

Before she could finish her sentence, Nade pulled Maya into his arms. The moment Maya blushed and opened her mouth to protest, Nade raised his fist toward the Screamer and shouted, “Let’s stick together, aaaaaaa!”

Nade’s voice boomed through the training ground. There were no regrets. On the contrary, it seemed as though all his pent-up frustration was finally being released, regardless of whether he was facing a senior or a junior. *Yes, this is the Shirone effect.*

Maya’s face turned pale, but the fiery momentum of Shirone and his group was too intense to dampen. The Screamer forced himself to control his anger. *Honestly, this is a win for me. They’ll lose 20 points, and I’ll gain 20.*

“See you in two weeks.”

The Screamer turned and left the training ground. The surrounding students stared blankly, unable to believe what had just happened. Fermi and his group were bad enough, but now they had openly challenged a well-known Screamer from the second class. *No, what kind of reckless kids are these?*

Sabina, standing next to Dante, sighed. No matter how hard she tried to stay uninvolved, she couldn’t help but be drawn into the drama, especially with Nade involved. “This is going to turn into a mess. Why do they just run around like that? Of course, involving Maya is a disadvantage. It looks like only Nade will be deducted 20 points. Shouldn’t Shirone’s group be penalized too?”

“I wouldn’t know,” Dante smiled, turning around. “Shirone is the worst captain.”
