Chapter 402  
Senior Class Information Session (2)  
~*~

The Courage of Truth had begun, and only the first question had been asked. Even so, a subtle tension settled over the students.

There was an unwritten rule in the senior class: nothing said here today was to be brought up again afterward. Still, the people involved could not help being hurt by what was said. Every one of them was fighting desperately.

‘So that’s how it is. If I hadn’t known, I might’ve been in serious trouble.’

Shirone glanced back at Amy with a much lighter heart.

No matter what question came his way, all he had to do was point to her. Thanks to that, the risk of being dragged into a nerve-racking psychological battle from the very beginning had disappeared.

Amy met Shirone’s eyes and shrugged.

She had already gone through the senior class last year. To Shirone, who was new to it, she was an ally worth more than gold.

‘Thank you, Amy. Seriously, thank you.’

A psychological burden disguised as tradition.

If he could avoid his competitors’ mental traps from the start, that would definitely become an advantage in the senior year.

“Haha, good! Then shall we move on? Pony, how about you this time? You have to do it eventually anyway.”

Pony, her face as cold as ice, asked Nade, who was standing across from her.

“Hmm. Who was the first person you noticed when you came here?”

It was such a bland question that there was no room for psychological warfare. She seemed completely uninterested in the Courage of Truth.

Ficho looked back at her in disappointment.

“Hey, is that all? Make it more interesting.”

“It’s fine. This is childish.”

Nade looked over all of Class Two. Any question could be interpreted in countless ways, but since Pony had asked so lightly, he simply answered honestly.

“The first person I noticed was that old man.”

Everyone’s eyes turned to the person Nade pointed at.

He was tall, with perfectly curled hair, dark skin from long exposure to the sun, and thin cheekbones.

Shirone realized Nade was not playing psychological games. In fact, Ficho had been the first person Shirone noticed as well.

‘It’s Ficho.’

Ficho was twenty-five years old, the oldest student in the senior class, and from his appearance alone, he looked closer to thirty-five.

His major was Insect. As a branch of magic that dealt with insects, it occupied a very unique position among the many types of magic.

Shirone knew that the Insect system was more developed in scientific fields than in magic.

Because it was difficult to harmonize with other classes, most Insect majors became scholars. However, Ficho had remained at school until the age of twenty-five because acquiring a wizard’s license would allow him to receive far better treatment.

The result was as trivial as the question itself, and Ficho clicked his tongue. Then, perhaps to change the mood, he decided to step forward himself.

After all, they could not let the chance to trample the freshmen who had just entered the senior year go to waste.

“Then I’ll ask this time.”

Ficho’s opponent was Dante. Since they were first place in Class Two and Class Three respectively, the air between them felt like the beginning of a grand battle.

“Who here do you think you could beat unconditionally? I mean a student so weak you could knock them down with one finger.”

Even though the question was not directed at him, Shirone naturally grew tense.

No matter whose name Dante gave, that person’s pride would be crushed. It was a question designed to leave a scar no matter what choice was made.

Dante frowned and thought.

The students of Class Two watched him with sharp eyes. Their faces seemed to say, Go on, choose someone.

“Hmm, this is really difficult.”

Ficho laughed, revealing his characteristic red tongue.

“You just have to be honest. No matter who you choose, the unwritten rule says no one can retaliate.”

“Can’t I just say all of Class Two? They all seem about the same anyway.”

The atmosphere, which had seemed ready to burst with excitement, cooled in an instant. The expressions of the Class Two students turned icy, and Shirone’s eyes widened.

Those were words only Dante, once hailed as the kingdom’s greatest prospect, could say.

‘Seriously… your personality is amazing too, really.’

Ficho was not amused.

It was not that he failed to understand Dante’s intention. Dante had chosen a strategy that distributed the hostility among everyone, thereby lessening the burden on himself.

“Sorry, but that’s not allowed. You have to choose one person. That’s the rule of the Courage of Truth.”

Dante casually pointed at Ficho.

“Really? Then I’ll choose you. Since you’re number one in Class Two, if I pick you, that means everyone else is below me too.”

‘Is this bastard actually insane?’

Even Ficho could not help feeling his temper rise this time.

Air Servant Dante.

He was the number one prospect at the Royal Magic School, evaluated as the best of the best. Even now, he was one of the strongest candidates for the top rank among the graduates.

However, the public’s evaluation of him had been based on the standards of the advanced class.

Beyond the steel door, this was no longer the advanced class.

No matter how skilled someone was, one stumble could send them tumbling into the abyss. That was the competitive system of the senior class.

‘This is why I hate kids. They come up from the advanced class and run wild without knowing how high the sky is.’

Dante’s declaration of war was effective. The faces of the Class Two students, who had been smirking until a moment ago, finally began to turn serious.

“Can I ask a question this time?”

Maya raised her hand as she spoke.

She did not actually need anyone’s permission, but Ficho nodded as though granting her the right.

“Go ahead.”

Maya smiled tightly and looked at Shirone.

Waiting for the question, Shirone swallowed. What would she ask?

At first, he had thought it was nothing, but now that his turn had come, his heart could not help beating faster.

‘All I have to do is choose Amy. We already agreed on it. No matter what question comes up, there’s no burden.’

Maya’s eyes shone as she asked, “Which girl is so lovely that you want to hug her and kiss her right now?”

“Well… huh?”

Shirone’s eyes trembled.

This time, even Amy could not hide her flustered expression, as if she had not expected that question.

Shirone stared at Maya’s mischievous smile.

This was not a matter of choosing an answer. The question itself had been chosen on the assumption that the answer was already known.

‘What should I do? Should I just name someone else?’

He tried his best to think, but in the end, there seemed to be no better solution than sticking to the original plan.

“I-I…”

Shirone lowered his blushing face and pointed at Amy.

Amy, unable to endure the embarrassment, quietly turned her head and looked off toward the distant mountains.

“Wow! Really? Were you two actually dating?”

Eider made an exaggerated fuss as he pressed Shirone.

In fact, rumors had been circulating in the senior class that Shirone and Amy’s relationship was fake.

In the senior year, students avoided dating on campus. The odds of lovers joining hands and both making it into the final ten were extremely low.

Ficho clapped his hands, livening up the atmosphere.

“Hey, but honestly, that takes courage, doesn’t it? Since we’re already talking about it, why don’t you show us?”

Ignoring him, Shirone turned to Maya. She was just laughing as though she found it amusing.

Why had Maya asked him that question?

After this moment, he would never have a chance to hear her reason, but a question like this did not pressure a freshman, nor did it seem especially interesting to her.

The Courage of Truth continued, and finally, it was Luman’s turn to ask Iruki a question.

Luman, who had been eating a bag of potato chips while waiting, stuffed the last crumbs into his mouth and said, “Then I’ll ask you a question. Which girl in Class Two do you think is definitely not a virgin?”

Pony’s golden eyebrows slanted sharply.

“How vulgar…”

This was why she disliked the senior class tradition. Why should a person of royal blood be treated as a candidate for a question like this?

Regardless of Pony’s thoughts, Luman elaborated, as though he was not satisfied yet.

“I mean, a woman who looks like she’d roll around with any guy.”

The women’s unpleasant rage seemed ready to flare up.

This was one of the reasons Luman could not get out of Class Two despite his excellent crowd control skills.

“Hmm, I see.”

Iruki rested his chin on his hand and began searching for his own reasoning.

Of course, there was no way to know just from appearance. However, choosing purely based on intuition was not in his nature either.

Excluding Amy, there were four women in Class Two: Pony, Maya, Suabi, and Dorothy.

Iruki looked at Pony’s face first. As if insulted by that alone, Pony gently turned her head away.

‘She’s royalty…’

Pony’s first impression was that of a cold and lofty ice flower.

If she was royalty, then at the very least, she would not be mingling casually with common nobles. In terms of standard deviation, the male group was bound to shrink around her.

‘First of all, I’ll set her aside. What about Maya?’

Maya watched with excitement to see what answer would come out. She herself was one of the candidates, but she seemed to be simply enjoying the situation.

‘She’s enjoying this too openly. That’s a bit…’

Iruki turned his gaze to Suabi.

With her brown hair braided short, she had not said a word from the start of the senior-year event until now, nor had she made eye contact with anyone.

Her personality seemed as gentle as her rabbit-like appearance, and now she was trembling with fear.

‘She’s too shy. Isn’t her major Utility?’

Utility magic was an auxiliary class that supported other wizards rather than doing something alone.

If Sound magic was strong in wide-range buffs, Utility focused on personal enhancement and had excellent effects.

In the professional world, it was the first class sought out for a party. However, since it was more dependent than crowd control, not many wizards chose it as their major.

Since she had to work with numerous wizards, Suabi’s timid personality was certainly surprising.

However, seen from another angle, it could also mean she had chosen her major because she had low self-esteem and a passive personality.

‘She’s very anxious. On the other hand…’

Iruki moved his gaze to Dorothy last.

She was a girl with oversized glasses and vaguely open eyes.

One could say she was pretty, but she did not seem to care about her appearance at all.

She was an eccentric girl who always carried around a tin doll made of scrap metal to manipulate.

Shirone also took a special interest in Dorothy.

Having heard about Jonah from Fleur, Shirone thought this year’s Dorothy looked quite different from last year’s.

‘That doll moved by an algorithm. I thought it was something like Harvest.’

Iruki finished thinking.

His finger extended to the right and pointed at Dorothy.

“I’ll choose you.”

Everyone’s eyes focused on her, but Dorothy did not react. It was as if she alone were living in another world.

Iruki sheepishly lowered his hand.

After a while, she returned to reality and opened her mouth.

“Why me?”

Iruki nodded as though it was a good question.

“What I observed was resignation and resistance. If someone were seriously promiscuous, they wouldn’t want to reveal it to others. By that standard, you were the best at blocking your emotions.”

Dorothy drifted off into another world again, as if asking what he expected her to do about it. Only after ten seconds did she come back and open her mouth.

“Right.”

The boys turned to Dorothy with surprised eyes.

She did not have to answer. In the Courage of Truth, only the person being questioned was obligated to respond. But now that she had confessed it herself, everyone was curious about what she would say next.

“I love all men.”

Dorothy met Iruki’s eyes for the first time.

“Iruki. I love you too.”

Iruki’s eyelids opened and closed rapidly.

“And I love women too.”

“Huh?”

Someone blurted out a question.

Regardless, Dorothy slowly lowered her eyes as she hugged the tin doll.

“But I hate humans.”

“…”

The gazes that had been focused on Dorothy scattered in different directions. It was an instinctive reaction.

‘What is she saying? Is she crazy?’

Shirone was just as flustered. Did he really have to compete with people like this from now on?

And Class One had not even stepped forward yet.

From the very first day, he realized this would not be easy.
