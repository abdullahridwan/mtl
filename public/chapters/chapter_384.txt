Chapter 384  
Magic Association (2)  
~*~

After getting ready and stepping out into the hall, Shirone found the guards lined up in front of Flew. He hesitated for a moment, then slipped into the back of the formation.

As if that was all the answer she needed, Flew glanced at him and began giving the day’s instructions.

“This is the Magic Association, the cradle of the kingdom’s magic. Great wizards work here, but that also means criminals are drawn here, and the risk of terrorism is high. Today, we will secure the third floor so tightly that not even an ant can get through. Remember: the Magic Association is the power of the nation.”

Flew swept her gaze over the guards’ faces and shouted, “Now disperse to your assigned positions!”

The guards scattered to their respective areas, leaving only Shirone in the hallway. Flew crooked a finger at him and turned away.

“You follow me. From now on, our job is to patrol and manage the facilities. If you see anyone suspicious, check them. If they don’t have a registration certificate approved by the Association, you may arrest them on the spot.”

Even to Shirone, that sounded like tremendous authority. On the other hand, he doubted there were many suspicious people wandering in and out of the World Magic Association.

As expected, Flew’s words amounted to little more than a warning. For the next hour or so, there was almost nothing to do. Guards greeted her as she passed, and when minor incidents occurred, she simply took a look, gave a few words, and continued her patrol.

Isn’t this incredibly comfortable?

Shirone wondered if this was what people meant by a secure, cushy position.

Of course, he did not know that the reason the job seemed so easy was because generations of outstanding wizards had passed through this place and built its security to such a level.

At that moment, a lamp on the ceiling rotated, flashing red. A siren blared for about three seconds, then cut off with a pop, as if someone had blocked the device.

Startled, Shirone looked around. Flew, however, stood calmly and waited.

A guard came running around the corner of the hallway, though his expression was not particularly tense.

“Chief of Security.”

“What happened? The siren went off.”

“Well…” The guard scratched his head. “The board on the magic control device overheated and burned out the internal circuit. I’ve already contacted the maintenance team.”

Almost every district of the Magic Association restricted magic through magic control devices. Due to technical limitations, they were said to be ineffective against wizards of second-class rank or higher, but even so, they prevented most acts of terrorism and helped reduce damage from the occasional clash between employees.

“Hmm. That thing keeps causing trouble.” Flew clicked her tongue and gestured to Shirone. “Hey, follow me. Let’s go.”

When Flew arrived at the machine room, alchemists and wizards were already opening up the wall panels and working frantically.

Shirone looked at the magic control device’s board with interest. The technology had been introduced into the Magic School’s 2,000 system, but this was his first time seeing the internal circuitry.

As expected, it was a concentration of precise magic engineering. Shirone was now at the level of entering the graduating class, but even so, he could not properly analyze the circuit.

Flew approached the maintenance team and asked, “How is it? Can it be fixed?”

“Ah, Ms. Flew. This is going to take some time. The board is completely burned out. Now that I think about it, this is already the third time this year. There seems to be something wrong with the auxiliary adjustment device. I think we’ll have to tear it out completely.”

Flew’s expression turned serious. She was in charge of the third floor, and the magic control device there was not working properly.

“I’ve already made a recommendation to the higher-ups. It will probably be replaced with a new one during this quarter’s maintenance, but until then, can’t we handle it with a temporary repair?”

No matter how powerful the Magic Association was, it could not allow gaps in security. A broken magic control device meant a serious hole in the third floor’s defenses, and it could not be overlooked.

“If we repair it, it should last another month. However, the Mutol has melted, and the Janes device needs to be replaced as well. The problem is that those are non-supply items, so the Association doesn’t keep them in stock. We’ll have to file a request.”

“Hmm. But if we request them now, it’ll take too long…”

Flew bit her nail as she thought.

The magic control device was directly related to security, so it fell under her jurisdiction. Normally, even non-supply items were kept in stock somewhere, but since the storage was on the eighteenth floor, whoever needed them first effectively had priority.

Flew turned to Shirone. After watching his blank face for a while as he studied the board, she finally made up her mind.

“Hey, you.”

“Yes? Me?”

Flew wiggled her fingers, telling him to come closer. When Shirone approached, she pulled him to a corner of the machine room and lowered her voice so the maintenance team could not hear.

“Run an errand for me.”

“An errand…?”

“Ask for a carriage and go to Gold Street. If you turn right at the central fountain, you’ll find the Achim Alchemy Company right away, got it? They’re the Association’s designated business partner. Go in and pick up a few things.”

Flew took a notebook from her inner pocket and wrote down the names of the components. Then she tore the page out with a crisp snap and handed it to Shirone.

Holding the note, Shirone read through the unfamiliar names one by one. It was a simple errand, but since he was not used to this kind of work, he felt a little uneasy.

“All right. I’ll go right away. But how do I pay?”

“You don’t. The Association pays in a lump sum at the end of the month. For now, just show them this.”

Flew handed Shirone the employee ID hanging around her neck. It had a serial number written on it, and beside the name Ravid Flew, her official eighth-grade rank was engraved in bronze.

The picture had been taken two years ago, when she had just joined the Association. In it, she wore huge, unfashionable glasses, making her almost unrecognizable compared to now.

Flew’s face flushed red. She glared at him sharply and snapped, “Hey! Why are you staring at someone else’s photo like that? Put it away already!”

“Oh, sorry.”

Shirone quickly slipped the employee ID into his inner pocket.

For a woman, certain photographs could be terrifying. Flew immediately regretted handing it over, but it was too late now.

Damn it. I should have changed it right away.

She had been meaning to replace the picture for a long time, but she always got buried in work and forgot.

Embarrassed, Flew cleared her throat and straightened Shirone’s clothes.

“Anyway, this works out. Don’t wear it around your neck just to show off. Keep it in your pocket, no exceptions. A Magic Association employee ID isn’t something just anyone can carry.”

Shirone also knew the importance of a Magic Association employee card.

More than ninety percent of the kingdom’s accredited institutions would open their doors to it, and more than sixty percent of Red Line-affiliated facilities could be used free of charge by a staff member with the same color card. In addition, within a designated area, its holder could exercise the authority to arrest and prosecute all classes except the royal family. At the request of a state agency, during territorial inspections, it could also authorize duties such as eavesdropping, wiretapping, infiltration, special attack, and special missions without restriction.

From the outside, it looked like an ordinary plastic card, but quantum cryptographic code was integrated inside it, making duplication impossible.

Knowing he was carrying something with such immense authority made Shirone’s body stiffen. After checking once more that the employee ID was safely in his pocket, he finally nodded.

“Yes. I’ll be careful.”

Shirone turned and left the Magic Association. It was a chance to ride the elevator he had dreamed about, but nervous over the unfamiliar errand, he took the familiar stairs instead.

When he arrived at Gold Street by carriage, countless shops were arranged in concentric circles. Roads stretched in eight directions, and each area dealt in different kinds of goods.

The words Alchemy Street were written on the central sign. Following the arrow to the right, Shirone soon found a building that looked five times larger than an ordinary one.

This is it. Achim Alchemy Company.

When he opened the door and entered, he saw eight rows of shelves, each twenty meters long. No two items on the shelves were the same, and even Shirone, a student of magic school, did not know what most of them were used for.

If Nade came here, his eyes would roll back in his head. I’ll have to bring him after graduation.

The counter was positioned farthest from the entrance. There were about twenty customers inside, and in one corner, alchemists sat at tables examining dozens of items.

The person watching the counter was a young man with an intelligent air. Judging from the book he was reading, titled Soft Intermediate, he seemed to be either an alchemist or someone aspiring to become one.

“Excuse me, I have something to ask.”

As Shirone approached, the man closed the book and stood.

“Yes. What are you looking for?”

“I’m from the Magic Association. Do you have these?”

Shirone placed the note Flew had written on the counter and pushed it forward. With the types and quantities of the items listed, it seemed impossible to explain them verbally anyway.

The man glanced over the note and quickly understood.

“It looks like the magic control device overheated. I’ll get these for you. By the way, may I check your employee ID? Transactions with the Association are settled at the end of the month.”

“Ah, yes. Here.”

Shirone took out Flew’s employee ID.

The man reached beneath the counter and placed the card on a reader. A beep sounded, followed by an approval sign. The man returned the employee ID to Shirone and said, “Thank you for confirming. Please wait a moment.”

The man took a thin metal box and moved along the shelves, placing items inside. Some alchemy components were difficult to handle, so he could not simply toss them in.

While waiting, Shirone drummed his fingers lightly on the counter, hummed to himself, and looked around. Then, as his gaze shifted toward the alchemy books, two men in black hoods quickly turned their heads away.

What? Why are they wearing hoods? Are they wizards too?

Shirone watched them a little longer, but the two showed no further suspicious behavior. Pretending to search for something, they disappeared beyond the shelves on the other side.

Once they had escaped Shirone’s gaze, the hooded men entered a corner with no people nearby. The man standing on the right whispered in a low voice.

“Damn it. He’s from the Magic Association. What do we do? It’ll be hard to act here.”

“There are always risks in carrying out a revolution. Let’s just do it here.”

“But if we fail, we can’t save our brothers. Let’s not. We should move. Let’s go to the Gold Tower. You created an infiltration route for situations like this.”

“Tch. Fine. I was planning to save that place until the end.”

The hooded figures quietly left the alchemy shop.

Shirone waited at the counter for about five minutes. The man returned with the collected items, placed the metal box on the counter, and handed it over. There were various components inside, but the box was surprisingly small and did not seem heavy.

“Here are the goods. We also offer delivery, but it won’t arrive until after five in the evening. If you’re in a hurry, you can take it now.”

“Then I’ll take it with me. It doesn’t look very heavy.”

“Please be careful when handling it. Some of the items are sensitive to shocks.”

“Yes, I’ll be careful.”

Shirone carefully carried the box as if he were holding a newborn baby and left the store. He did not know exactly how careful he needed to be, so he moved with the utmost caution.

When he came out onto the main road, he looked around for a carriage.

Just then, a deafening bang erupted from across the block. The ground lurched, and screams rushed in like a tidal wave.

“What?”

From the many alleys, people poured onto the main road. The faces of those at the front were filled with terror, and the shock of what had just happened was still engraved in their half-dilated pupils.

“Terrorists! The Gold Tower exploded!”

“Terrorists?”

Shirone looked up at the smoke rising into the sky.

A terrorist attack in Baska, the capital of the kingdom.

It was something he had never even imagined.

The citizens reacted in various ways. Most stood frowning as they looked toward the Gold Tower, but those with places to be cursed under their breath and pushed forward, urging others out of their way.
