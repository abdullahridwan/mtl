Chapter 372  
Main Character of Life (4)  
~*~

Released from the Law, the tourists regained their human forms—and were horrified to find themselves naked.

An uproar broke out at once. The women screamed and covered themselves, while the men panicked and searched for places to hide. Shirone’s party asked the people outside the city wall for help and somehow managed to evacuate everyone to an inn.

Shirone sat on the railing of a café terrace, watching the tourists packed into each building.

Some were threatening to sue the tourism office. Others were shouting for the manager.

They had also encountered beings from heaven. There was no telling what kind of repercussions today’s events would bring.

Shirone turned to look at Kang Nan. She was facing the tourists as well, but her mind seemed to be somewhere else.

“What now? Are you going to persuade them?”

“Is there any need?”

“What?”

“We don’t have to worry about trifles. People from the Association will come. They’ll handle it.”

“But it’ll be difficult to clean this up if too much time passes. What if rumors spread?”

“We just need to spread the story as if the Ice Queen myth was real. Whether people believe it or not, the information will get muddied. And even if the truth gets out, it doesn’t matter. Ordinary people wouldn’t be able to do anything with it anyway.”

Kang Nan had seen situations like this many times. Whenever information about heaven leaked into the world, people assumed chaos would follow, but she could say with certainty that nothing would happen.

People would still worry about what to eat that day, wake up anxious about an important contract tomorrow, and fret over how to raise their rapidly growing children.

Kang Nan looked back at Zocre and the others. Though she had given them no particular orders, they remained close to her, not daring to leave.

They knew it, too. This was not a situation they could resolve.

To Kang Nan, however, their presence was simply a nuisance.

“The Association will decide what happens to you as well. So stop following me and go away. If you want to run, go ahead.”

Zocre and the others bowed their heads gloomily.

They understood perfectly well what it meant to make an enemy of the Magic Association. Even if they went to prison, their wizard licenses would not be revoked as long as they remained on the Red Line. But the moment they fled, the only place left for them would be the Black Line. And in that world of people swallowed by madness, Zocre and his party did not have the strength to survive.

“Yes. We’re sorry. When the Association members arrive, we’ll surrender.”

Amy silently watched as Zocre and the others walked away. This was the life they had to live. No matter what happened, a person had to keep living, and to become the main character of their own life, they had to fight fiercely until the end.

After taking care of a few more matters, Kang Nan prepared to leave. Before she spoke, she stared at Shirone.

Now that she had released Keumgang, she looked much younger than Shirone had expected. Of course, Kang Nan was not foolish enough to judge someone by appearance.

Still, if Shirone entered the Association, that trait might become a headache.

That was why Gaold’s proposal was so unconventional.

“Shirone. We need to leave now. I’ll wait in the carriage, so hurry and follow me.”

“Yes. I’ll be there soon.”

Shirone planned to follow Kang Nan to the Magic Association. Part of the reason was that he had not yet had a chance to meet Gaold face-to-face, but he was also curious about the circumstances surrounding the labyrinth.

‘If I go to the capital, I’ll be able to meet Lian. And Tess too.’

Shirone put on his backpack and approached Amy.

“I’m sorry. Things ended up like this again.”

He could hardly bring himself to face her. He had wanted her to enjoy this vacation properly, yet once again, he had dragged her into danger.

“I’m telling you now—I’m never traveling with you again.”

“Haha! I’m really sorry. I have nothing to say.”

Amy smiled and shook her head.

“Thank you, Shirone. Thanks to you, I’m confident again. I think I can work hard now.”

Shirone had felt it too. Amy had not merely subdued Zocre’s party—she had used Inferno, a spell of such extreme heat that it was difficult for someone her age to manifest at all.

She must have had a realization during the battle, but Shirone did not ask about it. If there were a hundred people, there were a hundred different realizations, and no one else could follow Amy’s style. One day, the time would come for her to speak about it and for him to listen.

“I’ll call you when I arrive. And Dante, thank you for doing me this favor. Please take good care of Amy.”

Dante shrugged. He had somehow ended up escorting his rival’s girlfriend, but it was not a loss for him. Heaven, the time-space of the labyrinth—Shirone and Amy seemed to know something about it. There would be plenty of time on the road, and Dante planned to ask Amy about heaven.

“I don’t know what’s going on, but have a safe trip.”

“Yeah. Then I’ll see you at school.”

After Shirone left, Dante and Amy also turned around. On the way toward the exit, Dante pointed to the café and asked,

“Coffee before we go? Your treat, of course.”

“How far are you going to take this? You buy it! You have a lot of money!”

“Hey, now. I’m escorting you all the way home. At least coffee should be—huh?”

Dante sensed someone’s presence and looked behind him.

Lyria was standing there with a large backpack on her back, just as she had been when he first saw her.

Now that she had sealed the Ruler, she had to return to the Acheanis Shrine and make her report. She probably thought she would never see Dante again after today. A wizard and a mage had different paths from the very beginning.

Even so, Lyria still had regrets. If she had not met Dante, this mission would most likely have failed. It was difficult to imagine a wandering mage sharing feelings with someone, but because she had relied on him so much, she could not cut away those emotions as cleanly as she would with a sword.

“I’ll buy you coffee.”

“Hmm.”

Dante read the affection plainly revealed in Lyria’s eyes. She truly was a woman who could not hide her emotions. Dante was not the type to enjoy dating for its own sake, but he was also not so cautious that he would reject a woman who came to him. He put an arm around Lyria’s shoulders and pointed somewhere nearby.

“Great. Forget the coffee. Shall we go somewhere quiet?”

Lyria’s face heated up as she followed the direction Dante was pointing.

To her shock, there was a sign for an inn.

Lyria, inexperienced as she was, could not help but be stunned by his direct approach to romance.

“Wh-what are you… saying… all of a sudden…?”

“Haha! No need to play coy. You’re old enough to know how things work. Let’s not waste time.”

Lyria let out a low groan. She had thought she could accept his self-centered personality, but his attitude toward women was absolutely unacceptable.

“You flirt! Let go of me!”

When Lyria pushed him away, Dante obediently stepped back.

“A flirt? We haven’t even done anything. Aren’t you getting ahead of yourself?”

“Forget it! I suddenly came to my senses! I don’t need any of this. I’m going back to the shrine!”

Dante laughed. If their relationship only went this far, he had no reason to hold her back.

“Sure. Be careful.”

‘You indifferent jerk…’

When Dante truly let her go, Lyria felt a pang of sadness. But she did not look back. If she turned her head now, she felt as though she really would not be able to leave.

“If anything interesting happens, write to me!”

At the sound of Dante’s voice behind her, Lyria blinked, then burst into laughter.

Air servant Dante. Eighteen years old.

Second in the order of aspiring wizards in the kingdom of Tormia.

‘Hehe. I’ll remember.’

Leaving Lyria to walk toward the horizon with a bright expression on her face, Dante and Amy headed for the exit again.

They were about to leave the ruins of the castle, which Amy now hated even looking at, when they found Zocre and his party waiting by the ticket gate.

Their faces were solemn. They could not completely hide their fear, but they seemed determined not to appear weak.

Vivian approached Amy.

“I’m sorry.”

Amy waved a hand. This incident was not child’s play, but the fact that they would be arrested made the situation even.

“It’s okay. You and I fought according to our convictions. You don’t have to apologize just because you lost.”

“No. In the end, you saved me. If I had died then, there would have been no next time.”

“That’s…”

Amy shyly turned her head away. To be honest, she had not done it solely for Vivian. Vivian had been the one who allowed her Jade King Park Yojin to succeed, and the words Amy had shouted at the end were words she had wanted to say to herself.

“I don’t know why you said that to me, but do your best. Make sure you become a wizard in the next graduation exam. You deserve to be the main character.”

Amy’s eyes stung. She knew it was a cruel thought, but those words from the mouth of someone she had defeated gave her more confidence than any comfort she had ever received.

Vivian’s shoulders drooped helplessly as she returned to her friends. Amy watched the miserable loser for a moment, then opened her mouth.

“Hey.”

When Vivian turned around, Amy slipped one shoulder strap off her bag and rummaged through her pack.

“Here. Take this.”

In Amy’s hand was the Ice Queen doll she had won as a prize in the mini-game room.

Vivian took it with trembling hands and stared at the Ice Queen for a long time. The Ice Queen—the one person in Vivian’s life who seemed not to care about anyone else’s gaze—was smiling as brightly as a child.

“Sniff. Hic…”

Vivian burst into tears and hugged the Ice Queen to her chest. Zocre came over and patted her on the back. Then he looked at Amy for a moment before leading Vivian away.

Amy watched them leave for a long time. They would fight to the end for the sake of their own lives. If so, wouldn’t the day come when they could become the main characters too?

‘Yes. I’ve only failed once. If this is my life, then I’ll fight and win.’

Amy turned around with her fists clenched. Unlike yesterday, her steps toward Dante did not hesitate.
