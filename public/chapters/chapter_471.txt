Chapter 471  
The Day of the Full Moon (5)  
~*~

Shirone and Flew peered at the warehouse where the captured rebels were being held. Reisis’s men, accompanied by Akamai, guarded the iron gate.

“What should we do? If I get close, I’ll be taken down.”

“It seems Akamai forms contracts with its users through blood ties. We’ll have to eliminate the user first.”

Three Akamai floated among seven men. It was impossible to tell which person each was bound to just by looking at the distance.

“Should I take them all out at once?”

“It’s a matter of timing. If we give the enemy time to react, we’ll get bitten.”

“What about using teleportation to get in?”

“That’s the simplest way, but I’m not sure. They’re Norse wizards too. If they’re aides to Reisis, their skills must be excellent.”

Shirone thought of Armand. After being captured by Jin Akamai, the magic sword had been taken away, but there was something Reisis didn’t know. Armand, integrated into the Ultima system, was connected across time and space by a single signal, so information could be shared regardless of distance.

“I’ll call Armand instead.”

If he transformed into the Geumgang Armed Forces, he could eliminate the guards before Akamai activated. It was entirely possible if his physical ability matched Ikasawa’s.

“But I’ll get the location. What if they still don’t know we escaped? The more time, the better.”

“Why assume they haven’t figured it out already? If we free the rebels anyway, the location will be revealed.”

There was truth in Shirone’s words. The later Reisis realized, the higher the operation’s success rate, but it was dangerous to base strategy on uncertain assumptions.

“Hmm, fine. Let’s do it. But don’t make a mistake.”

Shirone drew his hands back and attempted normal control. He couldn’t accurately gauge Armand’s condition, but its location came through as a vague feeling.

*Come this way.*

He felt a sense of unity with Armand, as if he had become a magnet. As he amplified the feeling, Armand flew into his grasp, its trajectory bending at a right angle along the corridor.

Geumgang Armed Forces.

Shirone took a deep breath, watched the guards’ reactions, and then threw himself forward without hesitation.

“What?”

A hazy afterimage swirled before their eyes—the last thought of the guards in their lives. As Shirone’s body rotated, the tentacles sliced everyone’s throats and passed through at even greater speed.

Chin. Turtle tuck.

Shirone frowned slightly at the unpleasant sensation that surged through Armand. He had no hesitation in punishing those who carried out cruel biological experiments, but it felt different from magic.

*Calm down. This mission isn’t just for me.*

Flew watched the Akamai’s reactions. The users were dead, but if the blood bonds still lingered, there was a chance of a delayed response.

*Should I deal with them? Or are they just scrap now?*

Shirone attacked the Akamai without hesitation. As his robe fluttered, a black afterimage swept around. The three Akamai fell to the floor, split in half like soft tropical fruit.

Flew stuck out his tongue at the speed; he couldn’t even react.

*The Ultima System… definitely amazing. At that level, it’ll be more than helpful when we fight Heaven.*

Shirone and Flew exchanged glances in front of the iron gate. When Shirone unlocked it and opened the door, a sharp gale blew from inside the warehouse.

“Keugh!”

Armand’s robe, strong against impact but weak against cutting force, was shredded just before it could be cut. Feeling the pain of the slash, Shirone hurriedly shrank back and looked inside.

Dozens of glowing pupils appeared.

“Krrrr.”

The warehouse didn’t hold the captured rebels. Or maybe it did—Shirone recognized some faces. But their appearances had completely changed, with only a few traces of their former humanity remaining.

Flew, who had dodged the surprise attack with the Dawkins algorithm, frowned and clutched his aching shoulder.

“Like a wicked woman…”

Reisis had turned all the useless rebels into soldiers. Though their bodies weren’t made with a Fuzix machine, the physical ability from raising their telomere levels to 5 was not negligible.

“Kuaaaaa!”

The Norr crew members who had lost their reason rushed at Shirone. Over a hundred of them poured out of the warehouse, and not one retained the intelligence they’d once had.

Wide!

A curtain of light struck the monsters, tearing their cheeks and snapping the bones in their limbs. But the monsters didn’t stop charging.

*What’s going on?*

Flesh pushed out from the destroyed areas, and organs regenerated in an instant.

*Infinite cell proliferation sensor.*

Flew, realizing the monsters’ condition, said, “They’re equipped with cell regeneration. They won’t go down from any kind of attack.”

The monsters split into two groups, targeting Shirone and Flew, but the burden of battle from the victims’ perspective wasn’t halved. Telomeres are the ends of chromosomes that determine an organism’s limits, and LACES had enhanced bodily functions by strengthening that part. Moreover, by combining the traits of Kenser, which has no telomere limit, they had created soldiers that neither rotted nor died.

Originally, this was a tactic intended to produce troops equivalent to the Kuroi of Mecca for the war against Heaven, but now it was acting as a major biological weapon to punish the light of Area 73.

“Kuaaa!”

People with snakes for eyes, people with one arm drooping like an earthworm, people who had turned into wolf-like beasts—all with different forms and abilities, but their ferocity was second to none.

Armand’s tentacles moved at great speed, slicing their flesh, but Shirone felt like he was swinging against a wall.

*At this rate, I’ll tire out first.*

The monsters, gradually closing the distance, pressed down on Shirone with a wall of flesh.

“Kurrrrr!”

As the monsters crushed Shirone like a grave, the one at the top that hadn’t yet tasted flesh let out a pitiful cry.

Kwadeuk!

Then the tomb shook.

Quadduk! Quadd Deuk!

The shaking intensified, and a monstrous scream rose from the pile of flesh as if from the abyss.

Quad de de de de de de de deuc!

The sounds of bones breaking, muscles being mashed, blood and internal organs mingling—all blended together, and at last they all began to be sucked toward the center.

“Aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa … !”

The last remaining monster crumpled like paper and vanished into a black orb.

Shirone got to his feet, struggling to stand. His body convulsed after casting a dark ball three times larger than usual.

“Whoa!”

Since he had pushed his mental transcendence to the limit in Immortal Function, the aftermath was considerable.

“Shirone, are you okay?”

Flew came running. The area where he had fought was strewn with numerous corpses burned like coals. If Shirone was a hard puncher who competed with power, Flew was an irregular who fought with precise algorithms. No matter how good Kenser’s regenerative ability was, it couldn’t withstand being set ablaze with a phoenix pavilion and having time dragged out with the Dawkins algorithm.

“It’s okay. I just overloaded a bit.”

Flew felt a slight chill as he looked down the hallway where five tons of mass had vanished without a trace. Gather the monsters in one place and cast a dark ball. It was a brute-force tactic that didn’t seem like an anomaly.

“In the end, the rebels who were supposed to help us ended up like this. Now it’s just the two of us. We have to stop Ilhwa from drinking.”

Shirone had no intention of retreating either. Besides punishing Reisis, he had a promise to return to Ikasa.

“There! Catch them!”

Reisis’s aides appeared from the corner of the hallway. They were elite fighters who had begun searching for Armand after it left its original position. But what was most disheartening was that each of them was accompanied by an Akamai.

At first, they reflexively took fighting stances, but the same thought crossed their minds.

*Damn! Too late!*

When the Akamai’s eyes flashed open and the Antithesis activated, Shirone and Flew’s mobility dropped sharply. As if they had been born as a species that couldn’t move from the start, they felt lethargic, with no strength in their muscles.

*No, but it’s weaker than being guarded behind bars. With this, I can still land a blow.*

The problem was timing. They had to either decapitate three people at once with a single attack, or kill three Akamai.

*I have to wait until everyone enters the horizontal cutting trajectory.*

When Shirone and Flew couldn’t move, Reisis’s aides relaxed their expressions and approached.

“Whoa, we finally caught you, you rat-like things. At least we almost made it to the goal.”

When the plan went wrong, Reisis became extremely violent, and seven deaths had already been swept away while they were by her side. If they let Shirone and Flew go now, they would suffer the same.

A bald man approached with a fishy smile. He, who had lost his pride to Flew in the affinity measurement, smiled bitterly as he savored the reversal.

“Hey, we end up meeting like this in the end.”

“You piece of trash. Aren’t you afraid of Heaven after doing this?”

“Heaven? I don’t know about that, but this is Heaven. It means this isn’t a place for people from remote lands to strut around.”

The bald man stroked Flew’s chin.

“Hmm, after all, the real thing is different.”

“Get your hands off me.”

“Hehe, don’t be like that. All your clones are already dead anyway. You only have one life—you should cherish it.”

Flew kept an eye on the bald man’s movements while meticulously calculating the positions of everyone in the hallway.

*Just a little more.*

He wouldn’t strike until the three Akamai were within range. But the smirking man leaning against the corner showed no sign of coming all the way.

“Hehe, we only need to take Shirone anyway. So rather here… … !”

“Kiaaaagh!”

A black object suddenly shot out from around the corner, grabbing the man and slamming him to the ground. Then, like a swarm of insects, black things flooded the hallway, quickly turning and glaring at them.

“Aaaaaagh!”

The creature that had knocked the man down pulled its belly together and shook its torso, causing its legs to stick out from around the corner.

“Oh, no! Don’t! Aaaaaa!”

It was Garas. It looked completely different from the one Shirone had seen in the containment room, but then again, none of the Garas gathered here looked the same. Garas attacked all living things, regardless of species.

“Kyik! Kiik!”

The Akamai that was grabbed by Garas let out a loud noise, its pupils widening, and it trembled and fell. The Garas, which had been bound by the Antithesis for a moment, screamed as if vomiting resentment for missing their breeding ground, then charged down the hallway.

“Th-this… … !”

The bald man’s face turned pale at the sight of Garas’s charge, a creature that mixed the characteristics of all kinds of beings. It wasn’t even a threat that could be dealt with using magic. Even if his whole body suffered egg death, it was certain they would plant the second generation in his stomach.

Shirone pulled Flew with the strength he had been saving for a counterattack. As he planted his tentacles into the wall and widened the distance, he could see the gruesome scene at a glance.

Dark creatures teemed in the encroaching hallway. The bald man crawled out from beneath the darkness and stretched his hand toward Shirone.

“P-please… … .”

It was an expression seen only at the end of despair.

“Kill me.”

“Hey hey! Hee hee hee!”

“Aaaaaagh!”

The bald man’s upper body was lifted wide, and surprise, shock, and fear flooded his face all at once.

Shirone and Flew felt their hair stand on end. It was fortunate they couldn’t see what Garas was doing because it was covered in darkness.

“Let’s go, Shirone.”

As Flew tugged at his collar, Shirone also rolled his eyes, looked at the man’s drooling face for a moment, then turned away.

The maw of desire was devouring the headquarters.
