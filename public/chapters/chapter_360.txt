Chapter 360  
Midwinter Night (3)  
~*~

“So, what? Do you have any other ideas?”  
Lyria drew her totem and held it out.  
“I’ll isolate this area with the law while the cerebral body stays. I need to install a gold margin, so help me out. Do you know how to calculate a three-dimensional magic square?”  

Dante shrugged as if it were no big deal.  
“What…? Because it’s my specialty.”  
Since she majored in magic circles, she was qualified to assist with Lyria’s work.  

Still, Dante had her doubts.  
Would this be enough?  
Even if the mental body was isolated in the old castle ruins, confining it later to the barracks was a completely different matter.  

“Gold margin—can I finish it by tomorrow morning?”  
“That depends on your skill level, but if you and I split the work, it’s probably possible. But why?”  

Dante glanced back at the still-lit shopping street.  
Countless couples were wandering the streets there, following the light like moths.  
“I think this side needs some reinforcement.”  

* * *  

Since breaking up with Dante, Shirone and Amy had clearly become less talkative than before.  
But that didn’t mean the atmosphere was bleak.  

Amy had taken Dante’s advice and was busy thinking about how to find her own style.  
Shirone, meanwhile, couldn’t fully control the incarnation of anger lurking inside her, so her mind was complicated.  

“That was really dangerous earlier,” she said.  
Armand had lost control and aimed at a person.  
It wasn’t that Shirone’s mental training was insufficient; rather, it was clear that the size of her anger had grown stronger than before.  

Of course, it’s natural for people to change their personalities as they go through life.  
But Shirone wasn’t heading in the direction she wanted.  

“I need more guidance. While she’s on vacation, she should visit Miss Ethella and seek her advice.”  

After coming to that conclusion, Shirone visited the Snow Crystal, the lodging Shakora had told her about.  

“This is the place I was talking about.”  

Amy didn’t comment; she just observed the atmosphere from outside.  

Shirone scanned the exterior of the building.  
She was in good spirits, just as Shakora had said. The decoration was luxurious, and the view of the endless snowy field was to her liking.  

Neither she nor Amy had any complaints about the accommodation. But Shirone still had her doubts.  
There was no reason for Shirone, on her first trip, to wander in search of a special inn.  

“Why are you so insistent on this place?”  
“Yes. I recognized it, and the atmosphere is very good. You’ll be able to rest comfortably.”  

“Yes…”  

If it were Shirone, Amy didn’t need to say anything more.  
Although practical by nature, this trip was also meant to be a consolation, so Shirone must have prepared in her own way.  

On one hand, Amy felt sorry.  
On a day like today, it would have been fine to spend the night together, but Shirone’s current state of mind was indescribably complicated.  

“Welcome. This is the Snow Crystal.”  

Shirone, who opened the door and entered, was startled by the blindingly bright light.  
Numerous lights illuminated the hall like daylight.  

Passing through a passageway at right angles, there was a wide terrace where tourists were drinking tea by the torchlight of a fire pit.  
The bard’s music played.  
The desperate feelings of an ice queen who freezes everyone around her were contained in the lyrics.  

“Are there two of you?”  
The staff at the counter asked with a smile.  

“Yes, there are two of us.”  

While Shirone contracted her room with the staff, Amy was looking elsewhere from a distance.  
It didn’t matter if she was traveling with friends, but it felt strange to come into a place like this with two men.  

“Two rooms, please.”  

The clerk looked at Shirone and Amy for a moment, then nodded and placed the keys on the table.  
“Yes, use 311 and 312. Take the stairs to the third floor and turn right down the hallway. Accommodation is prepaid.”  

After paying, Shirone looked back at Amy, who slowly changed her pace and headed for the stairs.  

Shirone spoke as they walked down the hallway on the third floor.  
“First, unpack your luggage in the room. If you’re hungry, shall we go out and eat something?”  

“No. I just want to rest today. My body feels a bit heavy after walking around in the cold for so long. Are you okay?”  

If Amy was really tired, she could rest as much as she wanted, but for some reason, Shirone felt she was lying.  
She might be more upset than she expected.  

Although she understood Dante’s advice intellectually, her feelings were another matter.  
She argued about the results of the graduation exam, and when Dante asked her opinion, she realized she should have sided with Amy.  

“Amy, earlier…”  

Amy, who read Shirone’s thoughts, gave a small smile.  
“Don’t misunderstand. It’s not like that. You know I have no regrets.”  

Shirone’s heart grew heavier.  
Doesn’t the fact that the word “endgame” came up mean that there is an end in itself?  

After all, if Amy’s mind was already a mess, no words would ease her mood.  
In this case, what was needed was time, not conversation.  

“Okay then, see you tomorrow.”  
“Yes. Rest well too.”  

Amy unlocked her door with her key and entered. She placed the Shirone key in the center of the doorknob.  

When Shirone opened the door and entered, the first thing that caught her eye was the endless snowy field outside the window.  
A few hundred meters away, a small torch flickered like a dot, and people were ice fishing there.  

How nice it would have been to see this scene with Amy.  
At the thought, she let out a sigh.  

“Whoa.”  
“Whoa.”  

Shirone and Amy looked at each other simultaneously. Then, as if on cue, they turned toward the door.  

“What is this?”  

There were two doors, but only one room.  
And there was only one bed.  

The two looked at each other with puzzled expressions.  
Silence lingered for a while.  

Shirone’s mind was flooded with all sorts of thoughts.  
Above all, since this was the lodging she had chosen, she had to come up with an excuse.  

“Oh, what should I do? Should I ask for a room change? But all the rooms here are like this. Should I just get a refund? Wouldn’t Amy be offended by that? No, maybe she’d be the opposite? I need a refund…”  

As Shirone didn’t know what to do, her face pale, Amy let out a small sigh.  
She was acting somewhat out of place, she said. And now that the situation had come to this, the decision rested with Shirone.  

Amy went to her closet and put down her backpack.  
“I can’t do anything. Let’s unpack first.”  

“Uh? Uh, yes!”  

Shirone was still uneasy.  
As she unpacked, barely knowing what was in her bag, she asked Amy in passing,  
“Who told you about this place?”  

“Yes? What?”  

“There’s no way I would have come here knowing it was like this. Didn’t someone else introduce me to this place?”  

Shirone confessed honestly despite her embarrassment.  

“Actually, your father…”  

Amy grunted, as if she already knew.  
Since the spy was in her family, she couldn’t argue with Shirone.  

“They said the atmosphere is really good. I think you’ll like it too…”  

The night view was definitely beautiful. However, it was a dangerous place for the two of them.  

Amy, roughly organizing her clothes, pointed to the door and said,  
“Let’s go down and have dinner.”  

When Shirone and Amy left the room and came down to the counter, the staff asked with a mischievous smile,  
“Do you like the room?”  

Amy ignored the question and said,  
“I’m going to grab a quick bite. Where’s the restaurant?”  

It was around 11:00 when Shirone and Amy returned to their lodging after satisfying their hunger.  
It was as if they were trying to spend as much time outside as possible, but both knew this was the final destination.  

The whole process of washing and changing clothes was completely different from usual.  
Her body felt as heavy as wet cotton after riding in a carriage since yesterday afternoon, but her mind grew clearer and clearer.  

No one touched the only bed.  
They didn’t even discuss who would sleep in it.  
Beds were taboo here.  

The two sat side by side, looking out at the horizon where the white snow met the black night sky.  
They were able to forget reality for a while in the mystery of the night view.  

But suddenly, a strange noise from the next room pulled their minds back to reality and threw them off balance.  

When their eyes met, Shirone’s face brightened.  
Amy desperately feigned composure, avoiding her gaze.  

“What? Why is your face red?”  
“Yeah, but you seem a little flushed too.”  

Amy definitely understood.  
No matter how you looked at it, this wasn’t normal.  
If she had to continue this atmosphere, she’d rather sleep on a bed in hell.  

“Great! Let’s be honest. We’re not kids; we don’t have to pretend, right? We’re spending the night together at an inn, and I hear something going on next door. And unfortunately, there’s only one bed. This is a very strange situation. But now it’s over. I know it’s strange, so it’s not strange anymore. Admit it? Now, I’m tired, so let’s just go to sleep.”  

Shirone felt more at ease. But on the other hand, she didn’t like it either.  
How could she cut off emotions like slicing a radish?  

“Are you really going to be okay? I mean, I’m a man too.”  
“I know. But nothing will happen, right?”  

Of course something would happen.  
However, Shirone, whose pride was hurt inside, added in a hesitant voice,  
“That’s something I don’t know…”  

Amy laughed at the timid protest. She thought of her family, who must be imagining all sorts of things at home.  
She thought, ‘Anyway, the person called Dad…’  

To make them share a room with a man at an inn with such exquisite traps installed—  
This was something that would never be possible with an ordinary aristocratic mindset.  

However, the Karmis family, which valued individuality, boasted an open-minded family style even in love.  
Of course, no matter how open they were, it was unknown whether they would accept commoners.  

Even so, making this decision meant they liked Shirone that much.  
In any case, the tension eased a little when she thought that she wouldn’t have to worry about the family in the future regarding her relationship with Shirone.  

‘Come to think of it, it’s amazing.’  

The Karmis family’s crimson eyes were better than any other ability at recognizing an opponent’s temperament.  
However, it was also true that some talents went unnoticed.  

Shirone had grown at a terrifying pace to be recognized by her strict father.  
Even though he was also the master of elimination, he was put on the same level as the graduating class, as promised a year ago.  

One year.  
She had caught up to that huge gap in just one year.  

But she didn’t feel embarrassed.  
Even if Shirone were to turn her life back to the beginning and start over, she would have come to this place in the end.  

“To be honest… I was angry with you earlier. I’m sorry you didn’t stand by my side when Dante asked.”  

Shirone turned her head to look at Amy.  
“I don’t know either. I just felt sorry for myself. When I heard that Zocre had passed, I was honestly very envious. I wondered what it would have been like if I had transferred schools. I wonder if I was stubborn about a subject I had no skill in. Maybe I’m just not talented enough to go to a prestigious school.”  

“Amy, to think so…”  

“I know. If you think negatively, there’s no limit. I think that’s why I was more pissed off at you. Because I was afraid to admit I was wrong.”  

“But now you know what the problem is.”  
“Right. Dante’s analysis is inaccurate, so I’m grateful. But… I’m still not sure. Will I be able to graduate just because I have a style? I’m not confident no matter what I do.”  

‘Amy…’  

This is why defeat is scary.  
The graduating class is the top position at the magic school.  
The feeling of falling from the pinnacle was nothing compared to the feeling when the advanced classes failed the promotion test.  

This is why the probability of passing the second graduation exam for those who failed the first was lower than average.
