Final Decision (3)  
~*~

After completing the battery implementation evaluation, Shirone headed to the restaurant with her friends.

Although she had scored perfectly, there were several sections that left her uneasy, likely due to the lingering disturbance. As if her friends had already noticed, the atmosphere at the table felt different from usual. Shirone wasn’t in the mood to care about anyone else’s feelings. *When will I hear back from Gaold? No, even if they contact me, what choice would I actually make?* Everything felt unsettling.

“Shirone, what have you been worrying about lately? You don’t look so good. Are you sick?” Amy asked, setting down her cutlery.

Even aside from Shirone, all the students were feeling down, but today their faces were so pale it was hard to ignore.

“It’s fine. I’m just not feeling well.”

“Sometimes students struggle to control their condition before midterms. I was like that last year, too. Just hold on a little longer. Vacation is coming soon.”

Shirone forced a smile. As a senior who had already experienced this, Amy must have been projecting her own anxiety about the upcoming turning point of the fifteenth week.

“Shirone, here’s some water.”

Maya personally poured a glass and handed it to Shirone. It was pitiful to see her barely touching her food, just playing with her fork.

“Yeah, thank you.”

Shirone obediently accepted the glass. When dealing with others, her attitude toward Maya was unusually cautious; she carefully measured the boundaries of their relationship before letting emotions run free.

Amy pouted, watching Shirone and Maya exchange words. Maya was a kind, bright girl. It wouldn’t be a bad thing for her to become friends with Shirone. But at the same time, Amy felt a strange unease. *Do I feel like I’ve been pushed out of a space I didn’t even know I occupied?*

“What? Maya, why aren’t you eating?” Amy asked, examining Maya’s plate.

“I already finished. These days, I don’t eat as much as I used to.”

After the high-ground occupation evaluation, Maya had reduced her food intake. To be precise, her original body had returned to its natural caloric needs. Realizing she lacked talent for vocal music, Maya had decided to enhance her sonic magic using her natural voice, following Iruki’s advice. Becoming a wizard was her primary goal, the key to her future. But more important than her career path was the aspiration burning in her heart: *I want to be loved by Shirone. I want to appear before her looking more beautiful.*

Simply by stopping her binge eating, Maya’s appearance had noticeably improved. As the weight left her face, her eyes grew larger and deeper, and the bridge of her nose seemed to lift. Her once-plump features had slimmed down, her clothes hung loosely, and her shoulder line tapered gracefully. Even the upperclassmen could no longer openly ignore her. In a way, it was bittersweet, but human nature seemed helpless in the face of beauty. Nade and Iruki watched Maya’s daily transformation with quiet curiosity.

*Certainly, a lot has changed. The faces from storybooks are gradually appearing.*

With this strange tension thickening, everyone knew Shirone was a miser, but Maya was bad at hiding her emotions, so she had clearly fallen for Shirone.

Amy suddenly raised her voice. “Do you have rice now? It’s fine if you just focus on evaluating the battery today. If I hadn’t been careful, I would have made a mistake on one question. Class Two is the stage where you compete with Class One. If you make a mistake in the first half, it’ll be much harder in the second half when your stamina is low. Keep your mind straight and push through.”

Amy, who had already failed her graduation exam once, knew this was the most critical time. Even if everyone was depressed about the Magic Association incident, they had to face it with a firm sense of purpose. *It’s just rice. It’s not like dying from starvation. If you’re not alert now, you’re missing out on the biggest part of your life.* Amy hoped Shirone wouldn’t regret it.

“Shirone, try eating this too.”

Maya cut a piece of Shirone’s favorite meat into bite-sized pieces and placed it on her plate. It was an act meant to cut through the nagging worry, though it also revealed a tearful vulnerability. As the group grew closer, the question remained: did Amy truly love Shirone? Of course, Maya also wanted Shirone to become a great wizard, but she believed that what someone stressed by nightmares needed wasn’t advice, but comfort.

*It’s too much, Amy. If I were you, I’d be overjoyed. I should be able to do anything Shirone asks, but she treats me so carelessly.*

Shirone took the meat Maya had offered and put it in her mouth. She picked it up because it was in the best condition to eat, but she didn’t even register who had given it to her. All her thoughts were racing, and her head felt like it was about to explode. *Had Gaold met Alpheas? If the operation ever gets caught, continuing the evaluation as planned could be dangerous. Just as navigation doesn’t guarantee student safety, it’s foolish to be optimistic about entrusting one’s life to someone else.*

Shirone set her utensils down.

Since Amy and Maya were locked in a silent war of nerves, Nade and Iruki flinched and looked up.

“Sorry. I’ll head to the dorm first. I’m very unwell.”

Maya immediately stood up, but Shirone left the restaurant without making eye contact with anyone. Amy was just frustrated. She knew Shirone had gone to the Association. She wanted to be told first. *Like when I went to heaven, like when I went to Kazura, like when I went to the old castle ruins—I wanted you to come to me first.* *What’s the reason? Why didn’t you say anything this time?*

Maya watched the door until Shirone disappeared, then turned to Amy, biting her upper lip. There was no reason to hate Amy. What made her angry was the current situation, where even her own feelings had to be approved by someone else.

Feeling Maya’s gaze, Amy looked up, then hurriedly turned her head away. When she felt a firmer determination than ever in Maya’s eyes, she realized something had finally shifted.

“Amy, can you talk to me for a minute?”

Amy’s heart sank. She thought it couldn’t be, but deep down she knew this moment would come. When it finally arrived, she told herself, *just a little later.*

“Can’t we do it next time? I’m a bit busy today… … .”

“It only takes one minute. I’ll wait outside.”

As Maya walked away with the tray, Amy’s face went pale. But in the end, she rose from her seat with a sliver of reasoning that the future would come anyway. Nade and Iruki shrugged as they watched Amy follow Maya as if possessed.

* * *

The book *Looking into the Darkness*, which had been resting in the principal’s office library, slid itself back onto the bookshelf, tilted at a forty-five-degree angle. The lock clicked with a heavy thud from inside the closed library, accompanied by the sound of turning gears. It was a secret vault designed by Alpheas long ago to store Erina’s memories. But now, all of her belongings had been moved to the annex, leaving the room on the 3.5th floor completely empty.

Alpheas and Olivia descended the stairs side by side. Unlike Alpheas’s bitter smile, Olivia’s expression was fierce, perhaps masking nervousness.

“It’s been a while, old man.”

Gaold, sitting cross-legged on the bare floor, raised a hand resting on his chin and offered a greeting. Fleur stood guard beside him.

“Hello. My name is Fleur, a Certified Level 8 Wizard.”

“Long time no see, Fleur.”

Fleur lowered her gaze at Olivia’s greeting. He had no shame before the heavens, but socially he appeared as a traitor, so he had no face to look his teacher in the eye. *I was a clever kid, but why did someone like Gaold… … .*

He had been a student under Alpheas’s watchful eye since his days as headmaster of the Royal Magic School. But the bright road ahead was now blocked. No, he was a fugitive with no guarantee of a future. All of this was because of Gaold. How many people could one lunatic tire out and disrupt society?

Gaold looked around curiously. “This is the Panic Room that’s been rumored about. I didn’t know it actually existed, but anyway, the inspiration is cunning.”

Alpheas ignored the gossip. “Why did you come to see me?”

“This… … A disciple visited his alma mater, and it was a congratulatory order. Isn’t that too vulgar?”

“The school was turned upside down because of you alone. Not a day goes by without agents coming in and tearing it apart. If you had come just a few days sooner, everyone would have suffered.”

“Hmph, Alpheas of the world is finished. The gore that walks around here wouldn’t even know the agents came and went, and if they still had agents, they wouldn’t let me in.”

“You know that. So don’t ask me again. Why did you come to see me?”

Gaold sighed and exhaled. If he had come to ask for a difficult favor, he would have to lower his stance, but this guy’s school didn’t go far. “I’m thinking of going to Eastas.”

“Finally… … Are you saying you want to meet Miro?”

“Then I suppose you came to retrieve something you left behind?”

“Gaold, your dogma is disturbing the world. Miro also doesn’t want to see you anymore. She took it all in and went in on her own.”

“How does the inspiration know that?”

Gaold’s eyes widened. Fleur, who sensed superficial self-denial, squinted one eye, but Gaold only changed his expression in a frightening manner, without radiating any murderous intent.

“You know how many times I have sent in goal the guys approaching Eastas. Not a single person has been let in for twenty years, including me. But how do you know what Miro is thinking?”

“Gaold, Miro… … .”

“It’s okay. I will go into Eastas myself. Because we made it anyway. In fact, I have one more favor besides that. Let me take care of you.”

Alpheas asked in a resigned voice. “So, what kind of situation are you talking about?”

“You know, my limbs are all cut off. I got them all from the association. Of course, the key factors remain intact, but you can’t do anything with your head alone. Lend me someone. Become a competent guy.”

No matter how skilled Gaold was, it was impossible to carry out all the operations alone. A person with a powerful one-shot was necessary, but a minimum party had to be equipped: a tactician, a caster, and a supporter.

“You say you are at this school, the Bishop of the Karsis Monastery.”

Olivia stepped out in a huff. “Do you think we will send our precious school personnel to mission? And what you’re about to do is against the survival of mankind. Rebellion is not enough; are you trying to disturb the world now?”

“You are shutting up.”

Gaold entered the room and saw Olivia face to face for the first time. His stamina didn’t explode, but an energy that seemed to pierce the body emanated from his eyes alone. “Do you know why I’m still keeping you alive? Huh? Do you know! Please answer me.”

Olivia kept her mouth shut and glared fiercely.

“Because it’s not worth killing.”

The sound of grinding teeth came from Gaold’s mouth. “Abstention? A person from the Teachers’ Association, a student from the Kingdom, seems to be dragged into the dimension of cowardice, but what, abstaining? Do you know what they say about someone like you? Dissembler. You don’t like this or that, you have one of twenty cards that can determine the survival of mankind, and the only thing you choose is to give up?”

Olivia’s lips trembled. The gaze that had been glaring at Gaold slowly descended. “Oh, it was inevitable.”

Whatever the choice, it wasn’t the result Olivia had hoped for. No, actually, that thought was an excuse. It was just scary. He was so terrified that he couldn’t choose anything. Transparent tears formed in Olivia’s eyes, vividly conveying the sense of helplessness at the time.

Gaold turned away with an embarrassed expression. She was a second-class wizard recognized by the world, but in the end, she couldn’t even shed her skin. That’s why she was only a 2nd class Grand Wizard.

*This is why I didn’t want to come to school.*

It was all about the past here. A past that couldn’t be erased no matter how much you tried. Gaold couldn’t even remember what happened an hour ago, but the scenery reflected in his eyes on the Day of Judgment of the Twenty could clearly recall even the feelers of insects attached to leaves.

“Sigh.”

Gaold looked back at Alpheas. It was a very human expression that probably everyone who knew him would have seen for the first time. “Help me, please. Master.”

“… … .”

There was a day called the Twenty Judges. The day when twenty people representing the world and Miro’s position gathered to decide the future of mankind. The result was sixteen votes in favor, one against, and three abstentions. There was only one person, Mirhi Alpheas, who valued one life more than the lives of all mankind. The only one on Gaold’s side, he gladly carved opposition into his cards.

“You know. I don’t trust anyone but Master. Help me. You have to find Miro. It’s not even dead. He’s still alive there, so why the hell are you not looking for him?”

Alpheas was immersed in anguish. Perhaps Olivia’s choice in the Judgment of the Twenty was the most rational decision anyone could make. But he knew how it felt when a precious person who could not be replaced with anything in the world disappeared right in front of his eyes. It was not a choice made for the sake of the world. Alpheas was also an inescapable madman.

“Dear Ethella… … I beg you.”

Perhaps Ethella would give permission. The Bishop of the Karsis Monastery and the immediate successor of the Yin-Yang Wave Fist. If he hadn’t humbled his body and mind with the conviction of one mind and three thousand, he would not have the talent to be in the current position. It was enough wood for Gaold to use.

“Stay here for a while. The agents will take care of me and Olivia. Let’s get ready to open Eastas.”

When Alpheas turned around with a tired face, Gaold finally regained his smile. Then, as if remembering something, he raised a hand. “Oh, wait.”

Alpheas turned around in front of the stairs. “Since you asked for it, invite one more person.”
