Chapter 406  
Entering the Competition System (1)  
~*~

The morning dawned bright on the first official day of the senior-year schedule.

The first evaluation of the first week was interpersonal combat. Thirty students would be divided into fifteen pairs and fight one-on-one.

The venue was Training Ground 2, located on the mountain behind the senior class building. A huge, 120-square-meter 2,000 battlefield had been prepared there.

The senior class gathered at the training ground by class and waited for the instructor in charge of the interpersonal combat evaluation.

Shirone stood between Nade and Iruki.

There were occasional conversations among students from the same class, but overall, the atmosphere was still awkward.

The instructor arrived exactly on time.

He was a man in his mid-fifties with gray hair and sharp eyes, and he was known for never saying anything unrelated to evaluations.

“Interpersonal combat evaluations begin today. Those of you from Class Two have already experienced this, but I’ll briefly review the rules for Class Three. Under the Kingdom Education Act, the synchro rate for 2,000 is set at 80%. Of course, for the graduation exam, it will be 100% without exception.”

Even 80% was nothing to take lightly.

Considering the superhuman power of magic, even 80% of a spell’s force was not something the body could withstand if struck directly.

Shirone had once faced Dante at a synchro rate of 50%, and even then the battle had been so fierce that it had hardly felt like a simulation.

“You may not bring weapons. Only embodied weapons are permitted.”

The students of Class Three tilted their heads, and as expected, Boyle, the model student, raised his hand.

“I have a question. What counts as embodied?”

“The simplest criterion is whether the weapon can be taken away. If it cannot be removed or stolen, it is considered embodied. In addition, even if it can technically be removed, if doing so would risk damaging the user’s bodily functions, the Association recognizes it as embodied.”

By that standard, Shirone could not use Armand.

On the other hand, Canis was free to fight alongside Harvest, since Harvest could not be taken from him.

Another person who came to Shirone’s mind was Richard from Fermi’s group.

Just as his nickname, Richard of Alchemy, suggested, he had modified 35% of his body through alchemical technology. The devices could be attached and detached, but because his body would not function normally without them, they were classified as embodied.

If Shirone cut off his right arm and connected it to Armand, he might be able to use Diamond Arms in the graduation exam—but he had never even considered doing something that extreme.

Some of the Class Three students looked displeased.

They understood that they themselves would never go so far as to modify their bodies, but it still felt unfair that someone else could gain an advantage by doing so.

Boyle turned to Canis beside him and said, “Good for you. It passed.”

It was only natural for Boyle to be envious. As someone who had to use slots for summoning magic, he could not help but envy Canis, who handled magical creatures without relying on slots.

Don’t worry, Canis thought. I can beat the likes of you even without Harvest.

He did not bother responding aloud.

Because of the Arcane incident, there was no student who did not know about Harvest’s existence. However, only Shirone and Amy knew the magical creature’s true nature.

The longer that secret was kept, the better.

Unless a truly critical situation arose, Harvest would not have a chance to take an active role.

Of course, Canis had no intention of entrusting his future entirely to a magical creature either.

I’ll win this time, Shirone. If you think Harvest is all I have, you’re mistaken.

After the questions ended, the instructor proceeded with the evaluation.

“Then we will begin the interpersonal battles in order. Battle time is ten minutes. Wear your 2,000 bracelets and wait for your turn.”

Aside from the pair being evaluated and the pair waiting next, the students were free to move around the training ground.

The atmosphere was different from the advanced classes, where discipline and principles had been emphasized.

Shirone found a seat with a good view of the training ground and sat down with his friends. Then he opened his schedule and said, “My opponent today is Dorothy. Iruki, what about you?”

“Hm? Oh, someone called Binder.”

Shirone turned his head toward the opposite side of the 2,000 training ground and looked at Binder, who was standing in order with the others.

He was a member of Class Two. Shirone had seen him at senior events before, but he had not left much of an impression.

He had light brown hair, glasses, and a sparse beard beneath his nose and on his chin.

Since he had failed to stand out in last year’s graduation exam, even Iruki, his opponent, seemed to have no doubts about winning.

“Well, should I call this a reserved three points?”

“Still, don’t let your guard down. You haven’t even fought him yet. Things can be different once you’re inside.”

“I said that while taking that into account. According to my calculations, he’s an opponent who can’t possibly become a variable.”

Shirone felt a little worried by Iruki’s confidence.

As Flew had said, it was foolish to decide the result in advance. Battles were not won or lost by numbers alone.

Well, I can’t really imagine Iruki getting impatient either.

In any case, for them, today’s main match was neither Shirone’s nor Iruki’s.

“By the way, Nade, are you okay?”

When Shirone asked, Nade shrugged.

“Well, I’ll try fighting him first.”

His opponent today was none other than Fermi.

Fermi was a mysterious student who had failed the graduation exam yet always took first place in the final evaluations.

When Shirone had first heard about Fermi, he had only been able to make a vague assessment of him. But now that Shirone understood what rankings meant in the senior class, he could not help but worry.

The other students seemed to be thinking the same thing, because they were all watching Fermi’s attitude.

Then, sensing their gazes, Fermi turned his head toward them. He smiled and waved at Nade, his opponent for the day.

“Is that bastard provoking me?”

“No, I think he’s just saying hello.”

Nade looked back at Shirone with an expression that seemed ready to bite.

“That’s provocation! Since when did that guy pretend he knew me well enough to greet me? Just watch. Today, I’m going to expose that bastard’s skills in detail.”

As Shirone watched the evaluations taking place on the training ground, he realized his turn was approaching and rose from his seat.

“Anyway, I should get ready.”

When Shirone entered the 2,000 training ground, Dorothy approached while dragging a tin doll behind her.

Every time she took a step, the tin doll scraped against the ground with a loud clacking sound.

Dorothy stopped right in front of Shirone. Her face was small, while her glasses were so large they looked like part of a masquerade mask. Her expressionless face and unfocused eyes made it impossible to read her emotions.

What should I do? Should I greet her?

The waiting procedure for interpersonal combat was the same as the 2,000 battles they had done in the advanced class.

Once both students checked their 2,000 bracelets at the center of the training ground and returned to their positions, a ten-minute timer would begin and 2,000 would activate.

There was no particular need to greet one’s opponent. Turning away coldly could even be considered a form of psychological warfare.

However, this was the first evaluation of the first week, and the students still had not fully gotten to know their peers. It would not feel right to abandon all courtesy.

Above all, that was not Shirone’s personality.

“Nice to meet you. This is an important evaluation for both of us, so let’s do our best.”

“How about a draw?”

At Dorothy’s sudden suggestion, Shirone immediately asked back, “What?”

“Let’s make it a draw. Then I’ll do anything you want.”

Before Shirone could even fully analyze what she meant, his face turned red. The words might not have sounded like much, but when a girl said them, it was a different matter.

“What do you mean by that?”

“I mean it literally. I’ll do whatever you want. You must also think revealing your skills from the very beginning is a disadvantageous strategy. So let’s take one point each.”

Shirone had no intention of taking part in collusion, but he still fell into thought, because it was important to determine whether the proposal itself was advantageous or not.

Her suggestion certainly had potential uses. It could allow him to create variables later, when they mattered most.

If she really agrees to my conditions.

In the case of a draw, each student received one point. What Shirone did not know was whether the value of the two points he would be giving up by not winning was included in her offer.

He wanted to win his first match if possible.

A good start would allow him to face future evaluations with confidence, and realistically, it would give him an advantage in the ranking competition.

“No, I’ll decline. I want to fight fair and square. So you should do your best too.”

Dorothy’s expression still did not change. She simply nodded as if she accepted his answer and turned away.

“Then it can’t be helped. Let’s do our best.”

Shirone stared blankly at Dorothy’s back as she returned to her position, dragging the tin doll after her.

If that had been psychological warfare, it had been devastating.

It had genuinely shaken him.

Calm down. Nothing happened. I just have to fight like I always do.

When Shirone arrived at the waiting area, the lights on the 2,000 electronic display changed from green to yellow to red, signaling activation.

Shirone waited with his knees slightly bent, ready to switch quickly between offense and defense.

A beep sounded, followed by a mechanical noise, and the light on his 2,000 bracelet came on.

Dorothy stood motionless, still holding the tin doll in her arms.

Shirone leaned forward, charged the teleportation battery, and raised his omnipotence.

Go on the attack!

At that exact moment, Dorothy turned and said, “I lost.”

Shirone, who had hurriedly canceled his magic, pitched forward.

By the time he barely managed to keep himself from falling and looked ahead, Dorothy was already walking down from the training ground.
