The Meaning of Survival (2)  
~*~

“Then I’m definitely going with Shirone.”

Amy moved to the red team, high-fived Shirone, Iruki, and Nade in turn, and settled in beside them.

“Sheesh. He’s just itching to stand out. This is why freshmen are hopeless.”

Screamer did not like the fact that Shirone had been the first to choose.

Even students who had spent two or three years in the senior class were cautious when it came to picking a team. In the middle of that, Shirone had suddenly raised his flag for one side, as if the hierarchy of the senior class meant nothing. To Screamer, it looked like nothing more than showing off.

Still, his gaze shifted to Dante.

‘That guy, at least, is a little better.’

The fact that Dante was still thinking meant there might be something similar between the kingdom’s former number one and its current number one. Screamer still did not like him, but letting emotions affect his judgment in an evaluation was a shortcut to elimination.

“Hey, join Fermi with me. I know because I did this last year. If you’re with Fermi, victory is guaranteed. Of course, it won’t be easy.”

If he brought Dante over, Shirone would be isolated. Then more students would be willing to join the blue team.

“Hmm.”

Dante did not even seem to be listening.

After standing alone in thought for a moment, he moved with his companions.

“Let’s go. I’ve decided.”

Screamer’s face twisted.

Even Dante, whose talent was said to be the best in the kingdom, had chosen to stand on the same side as Shirone.

‘What the hell? Are they children?’

When Dante, Canis, and Arin crossed the line and joined the red team, Screamer’s irritation went beyond disbelief.

‘Have they lost their minds? Do they even know what survival is? If they had just looked up last year’s results, the answer would have been obvious.’

In the previous year’s survival evaluation, the ratio between the red team and the blue team had been a staggering twenty-two to eight.

In other words, the majority’s choice had been correct.

Of course, Fermi had been among the twenty-two, and he had endured until the fifth stage to become the final survivor.

‘This is annoying. I can’t believe I have to go through this again.’

Screamer trembled.

What they would experience in the Virtual Zone today were extreme situations that would not appear even in nightmares.

The difficulty had been prepared up to level 7, but there was no need to reach that far anyway.

He had heard that, in the entire history of Alpheas Magic School, only one person had ever passed level 7 of survival.

Even that was nothing more than an unconfirmed rumor.

“Fine, whatever. Become sacrifices for my score. Let’s go, Pony.”

Screamer turned and headed for the blue team. Pony, who had been staring at Shirone for a while, followed him.

As more students chose their teams, Boyle made his decision after long deliberation and spoke to Pandora.

“I’m going with the blue team. You should choose quickly too, unless you want to hear later that you only put your fork on someone else’s plate.”

Pandora tilted her head.

“Blue? Do you have a reason to be confident?”

“I have my own reasons. But I won’t tell you. I don’t want to influence your judgment.”

“It’s fine. Tell me. I won’t be swayed.”

Boyle glanced around, then leaned in and whispered into Pandora’s ear.

“As you know, survival is an event that evaluates self-denial. It’s not about skill or anything like that. It’s about endurance.”

“And?”

“I heard there’s only one person in the school’s history who has ever completed survival. We probably won’t make it to the end either. That means the best choice is to leave it to someone who specializes in this and focus on our records.”

“Hmm…”

“You know Fermi has been the final survivor four years in a row.”

“I understand what you mean. But if that’s the case, Shirone won’t be easy to beat either. He’s an unlocker. His mental strength must be considerable. In fact, he showed good results in cognition and judgment.”

Boyle let out a weary sigh.

Why was the difficulty level of the survival evaluation set so high?

Because if even one person could endure it all the way through, the confrontation itself would not work in the first place.

If someone could pass level 7 without fail, then unless everyone else was an idiot, they would all cling to that person. The team balance would end up thirty to zero.

Therefore, both Shirone and Fermi would be eliminated eventually.

If that was the case, then Fermi, who had taken the survival test for at least four years, clearly had the advantage.

The fact that students from Class Two and above were heading to the blue team without hesitation also supported Boyle’s reasoning.

“Well, you decide. Either way, I’m going with Fermi.”

Boyle did not say everything he was thinking.

In the end, it was a matter of value judgment. It was one thing to share information, as they had studied together since childhood, but the moment he tried to persuade her, their alliance would start to crack.

Pandora watched Boyle enter the blue team.

She was troubled too, but in the end, the one whose ability she had confirmed with her own eyes was Shirone.

The reason most of Class Three had joined the red team was probably because they had thoughts similar to hers.

“Ah, whatever. It’s one or the other anyway.”

Pandora followed Boyle to the blue team.

Even in the senior class, where there was no blood or tears, she understood the importance of allies.

‘If it’s fifty-fifty, then let’s die together.’

By the time Pandora chose the blue team, the shape of each team was beginning to emerge.

As expected, the students who had not achieved high scores in the evaluations remained cautious until the end.

One of them was Maya.

She had been in the senior class for a long time, so she knew it was advantageous to side with Fermi.

‘Hmm, I suppose…’

Maya turned toward Fermi. Then Screamer, who was already on the blue team, lifted his chin and called out.

“Hey, are you sure you want to come here?”

Collie spoke sharply.

“Screamer, outside pressure is prohibited during team selection.”

“I was just asking.”

“Warning. If you receive one more warning, you will be eliminated.”

“Sheesh!”

Screamer clicked his tongue and turned his head. But even this was part of his calculation.

Maya was worth pushing away at the cost of a single warning.

Until now, he had always been grouped with Maya in strategy and tactics, and as a result, he had suffered severe point losses.

If they ended up on the same team here, the difference in their points would remain the same whether they won or lost, and he would not be able to avoid being grouped with her again in strategy and tactics.

On the other hand, if their teams split and he won, the forty-point gap would widen, allowing him to escape the disgusting Maya.

‘Please, let’s split up here. You go to Class Three, and I’ll go to Class One.’

Depressed, Maya moved to the red team.

Even if it was not external pressure, she did not have the courage to become teammates with Screamer after seeing such open hostility from him.

She approached Shirone.

Since he had been the first to pick the red team, she thought of it as Shirone’s team.

“Will you accept me?”

Shirone answered with a bright smile.

“Of course. It’s advantageous to have more people. And it’s not that I accepted you—you chose our team.”

Iruki and Nade stepped aside to the left and right, opening a spot for her as if inviting her in.

Although they were irritated by Screamer’s pettiness, it was also a strategic decision to give Maya a sense of belonging.

‘Self-denial is governed by psychology. It’s important for even one more person to last longer.’

In the end, those with strong endurance would remain, but being together with a teammate could itself become a powerful weapon.

“I want to go this way too.”

Dorothy entered the red team with sleepy eyes.

Then Luman followed her, and the timid Suabi also moved cautiously after him, keeping an eye on the others.

‘Oh, this is unexpected.’

Nade glanced sideways at the three who had just joined.

Considering that Luman, who did not like Shirone, had chosen the red team, it was highly likely that Dorothy had been the reason behind his decision.

Of course, Dorothy was almost the only person Luman was close to, but the fact that indecisive Suabi had immediately followed them also deserved reconsideration.

‘Some people in Class Two have considerable faith in Dorothy’s judgment. Which means… is she really the brain type, like Iruki said?’

Once everyone had chosen, only Eider remained.

Being the youngest was troublesome even in a situation like this.

The older brothers and sisters he was usually close to had split between the two teams, so he was worried about which side he should join.

‘Hmm, where should I go if I want people to say I lined up well?’

Shirone was looking at him with a kind smile.

Feeling a sense of trust from the red team, Eider smiled and was about to jump over to them.

“Hmm! Hmm!”

Then Screamer covered his mouth and cleared his throat.

Since he would be eliminated if he received one more warning, all he could do was send a signal through sound.

Fortunately, Eider turned his head, as if the signal had worked.

Collie’s sharp eyes were watching the students, so Screamer looked away and pretended to be distracted.

‘Screamer hyung…’

In the senior class, the person who spoiled Eider the most was Screamer.

However, because Screamer took care of him that much, he was also someone Eider could never afford to look down on.

After changing his mind, Eider bowed his head to everyone on the red team, then turned and ran toward the blue team.

“Teacher! I’m sorry! I’ll change teams.”

Collie sighed.

However, she could not give a warning based only on a feeling, so the procedure was concluded.

Coincidentally, the red team and blue team ended up exactly even, fifteen to fifteen.

‘Hmm. An even split. It’s been quite a while.’

No, it was the first time since Fermi had begun standing out in the survival test.

‘Shirone’s presence is that significant.’

The advanced class and the senior class were completely different worlds, but the title of the kingdom’s number one prospect was still powerful.

The fact that Shirone had earned that title by defeating Dante, the former strongest, must have played a part as well.

“Now that the teams have been decided, enter the Virtual Zone.”

As the thirty students moved together toward the center of the Virtual Zone, Collie followed them and explained.

“Listen carefully, everyone. This is a matter of life and death.”

The students of Class Three, who were taking the survival test for the first time, looked especially tense.

“This Virtual Zone does not use the two-thousandfold acceleration. That means information will not be converted into anti-magic and transmitted. The runes engraved on the floor use sophisticated magic engineering techniques to inject certain senses directly into your Spirit Zone as if they were real.”

If the body was under the control of the mind, then it would not be strange for a person locked in a room-temperature cold storage to freeze to death. This time, however, information would be driven directly into the Spirit Zone, an extremely sensitive state of mind.

“No matter how virtual it is, it can still strain your mind. Once you enter the survival test, the shock will put your brain into a hemispheric state. Therefore, it will be impossible for you to consciously leave the Virtual Zone.”

A dark shadow fell over the faces of the Class Three students.

“From level 4 onward in particular, it becomes truly dangerous, so the teachers will monitor your condition through visions. However, since this is an official red-line evaluation, remember that even if a dangerous situation occurs, we may not be able to save you.”

Collie raised two index fingers.

“There are two ways for you to leave the Virtual Zone. First, release your Spirit Zone. Second, if you cannot release your Spirit Zone no matter what, shout ‘Toako.’ Remember this clearly. It is Toako.”

Toako was a meaningless word, a password randomly generated for the survival test.

That was because using a meaningful word left room for abuse during the test.

“The teachers will never respond to any word other than Toako. ‘Please save me,’ ‘I give up,’ ‘Something is wrong with the Virtual Zone’—no matter what you say, if it is not Toako, we will not save you.”

All the students in the graduating class looked solemn.

“What I ask of you is that you do your best, but do not be stubborn. This is only a rehearsal. Also, if you feel anything abnormal after leaving the Virtual Zone, speak up immediately and receive mental treatment. Do you understand?”

“Yes!”

The students’ voices echoed through the mountains of the training ground.
